Todd H
Bass, Drums, Backing Vocals

Todd joined Triple Threat in March of 2001. Todd has been in bands since the age of 12 on drums and on and off on bass since he was 18. "Drums were my first love. I tried guitar--but it never really took. Then, in 1989, I saw an amazing bass player who broke all the stiff bass player stereotypes--he transformed mundane 70's tunes into funk wonders and made me think 'I wanna do that.' Before I knew it, I traded my guitar rig in on a bass rig."

Todd jokes: "My goal on either instrument is to rise above the shackles of my race and help the rhythm section cut a groove deep enough to swim in."

Todd's bass influences include Tad Kinchla of Blues Traveler, Geddy Lee of Rush, friend and former bandmate Jim Billmeier (who would be mortified to know he was mentioned in the same sentence as Rush), and funk/blues master Sam Cockrell (Chicago local). On drums, Todd was heavily influenced by Neil Peart of Rush, but admits "I'm feeling much better now." Other heavy influences include Carter Beauford of the Dave Matthews Band, Stewart Copeland of the Police, Dan Tomlinson (tour drummer with Lyle Lovett), and John Von Ohlen of the Blue Wisp Big Band in Cincinnati, OH. Prior to Brian joining Triple Threat, Todd would frequently fill in on drums for half a set allowing Tony to sing lead, with the keys player covering the bass parts. Now, Todd only gets to drum on acoustic duo gigs as one of Dean's"Drummers du jour."

Random q/a with Todd:

Favorite drink: Pint o' Bass (pithy, yes) and a water.

Favorite TT original: Into the Night Hey--Brian and I agree on something!

Favorite TT cover: Popa Chubby's "Palace of the King" off Booty and the Beast. Drivin, funky, scorchin Chubby-ness.

Favorite piece of musical gear: "Bub," Peavey Cirrus 4 w/ a Bubbinga top. I really like my Eden cabinet too....and my GK head. Ah hell, I just like GEAR!

Most shameful piece of musical gear owned: Oh that's easy because it's really shameful: music stand with chart book at the ready. While Dean remembers every song he's ever played since 8th grade, on bass, I can't remember anything we haven't played in the past 8 weeks...and our catalog is rather largish!

What motivated you to start in music? MTV and cable came into my home in the very early 80's. You know, back in their first year when they played music videos. Everyone looked cool--especially that little Italian drummer from Loverboy with all those drums around him on Workin for the Weekend.

What artist do you like that you feel really guilty about liking? Duran Duran. John Taylor is a monster bassist mostly obscured by the group's panty-moistening pop stardom in the 80's.

If I were on a desert island, was smart enough to build a CD player and headphones from coconuts and palm leaves and fashion a solar panel to power it, and could only have 1 album to play in it, what would it be: Popa Chubby Booty and the Beast (this answer changes weekly though)

Most interesting experience had while peforming (music that is): At a Blues benefit at a home for the mentally challenged, seeing people dancing their hineys off while wearing soft helmets at 4pm in the afternoon--that was cool. Followed closely by at that same gig, seeing a resident come into the gym with his pants down with another resident trailing behind him trying to pull his pants back up.

Song that changed your life and why: Probably the first 4 songs by Rush off the Exit Stage Left live album experienced shortly after I took up drums. Neil and Geddy set me in motion for being a bass/drum rhythm section maven.

Dream car: Still searching for it, but it gets 200MPG, has zero emissions, can hold my PA and bass rig, does 0-60 in 5 seconds, costs $1000, requires no maintenance, has a kickin sound system, and can't be broken into without mortally wounding the would-be thief. Oh and cup holders....lots of cupholders.

What you actually drive: 2001 Subaru Outback. Chicks dig guys in glorified station wagons, yeah baby! It does fit a lot of gear though....

Most frequently encountered musical question or request: Freebird. I mean, in Chicago? We're closer to Canada than the Mason-Dixon line. You know, one day we'll play it--The whole damned thing. And keep playing it until the (invariably drunk guy trying to make his buddies laugh) that thought it'd be funny to yell "Freebird" pays us $100 to stop, or gets pummeled by others in the bar. News would spread, and this phenomenon might end. At least that's the dream. Am I still talking? Really, I'm not this bitter. :-)

Favorite clean/short joke: A horse walks into a bar, walks up to the bar. Bartender looks him up and down and says "Hey buddy, why the long face?"

Name something about your bandmates that most people wouldn't guess: Dean's a 3rd dan blackbelt, a former semi-pro baseball and football player, and the only person I know that's busier than I am. Brian is the slammingest drumming middle school health teacher I've ever met! (Ask him all your STD questions.) Tony was once a Roman Catholic bishop before he discovered women. Note: One of the prior 3 statements is false.
