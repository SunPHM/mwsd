:: Facts
Bass have been brewing ales in Burton-upon-Trent since 1834.

The UK has the best draught and tap selection of beers in the whole world.

Britain's brewers brew nearly 27 million pints a day. There are over 250 brewing companies operating. In addition about 150 pubs brew their own beer.

The British enjoy their beer, drinking on average 180 pints a year.

Profile >>>
Information from here down has been submitted by Harry Butt from Stafford - who describes himself as the most-travelled man in Staffordshire, and a complete tourism nut! Thanks Harry.

Mostly this is all about beer, Bass beer in particular of course, - its manufacture, and its uses (you didn't know it had more than one use, did you?). But it does have more extras and events than you'd think.
Ticket includes a free drink. Yee-ha!
Fairly top place.

 What's on?
Check the museum's own website of course!

Admission >>>
See Coors/Bass website

Brewery Tours are available Mon-Thur 10am, 1pm and 7pm. Daytime tours include museum admission

Please please use their website to check details - then you can't blame us if things have changed!

Facilities >>>
Full disabled access to all museum galleries and public attractions - some outside areas outside are cobbled. Ramps and lifts are positioned where appropriate. Seating available throughout the Museum. Enabling dogs are welcome.

Free audio guides are available in English, French, Italian, German and Spanish. Toilets - specially adapted toilets and baby care facilities available.

Free parking with disabled parking available.

Extra Information >>>
Any place that features Britain's oldest micro-brewery has my vote.
