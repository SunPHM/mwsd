WFDU "Sounds of Blues" Fundraiser
2/21/04 Teaneck NJ
6 Hour All Live Radio Show
Photos at Bottom

Thanks to all who pledged to Saturday’s, “Sounds of Blue, extended edition”! If you were fortunate enough to catch this unique 6 hour all live radio program, you would have heard: The Aces aka: The Fins, Rob Paparozzi with Dave Keyes, Sarah Partridge, Nikki Armstrong’s Whole lot of Blues, The Vivino Brothers Band with Michael Merritt, Dave Keyes, Eric Kalb, and Melvin Sparks sitting in, The John Tropea Band, Melvin Sparks Band with Dave Keyes & Stanley Banks, Tommy Mitchell with John Tropea & Baron Ramonde, and last but not least, Jay Monterose, Matt Daniels, and Charlie Cowles tribute to Danny Gatton. A warm hearted thank you to all the musicians who made this night an incredible ground breaking broadcast! Also thanks to our supporters, Victor’s House of Music, Mexicali Blues, Dae Bennett who did a superb job with the sound, Allen Rosenberg who shot the video, Judy MacKenzie for photography, Glen Scharback for recording and lots more, and Bill Galbraith for his drum kit. Finally, thanks to everyone involved at WFDU, Carl, Barry, Mario, Dan, Damian, and Beverly.

Bob Putignano
President NY Blues & Jazz Society
Radio host of “The Sounds of Blue”
WFDU-Fm 89.1 & www.WFDU.Fm.

To All Friends of The Blues:

I write on behalf of Barry and all of our staff to thank you for sharing your time and talent with us and our listeners during the recent live broadcast this past Saturday(2.21.04). As you may know, this was the initial broadcast from the newly christened Atomic Underground studio. Having been in the broadcast game for a few decades and having been born outside the boom days of live radio, I truly can't tell you how wonderful it was to merge the extraordinary talent present that night with the magic of radio and the internet to reach the world! It is absolutely a wonderment.

I thank Nikki Armstrong and Bob Putignano for recruiting the talent, staff and equipment that evening and managing the very smooth flow of acts through an "intimate"space. I(we) learned a lot that evening and will bring those lessons forward to our remote broadcasts of the future.

Meanwhile, please accept my sincere gratitude for your contribution to this radio station.

Carl J. Kraus
General Manager WFDU(FM)
