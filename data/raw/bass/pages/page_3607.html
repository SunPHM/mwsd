The Seawater Room is a 2,000 square foot facility with multiple stations providing a continuous flow of raw or filtered, heated or chilled, seawater. It is an ideal facility for investigating marine biota and the environment.

With the capacity to raise 1200 gallons of marine phytoplankton, billions of rotifers and millions of Artemia, a food chain can be constructed to rear tens of thousands of larval fish.

The facility has had great success rearing summer flounder larvae through metamorphosis and there are plans to rear many more marine fish for release in local waters. Currently, the state-of-the-art facility is holding summer flounder and black sea bass brood stocks for production of juvenile fish.

Other ongoing projects in the Seawater Room include:
• A study of the differences in growth rates and spawning cycles for sea scallops from two different aggregations.
• The non-indigenous, invasive crab Hemigrapsus is being studied for its influence on the ecology of intertidal and littoral habitat.
• Biofouling investigations.
• The construction of a display tank that will exhibit some of the amazing fishes of Buzzards Bay and demonstrate the workings of an aquaculture recirculating system.
