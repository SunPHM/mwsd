They have created a Bassman's dream!

Huites is quickly becoming the premier bass lake in the world, some argue it already is. Huites is a bass fisherman's dream, and you don't want to miss out. Hundreds of bass over 10 pounds have been caught and released at this great new lake.


Where is Lake Huites?
Lake Huites is nestled in the foothills of the Sierra Madre Mountains, and was created by damming two rivers. Huites is 4500 feet above sea level and at full pool in the fall, this 30,000 acre lake is 18-miles long in one direction and 15 miles long in another. This beautiful lake has over 100 miles of shoreline to fish and has every conceivable structure a bass could want.

What kind of bass are we talking about?

Initially, it was stocked with Florida Bass from the El Fuerte River system back in the 70's. Once the dam was finished, it was repeatedly stocked with Florida Strain Largemouth Bass. These bass are growing at a rate of 2 pounds per year, so it's no wonder so many fishermen are looking for their largest Bass to be caught at this beautiful reservoir. One of the reasons Lake Huites is so full of fast growing Bass is because of the huge forage base and the structure that exists along its diverse banks and shores. The average Bass in this young lake is already around 4 to 5 pounds, and it's not uncommon for experienced anglers to have catches of 100 + bass per day! The bass normally weigh in anywhere from 2 to 14 lbs. The current lake record stands at a whopping 15 pounds 3 ounces! Since Huites a strictly catch and release lake, what that means is that monster is still out there! Easily 18 pounds by now. Considering the age of Huites, that has got to be impressive. This lake has the potential to produce the next world record some day.

 When is the best time to go?

The Best times to go are between September and May, and you can use a variety of techniques depending on the season and time. From early October until December, the bass at Lake Huites will hit almost anything you throw at them. You can get awesome top water action, or work the humps with worms and lizards. In January the bass begin to make the move into their pre-spawn mood and you can nail them with a spinner bait or switch up to plastics and crank baits, which do almost as well. When March arrives it bring spawning time around and females are bursting with eggs. If you work it slow and deliberate the females are liable to hit just about anything you put in the water. March can bring some of the most ferocious fishing action on Lake Huites.
During April, May and June, it's post spawn season and you, the angler, can expect to get into the good numbers. Head for deep water, and why not try rolling a lipless crank for results? Maybe roll a big spinner real slow? This is what makes Huites such a great lake, and what real bass fishing is all about, you can try just about any technique with good results. Late fall to late spring Huites is rocking the world with some of the best bass action ever!
The advantage of Lake Huites is that not many drive-in boaters from the States or Mexico fish these serene waters.
Commercial netting has some good restrictions and enforcement. In fact, there are no nets at Huites between April and October, some of the peak months. These measures have been taken to insure that Huites stays one of the best lakes for anglers to fish for the years to come, when other lakes will have been depleted, Huites will continue to churn out the monster bass anglers want!
