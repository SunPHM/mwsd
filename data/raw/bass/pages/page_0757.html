Radio Special with Linda Evans Shepherd

Can romance be a lifelong adventure with the same spouse? Yes! But a marriage like that is no accident. It requires commitment, trust, communication, love, and the ability to survive the transitions of life. Join your host, Linda Evans Shepherd, as she takes a look at the five stages of romance:

STAGE ONE: Preparing for Romance

Eric and Leslie Ludy talk about foundations for marriage, the importance of purity, and explain how to let God write your love story.

Eric and Leslie are best-selling authors, recording artists, and nationally known speakers. The Ludys are widely recognized as leading voices to the younger generation on relationship success. They are the author’s of When God Writes Your Love Story and When Dreams Come True and the co-founders of R Generation, which trains young Christian leaders.

STAGE TWO: Early Marriage

Mark and Darla Sanborn discuss how to adjust from singleness to life as a couple.

Mark and Darla have been married over 5 years so have ‘survived’ their newlywed days. Mark is an internationally known speaker in the area of leadership. He is a contributing columnist for Life@Work and coauthored or wrote 8 books including Meditations for the Road Warrior. Darla is a native of Memphis, Tennessee, and formerly an HR director for an HMO, and now a mother of two.

STAGE THREE: Romance When the Kids Come

Pete and Lorraine share how to find romance with the children wrapped around your knees.

Pete and Lorraine Pintus, are speakers and founders of Hearts at Home Ministries, an organization dedicated to building Christ center marriage and family relationships. Lorraine has authored Diapers, Pacifiers, and Other Holy Things and is the co-author of Intimate Issues. Pete and Lorraine have been married since 1980 and have two children, Amanda and Megan.

STAGE FOUR: Middle Age Romance

Bob and Bev Weston discuss the benefits of deciding to stay married and rekindling romance in the midst of mid-life.

Bob and Bev have been married over 40 years and parented four children. Bob is a psychotherapist and marriage counselor. Bev is a speaker and both Bob and Bev are life coaches, helping people find direction for their lives and careers.


STAGE FIVE: Romance in the Golden Years

John and Betty Bass look back at how they kept romance in their marriage for over 50 years.

John and Betty have enjoyed a lifetime of romance. Betty is a homemaker, and John is the former President of the Christian Booksellers Association. They make their home in Colorado Springs.
