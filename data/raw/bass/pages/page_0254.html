1st Annual United Way Pick Your Partner Bass Tournament
November 2, 2002
Pickwick Dam, TN

196 competitors arrived at a cold and foggy Pickwick Lake for the First Annual United Way Pick Your Partner Bass Tournament. As the the fog began to clear our competitors were already in route to their favorite fishing holes by 6:38 AM. Only 52 of our 101 teams weighed in fish. The bite was truly tough as the entire field only weighed in 184 fish with an average weight of 1.74 pounds.
Our first place team of Phil and Cathy Burnett from Ramer, TN topped the field with a 12.81 pound sack of fish. They also missed the big fish trophy by a mere .59 pounds! All of their fish were caught from 10:30 to 11:30 AM. Phil told us they did not catch another fish before or after that time of any size. They were waiting for the bass to bust on the shad and then threw spinner baits at the swirl! Cathy was using a chartreuse LEDGE BUSTER spinner bait with painted blades. Phil caught all of his fish on a white STRIKE KING spinner bait with silver and gold willow leaf blades. Phil and Cathy live only 30 minutes from Pickwick Lake and fish it most weekends. Phil found out about our tournament while fishing as an amateur in the B.A.S.S. Alabama Southern Open where he finished in 3rd place. He earned $4000.00 for his efforts and Cathy helped him add $1500.00 from our event. Congratulations to Cathy and Phil from all of us at FedEx and The United Way!
Second place went to one of the two STRIKE KING Teams of Allan Ranson and Jim Lovin from Collierville, TN. They managed an 11.93 pound catch with series 3 and 4 STRIKE KING Pro Model crank baits in a fire tiger pattern. They fished shallow water near secondary tributaries. Although they caught fish all during the day, the middle of the day seemed to treat them the best. Thanks to Chris Brown of STRIKE KING for sending two teams and supporting us with crank baits, plastics, and spinner baits for all of our fishermen.
Rounding out the top three teams were John Paulk from Crump, TN and Clay Talley from Savannah, TN. All of their fish were caught on ZOOM green pumpkin baby brush hogs in about four feet of water. They went to three different holes before attempting to fish the fourth and last spot they would need. When they arrived they found a boat sitting on the spot. They waited for that team to leave this spot before moving in for the kill. They paralleled a point in the back of a creek with a Carolina rig to weigh in 11.48 pounds and claim third place.
Big bass honors went to the team of Mike Gowrylok from Oakland, TN and Kevin Murchison from Bartlett, TN. Mike caught his 5.81 pound largemouth on a Zoom lizard to take home a $1000.00 check. The rest of the story will remain Mike's secret! Mike also fishes many charity tournaments and with the Fishers of Men Tournament Trail.
The event raised $5000.00 for The United Way of the Mid South. We paid out approximately $12,000.00 in cash and prizes. All of our goodie bags and door prizes were donated by our sponsors. Please take the time to click on our sponsor page and say thanks on our guest book. You can also click on the sponsor icon and go to their web site. Please support them by buying their products or even give them a call and say thanks.

Thanks to all the volunteers who helped put this event together. Without you it would not have happened.

Good luck on the water!

Brad Ammer
