Violin by Jacob Horil
Rome, ca. 1750

 

Label: Christianus Franciscus Partl / Fecit Wien 1771

Jacob Horil was a native of Bohemia, resided in Viennna from 1720 onwards and settled down in Rome ca. 1740. His style retains the overall characteristcs of his Northern Provenance, abstaining from incorporating Italianisms..As a matter of fact, this violin, with its swung scroll and short, round sound holes is remniscent of the work of the Viennese School, leading some experts to doubt the certification.

Body length 352 mm
Upper width 159 mm
Middle width 107 mm
Lower width 197 mm
Rib height   32 mm
