WELCOME TO CAROL GRAVELIN'S FISHING PAGE

Thank you for visiting my fishing site, where you'll find the latest fishing GPS coordinates and current pictures of the latest catches and updates on where the fish are and aren't.

Another beautiful day in paradise. The water temperature was around 84 degrees and the wind was minimal. We went out to the Dunedin Reef hoping to have the same action that we had a few weeks ago but no such luck. The water was just too warm for the bigger fish. The pictures above are in Hurricane Pass, Dunedin. As you can see it was a really Low tide and our swimming site was sand. There is the ferry that go to Calidise Island.

While everyone was recovering from Memorial Day weekend we were fishing on the Dunedin Reef. We were the only boat there and the fishing was great. We went thru all our tackle and lost one pole.

This barracuda spent the whole day with us swimming around the boat. We tried catching him with everthing and he just didn't want it. Around 2pm I deceided to catch more bait fish and started reeling in this sheepshead. That must have been what the Barracuda was waiting for cause he ate more than half of it as I was reeling it in.

What a beautiful day on St Joseph Sound. We spent the morning fishing a caught a huge 8'+ Nurse shark just between Honeymoon & 3 Rooker. She was longer than the front of the boat which is 8' wide. We also caught this Black tip Shark. We saw Grandpas Dream going out, what a beautiful boat.

The fishings been really good for Trout, Whiting and Ladyfish. These are just a few pictures I took during spring break. Isn't that why we live here?

The water temperature was around 71-73 degrees and a lot of sea fog. Here's a spanish mackerel and a 21" sea trout.

What a beautiful February 26th day it was. The water temperature was at 68 degrees where we caught these fish, Bluefish, Trout and Spanish Mackerel. We actually got a sunburn. The island is 3 Rooker just north of Honeymoon Island.

This is a 4' Nurse shark that I caught north of Honeymoon Island. We didn't keep her, we also caught lots of Black Sea Bass, lots of Black tip sharks and for our next outing squirel fish for grouper.

This blacktip was caught north of the second bridge on Honeymoon Island, we didn't keep it so next time he'll be bigger.

I'm holding a 33" Covia and Tom's got a Spanish Mackerel

Wow what a catch I just had to take a picture of this. Just north of honey moon island using live bait we caught 3 Cobia, flounder, a flying fish and bluefish.

Well the fish have finally come back to St Joseph Sound and we caught just about everything except Catfish YEA!. Above is Chuck & Tom with Blacktip Shark and Spanish Mackerel.

Look at the size of this Cobia! Caught by Chuck in between 3 Rooker and Honeymoon.


Clean the fish & pack in ice. For cooking I soak the cleaned fish (Cobia & Shark) in milk overnight Rinse the fish before cooking and season with butter, season salt. lemon, parmasian cheese, italian breadcrumbs and white wine. Bake in the oven at 350 degree for about 30 min. or until light & flaky. Enjoy!
