Lake Huites
The rising star of Mexico's bass producing lakes is Lake Huites (pronounced wheat-us) which opened to the public for the first time in 1997. Huites is located east of El Fuerte in the foothills of the Sierra Madres a 1½ hour drive from EL Fuerte.

Huites is a relatively young lake with a reputation for producing large numbers of aggressive large mouth bass in the 3 to 7 pound range. As the lake has matured fish in excess of ten pounds are being caught more regularly.

Villa Del Pescador operates a satellite Lodge (El Pescador) on the Shores of Lake Huites with a maximum capacity of twelve fishing guests. The Lodge was built in the fall of 1997 and has a dining room with a bar, bedrooms with a bath and shower and a large outdoor portal. The lodge is comfortable and is ideally located for easy access to the boats and the lake. The view of the lake and surrounding mountains is magnificent.

Lake Dominguez & Lake Hidalgo
Two of Mexico's older but stable and productive bass lakes are a short 15 minute drive on paved roads from the "Villa." Lake Dominquez and Lake Hidalgo have been fished by guests of Villa Del Pescador for over 20 years, and continue to schedule trips to these two lakes for an opportunity to catch bass in excess of 10 pounds.

Although daily catches are often in the 12 to 15 fish range, these lakes afford a real chance to catch a trophy bass. Recently a 12 pound 9 ounce largemouth was caught and released by one of our guests and a local resident caught a largemouth weighing just over 16 pounds. These lakes continue to be great bass fishing lakes and should be considered as an option when fishing bass in Mexico.
