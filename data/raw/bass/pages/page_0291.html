Bass Ageing 
2003 Summary

It has been, without doubt, an excellent period for Bass fishing along the Fylde coast over the last couple of years.  See our catch reports pages for some of the details.  Have you ever wondered a little more about the life history or age of the fish you have caught?  Bass grow quite slowly, depending on water temperature and food supply. Sexual maturity is reached around the age of 4 - 7 years that's when males reach about 36cm and females 42cm.  These fish are typically 3 to 4lb in weight.

Click image for larger view

Larger fish in the 7 to 8lb class can be in the region of 10+ years old and have proved themselves beyond doubt.

Bass, over the last decade or so, have had a number of good spawning years, especially since 1989.  Along with increased conservation measures, the Bass population appears to be in good health and the proportion of larger fish caught appears to be on the increase.

In recognition of the this, a number of club members have decided to increase awareness and obtain a better understanding of the Bass population found in Morecambe bay by offering a Bass ageing service to members of the Fylde Boat Angling Club with the details being publish on the website.  The idea is to gain a better understanding of the fish age population, growth rates and additionally raise awareness within the membership.  

Hopefully this will lead to members returning the larger mature spawning fish and therefore conserve our home waters stock and have instead a photograph, a slot on the website and potentially a brief life history of that Trophy fish.

Ageing of Bass is relatively simple, all that is required is the removal of a scale or two from the shoulder of the fish, ideally with a small pair of tweezers.  The scale can then be read under magnification and the fish aged like reading the rings on a tree.  However not every body has access to a suitable microscope, so Phill Williams has offered to undertake the reading on behalf of club members.

The process is quite simple,  one or two scales are removed from the shoulder of the fish, placed in a small bag which can be obtained from the FBAC Clubhouse, Phill Williams, Dave Woods, Steve Bateson or Dave Wilson and sent to the address on the form on the small bag.  All that is asked is that the following details are also recorded on the form: date captured, length of fish from nose to bottom of fork in tail, see how to below, if possible weight and general location i.e. Kings scar, Rossall, etc.

As the data is gathered then the information will be displayed graphically here.
