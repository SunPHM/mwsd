Comments:

     Although this bass has its share of pretty details, at its heart it is a back-to-basics kind of instrument. There is no preamp (though there is a large cavity able to accept one later if you should choose to add one), just the traditional volume-volume-tone controls mated to a set of single coil Aero jazz-type pickups. These pickups are 1/8 inch taller than the standard jazz pickup depth, allowing for more winding for a deep, defined sound. With the tone control full on, the sound has a pronounced, wiry snarl, with lots of punch and detail on top. The mids are clear and present and with the neck pickup turned down a bit the bridge pickup really begins to growl. With the highs intact the tone is very forward and aggressive - this is the kind of fretless bass that one could easily play hard rock or even metal with - the possibilities are exciting to consider. On the other hand rolling off the high end produces everything from a surprisingly mellow, round sound, to producing a dull, inarticulate dub bass vibe - a real split personality bass!
