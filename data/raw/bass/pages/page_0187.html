Traitor Jam Session
  
  

Held Saturday August 25, 2001

L Room Studios, Concord, AL
 

 
  
First we should acknowledge this session was held on Gene's Birthday.  Happy Birthday to Gene Simonio aka Davy Crockett.  This session was labeled the Traitor Session by Barry.  It all started with Jessica's Baby Shower, which was held this day as well.  Brooke was gone to the shower, and Crowe made the trek out to Dan's, so we decided to jam. We had just warmed up when Barry and my Dad arrived.  Due to the controversy over the last jam session, Barry called us traitors.  However, it should be noted that Barry participated on one song in this session, Screwed Up.  I think it was more than he could handle, because they left after that one.  Dan, Crowe and I went on to record Doody Hole Revisited.  This was an old song written by Kevin Blackwell and Joey Lagle.  Ironically, Dan's amp crapped out on him before this song and he had to play through my Ampeg Reverberocket Model GS-12R, with a pre-amp Digitech RP3 for distortion.  What's ironic about that is that the Ampeg used to belong to Kevin Blackwell.  I think the Widow had something to do with this.  Dan said that weird things were happening to his amp when we were getting ready to play the song.  The Widow's powers may still yet be great.  Controversy abounds in L Room Studios. 
  
  

Performing musicians

  
 

Scott "Crowe" DeFreese -Percussions

Crowe played Matt Arson's drums, which are Tama Rhythm Mates.  He also added a floor tom and two cymbals
 
  

Barry Welch -Bass Guitar

Barry played Brian Burton's Yamaha RBX 800A, through his Peavy Mark VI XP Series Head and full Bass cabinet.
 

Brian Welch - Rhythm Guitar

Brian Plays a Ace Frehley Custom Les Paul.   His amplification is through a Marshall S80 Valvestate Combo. 
 
 

Dan Taft - Lead and Rhythm Guitars

Dan Plays a Les Paul Classic '58 Reissue.  His amplification is through a Marshall S80 Valvestate Combo

Songs
 
  

Screwed Up

Doody Hole Revisited
