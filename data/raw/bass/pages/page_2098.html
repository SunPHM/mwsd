Comments:

     This bass sounds very, very good. With everything set flat, the bass has a satisfying dry sound full of growl and warmth. The powerful EQ section only makes this already great sounding bass a more flexible and powerful tool for self-expression. We are extremely impressed.
     Aesthetically, this bass is not super-fancy, but still elegant and utilizing the highest quality materials - looking at the bass closely you can easily see the incredible care that went into selecting only the best pieces of woods for this bass. The quilt maple top is mesmerizing in its sheen and movement, and even the ash is pretty (it's so clean!). Pete Hanewinckel is obviously a master woodworker, and every aspect of the bass's fit and finish is incredibly well done. The fretwork is first rate, and the neck is also very comfortable and plays effortlessly, as one would expect from such a lovely bass.
     We are very excited with what we are seeing - a 'new' group of luthiers, after many years of hard work and careful development of their skills are finally becoming more well-known in the bass world: Pete Hanewinckel, Mike Kinal, Carey Nordstrand, David King, et. al - the body of bassdom is afflicted with a serious case of rigor mortis, and the dedication of these builders to create beautiful, well-conceived, solid tools for working musicians is really one of the few bright spots going on at the moment.
