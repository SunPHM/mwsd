Work

(gotta put this stuff on here so nobody thinks this page is just a place to put baby pictures for the family)

I am an associate in research (soft-money faculty) at the Institute of Biological Chemistry at Washington State University I work in the laboratory of Rodney Croteau on the biosynthesis of the terpenes. If you didn't already know, TERPENES ARE COOL! The oldest known biomolecules are terpenes, and today they make up the largest class of natural products, encompassing over 30,000 known compounds. All terpenes are initially made from a simple combination of C5 isoprene units, which often undergo cyclization, rearangement and secondary modification to make a mind-boggling diversity of compounds. While every living thing makes terpenes, it is the plants that make the most diverse and remarkable numbers of compounds. The Croteau group is primarily interested in the fascinating enzymes that synthesize these molecules in plants. Many of them employ interesting reactions mechanism which we would like to study at the structure-function level. This is where a molecular biologist like me fits into this group that has been traditionally made up of enzymologists, synthetic chemists, biochemists and analytical chemists. Since many of these compounds are made in relativly low abundance in plants (the anti-cancer drug taxol is found in the range of mg taxol/kg tissue) to study these enzymes requires first identifying the gene that makes the enzyme, then cloning it so the enzyme can be mass produced in bacteria. Along the way we have also pursued and come across genes with commercial interest. To date I have eight patents issued or submitted and my success at identifying these genes has helped attract more than $2M in industrial contracts for the lab. Toot..toot...(that was my own horn). Ouch...I just hurt my elbow trying to pat myself on my back. Really though, I am only one part of a very large research group that has enormous amounts of fun tracking down these really neat enzymes, which are according to Robert "Bob" Long (AKA Oblong), "the closest things to magic on the planet." Living in and around Pullman, we all get the fantastic luxury of having challenging, interesting work in a rural academic setting. I also have the pleasure of calling Rod Croteau, a member of the National Academy of Sciences, a friend and collegue. He is the best damn guy anyone has ever been lucky enough to work with, and he appreciates the wonderfull synergy that can occur when the right team of people with diverse backgrounds is assembled and then allowed to get creative. I don't know how the science or the people could get any better. 10234, 10235, 10236, 10237, ...counting my blessings!!!

Home

My lovely wife Kristine, our beautiful daughter Claire, our adorable son Ben, and I currently live out in the sticks of rural Pullman. Kristine is lucky enough to be a stay at home mom, though she always has something going and never seems to stay at home. Claire is just tooo cute. Ben has a great nature. We are currently breathing life back into an old farmhouse who's core structure dates to 1878, which makes it an awfully early structure for this part of eastern Washington. The area around the house is frequented by white-tailed deer, mule deer, the occational elk (all of which have been taken by bow and arrow, and sometimes a rifle, and put in the freezer), pheasants, partridge, quail, coyotes, porcupines, the damned racoons, and over fifty species of common birds. We have also been fortunate enough to spot bobcats often and cougars almost too often for comfort, though it is always a joy to see them. We maintain a very large garden, but only get to eat what the deer leave behind. We live only a few miles "as the crow flies" from the Snake river and usually take our fair share of crayfish, smallmouth bass and steelhead. The home is defended by a black powder cannon that hurles concrete filled pop cans (got some great video to get linked here) harder, faster, and farther than you might think is possible. If you've never seen a pop can full of concrete fly well over 500 meters, you've missed one of life's great pleasures. It is also kinda fun sometimes to but things in front of the cannon.
