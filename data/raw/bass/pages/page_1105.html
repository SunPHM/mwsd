Bass guitars

Into bass guitars? The AFM regularly spotlights the latest in bass guitars. Here are the most current bass guitars spotlights.

Bass Guitars on the Fly


Ultra-lightweight bass guitars: Parker Fly Bass guitars. Just under 7 lbs., the Parker Fly Bass guitars offers unmatched range, with two custom-wound DiMarzio Ultra-Jazz pickups and a Fishman active piezo pickup pre-amp/mixer system. It's available in four-string and five-string bass guitar models, and boasts a quilted maple top and back, spruce body, and mahogany neck. A combination of carbon-fiber composite fingerboard, stainless steel frets, and Sperzel locking tuners provides superb playability.

The new Parker Fly Bass guitar received praise from Aerosmith bassist Tom Hamilton. "They play great, sound incredible, and are just rad to look at," he said.

Find out for yourself by e-mailing Parker Bass Guitars at info@parkerguitars.com, or on the Web at www.parkerguitars.com

CB5 Chambered Bass Guitars


The Citron CB5 bass guitar, is an offset, double cutaway, 2-inch thick chambered electric five-string bass guitar. It has a Swamp Ash body, capped in Curly or Quilted Maple, and a three-piece laminated Hard Rock Maple neck. Its 35" scale length enhances the tension giving the B string the additional tension necessary to vibrate properly. The CB5 bass guitar features two custom blended Citron hum bucking pickups and an Aguilar preamp. The combination of custom pickups and construction creates a sound that is mega deep.

For more information visit www.citron-guitars.com

Bass guitar sounds of Mo' Bass


Bass guitar players the world over have praised SWR Sound's Mo' Bass, the groundbreaking bass head that brings gorgeous, classic analog bass guitar effects to the tried-and-true sound of SWR bass guitar amplification. Now, those glorious Mo' effects can be controlled with SWR's Mo' Control footswitch.
Mo' Control is a pedalboard-style footswitch that lets the Mo' Bass user switch between various effects. There are individual on/off controls for each effect, including Drive, SubWave, Bassynth, Mo' EQ, Chorus, and Dual Mode. Four preset banks allow the user to store any combination of Mo' Bass guitar effects and switch back and forth between those presets, as well as add or subtract any effect from the currently selected preset. Mo' Control's bypass switch lets the user bypass the effects entirely. Finally, at the right of the Mo' Control is an expression pedal for opening and closing the Bassynth effect's filter. Basically a footswitch version of the Mo' Bass control knob, the expression pedal gives the bass guitar a never-ending stream of possibilities with "wah-wah" pedal-style control.

For more information visit www.swrsound.com.
