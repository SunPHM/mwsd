Steve's Bass

I started off playing drums many years ago, however they are still over in Ireland while I dont live somewhere that would appreciate them. A friend of mine suggested taking up the bass in the meantime and was even kind enough to lend me his old one for a bit.

I started off with an old Squire, but ended up with the Fender in the end. I like the Precision's sound though I can see why people put a jazz pickup in them as well. It's been sat about in its case for a while but lately I've been digging it out more and playing the thing. It makes a good sound.

For those interested it is a Standard Precision Bass with a Seymour Duncan Quarter Pound pick up, a Leo Quan Badass II bridge, and a brass nut and black scratchplate made by KGB Music. The strings are Rotosound and the amp it gets run through most of the time is a Marshall practice combo. In an ideal world though it would have to be an Ampeg SVT.
