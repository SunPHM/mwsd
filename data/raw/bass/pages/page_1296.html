Chance's music is acoustic-based rock/pop with raw electric elements; it is honest and personal, catchy and accessible. The band is fronted by two singer-songwriter-guitarists, Mark Nensel and David Chambers, and also features Joe Basso (on bass) and Matthew Richkus (drums). Mark and Joe are long-time DC-band music vets who have joined forces with their younger cohorts Dave and Matthew to create a band that blends rock and pop elements from several decades into a seamless and enjoyable new sound that goes down easy. Chance has been playing the DC-VA-MD area regularly for the past two years. They are regulars at the Staccato in Adams Morgan, they've played Jammin' Java in Vienna, VA; Zig's in Alexandria; the IOTA in Arlington; the Velvet Lounge in DC; Parker's in Bethesda; the Vault in Baltimore, among many other venues. They've been interviewed by Damian on WRNR radio in Annapolis, and played on-air. And the band was thrilled to perform at the Taste of DC festival in downtown Washington this past Columbus Day weekend.




Mark Nensel (Guitar/Vocals)

On every tune, Mark uses his songwriting to open his heart and soul, singing about what we all experience but rarely say. A multi-instrumentalist and master of none, he nonetheless lives for music and always strives for new melodies and new things to say; always swinging and sometimes hitting em outa the park.


Joe Basso (Bass Guitar)

Joe is a veteran DC area musician. Not only does he come up with perfect bass lines, Joe provides depth to Chance Gardener with his ability to collaborate so well with the songwriters.



David Chambers (Guitar/Vocals)

Often a simple phrase will lodge itself so deeply in David's head that only the creation of a new song will drive it out. Somewhere between a fondness for melody and the revelations of loves lost and found lie the tunes he is most proud of.



Matthew Richkus (Drums)

A punk rock guitarist at heart, Matthew has found a home behind the drums providing an exciting and strong foundation for Chance's power-pop sound.
