LAKE OKEECHOBEE

The Seminole Indian name Okeechobee actually means big water, an appropriate title for the largest freshwater lake in the United States occurring entirely in one state. The lake is approximately 37 miles long by 30 miles wide (448,000 acres, 700 square miles) with an average depth of almost 10 feet. To fishermen nation wide, Okeechobee is renowned for the sheer numbers of bass it contains per acre and the fact that it produces more Florida trophy bass over 8 pounds than any lake in Florida and the United States!

FISHING WITH SHINERS

Is by far the most productive way to land a Florida trophy bass. The beauty of shiner fishing is that you don't have to be a bass fishing angler of the year to really experience the thrill of fighting trophy size bass. Fishing with shiners is a sure shot method of putting a Florida trophy bass on your wall. The going price for shiners is $14.00 per dozen. During the winter months when they become scarce they can cost as much as $18.00 per dozen! A party can expect to use - depending on how good the fish are biting - anywhere from 6 - 8 dozen wild shiners during a full day of fishing. That's $84.00 - $112.00 per day that you would pay in addition to your guide rate charges. Keep this in mine when you are planing your trip's budget. To take the sting out of this additional expense - which can amount into hundreds of dollars over a period of days - Captain Ron provides his party with 6 dozen wild shiners per day FREE OF CHARGE. WHEN'S THE BEST TIME TO COME?

Is the foremost question I am asked by every angler planning a trophy bass fishing trip to Lake Okeechobee. In the 20 years I have guided on Okeechobee I would honestly have to say that there really isn't a bad time to fish Okeechobee. Take for example the 2 pictures on this page. The first picture a 10 lb. trophy bass was caught using a wild shiner in October along with 47 other bass that also included an 8 1/4 lb, 7 3/4 lb and a 6 lb'er. The second picture a 7 lb'er and 84 other bass up to 6 lbs were caught on wild shiners in July. The summer months and early fall months produce good numbers of fish as well as trophy bass. The spawn (November - April) when the fish are bedding is considered to be the better months to catch a trophy Lake Okeechobee bass. SEASONAL WEATHER

Winter's general day temperature is 75 degrees. Nights and mornings are cool to cold. Late spring and early fall days are in the 80's. Summer days range in the 80's - 90's. Rainfall during the winter, early spring and late fall is slight. Brief afternoon showers are common in late spring, throughout the summer and early fall. Guides fish "rain or shine and under all weather conditions". Rain gear is a must bring item. If you are fishing during the winter months, be sure to bring some warm clothing with you -- it can get quite cold at times.
Hopefully bad weather does not interfere with your reserved fishing trip. We do not offer rain checks for our fishing. We have no control over the weather and therefore we cannot be responsible for the disruption of your trip due to bad weather. Please bring rain gear with you. If bad weather moves in the decision to leave is up to the guide. We can make up the hours lost on the following day or days if the party is still fishing with us.
