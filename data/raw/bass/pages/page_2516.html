The John Day River Valley borders on the heavily wooded Umatilla National Forest. It has earned a reputation for an abundance of deer and elk. With public lands available, both bow and rifle hunters come to the area each fall for a successful hunting season

Plan Various Activities in the Umatilla National Forest.

Fishing

The John Day River is among Oregon's unspoiled rivers. It is opened to steelhead and bass fishing year around in both the Service Creek, and Spray Areas. With 20 miles of access (25 by boat), the John Day River is the ultimate in steelhead fishing. Battling with a small-mouth bass is an exhilarating experience on the John Day. Rainbow trout can also be found in abundance in the John Day River, local streams and near by reservoirs.

Rafting

Rafting and drifting on the John Day River is an attraction for many. The most popular section is from Service Creek to Cottonwood bridge. Road access between these two points is limited to three places.

Other popular sections include the stretch from Spray River Front Park to Service Creek and access points south of Spray to Spray Riverfront Park.



* Photography Scenic pictures can be taken virtually anywhere. Sites include fossil beds, rim-rocks, farmland, waterfowl, inland birds, deer, elk, antelope, the river valley, numerous wildflowers, and buffalo to name a few.
