 Construction
The exterior design of this instrument is similar to the traditional hexagonal 48 key English. It has 48 Wheatstone type metal  buttons (metal sleeve over a  wooden core) and German silver finger rests. The fretwork  is a traditional vine pattern. The instrument  is constructed entirely of solid hardwoods.
All Geuns-Wakker concertinas are produced in small series, using the latest machines, specialized software, and professional production methods. The specially developed tools and machines for our concertina production guarantee tolerances which are not obtainable when all parts are produced by hand with standard tools.

This, and our modified reeds,  is probably the most important feature that sets our instruments apart from our colleague makers. We believe that taking advantage of  the newest techniques and machines produces a better instrument at a more economical price. We are convinced that  using computer software to calculate and produce an even harmonic spectrum  beats the 'guessing'  method.

There is, of course, still room for personal preferences. Besides metal, we have a large selection of veneers to choose from for the ends, flat top or rounded key tops, and 13 different colors of bellows, either with or without bellows papers.

  Reeds
The Baritone has modified accordion type reeds screwed directly on the reed pan, like in traditional concertinas.  Although there are more makers that use accordion type reeds in concertinas, we are the only ones that have succeeded in altering the harmonic spectrum of accordion type reeds, coming very close to the sound of traditional concertina reeds.
The sound difference between accordion  and traditional concertina reeds is the result of  the number and amplitude of the harmonics.
During the last century the objective in accordion reed production has been air economy (better fitting reeds), and brightness, which is obtained by a high number of harmonics with a slow and even decrease of amplitude.  Nowadays the manufacturing of accordion reeds is a high-tech industry, where every aspect of the reeds is scientifically tested and monitored.
Concertina reeds were developed in the 19th century, and miss the development of the accordion reeds in the 20th century. Compared to accordion reeds, concertina reeds have a lower number of harmonics with a strong amplitude drop.  When you examine concertina reeds and frames with today's knowledge of reed production, it becomes obvious that the 'concertina' sound is most likely the result of 19th century production methods. Especially when you consider that when the production methods and tools improved in the early 20th century, the concertinas became brighter (more harmonics).
Harry Geuns has developed a method which reduces the number of harmonics as well as their amplitude. The result is a tone which comes very close to 20th century concertinas.

                                                                                

Bellows 
The bellows of this instrument are made by the Concertina Connection. They are identical to the traditional concertina bellows and have six folds (standard). We use the finest quality goat skin, tested for impurities and air tightness, and use natural glues. The standard color is  black. See our

Action
The action of the Geuns-Wakker concertina is probably its most impressive feature. Similar to top Wheatstone      instruments, this is a riveted action. The design of the brass post, through which the lever moves, eliminates all  side movement of the button, which makes the action very quiet, smooth and fast.
