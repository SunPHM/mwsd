A magnificent double bass instrument by Bernard Simon Fendt circa 1835 - #2154

A true English classic now so rarely available. This is one of the copy models after Maggini with double purfilling and Brecian style sound holes. A wonderful straight medium grain on the table seems to stand out and exude strength.

A flattish table arch means plenty of power in the sound as does the incredible depth of the ribs at 24cm (just under 9.5 inches including the plates). The varnish of a rich orange-brown colour over a yellow ground. This instrument is sheer poetry to look at and a sheer delight to play. A real gem.

Please refer to the Strad Feb 97 article on Fendt by Thomas Martin and Martin Lawrence for pictures of an identical instrument.

LOB 46in (116.9cm) 
St. Length 43in (109cm)
