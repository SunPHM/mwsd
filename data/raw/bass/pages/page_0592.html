Tuesday 13 February

Well we've been in Greenland now for about one and a half weeks and the experiences I have gained are very unique and definitely eye opening. Before we left I had no idea of what it would really be like. I mean I knew there would be a hell of a lot of snow and it would be freezing cold but hey, not this much snow and this much cold!

Each morning when we go out, it can take up to ten minutes to get fully dressed. After a while, I don't think I'll want to be going out any more.

Smile for the camera

This was the day that we had all been looking forward to since we first arrived. We were off to school. Prior to our arrival, various activities had been planned with the local schoolchildren. We arrived at school at the typical time of 9:00 and were introduced to several Year 10 and 11 students who would eventually be our guides for the day.

 

The Greenlandic school system only goes up to Year 11 so many of them were younger than us, which really didn 't matter because hey, don 't all teenagers like the same things? So for several hours they took us around the town, occasionally pointing out things of interest and taking us inside the few shops they have.

One of the highlights was the lakeside museum, inside there were displays on Greenlandic clothing, hunting, history and culture. From here we made our way back to the school, were shown around and then we all had a chat in which several language barriers were smashed down.

Kathryn enjoying school

There were many things I noticed from the tour of the school which were so different from that of Australia. Each class only has nine students and there are mostly only two classes per year level. So, the school is very small but ranges from Grade 1 to Year 11. Most of the teachers are Greenlandic but several come from Denmark and this adds to the children's cultural and social development. They can all speak at least three languages: Greenlandic, Danish and English but some better than others.

Following this we returned to the station for lunch and then were back at school for a 4:00 lesson in traditional Greenlandic clothes-making. The children in this class make such items as sealskin gloves, pants, jackets and a wide range of other items like pencil cases, pillows and bags. As well as learning some of their techniques we were also given the opportunity to have a chat with the kids-in English that is!
