Florida Wild Shiner Bass Fishing Brag'n Board
6 1/2 lb. & 5 1/2 lb'er - September 29, 2001

Florida wild shiner bass fishing anglers Pete Van Den Berg (Wilderness, South Africa - e-mail: Petervan@cfan.org) and Greg Pearce (Orlando, FL - ph: 407-859-0505) put up with light to heavy rain showers and gale force winds all day during their wild shiner bass fishing trip on September 29, 2001 with Cap'n Ron. Although these conditions made the fishing difficult, Pete did manage to catch and released a 6 1/2 lb. (left) and a 5 1/2 lb. (right) bass. Three channel catfish up to 6 lbs were also boated. Bass and catfish were caught in the North Shore area of Lake Okeechobee.

Lake Okeechobee
Channel Catfish

Pete is holding a big channel catfish, one of 3 that were caught on wild shiners during their day of trophy bass fishing with wild shiners. Catfish (channel and Blue's) of this size ( 5 - 6 lbs) and larger are caught frequently on wild shiners. Cats put up a fierce battle and you'll swear when you have one on the line that it must be that trophy bass you have been dreaming of.....!

Florida Wild Shiner Bass Fishing Brag'n Board
two 5 1/2 lb'ers, one 5 1/4 lb'er, one 5 lb'er - September 20, 2001

Honeymoon bass fishing couple Josh and Sara Zenner (Athens, WI - ph:715-829-2952 - e-mail: saraannzenner@yahoo.com - e-mail: joshz@negconsulting.com) caught 13 bass up to 5 1/2 lbs during 1 day of wild shiner bass fishing on September 20, 2001 with Cap'n Ron. They each caught a 5 1/2 lb'er which is the biggest bass she or her husband ever caught. They also had a 5 1/4 lb, 5 lb, 4 1/2 lb, 4 lb and seven others from 3 - 3 1/2 lbs that they caught and released. Fished the Monkey Box and Point of the reef areas of Lake Okeechobee.
