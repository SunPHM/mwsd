Pros: Solid, warm low-end and unrivaled top-end
Cons: Nary a one!

GIG/RIG:

Medium-sized, rectangular room with a small raised, wooden stage on one end, enclosed on the sides. This room (Moose Lodge) acts as a virtual bass reflex; the band sounds great onstage and very lively throughout the room. The band was my classic rock trio, The Lost Patrol and the place was packed. Rig was the Walter Woods Ultra driving two Bergantino cabs, the EX112 and HT112. The HT was on top and the stack was sitting on a Gramma pad. Xwire between the bass and WWU. (What a great small rig, BTW. Simply terrific.)

SOUND:

Like every Hanewinckel I\'ve enountered, this one has an almost amazing degree of balance from top to bottom. No range is enhanced or weak; everything is the same volume from the lowest to highest note. This one also has the characteristic Hanenwinckel Low B, too--fat as they come. In general, the range from Low B up to C on the G string is warm and detailed. Very articulate and smooth. The register on up to High G is \"open\" without a touch of compression and clear without brightness. Treble boost provides a superb slap tone. I found no need to boost bass at all. Mids are smoothly integrated and responsive causing the bass to cut through very well. The drummer couldn\'t stop complimenting the bass sound all night (admittedly, he\'s a jovial cuss but the bass did sound GOOD!). Fingerstyle solo work brought out a pleasing J Bass-style \"boink\" that was just...well...downright great. Sustain is also bordering on spectacular. The combination set-neck/bolt-on is perhaps the reason, I\'d say. In any case, a note will seemingly go on forever. Makes for very effective end-of-song dramatics and wonderful fingered harmonics. What can I say? This bass is an A+ in the sound department.

PLAYABILITY:

Lightweight overall, fast neck, perfectly balanced on a strap. An absolute joy to play for 4 sets and that\'s no exaggeration. I literally found myself wishing we had another set and I was worn out and drenched in sweat! Insane, I know. But this bass was a treat. No other way to say it. A+

COMPARISONS:

Since I traded a very nice MTD535 for this bass, that seems like the natural comparison. The MTD might have had a bit more growl given specific EQ adjustments but also suffered a bit form weak notes here and there on the neck, possibly due to the one-piece neck. The Hanewinckel is a 7-piece lam neck and, as stated above, has no such weaknesses. And while the MTD asymetrical neck was very comfortable, this one is even faster and the string spacing is narrower (which I prefer). I would rate the slap tone on both basses a dead heat. Fingerstyle tone is slightly better on the Hanewinckel. You know which one I prefer! Call it self-justifying if you like (we all want to hear things that make our decisions seems smart) but the Hanewinckel is my preference. Of course, I prefer Hanewinckels over all other electric basses, anyway. So keep that in mind when evaluating my comments.

I don\'t currently own another high-end fiver so it wouldn\'t be fair to talk about basses I haven\'t heard/played in a long time.

CONCLUSION:

At the risk of fawning over another of Pete\'s basses (I currently own 8), this is essentially my favorite fiver to date. The wood combinations work together beautifully and the bass is a pure joy to play on a gig. I won\'t indulge in further hyperbole but I will state that this bass is now a solid member of my gig rotation. I expected no less.
