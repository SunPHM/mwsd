Friday July 11th, 1997
Epiphone brings Gibson classics to NAMM

Epiphone comes to Summer NAMM with four new models that offer classic Gibson designs on a workingman's budget.

Korina Flying V and Explorer

In 1958, Gibson president Ted McCarty countered criticism that he was too conservative by creating two guitars that fit perfectly with the dawning of the Space Age. Made out of "Korina" wood, (an exotic relative of mahogany that grows in Africa), the guitars were named Explorer and Flying V. Original '58 Explorers and V's are the most sought-after Gibson electrics on the vintage market.

Assuming you don't have $50,000+ to drop on a vintage guitar, Epiphone has recreated these beautiful guitars at a far more resonable price. Both feature the original style gold-plated hardware, natural finish and radical shapes of their predecessors.

ES-295

Some of the most influencial rock and roll recordings of all time were cut in a small studio in Memphis. The artist was Elvis, but a great deal of credit for the Elvis Sound belongs to a guitarist named Scotty Moore. Moore cut all those legendary tracks with a Gibson ES-295.

Epiphone brings back the 295 in all of its metallic gold glory. Also present is the vibrato bar and distinctive flower design on the pickguard.

Thunderbird Bass

The most powerful sounding (and looking) bass guitar on the market, hands down, is the Gibson Thunderbird. This versatile instrument has been a workhorse for rock and roll players ranging from John Entwhistle to Nikki Sixx.

No detail has been overlooked in designing a bass that produces the "thundering" bottom end every band needs.

Les Paul Pee Wee

Not forgetting the little rockers, Epi introduces the Les Paul Pee Wee. The small body and shortened scale is perfect for little hands while the matching amplifier makes a big sound in a small package.
