The Final Report

B.A.S.S. Tour: Lake Hamilton, AR

Winning at Home

     Ron Shuffield of Bismarck, AR, took the recent Lake Hamilton Bassmaster tournament, which was held only a short drive from his home. Shuffield never left the top 5 during the entire tournament. Overcoming a nearly two-pound deficit the final day, Shuffield managed to win by 14 ounces, thanks to a change in strategy and lure selection.

     Shuffield used a Zara Spook the first three days around the last few boat docks leading out of spawning coves; he covered about twenty docks during the course of the tournament. "Every one of those docks would have a school of really good fish underneath them,'' he said. "I could see them come out and follow my Spook, but it was always the smallest one that took the lure. I kept thinking those bigger ones would eventually hit.'' On Day 2, Shuffield commented that "it's a definite pattern that continues to get better each day.''

     More specifically, Shuffield's coves had large amounts of shad in them, which he mimicked with the Spook. In the early morning, he targeted the most shallow portions of the coves. After the sun rose, the bass could be caught near the backs of the docks, and they moved to the front corners as the day progressed. "All of the docks were floating docks,'' Shuffield said. "But they had to have corner poles on front and back. I guess the fish were just suspended on those poles.''

     On the final day, clouds blew in to cover Lake Hamilton, and when they did, Shuffield changed lures. That change gave him the bigger bass that he was looking for. The switch was to a Stanley golden shiner spinnerbait, which he cast to windy banks that had a mud line and docks with pilings. The new technique gave him his three biggest bass while the rest of the field was struggling to adapt. "We all knew coming in that whoever won would have to catch a 3-pounder a day,'' Mark Davis remarked. "Obviously, I couldn't find one today. Ronnie found mine and Jay's too.''

Thomas: Figuring out the Bass

     Joe Thomas of Milford, OH, started out this tournament in 14th place, but managed to squeak in just ahead of the top-12 cut by a mere 6 ounces. Thomas figured out how to deal with the cloud cover and winds even better than Shuffield; he caught 13-1, which was the heaviest catch of the final day. The large catch moved him from 5th to 2nd. That heavy catch earned him $41,000 instead of the 5th place award of $20,000. Thomas caught his bass on a Sammy topwater and a jerkbait around boat docks in the mid-lake area.

The Home-Town Favorite

     Day 3 leader, Mark Davis, slipped to third on the final day, after bringing in a limit of bass that weighed 8-13. The home-town favorite expected to stay on a long point, but changed his tactics with the weather to hunt for big bass. However, that change did not pay off.

     A Zara Spook played a major part in Davis' game plan, as it did in Shuffield's. His Day 3 catch was caught in less than ten minutes, and after the bass had finished their feeding spree they quickly developed a bad case of lockjaw. The result was that Davis struggled for the remainder of the day. At the end of the next day, Davis had not been able to find his 3-pounder that would have been nearly worth its weight in gold. "We all knew coming in that whoever won would have to catch a 3-pounder a day," Davis commented after the final weigh-in.

As For the Rest...

     Jay Kendrick, of Ooltewah, TN, took fourth place after leading the first two days of the event with his famous chicken-feathered topwater. Kendrick's final weight was 42-10, only 2-2 out of first. In at fifth place was Randy Howell of Springville, AL, who caught 40-01. All of the top five pros caught limits every day. Rounding out the Super Six, in sixth place, was Kenyon Hill of Norman, OK. Hill caught a total of 31-02.
