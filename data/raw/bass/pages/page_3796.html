Wachapreague, Va Fishing Report

October 13, 2004:  Not to be outdone by his girls, Dave lands his own citation sauger catching a 2.44 pounder measuring 19.5 inches.

   

October 9, 2004:  Cap'n Dave's girls land two citation saugers,  one measuring 19.25 inches and 2.34 pounds and the other at 18.5 inches and 2.1 pounds.

September 2004:  Mr. McGlumphy with some small mouth bass caught during September--a few kept for the frying pan.

September 11, 2004:  Ken Mergenthal, joined by his wife and daughter, spent the day aboard MIYOT fishing inshore.  Their catch included 3 or 4 sea trout, a big croaker, 6 to 8 sea bass, a sea robin, and a couple of small flounder. 

August 31, 2004:  Jim Baker's family fished the day after weather from Hurricane Gaston passed through Wachapreague.  Strong tides and a northwest wind in the morning made fishing difficult, but it calmed in the afternoon.  Three generations fished aboard MIYOT, and the crew landed 11 or 12 flounder--one a five pounder measuring 23 " along with a 17-1/2" and a 19" keeper.  Eight small sea bass were also among the catch for the day.

   

August 17, 2004:  Andrew, a 10 year old angler in the Ritz party,  landed and released a 34" cobia at the #10 buoy in the a.m.  After returning inshore, the party landed a dozen flounder and a dozen croaker.  There were 2 keeper flounder measuring 19" and 18-1/2".

           

August 16, 2004:  Wachapreague weathered the aftermath of Hurricane Charley fairly well but heavy rainfall caused brown water inside.  A half-day flounder charter for the Ritz party resulted in a poor catch of 3 flounder and a bluefish.  Although the fishing was slow, cousins Matthew and Andrew, enjoyed riding on the tuna tower aboard MIYOT.   

   

August 14, 2004:  The imminent approach of Hurricane Charley resulted in the postponement of a spadefish charter today.

August 8, 2004:  An offshore trip trolling at Sam's Hill hooked a 40 pound yellowfin tuna for Matt Whipkey after 3 ballyhoo were bitten off.  Water temperature was 77 degrees. 

   

August 7, 2004: A half-day charter for the Spalding family produced a catch of 25 to 30 flounder with 5 keepers.  Later in the day, the Whipkey party boarded MIYOT for some inshore fishing and hooked 20 flatfish with 1 keeper and a sea mullet.

August 6, 2004:  A half-day trip on the inside produced a catch of about 80 flounder with 5 keepers for the West Virginia crew. 

   

August 5, 2004:  The Whipkey party from West Virginia set off for a trip to the hills--but these hills were 21 and 26 miles offshore in the Atlantic.  They trolled ballyhoo and green machine on the 26 mile hill early in the morning with 4 to 5 foot seas.  They had a pull off that got everyone ready for some action.  This was shortly followed by the catch of a small bluefin tuna which was released.  By late morning, the seas calmed, and they trolled north to the 21 mile hill.  On the way, Phil McCabe caught a 15 pound gaffer dolphin on green machine.  Jason then landed a 10 pound dolphin.  They ended the day early because of an approaching strong cold front, but they plan a flounder trip tomorrow afternoon. 

August 4, 2004:  A half day charter for the Schmitt party produced 35 flounder--8 over 16-1/2" and the largest at 17-1/2" plus two croaker.  The kids had a great time. 

       

July 31, 2004:  A half day charter for the Doyle Turner party of 1,000 Trails at Virginia Landing resulted in a catch of 25 to 30 flounder with 4 keepers--the largest at 21-1/2".  The group also caught a nice trigger fish, a sea robin and half a dozen sea bass.

July 29, 2004:  Jason Harloff's party braved some nasty weather to try the spadefish action at the #10.  With no luck by noon, they headed back through the inlet for some flounder fishing and caught 20-25 flat fish with 4 keepers and a few croaker. 

July 25, 2004:  Chris Bozak's party spent the day flounder fishing after a planned spadefish trip was called off due to wind.  Their catch included 50 flounder with 5 keepers--one measuring 22".  Also landed was a 14" sea trout.

July 23, 2004:  On a half-day charter, the Rogers party (including 3 generations) caught 40-50 croaker and a dozen flounder.  There was one keeper among the flounder, but it was lost at the boat. 

   

July 20, 2004:  Cap'n Dave took his family for a brief fishing excursion.  His girls, ages 10 and 6, were excited to catch two flounder, a sea bass, sea trout, croaker, and an oyster toad fish (ugly!).

               

July 17, 2004:  The Meiggs party did it again!  They started their charter in a quest for spadefish at the #10 buoy, but a member of the party quickly became seasick.  The party decided to return inshore and flounder fish.  They landed 5 keeper flounder--the largest weighing 4-1/2 pounds and measuring 23".  This is the biggest flounder of the season for MIYOT.  They also had some 19" flounder in the fish box.    In addition, they reeled in a nice little haul of croaker.  

   

July 16, 2004:  The Brown party returned for another fishing adventure aboard MIYOT--this time for spadefish action.  They found it at the #10 buoy.  The group caught 8 or 9 spadefish and put 5 in the fish box--the largest weighing 8 lbs. 4ozs.  Patrick, Debbie's son, reeled in 2 spadefish.  They also caught several blue fish.  For the last hour of fishing, MIYOT returned to some inshore fishing where the crew caught about a half dozen each of flounder and croaker. 

               

July 13, 2004:  Bob Well's party booked MIYOT for a 1/2 day inshore charter.  They caught about 80 croakers with 65 keepers.  They also landed two flounder measuring 19-1/2" and  20" with 10 additional flounder under regulation size. 

       

July 11, 2004:  Cap'n Dave fished MIYOT on the 26 mile hill today with Todd and Ed Lowery.  The water temperature was 84 degrees, and the seas were like glass.  Trolling ballyhoo, they caught 6 small bluefin tuna--all throw backs and 5 nice gaffer dolphin with the biggest weighing 19-1/2 pounds.  Two of the five dolphin were caught on the "way back" pole. 

       

July 10, 2004:  The Owens party boarded MIYOT for a half-day flounder charter.  Their catch included about 60 to 70 flounder with none measuring keeper size, but they put 18 croaker in the fish box.  The youngest angler of the crew was happy to land a small shark. 

   

July 4, 2004:  The Mummey party departed at 5:30 a.m. from the Wachapreague Marina aboard MIYOT and fished the 26 mile hill.  There they caught four bluefin tuna, one keeper weighing about 35 lbs., and a nice dolphin.  The dolphin and bluefin tuna made a fine Fourth of July feast later that evening.  (It sure beats burgers and hotdogs!) 

   

June 30, 2004:  The Farrer party's charter was delayed just over an hour due to early morning thunderstorms, but as the weather cleared they headed for the #10 buoy for some spadefish.  They fished the waters there for four hours.  Although they could see the spadefish in the waters around MIYOT, they never got a bite.  They headed back inshore for flounder.  They caught about 50 flounder with 9 keepers ranging from 17" to 19-1/2". 

       

June 4, 2004:  Debbie Brown's party had fun reeling in the fish at the Hummocks.  Her party caught 30 flounder with 6 in the box.  Water temperature was 73 degrees.  Debbie's party enjoyed their catch at the Best Western in Cape Charles where the chef prepared their fillets.  They're booked for spadefish in July.  One of the crew just returned from the Antarctic where he studied penguins for three months, and he knows his birds!  I won't forget the double-crested cormorant--a large, voracious sea bird.

       

May 28, 2004:  The Nuckols party had a good flounder bite with about 40 flounder caught and 2 keepers.  They worked hard at the rail and earned these two keepers.  Carol's quite a fisherman!

   

May 22, 2004:  The Meiggs party of six had a good day catching 60 flounder with 18 keepers ranging from 17-1/14" to 21-1/4" at The Hummocks.  MIYOT left the dock at 7:30 a.m. and returned at 3:30 p.m. with a happy crew.  In addition to the flounder, the party caught 2 small shark, several clear nose skates, a half dozen blue fish, and 4 or 5 small sea bass.  The party left MIYOT and Wachapreague with a great time and some nice fillets to enjoy later.  The Meiggs party are booked for a July spadefish trip.

           

 

May 1, 2004:  Cap'n Dave made a freshwater trip and caught 6 hybrids, 1 freshwater drum, a 4 lb. largemouth, a 3 lb. smallmouth, and 12 other smallmouth bass measuring 11-16 inches.

               

 

September 21, 2003:  Two days after Isabel, tuna were hitting good at the 30 fathom line.  Water was pretty in spots and green other places. 

September 19, 2003:  MIYOT, with luck and a prayer, survives Hurricane Isabel.  Waves were breaking in the marina parking lot.

August 26, 2003:  Capt. Dave fished aboard Catch 33 with Roger and Norman at the South Lump off of Washington Canyon for a 40 lb. yellowfin tuna and a handful of skipjack.  The water offshore 45 miles was dark looking--not a pretty blue you'd normally see this time of year.

August 22, 2003:  MIYOT fished Sam's Hill and the Lumpy Bottom for 1 blue fish, then the #10 buoy where they caught 10 spadefish to 6.5 lbs.

August 9, 2003:  MIYOT fished Saturday for 6 keeper flounder and a good haul of croaker and sea mullet.  Angler Ed McKay from Leesburg, VA and his party had a good day of fishing.

August 8, 2003:  Flounder, croaker and sea mullet were biting.

August 3, 2003:  Fished the #10 buoy and caught 16 spadefish.  Angler Phil McCabe

of Glen Dale, WV caught a citation spadefish weighing in at 9 lb. 3 oz.

 

     

August 1, 2003:  Fished the Fingers on Friday for 10 blue fish and a small tuna

that pulled off.  Tuna bite still slow.

 

July 15, 2003:  Flounder good but only one keeper and a few croaker.
