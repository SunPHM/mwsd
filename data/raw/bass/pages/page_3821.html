DINING CAMPANIA

 

La Brace, (See Photo) in the town of Priano, just south of Positano. Serving wonderful local dishes, such as sea bass in a thick salt/herb crust, incredible pizza, melon and procuitto that was extremely fresh and delicious. Our last night there , Giovanni, the friendly owner, made us a seafood pasta dish that was sublime. His charming wife, Martine , from Belgium is the pastry chef. Her special cakes are lemon, chocolate and cherry. I highly recommend the lemon, while my husband was wild about the chocolate. It is perched on the second floor directly on the Amalfi with a beautiful view of Positano and the sea.

In Naples, La Chiacchierata on the Piazzetta Matilde Serao 37 phone 081 41 14 65. Neapolitan specialties in a local trattoria.

La Cantinella on the Via Cuma 42, phone 081 764 86 84, is one of the most famous eateries, with extremely well prepared dishes.

The Osteria della Mattonella is one of the few restaurants that continues to serve Neapolitan food. Specialties of the establishment include spaghetti alla puttanesca with a tomato, black olive and caper sauce. On the Via G. Nicotera,13. Tel. 39 81 416 541

For great pizza visit Ciro a Santa Brigida. Located at Via Santa Brigida. Tel 081 553 4072.

For a great place in Naples to people watch, stop at Cimmino’s. The other watering hole is Gambrinus, beautiful people in an elegant setting. Cimmino is on the Via Gaetano Filangieri. Tel 39 81 418 303. Gambrinus is on the Piazza Trento e Trieste, 38. Tel 39 81 417 582.

In Capri, the La Savardina da Eduard is thought to be the best restaurant on the island. It is a 40 minute walk up a narrow path, that is lined with homes and villas. Take a flashlight, as it can be very dark, it is really spectacular. The views were incredible. The food is just wonderful.. We started our meal with a vegetable and ravioli misto. All lightly breaded and fried, topped with a fresh squezze of lemon juice. I had the pasta con lemoni, which had slivers of fresh lemon peel in a light sauce. The house special is an herb stuffed chicken that was excellent. A digestif was served at the end of the meal. It was iced lemoncello with a hint of laurel. This is the speciality of the house. You must go and savor this wonderful cuisine. On the Via Lo Capo, Tel 081 837 63 00.

For a unique experience in Capri, visit Da Augusto Tiberio. It is located near the Villa Jovis. A flashlight and a light sweater will be needed for the walk up to the mountain.

This is the place to watch the sunset. There is no menu on display, the waiter will tell you about the evening’s specials. Tel. 081 837 7820.

A favorite in Capri is Da Paulino’s, where you dine under the lemon trees. You can pick out the cut of meat or fish you desire and it will be covered in salt and grilled to perfection. The antipasti includes dozens of items to choose from. A favorite is the cold peas from their garden, with pieces of ham. It is filled with dinners, however i we were so spoiled from our previous meals that we weren’t impressed with the quality of the food served here. It wasn’t one of our favorites. 

Da Adolfo in Positano has a special treat. You have to go down to the beach and get on their boat. (See Photo) Great pasta with clams. The boat leaves every half hour. 089 87 50 22

In Amalfi, the restaurant Da Gemma on the Via Fra’ Gerardo Sasso 10, Tel 089 87 13 45 has great seafood and a very special chocolate eggplant. Closed on Wednesdays.
