Ace of bass


Paul Danabie, executive chef at Sprowston Manor Hotel and Country Club, prepares a dish in which the freshest of fish combines with sautéd vegetables to produce subtle Mediterranean-style flavours.
Photograph: James Bass.

Paul Danabie, originally from Fort William in Scotland, has worked in a number of top establishments including Gleneagles, Cullodeon House, Cameron House, Oakley Court in Windsor, the Landmark Hotel in London and was at the Goring Hotel in London for nearly five years before coming to Norwich.

He describes his food as “modern British, uncomplicated, simple and balanced” and also tries to use local produce and is now using Norfolk beef as opposed to Scottish in the hotel.

He joins the Saturday Magazine’s regular team of food columnists today.This is a versatile dish, it makes a great starter or main course, is very colourful and light.

Here I have used sea bass, but you could use red mullet or any Mediterranean-type fish. When you eat this dish it is always nice to imagine yourself sitting in the blazing hot sun in the South of France with a crisp glass of white wine!

With any fish dish, the freshness of the fish is vital. Much of the fish we use in the restaurant comes from Howards in Magdalen Street and it is always immaculate.

You are also using Mediterranean vegetables such as courgettes, aubergines and peppers – plus, of course, garlic.

We often serve this dish as the fish course for a formal dinner as it is a very light dish and there is no butter in the sauce.

The colours give it a “wow” factor when you serve it to your guests. Serve with a good Chablis or New Zealand Chardonnay.

FILLET OF SEA BASS WITH PROVENçALE VEGETABLES AND A TOMATO AND BLACK OLIVE DRESSING

Serves four

4x160g fillets of seabass
1 aubergine
2 green courgettes
1 yellow courgette
1 each of a green, yellow and red pepper
6 plum tomatoes
1 bunch of chives
6 cooked new potatoes (peeled)
black olives
olive oil
2 shallots
1 clove of garlic
salt and pepper

Dice and sauté the vegetables
Cut the aubergine, courgettes and peppers into a small dice and sauté in olive oil with finely chopped shallots and garlic and season.

De-seed and dice the tomatoes
Cut the tomatoes into quarters and de-seed, then cut into a small dice.

Prepare the garlic and shallots
Place some olive oil into a pan with a crushed garlic clove and some finely chopped shallots. Warm this liquor enough for the shallots to cook and take off the heat.

Add the tomatoes and olives
Add the diced tomatoes and chopped black olives just before serving and season.

Cook the potatoes
Slowly cook the potatoes in lightly salted water then refresh. Cut the potatoes in half and lightly grill them for a couple of minutes on each side.

Sauté the fish
Take a non-stick pan, put in some olive oil, and sauté the fish – skin side down for two minutes. Carefully turn the fish over and continue to cook for another minute. Place on a baking tray, skin side down and put in the oven to finish cooking, three to five minutes at 180C.

Assemble the dish
Place the provençale vegetables in the middle of the plate, place the fish on top, then arrange the potatoes around the fish with the tomato and olive sauce.

Optional
You can add a little reduced balsamic vinegar to the tomato sauce for colour and sharpness.

Vegetables
Broccoli florets or asparagus spears are good vegetables to accompany this dish to make it a little more substantial.
