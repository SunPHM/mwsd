000/OM Acoustic
Completed December, 2001
Finished - April, 2004
This is my first handmade acoustic. It has solid East Indian Rosewood back/Sides, solid Sitka Spruce top. Sitka Spruce Bracing, Mahogany neck, kerfed linings, and back reinforcement strip. Bubinga Binding, End Graft, Headstock Overlay, Heel Cap. Herringbone Purfling/Rosette. Ebony Fretboard with Abalone fret marker dots. Click Here for more pictures.
Suzuki  Mandolin - approx. 1972
Recently repaired.

Yamaha FG-75 - 1974
My first acoustic guitar. It has traveled with me all over the country and overseas. It's sadly in need of some serious repair.
Goya G-430 - 1984
My favorite guitar all around. It has great tone and action. I've spent many hours with this guitar and it sounds better and better as the years go by.

Yamaha CG-150CCE - 1997
My newest favorite, my first classical. It's great for just sitting and picking quietly. It has a sweet, warm tone, good action and a good sound from the electronics.
 

Ibanez PF-5CENT - 1998
My newest guitar. I've replaced the tuners, replaced the plastic nut and saddle with bone and have done some minor adjustments to improve the action and sound of the electronics output. I'm enjoying playing it more and more.

"The Bruiser"
My first "from scratch" guitar. It's a 5-String Neck-thru bass. Plays and sounds pretty nice. Lots of sustain. Click here to see how I built it.

Flat-top Mandolin
Built from a kit from MusicMaker's Kits. I customized it with kerfed lining inside top and back, a bolivian rosewood overlay on the headstock, and pearl fret marker dots. It has a pretty good sound for a flat-top and with laminated wood. I need to do some work on the action yet.


This was my first instrument, a mountain dulcimer built from a kit from Musicmaker's Kits. The back is laminated cherry, the sides, fretboard, end block, and tuning head are solid cherry, the soundboard is laminated spruce. I think it came out pretty good, and I definitely learned from the experience.
