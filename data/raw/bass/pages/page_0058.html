Pioneers Baseball Coaches

BASS  |   HILL  |   ATHLETICS  |   P. E. DIVISION
Head Coach Brad Bass
Coach Bass is entering his 6th season as head coach of the Pioneer baseball program. Coach Bass is a graduate of Connally High School in Waco, Texas. He began his college playing career at Ranger Junior College. While at Ranger he played on a team that won the 1978 Junior College World Series. From Ranger Junior College Coach Bass went on to play two years at Hardin-Simmons University, where he also served at an assistant coach for 5 years. Before coming to Wayland Baptist, Coach Bass was the head baseball coach at Texas Wesleyan University in Fort Worth.

Coach Bass experienced his best season at Wayland Baptist in 2000. That year the Pioneers won 40 games, the most wins in a season in school history. The Pioneers compete in the Sooner Athletic Conference, arguably the toughest NAIA conference in the nation. Coach Bass is currently overseeing the final stages of Wayland's new baseball facility, David & Myrt Wilder field.

Coach Bass and his wife Sally, have three children: Calvin age 12, Meredith age 11, and Will age 6.


Assistant Coach Brad Hill
Coach Hill is entering his second season as assistant baseball coach at Wayland Baptist. Coach Hill is a graduate of Portales High School in Portales, New Mexico. He played college baseball at Lubbock Christian University. In May 1991, he threw a shut-out in the finals of the District 8 baseball Tournament to beat Dallas Baptist 4-0. He was then named NAIA National Pitcher of the Week. That year he threw a complete game in the NAIA World Series in Lewiston, Idaho to beat Southern Arkansas 9-4. He was named to the 1991 World Series All-Tournament Team.

Coach Hill spent two years as assistant coach at Lubbock Christian University and one year at Eastern New Mexico University. He then spent four years as head baseball coach at Muleshoe High School. Coach Hill and his wife, Emily, have been married for three years.
