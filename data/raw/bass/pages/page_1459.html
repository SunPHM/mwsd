This month,  it's misadventures in lo-fi with the fabulous Fontanelles.

Who are they?

guitar and voice  Jonathon Fell
bass guitar  Daniel Jennings
drums  Rob Malton (early gigs featured Fake Ideal's Richard Whitlam)

Where might you have seen them?

They're cutting their teeth supporting bands around Doncaster and Sheffield. Recent gigs include the Tut 'n' Shive (Doncaster) and The Leopard (with Manchester lo-fi groups Donut Robot and b-fab.U.K.)

Summer 1999 saw them reach the finals of Doncaster's Battle of the Bands, narrowly losing to punk firebrands Fake Ideal.

A history lesson: back in the day John Fell ran around with indie-rock types Thumper, while more recently Rob Malton did drum duties for rockers Skyfall (see last month's spotlight).

What they say about themselves.

"I'm trying to throw all the elements of music I love into what I write, and try to do something different with each song. Live, we're slowly getting there. The loud songs go down well, the quieter ones less so. I'd like to try to offer something new and 'different' from the standard white-boy-rock-pub-band-fare. That'd be nice.

I confess I am a whore for a catchy tune. The kids love it. "
         - John

What I say about them.

"For those who remember mid-nineties Doncaster stars Thumper, The Fontanelles formula of deceptively simple lo-fi indie thrills married to first rate songwriting will seem delightfully familiar. The link is guitarist, singer, songwriter and general creative powerhouse John Fell whose soft, melancholy vocals make Lou Barlow sound like that that guy out of Aqua."
- Free Press, May 99

"And first in the firing line is the debut tape from new Doncaster band The Fontanelles...Freed from the pressures of pleasing a live crowd they seem more able to be themselves – sometimes less immediate, but always more rewarding.

"Impatient types may choose to skip ahead to the scathing rock ‘n’ roll of Permanent Record, the blasting scuzzy riffs of which most vividly remind the listener that The Fontanelles’ mainman is John Fell, former creative powerhouse behind should-have-been-big mid nineties types Thumper.

"Then again, those wanting to hear a little musical progress may be more interested in follower Less Of The Four-Four with its oddball excursion into dub bass weirdness. Between those two poles there’s tracks like the opener Slipped Away with its surging lo-fi dynamics and tangy atmosphere of palpable desolation. It’s an acutely affecting song about those situations when three’s a crowd and – whoops – the third one is you.

"Should Have Know and The Man are quietly haunting takes on varieties of emotional devastation, while for a bit of light relief there’s the equally bleak Too Old To Die which at least sets its misery to a rocking good late John Lennon-esque tune.

"The prime Wedding Present scuttle of Overrated impresses with its steely guitar melodies, but for closer Out it’s back to the depressive majesty that The Fontanelles seem to be staking out as their marvellous own territory. Nurse, pass the Prozac please. Make that a double."
- Free Press, July 99
