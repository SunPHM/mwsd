STUART L. BASS
Associate Professor, Department of Accounting, Taxation, and Business Law and Director of Graduate Programs
M.P.A., New York University; J.D., Hofstra University

Professor Bass is the 1997 recipient of the Frank G. Zarb School of Business “Distinguished Teacher of the Year” award. Professor Bass is a member of the panel of arbitrators of the American Arbitration Association, involved in commercial, insurance and labor arbitration, and serves on the panel of mediators for the New York State Public Employment Relations Board (NYSPERB). In addition, he serves as an arbitrator for the New York State Employment Relations Board and is on the board of arbitrators for the National Association of Security Dealers (NASD, Inc). Professor Bass has authored numerous articles on legal ethics, sex and employment discrimination, reform of health care contracts, sexual harassment and arbitration of discrimination claims. Professor Bass formerly served as chairperson of the Executive Committee of the Frank G. Zarb School of Business and currently serves as chairperson of the Planning and Budget Committee of the University Senate.

ROCHELLE COOPER
Adjunct Assistant Professor, Department of Management, Entrepreneurship and General Business
Ph.D., Columbia University

 Dr. Rochelle Cooper teaches Executive Leadership Skills at Hofstra University’s Frank G. Zarb School of Business, and has spent the last 18 years providing effectiveness training to over 750 managers. Her clients have been Fortune 500 companies, such as AT&T, J.P. Morgan Chase and Co., Merrill Lynch, Bristol-Myers Squibb, Prudential, Pfizer, Metropolitan Life, Northrop-Grumman, Olympus and Time Warner, as well as many other companies. She currently owns an independent practice coaching managers and senior executives, conducting assessments, and designing and delivering management training in the areas of team-building, communication skills, conflict resolution, creative problem-solving, career development, delegation, diversity and stress management.

RICHARD C. JONES, CPA
Assistant Professor, Department of Accounting, Taxation, and Business Law
Ph.D., Rutgers University

Dr. Jones received the Hofstra University Distinguished Teaching Award in 2000 and was the recipient of the 2001 Frank G. Zarb School of Business “Distinguished Teacher of the Year” award. Dr. Jones’ research interests include auditing, auditor’s decision-making behavior, and international accounting and auditing standard setting. He is a certified public accountant and received his bachelor’s degree in accounting from Drexel University and his Ph.D. in accounting from Rutgers University. His business experience includes six years as a vice president at Chemical Banking Corporation. He was also a member of the Financial Accounting Standards Board staff where he specialized in public utility issues. Dr. Jones has served as a discussant and reviewer for several academic conferences. He has authored and co-authored accounting article that have appeared in several conference proceeding and professional journals. Dr. Jones also edited a chapter on international accounting issues for The Accountants’ Handbook and contributes extensively to American Institute of CPAs’ publications, including the Audit and Accounting Manual, Audit Risk Alerts and Peer Review Alerts.

JAMES P. NEELANKAVIL
Professor, Department of Marketing and International Business
Ph.D., New York University

Dr. Neelankavil was named “Outstanding Faculty of the Year” by the Executive M.B.A. class of 2002. Dr. Neelankavil received a B.Sc. from Kerala University in India, an M.B.A. with distinction from the Asian Institute of Management, Philippines and a Ph.D. in international business from the Stern School of Business, New York University. Formerly Acting Dean of Hofstra’s School of Business, Dr. Neelankavil has also served as Associate Dean. Dr. Neelankavil has had extensive industrial experience with Firestone Tires, Inc., in Bombay. He has also served on the faculty of the Asian Institute of Management, Manila, Philippines, as well as the Stern School of Business of New York University. He has had visiting professorships at Bocconi University in Milan and at the Rotterdam School of Management in the Netherlands. Dr. Neelankavil is the author of International Business Management Simulation; Global Business; Contemporary Issues, Problems and Challenges; Advertising Regulation Worldwide; and Advertising Self- Regulation: A Global Perspective. His research papers have appeared in many leading journals, including the Journal of International Business Studies, European Journal of Marketing, the Journal of Management Development, the Journal of Global Marketing and Business Horizon. Dr. Neelankavil’s research has included cross-cultural study of managerial skills, international research techniques, international advertising and global strategies. He has been a consultant to GTE, Health Chem, Victorias Milling and Everest Financial Corporation.

LUKE C. NG
Adjunct Associate Professor, Department of Marketing and International Business
M.B.A., Harvard Business School

Professor Ng is currently an adjunct faculty member within the Frank G. Zarb School of Business graduate program. He came to Hofstra with 30 years of industry experience at such companies as Procter and Gamble, American Home Products, Nestle and Pan American World Wide Airways. In addition, he was a consultant to Lifetime Hoan Corporation; marketer and distributor of Cuisinart, Farberware, Kitchen Aid, Hoffritz and private label products and adviser to the managing director and chairpersons of a corporation listed on the Hang Seng Stock index. Professor Ng was also a consultant to Chinese companies regarding marketing strategies and foreign expansion while advising United Statesbased companies looking to venture into Hong Kong and China. Professor Ng is also the founder and chief operating officer of a group of upscale restaurants.
