ABOUT THE BAND

George Dimitri - Piano, Keyboards, Acoustic and Electric Rhythm Guitars, Lead and Back-up Vocals, Harmonica

A seasoned veteran of the live music scene, George began his playing career back in 1985 when he purchased a Cort hot pink Titan bass (which he still has). He thumped out the low end in 80’s local bands Taz and In N Out. George has 2 CD recordings of original music from those bands. In 1992, with the coming demise of the 80’s metal scene, George moved on to Top40 and for the next 10 years played in successful Top40 variety bands such as Impostor, Skyline, and Wildfire (voted Best Band overall – 2000 Western Maryland Times/News Annual Reader’s Poll). It was with Wildfire that George moved from bass to playing keyboards, guitar, and harmonica.

George has years of theory training, vocal coaching, and instrument lessons under his belt. The versatility necessary to playing all the styles of music that Top40 demands has made George an exceptionally complete player on several instruments. You can check out George as a featured artist on the Conklin guitars web page at by clicking here.

Accenting his playing, George is a very active entertainer. His energy is unparalleled on stage. “I have always felt that musicians need to entertain and showmanship on stage is part of the entertainment package.”

George resides in LaPlata, MD and has a top-flight cosmetic dental practice in Waldorf, Md. If you need dental advice George will be happy to give you his card but, please, do not ask him for dental treatment while at a band job. Check out his website at www.dimitridental.com.

Alan Colvin - lead & rhythm guitar, 6 string acoustic guitar, lead vocals, back-up vocals, slide guitar

Alan started his musical training at the behest of his mother who insisted that he take piano lessons at the age of 10. Even though it was not his choice, he continued the lessons for 2 years before his father, without any prompting or request, bought him an acoustic guitar and a 30-page beginner book just to see what he would do with it on Christmas morning. Well it turned out to be a lot more entertaining than anyone expected. By the end of Christmas day, Alan had learned how to string and tune the guitar and before he went to bed that night, was playing along with the vinyl 45 copy of “She Loves You” on Swan records by the Beatles, which was also a Christmas present. And the rest, they say, is history.

With his love for the guitar and his natural ability well recognized, he entered his first band when he was 14. Along with a group of fellow members of a drum corp. called “The Pink Panthers” that he maintained a membership which brought him, his colleages, and his community great distinction in winning 10 national championships in 12 years, he got his first band and stage show experience.

Since then Alan has played in several different bands. The two most notable of these were “Side Effects” which was together, and very successful, from 1984 – 1986 where the band worked all over the Washington metro area. The majority of there work was in College Park and Georgetown in clubs like “The Bastille”, “The Paragon”, and “The Bayou” where the band got their opportunity to play there own material while warming up for national acts.

The last band Alan was in before joining with Alive-n-Kickin' was called “Back Ally Jones”. He formed BAJ after his previous band “Licking Post” disbanded over their displeasure of Alan’s choice of ME Jones as co-lead vocalist and true to his conviction, she was his first choice when picking members for the new band. BAJ enjoyed great success playing all over the metro area, as well as Baltimore, Annapolis, and Ocean City. During the seven years that BAJ was in existence, there were several bright moments including gigs at Massenutten Ski Resort, The Purple Moose, and a new original CD release called “Set Me Free” where Alan either wrote or co-wrote nine of the thirteen songs on the CD.

After the breakup of BAJ, Alan found himself without a gig in the works for more years than he’s got fingers but that didn’t last long. Drummer Steve Miller had found out about the breakup and in less than two months of the previous band’s ending, was knocking on his door asking him to join with him in this new band.

Now here we are! With his years of experience and his vocal and guitar talent he has become a valuable member of Alive-n-Kickin' and expects that this will last a good long time.

Chris Welborn - 6 string bass guitar

Chris has been playing bass for 16 years. He was a founding member
Of the power metal band "Division" (91-94). He played in "Popp Culture" (with Steve), a top 40, swing, horn band fronted by John Popp (producer of the G. Gordon Liddy Show)(95-97). He was also the recording and touring bassist for Dark Symphonies recording artists "Arise From Thorns / Brave". He recorded three albums with them: AFT- Before an Audience of Stars, Brave- Waist Deep in Dark Waters, Brave- Searching for the Sun. Chris also toured the US extensively under both band names. He is an accomplished audio engineer and is currently working in the world of pro audio retail.

Steve Miller - drums , lead vocals, back-up vocals

Drawn to the energy and passion of music, Steve began playing drums after seeing his first KISS concert in 1983. Eric Carr became his instant hero that night, and remains so to this day. "I remember feeling the bass drums hitting me in the chest with every beat!!" Ever since, Steve has been driven to succeed in drumming, no matter what scale or stage he may be on.

His passion has taken him from Baltimore to the sunshine of Los Angeles, in search of the best musicians, bands, and teachers to help him hone his craft. This has included a long tuteledge under his original teacher, Myles Evans (DC STAR, KIK* The Dog) and several sessions with the legendary BOBBY ROCK (Nelson, Vinnie VIncent). "I am constantly learning and practicing. There is always something new to work on, rudiments; hand speed; polyrythyms; DOUBLEBASS; technique; POWER!!! I will never stop!!!!'

With Alive-n-Kickin' Steve hopes to achieve the goal of making this the best band in the mid-Atlantic area. "The music we are playing is what I grew up on. It is the music I used to practice to in my bedroom after school. It is the music that was always the most fun. It is the soundtrack from the era of EXCESS!!!! " That is exactly how Steve plays, with EXCESS!!!! Excessive Hitting, Excessive Energy, Excessive Charisma...Excessive POWER!!!!

Dave Meagher - lead vocals

Dave started listening to heavy metal as a teenager, with Back In Black and KISS Alive being the first albums he ever purchased. A friend later introduced him to Iron Maiden, and that was the beginning of a long love affair. After he was hooked on The Scorpions and Queensrÿche it became apparent that he had a natural affinity to music with soaring, killer vocals. He worshiped the vocal gods - Dickinson, Tate, Halford, Dio... "I would sing along with these guys and attempt to emulate their styles, but I quickly realized you can only sound like yourself."

In 2001, Dave co-founded the progressive metal cover band, Critical Path, with two friends. "We started from scratch - Rick played guitar, Ron played bass, and I offered to sing. It was my first real band so I didn't know what to expect - I just knew I loved the music and I wanted to try my hand at singing. It soon became clear that although I had some raw talent, I didn't know how to control it. After months of practicing and playing out I had improved significantly, but felt I had reached a plateau so I sought out a vocal coach." Dave hooked up with Steve Whiteman (KIX, Funny Money) in early 2003 and has been training with him ever since. "It is a real revelation when you realize how much theory and technique there is behind singing properly."

Although relatively new to the band scene, Dave aspires to help raise Alive-n-Kickin' to another level of excellence and to provide the people who watch the band with a musical experience they will enjoy and remember.

Dave currently listens to music by Fates Warning, Iron Maiden, Dream Theater, Queensrÿche and admits that he loves the new metal: Disturbed, Godsmack, etc.. He is a software engineer and resides in Monkton, MD with his wife, children, canine, and reptiles.

"When you know that your time is close at hand, maybe then you'll begin to understand that life down here is just a strange illusion..."
