I've been very happy with the performance of the KIII bags (also available, below) but always thought it would be nice to have a very special, high-quality bag, one that I could truly call my own. You'll find that this bag reflects some of my favorite features of that KIII bag that I've used and sold for so long, but adds a few touches I liked. Here's the result - a roomy, well-padded, special case at a great price!

If you've been to my site before, you know that one of my most important criteria for a bass bag, aside from the obvious protection and price, is how easy it is to get on and off. I don't feel like having a wrestling match with a bag with one hand while trying to keep the bass steady with the other. These bags have zippers on both sides of the bag -- one that goes from the endpin to within a few inches of the top, and the other that just about opens to the top of the lower bout of the bass. As you see from the photo, this bag opens w-i-d-e, ready to swallow your bass without the aggravation other cases have caused me in the past. My old Kay bass is inside the case in the multi-view photo to the left - it fits it like a glove! Click on the image (detailed diagram) below to see detailed measurements of the case.




Features:
  • FIVE double stitched handles for easy maneuvering, carrying and transporting - a heavily reinforced double suitcase style on the side for both vertical and horizontal carrying, plus an adjustable length lower bout handle so you can set it for your reach length. Plus, this case adds a trio of what I like to call "wrangling" handles; one down near the endpin, a vertical grab type on the side and one up above the front pocket - these handles give you more handholds when you're trying to maneuver your bass into a small car or through a doorway. The latest version of the case adds a second suitcase-style handle on the other side.

  • Monster sized, self-healing zippers on both sides and all pockets. Smooth, luggage grade zippers provide long and trouble-free service.

  • "No stress" zipper stops at the end pin avoids "zipper-strip" and ensures a long life. Plenty of room for your endpin to support the instrument in transit, and it is accessible if you use a bass wheel.

  • Large padded bridge protector with our International BASS logo.

  • One bow pocket on the front. The bow pocket has been lengthened to 31 inches to accommodate longer bows.

  • Three accessory compartments on the back, including a string package-sized at the top of the headstock, and one on the front in the traditional position.

  • A pair of METAL D-rings (one on each side, near the upper bout) to help secure or carry the instrument. (See detail photos for closeups of the rings). Some owners have attached straps of their own to these anchor points (not included).

  • Rich lining with finished seams -- no rough edges to rub your bass the wrong way.

  • Extra thick, high density, 20mm two-stage foam padding that is nicely firm, not floppy, and sealed within the fabric of the case; the two complementary foam layers consist of a 15mm "cushy" foam for soft protection and a 5mm "stiff" layer to provide body and rigidity.

  • Durable Cordura style, heavy duty material outer shell, not that lightweight nylon fabric - and it's in a very tasteful hunter green, adding a touch of class and individuality over the far more common black or brown.

  • Custom-embroidered "Bass" emblem - when the inevitable comedian sees you walking with the bass and says "gee, what a big violin!" you can point to it a give them the appropriate look of disdain. Click on the image at right for a close-up. You'll also receive a BASS Oval sticker (see below) with your order!

Professional Upright Bass Case


I've been looking for a new case for a while, one with truly professional features. While the Gollihur and the K-III Bags are great cases for the money, some of my customers who are gigging more frequently have asked for a bag with heavier padding and backpack straps. I'm happy to announce that I've finally found such a bag, and it still won't break the bank!



This new bag has dense, lined padding that is approximately 1¼" thick. How dense? In the pictures on this page, the bag is standing on its own with no instrument inside it! This is no floppy bag. Internally, the padding extends over the zippers to protect your instrument's finish from being scratched by the heavy-duty zippers.



The seven carrying and "wrangling" handles have some serious stitching, and use reinforced leather tabs for rugged reliability -- the photos tell the tale. Many of the handles have a soft vinyl wrap for comfort, and the handles are in sensible, useful locations for carrying the bag - as well as maneuvering it through a doorway or into your car.


Another useful feature: wheels!

The case has a pair of durable 1.5 inch luggage-style wheels at the bottom of the back. They're mounted to a sturdy but lightweight internal plate to provide structure. Also note the cinch-straps at the bottom of the case; these allow you to tighten the bag to provide a more secure fit for your instrument. There is also a cinch-strap around the base of the neck to keep the neck from moving around inside the case.

This deluxe case also has plenty of skid-patches at points where the back might contact the ground; these provide additional impact protection to your bass, as well as protect the fabric of the case from premature wear.

Pockets? This case has plenty! On the front, an extra-large pleated bow pocket has room for many hard and soft bow cases (unfortunately ours is a little too big) and there's also an expandable accessory pocket for rosin, tuner, pickup and preamp, or other small "bassic" items. On the back, the large pair of flat pockets are great for music, lesson material, and more. And the back pockets have protective flaps over the zippers to protect your back when carrying the bass...

Oh, did I forget to mention? This bag has BACKPACK STRAPS, a feature that I have often been asked about! They're adjustable, well-padded, and have a harness across the front to keep them from slipping off your shoulders. And like the handles, they're reinforced and heavily stiched.

Professional Bass Case Features:
  • 1¼ inches of firm, lined padding to protect your bass - extends over zippers to protect your instrument's finish
  • Seven heavily stitched, reinforced handles for easy carrying and loading
  • monster sized, smooth luggage-grade zippers on both sides; and the main zipper goes completely around the scroll to allow the case to open completely
  • large leather bridge protector
  • Padded, adjustable backpack straps INCLUDED
  • Luggage-style wheels for easy maneuvering
  • Large pleated bow pocket on the front, approx. 3.25 x 5 inches
  • Expandable accessory pocket
  • Two flat oversized zippered compartments on the back
  • Lots of rubber bumpers/skid patches
  • rich lining with finished seams
  • Durable heavy duty cordura-type outer shell, not that light nylon fabric
