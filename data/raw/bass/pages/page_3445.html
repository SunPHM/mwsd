Molto Pasta
Source: Businessworld
By: Raoul J. Chee Kee

Email the editor
Print this article
Email to a friend
Subscribe to our newsletter

Ever wondered why pasta is considered by many as the perfect time-saving comfort food? If cooked correctly, meaning the noodles or shells or what-have-you are firm but pliant, it can easily serve anywhere from one person to an entire family. Sauces can range from a simple ground meat and tomato sauce or olive oil and garlic to more elaborate ones with multiple ingredients like mushrooms, cured meats and capers.

Pasta is also a good example of a one-dish meal. For harried mothers, pasta is a godsend because in less than 30 minutes, they can whip up a filling meal their kids are bound to appreciate. Even those living solo can enjoy a plateful of hot pasta with a bit of extra virgin olive oil and parmesan cheese.

Perhaps realizing how much Filipinos enjoy pasta, Mi Piace at The Peninsula Manila has an ongoing month-long promotion that began early this month and ends on July 6.

Guests that come for the pasta can choose from one of two options. The first one is the a la carte menu where the selection consists of seven preparations by resident Italian chef Michele Mingozzi. The second is the pasta "buffet" where a group (minimum of four Persons) can choose two types of pasta from the six available, and six types of sauces from the 10 available. Instead of having to stand up to fill and refill one's plate however, the pastas and sauces will be placed on the guests' tables.

At the press preview, the chef gave us a taste of items from both the a la carte and the buffet menu. Although there were only seven to choose from in the first group, many of us had a hard time deciding what we liked best so we ended up trying and retrying all seven.

The ravioli of ricotta and spinach with home-made smoked salmon and fresh tomatoes was light and tasty while the green fettuccine with duck and fresh artichokes was obviously one of the heartier. The Tortelli filled with chopped chicken and turkey had an olive oil sauce and was topped with generous slices of salami and pepperoni -- a meat-lover's delight, no doubt.

The cannelloni of mushroom with crab meat and venison was proclaimed by one as "the best I've ever tasted" although I felt certain that the combination of crab meat and venison was not a traditional pairing.

Mr. Mingozzi agreed saying that both items were known for their strong and distinct flavors.

"I needed to figure out a way to combine the two without one overpowering or clashing with the other."

The other a la carte pasta dishes in the menu include Garganelli with lamb bolognaise and pesto; Agnolotti of potatoes filled with goat cheese and a sauce of bell peppers, dill and trout caviar; and Fagottini filled with sea bass and shitake mushroom-broccoli sauce.

Mr. Mingozzi also prepared all 10 sauces that included traditional favorites like the spicy Arrabiata, Napoletana (tomatoes, olive oil and garlic), Bolognese (tomatoes, ground beef, onions, garlic and oregano), carbonara and pesto.

There was also Puttanesca, a sauce that Italian prostitutes were supposed to have concocted after a busy night on the town. Using whatever was in their larder -- tomatoes, anchovies, capers, black and green olives -- they managed to come up with a dish that staved off hunger pangs. In another version, the ladies were said to have whipped up the dish for hungry clients.

During the hotel outlet's pizza promotion two months ago, Mr. Mingozzi came up with a Filipino-inspired pizza that was topped with crispy squid and had tomato ketchup as a sauce. It became one of the promotion's bestsellers and is now part of the outlet's regular menu.

Now, the chef has come up with a pasta counterpart. The EDSA Stuffer sauce has clams, mussels, fresh pineapple, chopped tomatoes, tarragon and lechon kawali.

The other sauces to choose from are the Amatriciana (tomato sauce, dried ham, chili and parsley), the Mediterranean Matinee (tomato sauce, button and abalone mushrooms, prawns, garlic, onions and parsley), and La Dolce Vita (pesto, onions, cherry tomatoes, tuna and olives).

The pastas from the a la carte menu are each priced at PhP395++ (US$7.401++ at PhP53.373=$1) while the pasta buffet (for a minimum of four) will cost PhP300++ per guest.

For inquiries, contact Mi Piace at 887-2888 local 2947 and 2592.
