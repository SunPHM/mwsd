My buddy Karman had suggested going to Roattan to see some whale sharks.  I couldn't make the time for that but I could make the time for a Cayman trip to do some work on the house and knock off a few more days on the divemaster.  Planned a few days on the Brac followed by a few more on the big island.

Thursday:

Landed on Grand Cayman.  The air was pleasantly warm and humid.  It was great to be back!  Katie pulls up in her red Jeep and Karman and I pile in with all our gear.  A few minutes later we're at my place for our layover before the flight to the Brac in the morning.  Went next door for a visit with Lenny and Georgette.  Visited Bob.  He proudly showed off the boat he recently acquired.  He's looking forward to some good fishing.  Katie fixed a great dinner and we all enjoyed talking about the pleasures of island life before hitting the sack.   

Friday:

Up at 6:30 for coffee, then Katie dropped us at the airport.  Had a good breakfast at the airport restaurant then waited for the Island Air personnel to arrive to check us in for our 10:30 flight.  Hopped aboard the DeHavilland Otter for a scenic flight to the grass landing strip on Little Cayman, then back up to 500' for a 5 minute flight to Cayman Brac. 

Cayman Brac - 4 other people got off the plane with us.  Island shuttle took us a couple of miles from the north side of the runway to the south, dropping us at the Divi Tiara.  Checked in and signed up for the afternoon dive and the destroyer dive for the next morning. Lunch at the hotel restaurant.  Got our dive gear ready for the afternoon dive then rode some bikes a couple of miles to the local convenience store, I needed a toothbrush!  Raced back, picked up camera gear, and headed down to dock where new arrivals got the boat briefing then headed out for our first dive!

Charlie's Reef - click for pics

Followed the dive up with a few red stripes and dinner at the Divi.  Met Gary and Christine, a couple celebrating their 1 year anniversary with a weekend on the Brac.  Gary is one of the 2 construction managers building the new Ritz Carlton on Grand Cayman.  Then hit the sac.  Jumped out of bed about 3am when I thought we were about to get hit by a 747!  Found out later it had been an air ambulance taking off right across the road.

Saturday:

Started off with coffee and a made to order veggie omelet, thanks Cliff!  Got camera gear prepped with wide angle lens for our morning dive on the Tibbets, a Russian destroyer sunk in 1996.  Everything was working well.  

Tibbets - hopped on board the dive boat and a few minutes later we were tying up to the wreck.  Gary dove with me and Karman as Christine wasn't feeling well from the swells encountered on the way over.  Took a couple of test pics at the top of the wreck, and the strobe quit working again!  Dove down to the main deck and the camera shut down altogether!  Swam out to the wall to look over the edge at the deep blue.  No whale sharks.  Karman's computer hickuped indicating he was in deco already so he stayed at the top of the wreck while Gary and I explored it further.  Eventually did our safety stop and got back on board.  The camera now worked OK but I swapped batteries just in case.  

Kissame - click for pics

After lunch at the Divi, I examined all strobe contacts.  Saw that the cable connector that connects at the strobe had a tarnished contact.  Scraped the suspect contacts down and voila, reliable operation again!  Note to self:  After the trip, soak all contacts in fresh water for an hour then air dry for a few days.

Grunt Valley - click for pics

We talked to a few others interested in a night dive.  Liz at the Divi recommended Radar Reef for easy access.  Divi  left tanks out for the 6 of us, Jeff, Kristi, Kerry, Carol, Karman, and myself.  Only had one car so we shuttled over in 2 trips.  

Radar Reef - click for pics

Sunday:

Another great veggie omelet, then we were off to dive Bloody Bay Wall at Little Cayman.  This was the best diving of the trip! 

Bloody Bay Wall - click for pics

Monday:

Up for our final breakfast.  Packed up and caught the shuttle to the airport for 10:30 flight back to Grand Cayman.  Got our rent car at Coconuts, a Susuki Jeep.  Immediately removed the rag top in favor of the sun.  Checked in at Tarquynn then loaded up our gear for a shore dive at Sunset House.  

Sunset House - click for pics

Joined Karman at the bar for a few red stripes and a mahi mahi sandwich.  Got cleaned up at the condo then went to Deckers for tuna sashimi and drinks.  Joy was working and I found out she and Jae are now engaged!  Congratulations to both!  

Tuesday:

Turtle Reef - Karman left at 7:30am to meet up with Patrick for a boat dive.  Sebastian from Divetech picked me up to continue my divemaster training.  Helped Ken do the 1st 2 open water checkout dives with 3 guys from N.Carolina.  After the students practiced the requisite skills, Ken led a short pleasure dive and I pulled up the rear.  Both dives at Turtle Reef.  A big variety of stuff:   turtles, cudas, lobster, spotted moray, golden tailed moray, tarpon, scorpionfish, and all the usual reef life. 

Got a ride back to Tarquynn.  Karman had gotten narced on his am dive as they came out of a swimthrough on the wall at 128'!  Got a bite then went up to Turtle Reef for a long snorkel as I was working on my mapping project for the site.  Got a couple of tanks and dove the site left and right to get the depth readings.

Hung out with Martin at the Cracked Conch's outdoor bar.  Martin is from Sweden but has spent the last few years in Brazil and the Caribbean.  After a few red stripes Karman bought a couple of Kamakaze rounds for everyone.  Then Martin treated us to another round.  Sashimi dinner at the Bamboo, the suchi bar at the Hyatt.  Then across the street to Deckers where Joy was working again.  After a few grand marniers and a cuban cigar, it was all downhill.  Made it home somehow.

Wednesday:

The next morning neither of us were in shape to go diving.  Each wisely cancelled.  Felt human again in time to confirm my afternoon schedule at Divetech. 

Turtle Reef - Helped do a guided scooter dive with Kym for a couple from the UK.  Kym led the dive while I pulled up the rear.  What a blast!  Squeeze the trigger that turns on the battery driven prop and hang on.  Just have be careful about getting too close to the coral or making any sudden depth changes.    You can cover a lot of ground in a hurry. 

Sea bass at the Reef Grill for dinner.  Delicious as always!

Thursday:

Up at 5 to drop Karman at the airport for his 7:30 flight.  Divetech at Cobalt Coast to help Kym do a guided dive with a couple from Michigan.  Kym guided the first one right, then I guided the next one left.  Saw a nice green moray out on the prowl.

Friday:

Back to Divetech at Turtle Reef to help Gary guide the same couple from day before on a couple of dives there.
Ran some errands at lunch then back to Divetech for my final written tests with Seb.
Drove down to the Seaview to say hi to Jae and got an invite to their beach get together on Saturday at Silver Sands.

Saturday:

Back to Divetech at Turtle Reef to help Daniel with confined water checkout portion of an open water dive class.

Silver Sands in the afternoon to see what Jae and Joy were up to.  They and about 20 friends were having a cookout, playing some volleyball, and doing some wakeboarding.  Did a snorkel to get some final pics.

Snorkel - click for pics

Sunday:

Over to Georgette's for breakfast and coffee.  Went by Divetech to settle up and say goodbye.  Have about 3-4 days left on the DM.  Met Dave back at the house as he had the railing for the loft.  Just stored it up there for him to get to later.  Took care of a few maintenance items and then packed up my stuff for the trip back.  Goodbyes to Georgette, Katie, and Bob, then headed to the airport for my afternoon flight.   Miami, Dallas, then Denver.  Home about midnight.

End of another great trip!
