Great Barrier Reef

Check-Out Trip

 

 

 

 

 This experience was an awesome surprise - filled with adventure!

by
Truxton Terkla
 

Click the photos below to enlarge them!

 

For as long as I can remember, Australia's Great Barrier Reef was one of the diving destinations "on my list" to travel.  Watching National Geographic's Explorer, seeing video specials on the reef by NOVA and reputation alone, made this historical reef the one place in the world I wanted to dive; actually, the GBR is why many people learn to dive at all - it certainly was in my case!

After considering travel to this popular diving "Mecca", I was discouraged by several operators and travel professionals; they felt the reef was over-dove, poor vis, never lived up to its reputation - the negative press went on and on.  It was incredibly disappointing to learn from those (with a great deal more experience than myself in the diving travel industry) that my 'reason for diving' was no more than just hype.  We then planned our 2004 Winter Warm-up Adventure trip to Fiji, where Australia was less than 4 hours away.  This seemed like an ideal opportunity to see for myself.

After a brief layover in Brisbane, Lavonne & I boarded Qantas Air for Cairns (pronounced "cans") where we would spend only one night before embarking on a 3-day voyage to the Ribbon Reefs & Cod Hole aboard the Spirit of Freedom liveaboard.  

Staff from the ship transferred us to the 120 foot vessel - only in service as a liveaboard since October, 2002.  We were shown to our cabin and then directed to meet in the spacious dining area.  Since this ship can hold up to 28 divers comfortably, we had more than enough room with 22 of us!  A general safety briefing & staff introductions were conducted by the captain and each of the other 7 crew-members.  We were then told to set up our gear and check in with the dive deck as we were now underway to the outer Great Barrier Reef where we would do 2 dives that afternoon, the first being the customary "check-out dive".  A delicious, filling lunch with gourmet salads and sliced deli meats was then served and we relaxed on chaise lounges on the sundeck until we reached our first diving destination.  The weather was beautiful - calm, clear, 83 degree seas with an air temperature of roughly 85 - 90.

A thorough dive briefing commenced once we arrived to the first spot.  Excited, we quickly donned our gear (as we would do 9 more times during this 3-day adventure) and proceeded to step off of the boat into the beautiful, still water.  The beauty, however, diminished once we left the surface and reached the bottom; the conditions were murky with a fine silt coating all of the hard corals.  The colorless landscape not only shocked us, but burst our balloon of happiness, replacing it with absolute disappointment.  Underwater, we looked at one another and telepathically agreed the other operators we spoke with were right - the Great Barrier Reef isn't so hot after all!

We struggled to find some active sea-life and make the best of the dive.  After one hour, we came up and immediately voiced our concern to the ship's Dive Director, Andy.  Andy told us that this was only a close-in dive - one that many of the day boats and smaller liveaboards usually take divers to.  They use it for check-out dives as it's only a little over two hours from shore and gives people a chance to get wet before the awesome diving on the outer Ribbon Reef.  Andy encouraged us to let him know what we thought after tomorrow's dives and not to judge the GBR diving by this spot alone.  He told us that our first dive site was one of the most popular for day boats and it's where most people dive, who do not have access to a liveaboard.  If this were true, putting all the facts together, we could understand why people thought the GBR had terrible diving; they were never on a liveaboard! 

Keeping this in mind, the jury was still out until we did our first morning dive in the outer reef - it was absolutely amazing!!!  We've been to Palau, Thailand, Fiji - some of the best diving in the world and this ranked with Palau for the big stuff & hard corals and Thailand for the many schools of fish & color - it was just like I always hoped it would be!  School after school of barracuda, trevally, fusiliers, jacks, red sea bass, king fish, mackerel and others completely dominated the 200' plus crystal blue waters.  White tip and gray reef sharks sailed by from time to time as did several hump-nosed wrasse, huge 300 pound cod and thousands of schooling athians.  Countless beautifully shaped table corals adorned the sheer reef walls as well as the many pinnacles from which this abundantly rich environment of sea-life feeds.  We also saw several scorpion fish, spotted rays, curious moray eels, trigger fish, hundreds of Moorish idols, clownfish, nudibranchs, banded shrimp, crabs, giant clams and more!  We were so pleasantly surprised (not to mention relieved) to find the diving to be so fantastic!  Each dive progressively got better - we were in awe at the way the fish were not afraid and would come right up to us!

Logs are signed by each passenger after every dive to avoid any "accountability issues" and your tank is set up only once and then you never touch it, except to use it!  The competent and fun-loving dive staff takes care of everything!!  Your cameras are carefully looked after and placed in the rinse tank by the staff and your equipment is rinsed daily.  

Perhaps the one thing that amazed us most with the staff, was their positive attitudes and willingness to help one another - in any capacity.  Rather than have a captain only run the vessel and not speak to guests, Darby (the skipper of the Spirit of Freedom) would help clear plates after meals, wash silverware and haul garbage.  Each of the staff-members really worked hard to make the operation successful and were actually "fun" people who liked their jobs and showed it in both their professionalism and ability.

We never went hungry and were treated to mouth-watering fare at every meal, culminating with a sundeck barbeque of lamb, chicken and fish on the final night.  If you're into staying on board instead of participating in any of the 4 dives per day, a complete library of DVDs is available anytime.  While beer & wine is served with meals, the bar is on an honor system for those who have completed diving for the day - it's wonderful!

Our 3-day adventure came to an end and we reluctantly left the ship via transfer by the boat staff to our hotel.  We were now on our own with only 2 days to get some more adventure!

After dropping our bags at the hotel, we were whisked away to one of the popular tourist attractions:  the crocodile farm.  Here, we learned about how crocs are farmed and raised to protect them.  Aside from the crocodile show, snake experience and crocodile boat cruise / feeding, we were fortunate enough to see the crocodile attack show.  Here, the handler feeds a chicken to the snapping jaws of one of the huge reptilian swamp creatures and has the animal demonstrate its primal behavior for the visitors - it was outrageous!  An actual echo was heard as the wily beast lunged toward the raw chicken clapping his powerful jaws in an attempt to capture his prey.

Our final day in Cairns was called the "Big Day Out" - an experience consisting of an early morning hot air balloon ride, champagne breakfast buffet in the "bush", guided tour through the tropical rainforest via an Army Duck (an amphibious jeep produced for WWII) now used to travel both on land and in the rivers of the rainforest, an aborigine show, learning how to throw a boomerang, holding koalas, petting kangaroos, viewing huge waterfalls and a 20 minute flight over the rainforest on the skyrail (tram).  Normally, people don't do all of this in one day, but we wanted to experience the most we could and we didn't have a lot of time!  

For those less 'faint of heart', Australia rivals New Zealand for adventure by offering multiple sky-diving opportunities, bungee-jumping, parasailing, bungee-jumping from a parasail, and more!  If you want to do it, it's here!!  If it's not, all you need to do is ask - Aussie's want to be known for their adventure-seeking & excitement!  It was such an awesome place, we have already booked the liveaboard for February 21 - 28, 2005 for our "Aussie Adventure"!  

Our plans include one week aboard the Spirit of Freedom, diving both the Ribbon Reefs of the Great Barrier Reef and the Coral Sea, and an additional week doing land-based activities in both Cairns and Sydney.  Our 7-day diving excursion will feature 4 dives each day, all accommodations, meals, diving, tanks and weights.  Nitrox will be available for $10 per tank or $180 as a package price for the week.

We are planning to spend 2 nights in Cairns before boarding the Spirit of Freedom.  This will give us enough time to begin acclimating to the time change as we experience the outback, fly high above sprawling farm-lands from a hot-air balloon, rock down raging rivers aboard a raft, and visit the lush, tropical rainforest.  

Once we have disembarked from the liveaboard, we will have one more night in Cairns.  As we'll be getting off the boat in the morning, we have scheduled a trip to Hartley's Crocodile Adventure Farm.  Here, we will be able to learn more about the crocs and see the ferocious crocodile attack show.  While it's not an all-day adventure, we will have some time to relax at our hotel and do some last-minute shopping.  

The next day, we will travel to Sydney - Australia's first settlement and largest city.  Here, we will spend 4 nights, giving us ample time to enjoy the culture and participate in a variety of excursions.  We have included one outing where we will explore the city as well as the Southern Beaches.  We then will board Matilda Cruises for a spectacular lunch buffet, cruising around Sydney Harbor where we will see the famous Sydney Opera House, Sydney Harbor Bridge, Fort Denison and other awe-inspiring views.  

We have time to really enjoy the culture and experience many things such as the Hunter Valley Wine Tour, famous Sydney Harbor BridgeClimb, and Blue Mountains Tour before leaving Sydney.  There are other things to do as well to experience the culture and excitement of the "Down Under"!

CAPE TRIBULATION 4-WD (TREK NORTH SAFARIS)                   $119.00 pp

Collected from your hotel by Trek North Safaris for your full day Cape Tribulation Tour. A four wheel drive day tour into the World Heritage rainforest of Cape Tribulation north of Cairns Australia, includes a river cruise, guided rainforest walks and BBQ lunch. The coastline between the Daintree River and Cooktown north of Cairns in Far North Queensland. Cape Tribulation is a rare example of the co-existence of two of the world's most diverse ecosystems, the tropical rainforest and coral reef. The Daintree and Cape Tribulation rainforest is one of the oldest in the world, with a high proportion of primitive species, and was given World Heritage status in 1988. Your experienced guide will give a full interpretation of this rainforest and will expand on the geographical and historical features of the region. Cape Tribulation is known to the locals as "Cape Trib".

On your Daintree River cruise your guide will help you find Estuarine crocodiles basking on the banks, egrets, kites and other bird life overhead or an Amethystine python in the tree tops. British naturalist, David Attenborough, described his time spent in the Daintree rainforest as "One of the most magical experiences of my life". Trek North Tours from Cairns or Port Douglas will help ensure that you have the same experience.

FULL DAY BLUE MOUNTAINS TOUR (10 hrs)                                 $120.00 pp

Depart from your hotel by private luxury coach and travel via the western areas of the city. Travel on to the Blue Mountains, named because of the blue haze caused by light rays striking dust particles and small droplets of moisture and eucalyptus oil in the atmosphere. The Blue Mountains are part of the Great Dividing Range which runs parallel to the East Coast of Australia for more than 4,000 kilometers from North Queensland to Victoria. 

See the world famous rock formation "The Three Sisters", enjoy distant views of Jamieson Valley, Mount Solitary, Katoomba Falls and many other scenic delights.

Enjoy a delicious BBQ Lunch at Jemby Rinjah Lodge prior to your visit to Scenic World, where you will have the opportunity to ride the world’s steepest incline railway down into the valley and return back up by the new cable car called the Sceniscender. Connecting the two bottom stations is a 300 meter wooden boardwalk through the rainforest passing the entrances to the old coal mines where there is a high tech visual display.

Return to Sydney, passing Homebush Bay - the site of the Sydney 2000 Olympics.

Includes: private luxury coach and commentary driver, Buffet lunch and return scenic rail journey at ScenicWorld..
