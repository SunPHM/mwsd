Event: Glide

STAR DATE: Apr.08.2000
WHO: Atomic Dog Productions presents Glide
LOCATION: Rollerland (Calgary)
TIX: $26.00 advance, $30.00 door
DETAILS:

This event was heralded by many as the best event in 6-8 months. There were over 33 posts about this party on the local mesage board and not one was negative.

DJ Trancelott played amazing and wrote us this note: "... That party was the best I have ever played, largely in part because of how good the sound was. It was perfect, no reverb off the back wall and clear as a bell, you made me sound awesome. ..."

DJ Emotion writes: "... quite impressed with the sound. Better than most in Calgary. Good visuals. ..."

There was a 'pumpin system, 1000 lumen projection screen, live video visuals, 4 Technobeams and a some conventional lighting.

Atomic Dog's Parties kick ass, we have never been treated so nicely before. Vik from Atomic Dog is one of the most classy and together people we have ever met. If we were 1/10th as cool as Vik, we'd be up for sainthood. All the staff at Rollerland deserve some credit too. You guys are great!!!

Event: Oasis

STAR DATE: 31.Aug.1999
WHO: Technique Crew
LOCATION: Wayne, Alberta (near Drumheller)
IN ADVANCE: 40
DETAILS:
Featuring:

G.I. Jody, Geoffry J, Triptomene, Meek, Paradise, Mahdi, Honeey Mustard, Paul Phreak, Binary, Fersohi, Tracerburn Tribal Collective, Evil G, MC Black Rose, Frensh, Koosh, Karizma, Delerious, Domenic G, Alcheme, Anonymous Flow, High & I

This was the first outdoor event of it's kind in Alberta. There was dust, rain, sun and cloud and we had to bring in 2 generators to power the light and sound. This entire weekend was a non-stop party!!! We got sound complaints from 14 Km's away (turns out sound travels very easily in the badlands). You should have seen the looks on the RCMP's faces when they showed up! That was more excitement than they had seen in while!

Some of our raver friends wondered why had to shut everything down at 6:45 AM. The reason was we were contracted (in writing) for quiet time between 6AM and 2PM Saturday morning so the sound techs could get some ZZZ's after working 24 hours straight. Unfortunately, the DJ's ran past the contracted time, the techs gave the unfortunate DJ an extra half an hour, but then they had to pack it in. Regrettably, there are certain labor laws we must abide by as to length of shift. By the time the guys got back to the motel, slept, ate and got back to the gig for 2PM, they had only managed to get about 5 hours sleep before having to go till 8:30 AM the next morning (Sunday). So, the crew worked 24 hours, slept 5 hours and worked another 16.5 hours straight, slept 3 hours, ate, then started striking down to be back in Calgary for 4PM.

If anyone at the event was curious as to why the sound would cut out from one side of the sound system, it was due to one of the turntable cartridges and a turntable RCA plug being worn out. The tables and mixer were donated from one of the DJ's. We shut things down for a few minutes to re-wire and hopefully alleviate the problems. It helped a little and we switched the system to mono which stopped the one side from cutting in and out. Being two hours from Calgary, we didn't have access to our repair shop so our ability to do on the spot repairs was limited, which totally sucked. It's always a good idea to carry a spare cartridge even if someone else is supplying the tables. It's a lifesaver!

We apologize to everyone and the DJ's who thought we were playing 24 Hours a day, but we were not contracted to do so and if we had, we would have gladly supplied extra staff and made the necessary arrangements. We love a good party as much as anyone! We did play out till 9:00 AM Sunday though!

Peace, and once again, our sincerest apologies to anyone who thought our staff just left the party without good reason. We'll always be there to the bitter end unless someone with a big stick tells us otherwise!!!
