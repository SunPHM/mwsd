By Zaki: Lets groove

Bassist

  • Puts the bottom on the rhythm section/ work closely with the drummer

  • Has the responsibility of maintaining an absolutely solid, steady beat, and at the same time providing a "feeling" of pushing ahead, in a fast tempo, or laying back, at a slower tempo, while never varying the pulse even the slightest

Tips & Tricks

  1. Good sense of time - listen closely to the drummer and the rest of the rhythm section, lock in with the drum- ride cymbal beat
  2. Work for long , pizzicato sound and good linear playing - notes moving with harmonic smoothness from one to another.
  3. Play basic notes of chord and outline the harmonic structure of the tune
  4. Learn chords and form of tune
  5. Develop left hand and better intonation
  6. Develop reading of chord symbols and written rhythms

Zaki's Guitar

Hey guyz... Lets take a look on the part of a bass guitar :

  • A demanding job - it's the only instrument which has a dual function - provides rhythmic and harmonic foundation simultaneously.
  • In a rhythm section, bassist spend 80-90% of the time accompanying others.
  • Good or average player depend largely on the ability to accompany.
  • The bass line should stand on its own without any assistance from others.

Technique

  • Basically roots and fifth , arpeggios
  • tonic / dominant - importance in harmony
  • what to practise - scales, techniques, melody playing , sight reading, chord changes, style/patterns - pizzicato, picking, slap & pull, muted technique, strumming/ plucking
  • Walking bass
    • a succession of quarter notes using scalar, chordal, or chromatic approaches
    • is a continuous stream of quarter notes that outlines the harmony and locks up with the ride cymbal provide a strong, consistent pulse.
      • two feel
      • quarter notes
  • Approaches
    • chordal ( arpeggios)
    • scalar
    • chromatic
    • semitone approach
    • in thirds ( intervals)
    • substitutions
    • pedal point

Things you should know & remember playing the bass

  1. Have good rhythmic feeling. You are the pulse of any ensemble.
  2. Have good constructed bass lines based on the chord progressions
    • use chordal approach
    • use scalar approach
    • use chromatic or passing note approach 
    • use rhythmic variation complimenting to the musical style
  3. Listen:
    • to the piano or whatever chordal instrument is used
    • to the drums and/or percussion instruments
    • to the horns if any
  4. Watch the piano players left hand. Know the keyboard well.
  5. Have eye contact with all the musicians
  6. Be sensitive to all dynamics. Learn to adjust instantly without losing the energy
  7. Be authoritative. Be deliberate
    • be precise, intonation is very important
    • be very flexible
