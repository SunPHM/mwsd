Positano

June 4 - 6, 2002

Day Quattro - From Rome, it took us nearly all day to reach our destination, Positano, but it was worth the wait!  What a completely charming little town, with quaint shops and restaurants built right into the rock cliff.  After haggling for a better room, we had a late dinner at Max, a recommendation from our hotel (Hotel Marincanto). We dined outside in a very secluded lush patio, with music and lots of artwork displayed.  Miguel had ravioli al vongole (excellent ravioli), I had the gnocchi al zucchini and shrimp, with Pompeii wine. For dessert, lemon cake, chocolate soufflé and lemoncello, compliments of "Max" the owner, not the cat in the restaurant.

 

Day Cinque - After sleeping in, we dined on one of the better breakfast buffets we've had.   Cereals, juices, lemon cake, yogurt, fruit, cheeses, and meats, topping off with cappuccino.  We began a walking tour of the town.  Mike bought some leather sandals, we purchased lemoncello, lemon soap and candles at a great lemon shop (lemons are huge in Positano).  browsed antique stores (antiquariato), snapped pictures and felt lucky to be in such an impossibly charming place.  Back to the hotel to freshen up and bask in the sun on our balcony.  After awhile we took the stairs down to the beach and snacked on pizza and Italian beer.  Then, more shopping and booked our night train to Venezia (Venice).  After contemplating the purchase of an adorable antique rosary, we headed back to shower.  Dinner was another hotel recommendation.  It was a good choice, with lots of young couples, candles, and mandolin music.  We didn't even have to request The Godfather, they played it before we could!  I had sea bass and zucchini, Mike had seafood ravioli.  Both were ok, but the Nobile de Montepulciano wine (Poliziano) was excellent!  We grabbed two lemoncellos and a chantilly lemon cake to go, and went back to our private balcony for a candlelit dessert!   Off to bed in hopes of rising early for our last short day in Positano.

 

Day Sei - We slept until 10am again (its amazing how long you will sleep if you don't know what time it is) and rushed to get our breakfast before they closed it.  We showered and checked out of the hotel.  We bought a lemon slush and caught the inter-city bus for a cheap tour of the rest of the city.  We figured out that we weren't missing anything and jumped off in front of the nicest ceramic store we had seen.  In honor of the lemons of Positano, we bought a beautiful bowl with bright yellow lemons.  The old man, original proprietor since 1960, gave us a candle as a gift and a darling cloth shopping bag.  I also bought two t-shirts (Positano Italia w, daisy and Vino Italiano) and a great painting of Positano from a beach vendor.  We think it will look great in an old frame.

We headed up to catch the SITA bus to Sorrento, only to be turned away because the bus was too full.  We decided to split a cab with a nice German couple who was also headed to Sorrento.  Money very well spent as we rode in comfort and arrived in time to buy a gelato before catching our train to Napoli (Naples).  We have a first class private sleeping car, and hope to get lots of sleep since its a direct route and we'll need our rest for Venezia (Venice) in the morning.  Can't wait!
