— 2005 —

2005 was an interesting year for PH. We played many outdoor shows this year which is always fun when the weather cooperates, we only had a few run ins with Mother Nature.

Gigs worth mentioning:

3/28 BB King Blues Club, NYC. Opening for '60's Rock Icon Terry Reid.

4/23 Geneseo Flyday, Geneseo, NY. A yearly "mini" Woodstock with rain, mud and of course naked people running around.

6/18 Kinderstock Festival, Walton, NY. Another yearly outdoor gathering with a wonderful bonfire.

7/9 CNBC Summerfest 2005, Pamona, NY. BIG party, we were backround music but the under 15 crowd loved us.

7/9 Casa DelSol, Nyack, NY. Our first gig in this nice room with a great voodoo vibe was hours after the CNBC gig.

7/24 Rockland World Radio, Hudson House, Nyack, NY. A live webcast with live music, interviews and a Q&A session.

7/27 BB King Blues Club, NYC. Opening for Another '60's Rock Icon Rick Derringer.

8/6 Coyote's Cove, West Milford, NJ. Outside gig at a former biker bar in the woods where a small percentage of the audience were bears.

9/10 Let It Grow Festival, RiverEdge, NJ. Our second year playing this benefit show to a BIG outdoor crowd.

9/26 BB King Blues Club, NYC. Our third go round we felt right at home. This time opening with an "Unplugged" type set with Percussionist Larry McDonald joining us. We then backed Blues Legends, Louisiana Red and HoneyBoy Edwards.

12/31 Kelly's Pub, Tappan, NY. Our 9th gig at Kelly's and it was New years EVE.We shared the stage with the great George Bragg Band, a rippin' R7B band.

2005 also was a good year for composing as we rehearsed 6 or 7 new original tunes as well as putting our spin on some classic B-side type tunes from the age of Classic Rock.

 

 

 

— 2004 —

Well,well,well, here it is FEBRUARY 2006 and I am finally able and willing to start updating this site to a state where I feel it should be.

As previously predicted my job (which I love by the way) as well as the on going trials and tribulations of being a member of a "Band". Or as my late Father would say, "Combo" "How's the Combo coming along?" he would ask. These gifts I shall call them have not come without some neglect to my own personal direction in improvised music. Plans to record a 2nd solo CD have been on hold for a while and the world will have to wait to hear any new music from me, at least on CD. I will resume posting MP3's on the Music page of this site.

The big story is the "Band", Papa HooDoo Medicine Show.

Starting in February 2004 PH averaged 3 gigs a month in our first year. This due to the efforts of our Friend, Fan and Shrink, Phil Nazzaro, who went above and beyond to help this group of "diverse" musicians to keep on searching to find our "own"!

After our first 2 gigs original member "Cousin Dave" moved on to bigger things and we were also fortunate when ex-Stir Fried bassist Billy Cristiani was able to join us "yesterday" as you could say!

Early on we were lucky enough to get some studio time at the famous "Bennett" Studios in Englewood,NJ to record a Demo CD.

We also did some tracking/mixing at Music Machine Studios in Hackensack,NJ for the demo CD.

Gigs worth mentioning, Mexicali Blues, Teaneck, NJ. Played there a number of times.

Worlds Largest Tattoo Show, Meadowlands Expo Center, Secaucus, NJ. Played 2 days, opening for the Marshall Tucker Band on the second day.

Our fan base was growing and 2005 was looking to be a good year. I will add to my recollections of 2004 if and when they happen.

Also of some interest has been my slow progress on the Tabla, a gift from good friend Jim Walck given to me in 2002. I have always loved this instrument for its pure sound and incredible versatility. Since 2002 my time practicing has been sporadic at best but I must admit that the last 2 years or so I have made good progress. Technique and independence have come a long way and I find that I break down barriers when my "conscious mind" is distracted by something like watching the Simpsons. Theres also the wonderful feeling of being "One" with the instrument and finding myself easily going into a "Zone". To be honest, there have been times where I felt that this is the percussion instrument I was truly born to play.

— 2003 —

May - September, 2003. Having to get used to a 50 hour work week again did put my plans to record my second and possibly third CD's on the back burner but I did manage to spend the time finding some new avenues of exploration on the DrumKAT and the Roland HPD-15 as well. The beginning of September saw the start of a new band, The Papa Hoodoo Medicine Show, featuring Poor Bob (guitars/vocals), Doc Lane (guitars/vocals), Jay "Jbird" Roosa (keyboards/harp/vocals), Billy Cristiani (bass/vocals) and myself on Electronic Percussion. Gigs are forthcoming so check the Papa Hoodoo Medicine Show WEB SITE for gig calendar, MP3's and mailing list info.

Saturday, April 19,FRANK MESSINA & SPOKEN MOTION'S 10th ANNIVERSARY Blowout Party! We all had a fantastic time and the Bowery Poetry Club is a great venue. Plenty of special guests and lots of music, two sets of Frank and the band and the third set a jam session that last 'till the wee hours. Pictured above is Larry McDonald, myself, Joe Isgro, Elliott Levin, and Bob Sushko. Other pics and video clips of the gig can be seen on the Cool Links page of Frank's site.

Febuary, 2003, Frank Messina and Spoken Motion begin rehearsals for upcoming gigs beginning in April. Go to Frank's Site for details.

2003, I'll be recording and collaborating with Ken Rubenstein, Chaz Jones, Bob Sushko, Dan "Doc" Lane, Joe Isgro, Joe Vervoordt, John Hajeski(Busyditch), James Walck and others.

— 2002 —

December 31st, 2002, Ken Rubenstein and I had our third New Years Jam here at my studio. Ken brought along his guitar and midi converter so I was able to midi him up to some of my gear as well as an RC20 Loop pedal to make him feel more at home. We tracked a few hours of improvizing and I will be posting some of the better bits on my Music page as well as the IUMA site.

November 2002, I started recording Electronic Drums for Chaz Jones' next CD of originals, and I do believe I will be the only drummer featured on this CD.

October 8th 2002, I had a great time performing with Frank Messina and Spoken Motion at the Ristra Lounge in Hoboken, NJ. A very hip venue with lots of atmosphere, we played two sets to a very responsive audience. I not only got to groove with Joe Isgro/Bass and Bob Sushko/Guitar, and "Roll some Thunder" with percussionist Larry McDonald (Gil Scott-Heron / Soulfly) but played my Roland HPD-15 as a straight "Drum Kit" for the first time at a gig. I drove to Iowa to visit and jam with my old friend Ken Rubenstein during the week of July 4th 2002. We managed to record about 5 hours of music which I will be featuring on my page at IUMA. Between Ken's Guitar Synth/Looping gear and my Electronic Percussion we are able to generate a wide range of music, from song oriented Guitar/Drums duos to "Shakti,like" jams to Orchestral suites.

I spent the evening of June 8th 2002 performing with Spoken Motion and Jamming with members of Stir Fried at Kelly's Pub in Tappan, NY to celebrate the release of Frank Messina's new book of poetry, "Disorderly Conduct".
