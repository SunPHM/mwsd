Spring 2001, somewhere in the pacific..................

I had already shot a 18lb fish, and the boat moved to another spot.  It dropped me off about 300 yards away from where it would anchor, and I was to look for halibut in the sand.

The water was 61 degrees, about a clear 20 foot visibility.  I had just descended when I heard a bunch of noise, not really croaking, maybe their tails as they all moved in unison.  As I slowly rose to the surface, I saw a wall of fish coming towards me, not unlike a wall of sardines, except these were all BIG fish.  I dropped the gun and slowly grabbed my camera.   I took a little chance that the fish would spook, as I wound the disposable underwater camera.  The fish began to circle...............


 

First it starts with a feeling in your gut, you can feel the presence of the fish......... Sometimes you can hear them, sometimes you can feel them.

Then they begin to appear out of the blue, you freeze and take in the beauty of the moment.

As they draw closer, you can see their eyes looking at you, you scan the school looking for the big fish.  The smaller fish are all over 35 pounds, and are they closest ones.  They are now only 8 feet away, and circling......

Then they draw tighter, and the closest fish are only 3 feet away, looking at you.  The bigger fish are well over 50 pounds.

There were over 100 fish in this school, most in the 35-50 lb range, but there were some HUGE fish on the outside of the circling fish that were very impressive in size, much longer than the others.

In retrospect, I was glad that I had already filled my limit, as I am not sure that they would have come in that close, or that I would not have spooked them away with my actions, hunting them as opposed to watching them.  I would have missed this unbelieveable sight.

Marco Farrell
Spring 2001
