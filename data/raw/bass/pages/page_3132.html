Trophy Northern Pike Fishing

Alaska Land of the midnight sun.


Just the name itself carries a sense of greatness. For years, Alaska has been known for its wondrous size, its magnificent mountains, its trophy size game, and of course its salmon when you are Alaska fishing. But very little has been said about its huge northern pike. In fact Alaska fishing’s best kept secret is its large northern pike. We are not talking just 10 or 15 pound northern pike, but 25, 30, and the occasional 40 pound monster northern pike! We are talking about 45 to 55 inch northern pike! Located on a tributary that feeds the Yukon river, Midnight Sun Trophy Pike Adventures offers the northern pike fishing trip of a lifetime for those anglers interested in hooking the largest northern pike of their dreams. Midnight Sun Trophy Pike Adventures offers luxury, and comfort, while enjoying Alaska's remote wilderness on our custom-built houseboat (a new twist to the sport of Alaska fishing) when you are northern pike fishing with us. This boat is equipped with all of the modern amenities and plush trimmings. It also allows us to become mobile, moving with the northern pike as the season changes to provide you the best northern pike fishing available anywhere.

The Yukon tributary you will be fishing provides the perfect environment for growing big northern pike; cool, slow-moving water; a food source of unlimited quantity; and the time to grow old and fat for great northern pike fishing! With little competition from other anglers, these big northern pike are readily attainable when in Alaska fishing for northern pike.

Sheefish?

What's a Sheefish?

Locals call this hard-hitting, fast moving, chrome marauder, the "Tarpon of the North". Migrating in schools up river all through the summer, sheefish provide fast and furious action for the spin cast, or fly fishing enthusiast. And the taste; sheefish possess a very tasty, white flaky meat making a great meal. The largest sheefish we've caught out of the area was 45 pounds.

Plus, being centrally located between the lower Yukon and Kuskokwim rivers, a short float plane ride provides endless opportunities for Alaska's mighty king salmon, the feisty chum, or the hard-hitting silver salmon. Your trip for an extra cost can include this fly-out.
