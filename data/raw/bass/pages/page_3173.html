Delving into Dynamix
The creators of Tribes 2 have a lot more up their sleeves than the latest sequel in their popular squad-based combat series. The Adrenaline Vault traveled to the home of Dynamix to find out what's next from these renowned game developers.
 
Trophy Bass 5

Then I was shepherded to the fresh-water fishing zone, a cluster of offices full of décor and paraphernalia reminiscent of this sport. There I discovered Trophy Bass 5, the latest in the best selling fishing series of all time. Richard Rayl, principal engineer, showed it to me. In addition to the PC version, there will be one for the Macintosh, and this will be the very first fishing title for both the PlayStation2 and Microsoft's forthcoming X-Box. Each will be a special version sporting unique features, not just a port from the PC. This forthcoming release supports 3D graphics acceleration and also uses parts of the Tribes 2 engine, though not the same modules as Trophy Hunting 5. It is also the most significant upgrade in the series since Trophy Bass 3D, touting faster game play, improved 16-bit graphics, and all new single- and multiplayer tournament and career modes.

I was amazed at the dynamic lake environment, both above and below water. Furthermore, the gameplay gets you deeply involved in the authentic dynamics of bass fishing. The expert advice from Hall of Fame expert Denny Brauer has clearly aided the dynamics of the actual fishing: Whether or not you catch a fish is a function of many things, including the type of bait, the speed of the fish, the angle at which the fish is swimming, the distance of the line from the fish, the water currents and the species. While many features, such as the canned splash effects, were not yet in place, I still witnessed stellar visuals.

There are 33 species of game fish and 30 fully scaled real-world lakes available. For those who know nothing about fishing, there is a lot of guidance about what to do and a set of default gear that is perfectly serviceable. While many exhibited scorn when Dynamix first developed the Trophy Bass series, the staggering sales have given the folks here the last laugh. This title, which Dynamix admits generates little enthusiasm in Europe due to the scarcity of bass fishing there, is scheduled for release in the fourth quarter of 2001.
