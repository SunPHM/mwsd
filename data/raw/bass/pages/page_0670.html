AU OUTREACH PROGRAM OFFICE TO HOST BASSMASTER UNIVERSITY 2004

Area anglers can learn techniques the pros use to put fish in the boat.


Ray Scott, founder of BASS, Bass Anglers Sportsman Society


Mike Auten, three-time Bassmaster Classic Finalist


1998 BASS Masters Classic Champion Denny Brauer

AUBURN — Auburn University’s Outreach Program Office (OPO) is again offering the highly popular BASSMASTER University, sanctioned by the Bass Anglers Sportsman Society.

The institute will be held on the AU Campus Jan. 17-18, 2004.

“Whether you are fishing competitively or for fun, BassMaster University will offer tips that will help you become more successful,” said Rob McKinnell, program developer in the OPO.

“Quality instruction is what makes BassMaster such an incredible experience,” said McKinnell. “Many participants are amazed that they can learn so much over the course of a weekend.”

AU’s BASSMASTER University offers area anglers the opportunity to experience a classroom situation where America’s top professional bass fishermen serve as instructors.

Instructors include six top pros including the 1998 BASS Masters Classic Champion Denny Brauer. Brauer also won the 1987 B.A.S.S. Angler of the Year title and is bass fishing's all-time leading money winner, having won more than $1.5 million fishing for bass.

"It's such a privilege to get instruction from bass fishing's all-time leading money winner Denny Brauer," said McKinnell. "I can't wait to get started."

Also instructing will be B.A.S.S. Founder Ray Scott as well as the 2003 Great Outdoor Games Gold Medalist Gary Klein. Other instructors include Mike Auten, three-time Bassmaster Classic Finalist; Don Iovino, 1985 U.S. BASS Angler of the Year in both the North and South divisions and recent inductee into the International Fishing Hall of Fame; and Tony Bean, one of America's premier smallmouth anglers.

Like any college course, BASSMASTER University follows a curriculum. These top pros are excellent at fishing any technique, but when fishing is tough they fall back on a favorite technique or approach. The curriculum is designed to let each pro teach the techniques that he fishes most proficiently. As a result, participants learn from the best of the best.

Classroom instruction begins at 8 a.m. both days, and participants will attend 12 different workshop sessions during the two-day event. Saturday instructors include Brauer, Klein and Auten. Scott, Iovino and Bean will be instructing on Sunday. Activities recess at 3 p.m. both days, but participants are invited to attend a “Think Tank” question and answer session with instructors until 3:30 p.m.

And in these days of higher prices and increased tuition, enrollment for the two-day event is still just $99. Spouses and children under 16 can attend for $50 with a full-paying adult.

For more information call the OPO at 334-844-5100, or visit the website at www.auburn.edu/outreach/bass.
