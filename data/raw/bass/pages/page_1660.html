History of the Double Bass

 

                 

 

Michael Praetorius (1571-1621)- the inventor of the double bass in the 17 century.

The instrument he created had five strings, and was over 8 feet tall. His drawings of the instrument basically looked liked a very large violin with sloped shoulders and a flat back.

During the baroque period it had huge strings that were impossible to play. If not for the invention of over wound strings, the bass of today would not exist. The strings also made it possible to create a smaller bass without messing up the sound. For instance, the bass of today is only a 3/4 size.

Domenico Dragonetti (1763 - 1866) - the first great player of the bass. In fact he is one of the main reasons that the bass is still alive today. To help him was his close friend Beethoven. He had many revolutionary performances that showcased his talent and techniques. He was so well known that many composers would see him next to the concertmaster. They did this so that he could play the cello and violin parts as he saw fit.
