Galveston 12-String Bass

(also Carlo Robelli, Hense & Venson brands by same OEM factory)


Click Thumbnail below for more pics!

 

Galveston 12vers are made by a Korean OEM manufacturer with a 15-year history and approximately 100 employees, that has been manufacturing and exporting 50,000 electric guitars every year for the past 10 years. SilverStar is the house brand of that manufacturer and they also make electric and acoustic guitars and basses for many well-knowm brands (you'd recognize them if you same the body shapes.) Galveston is the OEM brand belonging to an established American wholesale distributor in the musical instrument industry based in Ohio that distributes a number of otherproduct lines. Galveston is sold by a select number of US MI retailers but also on the internet and Ebay.   The factory where Galveston is manufactured lists body woods on various models as alder, ash and other types of solid woods. They generally use rosewood fingerboards but do show occasional use of ebony; some necks show intricate inlay work.  Their work runs the full spectrum from introductory models with bolt-on necks to high-end, neck-thru models with beautifully figured laminate tops. 

The Galveston 12-string bass is roughly modeled on a model in SilverStar's own "Fusion Series" but with the extended fingerboard, batwing inlays, larger headstock and other additions. There are some Galveston 12-string's with "Marcus Martini" on the headstock, believed to be an endorser model name, but otherwise the same as the others.  Galveston 12vers have been seen in both the original darker walnut-type burst and a later lighter natural color. (Note: Since OEM manufacturer information and wholesale distribution is considered trade-only information, we have left out the specific name of each company to protect their interests.) 

Known Specs:

Solid body (solid wood; specific type unspecified.)

A one piece hard maple 34" scale  neck with 
27-fret rosewood fingerboardwith a brass nut, 
Gotoh tuners or similar.

Two-piece bridge, black hardware and a 
walnut finish.

Two soap bar type active/passive pickups.

The Galveston's list price is US $1,200.00.

 

Venson

(a prototype from the 2002 NAMM Convention)

This Venson 12-string bass is believed to be a one-off prototype. It was originally bought as a display booth demo piece at the 2002 Winter NAMM Convention and later resold on Ebay in 2003. It was made at the same OEM manufacturing facility in Korea that also makes Galveston, Carlo Robelli and other brands. Note the body similarity to the maker's own Silverstar brand model UAB120. The neck is the same as the Galveston 12, although it has different inlays. The electronic controls are like the early Galvestons with four pots but the pickups are diffeent and carry a "Venson" imprint on them. The bridge, nut and tuning machines appear to be the same.

According to the new owner Rick, who was the Ebay winner, it was "made for Venson out of a mix of Galveston and Dean Rhapsody parts. According to the seller, it's the only one. His company bought the contents of Venson's NAMM 2002 booth and he was selling off the various show guitars."

Carlo Robelli 12

 

The Carlo Robelli brand name actually has a long history to it and was purchased by the Sam Ash Music chain several years back to use as it's in-house brand for OEM produced instruments. They make affordable, quality instruments that are sold at their many retail stores, as well as through the chain's on-line catalog.

Hense

 

 

Hense is the house-brand of Martin's Musikkiste, a German musical instrument retailer that has previously sold other 12-string bass models made at the same factory that makes Galveston and the other brands on this page. Certain similarities such as the batwing inlays, electronics and hardware can be seen. The Hense seems to use the Carol Robelli body style and the newer Galveston 5-pot electronics configuration....Store owner Martin Hense decided to have the factory make some changes and issue his own "Hense" model 12ver. What makes this an important development is that at $699 Euro, it is the first of the affordable entry-level 12-string basses to be originated by a Euro-based brand. Good news for European-based players looking to get into the 12-string bass and not get hit with a lot of big shipping and import/duty fees when buying one via the US or Ebay in the States.
