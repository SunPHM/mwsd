Bass Fishing in Vermont For Large and Small Mouth Bass

Prime Bass Fishing season begins the second Saturday in May, and Bill Engelmann has his boat in the water, ready to guide you to the best Bass Fishing spots in the Northeast Kingdom.

Fishermen in the Northeast and elsewhere may consider Lake Champlain as a prime bass fishing spot in New England. But tucked in the hills of Northeastern Vermont, Lake Memphremagog and Moore reservoir abound with both largemouth and smallmouth bass. Unsullied by large fishing tournaments, some big fish have never seen the pressure of some of the more well known bass waters in Vermont.

The best time to go bass fishing is when the season opens. For several weeks thereafter, bass are frequently found in the shallows. Around August, when the shallow water has been heated by the summer sun, the big fish head for deeper water. In early autumn, they return to the shallows for awhile, until the cold weather makes them retreat to the warmer deep water.

The best time to catch large and smallmouth bass is early morning, early evening and late evening. Smallmouth bass prefer submerged rocks around islands and along the shore such as those found in Moore Reservoir.

 

Largemouth Bass are more often found in weedy areas along the edges of lake marshes, which are abundant in Lake Memphremagog.
