REAL TIME WATER FLOW DATA IN MARYLAND

Real time water flow data is available 24 hours/day from the United States Geological Survey. We have inserted links in the report below for many of the streams and rivers discussed in the fishing report. For additional stations, go to the main real time USGS water flow page .

Casselman River: As of June 17, 2002 the Long Term Median Flow (LTMF) in Grantsville, Maryland is 49.5 cfs with a flow of 56 cfs and staging at 1.19 feet. Fly options include #14 - 16 caddis, #14 light cahills, and #14 sulphurs.

Click for the latest real time stream flows on the Casselman.

North Branch of the Potomac River: As of June 17, 2002, at the Barnum gauging station the LTMF is 199 cfs with a flow of 250 cfs and staging at 3.07 feet. Trout are readily feeding off the small blue winged olive hatches. Size #20 blue winged olives, #12 March browns, and #12-14 caddis are good options.

Click for the latest real time stream flows on the North Branch of the Potomac at Barnum.

Savage River: As of June 17, 2002 in Bloomington the LTMF is 63 cfs with a flow of 84 cfs and staging at 0.97 feet. Fly options include tan and olive #14-18 caddis, #18 blue winged olives, #14 pale morning dun, and #14 sulphurs. Light cahills (#12-14) have started.

Click for the latest real time stream flows on the Savage River near Bloomington, MD.

Youghiogheny: As of June 17, 2002 the LTMF near Oakland is 110 cfs with a flow of 85 cfs and staging at 2.30 feet. The LTMF in Friendsville is 260 cfs with a flow of 231 cfs and staging at 2.65 feet. Tan and olive #14-18 caddis, #18 blue winged olives, #14 pale morning dun, and #14 sulphurs have been recommended by area guides. Terry Rinker of Accident, Maryland had a good day last week. He was fishing with worms and caught a 19.5 inch rainbow trout as well as a 19.25 inch brown trout.

Click for the latest real time stream flows on the Youghiogheny near Oakland, MD.

Gunpowder River: Caddis (#16), sulphurs (#20-22), ants (#16 and #18), and beetles (#14 and #16) are good options. As of June 17, 2002 the LTMF at Little Falls at Blue Mount is 66 cfs with a flow of 30 cfs and staging at 3.27 feet.

Brown trout have been tagged with visual implant tags just behind the left eye socket (pictured on the left - click for a larger view). If you catch a tagged brown trout please contact Central Regional Manager, Charlie Gougeon, at 410-442-2080 or via e-mail at cgougeon2@dnr.state.md.us with the following information: date and location of the catch, tag number, tag color, kept or released, and any other information you feel is important.

Click for the latest real time stream flow for Little Falls/Blue Mount Road, MD.

 

Nontidal Potomac River: As of June 17, 2002 at the Williamsport Water Treatment Plant the River is 2.5 feet, cloudy, and 77 degrees Fahrenheit. Muskie Man Mullican provided the following report. Smallmouth bass action has been consistent in the Brunswick to Point of Rocks area. The Taylors Landing to Dargan Bend stretch has been very cloudy due to recent storms, but is beginning to clear. Crankbaits have been productive for walleye, particularly in bright colors. Anglers fishing below the dams have been having good success with channel catfish. Larry Ricks caught a 5 pound 3.25 ounce channel catfish below Dam 5 using a worm. Pictured on the right is Muskie Man Mullican with a 42 inch, 21 pound muskie from Washington County.

LAKES, PONDS, AND RESERVOIRS

Deep Creek Lake (Garrett County): Fishing worms in 3 to 12 feet continued to hook bluegills. Smallmouth bass continued to bite minnows and plastics. Surface plugs have been good on calm evenings. Streamers and poppers have produced for fly fishermen. Chain pickerel continued to be caught while bass fishing. Casting Mepps spinners or fishing worms on the bottom in 6 to 8 feet of water continued to be productive for yellow perch.

Information on whitewater boating releases is available on the Deep Creek Hydro Hotline at (814) 533-8911 or online at URL: http://www.deepcreekhydro.com/

Liberty Reservoir (Carroll County): This reservoir is down 17.03 feet as of June 18, 2002. Shoreline bass fishing has been good using medium to large shiners, crayfish, crankbaits, and 10-inch power worms. Fathead minnows, Mini Mites, and maggots have been catching small crappies at Nicodemus Bridge. Larger crappies are expected to move in soon.

Little Seneca Lake (Montgomery County): Wood structure is a good place to target bass. Jigs with a plastic trailer and Texas-rigged hula grubs fished in 3 to 20 feet of water are good options. Nighttime fishing opportunities for this year are June 26, July 17, August 22, and September 18th.

Loch Raven Reservoir (Baltimore County): This reservoir is down 3.61 feet as of June 18, 2002. Fishing opportunities are holding steady. Loch Raven Fishing Center reports that white perch have been caught trolling a spinner hook with a night crawler in 12 to 15 feet of water. Chain pickerel and bass have been active throughout the impoundment. Plastic worms, pig and jigs, or stickbaits worked on points and grassbeds in 10 to 15 feet of water. Plugs are catching in the morning and evening.

The Maryland Geological Survey has a bathymetry map on their website.

Piney Run Park (Carroll County): Largemouth bass have been hitting crayfish and plastics such as the "wacky" worm and 7 to 10 inch power worms. Big yellow perch and bluegills hit well last week. Redworms and night crawlers were the key. Muskies have been caught using shiners.

Prettyboy Reservoir (Baltimore County): This reservoir is down 29.52 feet as of June 18, 2002. Surface water temperature was 76 degrees Fahrenheit last week. At 16 feet it was 68 degrees Fahrenheit. Plastic worms, crankbaits, and surface plugs fished off points and bars have been catching bass.

St. Mary's Lake (St. Mary's County): Brion Picard caught the 5 pound, 13 ounce largemouth bass pictured on the right using a white and chartreuse spinnerbait around standing timber. The bass was caught and released June 10, 2002.

WSSC Triadelphia and Rocky Gorge Reservoirs: Water temperature was in the upper 70's last weekend with 3 feet of visibility. Anglers have been catching largemouth and smallmouth bass at both reservoirs fishing rocky points, humps, and flats. Carolina rigged lizards, jigs, and spinnerbaits have been productive. Angler, Chris Peloquin took 14 year old Brian Smith bass fishing for the first time last Sunday. His picture on the left demonstrates that he is a quick learner and had a great teacher. Jigs, worms, and crankbaits have been catching crappies. Live worms or minnows, crankbaits, and jigs have caught some walleyes.
