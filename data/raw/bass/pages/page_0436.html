Bass Stalker Jigs  



The unique design of Bass Stalker Jigs is the result of over ten years of experimentation with hook styles, head shapes and skirt materials. As a tournament angler, i understand the importance of a quality bait. That's why I've chosen to make my jigs with only the finest components, like Mustar Needle Point Hooks and the best in silicone and rubber skirt materials.

The challenge was to create a jig that fell slowly while retaining bulk. If the head is too small, bottom stability is lost. If it is too light, there are troubles casting, staying in contact, and reading the bottom. Furthermore, I knew that a bait lacking in weight would severly limit the depths I could fish, and we can't have that! The solution was almost too simple, and that's the beauty of it. The only way to slow the fall and still have a functionally jig are to adjust weight and resistance in a balanced manner, and that's precisely what i did. Resistance was increased by molding a jighead with a flat bottom and a concave centre, which also necessarily reduced the weight. The result is a jig that hangs in the strike zone longer and is a cornerstone of bass fishing, especially with trophy fish. The term deals with the area around the bass in which you can present a lure and elicit a strike. This zone is always changing with water clarity and temperature, light penetration, and time of day. Lunker bass are not just big... they're old too. They tend to feed more selectively and expend less energy as they wait for an easy meal to enter the strike zone.

These Jigs and Trailers are becoming a favourite on the tournament trail.
