REVIEW
'Sushi Bar' takes a modern approach to Japanese cuisine. Here a stylish, intimate, minimalist, street-side space sets the tone. One side of the room is lined with a timber bench and a row of small tables with rectangular, aluminum spotlights above.

On the opposite side the expert Chef prepares his delicate delights before the admiring gaze of the assembled guests. The daily fish selection is on display to the right of the Chef while behind him their smaller cousins swim about in the blue aquarium. A large paper sculpture floats above the bar projecting swirling, calligraphic shadows on the mint-green wall.

One side of the space is glazed, allowing daylight to wash the space in the mornings and also offering a view of the ornate surrounding buildings. The colourful Sushi and Sashimi dishes arrive at your table as artful compositions on a simple, square, wooden platter. Needless to say the Chef takes his Sushi very seriously. A quick glance around the room confirms the credentials of the restaurant- several of the guests are Japanese.
