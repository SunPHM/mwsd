Campus Master Plan

Relocations

Art - At the Fall 2000 Board of Trustees meeting, the purchase of the Coast-to-Coast building was approved. The 12,000 square foot facility has become the new home to the Art Department. Art moved from its spaces in both Archer and Kelsey after the renovation of the facility. The purchase cost for the building was $180,000 and renovation is estimated at $350,000. The majority of the renovation of the building was due to a gracious gift from Dr. Vivien Maves '28. The Maves Art Center will also house the Digital Media lab.

With Art leaving the Archer and Kelsey buildings, additional space has opened up at both locations.


Archer Hall - Archer Hall is an old historic home. It is difficult to use it for classroom purposes because of the "household" configurations. The study committee has concluded that the building would become the new home for Agape and Student Publications. The building is in need of renovation, and to date, no cost estimate has been determined.


Kaufmann Building - The building has served as the Advancement Office for more than 27 years. It's location adjacent to the City of Greenville parking area will be very convenient to the non-traditional student (GOAL and LAMP) coming to campus. In addition, they will be able to use the Archer classroom space.


Library - The library is in need of space due to the growth of student support services housed in the library. To accommodate space needs for the library, it has been determined student support services (AEC, multicultural programs, Honors program, and the career placement office) would move to lower Armington, and the Archives would move to the Burkhardt Center.


Burkhardt Center - The Burkhardt Center would become the new home of the college Archives. In close proximity to the library, it is an excellent location for historical holdings of the college. The building was constructed with the same type of bricks that went into Hogue Hall. The GOAL and LAMP would be moved to the Kaufmann Building.


Armington Center - The plan for Armington Center would include two main changes. First, the lower level would be converted into a student center, housing the student support services, student development, and a student lounge area. The second portion of the transformation would be a two-story addition on the east side of the building in the area of the current patio. The upper level of the addition would expand our dinning area. The estimated cost of the addition and renovation to the lower level is $450,000

.


Sims Student Center - With student support services and student development occupying lower Armington, the Admissions Office would move to Sims. Admissions and Financial Aid would occupy the first and second levels. The lower level would be converted and renovated into a large classroom. Estimated cost of retrofit/renovation is $200,000.


Snyder Hall - Home of the sciences since 1976, is in need of additional lab space in the future. The best option is to replace one of the large classrooms with a lab. The large classroom would be replaced by a future classroom building.


Bass-Mollett - This home has been a cornerstone within the town for over 100 years. Bass-Mollett has been used recently for student housing, but would make a very nice "Alumni House." Everyone knows where Bass-Mollett is located, whether you were a member of the first graduating classes or in recent years, making it a good place to house Advancement, Public Relations, and the Alumni Office. It is estimated the renovation could cost more than $250,000.


Hoiles House - In 1999 the college purchased a portion of the gullies from the Hoiles family. A part of the purchase secured an option on the homestead. We have since purchase the property. The usage of the home has not been finalized.




Future Classroom Building - As classroom space is needed in the future, the block of Elm and Oak (Burkhardt Center block) has been identified. The greatest need of classrooms is for larger rooms holding more than 65 students. The new building will need to have larger lecture halls holding 100 students.
