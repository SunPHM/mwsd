Fishing News:  10-6-03

Fishing is getting back to normal after all the weather and the hurricane we have had anglers are starting catch some nice fish.  Rockfish season came in on Saturday and anglers have already been bringing in some nice fish up to 38 1/2 inches.  The crew of the PIRATE LADY wirelined around the 3rd and 4th island tubes on Sunday and caught their limit of rockfish up to 38 1/2in.  Most anglers are trolling right now with wireline or pulling some type of soft plastic lure such as storms.  The crew of DEBRA ANN fished on Sunday around the High Rise, and around the 3rd and 4th Islands of the CBBT and caught some nice rockfish, 7 gray trout, flounder, and bluefish.  Pretty work guys!! 

I didn't hear too much about flounder being caught this past week and weekend.  There were a few caught around the 3rd and 4th Island and around the Back River Reef.  With the hurricane and all I am not sure how much that affected the flounder fishing.  There has been a few gray trout showing up in the past week or so.  Most are around the CBBT and some are being caught around the HRBT also.  The spot fishing has picked up pretty good now with spot as big as 1lb. 2oz. being brought in.  The crew of the A-TEAM, Don Forman and Danny Taylor fished around the HRBT had had 5 citations on Sunday with 3 of them weighing in at 1lb. 2oz.  Pretty work guys!!  All in all fishing is starting to pick back up and hopefully we will have a good rockfish season this year.  Come on down to the shop this coming weekend and go fishing.....ChrisB

Reminders to Fisherman:

  • This year the Flounder regulations have changed, the minimum length is 17 1/2 inches and you can keep 8.  That is a pretty big jump from last year but hopefully the fish will be bigger and we won't have any problems with that. 

 

  • The other change is in Cobia for this year, you can only keep 1 cobia per person this year instead of 2 last year.
