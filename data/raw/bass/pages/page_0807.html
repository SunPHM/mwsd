Bass Fishing


 
 





The first time I went fishing was during my junior year in with my two roommates.  Although the weather was terrible, the first time my rod dipped forward, I knew I was hooked.  Ever since then, I have pursued the infamous Largemouth Bass, also known as a "Black" bass.  The Largemouth Bass(Micropterus salmoides) is probably the most popular freshwater sportfish in America.  It body is characterized by a dark greenish color with a yellowish belly.  The Largemouth also has a broad lateral line strip that runs down the side of its body.  It differs from its cousins, the smallmouth by a spiny dorsal fin nearly separated from a soft-rayed second fin as well a large mouth with an upper jaw that extends beyond the eye.

Average- sized largemouth bass usually weigh from 1.5-3 pounds.  However every once in a while, people will catch bass over 10 pounds.  The current record is 22lbs 4 ounces which was set back in 1932 in Montgomery Lake, GA.  Ever since then, people, including myself have been trying to break that record.  My personal best is a mere 5 pounds.   Recently, there was some controversy when a fisherman claimed to have the broken the record, but didn't follow the proper procedures to record his catch.  Here is a picture of the fish that he caught, you can judge it for yourself.  The fisherman claims that this fish shatters the world record.
