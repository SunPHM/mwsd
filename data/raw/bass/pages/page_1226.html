ALIVE!

The "Reason"  I  Play the Bass Guitar.
by Low-End Monster

It started for me back in 1976.  I was 15 years old I bought my very first album. It was  "KISS Destroyer" and I bought it with my own money.  It cost me $6.99 plus tax.  My parents could not believe I spent that much for a record. I listened to it over and over.  The infection had begun to spread and I wanted more.  Soon after I bought "KISS ALIVE!" for $11.99.  I put the LP on the turn table and donned my headphones like a fighter pilot dons his helmet as he prepares for a combat mission.  I knew I was going to experience something powerful.   When I played that album I began to realize that there was something happening to me.  I started to hear something within the music.  It was there, something "deeper" than the guitars , drums and vocals.  It was dark, precise, powerful and alive!  I could feel it more than I could hear it.  Then like a revelation I realized I was listening to the bass guitar.  I focused and studied Gene Simmons' method.  The more I listened the more I could sense it.  It was if Gene Simmons' bass playing spoke to me.  I knew then I was destined to be a bass player.  

Three years later I bought my first Bass Guitar.  A friend of mine knew I was looking for one and told me about this guy who was selling his.  He wanted $175.00 for it.  This was a lot of money for me at the time but I managed to  scrape up the money (I used my savings and borrowed $100.00 from my Dad.)  I went to see the bass guitar this guy had for sale.  When he opened the case my jaw dropped. It was a Gibson Grabber with an Eggplant Sparkle finish just like the one Gene Simmons had on the cover of "ALIVE!".  Needless to say I snatched it up so fast, the guy was still picking up the money off the floor as I got on the bus to go home with my prize!  From that moment on I have never looked back.  Playing a bass guitar is a pleasure that I cannot describe.  It is my source of relaxation, enjoyment and expression.  

I recently purchased KISS ALIVE! (The Kiss Remasters) and as I listen to it again 20+ years later I still can appreciate the talents of these four gentleman during the performance of this particular album.  Gene Simmons' bass playing is as I remember it, and it stirs the feelings and memories of my youth.  It is something that I will always remember.  In my opinion, Mr. Simmons' best work!  Many may argue the members of KISS are lacking with respect to their musical talents.  I for one feel a loyalty to them, especially to Gene for I have no doubt without his influence, talent and bass playing style I would not be playing my 12-string or any other bass today.  That is why I chose to recognize Gene Simmons on my website as the biggest influence of my bass playing life!

Thank you Mr. Simmons!
