Frequency of Notes on a Bass Guitar
               Nic                            

Question -

How does the pitch of a note on a bass guitar relate to the frequency as you progress down the fret board?

What I hope to accomplish by this lab is to find the relationship between the note being played and the frequency in which it oscillates.

Background Research -
A bass guitar is somewhat different from a regular guitar. A regular guitar has 6 strings, most basses have 4, however there are those that have 5 or 6 strings. The bass that will be used in this experiment has 4.


(here is a fret board on a bass guitar)

For this experiment I will use the last G string, which is the smallest in size, and highest in pitch. I am using this string because it will produce the loudest sound. From my knowledge of sound waves I know that the pitch of a sound and its frequencies are directly related. I also know that the larger, or fatter the string, the lower the pitch and as you go down the fret board, the note's pitch becomes higher. 

My prediction is that as you progress down the fret board, the frequency will get higher. I think it might get higher at a constant rate, but I am unsure. The reason I think this is because the shorter the distance of the string, the higher the frequency.


(this is the pick I used)

Materials -
 - Bass Guitar
- Microphone
- Tuner

Procedure -
1. Tune the Bass to the standard tuning. (E, A, D, G)
2. Open Logger Pro
3. Pluck the string
4. Record displayed frequency
5. Repeat 3 and 4 tell all frets are recorded

The dependant variable is the length of the string and the independent is the frequency.
