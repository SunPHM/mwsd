Jack Daley gets 'Biggest Ashdown Rig' award with Lenny Kravitz

22/07/2004

If you've seen any of the recent Lenny Kravitz shows, you know that the man likes a BIG backline. For bass player Jack Daley, there are no fewer than twelve Ashdown 8 x 10 and four 2 x 15 cabinets with custom gold grilles, split into two '8 cabinet' rigs on either side of the stage. Jack's personal monitoring is supplied by a pair of Ashdown Labs 2 x 10 + horn dedicated bass monitors.

There are three custom gold Ashdown ABM900 amp heads on stage, but power for the cabs and monitors is actually supplied by a rack system off-stage comprising of an Ashdown RPM-1 bass preamp and three Ashdown PM1000 bass power amplifiers, controlled throughout the show by tech Alex Alvarez.

For Jack, the choice of Ashdown was all about the sound. "The Ashdown system perfectly complements the sound of my Jazz Bass. The tone is fat and warm with a massive, clearly articulated bottom end. The Ashdown Labs monitors are designed specifically for bass, and this helps to give a great sound on stage."

Eager to ensure the complete satisfaction of the company's biggest single customer to date, Ashdown main-man Mark Gooday travelled to Paris to attend final rehearsals before the European leg of the tour. "The show is fantastic" enthuses Mark, "and the custom-gold Ashdown rigs look and sound amazing."

In addition to his live work with Lenny Kravitz, Jack has worked with a wide variety of artists including Joss Stone, Natalie Merchant, Shaun Colvin, Everlast and Amanda Marshall.
