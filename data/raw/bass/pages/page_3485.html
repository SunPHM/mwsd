Chef Of The Month
NET would like to thank Patricia Unterman, owner and chef of the Hayes Street Grill in San Francisco. Patty was the first chef to officially "Take a Pass on Chilean Sea Bass" by signing the pledge not to serve it until illegal fishing and by-catch issues are resolved.





She also provided invaluable assistance to the cause by letting other chefs in the Bay Area know about the problems associated with Chilean Sea Bass and asking them to stop selling it. Thanks to Patty's help we now have 60 chefs in San Francisco, and nearly 300 nationwide, who have agreed to Take A Pass On Chilean Sea Bass; the pledges continue to pour in for all around the nation.

Patricia Unterman
"My philosophy about ocean conservation is to have a new consciousness about it. I want to know where and how every single item on my menu is caught. I ask my fish purveyor and if he doesn't have answers, he goes back to the fisherman or the co-op or his supplier. If I get the wrong answer, I won't buy, so this conservation consciousness kind of flows down the line. Since 67% of all seafood in the United States is sold in restaurants, informed buying by chefs could really have an effect. At Hayes Street we've always been particular about our sources, especially when it comes to freshness and handling, but not always about the fragility of certain species. There are items we used to feature that were very popular that we don't anymore. One of them was Chilean Sea Bass.
