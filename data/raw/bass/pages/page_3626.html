Roy Goodman, British Painter (far be it from me to comment on his work, on view at this site).  Born in London in 1947, Roy started drawing and painting as a young child and never stopped. He studied art at school, but apart from a short dalliance with the Hornsey College of Art had little formal training. He taught himself through a process of trial and error and of observation. Influenced by the usual artists and especially moved by the Fauves and by Kadinsky and Kokoshka. Trained as a scientist and currently practising as a research chemist, Roy would say that he is the archetypal amateur painter and has always thought that painting is too good to leave until you retire.  Look, he has some new paintings.
