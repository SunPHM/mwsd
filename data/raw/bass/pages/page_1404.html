Tom Petersson's "First" Royale

by Low-End Monster

The first  Royale ever built by Chandler was for Tom Petersson and  is quite different than the current production model.  It was made entirely by hand! This was the 12-String Bass Tom used during the famous 4-Night "Live @ The Metro" concerts which was the basis for Cheap Trick's second live offering "Music For Hangovers".   It has a few cosmetic differences.  The current production "Royale"   has a more rounded headstock than the original for ease of sanding during construction (whereas Tom's "Original" has a sharper, more rectangular shape) with a different style of white pearl headstock  accents.   The pickups and bridge are positioned higher on the current production model as well. (compare the distance from the bridge to the bottom of the Royale in the two photos on this page)  Also the "original" pickups have a Tortoise Shell cover with a white binding, whereas the current model has black covers. The pickguard on Tom's "Original" Royale was made exclusively for him and is not available for duplication at his request, making this first production  Royale "one-of-a-kind". 

Tom's "Original" has a thick graphite slab underneath the rosewood fingerboard and a Dual Truss Rod system to withstand the tension of a 12-String Bass tuned to concert pitch (440).  The current design has two Graphite Rods installed (on either side of a Dual Truss Rod/ which makes for easier adjustments)  with the correct tensile strength to resist the tension of the 12-String Bass tuned to concert pitch. This keeps the neck from bowing or twisting.  This improvement was the result of the continuing collaboration between Tom Petersson and Chandler as they strive to build the "Finest 12-String Bass Ever Made".  As the inventor of the 12-String Bass Guitar, Tom identified  areas on which to improve the "Royale.  Along with Chandler,  they re-engineered the original design producing a 12-String Bass to meet Tom Petersson' s high standards.   Tom no longer tours with his "original" Royale due to the fact that Chandler feels that it no longer represents the quality of the current design.  Tom now tours with a couple of the current model Royales (Black & an AWESOME "Blue Pearl Top" shown below) and has ordered two more. One will be White and the other will be Green.
