Charters

Miss Andrea's Cape May charter season starts in the middle of April and runs into December. After the weather turns cold, we take the boat down to Morehead City, NC and fish for giant bluefin tuna off the coast of North Carolina. 

A number of our favorite types of charters are listed below with brief descriptions. Please feel free to call or email us if you are interested in booking a trip or have any questions about one of our charters.

We provide our charters with all their fishing gear and bait. If you prefer to bring your own gear, please feel free to do so. We provide quality rods, reels and tackle. We also keep a large supply of ice on board to keep your catch fresh until we arrive back at the dock. 

We recommend that our charters wear boat shoes, sneakers or other sturdy soft-soled footwear. Food, beverages, sunscreen, sunglasses and cameras are also recommended. While beer is allowed on board, we do not recommend bringing hard alcohol on board. 

Departure times vary based a number of conditions such as tide and weather. Please consult the Miss Andrea crew shortly before your charter dates to confirm your time of departure.  

Spring Stripers

The Miss Andrea begins fishing for spring stripers in mid to late April via chunking with bunker, anchoring up with fresh clams or eeling in the rips depending upon weather and water conditions. Chunking trips produce large striped bass while eeling trips produce larger quantities of smaller fish. We fish for spring stripers up until the middle of May. Drum fish             

Drum fishing in 2003 was the best we have seen in many years. We had many trips where we caught up to 3 drumfish weighing near 100 pounds and many more trips where we have had up to 21 drumfish in an hour and a half weighing up to 60 pounds with a few 25-35 lbs. stripers mixed in. We look forward to repeating our success in the upcoming season. Please click on the above link for more information.

Sea bass

Sea bass trips are fun for all. Trips are usually 8 hours long and can be fished inshore, over wrecks or out in the Old Grounds. The Miss Andrea charters always seem to be pleased with the fast action, and large quantity of quality sea bass that we catch on these enjoyable trips. Flounder

Fluke trips generally consist of fishing in the Delaware Bay or fishing off the Old Grounds near the Delaware coast. These trips produce large flounder and sea bass from early July to early September. Please click on the above link for more information.

 

 

 

Mako / Shark

Shark trips can either be 12 hour charters or overnight excursions. The shorter 12 hour charter focuses on catching mako, thresher and blue Sharks. On the longer overnight trips we concentrate on catching mako between 200 lbs. and  700 lbs., and monster blue sharks weighing in at 200 lbs. or more. This year we will be setting aside a few dates for catching giant makos and tagging large blue sharks. Please click on the above link for more information.

 

Tuna

We have two types of tuna charters, 12 hour trips and our popular overnight trips. Our tuna season usually starts at the end of June and goes through mid-October. From June until early August we catch bluefin tuna and from August until mid-October we catch yellowfin tuna. Please click on the above link for more information.

Fall stripers

Our fall striper charters begin in mid-October and run through mid-December. We catch most of our fall stripers in the Cape May Rips and have many good trips up the coasts of New Jersey and Delaware. Please click on the above link for more information.

Giant Bluefin Tuna

In the winter months we head for Morehead City, N.C. to catch giant bluefin tuna. Several of the fish we caught weighed in at 500 lbs.  After the giants take their leave, there are always large sea bass to be caught soon there after and in late February yellowfin tuna, wahoo, and king mackerel take hold strong.
