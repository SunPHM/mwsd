The Shawm 

 The shawm is a double reed instrument with a conical bore. Unlike the crumhorn, the reed of the shawm is placed directly into the player's mouth. They were (and still are) very loud instruments, with a brilliant strident tone and were  usually played outside.  The present day oboe developed from the shawm. Our Soprano Shawm  is made by John Hanchet and our Altos and Tenor are  made by Eric Moulder of Leek.

The Curtal

The Curtal is another double reed instrument and is the ancestor of the present day bassoon. Our Curtal was made locally in Leek by Eric Moulder.

                 


Racket 
Our racket was made by Greg Lewin and is a bass instrument with a double reed. Instead of the wooden pipe being straight like a shawm it is bent up and down several times inside a chamber. If all the pipe were added together it would be about 8 feet long, but it is all contained within an instrument that is about a foot high. It can play even lower sounds than the present day bassoon.

The Bagpipes

The history of the Bagpipes   goes back to the Middle Ages and even earlier. The bagpipes played by the group have a single drone and a bag made of leather. Inside the bag is a double reed. Our bagpipes are made by Jonathan Swayne.
