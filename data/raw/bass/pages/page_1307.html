Here are a couple pics of my bass after rough cutting the body profile and a lot of the neck shaping done. On the left it's hanging in one of my home-made guitar hangers made from some scrap pieces of plywood. I still need to thin the upper horn and do some more shaping and smoothing of the body before moving to rounding over the edges. On the right you can see the neck-to-body junction, it also needs a bit more shaping and thinning out. You can also see there some of my bad filler in the black lines inside the long horn. That's why I've decided to finish the edges, neck and back of the headstock black, then fade that to a translucent blue for the top of the body, headstock, and back of the body. If I can figure out how to do it it should look really good...a big "IF" at this point!
 
 
 
 






 
 
 

Here's a couple closeups of the back of the headstock and another angle on the neck/body junction. On the back of the headstock I'm trying to carve a rounded volute under the nut but still have a little more work to do on it to make it symmetrical. Also, you can see where the router slipped into the nut slot and gouged the edge of the neck. I've worked that down a little bit but still have more smoothing to do. Just one of many "flubs" that, so far, I've been able to either hide or fix. On the other picture again you can see how fat the upper horn is still. I still have to take off as much as 3/8" from the inside of it. You can also see the hump on the back of the neck that needs to be smoothed out.
 



A couple more views of the neck. As you can see in the one on the left, the laminates didn't come out perfectly centered...that's one reason I've decided to finish it in black. On the right you can see the volute where the neck meets the headstock. It still looks a bit too lumpy to me, a bit more carving to do as you can better see in the picture below..

 






Here's the body after trimming the horns a bit thinner and rounding over the edges. I still have to do the roundover by hand on the inside of the horns close to the body, but that won't be too difficult. Next, on to routing the pickup and control cavities. I've just about finished shaping the back of the neck where it meets the body. There's just a little more to do rounding over the neck inside the horn to make it easier to reach across the wide fretboard. It's starting to look good now, I kind of hate to have to cut into the top and mess up that nice curly maple, but it does need pickups. Speaking of which, I've received my pickups from Carvin Guitars and have made my routing template for them as well as the control cavities. I'll have pictures of them on the next page.
