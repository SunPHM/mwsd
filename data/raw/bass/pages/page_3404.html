Morro Bay
The first signs of life appeared in the local salmon fishing when the commercial season opened on May 1.

"Two sport boats brought in big salmon on May 1," said Tammy Schuyler at Portside Marine. "Bill Hardesty landed a 20 pound, 3 ounce chinook while trolling purple haze hoochies at 60 feet deep off Diablo Canyon. Bob Hatcher also nailed a 24.7 lb. king while trolling in 300 feet of water off Diablo Canyon."

Commercial salmon boats reported good catches the same day. "One guy brought in 18 fish weighing an average of 18 pounds each," reported Schuyler.

"Another fisherman came back with 16 quality salmon."

Unfortunately, the storm moved in and made the seas rough from May 2 through May 4.

No boats went fishing for salmon or sanddabs out of Virg's Landing in Morro Bay on May 3 or 4, due to rough weather and tough fishing. However, the boats are expected to go for salmon soon, now that more chinooks are showing in the area.

Monterey/Moss Landing
Monterey Bay is producing big king salmon weighing 15 to 35 pounds for anglers fishing at a mixture of depths. However, scores are down, with an average one fish per rod or less for private and party boat anglers.

"Our fish counts are a little low, but this year we have seen a lot of big fish caught, mainly at the Soquel Hole," reported Roger Diaz at The Tackle Box. "Since the numbers of salmon are not as plentiful, I recommend trolling to cover more water. Also, mark areas where you catch fish and circle around to fish the same hole when you hook one."

Anglers fishing at the mouth of the Salinas River and Zmudowski Beach are catching quality surfperch and the occasional striped bass. "The perch are hitting motor oil plastic grubs with orange tails. For the bigger perch, try using a 2 inch grub rather than a 1 inch one," explained Diez.

Captain Norm Chapin of Hook'Em Up Sportfishing is taking one fish per rod while fishing at The Slide in 180-200 feet of water. "On May 4, the water had a wicked lump to it and my passengers came down with 'ocean allergies', but that wasn't until we had two fish in the box," Chapin. "We lost three other fish before getting back to the harbor at 8:30 am. Our fish weighed 14 lbs. and 17 lbs."

On a previous run on May 1, the two anglers trolling chartreuse double glow hoochies in Monterey Bay landed three fish weighing 14, 16 and 18 pounds, according to Chapin.

The Star of Monterey out of Chris' Fishing Trips reported decent fishing on the last salmon trip. "The eight people mooched anchovies between 60 and 80 feet deep off Moss Landing to nail 7 fish to 26 pounds," stated Todd Arcoleo. "Although we are not getting the numbers, the fish we are catching are big ones.

Santa Cruz
The fishing out of Santa Cruz remains tough. Some private boats had a fish per rod average or better. Some continue to come home with no fish. We are looking forward to some North/West wind the water is clearing up on us. We have had a lot of South winds we need the North/West for upwelling and desolve oxygen into the water. It is still early and we have a good chance at some great Salmon fishing. Fish can show up at any time and come out of the canyon. We have seen salmon come out of the canyon when we did not expect it before. In the mean time keep an eye out for Halibut we are working on getting live bait at Shamrock Charters soon.

Half Moon Bay
Salmon fishing enthusiasts experienced mixed results over the May 3-4 weekend, with one day yielding limits and the next one fish per rod or less.

"This weekend we fished about 8 to 10 miles below the Islands and caught fish trolling anchovies between 20-40 feet," reported Captain William "Smitty" Smith of Riptide Sportfishing. "On Saturday, weather conditions were perfect. The sea wasn't rough and we started hooking salmon first thing in the morning. By the end of the day, we had caught 11 limits up to 21 pounds."

"The bite was still good on the following day, but we were unable to put our limits in the box," said Smith. "We trolled all day hoping to get more fish in the boat, but we came up short with 13 fish for 8 people. The big fish of the day weighed 18 pounds."

Anglers fishing out of Huck Finn Sportfishing experienced consistent chinook action on Saturday, but struggled the following day fishing 6-8 miles below the main Farallon Island, according to Bill Beckett.

"The bite was best on Saturday," said Beckett. "The Queen of Hearts checked in with 19 limits, while New Captain Pete returned with 25 fish for 16 passengers. Ankeney Street came back with 19 salmon to 25 pounds for 16 anglers."

"On Sunday, May 4, fishing took a nose dive, but we still had fish in the box," he explained. The Queen of Hearts took 10 fish for 19 people, while the New Captain Pete brought in 13 fish for 22 anglers. Ankeney Street was the top boat with 26 salmon for 15 people.

San Francisco
Live bait drifters landed limits and near-limits of stripers, along with good numbers of halibut, in San Francisco Bay on the weekend of May 3-4.

The Bass Tub returned to the wharf with 16 stripers and 6 halibut for 12 anglers on Sunday, May 4, according to Captain Erik Anfinson. The bass and halibut are showing at a variety of spots, including Paradise Cay, the Berkeley Flats, the Alameda Rockwall, Bakers Beach and Crissy Field.

The salmon fishing is also going strong, with "terrific" action reported by Jacky Douglas, captain of the Wacky Jacky. Most of the boats have been running southeast of the Farallon Islands, about 14 to 17 miles outside the Golden Gate. Limits and near-limits of quality chinooks have been the rule when the weather allows the boats to get out.

San Rafael
The variable weather has slowed down fishing pressure in San Francisco Bay, but anglers are nailing good numbers of halibut and stripers on the favorable tides.

"Anglers drifting live bait are nailing halibut averaging 8 to 10 pounds in the Berkeley Flats and in Paradise Cay," said Art Donati at Loch Lomond Live Bait and Tackle. "Some bank fishermen are also catching a few halibut and stripers outside of the harbor jetty."

The water is clearing from recent storms and trolling for stripers is a good prospect. "Troll at the top of the tide with white and chartreuse wormtail jigs," said Donati. "The best area is off the Brickyards and San Quentin."

Live bait drifters are also bagging striped bass at the Brothers and Whaling Station. The bass caught on live bait are generally larger than those caught trolling. The live bait bass range from 5 to 20 pounds, while the troll-caught fish go from 15 to 25 inches, including both shakers and keepers.

Berkeley
Halibut and striped bass fishing has been very consistent in San Francisco Bay. For example, a Fish Sniffer trip on the Goldeneye 2000 with Captain Quang Vo on Wednesday, May 7 produced 38 fish, including 27 halibut and 11 stripers, for 27 anglers. Dan Bacher of the Fish Sniffer landed the largest halibut, a 30 pounder. Pat Athey of Ripon won the Lamiglas rod for the biggest halibut caught by the participants, while Roosevelt Washington of Richmond won the Ambasseduer Abu Garcia Reel for the heftiest striper. The halibut ranged from 6 to 30 pounds, while the stripers ranged from 3 to 12 pounds.

Tony Ngyuen of Granite Bay took his limit of halibut, as did Craig Patterson of Rio Linda. However, the hottest rod on the boat was Mike Prettyman, who works at Fisherman's Warehouse in Sacramento. Prettyman landed both his limits of striped bass and halibut.

The anglers drifted live bait off Angel Island and in Paradise Cay. Anglers also released many shaker stripers. The weather was nice, with a mixture of overcast and sunny skies. Other party boats and private boats fishing the same area also did well.

The salmon bite was wide open this week for anglers fishing on charter boats out of Berkeley Marina; the boats fishing on Thursday landed early limits of chinooks. Limits of bright kings averaging 10 to 15 pounds have been the rule as long as the weather cooperates.

"Salmon fishing has been fantastic this week," reported Chris Nelson of the Berkeley Marina Sports Center. "Most of the boats have been running south east of the Farallons about 14 to 17 miles outside the Golden Gate. They're trolling anchovies from 20 to 60 pulls deep."

Saturday's catch was very impressive with the New Easy Rider reporting full limits to 20 pounds for 13 anglers. The El Dorado also scored salmon limits with 46 fish to 25 pounds for 23 anglers. The New El Dorado fished a live bait charter inside the bay and checked in with 17 halibut and 1 striped bass for 30 anglers. The New Golden Eye also fished live bait inside the bay for a total of 6 halibut and 1 striped bass for 5 anglers.

Emeryville
King salmon action has been red hot for anglers fishing out of Emeryville Sportfishing this week. It's been limits or close to it on every trip and the fish are quality kings running 8 to 20 pounds.

"The salmon bite really broke loose this week," reported Craig Stone of Emeryville Sportfishing. "The boats have been running about 18 miles southwest of the Golden Gate and taking near limits to limits of fish.

Inside the bay, the halibut and striper action has been good for 1 to 2 fish a rod on the Berkeley Flats."

On Saturday, May 3, the Rapid Transit bagged full limits of salmon to 23 pounds for 15 anglers. The New Salmon Queen also brought in limits to 23 pounds for 16 anglers. The Dandy found limits as well with 16 fish to 18 pounds for 8 anglers. The New Seeker was the only boat to mooch during the day and they managed 35 fish to 19 pounds for 22 anglers.

On Friday, May 2, the Captain Hook ran a live bait trip inside San Francisco Bay and they checked in with 12 halibut to 12 pounds and 8 striped bass to 10 pounds for 12 passengers. They most of the day drifting live anchovies on the Berkeley Flats.

Richmond
Anglers fishing out of Richmond are still catching over one fish per rod while targeting halibut at the Berkeley Flats. The flatfish average 10-12 pounds each.

"We trolled for halibut in the Berkeley Flats and the south bay to land 7 halibut for five people on our last trip," said Captain John Paxson of Dreamcatcher Sportfishing. "We first picked up 4 fish in the south bay. After the bite slowed down there, we headed back to the Berkeley Flats and landed our 3 other fish. The big fish of the day weighed 12 pounds."

Paxson also ran a commercial trip in the flats the following day that yielded five halibut. "I heard there were a few boats nailing lots of striped bass at Paradise Cay," said Paxson. "The best action was on the outgoing tide."

"I managed to get a break from fishing in order to deal with other work over the May 3-4 weekend, but I did get out mid week for a commercial trip with a longtime friend, Mike Farmer," reported Captain John Badger of Barbarian Sportfishing. "We were out on the water for about five hours in the afternoon and put 15 halibut in the box. The biggest fish for the day weighed 22.5 pounds."

Point San Pablo
Live bait trips running out of Point San Pablo found decent action on a mix of halibut and striped bass last week. Over the weekend, striper action heated up with one boat reporting limits of fish at Paradise Cay.

"We tried running a 7 gill trip on Saturday, but couldn't find any willing fish," reported Frank Miller of Fury Sportfishing. "We decided to make a quick run over to Paradise Cay at the end of the trip and caught a handful of stripers. On Sunday, we ran straight to Paradise and caught full limits of stripers and 3 halibut to 12 pounds for 5 anglers."

According to Miller, the fish were all taken on live anchovies drifted 20 to 30 feet deep on the outgoing tide. "Most of the stripers are schoolie-sized fish from small keepers up to 7 pounds," he added.

"We've been taken close to a fish per rod on our live bait trips this week," reported Captain Chad Aho of New Keesa Sportfishing. "On Sunday, May 4 we landed 6 striped bass and 4 halibut to 8 pounds for 11 people. We had lots of action throughout the day and also caught and released a bunch of undersized stripers."

Aho said they concentrated on halibut at the Berkeley Flats the previous weekend. "We caught 6 halibut for 6 anglers on Saturday and 8 halibut and a striper for 13 people on Sunday," he said. Nine-year-old Ronald Fujita from El Sobrante landed the big fish of the week, a 30 pound halibut. Michael Fujita nailed a 13 pound halibut and 14 year old Nicki Katz caught an 11 pound halibut."

Bodega Bay
Bodega Bay salmon season started off with a bang last month and the action has held steady up to press time.

"We're going to be blown out for the next couple days, but we made it out over the weekend and the salmon bite was excellent," reported Sophie Powers out of Bodega Bay Sportfishing. "We had to deal with a fair amount of wind, but the fish are close to home so we didn't have to run very far."

According to Powers, the salmon have been running 8 to 12 pounds on average and the top method has been trolling anchovies at 25 to 60 pulls deep. "The New Sea Angler ran just south of Point Reyes on Saturday, May 3 and they landed 22 salmon to 16 pounds for 18 anglers," said Powers. "It was pretty rough on Sunday, but they found a big school of fish off Carmet Point and they limited out the boat with a total of 52 fish to 18 pounds for 26 anglers."

Fort Bragg
Salmon action has been on a steady pace off the Fort Bragg Coast this week. Party boats are checking in with 1 to 2 fish a rod on quality king salmon averaging 15 to 18 pounds.

"Ocean conditions have been fantastic this weekend and we've got a great salmon bite happening straight out of the harbor," reported Rick Thornton of Anchor Charters. "The Lady Irma ran morning and afternoon trips yesterday and brought in full limits to 30 pounds for 6 anglers in the afternoon and 12 fish for 8 people in the morning."

According to Thornton, the salmon are hitting trolled anchovies in 25 to 70 fathoms of water. "These are really nice fish, most in the upper teens and good numbers of fish in the 20 to 30 pound class," he added. "We also landed a 96 pound blue shark while trolling for salmon this week."

"Salmon fishing has been steady around a fish a rod or better this week," confirmed Ashley Diaz at the Noyo Fishing Center in Fort Bragg. "We weren't able to make it up to Punta Gorda for rockfish this week, but it looks like conditions should be good for our next trip scheduled on May 9."

According to Diaz, the Rumblefish brought in 18 salmon for 10 anglers on Thursday, May 1. Friday's trip produced 15 fish for 19 anglers. Saturday morning, they nailed 7 salmon for 7 anglers and the afternoon trip produced 9 salmon for 13 anglers.
