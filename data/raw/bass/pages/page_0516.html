Westone Rail Bass

Westone produced this bass line in the mid-80's. Similar in appearance to a Steinberger headless bass, it is a four-string electric bass guitar of headless design. The Rail Bass is distinguished by the missing central body, replaced with a pair of pipes. It features a rail mechanism that allows you to slide and lock the pickup along the range of the rails. It has a long scale neck. It was produced in three finishes: white, red, and black. The original owner of this Rail purchased it new for $425 in 1986, and I bought this particular bass (#49269) from him in in December 2000. It is currently strung with Rotosound strings.
Because of its size, it is easy to transport and easy on the shoulder when you are playing it.

From the Bottom Line bassplayer list archives comes this quote:
To my knowledge the [Dan Armstrong] Londoner is the first of only three production basses to have this feature, the others being the mostly unloved-and-unmissed Gibson Grabber and the el-cheapo Westone "The Rail."
