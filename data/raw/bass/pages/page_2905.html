BayBound Fishing Charters - Annapolis, MD

Call 301-908-1181 to book a fishing trip

BayBound Sportfishing

Fishing out of the scenic South River in Annapolis, Maryland. Come out with us on an unforgettable trip on the Chesapeake Bay to fish for rockfish, bluefish, croaker, flounder, spot, weakfish, spanish mackerel, and more. We can also make special arrangements to do an inshore trip out of Ocean City, Maryland, for flounder, rockfish, bluefish, tautog, shark, dolphin, and more.

The Boat

Light tackle and trolling aboard the BayBound for up to 4 anglers*. The BayBound is a 21' Seapro center console fishing machine. She cruises at 28 knots with a top-end of 45 knots. Her speed puts us on the fishing grounds fast so no time is wasted. The BayBound is equipped with the latest electronics, including fishfinder, GPS, and VHF radio. The boat was built in 1996 and is equipped with a large T-top to provide shade on those sunny days. There is a cooler for all of your beverages and food and ample dry storage compartments. In addition, there is plenty of seating in the bow area or the swivel chairs behind the helm. It is also equipped with all of the necessary safety equipment required by the U.S. Coast Guard.

Since the boat is trailerable we can also go to where the fish are biting. This would involve varying additional travel time on your part. This can be discussed with the Captain prior to your trip.

* If you have a party larger than 4 anglers let us know. We also provide fishing for larger parties aboard the Julie Anne (46' Bay Built licensed for up to 19 passengers). Call us for additional details.

The Captain

Captain James "Jim" Messina has been fishing since 1972. He grew up fishing for trout, pan fish, walleye, and more in Western Pennsylvania. He moved to Maryland to attend the University of Maryland in 1987. Right away he fell in love with the Chesapeake Bay and Atlantic Ocean and began fishing them. He learned fast by fishing with long-time charter captains and is ready to show you the best fishing around. Captain Jim also spent several years charter fishing offshore out of Ocean City, Maryland, and enjoys this type of fishing on his days off. Captain Jim is a U.S. Coast Guard licensed Captain as an operator of un-inspected passenger vessel (six pack) and a Maryland Resident Licensed Guide.

The Fishing

There are many different species of fish to go after on the Chesapeake Bay. The most popular include rockfish (striped bass), bluefish, hardheads (croaker), and weakfish (grey trout). Let's not forget about the flounder and spot that can be found in good numbers and the beautiful spanish mackerel. The seasons vary yearly by regulation but the following list is a good guide:

Late April to May: Trophy Rockfish Season (trolling). It is not uncommon to catch fish in excess of 40 inches or more.

Late May to September: Rockfish Season (chumming or trolling).

October to Late November: Rockfish Season (trolling). Typically we get a second chance at the trophy fish on their migration South along the coast. They typically head into the Bay to feed before wintering off of North Carolina.

May to August: Bluefish (chumming or trolling).

May-August: Bottom fishing season. You can expect a mixed bag of rockfish, bluefish, croaker, spot, flounder, and trout.

July-August: Spanish Mackerel (fast-trolling). These are the best times to catch Spanish mackerel in the Bay.

The Experience

When fishing on the BayBound we encourage angler participation. On many boats you can only reel in fish and not help out with anything. Well, things are different on the BayBound. Sure, we can do it all and let you fight the fish, or you can help. We encourage anglers to help set the lines out, fight the fish of course, net the fish, and more. We'll provide guidance and oversight as necessary. We also encourage people who have never fished the Bay, especially kids, to come out with us and have fun. We will teach you what you need to know to catch the big ones.

What's Included

The boat, fuel, tackle, all licenses, bait, and ice for your catch is provided. We also offer fish cleaning and bagging for a modest price. Our tackle includes all of the latest tackle by Penn, Okuma, Shimano, Pinnacle, and more. We will match the tackle to the species we are after, whether it's trolling for trophies or bottom fishing for spot. If you like the tackle you can also purchase it (new or used).

What To Bring

You should bring a warm jacket (hat and gloves are recommended for April/May trips) because it is usually cooler on the Bay than at the dock and because there is minimal area to block the wind on the boat. You should also bring food, beverages (cans only, beer is allowed but no hard alcohol is permitted), sunscreen, a hat with a brim, sunglasses, a towel if you plan to lay out, and a camera. We will be fishing around some very beautiful areas of the Bay and will make every effort to get you good photographic opportunities. You may also want to consider Dramamine or other motion sickness medication just in case.
