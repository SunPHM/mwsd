DC Olson 4 string bass


Click here for a larger picture.

Builder: DC Olson
Model: Reverse Body Style (a very special 2003 NAMM show instrument).
Serial Number: DCBG001
Gigbag: original Levy's Gigbag, dark grey.

Dimensions
Weight: approx. 9 pounds.
Scale Length: 34 inches.
Neck Shape: flattened oval.
Width at Nut: 1 1/2 inches.
String Width at Bridge: 2 1/4 inches.
Overall Length: 46 1/4 inches.
Widest: 13 3/4 inches.

Construction
Neck: Bolt on, 1-piece maple (flatsawn), tilt-back headstock w/ matching blonde facing, 5 bolt attachment to body.
Fingerboard: highly figured flame maple (lacquered), 21 frets. beautifully rolled edges for an already broken-in, vintage feel, small abalone side dots.
Body: Ash (grain visible under blonde finish).
Finish: semi-transparent butterscotch blonde.

Hardware
Nut: hand-cut brass.
Bridge: 2-way adjustable (height, intonation), chrome.
Tuners: Hipshot ultralight (satin).
Strap buttons: locking type, chrome.
Outputs: 1/4" mono.

Electronics
Pickups: 2 Villex pickups.
Controls: 2 volume, bass, mid, treble (all passive).
Preamp: none, Villex passive system built into the pickups themselves.

Condition
Fret wear: none.
Superficial wear: some very light fingernail scuffing on pickguard.
Deep wear: none.
Hardware wear: none.
Pickups wear: none.
Overall Condition: near mint (this is a new demo instrument, never sold at retail, though it was displayed at the 2003 NAMM show as an exhibition bass, and was also used to record two albums), sent directly to BunnyBass by DC Olson.

Comments:

     One of our favorite aspects of running the BunnyBass site is that we have the opportunity to meet a lot of very talented custom builders, some of them not widely known. And once in a while we are fortunate enough (maybe by pure coincidence) to meet someone from out of the blue who just happens to be doing something totally unique and, well, 'special'. This is what happened to us just recently when we were at the 2003 NAMM show - out of the thousands of instruments we saw there (many of them very nice of course), this particular bass is one of a very VERY small handful that jumped out as being just a little bit more special than all the other beautiful show basses.
     It almost didn't happen too. Jon actually walked past DC Olson's booth a couple of times without even picking up any of his bass guitars because he thought they looked 'a little too weird'. Then for some reason, perhaps on his third pass, he stopped for a moment and looked at them a bit closer - and saw immediately how beautifully made they are. Then he picked this very bass up and felt the neck and suddenly he was in love with this bass and it looked so beautiful in his eyes (isn't this how true love always works?). We're sure some people will immediately dismiss this bass based on its admittedly slightly idiosyncratic styling. It definitely has a strong 'retro' vibe, with an overall appearance that recalls the great, vintage instruments of the 50's and 60's. We think this bass is nothing but super-cool, and what a great stage bass - a totally unique personality, high style, and a most striking combination of sexy curves together with a slightly quirky playfulness (imagine Rosanna Arquette in a room surrounded by boring, plastic Pamela Anderson clones...).
     This bass also features one of the most playable necks we've ever felt. Though this bass doesn't look like a Fender wannabe, DC Olson (a longtime touring bassist himself) knows what he likes, and as soon as you pick this bass up you're immediately reminded of the slender, perfect form of an early 60's Jazz neck that's been played for 40 years. Butter! A perfect blend of early-Fender and handmade custom bass feels. And every aspect of this bass is beautifully executed. Unlike many other so-called handmade Fender-ish boutique instruments that aren't as much built as they are assembled, every part of this lovely instrument exhibits the great love and care that DC Olson puts into building his basses. Please check out the photographic details below, we hope you are able to get a little bit of a feel for the quality of materials and construction, it really is a beautiful instrument in person. And of course, what good would a beautiful instrument be if it didn't also sound great? The sound is full, fat, and warm, with a nice growl coming from the beautifully resonant woods as well as the innovative Villex pickups. A totally passive system, the pickups on this bass feature an integrated 3-band EQ (yes, a passive 3-band EQ!) that doesn't require batteries, a circuit board, capacitors, or any of that other stuff you usually find in control cavities. Open up the spacious control cavity and find... almost nothing, just pots. I suppose you can add whatever you'd like in there, since the control cavity is quite roomy, but you should really try the Villex system first, it matches extremely well with the acoustic properties of this bass and we think anybody who's into good bass tone will be extremely impressed with the sound of this bass.
     DC Olson handcrafts only a very limited number of these instruments every year, and this butterscotch blonde one definitely has that intangible "it factor". If you are looking for the great feel and sound of a perfect vintage Fender Jazz bass (but don't feel the need to have a bass that looks exaclty like every one else's), please drop us a line.  ~bunnies.
