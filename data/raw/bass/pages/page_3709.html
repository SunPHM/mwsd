Parque da Floresta is beautifully situated on the boundaries of the Costa Vicentina Nature Reserve, a huge swathe of unspoilt countryside which is widely recognised as a place of special environmental and ecological importance.

Stretching along this part of the coast are secluded bays, rugged cliffs and gloriously uncrowded stretches of sandy beaches with simple bars where you can feast on fish freshly landed from the clear blue waters of the Atlantic Ocean.

Inland, the area is dotted with picturesque hamlets, clusters of whitewashed cottages and sleepy cobbled streets where time has seemingly stood still.

But this is only part of the story of the unfolding picture book that is the Western Algarve...

With its maze of narrow, winding streets and bustling squares full of pavement restaurants, bars and night clubs as well as shops of all description, the historic port of Lagos offers a more vibrant option.

Brightly painted fishing boats can be seen lining the river and harbour where early each morning the catche of prawns, lobsters, crab, sea bass, bream and sardines are landed. The strong maritime heritage continues at a handsome marina with its berths for fine yachts and other sailing craft..

Lagos is home to Meia Praia, one of the longest and most spectacular beaches in the Algarve, with clear waters lapping on to lengthy expanses of golden sand.

Out to the west lies Sagres and the famous lighthouse at Cape St Vincent, the most south-westerly point in continental Europe which was known as O Fim Do Mundo - "the end of the world" - by the great Portuguese mariners who passed this way on their voyages of discovery.
