21st Century Central European Double Bass - #2638

There are some excellent hand made instruments coming over from Central Europe. Can you tell me where this one was made?

Its difficult to pin point exactly where this good looking hand-made "three-quarter" size instrument was made but if we were forced to proffer an educated guess as to the country of origin we would most probably say that it emanated from either Hungary, the Czech Republic or Russia. Its impossible to be any more specific than that because this particular model of instrument – viol shaped with a swell back and outside linings - has been manufactured on the continent since the early twentieth century. It is for this reason that the exact date of the instrument is difficult to pin point too. Once again an educated estimate based on the internal colouration of the timber would place its construction to be within the last five to ten years.

What will I get for my money?

We can assure you that you'll be getting a lot of value for your money if you buy this instrument. I keep harping on that to buy the timber alone – here in England - to start making a double bass of this quality would cost in the region of two thousand pounds, and that's even before you start putting a chisel to it. On top of this The Contrabass Shoppe has spent a considerable amount of time and effort improving both the acoustical and playability qualities of the instrument. The repair and adjustment invoice reads as follows; "Remove front, re-thickness front where necessary, make and fit new bass-bar, reglue front, shoot fingerboard, fit new top nut, bush endpin hole and fit new endpin unit, fit new bridge with aluminium adjusters, fit new sound post, clean instrument and touch in varnish where necessary".

Summary:

A very well presented instrument that sounds and looks good. An instrument such as this should easily see you through your student days at college or as an excellent companion should you be a touring jazzer.
