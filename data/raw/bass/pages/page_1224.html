Mombo Hernandez

 

My musical influences are quite diverse.  I started playing bass guitar at the early age of 12 years after being inspired by the bass guitar of Dennis Dunaway of the Alice Cooper group.  I was inspired by some of the early progressive rock bands such as Yes, Jethro Tull, Genesis, Emerson Lake & Palmer, Rush and The Who.  Those were my early bass teachers!

Lenny & Steve Oliver

 

Learning the lines of Chris Squire, Jaco, Tony Levin, John Entwissel, etc., was quite a challenge.  My career took me from playing clubs, rock concerts and casino circuits to eventually Smooth Jazz.  Some people hate the term "Smooth Jazz".  I say "get over it".  It's great music.  currently, I play for several Smooth Jazz artists doing concerts throughout the season.  I've had the opportunity to meet some great players and discover a great new musical frontier.  One of my new inspirations and friends if Kim Stone of "The Rippingtons".  One of the "Smooth Jazz's great bass players.

 

 

I currently play 4 & 5 string Roscoe basses, 4 & 5 string fretless basses, a Washburn acoustic and my old workhorse - the Steinberger (an original one).  I play music because it is a part of my soul and always will be.  I continue to enjoy all aspects of it - mostly the positive energy side of it.  After all, it's a lot more fun to be happy!
