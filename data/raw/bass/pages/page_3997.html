One of our guest's favourites is the soda bread we often serve with dinner, here is the recipe which is from La Potiniere Cookbook by Hilary Brown.

8 oz Plain White Flour
8 oz Wholemeal Flour
1 teaspoon bicarbonate of soda
1 tablespoon of baking powder
1 teaspoon of salt
1-2 oz of sugar (optional, but a little sweetness is useful)
1 egg (we use our own eggs - free range and not size graded - but a medium to large egg is right)
12 fl oz buttermilk(can use half milk half natural yoghurt)

Preheat the oven to 375F(190C)Gas 5
Place wholemeal flour in bowl sieve in other dry ingredients, mix evenly and make a well in the centre.
Beat egg and buttermilk together,  pour into well and stir with a wooden spoon until a fairly soft dough is formed.
Turn out onto floured work surface pat into even shape and split into 4. Shape each quarter into a neat oval.
Lightly butter a baking tray and lay the loaves on it without letting them touch.
Bake for 15 mins or so they sound hollow when tapped underneath.    Remove from oven and cool.
