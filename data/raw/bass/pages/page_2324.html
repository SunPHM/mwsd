October, 2003

JUDGING CHAIR: Larry Steagall
Judges: Jason Hunt, Chief photographer Coeur d'Alenne Press, Jerome A. Pollos Staff Photographer/writer Cheer d'Alene Press

DATE RECEIVED: 11-17, 2003 DATE JUDGED: 11-19, 2003 JUDGES' REGION: 9

 

SPOT NEWS - NEWS EVENT COVERAGE FOR WHICH NO ADVANCE PLANNING WAS POSSIBLE

1. Dan DeLong, Seattle PI, "Rooster Flood"

First Place: The photographer made a nice clean image introducing order to a chaotic situation. It looks like a scene from
a third world country.

  

2. Colin Mulvany,Spokesman Review, "Child Fire"

Second Place: The somber woman is obviously full of emotion337

"It just went really fast," said Wendy Krotzer holding her son Zach, 2, while Spokane Fire fighters put out a blaze in her basement of her home at 6803 N. Fleming St. Thursday afternoon. Krotzer said that her other son, Luke, 4, told her he started the fire which heavily damaged the basement of the house.

3. Allen Berner, Seattle Times, "Log Jam"

Third Place: Good perspective shows scale and the magnitude of the floods.

  
Workers attempt to break up a logjam threatening the bridge on Highway 2 just east of Everett next to Ebey Island.

 

GENERAL NEWS - NEWS EVENT COVERAGE FOR WHICH ADVANCE PLANNING WAS POSSIBLE

1. Brian Davies, The Register Guard, "Mock Hospital"

First Place: Good layering with two separate moments in the same frame.

  

2. Chris Pietsch, The Register-Guard, "Solder Kiss"

Second Place: Visually appealing image with a good moment.

  
Spc William Stout of Hillsboro breaks ranks for a moment to recieve a kiss from daughter Maggie Stout, 9, before a deployment ceremony at the Lane County Fairgrounds.

3. Rob Kerr, The Bulletin, "Accident Grief"

Third Place: Good isolation, left just enough road in the image to tell the story.

  

 

SPORTS - ACTION OR SPORTS-RELATED FEATURE IN ONE OR MORE PICTURES

1. Thomas Boyd, The Register-Guard, "Duck Goal"

First Place: Peak' action where every person in the frame is part. of the moment.
 
Oregon's Kevin Mitchell dives over the goal line after intercepting a pass from Stanford's Chris Lewis.

2. Elaine Tompson, Associated Press, "Ref Fall"

Second Place: Rare event kept shooting after the paly instead of instantly looking at their LCD to see if they got the image.

  

3. Troy Wayrynen, The Columbian, "Crying Ham"

Third Place: Shallow depth of field really isolates the emotion filled player.

  

 

FEATURE SINGLE - OTHER THAWSPOTOR GENERAL NEWS, SPORTS OR ILLUSTRATION

1. Colin Mulvany, Spokesman Review, "Weighty Subject"

First Place: Simple but a very effective feature.


"It's not really much of a problem." said fifth grader, Stephen Colvin, age 10, of his large bass instrument as he sits down on the front steps of Wilson Elementary School, in Spokane, Wash. "Last year I had a heavier backpack with all my homework," said Colvin as he waited for his mom to pick him up after school on Monday

2. Robert Hallinen, Anchorage Daily News, "Northern Lights"

Second Place: Good use of reflection and a nice angle showing scale.


The northern lights dance across the sky and are reflected in Potter Marsh Thursday night. The aurora forecast for the next several days is for moderate activity.

3. Robert Brawdy, Tri-City Herald, "Fence Guy "

Third Place: Graphically appealing informational image.

  
Chad Lucas, an employee of Frontier Fence in Pasco, fits vinyl privacy slats Tuesday afternoon into a chainlink fence along Homerun Road in Pasco near TRAC. Lucas and co­worker Craig Winston worked under mostly sunny skies following early morning showers.

 

FEATURE MULTIPLE - PHOTO STORY, ESSAY, SEQUENCE OR SERIES

1. Alan Berner, Seattle Times, "At Home on the Range"

First Place: Good variety of images with almost no repetition. Every image in the story could stand on its own telling a story.


With the base of Steens Mountain as his backyard, Scott Davies is fearless on the family trampoline near Frenchglen, Oregon (Harney County)

Stacy Davies, manaager of the 500,000-acre Roaring Springs Ranch, and his horse xxxxx, wait for his sons to bring in 400 head of cattle for sorting.

That's a lot of buckle for a six-year-old worn by Jeff Davies and won for mutton busting at the Harney County Rodeo.

Sammi Jo Stohler, 19, has taught her horse Rowdee (ck spelling) to be anything but as he plays dead in a corral near Unity, Oregon.

Just met and starry eyed are Terry Harper and Jorgi Wasson, at the Grant County Fair in John Day, Oregon.

This road-weary sign points the way next to the Alvord Desert just north of Fields, Oregon.



It's "open range" where cars, the few-and-far-between, have to watch out for cattle on Hwy xxx in Harney County.

Scott Davies, aka Scooter, 4, can ride almost as well as many seasoned buckaroos once he's lifted into the saddle.

 

2. Kevin Clark, The Register-Guard, "Chloe's Journey"

Second Plae: Great opening shot to a series filled with moments. The only thing missing in the first part of the series is a good detail shot.

Paul Fernandez holds his arms out to his daughter Chloe Fernandez, 1, as she takes her first steps for him. Chloe had taken a few steps earlier in the day, but Paul had been working. Chloe suffers from cystic hygroma, which causes the abnormal growth on her right cheek. The family is trying to raise $30,000 for a series of shots that will reduce the growth.

3. Karen Ducey, Seattle PI, "Crabbers"

Third Place: Lots of good images that both tell a story and are visually appealing.
