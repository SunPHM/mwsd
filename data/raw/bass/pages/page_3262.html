Sea Bass - Sustainable Alternatives

Why Choose Sustainable Seafood?

Almost every region in the U. S. has readily available, sometimes local, species of sustainably caught fish that are excellent alternatives.

  • Sustainable seafood supports U.S. fishermen and benefits regional economies, instead of a handful of foreign-owned corporations.

  • Sustainable fish offer better nutrition and taste, because local and regional fish are usually fresh, not flash-frozen and kept refrigerated for up to 8 months like most Chilean Sea Bass served in American restaurants.

  • Sustainable fish are less damaging to the environment. U.S. fishing regulations -- in contrast to voluntary (often ineffective) international agreements -- are intended to protect the environment and preserve marine resources.

  • Sustainably caught fish discourage illegal trade such as the trade in Chilean Sea Bass by pirate vessels and unscrupulous importers. Even the most reputable restaurants and markets cannot always be 100% sure if the frozen fish they buy is legal or if it is truly Chilean Sea Bass and not a cheap substitute.

  • Sustainable fish offer high quality at affordable prices. Even frozen Chilean Sea Bass typically wholesales at prices two or three times above fresh, regionally caught fish.

  • The closest alternatives to Chilean Sea Bass in terms of taste and texture are cod and Alaskan halibut.
Rather than risk the collapse of these stocks by recommending only two fish as alternatives, chefs and consumers are urged to consider using the following sustainably caught species based on their locality.

Fish available in specific regions tend to be seasonal, so they may not appear in markets and on menus year-round. Likewise, because of inadequate regulations for some fish, stocks can plummet in a few months without warning.

For more timely guidance on possible alternatives to Chilean Sea Bass, Seafood Choices Alliance, a national clearinghouse working with the environmental community and the food sector at www.seafoodchoices.org. The Monterey Bay Aquarium's Seafood Watch Chart is also an excellent resource for locating sustainable fisheries and for learning about other species whose survival is threatened by overfishing or which are caught or farmed in ways that damage the environment.
