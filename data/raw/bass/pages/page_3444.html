County Louth in Ireland's Northeast

The City of Dundalk (about 30,000 inhabitants) is the administrative centre of County Louth, near the mouth of the Castletown River at Dundalk Bay. It has a port that exports livestock, barley, and other crops. 

Industries include tobacco, clothing, shoe, and computer factories, distilleries and breweries, flour and linen mills, fishing. Dundalk has facilities for the manufacture and maintenance of railway equipment.

The town was often besieged because of its strategic location at a natural pass into Northern Ireland.

Monasterboice monastery was founded by an obscure saint named Buite who died in 521. We know that the monastery remained in existence up till 1122. The Vikings occupied it until they were attacked by Domhnall, King of Tara, in 9687.  The Cooley Peninsula Forms the Lough's southern coast, a place of fresh green hillsides, woodland, forest, mountains and myths.

This was the legendary home of the giant Finn M’Cool and the setting of Ulster’s greatest saga, the Tain bo Cuailgne, an epic tale of the hero Cuchullain, the Red Branch Knights of Ulster, wicked Queen Maebhe of Connaught and the mighty Brown Bull of Cooley. The place whispers of thousands of years of civilisation through the Long Woman’s Grave, the Proleek Dolmen of Ravensdale and the Clontygora Court Graves of Omeath.

Carlingford is one of Ireland's foremost medieval towns - a place of narrow streets and castles, over-shadowed by mountains and lapped by the sea.

The many historical buildings include the 12th century King John's Castle, the fortified Dominican Abbey dating from 1305, The Mint and The Tholsel, a medieval toll house, both dating from the 15th century.




King John's Castle - 12th century.  This early Norman fortress was named after King John who visited Carlingford in 1210. The western portion of the castle predates this visit and was probably commissioned by Hugh de Lacy around 1190.




Carlingford Lough southern shore offers dogfish, flatfish, occasional ray and conger. The area below the lighthouse at Greenore has mackerel, sea trout, bass and small pollock.
