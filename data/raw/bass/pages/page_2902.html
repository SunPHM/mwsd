FLORIDA BASS FISHING BRAG'N BOARD: NOVEMBER 1998

Florida bass fishing information, Florida trophy bass fishing pictures, Florida bass fishing reports.



Florida Bass Fishing Lake Okeechobee 6 1/2 & 7 1/4 lb. Bass

Doug Headquist (mound, MN - ph: 612-472-1442 - e-mail: rmenard1@isd.net) and Judy Sivula (Blaine, MN - ph: 612-755-6034) caught 23 bass up to 7 1/4 lbs. during 2 half days (Nov. 26, 27, 1998) of wild shiner fishing with Ron’s Guide Service. Dick and Judy each caught and released a 6 1/2 lb'er. Judy caught the 7 1/4 lb'er. All bass were caught on the North Shore of Lake Okeechobee. All bass were released.


Florida Bass Fishing 6 lb. Okeechobee bass
Charlie Geiger (Lithonia, GA - ph: 770-483-2398 - e-mail: Stang83GT@aol.com) caught 21 bass up to 6 lbs. during 1 day (Nov. 24, 1998) of Florida wild shiner bass fishing with Ron’s Guide Service. This catch of Florida bass came out of the North Shore area of Okeechobee. All bass were released.



Florida Bass Fishing 7 lb. Lake Okeechobee
Trophy Bass

These two 7 lb'ers were caught by Dick and his dad Bob Monsky (Central Islip, NY - home ph: 516-582-6246 - bus. ph: 716-753-8503). They caught a total of 63 bass during 3 days (Nov. 10 - 12, 1998) of wild shiner fishing with Ron's Guide Service. Besides the two 7 lb'ers, they also had a 6 1/2 and two 6 lb'ers. All the bass were released.


Floroda Bass Fishing 8, 7 1/2 & 6 lb. Lake Okeechobee Trophy Bass


Stan Felton (Canton, MA - ph: 781-828-1635) and John Kennedy (Roslindale, MA - ph: 617-323-4294) caught only 76 bass in 5 days due to hurricane Mitch high wind and rain conditions. Some nice bass however were caught and released which included a 8 lb. (top left - John Kennedy), 7 1/2 lb. (top right), 6 lb. (bottom left - Stan Felton) and eight bass that were from 5 - 5 1/2 lbs. during 5 days (Nov. 2 - 6, 1998) of wild shiner fishing with Cap’n Ron. This trip was their “tenth year” of fishing on Lake Okeechobee with Cap’n Ron. Stan caught the 6 lb’er while John caught the 7 1/2 and 8 lb’er. All bass were caught in the Monkey Box area of Lake Okeechobee.
