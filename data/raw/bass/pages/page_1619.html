Hamer FB4 8-String Bass
Submitted by Jon Maye 04 Apr 2003

No its not a 12-string bass, but its the next best thing!  Number 1 of 1 Hamer T-Bird 8-string made for Tom Petersson in 1990.  

So....This is the infamous one-of-a-kind Hamer FB-4 (don't ask me why it's a Firebird code # when technically it's a Thunderbird since it's a bass) made for Tom Petersson in 1990...used in the Cheap Trick video...on the Concrete Blonde tour, etc. Confirmed the SN#  with Hamer...it's the real thing.

Bass DID come originally from Hamer with two Gibson Thunderbird pickups (a '64 and a '66; nickel/chrome) loaded in it that Tom supplied them. The guy I got it from swapped them with two newer black Gibson Thunderbird ones because the older ones' magnets were going and the output weak (have the correct 64/66 in the case.) That T-Bird logo on it is also just a heat transfer one the other guy added...I was going to take it off but it sort of looks cool, so it's staying for now. And yes, it plays and sounds great...although it has the Petersson three output jacks/three volumes/three on-off switch wiring (no onboard tone control, meant to let the 3 amp rigs do that.)

Oddly enough, the only Hamer guitar I currently have is a custom '89 Chaparral in the identical custom color..."Cherry Metal Flake." How's that for a cool coincidence? The guitar says it's embarrassed to be posing in a photo for a bass board...but I told it to shut up and smile

Hamer Standard & Double Neck B-8L-String Chaparral
Submitted by Bill Kron on October 26,  2001

It's an 8-string double neck (hardware and pickups are like a 12 string chaparral), with a matching forked headstock 4 string chaparral on the bottom with p and hband a hipshot on the e string. it has an emg exb for each neck, a volume for each neck, and although there's no pickup selector I have accomplished what I need out of it with push/pull pots.  Additionally it has a preamp/booster with a mini toggle. 

The standard is absolutely a killer bass.... it's old (had a single truss rod when I got it) and was somewhat abused; leaving the neck improperly seated. I took it to a top-reputation dealer in NYC, and they were going to fix it by planning the neck and regretting it....until the tech snapped the truss rod...then they said too bad...nothing we can do; and left me high and dry. I called Kim at Hamer and explained what happened, and he said they would see what they could do, so I sent it to them. to make a long story short; when I got it back 6 months later it was like brand new...paint, new double truss rod neck, but it was thin like the original!!!  It cost about $800 but if you add that to what I paid for it to begin with, it still cost a lot less than what a used USA Hamer 12 was going for and is a pretty unique bass. The OBL humbuckers are wired with push/pull pots so I get series/parallel switching on each pickup, series is meaty and parallel is clear with a single-coil attack and clarity -without any hum. it has an EMG exb control..which isn't intended for use with passive pickups, but absolutely kills on this bass!

Early Hamer B12S and Standard
Submitted by Peter on October 25,  2001

These basses were those used by Pete Comita on tour when he took over for Tom Petersson.  Most notably from the Chicagofest and SNL appearances.  Both are short scale basses, single truss rod, with MANY lacquer coats (much thicker finishes than those used on most Hamers I have seen from this time). Interesting features are  the B12S a preamp and LED on the face, and the Hamer logo used on the bass is sort of unique in that the "r" is in lower case, which I have only seen this logo used on the "prototype" first Hamer Vee bass that Jol Dantzig built for himself.

The standard bass has a smaller body (not quite a 3/4 size, but still much smaller than a "normal" standard guitar or bass). Wired for separate output jacks for each pickup. Also has the pre-amp and LED on the face.  Interestingly, in very small script, between each of the stripes are the individual letters that spell out "Pete Comita".  On the headstock under the logo it says "spacematic". Both basses have held up very well over time, they sound killer and the necks have remained straight and true.  I have had the B12S since the late 80's so when the opportunity came to get his "brother"  I wasn't going to pass up the chance to reunite the two.  Many thanks go to my collector friend for scoring the bass directly from Pete Comita.

1990 Custom Hamer B12S
Submitted by Kevin Mazca on May 30, 2001

These pictures are of my first Hamer B12S which I bought in 1990. I think that they were taken in the spring of 1991. As you can see from the photos, it has four control knobs instead of the usual three. I custom ordered this bass from Hamer with stereo wiring, two EMG EXB expanders, and a one-piece curly maple top. Shortly after receiving it I swapped out the EXB circuits for BTC circuits instead, finding the loudness boosting effect of the installed EXBs less versatile.

I sold this bass in 1996 when I purchased my black B12L. Although I prefer the B12L, I wish that I would have kept the B12S, mostly for sentimental reasons, though it had its own vibe which I miss. From a beauty perspective I much prefer the cherry transparent B12S.

1996 Hamer B12A & 1994 Hamer B12S

These are my girls. I believe the B12A is a '96 in your basic flame top '59 'Burst. (Had I known at the time that nearly every B12A on the planet would be this color, I'd have chosen something else!) 1990 Hamer B12A Shortscale
Submitted by Tony Babylon on May 1, 2001

I thought you might be interested in posting my 1990 short scale acoustic 12.  This bass is unusual for a couple of reasons,

1. It has black hardware. 
2. Original type control lay out. 
3. Dual truss rods!  

This bass contradicts a lot of ideas about availability of these basses.  I had two other short scale acoustic 12's built in 1989.  They were both single truss rod models one natural one black.  This bass was built the following year.  The only other I know about was another I no longer own.  It was an acoustic short scale, dual rods, Cheap Trick Busted! album graphic finish, w/ a maple board that in black says, BUSTED!  I had spoken to Kim Keller at Hamer and he said the short scale dual rod AC12's are extremely rare.  They are the bridge between the original AC12 and what is currently in production.
