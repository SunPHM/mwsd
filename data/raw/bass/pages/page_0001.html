Fender Expands Rumble Series Of Bass Amps With New Model

January 21, 2006

(Click for a close-up)

At this winter's NAMM show in Anaheim, California, The Rumble™ Series from Fender® Bass Amplification welcomes the addition of the new Rumble 100/210 bass combo to its industry-leading line of bass amplifiers. Offering great tones, ease-of-use and undeniable value, the new Rumble 100/210 is ideal for discerning players on a budget, and retailers who know the power of the Fender Bass Amplification line of products.

Building on the existing line of Rumble amplifiers, the new 100/210 features 2x10" speakers, offering a quicker and punchier tone most commonly associated with the 15" speakers found in similar combo amps. This 100 watt combo features a piezo horn, two midrange controls, a "mid-scoop" button and removable castor wheels for easy portability.

Advertisement

Loud enough to play with a drummer, the new Rumble 100/210 is also ideal for practice, rehearsal and studio applications. The RCA input allows players to practice along to any external device using RCA outputs, and the headphone output allows for practice without disturbing anyone nearby.

The new Rumble 100/210 from Fender Bass Amplification can be seen for the first time at this winter's NAMM show at Fender's booth.

For more information, visit their web site at www.fender.com.
