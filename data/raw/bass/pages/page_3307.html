Sesame & Lemon Grass Encrusted Sea Bass




(Serves 4)

White Wine Fish Sauce:
1 cup (240 ml) white wine
2 shallots, minced
1/2 cup (120 ml) fish or chicken bouillon
1/2 cup (120 ml) heavy cream
1 to 2 tablespoons cornstarch
Butter to taste

Directions:
Reduce white wine and shallots to 1/4 cup (60 ml). Add fish or chicken bouillon and reduce again to 1/2 cup (60 ml). When reduced, add heavy cream and reduce again to 1/2 cup (60 ml). Dilute cornstarch in water and add just enough cornstarch solution to lightly thicken fish sauce. Finish with butter for desired richness.

Soy-Mustard Sauce:
Soy sauce
Dry mustard

Mix soy sauce and mustard together and adjust to taste. It should have a slightly thick consistency and be spicy as the white wine fish sauce will dilute and smooth the flavor. Pour into a plastic squeeze bottle such as a cleaned catsup bottle.

Fish preparation:
2 tablespoons minced fresh lemongrass, tough outer layer removed and green ends cut off
4 - 4.5 ounce (125 g) portions of sea bass fillets
4 tablespoons white sesame seeds
Salt and pepper to taste
Grape seed oil for saute
Clear butter for saute
8 ounces (255 g) high-quality Japanese rice, steamed
8 ounces (225 g) white wine fish sauce
4 tablespoons Tobiko (flying fish eggs)
2 tablespoons Russian black caviar (optional)
20 asparagus spears, cooked
4 mounds of shredded spaghetti vegetables (raw Daikon sprouts)

Press lemongrass on one side of the fish fillet. Press sesame seeds on fish and apply enough pressure to make them stick without damaging the fish. Season with salt and pepper. Saut&#233; in hot grape seed oil and clear butter. Remove and keep warm in oven.
Form a circle or mound of rice on a dinner plate. Pour warm white wine fish sauce around rice, and decorate with soy-mustard sauce. Dollop Tobiko and caviar in an attractive fashion on mound. Place fish on top of rice and finish with asparagus spears and spaghetti vegetables or sprouts.

Wine Recommendation: Chateau Ste Michelle Horse Heaven Sauvignon Blanc, Columbia Crest Estate Bottled Merlot
