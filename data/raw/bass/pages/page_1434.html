Guitar
     The common guitar has six strings which are strummed or plucked. There are many forms of the guitar; the acoustic guitar, electric acoustic, electric guitar, the acoustic bass electric, and the electric bass.
     The electric types of guitars sound can be amplified and modified for concert use. Bass guitars have only four strings which are thicker then regular guitar strings, bass guitars pitch is much lower than other instruments and is used to keep beat and rhythm for the other instruments.


Banjo
     A banjo typically has four strings plucked or strummed; the bango has a very high pitched twanging sound made by the body of the banjo. The banjo is sometimes played as a classical instrument and more commonly as a bluegrass/country instrument.


Violin
     The violin is one of the smallest stringed instruments, and is also known as the fiddle when played as a country/bluegrass instrument. It has four strings which are played with a bow. A bow is a long piece of wood that holds a thick string of horsetail hair at two ends. A bow is about 30 inches long, and is run across the strings rubbing to make the beautiful violin resonance.
     The violin is played as a classical instrument, playing in triplets, quartets, Symphonies, orchestras, and solos. The violin usually plays melody. It is also played as a country/bluegrass instrument. The violin has a very large family of instruments; other forms are the viol, viola, and the cello. Another distant relative is the double bass.


Other Stringed Instruments
     The category of Other Stringed Instruments that I've made are for the lesser uncommon stringed instruments you don't know much about. These are only a few of the hundreds of uncommon stringed instruments!

Lute
     The lute is what the guitar evolved from; it can have up to 43 strings. It is, like the guitar, strummed or plucked. The luter is believed to have originated during the B.C. period.

Mandolin
     The mandolin is a small country/bluegrasss instrument that sounds like a banjo, only that its sound is softer and less of a twang.

Harp

     The harp is the oldest instrument known to man. It is played by being plucked: Making a symphonic sound. The harp plays mainly in orchestras.

Double Bass
     The double bass is the largest stringed instrument. It keeps the beat and rhythm of the music. It is played as a classical and country/bluegrass instrument. It is also one of the lowest pitched instrument which is why it keeps beat.
