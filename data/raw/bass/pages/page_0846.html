White Bass

White bass are an enormous amount of fun to catch (try to imagine bluegills that run up to 3-4 pounds.) If you catch one, it will fight so hard you will swear it is twice as big as it actually is. They eat almost anything, but mostly small fish, so I would suggest 3-4 inch streamers. Bright and flashy ones will usually work, but sometimes they will be a little fussy about color (sometimes they will insist on a perch-colored lure.) I have also seen white bass hitting grasshoppers and moths on the surface in mid-summer, but unfortunately, never while I had a fly rod in my hand.

White bass usually travel in large schools, so if you catch one, work the area thoroughly because there are probably a lot more. They travel a lot, so finding white bass in an area one day is not a guarantee that they will be there the next day.

They are good to eat, too. I especially like to smoke them.
