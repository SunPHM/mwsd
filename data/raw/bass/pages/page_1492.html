Ingles Upright Bass Stand -
one of the most stable stands you'll find!

For years I reluctantly used my old bass stand -- the type that you stick the endpin in and has arms to hold the shoulders. I hated it, because it was hard to hit the little cup and I never had much confidence in the stand's stability.

I've tried a number of stands, including the very nice MBS stand, and the old Hamilton stands that had the little "cup" you had to aim for (and often missed on a darkened stage!) Of all of the ones I've used, the Ingles has a specific feature that makes it more flexible - and well-suited for taller bassists and others who extend their endpins more than six inches: the cradle arms are adjustable. The Ingles folks have taken the MBS design and enhanced it. If you have an extraordinarly long endpin, please contact me with the length so I can be certain your bass will fit.

The Ingles stand includes an extended arm with a hook for hanging your bow. All contact surfaces are covered with a protective foam type of material. As solid as it is, the stand still folds flat and to a reasonably compact size, as the photos show. This is no wimpy plastic stand -- it is of all metal construction, except for the padding and vinyl tips, and weighs 8.2 pounds.

The Ingles has the advantage of the movable bottom cradle- it can go up higher to allow for an unusually long endpin (for many basses, depending on dimensions, it can be as long as 10-11 inches!) Stands like the MBS can accommodate a 6" endpin extension, but that's it - so if you are a taller player the Ingles is the appropriate choice - and it also costs less!

The Ingles' flexibility can also enable it to accommodate a Baby Bass type instrument, as it is designed to handle a bass or cello; that's my Eminence Portable Bass in the photo above. It works with Palatino EUBs as well, as I received an email from Nicholas K*** Jr on August 26, 2005: Bob, I received the stand today. It works great with my Palatino EUB. Thanks for your prompt service, Nick
