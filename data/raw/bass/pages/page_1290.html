Back story: my friend Aaron and I were two of several planning to go on the annual Crop Walk to stop world hunger and such. Well, we were going to walk through downtown Raleigh, passing several government buildings, so when the news broke today that we were bombing Afghanistan, the cops were unhappy with the idea of us being around government buildings and it was canceled.

Then, some of us headed downtown (hmm...) to stand near some government building (hmmm...) and hold up signs protesting for peace n' stuff. A couple "honk for peace" signs, that kind of thing. Some Jesus stuff.

Anyway, there was this old American flag lying around with some of the signs, so Aaron and I figured we'd hold it up along with the other signs (no, that's not a contradictory message. If you can't understand that, you might as well leave now).

Now, this was an old flag. It was still missing a few stars. And the front side (the side which I spent several minutes convincing Aaron was the front) was really faded, apparently from hanging in a window somewhere for fifty years or something.

So Aaron and I are holding up this faded old flag on the street corner, cheering for people who honk at us and stuff, when the press arrive. Patrick O'Neill, who was kinda in charge of the whole thing, had called in some photographers and cameramen and reporters and stuff. Now, we're just hangin' out on the corner when this dude cruises past with his window rolled down playing some Mexican tune or something, and for some reason this is drop-dead hilarious to Aaron.

Now, if you've ever heard Aaron laugh, you'll understand why I was not able to stand for the next five minutes. He kind of emits this womanly shriek, and his face assumes a deathlike rictus of terror. Really.

So while my face is assuming its own deathlike rictus and I'm stomping up and down at Aaron's hilarity, apparently this schmuck cameraman decides it's time to feature Leigh on the TV news. Yay.

I didn't see it on TV, so I've only got Aaron's word to go by here, but I can imagine the footage is quite embarrassing. Why they went with that shot for an antiwar vigil, I dunno.

Oh, I almost forgot. James and I also played for money once at UNC-Charlotte, which was hilarious and exciting at the same time. We were walking back from the talent show and James had his guitar with him, and it was kinda getting dark as we walked through the middle of campus, and we were like, "y'know, we could just start playing right here and leave the guitar case open for people to drop money in." It took each of us a minute to realize the other one was serious, and then we sat underneath the Belk Tower and played for all these people going past. We did some Clapton tunes, "More Than Words" a few times, that kind of stuff. And the evening sky was dimming, and it was just really relaxed and peaceful, and it felt really cool because we had just decided to do it spontaneously and we didn't have anywhere to be, and we just hung out for like an hour and felt like college students.
