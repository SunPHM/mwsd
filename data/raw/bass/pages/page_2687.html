Lake Kabetogama Fishing

About Lake Kabetogama

Rocky Point Resort brings you a basic overview of the species, migrations, lake characteristics, fishing tips, and much more. The lake itself covers approximately 25,760 acres and the maximum depth is in the neighborhood of 50 feet.  Nearly a third of the lake is around, or less than 15 feet deep.  The lake has an unusual shoreline, as it is bordered by rock formations, and forests.  The lake contains numerous islands.

Minnesota Fishing Species

The lake is also home to Perch, Sauger, Northern Pike, Crappie, Smallmouth Bass, Rock Bass, Burbot, and there are also Whitefish, Lake Sturgeon, and Cisco (Tullibee). The Crappie in the lake are abundant throughout the lake.  Many of the fish do reach trophy size ranging from 1 to 2 pounds.  Pike however have been struggling in recent years.  Most fish are less than 5 pounds, and some reach between 8 and 10.  Few fish between 16 and 20 pounds are taken out of the lake.  Smallmouth bass are present throughout the lake, but angling pressure for the species is light, and many focus on walleye, as Lake Kabetogama ranks among the top 10 walleye lakes in Minnesota.

Types of Fishing

Many people have come upon systematic techniques for fishing the lake.  Many anglers prefer to troll around drop offs and let their lure out at a depth of 7 to 8 feet nearly 50 to 80 yards behind the boat, in hopes of catching larger pike.  Most anglers prefer a heavier line, around 20-pound test.  Many anglers also like to work in the bays and vegetated areas in hope of catching a lurking predator.  In spring, walleye are more likely to be found in water less than 15 feet deep, and the larger fish between 3 and 4 feet.  On windier days, walleye are known to move into rock reefs to feed, and respond to crankbaits over these structures.

Walleye Fishing Season

From September on, Walleye are usually taken in water around or less than 15 feet of depth  At this time of the year, anglers prefer to use jigs and crank baits. 

Smallmouth Bass Fishing Season

Smallmouth bass spawn between May and June over shallow gravel areas, where a constant current is present.  Anglers often aim for rocks or drop holes in the shallower water, and fish with 4-inch plastic worms, and Twister Tails.  During the summer, smallmouth move between the depths of 10 and 15 feet.  Once again, anglers prefer to cast spinners and crank baits around rocky points. 

Crappie, Perch & Rick Bass Fishing Season

Crappie and perch, along with rock bass are found throughout the lake, and are primarily caught with smaller baits, in areas over 15 feet in depth.
