Some info for the trip that might be helpful to others:

The fishing starts each day after breakfast at approx. 5:40 a.m.. The temp. each morning ranged from 47* to 51*. The first two hours were spent using topwaters and all of our success was on the smaller Pop-Rs (lighter colors). We had some luck with the black baby torpedoes. I heard some of the fishermen complain that they were "wasting" time with the topwaters. Personally, I found it very productive and we caught plenty of fish with lots of size. We would eventually switch to crankbaits (shad/blue) and these were also productive. After one to two hours of crankbaits we would switch to worms and lizards for the rest of the day. I used 7 inch green/chartreuse lizards exclusively ( #4 hooks and 3/4 oz. sinkers) and had constant action. My son used red and purple 7-9 inch worms and also had great action. The wind picked up significantly in the afternoon but was never a big problem and provided relief from the 90* heat. At the end of two of our three days of fishing we spent the last hour with topwaters. Lots of fun but not as productive. The most productive baits, by far, were the worms and lizards. The early morning topwater was great fun. There were no biting insects but sunscreen is a must.
My son caught his big fish on a 7 inch purple worm, #4 hook, and 3/4 oz. sinker. He was using 20 lb. test line. This is amust, as you know, because of all the timber. We probably lost at least 5 fish in the 7-12 lb. range because they got tangled in the wood.
