Phylum Chordata          Sub-Phylum Pisces          Class Osteichthyes

Order Perciformes - Group 1

Groupers, bass, jacks, corbs, red mullet, breams & relatives

 

Family Serranidae = Groupers, combers, sea bass, sea perch and relatives
A proup of predatory, demersal fish including the groupers, relatively large teleosts typically inhabiting rocky seabeds or coral reefs. The group is very varied so it is difficult to define in terms of key characteristics shared by all.


The lyre-tailed grouper Variola louti occurs widely on coral reefs in the Indo-Pacific Ocean. Click on image to enlarge


The dusky grouper Epinephelus guaza of the Mediterranean Sea and adjacent Atlantic Ocean. Click on image to enlarge

The sea bass Dicentrarchus labrax is native to the NE Atlantic Ocean and the Mediterranean Sea. Many other fish around the the world referred to as 'bass' are not members of the Serranidae.

Family Carangidae = Jacks, scad, horse mackerels and relatives
Spindle-shaped fish resembling tunas and mackerels superficially, but lacking finlets behind the dorsal and anal fins. These are pelagic, predatory fish that often form schools.

The amberjack Seriola dumerilii is a large, commercially important species that occurs across the warm temperate and tropical Atlantic Ocean and the Mediterranean Sea. Click on image to enlarge

Family Sciaenidae = Corbs, meagres, drums & relatives
Perciform fish with two dorsal fins and a lateral line that extends onto the tail stalk.

The brown corb Sciaena umbra is found amongst rocks and sea grasses in the Mediterranean Sea and the warm-temperate / sub-tropical North Atlantic. Click on image to enlarge

Family Mullidae = Red mullet and goatfish
Bottom-dwelling, ventrally-flattened fish with a pair of elongate sensory barbels on the chin, two dorsal fins and large scales.
 


The red mullets Mullus barbatus (shown) and Mullus surmuletus are much valued food fish found in the Mediterranean Sea and Eastern Atlantic Ocean. Click on image to enlarge

Family Sparidae = Breams and relatives
Oval, laterally-compressed fish with a single dorsal fin, the first part with stout spiny rays. Body and head covered by large scales. Teeth well developed and adapted for scraping algae, crushing shellfish or capturing smaller fin-fish.
 


Schools of the saupe Sarpa salpa are a common sight over rock reefs bearing seaweed turfs in the Mediterranean Sea.  Click on image to enlarge
