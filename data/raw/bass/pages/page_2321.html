Comments:

     This unique, handmade instrument from luthier Joe Cheevers is the first of his line of Ankh basses. The design is at once quirky, original, and even practical. The most striking aspect of this bass is of course the shape and color - with only one horn and a petite body, the swan-like form looks almost as if it is glowing from within with a very pretty, highly saturated blue-green burst. The downside to having only one horn is that of course you cannot play it on your lap (it slides off), but put it on a strap and it's very comfortable due to its light weight (the body is not only small but it's also chambered) and good balance (strap button near the 12th fret). It also feels very resonant, the body being constructed of mahogany and maple (hard to go wrong with this tried and true combination), and the bass has good sustain with a pleasant, even decay. Plugged in the bass has a very open, full sound, with an overal warmth and sweetness that we really liked. The passive treble roll-off doesn't allow for an extremely wide range of tones but the sounds available here are all quite good and musically useful - as we've said many times before, we prefer a bass with one or two good sounds to a bass with a whole lot of mediocre sounds. The tone control (very nice ebony knobs, by the way) has a bit of drag in it though - most people will not be turning this knob all the time and it's definitely something that can be lived with, but still, it should have been corrected at final inspection.
     Which brings us to the issues of this instrument's woodworking and finish. It is, in a word, 'rough'. There are a few small details on this instrument that are executed with a lot of care - for example the delicately carved stepped design motif of the headstock and the 'slap contour' on the body. But curiously enough, there are also other parts of this bass that look as if they were rushed - the pickup routs aren't precise, and the control cavity rout looks very tentative. One could argue that these are are aesthetic considerations and don't affect the playability of the instrument, but these will probably bother many bassists used to the level of fit and finish typically found on production instruments. There are other aspects that are simply not done correctly - for example, the nut is cut too high, so playing in first position feels stiff. The pickups are mounted incorrectly (not enough springy material underneath the pickups so they move up and down too much), and the soldering in the control cavity is sloppy. The thumbrest (which, by the way, smells very good - it is soaked in an oil that is said to soothe skin) is somewhat questionable in its size, shape, and location - both reviewers wished it would disappear or morph into a different shape (though they both enjoyed sniffing it). The finish too - which looks so pretty a short distance - upon close examination turns out to be rather roughly applied, and there are also an assortment of various small blemishes scattered over the surface of the instrument. In short, this bass has a somewhat "home made" or "prototype" feel to it. Some of the above issues are not simply 'subjective' and should be corrected (the nut, the soldering in the control cavity are easy fixes), and many of the fit and finish issues we've listed just seem to be the result of the process by which the bass is made, like it or not. Like a lot of art we see, this bass looks its best from a certain 'viewing distance' (if you're planning to use this as a stage bass, obviously nobody in the audience is going to notice or care) and probably wasn't meant to be scrutinized up close. This will matter to some and not to others - let your own values guide you.
     Regardless of the above-mentioned issues, we're hoping that someone will give this unusual bass will a nice home. It's certainly visually striking and it does sound very good, so someone with an open mind (and who's handy with a file and soldering iron!) will end up with a great stage bass if she or he is willing to give it some love. We're quite sure, too, that this bass will bring you a lot of attention... yes, we think we can pretty much guarantee that!     ~ m. & j, reviewers. ^_^
