Newtone Strings ist eine kleine englische Saiten-Manufaktur, die ein umfangreiches Sortiment qualitativ hochwertiger Saiten fertigt. Malcolm Newton gründete die Firma in den späten 80igern, nachdem er viele Jahre als Seilmacher arbeitete. Es wird jeweils eine Saite zur Zeit gefertigt, jeder Fertigungsschritt wird genau überwacht. Diese Saiten sind tatsächlich handgefertigt und erfüllen höchste Qualitätsansprüche.
Einer der wichtigsten Händler für Newtone Strings in Europa ist Celtic Sounds, die sich ebenfalls durch Ihr Angebot von handgemachten Gitarren aus Irland und Schweden einen Namen gemacht haben: www.celticsounds.de

Was ist das Geheimnis von Newtone Strings?
Wenn man diese Saiten zum ersten Mal spielt, wird man feststellen, daß die Saitenspannung spürbar niedriger ist wie bei anderen Saiten. Dies wird durch die Verwendung eines Innendrahts (Seele) mit rundem Querschnitt erreicht. Industriell gefertigte Saiten haben im Gegensatz dazu eine Seele mit sechseckigem Querschnitt (Hexcore). Der Hexcore verbessert die Verbindung zwischen Seele und Wicklung, er macht die Saite jedoch auch steifer und es ist eine höhere Spannung erforderlich. Außerdem befinden sich immer winzige Hohlräume zwischen der Hexcore-Seele und der Wicklung, in denen sich Feuchtigkeit und Verunreinigungen sammeln, welches schnell zu einem stumpfen und matten Ton führt. Im Gegensatz dazu erreichen Newtone Strings mit der Roundcore-Seele eine höhere Lebensdauer und bieten durch die niedrige Saitenspannung ein angenehmes Spielgefühl.

Resonator

Nationals
These strings were designed in collaboration with blues guitarist Michael Messer to suit both National electric and acoustic players. They are now licensed by the National Guitar Company and are the most popular strings around for those instruments. "They make resonator guitars sound amazing, and because they tune to pitch at lower tension there is no strain on the neck or cones."
Michael Messer, Blues guitarist

Choose the string gauges that suit your favourite tunings. For all tunings achieved by raising the pitch of certain strings from standard tuning it is recommended to use Michael Messer Medium Light or Michael Messer Medium.

Examples of "raised pitch tunings":

G (G B D G B D)   (high bass G)

A (A C# E A C# E) (high bass A)

A (E A E A C# E)  (low bass A or Hawaiian A)

E (E B E G# B E)

For all tunings achieved by lowering the pitch of certain strings from standard tuning (Slack key tunings) it is recommended to use Michael Messer Heavy.

Examples of "Slack key tunings":

G (D G D G B D)  (low bass G or Hawaiian Tuning)

D (D A D G A D)

D (D A D F# A D) (Louisiana Tuning)

Dobro
Specially designed for resonator guitars with a spider bridge setup.

Weissenborn

Hawaiian Guitar
Designed for use on wood-bodied Hawaiian guitars with a hollow neck. They are made using a light core enabling them to tune to pitch at lower tension than regular strings. It is still advisable to exercise caution when using high bass tunings such as GBDGBD on old Hawaiian guitars (tune it down again before putting it back in the case).

Examples of open tunings for Hawaiian guitar:

D (D A D F# A D)  (Louisiana Tuning)

D (D A D G A D)

E (E B E G# B E)

G (D G D G B D)   (low bass G or Hawaiian Tuning)

G (G B D G B D)   (high bass G)

A (A C# E A C# E) (high bass A)

A (E A E A C# E)  (low bass A or Hawaiian A)

Archtop Jazz

they have a more 'open' sound. This makes it very
easy to distinguish between notes in a chord. Also,
they have a nice punch, giving my guitars the woody sound that I am looking for."

They will improve the tone of any steel-string acoustic guitar and, consequently, your playing."
Ian Cruichshank (Author, "The Guitar Style of Django Reinhardt")

Custom Sets

This bass guitar is made by luthier Richard Heeres from the Netherlands. The Newtone Strings fitted on this guitar are custom made and of "Piano type" which means that the first inches from ballend there is no wrap string so the core string can rest directly on the saddle.
Richard writes about the guitar: "It has a swamp ash core with a carved maple top. It is fitted with a rosewood bridge (with Fishman pickup) and tailpiece. The magnetic pickup is a Seymour Duncan. Machine heads are Gotoh 510-series. The strings sound great on it! The basses are warm but without sounding muddy, with rich overtones. String tension feels comfortable and the string-to-string balance is excellent."

Check out the links to visit Richard's website: www.heeresguitars.nl
