Interpretation of these rules shall be left exclusively to the tournament director. Failure to comply may result in DISQUALIFICATION. The decision of the tournament director shall be final in all matters.

PARTICIPATION AND ELIGIBILITY: Participation is OPEN to all 18 years or older. Applicants under the age of 18 may fish but only with a parent or guardian. Proof of age to be satisfaction of tournament director is the responsibility of the competitor. A properly completed and signed official entry form and the entry fee must be presented to the director prior to the tournament. The tournament director reserves the rights to refund/refuse any entry fee.

The Winners and Events of the 2nd Annual 2002 Tuff Man Series Bass Tournament Weigh-In at the Bell County Expo Center Boat Show.

January 19, 2002
Randy Berenger (known as the Bassin Man)  was the M.C. for the Tourney Weigh-In.
"Thank You Randy"

46 Teams Competed - 34 came in for Weigh-In.

1st Place Won - $5,000.00
2nd Place Won - $1,200.00
3rd Place Won - $1,000.00
- AND -
Big Bass Pot Paid - $430.00
Once again it was a wonderful turn out as the contestants came in for weigh-in.
Thank You all for your support in this event. Hope you found a good deal on a boat,
preferably from Maine Outlet "of course", but most of all we hope you had a good time.
It was an exciting event as the contestants came in for weigh-in. 5 fish was the Tourney limit.
  But most everyone found that 2 was a good number.
However there "were" two teams that came in with the limit.
Wade and Jonathan Watts with a total weight of 8.72lbs.,
  and Windle Resse and Jimmy Bennet with a total weight of 13.12lbs.
The weather conditions out there on the Lake were very windy and cold,
but that didn't slow down these TUFF MAN TEAMS!

"CONGRATULATIONS ON BOTH YOUR WINS!!"
(And a special "Thank You" from this photographer)
Bit of Humor From the Photographer: I am all prepared for the event. Plenty of disc. Fully charged battery. Arrived early for good seating. Happily taking photos of contestants. "Then" in comes the largest and winning catch. Taking photos as they pull out their catch. Prize winning shot coming up. OH NO! Camera says Disc Full! I quickly pull out a new disc - having to turn off my camera to insert the new disc (as mine is well worn) other photographers still shooting. I have time. Insert the new disc - turn camera back on - press button for the shot - OH NO! No click - try again - No click! Look at screen -   it says disc error (bad disc). Winners starting to leave the stage. PANIC- What do I do now??!! I'm losing the shot. Jump up in the crowd of HUNDREDS of spectators - "WAIT - WAIT" I didn't get the shot! They go back up. Turn camera off - quickly insert a new disc - turn camera back on. Needless to say Our Prize winning LIVE  fish were turning a little "green around the gills as they gasp for air" I don't think they were quit as lively either. Quick - Got the shot! Dump them in the tank. A special Thank You again to the winners for their patience. And Thank You to Rick Smith for putting up with me. Hope everyone had a good time.
