Photos of Guitars

If you've got this far, then you've done well...This is a page devoted to the guitars I have come to know and love throughout the years.  All but one has managed to find its way out to the U.S., even though it meant taking three of them to pieces...a little scary.  Incidentally, if any of you need work doing, then the guy at Rainbow Music on College. Ave has done great jobs on three of mine for not a lot of money...

Anyway, if you're a guitar affecionado, you're in for a treat, and if you're not, then they're just pretty pictures (I think, anyway...).  So, in no particular order...

The beauty above is a telecaster thinline, made in Fender's squier protone factory in korea...don't let the label or place of manf. fool you - these little puppies will outplay the U.S. standards!

Below, is an Ibanez Blazer - from the first time they started making them.  I've never been able to date this guitar, but they stopped making these at the end of the '70s, and started reissuing them around five years back.  Beaten to death, but a lovely player...

Onto the acoustics...The black guitar below is a Yamaha APX-7.  It's an electro-acoustic that has served me well over a good many years.  It's not the best acoustic in the world (not shabby either, mind!), but it has great electrics and a really playable neck that's unbelievably easy to play.

The next acoustic is a Sigma DM-2 I bought in Cardiff about five years ago.  Sigma are the offshoot of Martin Guitars.  The good part is that you get around 80% of the quality for around 10% of the price (I like Yamaha stuff for similar reasons).  Anyway, the picture isn't great, but the sound of this guitar is incredible considering what it cost.

The final guitar in my collection is a Yamaha BB1500 Bass.  I'm not sure if they even sold these in the U.S., but basically this is a superb piece of work from Yamaha.  It's the four string version of the Nathan East series of basses.  I remember trying this in the stores after playing a Musicman Stingray (which was a gorgeous bass...should have bought it just for the resale...), and a few different Fenders.

Anyway, if any bass players need a recommendation, go for this if you ever get the chance.  I've seen the actual Nathan East 5 String signatures go for around $700 on Ebay, which is an absolute bargain - they list at well over $3000, and come in a beautiful translucent turquoise/topaz blue kind of color...if I had the money I'd buy one, but I'm just a poor TA...

And finally, just a picture of tribute really.  The photo below is of the guy who taught me pretty much all I know about guitars, and was a truly good man.  The guy to my left is my childhood best-bud, Mr. Tim Partridge, who is the best drummer I've ever been lucky enough to play with.  The fella to my right is Stu Calver.  His name probably won't ring a bell, but he toured with a bunch of British stars such as Cliff Richard, and actually turned down a backing vocal spot with Elton John at the Live Aid gig at Wembley Stadium back in '85.  Anyway, Stu died on October 2nd 2000, but has this kind of Obi-Wan kind of thing going...
