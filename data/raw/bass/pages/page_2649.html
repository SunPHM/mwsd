Florida Bass Fishing
Brag'n Board: December 2001

Enjoy viewing hundreds of pictures of Florida bass fishing trophy bass that were caught while wild shiner fishing on Lake Okeechobee by anglers from across the United States. Our Florida bass fishing trophy bass pictures are also cataloged under each month they were caught during the 2003, 2002, 2001, 2000, 1999 and 1998 calendar years. A Florida bass fishing report on amount of bass caught, area caught and other information is listed with each trophy bass picture. To ensure validity the angler's name, address, phone number and e-mail address is also listed with every Florida bass fishing report.

Lake Okeechobee is almost 15 feet above sea level. Tons of vegatation on the lake has made finding the fish difficult now than ever before. Baitfish are just about everywhere in this dense grass. Crappie fishing has been excellent. Trophy bass fishing should be getting better due to the spawn that should occur this month. Heavy wind conditions and cold fronts have slowed down the fishing lately.

Florida Bass Fishing Brag'n Board
December 30 & 31, 2001

Florida wild shiner bass fishing anglers Jeff and Pattee Bender and their son (Kentwood, MI - ph: 616-530-1908 - e-mal: pbender@uniformcolor.com) caught and released 8 bass up to 6 lbs, 1 channel catfish and a pickerel during during 2 half days of wild shiner bass fishing on December 30 & 31, 2001 with Cap'n Ron. Jeff caught the 6 lb'er. We fished the North Shore and Monkey Box areas of Lake Okeechobee.

Florida Bass Fishing Brag'n Board
6 1/4 lb'er - December 13 & 14, 2001

Florida wild shiner bass fishing anglers Jason Bayes (Lakeland, FL - ph: 863-646-1490 - e-mail: bayes1@mindspring.com) and father-in-law Joe Quattrocki (Clearwater, FL - ph: 727-536-0675) caught 2 catfish, 1 pickeral and 5 bass that included a 6 1/4 lb'er that Jason landed during 2 half days of wild shiner bass fishing on December 13 & 14, 2001 with Cap'n Ron. Fishing was poor due to cold front and windy conditions. We fished the North Shore and Monkey Box areas of Lake Okeechobee. All bass were released.

This was a big bass that only scaled out at a mere 6 1/4 lbs.
this bass was in good health but lacked girth which accounted for it's lack of weight.
