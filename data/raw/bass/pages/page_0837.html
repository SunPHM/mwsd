REAL TIME WATER FLOW DATA IN MARYLAND

Real time water flow data is available 24 hours/day from the United States Geological Survey. We have inserted links in the report below for many of the streams and rivers discussed in the fishing report. For additional stations, go to the main real time USGS water flow page .

Casselman River: As of July 15, 2002 the Long Term Median Flow (LTMF) in Grantsville, Maryland is 31 cfs with a flow of 28 cfs and staging at 0.98 feet. Caddis (#14 - 16) and #14 light cahills are a few fly options.

Click for the latest real time stream flows on the Casselman.

North Branch of the Potomac River: As of July 15, 2002, at the Barnum gauging station the LTMF is 141 cfs with a flow of 185 cfs and staging at 2.85 feet. The tailwater area is fishing well. Flies to fish include small blue winged olives, caddis, and stoneflies.

Click for the latest real time stream flows on the North Branch of the Potomac at Barnum.

Savage River: As of July 15, 2002 in Bloomington the LTMF is 52 cfs with a flow of 48 cfs and staging at 0.64 feet. The tailwater area is fishing well. Brookies and browns have been taking #14 deer hair caddis with a yellow body and # 16 beadhead pheasant tails. Sulphurs (mayfly) and green stoneflies (lime sallies) were recently hatching in good numbers in the Trophy Management area. Blue winged olives and red quills are also good options.

Click for the latest real time stream flows on the Savage River near Bloomington, MD.

Youghiogheny: As of July 15, 2002 the LTMF near Oakland is 72 cfs with a flow of 142 cfs and staging at 2.50 feet. The LTMF in Friendsville is 225 cfs with a flow of 342 cfs and staging at 2.86 feet. This river has been fishing well with deer hair caddis, blue winged olives, and tricos on cooler evenings.

Click for the latest real time stream flows on the Youghiogheny near Oakland, MD.

Gunpowder River: The river is up about 3 inches. Sulphurs are just about finished and the focus is now on terrestrials. Size 16 ants, parachute ants, small hoppers, and beetles. In the afternoons #18 to 20 caddis have been effective as well. Another option is a #18 Griffith's gnat. Long leaders (12 feet) with 5X or 6X tippet are suggested by local guides. As of July 15, 2002 the LTMF at Little Falls at Blue Mount is 44 cfs with a flow of 14 cfs and staging at 0.18 feet.

Brown trout have been tagged with visual implant tags just behind the left eye socket (pictured on the left - click for a larger view). If you catch a tagged brown trout please contact Central Regional Manager, Charlie Gougeon, at 410-442-2080 or via e-mail at cgougeon2@dnr.state.md.us with the following information: date and location of the catch, tag number, tag color, kept or released, and any other information you feel is important.

Susquehanna River above Conowingo: Some smallmouth bass have been caught using medium to large shiners, crankbaits, and plastic worms. Poppers are a good option in the morning and evening. Crappie fishing has been slow.

Nontidal Potomac River: River conditions at the Williamsport Water Treatment Plant are 1.5 feet and holding, cloudy, and 78 degrees Fahrenheit as of July 15, 2002. Fishing has been fair with catches of smallmouth bass, walleye, and catfish being reported. In addition to live bait, small tubes and grubs bounced on the bottom have been effective for smallmouth bass. There are a few trophy bass being caught, but most are less than 12 inches. Richard Elliot caught a 3 pound, 7.25 ounce smallmouth bass using a hellgrammite in the Conococheague last week. Mike Fiorita, Sr. had a nice one weighing 4 pounds, 7.25 ounces. It was caught below the P.E. Dam on a stonecat. Mark Burgan reeled in a 5 pound, 10.5 ounce largemouth bass at Big Pool. Don Cosden of Shady Side, Maryland is pictured on the right with a beautiful smallmouth bass he caught on a recent float trip using a flyrod. A Jim Anderson weighed in a 16 pound 3.75 ounce catfish he caught at Dam 4 on chicken liver. A few other nice whisker fish were also weighed in at a nearby Maryland Sport Fishing Tournament Citation Center.

LAKES, PONDS, AND RESERVOIRS

Deep Creek Lake (Garrett County): The evening bluegill bite has been great with many 10 to 10.5 inchers being checked in at a nearby Maryland Sport Fishing Tournament Citation Center. Young angler, Jessica Klotz, caught the largest one reported last week to the citation center. It measured 11 inches and was caught on a worm. Worms fished in 3 to 12 feet under the docks has been the ticket. Smallmouth bass, largemouth bass, and chain pickerel continued to bite minnows and dark colored tube jigs fished in 10 to 14 feet of water. Ryan Pavol (left) recently caught a 3 pound bass using a Gitzit. The best time to catch them has been in the evening. Fishing for northern pike has been fair with minnows. Places to try include Paradise Point, Green Glade, and North Glade in the grass. Bill Ream of Swanton, Maryland caught a 14 pound, 8 ounce northern pike that measured 40 inches. Walleye fishing has been slightly better than for northern pike.

Information on whitewater boating releases is available on the Deep Creek Hydro Hotline at (814) 533-8911 or online at URL: http://www.deepcreekhydro.com/

Liberty Reservoir (Carroll County): Liberty Reservoir is down 19.92 feet as of July 16, 2002. Shoreline bass fishing has been good using medium to large shiners, crayfish, chicken liver, crankbaits, and 10-inch power worms. Fathead minnows, Mini Mites, and maggots have been catching bluegills and crappies at Nicodemus Bridge.

Little Seneca Lake (Montgomery County): Nighttime fishing opportunities for the remainder of this year are July 17, August 22, and September 18th.

Loch Raven Reservoir (Baltimore County): Loch Raven is down 5.28 feet as of July 15, 2002. Loch Raven Fishing Center reported that anglers have been catching good numbers of fat bluegills. White perch averaging 8 to 10 inches have been caught trolling a spinner hook with a night crawler in 12 to 15 feet of water. Plastic worms, pig and jigs, or stickbaits worked over points, stumps, and grassbeds in 15 to 20 feet of water have been catching bass. Fishing for yellow perch and crappies has been a little slow.

The Maryland Geological Survey has a bathymetry map on their website.

Piney Run Park (PRP): PRP staff reports that bass fishing remains excellent. Anglers have been using 4 to 5 inch Cinco and Stik-O worms. Most catches are averaging 2 pounds but some have been up to 5 pounds. Places to target include the inside and outside edges of the hydrilla beds especially at daybreak and dusk as well as overcast days. Several 3 to 6 pound catfish were caught using chicken liver and nightcrawlers. Nightcrawlers and small crankbaits have been catching yellow perch (some up to 13 inches).

Prettyboy Reservoir (Baltimore County): Prettyboy reservoir is down 30.41 feet as of July 15, 2002. Bass fishing has been productive using plastic worms, crankbaits, and surface plugs fished off points and bars.

WSSC Triadelphia and Rocky Gorge Reservoirs: Water temperature was approximately 80 degrees last weekend with 3 feet of visibility. Anglers have been catching largemouth and smallmouth bass at both reservoirs fishing structure. Specifically mid-reservoir at Triadelphia, bass have been holding on points, underwater humps and steep rocky channel banks in 8 - 20 feet of water. Shad colored crankbaits, jigs, and Carolina-rigged plastics have been productive. Anglers using jigs have caught a few catfish while bass fishing. Jigs, worms, and crankbaits have been catching crappies. Live worms or minnows, crankbaits, and jigs have caught some walleyes.
