Florida Bass Fishing
Brag'n Board: April 2001

April and its needed showers turned on Okeechobee's trophy bass. The lake is still very low, however the showers have added several inches of water to it. Best fishing is still in the canals. Expect a major bass spawn this month on the full and new moons. Trophy bass forecast is expected to be excellent this month due to the low water conditions which has the trophy bass trapped in the deep water canals. Harney Pond Canal where the last BASSMASTER Eastern Invitational tournament was held (Jan. 11 - 13, 2001) and won from with a 44-pound, 4-ounce catch that included a trophy 9-pound, 7-ounce and an 8-pound, 5-ounce bass. Harney Pond Canal where all of our fishing is being conducted is producing all of our trophy bass for our clients.

Florida Wild Shiner Bass Fishing Brag'n Board
7 1/2 lb.and 6 lb'er - April 23 - 27, 2001

Florida wild shiner trophy bass fishing anglers Don McGowan (Walnut Point, PA - ph: 610-392-0538 - e-mail: dmcgowan@telcordia.com) and Ed Pollich (Farmingville, NY - ph: 631-981-0333) caught and released 63 bass that included a 7 1/2 lb. and a 6 lb'er during 5 days (April 23 - 27, 2001) of wild shiner bass fishing with Cap'n Ron. Ed caught the 7 1/2 lb. and the 6 lb'er. An unusual cold front and windy weather for this time of the year slowed down the fishing action. We boated about 200 fish counting the bowfin (mudfish) up to 10 lbs, catfish and gar. I fished the Point Of The Reef out on the lake when the wind was down. Fished the Harney Pond Canal and Moore Haven Canal areas with good results when the wind was blowing real hard. Both the 7 1/2 lb. and the 6 lb'er were caught in the Harney Pond Canal. Ed caught the 7 1/2 lb'er on a worm (Red Shar Green Glitter) which he was casting while we were wild shiner fishing. Seems best fishing is still in the Harney Pond Canal area of Lake Okeechobee.
