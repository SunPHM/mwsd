9-string and me (below) is a converted Aria Pro II 9-String Bass...A poor man's 12ver ;)
(Originally submitted by Pekka back on Jan. 22, 2001 before he recently bought his Hamer 12ver in early 2003!]


First let me thank you for an excellent site! I've been insomniac since
discovering it back in 2001! The Aria conversion has only
9-strings but the basic principle is same as on a 12-string bass.
I purchased the bass from my friend a couple of years ago. It's an early
80's Aria Pro II which was converted into a 9-stringed beast! It has E,
A & D as fundamentals and double octaves for each fundamental string.
EMG active electronics and EMG bass expander (which cuts mids and
increases high and lows). The bridge is self-made.
I too had dreamed of a 12ver ever since knowing it existed (I remember
staring at the cover of "At Budokan" and thinking "what the hell is
that?") So, getting this bass has helped me a lot! I use dropped-D tuning
all the time and string gauges are: 095, 075 & 060 for fundamentals and
026, 032 & 040 for octaves. My rig is 1973 Hiwatt Custom 100 tube amp
and Hartke 4x10? cab. It works well though the Hartke is a bit too hi-fi
for my liking (and it doesn't look half as cool as the Hiwatt!). Haven't
had a chance to experiment with two amplifiers but playing with a clean
sound has worked fine enough.

"I was sick and tired of paying $5 a pop for drinks at nightclubs in L.A. I thought, 'Hmm.. all I have to do is find 3 other women in L.A. who have amazing musical chops and love Cheap Trick. Les Fradkin
Edison Lighthouse 
Parker, Colorado
Dean Rhapsody 12

This is Les Fradkin, the 12-string bassist of Edison Lighthouse, the group which enjoyed the worldwide hit "Love Grows Where My Rosemary Goes" in 1970. I play the Dean Rhapsody 12 Bass exclusively on stage and in the studio on our new recordings. The following is my bio:

"Once upon a time, the 45RPM hit single was king. In that long ago day, Edison Lighthouse was one of the most phenomenally successful groups of the1970's selling over 15 million records worldwide. The band originated in England as an outgrowth of the successful group The Flying Machine, which had a US top #5 hit in November 1969 with "Smile A Little Smile For Me." featuring Les Fradkin on bass, mellotron and vocals.The producer of that smash hit assembled a new group to record a new song called "Love Grows (Where My Rosemary Goes)" and called it Edison Lighthouse.  Les Fradkin returned from New York City to join this new British group as an original recording member of Edison Lighthouse.

The recording of "Love Grows" proved to be a worldwide success reaching the #5 chart position on Billboard's Hot 100 singles chart in  February 1970 and ultimately becoming a #1 hit selling over a total of 8 million copies worldwide. In fact, for the entire year of 1970, it was the #2 selling pop record in the U.K.  The formula "feel-good music" hit sound created with that smash proved to be so successful that the Edison crew recorded other material under different group names which duplicated the sensation of their first hit together. As White Plains, their single of "My Baby Loves Lovin'" reached #13 in the U.S and #9 in the U.K. Under the moniker of "The Brotherhood Of Man", their "United We Stand" reached #15 in the U.S. and #1 in the U.K.in 1970.

 In addition to these lasting accomplishments, the group also recorded two other hits, "She Works In A Woman's Way" and "It's Up To You Petula" which both charted top 5 in Japan and top 60 U.S in 1971 under the Edison Lighthouse name. In the late 1970's Les starred on lead guitar as "George Harrison" in the Original Cast of Beatlemania on Broadway in New York City and on tour all over the world.

In 2001, original member Les Fradkin assembled a new version of Edison Lighthouse. The group is enjoying renewed success on stage with the dramatic inclusion of "Love Grows" in the sound track of the smash hit motion picture "Shallow Hal".  Les' exciting Edison Lighthouse show features 20 "one hit wonder" hits that Les has performed on plus new material from the brand new Edison Lighthouse Greatest Hits CD "The Soundtrack Of Your Youth" In 2002, Les discovered the wonders of the 12-string bass after trying a Dean Rhapsody at a local Denver-area music store. He fell in love with the power of the instrument and has adopted it for all his Edison Lighthouse concerts and recording.
