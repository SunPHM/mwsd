KILLARNEY COUNTRY - COME FISH WITH US

Killarney boasts of one of the most unique fishing areas in Ontario. We offer many very distinct habitats, from sheltered and secluded back water bays to wild rice shorelines with long weed beds combined with dramatic drop-offs. Numerous shoal areas and reefs that hide many different species of fish in our cool clean waters. Off shore the extremely contoured bottom can reach down to 400 feet, but by contrast, the back channels and protected inlets of Killarney provide miles of secure and safe small craft fishing. Big Waters produce Big Fish !

Ice Fishing at the Sportsman's is hot! Summers turquoise waters are covered by winters 2' of blue ice. We fish Pike, Lake Trout, Perch and Splake. Canadian Sport Fishing Magazine refers to Killarney as the "Splake Capital of the World". Splake is a hybrid back cross of Lake Trout and Brook Trout. We jig in 50' of water in heated ice fish huts. A 6 to 12 lb trout hits your hand held line like a ton of bricks. We supply transportation to the huts, ice fishing rigs, bait, etc... Our off season fishing package prices apply as we substitute the 25 hp boat for an ice hut.

Spring Ice Out - May 1, 2006 Season Opener

Northern Pike -Trout - Salmon - Walleye - Jumbo Perch Spring is a favorite time for Sportsman's Inn regulars. The Splake move into 3' to 6' of water and we fish with ultra lights. Splake have very hard mouths, so sharpen your hooks! The limit is only 3 per day, but it is common to catch and release, catch and lose, 15 - 20 trout on the light rigs. It's more fun than a fisherman should be allowed to have! The Pike are ferocious in the spring and are also in the shallows. Large Pike (20 - 30 lbs) are scavengers, eating dead or half dead fish. European dead bait techniques work tremendously well. All baits are a bit smaller this early in the season. We don't get many "hammer handles" our chunky Northerns average 6 - 8 lbs. In 2001 the Johnston party landed and released 3 Pike over 20 lbs, averaging 47" on the morning of May 12th. The excitement of hooking into a large Pike in the Spring in 2 - 3 feet of water, then having it explode on the surface is hard to match. When your fishing our waters that predominately have large Pike, don't expect to get one every cast like you see on TV from the little lakes in Canada's far North. Big Waters Produce Big fish!

Last Saturday in June - the magic date - marks the beginning of bass season. We recommend the outlying shoals and reefs for small mouth and the back bay lily pads for large mouth. We have great numbers of worm free bass in our cold, clear waters. Bass season opens Sat. June 26, 2006

Summer is beautiful - Great fishing fun for the whole family, kids can perch fish right off the many local docks. Large mouth & small mouth bass fishing is prime. Chinook salmon and steel head begin hitting in early summer only 1 to 3 miles from the harbour. The pike head for a little deeper and cooler water and the baits get larger. Splake are also deep...35 - 70 feet. Musky fishing steadily improves as the summer wanes. Swim off the rocks at shore lunch, pick fresh blue berries and enjoy the wonders of Killarney Country.

Killarney Country is big, it's wild, it's untamed. Until recently, you could only reach it by steamer or private yacht. Now it's an accessible wilderness. The village of Killarney was settled in 1820 and is smack in the middle of Ontario's Wilderness Paradise, next to Killarney Provincial Park and astride a quiet channel of Northern Georgian Bay. Beyond are the white quartzite Killarney mountains, a myriad of inland lakes, a sanctuary for fish and game. In this unique region, so special, the Ontario government has set it aside as a protected recreation area, the Sportsman's Inn is a focal point for all outdoor adventurers.

Fall " the season of the golden wolf" Muskellunge fishing at its finest...can you top our most recent record of 45.5lbs - 54" ... Cooler waters shallow the fish once again, Pike also hit in high gear prior to their winter chill out. Muskellunge fishing season opens on the 3rd Sat. in June, ( 19, 2006), To ensure trophy record class muskellunge fishing, our Ontario Ministry of Natural Resources has increased SIZE LIMITS here in Northern Georgian Bay for Muskie, to minimum TOTAL length of 54" (total length is a measure from the tip of the mouth with the jaws closed to the tip of the tail with the fin lobes compressed to give the maximum possible total length.) This is the largest size/length increase of any where in the Province of Ontario, Quote "where muskellunge have the potential to grow to extremely large and world record sizes" this body of water will be managed to provide "trophy" or "record class" fishing "Unquote.

The Ontario record for Muskie is from Georgian Bay, at 29.5kg (65 pounds)

DOWN RIGGING & SALMON-TROUT CHARTERS

Killarney N/W Georgian Bay 4.5 hours from Toronto

We have been told by experts that our recently developed/discovered Salmon and Trout Fishery rivals any other fishery found in the Great Lakes. Beginning at the East Killarney Harbour entrance, 3/4 of a mile from here, in 40 - 60 feet we consistently find huge numbers of Salmon and Trout, May thru early September.

14 - 18lbs Chinook are average size and most common.

Chinook, Great Lakes Steel head, Natural Rainbows, Atlantic Salmon, Lake Trout, Coho, Pinks and Browns are extremely plentiful. When running 4 electric down riggers, stacked, it is a daily occurrence to have multiple fish on. To the West in Fraser Bay (9 miles away) a resident school of Lake Trout suggested to be 300 - 400,000 in number will light up the screen for a mile square. Fishing the edges of this school of Lake Trout in 40 - 90 feet, produces limits every time.
