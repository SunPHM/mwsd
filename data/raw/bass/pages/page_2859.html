HUITES ADVENTURE

 

"What can I say.  I took my wife, my business partner Greg, my Canadian Sales Rep and his wife to good old Huites.  I have been there before so I had a lot to gauge the trip by.  I have experience with the fish, the transportation, the food and the rooms, etc.

The fishing was just short of incredible.  We had been told to be ready for a great topwater bite, but (we) didn't find one.  Other groups did though.  We killed them on spinner baits instead.  When we got back for lunch or dinner we heard that the other boats killed them on topwater.  So just because you hear this or that you have to remember this is still FISHING!

We caught over 40 bass on flukes by lunch the second day, that evening we caught over 60 more by actual count.  The 100 fish a day could be done easily if you got on the right bite.  For some it was topwater, for me it was a Terminator and Wild Bill's spinner baits, for my wife it was "Oh Son" Flukes.  At the same time one guy was killing them on a fly rod, another on 14 inch worms and yet another on a Mepp's trout spinner.

Now let's talk big fish.  Everybody and I mean everybody caught big fish, except me.  My biggest was a 7lb-6oz.  The guy with the Zebco 33 and the Mepp's spinner caught a 8lb 15oz hawg.


Ken Cross at Huites, October 1999

At lunch and dinner I had to listen to women who didn't fish, men who didn't fish, all talk about their big fish.  I just could not connect.  Bill and I were in the same boat on the same point and on Bill's first cast he got a 10lb-6oz Huites monster bass and I got a 2lb'er!  What can I say?

The days were great, sunny and lots of fish.  We really got them on the flukes and the spinnerbaits, right up on the rock walls.  Great fishing and really strong fish. 

A 3 lb Mexican bass fights like a 5 lb Arizona smallmouth.  So tonight as I write this, I am eating a hamburger, yesterday I was eating a T-Bone steak at Campo Buena Vista Lodge and drinking Margarita's until bed time. Ask me if I would rather be home catching up on bills or fighting hundreds of fish that snapped 20# test like it was thread.  I did lose the big one, my wife lost 3 big ones, my partner lost a giant one.  You have to check your knots and try to keep the bass from getting down into the thick heavy brush.

So that's my story, fish and lots of them and it can only get better.  There was no net problem that I could see beyond a couple of guys trying to put them out at 4 pm instead of 6 pm.  No problemo.  Great trip, food and fishing.  If you don't go, you are missing the experience of a lifetime.  We had two days where we caught by actual count over 150 bass of which many were 4 to 5 pounds.  My wife and I went through one full bottle of Kick'n Bass garlic in one day.  In Arizona a bottle will last me about 4 months.  Tell me that we didn't go through a lot of flukes and worms. 

Wow!  Saving my pennies already for next year.  I took $20 out of my wallet and put it in the Huites jar.  Got to save for what you want.  And I want to go back to Huites with Wild Bill!  Thanks Bill for some time in paradise.

Ken Kross (Cosmic)
