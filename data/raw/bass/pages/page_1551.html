This photo was submitted by Chris Matthes and was taken at Cheap Trick's show on 21 Jan 2001 at the 9:30 Club in Washington, DC.   Tom is being presented with a T12SBP T-shirt.  Here is an excerpt from an email  I received from Chris that best describes the events of that night...

I walked in the door 5 minutes ago, but wanted to give you a quick update:  Got to the sound check right at 5:30, just as they were finishing up. Rick & Bun E. were walking out the door, and they were still tweaking Tom & Robin's levels. Tom came to the wings where I was and said "hi".  I introduced myself, gave him a quick run-down about the site and presented him with the shirt and your letter/picture in a sealed envelope. He said he'd definitely wear the shirt, and was genuinely appreciative for it. Very cool!  While Harmony Riley (the opener, Rick N's sons play drums/guitar) were sound checking, Tom went back up behind the Bass cabs and put the shirt in his overnight bag. he opened the letter and I watched him read the entire thing.  Carla (Dragotti , CT's road manager) came to take him and Robin to dinner, and he thanked me again as he passed.  

I was also  invited to the After Show upstairs with the band and 8 other people. It was a nice, small informal setting outside the dressing rooms, and I got to talk to Tom for at least 10 minutes sitting down on a couch.  He seemed very flattered by the letter, and said that he will DEFINITELY check out the page. I mentioned that you had been conducting a few interviews and recently spent a few hours talking to Paul and Adrian Chandler.  I said that it would be great to see him on the page, and that that would be the ultimate (no Hamer pun intended) for that site. He said that things are crazy for the next few weeks, but he'd consider it. I think that you could have a good shot with that one! Tom was very easy to talk to, and I think he'd be a great interview subject. I managed to get one picture of me with Tom, holding up my 12 string bass page shirt ( I took it off so it would show up in the photo!). That'll be coming your way along with the others.

Thanks for the inspiration, and I'll be in touch soon - it's gonna be a long day at work tomorrow, but totally worth it!

Have a great week,

Chris
