Minutes of the 15th meeting of the Mr.Hanky Gastronomic Society.

28th-September-2002

Held at Chez Green.

Another Curry Club day arrived with great excitement in the air, and that was only the adult members. The junior members had been plotting with secret notes all week to provide their usual cocktails and very good they were, very cooling and refreshing, particularly in view of the vindaloo. We think the same recipes as last meeting, but with a little tweak here and there to accommodate Mrs KNH's tastebuds. But did they look like the pictures? The day started with the sun rising in the east, not that any of our members were up to see it…….correction…… our Chairperson had intimated a working day could be on the cards and his car did go missing rather early. (The writer is in no way trying to show that he was also up early as well).

Despite some consternation during the cooking time, most of it went well but after only 50 minutes we found ourselves 60 minutes behind in the preparation?? Work that out if you can! The impossible we can do……miracles take 60 minutes longer.

AP managed to splash turmeric over 20% of his very best golfing trousers, and 99% of PS's cooker, so any suggestions on turmeric stain removal would be appreciated. Otherwise it will cost him £2.99 for new trousers and £999.99 for a new cooker. Now this is considered to be a MAJOR incident in the CURRIE world and PRESIDENT BUSH and COLIN (pronounced ko as in go and lyn, stupid man!!!! ) POWELL should be made aware that any HANKY PANKY is not good for your image. For those who haven't tasted Chicken Dhansak, you guessed it, it tasted just like Chicken Dhansak. Lamb Vindaloo

A Vindaloo to behold. In the best tradition very hot, extra hot, no triple hot and more. Not that this had anything to do with the recipe. Half way through the cooking our official taster declared 'not much bite, have we done this right?' Out with AP's Hotty Bits and into the pot with gay abandon. Then with backs to the wall, well the Welsh Dresser…..too late lads he's gay as well…. someone suggested that during the cooking it would get more bite. It did, and some. Well for anyone who knows the locale, our playing fields are high up and usually very windy, not usually vindaloo windy. Sunday morning football will never be the same again. We have heard that the referee is recovering slowly and looking for two shifty characters; one carrying a mobile and a red book, the other still ravishing a win by QPR over Bristol City.

(text message charged at £99.99) KNH did up his effort score in view of his onion peeling marathon, poor chap, no stamina, It's enough to make a grown man cry. So are the onions.

Aloo Mattar

Rather a plastic flavour, but this was put down to the fact that whilst cutting the corner off the bag of frozen peas the corner of the bag disappeared. Potatoes diced so delicately into precise one inch (25.40mm) cubes you would have thought PB had brought a micrometer. Pity he wouldn't lend it to AP to prepare a one inch (25.40mm) cube of root ginger. Do the people who write these recipes know how root ginger grows? All knobbly bits! Perhaps we should go to Brussels and get the rules changed. If we must have uniform bananas, why can't we have root ginger in 25.40mm cubes.

Action:

CS to be sponsored for a trip to Mauritius to investigate and lobby MEPs. Mauritius? Surely you mean Brussels? No, I don't like Brussels.

Whole Roast Sea Bass

Right on the dot of 15.00 hours according to the meal plan PB arrived, refused a beer and asked for coffee(must be ill) and then quickly dived into the preparation of crushed garlic, chopped parsley and other bits in the recipe. Communication being one of our good points we waited 20 minutes to tell him that the Sea Bass had been given an early start and was sat in the fridge just longing to be popped into the oven at 200°C/400°F(Gas mark 6) Under the trades description act we could be in trouble here again. Due to PS's weak stomach and hatred of eyes looking at her whilst eating, 'off with their heads' was the order. Anything waving about causes her dizziness so the second order was 'off with their tails' Whole Roast Sea Bass then becomes Mogified Sea Bass. Get it ..Modified……Mogified. Those that don't just forget it. Lots of sea salt , lots of parsley, lots of butter. Not too much garlic, she hates that as well (heaven help us) and only thirty minutes in the oven. Wonderful. Selection of fresh vegetables, carrots, baby sweet corn and Uncle Ron's last of the season homegrown runner beans cooked to perfection made this dish even more wonderful. Anyone interested in recipe for Uncle Ron's last of the season homegrown runner beans take note: take newspaper wrapping off, wash, slice in automatic bean slicer gadget, discard edgy bits and cook in boiling salted water for 6 minutes 37 seconds. Not forgetting to put the water in a pot first. Followed by the beans, but not before the salt. This information is purely academic as you are not likely to get any of Uncle Ron's last of the season homegrown runner beans until next year and even then it would be easier for you to win the National Lottery.(search engines go….)

Sautéd Sea Salt Potatoes

Lightly boiled new potatoes, peeled and cut into chunky quarters. Pan cooked in olive oil until golden and then unsalted butter until crispy, with a good helping of parsley and sea salt.

Action

Cook three times more next time or cancel AP's official taster's contract.

Nutty Apple Crumble

Simple recipe à la Gary Rhodes Classics. Crumble baked in oven separately to give more crunch.

Should have had Cherry Crumble as well but no cherries available except in tins and we don't like packaged food do we?
