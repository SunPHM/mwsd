Striped Bass Fly Fishing On The Roanoke River


The annual return of anadromous (adj.[Gk. anadromos running upward]: ascending rivers from the sea for breeding) fish to the Roanoke River in North Carolina is truly one of the most bountiful flyangling opportunities in the southeast.  Hickory shad followed by striped bass provide almost non stop fly fishing action for about ninety days every spring... more >>>

Archived Fly Fishing Report 02/25/02
 For the most current report >>>

The news up at Weldon is that the shad fishing is still s-l-o-w.  There are a few hickory shad in this section of the river, but only a few.  If you are determined to fish up that way, I would suggest looking for slower pockets on the sunny side of the river.

Further downstream it is a different story.  Down at the US 258 bridge there is a respectable showing of hickory shad.  They are not as thick as they are going to get, but there are more than enough to keep a fly angler entertained.

Down in the "Three Rivers" area there is a mix of small stripers and hickory shad.  You'll have to fish real slow (and deep) for the stripers, but they are there.  If the striper is what you seek, you may have a much better chance out in the sound.  Both the Sound Bridge ((Rt 32) and the US 17 bridge have been producing plenty of stripers (although the action has not been consistent every day).  There are also stripers scattered about the "stumps" of Swan Bay.

There are also some hickory shad up in the Cashie River.  There are a few fish all the way up at the US 17 Bridge in Windsor,  but the better fishing is down below Sans Souci Ferry.  Down around the "Thoroughfare" you might find a mix of stripers and shad.

The full moon is almost upon us, it will not be long before the shad fishing is wide open.

Harry Hall  sefly.com
