Raezers Edge Speaker Cabinets are designed for a broad dynamic range.  The sound is clear and focused at all levels.  A deep full bass, well defined, adds richness to the total range of the instrument.  A cabinet you can push without distortion.

Rich Raezer started building speaker cabinets to fill a void in the market, a small cabinet with a big sound.  Many musicians have complained that in order to get a great sound from their guitar, they had to carry about 80 pounds of equipment.  After building many cabinets, Rich found the solution to the weight and size problem without sacrificing a big accurate tone.  The final result is a compact closed-back, tuned, ported cabinet that produces a warm, focused tone with no break-up.

"A Big Focused Sound in a Small Portable Cabinet"

The NY 8 has been scaled down to meet the needs of players who require an extremely portable cabinet whilst preserving a rich sound.  It's a great cabinet to use as a sidekick to the Stealth 12, Stealth 10 or Twin 8.  By itself it's surprisingly big in tone.  It's warm and aggresive and, when turned up at volume it's almost like a 12.  It works best with higher power amps - 150 watts and above as a single unit. In combination with other cabinets, it needs less power.  It features one 8" speaker @ 8 ohms, 225 watts total, 20lbs, 12" W x 15" H x 9" D, range is 55Hz to 5k Hz.                   Price £440

The Twin 8 is fitted with 2 x 125 watt speakers.  The 125 is very warm and tube like.  Being a 4 ohm cabinet it accomodates lower powered amps.  Features 2x8" 125 watt speakers = 250 watts total, 25lbs, 19.5" W x 15" H x 10" D, range 50 Hz - 5k Hz         Price £550

The Twin 8 Tower is the most powerfull of the RE guitar amps (400 watts).  The sound is very aggresive and focused like the Stealth 12.  Being a tower cabinet it has a smaller footprint on stage.  Features 2x8" speakers, 400 watts peak, 33lbs, 14" W x 21.5" H x 11" D  Price £572 

The Stealth 10 is 10lbs lighter than the 12.  Tonal characteristics are similar to the Twin 8 in that it is tube like and sensitive.  Many players use them in pairs with stereo amps.  On it's own it is a perfect cabinet for 6 and 7 string guitars.  Features one 10" 8 ohm speaker, 225 watts, 22lbs, 15.5 W x 17 H x 11 D, range 50 Hz - 5k Hz                Price £495

The Stealth 12 is the original and most popular cabinet of the RE range to date. The cabinet was designed specifically for 6 and 7 string archtop guitar.  The new model has a highly efficient 100db speaker. This sensitivity allows for lower and higher watt amps to perform well. The cabinet design is the template for all RE cabinets.  Features 1x12" speaker @ 8ohms, 300 watts, 32lbs, 17" W x 18.5" H x 10.5" D, range 50Hz-5k Hz            Price £525

The 12 ER (Extended Range) was developed to enhance the acoustic tone of the nylon string and archtop guitar.  The tweeter volume control in the"off" position gives the guitar the normal be-bop tone similar to the standard Stealth 12.  The volume control increases the amount of tweeter.  This is very useful for vocals, trad jazz, and acoustic/nylon string performances.  It's like having 3 cabinets in one.  Features 1x12" speaker with tweeter, 250 watts, 33lbs, 17" W x 18.5" H x 11.75" D, range 47Hz-20k Hz                                       Price £682

The Bass 10 is a very popular cabinet.  Being so portable, this is a big favourite.  The tone is warm and natural and can cut through any band with no problems.  Upright bass players comment on the way it brings out the natural tone of the bass and has lots of punch.  The tonal range falls well with the 4 string bass.  These cabinets are designed to give an accurate reproduction of the instrument without colouring.  Features 10" speaker (8 ohms), 200 watts, 30lbs, 14" W x 17" H x 11" D, range 50 Hz-5k Hz                                    Price £495 

The Bass 12 comes in two speaker models: the 300 watt and the 400 watt.  The 300 watt is great for upright bass and is very warm and natural in tone.  The 400 watt is best for electric bass as well as upright.
