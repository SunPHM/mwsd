Evers wins Lake Eufaula BASSMASTER Tour, Louisiana's Boler continues run for 2003 Classic

   Roger Boler didn’t win the CITGO BASSMASTER Tour stop at Lake Eufaula in early March. He didn’t even make the top-10 cut after the second day.
   But the Slidell pro’s 21st-place finish continued a consistent season that if continued will make him the third Louisiana angler to make the 2003 BASS Masters Classic set for New Orleans in August.
   Boler’s Lake Eufaula finish with a two-day bag of 29 pounds, 7 ounces garnered him a $4,600 paycheck and placed him in the 15th position in the Angler of the Year standings.
   The angler's top finish this season was at Florida's Lake Okeechobee, when Boler took second.
   There are five more Tour events, and if Boler remains in the top 40 after the last event, he’ll qualify to fish the Classic scheduled for Aug. 1-3. -Greg Hackney of Oak Ridge and Minden’s Homer Humphreys already have qualified for that event through the BASSMASTER Central Open circuit.
   The Lake Eufaula tournament, which wrapped up March 9, was won by Oklahoma’s Edwin Evers.
   The 28-year-old pro finished with a final-day limit of 17 pounds, 7 ounces, to outdistance the rest of the field by just more than 3 pounds.
   Evers, whose four-day total was 73-5, took home $100,000 for the win.
   The young angler’s fish came from a large, shallow bay where Evers camped out for the entire tournament, targetting bass moving into spawn.
   The fish were caught on a combination of Mad Man lizards, Storm Sub-Warts, Stanley spinnerbaits and Hart buzzbaits.
   “I just kept circling, throwing whatever felt right at the time,” Evers said.
   But while the angler may have constantly changed lures, he never considered moving from the area where he was fishing.
   “I’ve learned that there are a whole lot more bass in an area than I can imagine,” Evers said. “I knew that this place was just loaded up with an awful lot of fish. So, I wasn’t about to move.
   “The fish just kept getting shallower and shallower, and I just tried to stay with them.”
   Charlie Hartley of Ohio finished in second place with 70-3, while Kentucky’s Mark Menendez slipped from the third-day lead to third with 62-1.
   Rounding out the top six was California’s Mike Reynolds, 58-9; Tom Biffle of Oklahoma, 54-8; and Florida’s Peter Thliveros, 49-5.
