Weekly Instrument-making Classes in Norwich
2005/6

There are two weekly evening classes in stringed instrument making held during term time in my workshop in Norwich.
Sundays 6 - 8pm and Mondays 7 - 9pm

Autumn term: 5th Sept - 13th Dec (Half-term 24th Oct - 1st Nov) 12 weeks
Winter term: 8/9th Jan - 2/3rd April (Half-term 26/27th Feb.) 12 weeks
Summer term: 10th April - 11th July (Half-term 29/30th May) 13 weeks

The Workshop

I have run these classes in one venue or another in Norwich for over twenty years. During which time lots of first time and some more experienced students have made numerous stringed instruments very successfully. It has been particularly useful for music students at the university who have learned to play viol on the university's instruments and also been able to make their own instrument to take away for use after their course is over.

Among the instruments which have been made are: treble, tenor, bass, great dooble-bass and seven string viola-da-gambas, lutes of all sizes and types, violins, violas and cellos, hurdy-gurdies, medieval harp, dulcimers and renaissance guitars. However I specialise in early instruments and do not normally accept either modern or electric guitars.

Over the years, I have found that anyone who sticks at it can make a successful instrument, even without any previous woodworking experience.

At the moment both classes are full but do enquire if you are interested and I can let you know when there is a space.

Fees at the moment are £3.50 for each two hour session, payable by term or half-term in advance. For this I provide all the tools but the students have to buy their own wood and other materials.
