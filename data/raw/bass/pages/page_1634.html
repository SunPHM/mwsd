A fine English orchestral double bass instrument by T.Davies, Birmingham 1874 No3 in outstanding structural condition - #2452

Relatively little is known about the instrument maker T. Davies. The Dictionary of Violin and Bow Makers by D.G. Plowright does mention that his 'Violins and violas are quite well made' and from the quality of this instrument this certainly can be said of his double basses. Davies was obviously quite a prolific maker of the double bass for the Contrabass Shoppe has in the recent past sold two other instruments by him and proprietor Anthony Houska knows of the whereabouts of at least three or four others that are currently being used by professional players in London.

Located on the central back brace of this exceptional instrument is Davies's original label. In hand written black ink it reads 'T. Davies Maker Birmingham 1874 No3'. The question we must ask ourselves is - is this instrument Davies's third double bass or is it the third instrument made in that particular year? If it is only the third double bass that he ever made then it is a sure thing that he took a lot of care and pride over its making. Indeed, this is not the work of somebody who is learning from his mistakes as he goes along. The model is big and bold, there is a certain flamboyancy to the style and then there is the arching to the table that makes one gasp with admiration at its sheer perfection. Like all the other instruments known to be made by Davies this one bears his brand 'T. Davies' on the back button.

Another brand can be found on the root of the bass side neck. It is not the Davies brand but that of the maker and repairer William Calow of Nottingham. Carlow's obviously had high thoughts with regard to his own repair work for the inside of this instrument is absolutely littered with his pencil inscriptions. Unfortunately the work is not of the same standard that the Contrabass Shoppe demands from its restorers. Prime evidence of his sins comes in the form of an incredibly strange shaped bassbar with a handwritten inscription along the bottom that reads in capital letters 'REPAIRED AND FITTED BY F.W.CALOW. 24 BURTON ST, NOTTm. JULY 1937 FOR MR THISTLWOOD'. Besides being unconformist in shape it was also badly positioned and badly fitted. The Calow bar has been removed intact and is now offered with the instrument as part of its history.

Four other internal hand written pencil inscriptions from Mr Calow can be found.

  1. On upper table on treble side - 'This Bass Made By T Davies, Birmingham 1874 No3 - Should label be Missing'.
  2. On lower table on treble side - 'REPAIRED BY Fk W. Calow. 24 Burton St Nottm 1937'
  3. On upper back on angle break on bass side - 'Repaired By and Re-necked July 1937 Fk W. Calow. Violin maker 24 Burton St, Nottingham'
  4. On lower back on treble side - 'Repaired By and Re-necked July 1937 Fk W. Calow. Violin maker 24 Burton St, Nottingham'

Finally another so called bass-specialist repairer, a Mr J. Jones from a slightly earlier date to that of Calow has left his repair label on the central back above the central brace. It reads 'Mr J. Jones, 5 Leopold Street, Birmingham. For Mr Kirkby, Reginold Terrace, Leeds. 23rd November 1917.'

But I thought you said that there is very little damage to the instrument? Yes that is correct and I must admit that that statement does sound a bit funny particularly with all those repairer inscriptions inside the instrument. The fact of the matter is that our past restorers were rather overzealous in their approach with the end result being somewhat messy. To sort out everything properly has required an expenditure in excess of 4K. The nature of the work done is too numerous to mention here but it should suffice to say that it has been executed without compromise. By the way, you have my assurances that a correctly fitted, correctly shaped bass bar is now in position.

Words cannot really describe the overall condition of this magnificent instrument. Words will not do justice to the power and tonal qualities of this instrument. This is a Goliath of an instrument. As estate agents say here in the UK "An early viewing is highly recommended".
