(*Values for 2001 and 2002 are best approximations.)


Due to the lack of co-ordinated marketing in line with the annual leaps of output volumes, farm gate prices are declining sharply and are approaching the critical values of 3.50 €/kg for sea bream and 3.90 €/kg for sea bass. These latter values represent the minimum for profitable sales to be achieved, taking into account not only the variable cost structure but also the average annual fixed costs (including the cost of borrowing).

Necessary corrective actions would be: a) better utilisation of inputs and resources, b) restraint in bank loans, c) adherence to the decisions by the producer organisations as regards pricing and export co-ordination. Unfortunately, it has proved difficult for the Greek aquaculturists to unite behind any agreed pattern for exports, hence financial problems, which are already evident on many farms, are expected to worsen and become a major constraint to the expansion of the sector.
