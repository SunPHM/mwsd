REAL TIME WATER FLOW DATA IN MARYLAND

Real time water flow data is available 24 hours/day from the United States Geological Survey. We have inserted links in the report below for many of the streams and rivers discussed in the fishing report. For additional stations, go to the main real time USGS water flow page .

Casselman River: As of June 10, 2002 the Long Term Median Flow (LTMF) in Grantsville, Maryland is 48 cfs with a flow of 53 cfs and staging at 1.17 feet. Fly options include#14 - 16 caddis, #14 light cahills, and #14 sulphurs.

Click for the latest real time stream flows on the Casselman.

North Branch of the Potomac River: As of June 10, 2002, at the Barnum gauging station the LTMF is 307 cfs with a flow of 247 cfs and staging at 3.06 feet. Trout are readily feeding off the small blue winged olive hatches. Size #20 blue winged olives, #12 March browns, and #12-14 caddis are good options.

Click for the latest real time stream flows on the North Branch of the Potomac at Barnum.

Savage River: As of June 10, 2002 in Bloomington the LTMF is 63 cfs with a flow of 82 cfs and staging at 0.95 feet. Fly options include tan and olive #14-18 caddis, #18 blue winged olives, #14 pale morning dun, and #14 sulphurs. Green drakes are just about done, but #8 is the size to use while they are still present. Light cahills (#12-14) have started.

Click for the latest real time stream flows on the Savage River near Bloomington, MD.

Youghiogheny: As of June 10, 2002 the LTMF near Oakland is 123 cfs with a flow of 88 cfs and staging at 2.31 feet. The LTMF in Friendsville is 279 cfs with a flow of 875 cfs and staging at 3.44 feet. This river is fishing well. Fly options include tan and olive #14-18 caddis, #18 blue winged olives, #14 pale morning dun, and #14 sulphurs.

Click for the latest real time stream flows on the Youghiogheny near Oakland, MD.

Gunpowder River: This river continued to fish well last week. Caddis, #18-20, have been hatching all day. The sulphur hatch (#14-16 and gradually moving #18) has been happening between 2 to 5 p.m. Ants (#16 and #18) or beetles (#14 and #16) are also good options. As of June 12, 2002 the LTMF at Little Falls at Blue Mount is 57 cfs with a flow of 16 cfs and staging at 0.24 feet.

Brown trout have been tagged with visual implant tags just behind the left eye socket (pictured on the left - click for a larger view). If you catch a tagged brown trout please contact Central Regional Manager, Charlie Gougeon, at 410-442-2080 or via e-mail at cgougeon2@dnr.state.md.us with the following information: date and location of the catch, tag number, tag color, kept or released, and any other information you feel is important.

Nontidal Potomac River: As of June 10, 2002 at the Williamsport Water Treatment Plant the River is 2.0 feet and slowly dropping, cloudy, and 77 degrees Fahrenheit. Muskie Man Mullican provided the following report. The water remains cloudy from past thunderstorms, but is clearing each day. As conditions improve, smallmouth will hit topwater baits. Evening insect hatches have been prolific and should offer flyrodders good action as the water clears. Other baits for smallmouth include small chartreuse spinner baits, crankbaits, and the always effective tube jig and grubs. Pictured on the right is Muskie Man Mullican with a 36 incher that was caught and released last weekend in western Washington County.

LAKES, PONDS, AND RESERVOIRS

Deep Creek Lake (Garrett County): Water is clear and in the upper 60's. Bluegills continued to have a good bite with many catches around 10 inches coming from 3 to 12 feet of water. Worms fished off the docks have been the ticket. Walleye action continued to be hit or miss. Minnows and worms fished in 6 feet of water is one method used to catch them. Minnows and plastic worms have been catching (must be released) smallmouth bass in 4 to 6 feet of water. Surface plugs have been good on calm evenings. Streamers and poppers have produced for fly fishermen. Chain pickerel are biting well and have been caught with the bass. Worms fished on the bottom in 6 to 8 feet of water continued to be the hot bait for yellow perch.

Information on whitewater boating releases is available on the Deep Creek Hydro Hotline at (814) 533-8911 or online at URL: http://www.deepcreekhydro.com/

Liberty Reservoir (Carroll County): ): Liberty is down 16.88 feet as of June 11, 2002. As a general rule, fishing has been best at daybreak and late in the day. Bass anglers have been using medium to large shiners, crayfish, crankbaits, and 10-inch power worms. Shoreline action continued to be productive for bass. Fly fishermen have been catching on foam crickets. Fathead minnows, Mini Mites, and maggots have been catching crappies off points. Some small crappies have been reeled in from Nicodemus Bridge late in the day. Some striped bass have also been caught early and late in the day using extra large shiners. Crayfish patterns have caught carp in shallow water for fly fishermen.

Little Seneca Lake (Montgomery County): Water temperature was 75 degrees last week with 6 feet of visibility. Hydrilla was approximately 12 inches. Bass fishing has been good with many averaging 11 to 14 inches. Places to target were wood lay downs, submerged brush, beaver huts, and standing timber. Jigs with a plastic trailer and Texas-rigged hula grubs were productive when fished in 3 to 20 feet of water. Pictured on the left is Chris Peloquin with a 4 pound, 14 ounce, 20 7/8 inch largemouth bass. That bass was caught fishing standing timber in 16 feet of water. Nighttime fishing opportunities for this year are June 26, July 17, August 22, and September 18th.

Loch Raven Reservoir (Baltimore County): ): Loch Raven is down 3.68 feet as of June 11, 2002. Loch Raven Fishing Center reports that white perch have been caught trolling a spinner hook with a night crawler in 12 to 15 feet of water. Chain pickerel and smallmouth bass have been active throughout the impoundment. Bass fishing has been fair using plastic worms, pig and jigs, or stickbaits worked on points. Plugs are catching in the morning and evening. Move from shallows into deeper water as the rises. Some yellow perch have been caught on the bottom using worms. Nesting bluegills have been caught in coves.

The Maryland Geological Survey has a bathymetry map on their website.

Piney Run Park (Carroll County): PRP staff reported steady action on largemouth bass with small plastics including the "wacky" worm. Average catches were between 2 and 4 pounds last week. Staff expects action using crankbaits, buzzbaits, spinnerbaits, and topwaters to start soon. Big yellow perch and bluegills hit well last week. Redworms and night crawlers were the key. Brad Martin of Sykesville, MD caught a 13.128-inch yellow perch last week.

Prettyboy Reservoir (Baltimore County): Prettyboy is down 29.66 feet as of June 11, 2002. Launching a boat is possible although difficult. Crankbaits, jerkbaits, fished in 12 to 15 feet of water over points and ledges as well as around the islands have been productive. The average size bass has been 2 to 2.5 pounds.

WSSC Triadelphia and Rocky Gorge Reservoirs: Water temperature was in the upper 70's last weekend at Triadelphia with 3 feet of visibility. Anglers have been catching and releasing largemouth and smallmouth bass up to 4 pounds in both reservoirs at rocky points, humps, and flats. Carolina rigged lizards, drop shot-rigged plastics have been the most productive but spinnerbaits and lipless crankbaits have also caught a few bass. Pictured on the right is an 18 inch largemouth bass caught by Chris Peloquin on June 8th. This bass weighed 4 pounds, which is considered fat by us (DNR Fisheries Service). Crappies have been taking jigs, worms, and crankbaits. Live worms or minnows, crankbaits, and jigs have caught some walleyes.
