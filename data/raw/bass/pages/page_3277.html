Mediterranean Salad
Serves: 4
Cals per serving: 242
Fat per serving:10g (saturated fat 4g)
Preparation time: 25 mins
Min Cooking time:10
  • 2 peppers, halved and deseeded
  • 3 tbsp sun-dried tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 700g (1lb 9oz) Jersey Royals, cooked and kept warm
  • 115g (4oz) feta cheese, diced
  • 50g (2oz) olives
  • Basil leaves to garnish
Grill peppers, skin side up, for 6-8 mins until skin is blackened. Allow to cool, peel and cut into pieces.
Mix tomato paste with juice in a bowl. Add potatoes and peppers; toss to coat. Allow to cool. Stir in feta and olives. Season, garnish with basil leaves. Serve.

 
Thai-Style Fish Cakes
Serves: 4
Cals per serving: 110
Fat per serving: 1g (saturated fat trace)
Preparation time: 15 mins, plus 1 hr chilling
Min Cooking time: 12
  • 1 x 500g pkt Safeway Cod Fillets, cubed 2.5cm peeled 1 tbsp Thai fish sauce
  • 1 clove garlic, crushed
  • (1in) piece fresh ginger
  • 4 salad onions trimmed and chopped
  • 1/2 red chilli, deseeded and chopped
  • 1 pkt fresh coriander
  • 1 tbsp flour Low-fat spray oil
  • Chilli sauce and green salad, to serve
Put the cod, garlic, ginger, onions, chilli, coriander and fish sauce into a food processor and pulse until finely minced.
Tip the mixture into a bowl. Flour your hands and shape the mixture into 8 round cakes. Lay on a non-stick baking tray, cover and chill for 1 hr.
Heat a frying pan and spray with oil. Fry the fish cakes for 6 mins on each side. Serve with chilli sauce and a green salad.
 
Jersey Royal brunch
Serves: 4
Cals per serving: 297
Fat per serving: 18g (saturated fat 4g
Preparation time: 20 mins
Min Cooking time: 15 mins
  • 450g (1lb) Jersey Royals, cooked and sliced
  • 1 bunch salad onions, trimmed and sliced
  • 4 rashers streaky bacon, chopped
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh parsley
  • 8-10 cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 4 large barn eggs large
  • pinch paprika
Fry potatoes, salad onions and bacon in oil for 5-6 mins until golden. Add parsley and tomatoes and cook for 1 min.
Meanwhile, poach eggs in simmering water for 3 mins and then drain. Serve with potatoes and sprinkle with paprika.
 
Tender sirloin steak with ladykiller sauce
Serves: 4
Cals per serving: 957
Fat per serving: 40g (saturated fat 14g)
Preperation time: 10 mins
Min Cooking time: 20 mins depending on steak
  • 1 x 750g pkt oven chips 75g
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • small onion, finely chopped
  • 16 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1 red chilli, deseeded and finely chopped
  • 50ml (2fl oz) red wine
  • 1 x 450g tub beef gravy
  • (3oz) Gorgonzola cheese, roughly chopped
  • 4 x 275g (10oz) sirloin steaks
Preheat the oven to 230°C/ 450°F/gas mark 8, and cook the chips for 20 mins until golden and cooked through.
Meanwhile, heat oil and gently fry the onions, garlic and chilli for 3 mins. Add red wine, bring to the boil and rapidly boil for 2 mins or until reduced by half. Add gravy and cheese. Heat gently, stirring until the cheese has melted, then keep warm.
Heat a griddle pan and fry steaks for 2 mins on each side for rare; 3 mins on each side for medium to well done; and 3 mins on one side and then 6-10 mins slower cooking for well done. Serve with the rich sauce and oven chips.
 
Asparagus with an orange sauce
Serves: 4
Preperation time: 5 mins
Min Cooking time: 10 mins
  • Juice of 2 large oranges
  • 2 medium egg yolks, beaten lightly
  • 150g (5oz) butter, cut into small cubes
  • 1 tbsp crème fraîche
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 x 100g pkts fine asparagus tips (you will need about 12 per person)
Heat orange juice in a pan and boil rapidly for about 5 mins until syrupy. Add egg yolks* and whisk over a low heat for 1 min.
Gradually add butter, whisking continuously until thick; the mixture should coat the back of a spoon. Whisk in crème fraîche and lemon juice. Season to taste, then set aside and keep warm.
Plunge asparagus into a pan of boiling water and cook for 1 min until just tender. Drain and arrange on warm plates and drizzle with the orange sauce. Serve immediately.
 
Fillet of sea bass with pesto sauce
Serves: 2
Fat per serving: 44g (saturated fat 26g
Preperation time: 15 mins
Max Cooking time: 10 mins
  • 50g (2oz) canned white crab meat, drained
  • 15g (12oz) fresh breadcrumbs
  • 150ml (14pt) double cream
  • Finely grated zest of 1/2 lemon
  • 4 x 100g (3 1/2oz) sea bass fillets, descaled 5
  • 1/2 tsp lemon juice
  • 50ml (2fl oz) dry white wine
  • 1 tsp green pesto 50g (2oz)
  • watercress
Preheat oven to 220°C/425°F/ gas mark 7. Mix crab meat, breadcrumbs, 25ml (1fl oz) cream and zest together in a bowl. Season and set aside.
In a hot frying pan, cook fillets skin-side down for 1 min until crispy. Put 2 fillets skin-side down on an oiled baking tray. Spread crab stuffing over the top and sandwich together with remaining fillets, so skin side is uppermost. Bake for 6-8 mins until cooked through.
Meanwhile, bring wine and lemon juice to the boil, then rapidly boil until reduced by half. On a low heat, add remaining cream until sauce thickens. Remove from heat, stir through pesto and drizzle over fillets. Serve with watercress.
