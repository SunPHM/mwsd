30th April 2004
Following my letter to the North Western & North Wales Sea Fisheries Committee, I have received a reply from the Chief Executive Mr. Jim Andrews. Mr. Andrews thanked the WFSA for their letter and went on to reveal that he had received 70 letters and emails on the subject of the Conwy Bass Nursery area. 

It has been explained by the NW & NWSFC that much confusion has arisen with the numbering of the Byelaws. It is claimed by them that the new laws will further protecting fishing in the Conwy / NE Anglesey areas.
It is now clear from the fact sheet produced by the NW & NWSFC that the Bass Nursery areas have the same level of protection as previously afforded to them. However certain changes in the laws do effect nearby areas. To view more information please visit the NW & NWSFC website at www.nwnwsfc.org 
For example
 Old Byelaw 24 - Area 3 - Fixed nets
''no restrictions provided net mesh greater than 250mm'
I read this as 'No netting allowed at any time other than with a large meshed net (nearly 10" mesh)'
New Byelaw
'Netting prohibited 1st April to 30th June" 
I read this as 'All nets allowed, regardless of mesh size, from 1st July through to 31st March, in any year, covering the area directly next to the Bass Nursery Area!!
Please click here for diagram
It appears that the NW & NWSFC have now made it easier for the commercial fleet to intercept all undersize fish within this area
As much as it heartens me to know that 70 persons wrote to the SFC, how many were via the WFSA? and why haven't more anglers taken part in what is an extremely important subject? We should all be standing together and protecting our sport. 
Please continue to write further to your local MP, NW & NWSFC, DEFRA and the Welsh Assembly pointing out your disgust and total disagreement with the changes which now offer no restrictions what so ever in Area 3 for 9 months of any year. 

Gethyn Owen

03rd May 2004 - email and letter sent to NW&NWSFC
Dear Dr. Andrews,

RE: NW & NWSFC Netting Byelaws and the Conwy Estuary Bass Nursery Area

Thank you for your letter dated 30th April 2004.

Having read your letter and fact sheet regarding the new Byelaws I note that it now gives a very basic overview of the old & new laws, however I am still unsure of the protection now offered to bass and migratory salmon and sea trout from commercial nets men.

Having looked at the old byelaw 24 I refer you to paragraph c which states that persons were required to obtain authorisation for netting in certain areas e.g. area 3. Any other netting was strictly prohibited.

The new Byelaw 24 para c suggests that any gill net is now acceptable for 9 months of the year.

Moving on to paragraph diii, the authorisation would only be given to mesh sizes greater than 250mm in certain areas e.g. area 3.

The new Byelaw confirms that any gill net is now acceptable for 9 months of the year.

Please can you clarify exactly how the reduction in mesh size to ‘any size’ for 9 months of the year and the removal of required authorisation helps protect the fish stocks?

I am also concerned that the WFSA was not informed or consulted on this matter! The areas under discussion have a great draw for many anglers and bring ££££'s into the local economy - far more than commercial angling will do. With it having an obvious effect on WFSA members and traveling angers I feel it would have been more constructive for both parties to have been involved in the consultation stage to help formulate the way forward for all concerned.

I look forward to hearing from you in the very near future.

Yours sincerely

Gethyn Owen
