Delaware 104th Anniversary Dive - Saturday, July 6th, 2002


Sunk by fire on July 9th, 1898

We headed out to celebrate the anniversary of the sinking of this steamship as we do every year. This year the project was not only dive the wreck and look for artifacts, fish and lobsters, but salvage her bow bits or deck cleats. For this project we planned to use two 500 pillow float bags from SubSalve, Inc. to float the bits to while on the surface and one 500lb SubSalve Commercial lift bag to raise the bits from the wreck to the surface. The bits were deemed 'light' enough to use the boat gin pole and tackle and lift the bits onto our stern and bring them home. Weight was estimated at 300 pounds.

Conditions on the wreck were a very dark, but clear 20ft vis. Barbara & I found a leaded glass dessert cup, brass latch, some brass shoe buttons and several brass deck spikes. Jeff found some bell shaped copper weights. A few lobster, Sea Bass and a few Blackfish were also caught.
