2003 Welcome Letter

To book reservations call us at 631-668-5760 or E-mail      captskip2@optonline.com


All in all the 2002 fishing season, was one of the best aboard the ADIOS, yet !

The inshore fishing for Strippers, Fluke, Sea Bass, Porgy and Blackfish was nothing Less then great. The Stripe Bass and Fluke was steady all summer with only a few exceptions. August was very warm, so we found ourselves working a little harder Just to get the Strippers to bite.

The bottom fishing was the best in years. Humpback Porgys and Knothead Sea Bass Where biting their heads off. In keeping conservation in mind, we kept Seabass only 14" Or bigger ( the legal limit is 11 ").

Offshore fishing was for the most part, pretty good. Shark fishing saw lots of big, fat Blue sharks and some Makos, thrown in for good measure. The biggest Mako was  298lbs. Not to mention one at, 102lbs, and one also 85lbs. Plenty of action with almost Legal Makos as well Tuna fishing was hot and cold, Some days they were jumping into the boat, and some days, they were swimming for their lives! We supplemented on the slow days with HUGE catches of Mahi-Mahi (dolphin fish), the average catch was about 30 fish. An incredible catch on light tackle, and of course, probably the best eating fish in the ocean! Because of the sporadic bite of the tuna, this year, we suggested to our customers, Based on information on daily trips offshore, that on the slow days, to go for sharks, that way, there is always action on the ADIOS!

The largest tuna for the season was a 300lbs Bluefin, taken on a 50 lb class rod. Everyone got 3 turns at the rod to get the fish in the boat. Being that we had an extended summer, we wrapped up the season with some excellent bottom and Stripped Bass fishing. The herring showed up, a little late, (due to the warm weather) but made for some exciting bass fishing on light tackle.

On behalf of the crew of the ADIOS, we want to thank all our customers for their Patronage, but most of all their enthusiasm, because it is more rewarding as a Captain, to see people laughing and having a great time! That is what I fish for!

Hope to see you next year, remember to book early, my prime dates (weekends) Are usually booked by the early spring.

                 On behalf of my family and myself, May the New Year bring you happiness and prosperity.

 Tight lines!

Captain Skip Rudolph
