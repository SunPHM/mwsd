Ron Speed is known to many Americans from coast to coast as one of the pioneers of Mexican bass fishing. Over 30 years ago while coaching high school football in Texas, Speed decided to hang up his coaching shorts and cleats and concentrate on his first love ...fishing. Speed set up a full accommodations operation on Lake Dominguez and after 30 years and 10 operations on Mexico's best bass lakes, Speed is "king of the mountain" when it comes to offering the very best bass fishing and services.

Lake Comedero

Currently, there is not a better trophy bass lake on the planet. Lake Comedero is located 100 miles NE of Mexico's popular beach resort, Mazatlan. In 1989, Speed began stocking Florida strain bass into Comedero with the hopes of one day growing some 20 lb bass. Ten years after the first stocking of Florida bass in Comedero, the lake produced its first 18 lb bass. On December 1, 1999 Chris Brewer of Iowa boated the first 18 lb bass by one of Speed's clients. On that same trip, Warren Brewer (Chris' father) landed bass at 14 lbs, 13 lbs, and four at 12 lbs. Together, the father/son team landed 12 bass in excess of 8 lbs during four days of fishing.

Stories have circulated about a group of Americans that pulled their boats to Comedero and caught several bass in excess of 18 lbs, including one over 20 lbs. May be a "fish story", but one thing is for sure, Comedero is worth keeping an eye on for the next several years. With a first class beach resort in Mazatlan close by and the very best trophy bass, Speed has definately hit a grand slam with this trip.

Lake Huites

Mexico's newest bass hot spot! Huites is simply jammed with tons of quality bass from 2-8 pounds. Huites is one of the very first lakes in Mexico to be initially stocked with Florida strain bass. It offers the fisherman not only the opportunity to catch great numbers of bass, but a realistic chance at a trophy (10 lbs+) as well.

Ron Speed's Adventures was the first licensed promoter on the lake, giving Ron his choice of locations for a lodge facility (Huites Bass Club). Huites Bass Club is located on the water and can accommodate up to 28. This beautiful facility offers great service, food and the very best fleet of bass boats in Mexico. The 17' Fisher and Trackers mounted with 55 hp motors and bow mounted Motorguide trollers so you can operate your own if you wish. All boats are equipped with Uniden radios for emergencies as well as sharing those "secret holes". The guides for Huites Bass Club are second to none and are the most experienced on the lake.

Lake El Salto

Mexico's Lake El Salto is located 50 miles north of the beautiful seaside resort of Mazatlan. Lake El Salto has thousands of hungry bass that are easily caught all year on a variety of lures, but topwaters, crankbaits, and plastic worms are best. El Salto offers the amateur and novice angler the opportunity to easily catch lots and lots of fish most days without a lot of effort.

Ron Speed's clients stay at Club El Salto which is on the water's edge on the lake. Camp El Salto's design came from Casa Mar fishing camp in Costa Rica. It is a four-building camp with a screened-in circular restaurant overlooking beautiful Lake El Salto. The houses have two private bedrooms and an adjoining shower and bath combination. The restaurant has a large 20-ft indoor aquarium with trophy bass exceeding 10 lbs and clients can keep up with their favorite sports team at Club El Salto with its satellite T.V. Club El Salto's prevailing breezes from the nearby Pacific Ocean assure great comfort all year, and the screened-in rooms with ceiling fans are truly delightful. Blankets are needed each night.

Club El Salto's food is just wonderful. The cook prepares a mixture of American, Mexican, and local seafood dishes that will please the most difficult of palates. Mazatlan, with its fantastic beaches, is your gateway city to both El Salto and Comedero lakes, and is serviced by airlines from all over the U.S.A. Ron Speed's Adventures will arrange your transportation from Mazatlan to both lakes.
