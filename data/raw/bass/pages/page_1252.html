Chandler MGD "Flying V" 12-String Bass
Submitted By Adrian Chandler 

*Alan Woody (Allman Brothers & Gov‚t Mule) was the first one to ask us to make our first 12-string bass.  He was playing with the Allman Brothers at the time long before Gov‚t Mule. The advertising agency that does the advertising for Miller Genuine Draft wanted a bass guitar with the Miller logo and  the motto „Get out of the old, get into the cold.„ It was for a mini production video the were shooting with the band.  Alan told them that he had a friend that he thought could do it, and he gave me call.  He gave me a wish list.  

We built it and the ad agency bought it and presented it to Alan.  It was a natural finish Korina Flying „V‰.  It had two double „Lipstick‰ pickups they were wired as humbuckers but they could be individually split and it had one of Adrian‚s „Red Booster‰ pre-amps in it.  It was a 30" shortscale with a Brazilian rosewood fingerboard with a graphite slab parallel to and under the fingerboard, and it had a Bad Ass II Bridge.  Alan made it sound great!!

2000 Chandler Royale
Submitted by Low-End Monster

Well here she is...my own Chandler Royale!  It is painted with a Vintage SG Red Transparent Stain. I tried to capture the effect of the Swamp Ash wood grain in these photos, as always it is nicer when you see it with your own eyes and believe me it is BEAUTIFUL!  The fourth picture from the left shows my custom "TP" style tortoise shell pickguard!   I find this pickguard suits/ compliments the Royale's body style much better.  It also makes my Royale extremely unique as Tom Petersson has the only other one with this style as his first Royale is the original.   Photo # 5  displays the custom made  pickups, bridge and tailpiece.  Note the string spacing!  All the other 12vers that  I can think of space the fundamentals and octaves evenly,   whereas the Royale spaces the octaves as a group a bit further away from the fundamentals.  Photo # 6 shows the output jack and tortoise shell binding. The 8th and final photo shows my practice rig... if you look close enough you see the SansAmp Bass Driver DI sitting in front of the Marshall MG15R CD.   The bass amp shown below is my Fender Bassman 25. If you think the Royale looks nice...wait till you try/ hear/ play it!  For more info see my review.
