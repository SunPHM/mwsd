                  Squid Salad in Grapefruit Shells
                 

 

  INGREDIENTS

- 1 small ruby grapefruit
- 20cm (8 in.) celery stalk
- 60g (2.5 oz.) snow peas
- 1 squid, cleaned (120g/4 oz.)
- Salt and white pepper to taste

GINGERDRESSING

- 1 small piece ginger peeled and grated
- 1/2 t honey
- 1 1/2 T rice vinegar
- 1 T grapefruit juice
- 3 T salad oil
- 1/2 t salt
- 1 1/2 t soy sauce
- 1/2 t long onion, finely chopped

   METHOD
- Cut grapefruit in half, scoop out and set aside fruit sections. Save juice for use in the dressing.
- De-thread celery and cut fine julienne. Sprinkle lightly with salt, rub and squeeze out water.
- String snow peas and parboil in salted water. Cool down in cold water then slice diagonally.
- Parboil squid briefly taking care not to overcook. Cut the squid into 3-4cm x 2-3mm- (1-1.5 in. x 1/8 in.)-wide strips.
- Prepare the dressing; mix about half together with the celery, snow peas and squid. Add salt and pepper to taste.
- Mix in chopped-up grapefruit and add the remaining dressing to taste.
-

Spoon mixture into hollowed-out rapefruit shells to serve.

Note: Crabmeat or shrimp may be substituted for the squid; white grapefruit may be used with more colorful seafood.
