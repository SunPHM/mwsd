    Mark Tiggas

    		 

    Chair
    Mark SB Tiggas

    Mark has more than 20 years experience in distributed computing, including over 15 years of Internet development. Mark serves as Senior Vice President, Systems Architect for Wells Fargo Services Company, Minneapolis. He has been with Wells Fargo Services Company for 5 years. In that time Mark has led the technology for payments related eCommerce initiatives. Mark often serves as Wells Fargo & Company's representative to industry initiatives such as BITS, Financial Services Technology Consortium (FSTC) and the IFX Forum.

    Mark has been a key player in the IFX Forum, a member of the board of directors and steering since inception, active is several working groups, including Architecture, and a past President. The IFX Forum has been active in the development of an XML vocabulary for the communication of financial information. Initially this work focused on retail banking and bill presentment and has of late incorporated corporate payments, foreign exchange and ATM support.

    Prior to joining Wells Fargo Mark led two systems and development consulting organizations in the twin cites, while also participating as an active consultant. During this time, Mark introduced several companies including many Fortune 500 companies to the Internet, the Web and eCommerce. While serving as the chief technologist for an Information Services Company, twice on the list of Fortune magazines 50 fastest growing companies, Mark was the principal architect behind a distributed search and retrieval system used to house and research technical information. Mark began his career, as an Industrial Engineer, for a Fortune 500 company, introducing Mini and Microcomputers into the manufacturing environment for test, measurement and reporting.

    David Barkley

    		 

    Vice Chair
    David Barkley

    David R. Barkley is Director of eCommerce Relations in Freddie Mac's Mortgage Services Division. His responsibilities include the support of industry-wide technology initiatives such as mortgage related Electronic Data Interchange (EDI) and Extensible Markup Language (XML) standards. Besides overseeing these initiatives from an industry perspective, David leads Freddie Mac's internal consulting team responsible for integrating industry initiatives into Freddie Mac business processes. He also manages Freddie Mac's relationships with electronic commerce vendors to further automation in the mortgage process.

    David chairs the Governance Committee of the Mortgage Industry Standards Maintenance Organization (MISMO) and is an active member of the Mortgage Bankers Association of America's (MBA) MISMO Work Groups. These cross-industry work groups are responsible for developing standardized electronic data transactions and exploring emerging technologies for the real estate finance industry.

    David is also Chair of the American National Standards Institute's (ANSI) EDI organization known as the Accredited Standards Committee (ASC) X12. He is an ex officio member of the Board of Directors of the Data Interchange Standards Association (DISA). ASC X12 and DISA develop, approve and maintain national, cross-industry EDI standards in X12 and XML formats, and represent U.S. interests in international standards forums.

    Steve Bass

    		 

    Secretary
    Steve Bass

    Steve Bass is President and CEO of Washington Publishing Company (WPC), named in the HIPAA Transaction and Code Set Regulation as publisher and distributor of the X12N implementation guides. Bass has actively participated in EDI Standards development since 1975.

    Prior to founding WPC, Bass was Documentation Director for EDI, Inc., a pioneering producer of EDI translation software. He has been Art Director of a diverse group of trade and general interest publications, including New England Farmer and The Caledonia Independent. Bass is from the Maryland suburbs of Washington, DC, and has a BA from the Institute for Urban Studies at the University of Maryland. He lives near Seattle, WA.
