LAKE FORK: BASS HEAVEN
By Richard McCarty
HUNTER'S JOURNAL TV »  | POWERHOUSE PROMOTIONS »

Lake Fork ended the 2001/2002 Budweiser Texas Parks & Wildlife Sharelunker Season with entry number 15 and 1. The five largest bass ever caught in Texas were caught on Lake Fork, and 34 of the top 50 ever caught in the state of Texas were caught on Lake Fork.


Richard McCarty with big bass, Lake Fork, TX

Lake Fork produced the original entry that started the Sharelunker program on November 26, 1986 when Mark Stevenson caught "Ethel". This giant female weighed 17.67 pounds, and was put on display at Bass Pro Shops Outdoor World in Springfield, Missouri and amazed visitors until she died in 1994. On January 24, 1992, Barry St. Clair landed the new Texas State Record while fishing Lake Fork. This 18.18 pound mammoth bass has not been matched, but most anglers will agree that her conqueror now swims in Lake Fork and will be reeled in the near future.


Richard & Jason Housley on Lake Fork

To date there have been a total of 342 entries into the Budweiser/TPW Sharelunker Program from 50 public lakes and 9 private lakes across the state of Texas. Of the 342 entries, 201 came from Lake Fork! The 201 Lake Fork Sharelunkers account for 2,809.68 pounds of bass and the average weight of a Lake Fork Lunker is 13.98 pounds.

The good thing about Lake Fork is that everyone around the lake wants to see someone catch a big fish. If you ask for help there will be someone give you advice.


Richard McCarty is a professional fishing guide on Lake Fork, Texas. He is widely recognized as "Lake Fork's most experienced fishing guide", and has qualified several times for the BASSMASTER CLASSIC circuit. Richard has also caught or helped catch an astounding number of trophy fish out of Lake Fork. You can visit Richard on the web at http://www.forkguide.com/
