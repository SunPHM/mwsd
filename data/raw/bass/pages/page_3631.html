I have lived and fished on the Georgia coast all of my life. I have had a Captain's License for 24 years and made my living in the charter fishing business for 22 years.

Over the course of 3,000 trips, my clients have caught a lot of big fish, including several record fish. The 2003 season was a great success. We had a real good year on the Red Snapper, Amberjack, Vermillion Snapper, Grouper, Tarpon, Sharks, Kings and Sea Bass. We caught 500 legal Red Snapper, including many over 30 pounds. The Amberjack have made an amazing recovery. They are plentiful now with a lot of fish over 50 pounds. These big jacks are hard-fighting tackle busters. We caught 418 King Mackerel, including one monster 54 pound fish. We a fair number of Cobia and Dolphin, which were nice additions to the fish box. The big Barracudda were real thick all summer and fall, putting on some good shows and good battles.

We had some real thrilling trips on Tarpon and Sharks. Several days we saw over 100 Tarpon and had up to nine hooks ups in a single morning. We caught a lot of fish in the 150 pound range and one 84 inch fish that weighed around 180 pounds. The Shark fishing was probably the best ever. On some 8 hour trips we released over 50 sharks. We kept the limit of good eating sharks almost every trip. We caught a lot of 100 to 300 pound fish, and released two Tiger sharks around 800 pounds.

I specialize in bottom fishing for Snapper, Grouper, Sea Bass, and Amberjack. While bottom fishing, we also catch many other fish species. During the warmer months, and in the fall, we can also troll for King Mackerel, Dolphin, Barracuda, and other game fish.

I fish a 38-foot walk-around boat that can carry up to 6 fishermen per group. My boat is equipped with New Cummins 465 HP diesel power, modern electronics, Penn fishing tackle, and all USCG required safety equipment.
