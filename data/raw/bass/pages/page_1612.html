Tune 10-String and 12-String Basses
Submitted by Makoto Takano in Sweden

Tune basses are made in Japan and come in a 4-, 5- ,6-, 7-, 8-, 10-, and 12-string versions. The 12-string basses are set up as 2-course / 6-string basses. This is a 6-string bass with low and high B strings plus a single high octave string for each fundamental string. As such they are more closely related to 8-string basses than the 12-string basses that are our primary interest, which are 3-course / 4-string instruments. Modulus 18-String Bass

Modulus did make an 18-string (3 course 6-string) bass for Tom Peterson in the early 80s. It was a TBX neck-through model with what was probably a custom Stars Guitars brass bridge. The bass was originally shipped to SIR in LA for Tom. The bass was left at SIR for reasons unknown and shipped back to Modulus once found. It happened that the late Allan Woody called Modulus at that time and asked if an 18-string “just like the one you made for Tom Peterson” could be built for him. When he was told the actual Peterson bass was available, he became the new owner. It was still with him at the time of his death.

Dommenget 12-String Bass w/ Tremolo
Submitted by Kevin Macza

This 12-string bass with a tremolo was custom made by Boris Dommenget of Dommenget Custom Guitars of Hamburg, Germany for Andy "Moshela" Bolle.  It  features a  swamp ash body with a flamed carved maple top, birdseye maple neck with a macassar fretboard.  The inlays are 150 million year old ammonites that were supplied by the customer.

 

Warrior Double Neck 4 / 12 String Bass Guitar
Submitted by Jeff Jenkins from Knoxville, TN

I have been a 12ver fanatic since 1990's "Its Love" video from King's X.  I purchased my first 12ver in 1993, a red, long scale, Hamer Chaparral.  This was the first year the long scale was available as a non-prototype model.  Having been a fan of Doug and Tom for years, I couldn't wait to be playing this awesome instrument.  Now, being from where I'm from, I'm sure you can guess that most of the money I make as a musician is from the country music genre.  I had hoped early on that I would bring this instrument to life in country music, but as many would say, they just weren't ready for it at the time.  I continued to play my Hamer until 1997 when I signed on to endorse a new bass maker at that time, Warrior Instruments out of Rossville, Ga. I came into Warrior before they had made their 100th instrument.

Modulus 12-String Bass ( 2-Course  6-String )
Submitted by Rich Lasner on July 24, 2001

Two photos of a 1993 Modulus 12-string bass turned up at the shop. The first picture shows Jane McNall (who is currently the Director of Sales and Artist Relations) holding the bass outside of the Modulus shop in 93. It is a bolt-on Quantum with a 5A quilted maple top and a black cherry finish. You can see the shadow of the 12-string headstock on the wall behind Jane. The second photo shows Mike from EMG playing the same instrument. Joe Perman (now the Production Manager) built this bass, as well as another 12-string that year. The other 12 was a 3-course / 4-string with a Gibson Ripper-shaped body built for then Apple Computer development whiz Dave Fung. Dave’s bass made it to a NAMM show later in 93 where it was photographed and pictured in a trade magazine. Joe knows he built at least a few more 12-string basses, but the one in the photos and the Ripper-shaped bass are the two he can clearly recall.

Rich Lasner
President - Modulus Guitars 

Jesselli 12-String Bass Guitar
Submitted by Tom Panepinto on March 16, 2001
Serial Number  B00001-12 

This 12-string bass is a one of a kind collector's item, as are all of Joseph Jesselli's guitars.  The bass was commissioned in 1980.  I was looking for a bass that would give me the full bodied sound the band got by multi tracking guitars.  When Jesselli heard what I had in mind his face lit up, and he began to get a far away look in his eye.  Before any solid plans were made, he was already imagining a way to set the three sets of tuning pegs required.

The resulting instrument is a work of art that to this day stops even the most jaded collector dead in his tracks.  The solid body, with a shallow bowl sound chamber under the bridge, is hand carved from book matched mahogany in a fluid, art nouveau style.  The oversized neck and headpiece were fashioned from a special piece of fiddle back maple that Jesselli had been saving for a special occasion.  Three sets of gold plated Grover tuning machines were used, with the tuners for the second octave string set into the body cavity.  The bridge and tailpiece were fashioned by Jesselli from sand cast bronze and ivory.  The electronics are simple, with a single volume and tone control. 

The pickup was hand wound by Bill Bartolini.  The mother-of-pearl inlay on the ebony fingerboard is topped off with an ivory nut and mother-of-pearl  truss rod cover.  The headstock, already the largest one on any Jesselli instrument was too crowded with tuning pegs to allow the standard Jesselli name, so instead a large "J" was inlaid, also in mother-of-pearl.  The finish of the guitar is an antique white, with the mahogany grain coming through in the body. 

When Jesselli delivered this instrument to me, he insisted on looking me in the eye when I saw it for the first time.  He told me that he could see the magical moment when a player bonds with his instrument.  I know he wasn't disappointed.

Converted Aria Pro II  9-String Bass (A poor man's 12ver)
Submitted by Pekka Ranta on  Jan. 22, 2001

First let me thank you for an excellent site! I’ve been insomniac since discovering it! I’ve also sent a picture of my bass. It has only 9-strings but the basic principle is same as on a 12-string bass.

I purchased the bass from my friend a couple of years ago. It’s an early 80's Aria Pro II which was converted into a 9-stringed beast! It has E, A & D as fundamentals and double octaves for each fundamental string. EMG active electronics and EMG bass expander (which cuts mids and increases high and lows). The bridge is self-made. 

I too had dreamed of a 12ver ever since knowing it existed (I remember staring at the cover of ”At Budokan” and thinking ”what the hell is that?”) so getting this bass has helped me a lot! I use dropped-D tuning all the time and string gauges are: 095, 075 & 060 for fundamentals and 026, 032 & 040 for octaves. My rig is 1973 Hiwatt Custom 100 tube amp and Hartke 4x10” cab. It works well ’though Hartke is a bit too hi-fi for my liking (and it doesn’t look half as cool as the Hiwatt!). Haven’t had a chance to experiment with two amplifiers but playing with a clean sound has worked fine enough. When I’ll get rich etc… 


Pekka Ranta
Lappeenranta, Finland
