Capt. Steve Fallon reported they are running “open” charters Thursday and Fridays at $60 per person and first come-first serve with mandatory reservations. Long Island Sound Middle: John Richey joins Rich Johnson twice weekly, live on The Fishing Line Radio Show Thursday nights at 6:45 and Saturday afternoons at 6 p.m. to give current and live updated reports for the Huntington to Kings Park & Stony Brook areas.

Cold Spring / Huntington / Northport / Smithtown / Kings Park / Stony Brook
 Terminal Tackle (631-269-6005): John Richey reported so far this week some herring in the usual spots with semi-consistent action as they are good one day and other they just don’t bite. It is late in the season and while this is a warm winter, they really should be gone by now, but they are still hanging around. Trout action in White’s Pool is actually pretty good with routine trout 9 to 15 inch fish and some to 4 pounds with mostly browns caught and few brookies or rainbows in there. Actually pretty good with nightcrawlers but no large fish and no sea runs while one was spotted they have not caught it. A few small schoolie bass were caught but not many at all as it is not cold enough to force bait or gamefish into the warm water. Remember Terminal Tackle is open 52 weeks a year...with store hours of operation are Monday through Thursday 9 a.m. till 5 p.m. Friday 9 a.m. to 6 p.m. and Saturdays 9 to 5 p.m. and Sunday 9 a.m. till 2 p.m. On the web at www.terminaltackleco.com.

Capt. James Joseph (631-424-1253):Capt. James says the boat is done sailing for the 2005 season.  Capt. James Schneider and his crew would like to thank you for sailing and fishing with them this season and looks forward to seeing you again in the spring. Capt. James wishes you all the best for a safe and healthy holiday season. Don't forget this 38 foot Young's Brother charter boat with a 400 horsepower Cat engine that cruises at 20 knots! She is all rigged and ready to go round the clock chunking for stripers as soon as they show up in the western Sound in April. The Capt. James is located at the end of Rte. 110 in Huntington, 100 feet past Co-Co’s Club. On the web now at www.jamesjosephfishing.com.

Long Island Sound East:
Port Jefferson/Mt Sinai:
 Celtic Quest (631-928-3926): Capt. Desi reported the blackfish season has ended as they are done sailing for the season now. The cold winds, rains and snow put the end to a good going blackfish season and they expect to be sailing again the third week of April for blackfish again. They thank you al for sailing with them this season and wish you all a great, safe and happy holiday season. Also, gift certificates are available on our web site if anyone is looking for a nice stocking stuffer. This is a reservation only boat because he can get crowded weeks ahead of time so you need to plan the day you want to go fishing and get a group and make the phone call. Look for them on the web at www.celticquestfishing.com.

The Celtic Quest was the featured boat and guest on Rich Johnson's "The Fishing Line" TV show last June. This show featured some great fall blackfishing which we had with Rich this past November and the crew from The Fishing Line's message boards at www.thefishingline.org.
