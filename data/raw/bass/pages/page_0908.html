Lesson 1:

What is a bass?

body: This is the main part of the weight of the bass guitar. Make sure it's not plywood, because the quality of wood does affect the sound. Maple or mahogany are good choices.

strings: My bass has four strings. Some basses, however, have five, six, or eight strings. I tend to buy Ernie Ball hybrids because they're cheap and last for a decent amount of time.

nut: This is the slotted piece of ivory (plastic) that the strings fit into. Make sure that the slots are not too deep, so there is no fret buzzing.

tuners: This is how you tune the bass guitar.

head: The only function of the head is to hold the tuners.

neck: The neck is where all of the left hand action takes place.

volume knob: Although this can adjust the volume, I recommend using the volume on your amplifier instead. The stronger the signal coming out of the guitar and traveling through wires into the amp, the less noise.

tone knob: This is basically a passive way of adjusting the midrange.

jack: You plug in here.

bridge: The bridge holds the strings. You may want to raise or lower the bridge to fine tune your bass.

pickups: The pickups turn the vibrations from the strings into an electronic signal.
