A Fisherman's Diary

May 2001
Today is a report from a big fan of the MOR and I am more than delighted make the addition, eventhough I was atleast a thousand miles from the fishing grounds.
Russ Whitman has been a regular visitor of this website (I think there are about 4 of you guys) since my brother Matt inspired me to create it two years ago this month. Below are the pictures of White Perch and Stripped Bass as well as Russ' email message that included the pics and the details about the fish. If I'm not mistaken, Tom's River and Barnegat Sound, are spots near New Jersey. Russ, you can correct me if I'm wrong on that. And I like that t-shirt.

hey Tom,
Just a little fishing update. Hope you're doing well. I've attached a .jpg file or two of white perch. They've been the staple of early spring fishing on the Toms River. Easy dinner, excellent eating. The big guy is shy of 14" which in these parts is a rather large fishy. Most white perch range 8-10", 12" is big. Anyway, they're up here breeding and such. The big guys will disappear leaving us a fall fishery of smaller but feistier fish. Right now the weakfish fishery, (sea trout family) is busting open. Big tide runners in the spring. No electronic pictures yet but all fish in the 26" and up range. Last night at sunset or so I brought a 30", 71/2 lb. weakie on board. That's a pretty nice specimen and my biggest so far this year. I've decided to cut him up and fry him for the good folks here at Urner Barry for a snack tomorrow. Lots of bluefish are mixed in. We're going to try Striped Bass off the beach tonight but it's just the slightest bit early for catching them big time in the surf. Offshore you're doing fine. What the hell. I'll attach the file of my first Striper of 2000. We'll see what 2001 brings. May try the reef 3-5 miles off Barnegat Inlet for Sea Bass this weekend. Hope all is well!
Keep fishin'
Russ

Thanks to Russ for giving the MOR an entry for May. It has been too windy for six straight weeks off Miami. Maybe it'll lay down this weekend.
