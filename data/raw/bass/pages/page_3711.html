Daren's Fishing Palooza

 

Summertime in Alaska means a couple of things - 1) it stays light a hell of a long time and 2) the FISH are running.  The season starts with King (Chinook) Salmon taken from both offshore in saltwater and onshore in the rivers and creeks.  Favorite spots include downtown Anchorage's own muddy yet functional Ship Creek.  Seward and Homer are prime offshore locations with a tip of the cap to Alaska Saltwater Charters and the F/V Florette C out of Seward for a highly recommended day trolling for Kings.  

The season progressed well into the Sockeye (red) salmon season. These fish are harder to catch because of the technique that is a modified snag.  Imagine trying to drop a three-inch slight piece of metal into a milk bottle opening ten feet or more away and under water.  That's what it's like. The famed Russian and Kenai rivers are prime nearby locales.

The final part of the salmon season is the Coho (silver) season.  This is my favorite, with a pretty high success rate.  Casting and retrieving of spinner baits (Vibrax, Pixies, Mepp's Agilas) are the way to go.  This summer, silvers were caught in Shipp Creek, the Little Susitna River and Kenai River. Guided tours were taken with Big Sky  and Fish Tales.  I recommend the latter more so than the former.

Finally, NANA/BP employee Brad Fisher took my brother-in-law Glenn Corder and I on his boat, the Seminole, where we helped land Brad's 170+ pound halibut, plus seas bass and ling cod - all delicious fish.
