This Week Special


Dining in style Mandarin Oriental opens its buffet restaurant, Paseo Uno
Source: Businessworld
By: Raoul J. Chee Kee

Email the editor
Print this article
Email to a friend
Subscribe to our newsletter

After over six months of renovation, the Mandarin Hotel has finally opened Paseo Uno, its newest food outlet. The sumptuously designed restaurant -- built in a 612-square meter space at a cost of PhP70 million (US$1.311 million at PhP53.373=$1) and seating 143 -- is located at the hotel's lobby level. It replaced The Brasserie and offers both an a la carte menu and a buffet that is perhaps one of the best-looking and most extensive in the city.

Before they even get a chance to try the food however, patrons of the Brasserie are sure to find Paseo Uno's interiors totally different from what they were used to.

For one, the entrance has a water wall made of slabs of greenish glass, and a walkway of dark granite.

Inside, vertical glass posts divide some of the sections and provide ambient light.

That's another thing that is bound to be noticed and commented on. Instead of overhead lights that are said to cast less than flattering shadows, floor lamps of various shapes and makes dot the area. One that caught this writer's eye was a trio of contemporary mid-sized lamps made of capiz shell. There were also some made from what looked like stretched abaca fabric on a rectangular frame.

The chairs have also been getting a lot of attention -- not only because of their modern design but also because they are really nice to sit on.

Charisse G. Chuidian, Mandarin's director of communications, said that not a few people have been asking where the chairs were made. For the record, the chairs were made by the Cebu-based Mobilia.

At Paseo Uno, guests can choose to be seated in a number of spots. Instead of one big dining area, the restaurant is divided into cozy nooks and larger, more public spaces. One of the best spots would be by the plate-glass windows overlooking the hotel pool.

Dining Stations


Now, for the buffet.

Unlike other buffet setups that usually consist of a long table with chafing dishes, the buffet at Paseo Uno is divided into several stations.

According to material provided by the hotel, the Asian lifestyle and culture that is typical of the Filipino turu-turo or the Singaporean hawker stalls are manifested in the outlet's three open kitchens or live stations. These are the grilled section where the selection includes grilled meats and fish; the Asian station where guests can have soup or a noodle dish prepared in front of them; and the dessert station where the cooks can whip up a sweet crepe in a matter of minutes.

An a la carte menu proffers standard coffee shop favorites and Asian specialties including Hainanese Chicken Rice, Chicken and Pork Adobo, Malaysian Chicken Curry and Nasi Goreng. These and more are available all throughout the day, and are prepared by executive Chef Norbert Gandler, executive sous chef Humphrey Navarro and Mandarin Oriental's culinary team.

A suggestion: Leave room for the frozen peach schnapps that, like most of the desserts, are served in individual portions. There is therefore less wastage and less clutter.

David J. Thomas, the hotel's director of food and beverage told BusinessWorld that he also considered the salad station a "live kitchen" because guests are served paper-thin portions of smoked fish sliced by one of the kitchen staff.

That afternoon, we began with a few salad greens and smoked fish and began to feel full almost immediately.

Fortunately, guests can choose to partake of the salad bar for considerably less than the price of the full buffet.

Operating hours are from 6 a.m. to midnight. It serves a buffet breakfast from 6 to 10 a..m; buffet lunch from noon to 2:30 p.m.; and buffet dinner from 7 to 10 p.m.

The buffet breakfast is PhP680++ for adults and PhP360++ for children; the lunch buffet from Monday to Saturday is priced at PhP875++ for adults and PhP440++ for children; while the dinner and Sunday brunch buffets are priced at PhP885++ for adults and PhP450++ for children.

For more information, call 750-8888.
