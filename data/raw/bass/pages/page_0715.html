Smallmouth Bass
(Micropterus dolomiuei)
 

  
   

Smallmouth bass are found throughout the Grand River watershed. This species prefers swift-flowing, gravel-bottomed pools, shoals and rock piles near riffles and waterfalls. During the heat of summer they tend to seek out deeper, cooler water.

Smallmouth bass have dark olive or bronze-coloured backs with dark brown spots that converge to form vertical bars on their yellow-green sides. Dark lines radiate from the jaw and eyes to the outer edge of the gill covers.

In late May to early June, when water temperatures reach 16 to 18 C, these fish spawn over gravel or sandy bottoms near boulders or logs that provide cover for hatchlings. River smallmouth usually grow to about 40 centimetres and up to 2 kilograms. Lake or reservoir smallmouth tend to be larger.

Smallmouth bass are visual hunters attracted to the movement of small fish, crayfish, frogs and insects. Anglers can try their luck using live bait such as minnows, worms, leeches and crayfish, or spinner and crankbait lures.

Difference between smallmouth and largemouth bass

Other than the obvious difference in mouth size, the largemouth has different markings. A darker lateral line runs down its side rather than the vertical bars found on the smallmouth.
