AUGUST & SEPTEMBER 2003

Hello all you fishermen and welcome to the Carolina Princess update. I usually like to tell you all the exciting fishing news from the last month, which I shall, but first I'd like to share with you and get your opinion of something that is coming up, in fact has very much already come up, that you from North Carolina can do something about. If fact, even those of you from outside of North Carolina may be able to help with. I'm talking about of course the salt water fishing license. I am VERY, VERY much against this and you should be also.

This, in my opinion is just another tax. Don't you fell that you are taxed enough already? This tax will never add ONE, not ONE fish back to the water. In fact why should you have to buy a 10-15 dollar license to fish one or two days a year?

Tourism is North Carolinas second largest industry. Salt water fishing plays a major part in this. The fishing business has already been crippled by the National Marine Fisheries Service and the South Atlantic Fisheries Management Council and now North Carolina has intentions of making it even harder for you the fishing public to keep fishing. North Carolinians and non North Carolinians do not need this useless financial abuse.

We already have, as those of you know very many regulations on fishing. There is talk of season closures of some species and total closures on others already. Am I against regulation on the fish? No, I'm not and with the exception of the red porgy (silver snapper) I don't have a problem with what is already in place. A lot of my passengers do but I don't.

North Carolina has already hurt its tourist business with its school schedule. We now start school in mid summer and don't get out till almost the second week in June. I wonder how much tax revenue is lost because of that, and now you the fisherman are being asked to pay for that short fall under the guise of a fishing license. This of course is my personal opinion and I think it's a lot of bull crap.

I appreciate every person that fishes with us on the Carolina Princess. You do so much more than buy a ticket on by boat which I'm sorry to say is expensive by head boat standards but necessary in order for me to keep operating. You also buy gas here, you eat in our restaurants, and you sleep in out motels, and spend monies, all State taxable monies in our many shopping places. I thank you for it buy enough is enough. Where on earth would you buy a fishing license at 4:30 in the morning in the first place? I'm sure there are some state employees that start work that early. I just don't know any. Please call our state senators and representatives, and those of you from out of North Carolina can call them also and let them know you do not support this license. Call the representative and senator in your district. Those of you from outside of North Carolina can call State Senator Scott Thomas at 919-733-6275 and Jean Preston at 919-733-5706 or Lt. Governor Beverly Perdue at 919-733-6248. Thank you very much for your help.

Getting back to fishing - the first part of August was pretty rough weather but we sailed just about every day and still done some partly fair fishing. The last part of August was great weather and even better fishing. Good catches of trigger fish and large red snappers also some nice sized vermilion snapper showing up.

We are finished with our 1/2 day trips for the 2003 season but the Monday and Wednesday stand up sport fishing trips are starting so don't forget to make your reservation to catch giant Wahoo this fall. Dolphin and a few tuna are still around and so far it has lots of hot sailfish action. As always call at any time for fishing information or reservations either on the Carolina Princess or for a private charter boat.


The best to you,
Woo Woo
