Braggin' photos from Bassmex clients

Keith "Catfish" Sutton

I suppose every who fishes has memories of certain trips that rise above the rest.  Some of my best memories are of a four-day trip I made to Campo Buena Vista on Lake Huites last November.  I've been fortunate enough to travel throughout the United States (47 states), Canada, Mexico, Europe, Asia and South America.  But seldom have I enjoyed myself as much as I did at Campo Buena Vista.

Lake Huites is one of the most beautiful lakes I've ever seen.  The camp sits high on a mountainside with an incredible view of this magnificent canyon country.  And when you're actually in a boat, it's hard to concentrate on fishing because you're right in the midst of all this awesome scenery--incredible rock formations, lots of wildlife, blue water.  Thinking about it now makes me wish I was back there.

The fishing is the spice that adds even more wonderful flavor to an already tasty adventure.  I caught many dandy Florida-strain largemouths, and one afternoon, my fishing partner, Mark Davis with Shakespeare Tackle, caught one bass after another for two hours while I perched high above him on a rock ledge shooting photographs.  He literally caught a bass on almost every cast during that time, including many over five pounds.  I didn't catch any monster bass myself on the trip (I'm not that good a bass fishermen), but my buddies all caught some dandies.

Wild Bill Skinner caught some real nice fish on his Pop-A-Top chuggers, and Billy Murray, the famous pro bass angler and a representative for PRADCO, the lure manufacturing company, caught one nine-pound-plus largemouth that I was fortunate enough to photograph.  The big bass are definitely there, and there are plenty of two- to five-pounders to keep you busy all day long.

The camp itself is first-class all the way.  The rooms were comfortable and conveniently located to the open-air palapa where all the meals were served.  And I'll tell you without hesitation that Chi-Willy, the camp's A-number-1 first class cook, prepares some of the best meals you'll ever sit down to.  There's lots of traditional Mexican fare, that's far more delicious than the Mexican restaurant offerings we get back in the U.S.  Home-made tortillas and salsa at every meal (my favorite).  And entreés like you wouldn't believe--steaks, fish, shrimp and more.  The T-bones Chi-Willy grilled us one night were the biggest I've ever seen!  And there's so much food put out on the table, there's no way you could go away hungry.  Plan on bringing a belt with two extra notches punched in it.  Chances are, you'll get bigger around the middle with Chi-Willy cooking for you.

If you enjoy watching wildlife, like I do, you'll find plenty to fascinate you around Huites.  One day, near the dam, I saw five species of falcons.  That's incredible.  I don't know anywhere else in North America where that could happen.  Another day, I saw every species of dove in North America.  Ospreys are everywhere, and native birds you can't see north of the border.  There are deer, coatimundis, a variety of small mammals, and if you're lucky, you might hear or get a glimpse of a jaguar.  They're rare, but they're here. 

Bring lots of film for your camera.  You'll want plenty so you can take home lots of pictures of the big bass you catch, the incredibly beautiful landscape and this wonderful fishing camp.  The staff is one of the best I've ever had a chance to get acquainted with.  The guides are great.  Everything is first class.  I'm planning another trip already, and I can't wait to get back.

Keith "Catfish" Sutton
