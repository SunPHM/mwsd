KIDS
"Dragon" 12-String Bass Guitar

Kids 12-string basses  were made by the Hiroshigi Kids Guitar Company in Japan. They have subsequently gone out of business.

Tom Petersson played two Kids 12-Strings: A dark purple one (shown here with the Dragon inlay on the body) which he used on the Conan O'Brien TV show to play a song called Cold Turkey and a teal green one (shown below) during the "album" shows through the end of 98.  Tom used the teal green Kids bass on the song You're All Talk where he would tune the low E's up to G so he didn't have to hold that third fret "G" for  the whole song.

The Kids Dragon bass was based specifically upon Tom Petersson's Hamer B12A bass with the Planet Hologram paint job. That bass had a slightly different headstock and the neck was thinner than the standard B12A.

Using this bass as a starting point, a brainstorming meeting to design the actual Kids 12-string bass was held between a number of parties at The House of Blues in Los Angeles. Specifics like a smaller body and other changes from the Hamer design were finalized at that meeting.

There were only a total of 20 to 24 Kids 12-string basses made. Colors included purple, black, red, teal green, yellow and blue.

Tom has (or had) several of these basses, and Nikki Sixx of Motley Crue is reported to have five of them. These basses were a one-time special production run, with Tom and Nikki specifying such things as inlays, pickups and colors. There was a demand for additional basses, but Kids was unable to supply them.

The Kids design could be considered the predecessor of the Chandler Royale. Notice the similarities between the Kids and the Chandler design. The body, pickups, control configurations, and even the headstocks are very similar. Many Cheap Trick fans mistake the Kids for the Chandler.

Besides the Dragon 12vers, Kids also made the "Magical Mystery Tour" Strat for Rick Neilsen, the "Lightning Bolt" Korina and "a couple of V's".
