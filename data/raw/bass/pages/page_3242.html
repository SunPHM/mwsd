REAL TIME WATER FLOW DATA IN MARYLAND

Real time water flow data is available 24 hours/day from the United States Geological Survey. We have inserted links in the report below for many of the streams and rivers discussed in the fishing report. For additional stations, go to the main real time USGS water flow page .

Casselman River: As of June 24, 2002 the Long Term Median Flow (LTMF) in Grantsville, Maryland is 33.5 cfs with a flow of 18 cfs and staging at 0.89 feet. Caddis (#14 - 16), #14 light cahills, and #14 sulphurs are a few fly options.

Click for the latest real time stream flows on the Casselman.

North Branch of the Potomac River: As of June 24, 2002, at the Barnum gauging station the LTMF is 202 cfs with a flow of 222 cfs and staging at 2.98 feet. Flies to have on hand include #20 blue winged olives, #12 March browns, and #12-14 caddis.

Click for the latest real time stream flows on the North Branch of the Potomac at Barnum.

Savage River: As of June 24, 2002 in Bloomington the LTMF is 56 cfs with a flow of 84 cfs and staging at 0.97 feet. Fly options include tan and olive #14-18 caddis, #18 blue winged olives, #14 pale morning dun, #14 sulphurs, and #12-14 light cahills.

Click for the latest real time stream flows on the Savage River near Bloomington, MD.

Youghiogheny: As of June 24, 2002 the LTMF near Oakland is 86.5 cfs with a flow of 29 cfs and staging at 2.03 feet. The LTMF in Friendsville is 290 cfs with a flow of 601 cfs and staging at 3.20 feet. Tan and olive #14-18 caddis, #18 blue winged olives, #14 pale morning dun, and #14 sulphurs have been productive.

Click for the latest real time stream flows on the Youghiogheny near Oakland, MD.

Gunpowder River: Brown bodied caddis (#16), sulphurs (#20-22), ants (#16 and #18), hoppers (#14-16) and beetles (#14 and #16) are good options. As of June 24, 2002 the LTMF at Little Falls at Blue Mount is 56 cfs with a flow of 15 cfs and staging at .21 feet.

Brown trout have been tagged with visual implant tags just behind the left eye socket (pictured on the left - click for a larger view). If you catch a tagged brown trout please contact Central Regional Manager, Charlie Gougeon, at 410-442-2080 or via e-mail at cgougeon2@dnr.state.md.us with the following information: date and location of the catch, tag number, tag color, kept or released, and any other information you feel is important.

Nontidal Potomac River: As of June 24, 2002 at the Williamsport Water Treatment Plant the River is 1.6 feet and falling, cloudy, and 81 degrees Fahrenheit. Raymond Ply and his friend Steven Miller of Hyattsville, Maryland caught several smallmouth bass fishing upriver of White's Ferry last Friday. Pictured on the right is Steven Miller with one of the many bass he caught. Troy Brown of Williamsport, Maryland caught a 2 pound 14.5 ounce largemouth bass fishing Big Pool on a plastic lizard last week. Brook trout State record holder, Mike Fiorita, Jr. was fishing the Conococheague where he caught and released a 3 pound, 6 ounce smallmouth bass using a live stonecat.

LAKES, PONDS, AND RESERVOIRS

Deep Creek Lake (Garrett County): Fishing is holding steady. Worms fished in 3 to 12 feet continued to hook bluegills and yellow perch. Evening time has been particularly productive. Several 10 inch bluegills have been caught. Smallmouth and largemouth bass continued to bite minnows and plastics. Surface plugs have been good on calm evenings. Streamers and poppers have produced for fly fishermen. Ken Padgett of Pasadena, Maryland caught a 4 pound, 8 ounce smallmouth bass that measured 20.25 inches. Dennis Leatherman of Oakland, Maryland caught a 20 inch largemouth bass that weighed 4 pounds, 10 ounces. Chain pickerel continued to be caught while bass fishing.

Information on whitewater boating releases is available on the Deep Creek Hydro Hotline at (814) 533-8911 or online at URL: http://www.deepcreekhydro.com/

Liberty Reservoir (Carroll County): This reservoir is down 17.70 feet as of June 24, 2002. Fishing is holding steady. Shoreline bass fishing has been good using medium to large shiners, crayfish, chicken liver, crankbaits, and 10-inch power worms. Fathead minnows, Mini Mites, and maggots have been catching small crappies at Nicodemus Bridge. A few stripers have been caught at Snake Point and Eagle Point.

Little Seneca Lake (Montgomery County): Water temperature is approximately 80 degrees and there is 6 feet of visibility. Main lake points, wood structure and grass have been good places to target bass. Jigs with a plastic trailer and Texas-rigged hula grubs have been productive lures. Nighttime fishing opportunities for this year are June 26, July 17, August 22, and September 18th.

Loch Raven Reservoir (Baltimore County): This reservoir is down 3.88 feet as of June 24, 2002. Loch Raven Fishing Center reported white perch have been caught trolling a spinner hook with a night crawler in 12 to 15 feet of water. Chain pickerel and bass have been active throughout the impoundment. Plastic worms, pig and jigs, or stickbaits worked on points, stumps, and grassbeds in 10 to 15 feet of water. Plugs are catching in the morning and evening.

The Maryland Geological Survey has a bathymetry map on their website.

Prettyboy Reservoir (Baltimore County): This reservoir is down 29.58 feet as of June 24, 2002. Plastic worms, crankbaits, and surface plugs fished off points and bars have been catching bass.

WSSC Triadelphia and Rocky Gorge Reservoirs: Water temperature was approximately 80 degrees last weekend with 3 feet of visibility. Anglers have been catching largemouth and smallmouth bass at both reservoirs fishing rocky points, humps, and flats. Carolina rigged lizards, jigs with plastic trailers, and spinnerbaits have been productive. Pictured on the right is Chris Peloquin with a 5 pound 11 ounce largemouth bass he caught last Saturday at Triadelphia. Jigs, worms, and crankbaits have been catching crappies. Live worms or minnows, crankbaits, and jigs have caught some walleyes.
