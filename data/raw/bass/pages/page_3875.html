[13 Feb 2003|01:47am]
Eating's fun.

But dying's not, so after Italian "heart-attack inducing" week, I decided to go back to slightly lighter fare. More seafood, less butter. Same tasty.

Tuesday I was a little short on time (the perils of waking up really, really late) so I had to whip up something quick. I made penne pasta with peas in a lemon cream sauce & the same herb-crusted chicken as we made in Spectacular Spectacular topped with some shaved prosciutto. It was good, though I wish we had enough prosciutto to replace the chicken altogether. >=)

Wednesday, Mazie came over for a Roman-style fish soup (modified off this recipe). Hans (one of the roommates) and I had visited a fish market Tuesday (it was okay... good for this area, I guess) and bought a lot of seafood. No squid, though, so I used scallops instead and also used catfish for the fish (they were out of red snapper and sea bass is a little pricey!). Also added extra pepper for the kick (Josh, one of the roommates, really likes heat.) Yum! Served it with the 2001 J.&H.A. Strub Niersteiner Bruckchen Riesling Kabinett, which was a pretty light wine... a little less dry than I had expected, but still good. It's definitely a wine Father Yee would enjoy.

Last night I pulled an all-nighter working on a project, so I didn't wake up till three-thirty! So my schedule's all messed up! The thing about sleep schedules is that once you mess them up, it's difficult to get them back on track. Last year I was going to bed regularly at six-seven and waking up at four due to some all-nighters I pulled early in the year. Eep! Gotta stop treading on the downward sleep-spiral...

Anyways, today I cooked Javanese roasted salmon & wilted spinach. It turned out very well -- the sauce has this great delayed hit of lime that's just very refreshing. Jeff Feng, our guest for the evening, enjoyed it greatly as well, nodding enthusiastically and whatnot. Heh!
