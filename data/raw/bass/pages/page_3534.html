Centropristis ocyurus Bank sea bass Serranidae

Distinguishing Features
The bank sea bass has a white overall body color with bluish-brown areas on the head and among the spots that run along the base of the dorsal fin and along the lateral line. The caudal fin has three lobes.

Distribution
Bank sea bass range from North Carolina to northeastern Florida. They are also widespread within the Gulf of Mexico, but absent from the Florida Keys.

Habitat
This species is found on hard bottoms and shipwrecks at depths to about 55m..

Ecology
Bank sea bass tend to be solitary, territorial fish. Increases in the abundance of this species have been observed on reefs that have experienced increasingly targeted fishing pressure on snappers and groupers (1). This species probably feeds on benthic invertebrates when small and switches to small fish when larger. This species is a protogynous hermaphrodite.
