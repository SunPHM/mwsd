Bryan Adams has grown to be the most popular mainstream Canadian rocker of the late '80s/early '90s, but as this is a bass guitarists' website the question has to be "Yes, but is he legitimate?" Bryan Guy Adams was born on 5 November 1959, in Kingston, Ontario, Canada. He's unquestionably popular, and he's unquestionably a talented singer songwriter and six-string guitarist.

In a review in the Beacon Journal Pete Chakerian said 'No matter how much integrity or heart they seem to possess, pop rockers never get a fair shake. Even before the grunge and indie-rock trend taught the masses that success was lame, rock critics have been getting paid to professionally chide the pop genre. But follow those hated critics home one night after work. They're the first people to put on an old sweater, eat comfort food and watch reruns if they've had a bad day. Digressions aside, Canadian rocker Bryan Adams might be all of these things. Comfortable, like your favorite T-shirt and jeans. Simple, like vanilla ice cream. Easy, like Sunday morning. But he's a crowd pleaser that can keep the hits coming and the people smiling. This ``Peter Pan of pop'' did just that last night when he breezed into the Palace Theater. After nearly a four-year absence from the North Coast, Adams' delivered a veritable, two-hour tour de force -- generously laden with rollicking summer songs and gooey, heartfelt ballads. At times, it went down more like soft-serve than your favorite homemade variety of vanilla. But the minimalist approach -- stark staging, bare musical arrangements, Adams fronting a trio in lieu of his usual band -- gave the show a solid, undeniable vibe. ``Hello, Cleveland,'' Adams announced early on. ``My name is Bryan. Remember me?'' Backed by longtime bandmates Mickey Curry (drums) and Keith Scott (guitars), he then tackled a hearty, 26-song setlist. Adams also tackled bass-playing duties himself, an arrangement that yielded mixed results... But most importantly, Adams and his compatriots had a good time. And so did the crowd.' Hmmm. Mixed results.

With a career spanning over 20 years and worldwide album sales that rival the biggest recording artists in the world, Bryan Adams's "I'm just the bass player in the band" attitude seems rather simple,    compared to his career achievements, but that's just how he is.

In a interview for Marshall amps he said "Bass has become my passion and it all stems from making records and Keith buying me a Beatle bass to mess around with for my birthday a few years ago. We tour now as a power trio cuz it's a blast. We've trimmed down the arrangements to the point of silliness, but I'm not interested in going back to the large ensemble just yet. We're having too much fun at the moment. It took me some time to get my head around singing and playing bass, but I have it fairly together now. Jody my sound man, laughs at me, because he says I would never have hired a bass player with one year of experience. and he's right! But my naiveity towards the instrument is working, it's like playing in a new band." In Marshall artist news we're informed that on his most recent tour Bryan used four VBA400 heads on top of four AX 4x10 bass cabinets and four BX 2x15 EV bass cabinets.

Music Reporter Bartley Kives reviewed Bryan's January 2000 Winnipeg concert: "For most of the show, Adams played the bass guitar, switching to a six-string only for light arrangements of songs such as the ballad Everything I Do I Do It For You, the mid-tempo On A Day Like Today and a gentle version of Straight From The Heart. "Keith really is the star of the show. Forget about the bass player," Adams joked. Yeah right, Bryan. As much as I expected to despise you and your mercilessly unhip show, I have to admit the simple rock schtick does look good on you."

I saw Bryan Adams in concert in Adelaide, Australia in March 2000. I was watching Bryan's playing and was surprised to see him working away on simple runs and mainly thumping out root notes of chords. Of course we came to see Bryan and hear him sing our favourite songs, but the lesson for me was "lots of volume, keep up a steady bass beat, and it doesn't have to be complex to work." Bryan and the band seemed to really enjoy the show. The guys were laughing and smiling and really got off on what they were doing. The show was a bit shorter than I'd expected but the amount of energy was amazing. Those three guys were putting everything they had into their work and gave us a show I'll remember for a long time to come!

So is Bryan Adams a great bass guitarist? No way! His bass playing was at best basic, but it worked. He's legit.
