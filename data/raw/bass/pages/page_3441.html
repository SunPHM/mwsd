Brazilian Aquaculture

Aquaculture in Brazil until recently has been of relatively little importance globally. More recently, due to increasing demands on wild stocks of popular species, more attention has been turned to commercialise operations to meet local, and more recently, international demands.

Amazonian Redtail

The Amazonian Redtail, under massive pressure from wild capture fisheries was an ideal fish for consideration by the aquaculture industry. Culture of this species has largely been a success in some areas of the Amazon region, alleviating pressure on wild stocks to such a degree that restocking programmes from the farm, plus the availability of farmed product for local markets have both brought the fish back from the edge of extinction. The species is farmed in earth pond, cage and extensive lake systems, offering flexible production options in a controlled system with no environmental impact. In co-operation with fish farms in Brazil, Superfresh have been able to commercialise this species to a level which makes it available to a global market, offering a high quality sustainable product farmed without comprimising the species or its environment.



Tambaqui

Tambaqui farming has occurred across Brazil for many years now. The fish are ideal for a farming environment, growing quickly in earth ponds or cage systems, to produce a great tasting, unique product. Again, such farms, once controlled with correct management will remove some of the pressure on wild stocks, and offer a product of consistent quality and flavour. Farms under Superfresh are provided with fry from hatcheries using hypothisation techniques, so giving year round production capabilities. Growing on facilities are monitored regularly, ensuring the best water quality is available. Consequently, there are no disease issues with Superfresh farmed tambaqui. Using in house facilities, pellet formulation optimises this species' unique flavour and texture.
