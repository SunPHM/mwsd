Famous Monsters Earth, Wind, & Fire



Anybody can throw a party. When Monster gathers 5,000 of their closest friends and Earth, Wind, & Fire is the evening's entertainment it's not a party…it's an event. Indeed, on January 7, 2003, a few hours after Day 2 of the Consumer Electronics Show, of one of the world's biggest tradeshows, came to a close, Monster's Earth, Wind, & Fire concert became the talk of the town. And, with good reason.



Here's what went down…

After a thundering intro that shook the Venetian hotel, the EWF entourage took the stage and the lingering first notes of "In The Stone" cut through the crowd with a vengeance. The crowd, a "who's who" of Consumer Electronics heavyweights were instantly on their feet, cheering, dancing and getting off on the stone groove EWF layed down.

The band, which consists of founding members Verdine White, Phillip Bailey, and Ralph Johnson and a cast of incredibly gifted musicians wasted no time getting to the heart of the matter with a firing squad of hits including "Boogie Wonderland" and the timeless and moving "After the Love is Gone".

Next up was Verdine's bass spotlight solo. A flurry of endless energy, charisma and larger-than-life stage presence, Verdine thumped out funky bass lines and strutted right into the crowd, even spending a few moments prancing atop one lucky audience member's table!. A killer percussion solo featuring Phillips impressive chops was next.

Phillip's voice was in fine form. He hit notes only an elite few can, on "September" "Sing A Song" and "Shining Star." After "Star", the band beckoned Noel Lee, The Head Monster to the stage. Noel, the man who made the whole evening possible, presented EWF with a Monsterous Lifetime Achievement Award---A towering 24k gold contact connector complete with inscription from Noel to the band.
