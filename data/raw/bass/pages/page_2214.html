Comments:

     This wonderful electric upright bass is from Italy, homeland of the great masters of violin makers, and is for us a perfect combination of old world craftsmanship and innovative, modern technology. This Ballade model has instead of a conventional headstock just a very nice scroll on top of the neck which saves a few inches for ease of transportation, but also increases the mass of the tailpiece for a little more sustain and attack in the tone. The bass has the feel of a very fine handmade instrument and plays like a dream both arco or plucked. The engineering and craftsmanship is nothing short of amazing - we made quite a few photo-details in an attempt to illustrate how this bass is much more than just another wooden-stick with strings. The top of the bass is actually a suspended, free-floating structure, much like a normal upright bass, and the whole instrument resonates and responds somewhat similarly to a fully accoustic, full-bodied instrument. The clever and very elegant body extensions are ergonomically designed and easily/quickly assembled with the large nylon knobs. The bass travels in its padded and waterproof softcase, slings easily over the shoulder, and will fit in most cars (even smaller ones). If someone requires that it be even smaller, the bass is can be disassembled and fits then in a super small softcase (46x15x7 inches), but it does takes some time to fully disassemble and reconstruct the bass. The high end "dual hearing" class A electronics features next-to-normal output, and also a special headphone output is also provided - it is optimized for use with the included Sennheiser headphones. The onboard electronics also allow selecting between hi/low levels for the piezo and independently setting the volume for the headphones, and the bass sounds naturally warm, with a well-defined attack, and the right amount of sustain.
     For those who must travel extensively, or perhaps just gig around town a lot and have become tired of having to transport their enourmous upright, this bass seems to be a perfect solution. It gives the touring bassist an instrument that is both fully reliable and has all the indispensable controls required for professional-level performance - a working tool with a feel of a nice handmade instrument.
