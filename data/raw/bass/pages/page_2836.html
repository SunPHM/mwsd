MEXICO BASS FISHING TRIPS


AND PEACOCK BASS FISHING IN BRAZIL


Westside Adventures offers the BEST Mexico bass fishing and Mexico hunting available! Enjoy great Mexico bass fishing at Lake El Salto, Lake Comedero, Lake Mateos, Lake Guerrero, Lake Baccarac, Lake Huites, and Lake Agua Milpa . We also offer Brazil Peacock Bass Fishing on the Amazon River.

Each one of these Mexico bass fishing locations has its own unique characteristics. We arrange fishing trips and hunting trips into areas that have tremendous fishing and hunting, fantastic accomodations, and staffs that know how to treat people well.

PEACOCK BASS ON THE AMAZON RIVER: The Santana I is the ideal peacock bass fishing yacht for the expert or novice fisherman , your peacock bass fishing buddies, couples, youngsters, and corporate travel. You will enjoy the exciting top-water action of the peacock bass whether you fish with conventional equipment or on the fly! . Click HERE for more Brazil peacock bass fishing information.

LAKE EL SALTO: The most popular Mexico bass fishing lake. Lake El Salto, is located 50 miles north of the seaside resort of Mazatlan. Lake El Salto has a healthy population of trophy size bass. The aggressive bass of El Salto will strike a variety of lures all year, but topwaters and crankbaits are the best. Click HERE for more Lake El Salto information.


LAKE COMEDERO: This Mexico bass fishing lake had a great reputation years ago and then the fishing fell off. Well, it is back and the bass fishing is very strong. Without any doubt, Lake Comedero is the world's best bass lake for numbers of bass from 8 lbs. to 12 lbs. with some bass ranging from 12 to 15 lbs. Daily catches average 40 to 60 bass per day per boat. It is totally a catch and release lake. Comedero Bass Club began stocking Florida strain bass in the lake 10 years ago, and some of these bass now weigh 12 to 15 lbs. Click HERE for more Lake Comedero information.

LAKE MATEOS: Fishing on Lake Mateos at this particular time is an angler’s dream come true! Phenomenal numbers have been caught, and, like in the past, two good anglers can currently expect to catch 100 to 200 fish per boat per day, including trophy fish (depending on ability). Lake Mateos is a world-class topwater and spinnerbait lake. All your favorite baits will work. Click HERE for more Lake Mateos information.


LAKE GUERRERO: Lake Guerrero is still one of the best Mexico bass fishing lakes available for catching Trophy Black Bass, Guerrero is also the closest to the border. The drive takes approximately 3 1/2 hours from Harlingen, Texas. Fly in to the Lodge by air charter on your private aircraft. Click Over the last two years the water level has risen 67 feet making for better Mexico bass fishing trips.HERE for more Lake Guerrero information.



LAKE BACCARAC: Lake Baccarac is the best big bass lake in the entire world — was stocked in 1978 with a strain of Florida largemouth bass. The first fishing season was beyond most angler’s dreams and the lake has been improving every year. Lake Baccarac is actually unbelievable in the numbers of large fish 13 to 15 lbs. that it continues to produce every season. Click HERE for more Lake Baccarac information.

LAKE HUITES: Many are predicting that Lake Huites will be THE MEXICO BASS FISHING LAKE for this bass fishing season! Lake Huites is one of the most scenic bass fishing lakes a bass fisherman will ever set his eyes on. Realistic expectations are to catch 80 - 100 fish per day, per boat. The average size running 3 to 7 pounds, with an occasional fish over 7 pounds. Click HERE for more Lake Huites information.


LAKE AGUA MILPA: This Mexico bass fishing lake has shown itself to be a major contender as one of the best bass lakes available to the American fishermen. There is no doubt that Agua Milpa will continue to produce large numbers of quality fish for the next several years. Most clients will experience 80 to 100 fish per day, per boat. The bass will range from 2 to 6 pounds, and a number of clients will catch bass in the 7 to 9 pound range. Click HERE for more Lake Agua Milpa information.
