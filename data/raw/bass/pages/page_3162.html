First Drive: 2001 2500HD/3500 Series Chevrolet Silverado and GMC Sierra
Final Link in GM Truck Revamp Gives HD Line a Power Trip
 By John Clor
 Date Posted 01-01-1999


(Enlarge photo)
Both Chevy's Silverado and GMC's Sierra can be had as a 2500 HD (single rear wheel) or 3500 Series (dual rear wheel) model, available in Regular Cab, four-door Extended Cab, Crew Cab and Chassis Cab and in either two- or four-wheel drive. (Photo courtesy of General Motors Corporation)

(Enlarge photo)
As our grownup 'toys' grow bigger, so must our trucks. With a healthy economy, truckmakers expect to see continued growth in the heavy-duty pickup market. (Photo courtesy of General Motors Corporation)

(Enlarge photo)
HD trucks are just the ticket for the growing numbers of people who are using big 'fifth-wheel' or 'gooseneck' trailers towed from a receiver mounted in the truck bed, similar to the system used on semis. (Photo courtesy of General Motors Corporation)

(Enlarge photo)
An HD truck isn't just for work, as the 'play' part of ownership is now driving the segment. Here, a Silverado makes quick work of extracting a bass fishing boat from the water. (Photo courtesy of General Motors Corporation)

(Enlarge photo)
Some 'fifth-wheel' travel trailers are so big and heavy that they need to ride on three axles, but towing even these rolling homes couldn't overtax the diesel-powered GM trucks that we tested. (Photo courtesy of General Motors Corporation)

(Enlarge photo)
The addition of Allison automatic transmissions with 'grade braking' and 'shift stabilization' features to GM's HD truck line makes the task of towing tricky trailers such as this horse hauler far easier. (Photo courtesy of General Motors Corporation)

(Enlarge photo)
Thanks to class-leading horsepower in both its gas and diesel engines, GM's HD trucks have no problem pulling even hefty loads like large luxury watercraft. (Photo courtesy of General Motors Corporation)

(Enlarge photo)
One of the secrets to success in the HD class is diesel power, so GM teamed with Isuzu to develop an all-new Duramax 6600 turbodiesel V8 that packs 300 horses and a stump-pulling 520 foot-pounds of torque. (Photo courtesy of Jim Fets)

(Enlarge photo)
For those who prefer gasoline power, GM offers an all-new Vortec 8100 V8 with a segment-topping 340 horses and 455 foot-pounds of torque—both numbers more than the V10s offered by Ford and Dodge. (Photo courtesy of Jim Fets)

(Enlarge photo)
HD interiors are every bit as nice as GM's light-duty full-size pickups and SUVs. For commercial use, you can opt for all-vinyl seats and rubber-mat floors; for a luxury touch, fancy leather seating is also available. (Photo courtesy of Jim Fets)
