Bass fishing forecast for this month on Okeechobee is expected to be excellent. Lake Okeechobee water level is back to normal now at 14 1/2 feet. We have grass edges that are holding good amounts of bass in the Monkey Box and North Shore areas that now extend out to the open areas of the lake. Baitfish are everywhere and the water clarity is good.

Florida Wild Shiner Bass Fishing Brag'n Board
6 1/4 lb., 5 1/4 lb. & 4 1/2'er - October 9 & 10, 2001

Florida wild shiner bass fishing anglers Bob Craley and his 12 year-old son Lee (York, PA - ph: 717-757-7696 - e-mal: Butch918@aol.com or TeresaC@yorktwn.com) put up with gale force winds during the first afternoon of their wild shiner bass fishing trip on October 9, 2001 with Cap'n Ron. To stay out of the wind we fished the cuts of Harney Pond Canal where Bob's son Lee caught 2 bass up to 4 lbs. Bob accidentally hooked into a manatee that required the line to be cut before Bob was pulled overboard! The wind clalmed down overnight to allow us to get out on the lake the next morning October 10, 2001. Bob and his son Lee caught 7 bass during their morning trip that inclued a 6 1/4 lb. (top left), 5 1/4 lb. (top right) and 4 1/2 lb'er (bottom left) that were caught by Bob's son. Two channel catfish were also boated. All fish were released except the 6 1/4 lb'er which is being mounted. Bass and catfish were caught in the Harney Pond Canal and North Shore areas of Lake Okeechobee.

Lee (left) with a nice 4 1/2 lb'er he caught and released. Bob and son Lee and the airboat they fished out of with Cap'n Ron.
