The World's Best Bass Fishing
by Pete Bowman

Well, we finally did it. We took the Fish'n Canada TV crew out of Canada and into an exotic fishing location. Every year Angelo, Reno and I ponder the thought of going against the "Canada only" theme that we've locked ourselves into and possibly taking on the rest of the world. This time we just went for it!

Our destination was a man made lake called El Salto, near Mazatlan on the Pacific coast of Mexico. El Salto is without a doubt the most talked about Largemouth Bass lake in the world right now and justly so. It's recently had more 8 pound plus bass caught than any other single body of water anywhere! The really unique thing about this flooded reservoir is that it still shows signs of the once dry land. There are cities, cemeteries, hydro poles, roads and pretty well anything else one can imagine... all of which can hold lots of bass, really big bass!

El Salto also has a healthy water hyacinth growth on its surface, which not only provides the bass with good cover but also protects certain areas of the lake by not allowing access to would be trophy hunters. These weeds are so thick that on certain days you simply can't get to the fish. The weird part about hyacinth is it's not attached to the bottom, it floats freely so one day an area can be totally blocked and the next day the wind has completely cleared it.


El Salto's heavy Hyacinth growth provides bass with vast amounts of cover.

Our Trip

Upon arrival at the Mazatlan airport we were met by our host Fred Ward of El Salto Pro Bass Adventures and on our way to the small authentic town of La Cruz (15 minutes from the lake) where we'd set up home base. Now one might expect that you'd be staying in a seedy little dump out in the middle of nowhere but that's just not the case. Fred has you staying in new, air conditioned motel rooms with fantastic meals and ice cold cerveza's to boot. My kind of place!


A small town Mexican oasis.

The only negative we had so far was Fred's warning that our timing (August) was the worst time of the year for both the fishing and the anglers. As far as the fishing is concerned, the hot summer will drive the big fish deep and all over the lake, making them hard to find. As far as the fishermen are concerned it's the unbearable heat that drives you off the lake. Fred also stated that we would probably catch more big fish on this trip than we've ever caught. Pretty good for the worst time of the year but still bad in his opinion.

The Fishing

Our crew decided that Reno would take the first TV shift and he'd shoot a show with Fred pertaining to his operation. Angelo and myself would stay off in the wings and try our hand at figuring out an idea for a second show.


The Fish'n Canada Show at work in another country.

It didn't take the boys long to start rolling tape. They worked worms, Senkos and jigs in standing timber located anywhere from 5 to 12 feet of water. Fred would cast one direction and set the hook (this guy's got the touch) and Reno would yank one from a totally different direction.
The problem was the fish were small. "Lets go" Fred would say to Reno and they'd be off. Fred's also a run & gun type of bass fisherman.


Team "Run & Gun" sticking out a spot for a couple of casts.

As the boys took off to their second spot Ang, myself and our guide Tim Galloway of Angling Arizona picked a spot which Tim's never tried, a bluff wall. On my very first cast with a flipping tube, I set on a 6 pounder. This fish jumped, pulled drag and did everything a bass is supposed to do (not bad for the worst time of year, Fred). About twenty feet down the bank Ang would set the hook, then me, then him again. Although it wasn't every cast it was a great way to start.

Uh oh, Fred and Reno are on the move again, we gotta' follow. The next stop was a big flat between two stands of trees. We pull just out of camera view and start casting. Ang is using a jig and I'm throwing a deep Fat Free Shad crankbait. All of a sudden I feel a little "mush" on my bait, I give it a few slow cranks and whack, set the hook into a solid fish. Huge jump, huge splash, huge fight and into the net. Angelo guesses 8-15 and the fish goes 9 on Tim's scales. Good guess. A few minutes later Ang ties on a medium perch pattern Salmo crankbait, fires out between two big patches of hyacinth and up comes one close to 8... and about 5 more the same size and bigger trying to hit everything from his bait in the fishes mouth to even the fish itself! He actually had a chance to catch two bass on the same cast that would have teamed up to about 15 pounds! Unreal. We scoot off to another similar area and I get a 7 and a 5 on back-to-back casts (yeah right Fred, this fishing really sucks).


Fishing the "cemetery" can be a very haunting experience!

Meanwhile our illustrious TV stars are happily filling the boat with nice fish, but are still looking for that "Lunker".Fred's now got the boat screaming to the graveyard, the "cemetery" as it's called. What an eerie spot. The boys actually start casting to headstones and crosses, it almost feels sacrilegious. Not much happening here, the spot's dead (pun intended). All of a sudden 200 yards away the water erupts with a huge school of feeding bass, hundreds of them. It was almost like the fish gods were present and coaxing the boys away from the area. A quick jaunt with the electric motor had Reno and Fred casting exactly where the brief explosion occurred. We watched as they boated bass after bass until finally as if on cue, Reno nailed a giant on a spinnerbait. When the camera stopped rolling we yelled over "how big" and the response was "at least a ten."
Yeah baby!!! Mission one completed.


Reno Viola proudly shows his big "10".

The Other 2 Guys

Angelo and I were up next. We started at an area close to where Reno caught his hawg. The previous day I nailed a 7 and an 8 within a cast of where we were setting up. The fishing was pretty slow until Ang reared back and set into a horse.
"It's coming up, it's coming up," he yelled and up it came... spitting the jig in the process. "That was a 7+ for sure," he said.
What a bummer. Our first fish is a mule and we loose it! Not to worry because on Ang's next cast - to the exact spot I might add - he nails a clone to the first one, probably closer to 8 than 7. This one's in the boat and on tape. Great start.

Our next area was a wooded point Tim wanted us to try just in case something was there. He'd previously caught a 14 there so it was well worth a stop. Angelo was using his big jig while I tossed my deep cranker. Smack! I nail a small one. Smack! I nail another and another.
Ang ties on a Risto Rap and we both catch fish one after another. I finally hook a nice one, she jumps and SWISH, my crank flies through the air. Damn. Oh well, keep chucking.
Boom! I set on a beauty, a good 8 and on nearly the next cast the inevitable happens. Angelo sets the hook hard and his fish immediately heads for deep water. No other fish has done that on this point.
He yells, "Big fish boys". His line, which started with a cast directly in front of the boat, is now straight out 90 degrees beside us... and still going! "Really big fish boys," he screams strain fully.
Just then, POP, his 20-pound mono breaks like thread. Oh my god. We sat motionless for a moment while we pondered a long swim to the Cemetery to rest in peace.

Come on boys, time to recover, there's tons of fish here. Ang tags yet another 8 pounder. We proceed to boat at least 50 fish on this little point and that's being conservative (you're right Fred, this is awful).
Every time we pass the spot of our heartbreak, Angelo says, "OK boys, get ready..."
Sorry buddy that pig's out of commission for a couple of days.


Angelo Viola proudly shows off a giant Mexican Largemouth

Our next stop is an old road that goes in one side of the lake and out the other. Reno & Tim found it a little earlier. It tops out at anywhere from 3-8 feet and drops off into 15 feet at roadside. We get there, crankbaits loaded up and proceed to catch more double headers than I've ever experienced anywhere in the world. It was like spring Crappie fishing, except every cast was a bass. For the first hour or so, if we didn't get at least a hit on every throw then we thought something was wrong. We caught loads of fish from 1 to 9 pounds (yeah Fred, I don't ever want to come back here again).
Mission two accomplished.


I love big bass, especially when they're caught
on a crankbait off of an old paved road!

My friends, can you tell I'm a bit excited about our Mexican bass adventure? I'm sure you can. I'd like to state right now that if you're even remotely into Largemouth Bass fishing, then you best be calling Fred Ward at El Salto Pro Bass Adventures. I'm sure with world-class fishing like this, they'll be booked solid for years to come. Who knows, you may even have to be unfortunates like us and go in the OFF SEASON!
