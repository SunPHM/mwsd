News / Events

Squash Courts opening ceremony


Dan Jenson, one of the world’s top ranked players, visited the Hong Kong Cricket Club on Saturday, November 26th, to cut the ribbon marking the official opening of the Club’s refurbished squash courts.

In town to participate in the 2005 World Squash Open, the Adelaide-based pro followed up the officialopening with a clinic for the Club’s juniors. Dan spent the entire morning and a good part of the afternoon taking the juniors through their paces, first in groups and then individually, finishing by playing a set with each youth. This was especially thrilling for those few juniors who managed to get a point or two off one of the world’s best players.

The advantages of putting in glass backs to all four courts instead of just two, as was previously the case, were immediately apparent. Dan divided the juniors into four groups, each with their own court. He was then able to observe their skills before moving in to offer instructions. While he was working with one group, the club’s own pro, Chad Sunde, and its top junior, Robert Broadfoot, could supervise on the othercourts to fortify Dan’s lessons, while the fourth court was used for a competition. Meanwhile, parents and other spectators could see all the action simultaneously from the comfort of the viewing area.

Dan might have thought he was through with the Club’s juniors when he left on Saturday afternoon. Little did he know that in the opening round of the World Championship that began the next day at the Hong Kong Squash Centre, he was drawn to play Chiu Ho-fai. Dan beat Ho-fai in three straight, but it was a great showing by our Club’s former Junior Scholarship Player and Club Champion, who is now playing No. One in the Hong Kong National Junior Team. Other news on the junior front: Robert Broadfoot finished seventh in Hong Kong in the 2005 Hong Kong Closed Championship in the boys under-19 age group. Chiu won the tournament. Not a bad showing at all for our boys! It shows why HKCC has one of the strongest junior programmes of any Club in Hong Kong. But to keep it strong, we always need to get new blood!

So if you’re thinking about a racket sport, talk with Chad. We’re eager to introduce you to squash.
