Carefully converted by an architect the cottage has kept all its traditional external features whilst creating a modern interior. The Cottage has 1 twin bedroom and 1 double bedroom: in addition the living room has a sofa double bed.  A cot can be provided.  The living/dining room has a wood-burning stove and colour T.V.  There is no central heating but each room has radiators or convector heaters.  The kitchen has an electric cooker,  fridge,  microwave and washing machine.  It is equipped with crockery, cutlery and cookware. The bathroom has a bath but no shower.   The electric meter is  read on arrival and departure. 
In summer the electric bill is about £10pw, in winter about £25pw.    There is a payphone and  good mobile phone reception.  Linen, towels and tea towels are supplied.  There is car parking for four cars, a private enclosed garden area and patio with furniture.
       
About the area.
Crook of Devon is the nearest village with a shop/post office (6 am to 6 pm), The Inn which serves hot food, and a "catch your own fish" at the Fish Farm and Coffee Bar. There is also a petrol station and fabric shop in the village.  There are some local walks maps available.
There are several  good restaurants nearby includingthe Tormaukin in Glen Devon and Heatheryford on the way to Kinross.

Kinross town is close by with regular boat trips to Loch LevenCastle in the summer.  There are golf courses, fishing, a swimming poolwith health suite, sauna and squash courts.

You are surrounded by hills all of which offer interesting & diverse walks from the fossils in the Lomonds to the hill lochs ofthe Cleish Hills and the extended rough walking through the  Ochil Hills.  Within six miles is Loch Leven  and the RSPB Vane Farm Nature Reserve. and over the hill is Lochore Meadows  Country Park  which offers canoeing, , windsurfing, etc.   Knockhill racing circuit and Portmoak airfield are nearby as well as a range of golf courses from quiet and friendly 9 hole courses to the more challenging and scenic courses at Muckhart and on to the championship courses at Gleneagles. The gorge and waterfalls at Rumbling Bridge and Castle Campbell at Dollar are a must.
Edinburgh,  Stirling,  Dundee, Perth and  St Andrews are all within an hour's drive.
There is a limited local bus service. The Glasgow to St Andrews bus stop is a five minute walk away and Kinross is served by the fast Inverness inter-city buses.

Shops including the local supermarket are open for extended hours and Sundays.
