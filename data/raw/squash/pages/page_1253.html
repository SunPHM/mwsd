Gardening is the number one leisure activity in America. Over 34 million people grow vegetables each year. Gardening on the high plains presents some unique problems for the home gardener. For this reason, the Vegetable Demonstration Garden was established to allow students and members of the community to observe how various crops perform in this environment. Each crop is labeled for easy identification.

Cool season crops such as lettuce, carrots, and broccoli may be direct seeded in the early spring. Warm season crops such as squash, peppers, and tomatoes may be direct seeded after the danger of frost has past, usually April 15 in the Lubbock area. For an early start, many crops such as cauliflower, broccoli, peppers, and tomatoes may be started indoors and then transplanted into the garden in late April.

A good garden site should be free of shade and have a loose soil which drains properly. The addition of organic materials and fertilizers is also helpful. Mulches are popular as they retain soil moisture and inhibit weed growth.

Vegetable gardens can range from standard rows to elaborate bed designs, each of these may have containers or trellises with in them. No matter what style is chosen, it is important that the garden be in full sun and the vegetables be place so they do not shade one another.
