Fall/Winter 2001


Autumn’s best-loved squash lends its delicate flavour to these six sweet and savoury selections
Recipes by Jennifer Stamper

What comes to mind when you think of pumpkins? Grinning jack-o’-lanterns and tricks or treats, or perhaps fresh pumpkin pie? For me, pumpkins have always heralded the inevitable return of fall. The heartwarming sight of those cheery orange globes promising Halloween fun and holiday feasts always reminds me of why autumn is my favourite season of the year.

In addition to their role as a seasonal symbol, pumpkins have increasingly found their way into our cooking, bringing their delicate flavour and smooth texture to everything from creamy soups to spicy desserts. When selecting a fresh pumpkin for cooking, choose a smaller one for tastier and more tender flesh. Avoid squash with blemishes or soft spots. If you don’t have the time to prepare a fresh pumpkin, tinned pumpkin is an excellent substitute in baked goods or any other recipes that call for cooked pumpkin.

Fresh pumpkin is actually delicious on its own, simply boiled, steamed or roasted, but where it truly stands out is in recipes like our Thai Coconut Pumpkin Soup. This savoury dish combines a tin of pumpkin with chunks of fresh pumpkin in a fragrant broth flavoured with coconut milk, lime and chiles. For another interesting Asian twist, try our Pumpkin Potstickers. These delicious pan-fried dumplings are made from a spiced pumpkin mixture stuffed inside tender wonton wrappers. And if you’re looking for a unique dessert, bake up a batch of our Pumpkin Cheesecake Bars. You’ll find their creamy pumpkin filling and melted chocolate topping simply irresistible!

Of course, no collection of pumpkin recipes would be complete without a pumpkin pie. Our Pumpkin Maple Pie is a decidedly Canadian version of this traditional treat, made extra special with a touch of maple syrup.
