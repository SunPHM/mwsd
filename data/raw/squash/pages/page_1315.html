JICAMA
'hehcahmah'
Pachyrrizus erosus, Jicama de Agua
Jicama means "edible root vegetable" in Spanish
aka: Mexican yam, Mexican potato, yambean, Mexican turnip.

DESCRIPTION:
Jicama is a tuberous legume, 85% of which is water. The vine like plant is grown year round in Mexico with peak seasons from Autumn to Spring. It takes from 3 to 6 months for the jicama roots to mature. Jicama resembles a large radish in shape, but is a whitish brown in color with thin skin and can weigh as much as 5 lbs.

Having a similar consistency to water chestnuts, jicama maintains its crispness even after cooking and is a wonderful substitute in stir fry dishes, its mild flavor and crisp texture complementing and absorbing that of the other ingredients. Most often though jicama is peeled and eaten raw with a sprinkle of lime or lemon, some chile powder or salt and pepper, julienned into salads or dipped into pico de gallo.


*Jicama fields being harvested in Mexico.

NUTRITION:
Jicama is similar in nutrition to the potato, with good concentrations of Vitamin C and a good source of potassium, low in sodium with fewer calories than the potato.
Per 100 grams (approx. 1/2 cup raw)
Water: 85%
Protein: 1.4 g of protein
Carbohydrates: 12.8 g of carbohydrates
Calories: 30
Crude fiber: .4 grams crude fiber
Vitamin C: 25 mg.
Potassium: 190 mg.

STORAGE:
Store in a cool dark area. Shelf life of 3 weeks before cutting. Wrap, store in airtight container and refrigerate after slicing. Should remain fresh for 1 more week.
