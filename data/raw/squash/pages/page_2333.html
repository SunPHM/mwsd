Super Series Final: The Draw

BARADA COMEBACK SEES HIM FACING POWER, PARKE, HEATH
 14 June 2001, © 2001 Squashtalk, file photos: © 2001 D Tessier, F Borchert

2001 SUMMER OF AUSTRALIAN SPORTING CLASHES CONTINUES IN THE CITY
The draw for the Halifax Equitable Super Series Finals, starting at the City of London’s Broadgate Arena on Monday 18 June, was announced today (14 June) and sees Britain challenge the might of Australia in yet another sport this summer – squash.

Whilst England’s cricketers battle to regain the Ashes from Steve Waugh’s Aussies, and the British Lions take on the Wallabies Down Under, five of Britain’s finest squash players will be amongst those challenging Australia’s David Palmer – the newly crowed British Open Champion - for one of the sport’s most prestigious titles.

The 24-year-old world number 3 from New South Wales – who became the first Australian for 20 years to win the coveted British Open title in Birmingham last weekend - has been drawn in the Fleet Group alongside Englishmen Peter Nicol and Paul Johnson and Wales' David Evans. Palmer makes his debut Super Series Finals appearance on Monday evening when he faces world number four David Evans - the man he succeeded as British Open Champion. He then goes on to face top seed Peter Nicol on Tuesday night in what promises to be one of the highlights of the Group clashes.

Nicol, the current world number 2, will be bidding for an unprecedented third consecutive Super Series Finals victory. Meanwhile in the Harrow Group, drawn alongside Canada’s world number 1 Jonathon Power, are England’s Simon Parke, Scotland’s Martin Heath and Egyptian former world number 3 Ahmed Barada.

Twenty-four-year-old Barada, one of the sport’s most prodigious talents, will be making a long-awaited comeback to the game after a seven-month absence from the international stage.

Presented by The British Land Company PLC, the $70,000 Halifax Equitable Super Series Finals is contested by the top eight ranked qualifiers from the six PSA Hi-Tec Super Series Tour events of 2000.

The five-day tournament is staged in a specially constructed, state-of-the-art, glass court at London’s Broadgate Arena. The group stage will see competitors play each other on a round-robin basis, with the top two in each group progressing to Thursday’s semi-finals. Both semi-finals, and the final on Friday 22 June, will be screened exclusively live by Sky Sports.
