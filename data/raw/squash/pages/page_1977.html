April 20th

The Market Report

Aptos Farmers’ Market

(Monterey Bay Certified Farmers’ Markets, www.montereybayfarmers.org)

Saturdays, 7:30 AM to Noon

Cabrillo College on top of the parking structure; follow the signs

Spring’s early flush of strawberries, asparagus and greens continues. One of the prettiest greens you’ll see at the market is rainbow chard, which is shown below.

It’s great steamed or braised, with a little olive oil and garlic, or cold as a cooked salad. The colorful stems are tasty, but take a few minutes longer to cook than the leaves.

One of the great pleasures of shopping at the market is chatting with the vendors. Some of the people you’ll meet are the farmers, others family or friends of the farmers. Ask them about recipes or preparation methods for unfamiliar vegetables, and they’re happy to share. This time of year, the Minazollis have asparagus and spring onions. The onions are sweet and great raw or grilled. In Catalonia, grilled spring onions are served with romesco sauce, and a tasty combination it is!

TL Coke Farms have a loyal following at the market for their organic vegetables, including fellow Slow Food member Andrew, seen here talking to Mrs. Coke.

This week one of their more unusual offerings was the unlovely but tasty celery root, also known as celeriac. It is a form of celery grown for its knobby root. The French like it raw, grated into a salad called remoulade dressed with a mustardy mayonnaise. I like to cook and include it in mashed potatoes, where it adds an interesting, celery-like complexity.

For all of you Slow gardeners who haven’t planted your gardens yet, Upstart Nursery and Swank Farms have seedlings of tomatoes, peppers, eggplant, cucumbers, summer and winter squash, corn, snow, sugar snap and shelling peas, lettuces, radicchio, beans, tatsoi, mizuna, giant red mustard, flowering sweet peas and herbs. Both culinary & medicinal herb plants are also available from Lathe House Herbs.

Cadillac Farms has returned to the market this week, with bouquets of flowers from your dream cutting garden. The bouquets are generous, and come with advice on how to make them last. As you see in the photo at the top of this page, I had to have some for myself!

Kimberly Wright
