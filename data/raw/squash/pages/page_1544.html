Charlie Mott
Known for his altheticism, but relatively new to squash and still making the most of his beginners luck period. His game has been described thus:

Charlie never really gives up but he does get riled when he misses shots/points so if you can get the better of him on the run-around and get him worked up, you'll help break his composure. He gets worked up and misses more shots that way. He doesn't lob very much and is quite disciplined at getting back to position. It's important that you play every shot and if (and usually is) he's in the way, run into him to get the replay as he's not keen on let's when it would be dangerous to play the shot. He has good racket control when he's not riled and his favourite shots are very short ones and straight down the wall into the corner. Vary your game play as it throws him completely.
