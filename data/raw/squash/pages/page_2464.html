London

England's capital is one of the world's most cosmopolitan cities. It offers non-stop entertainment for people of all ages throughout the year and around the clock. London boasts 164 historic sites, 300 museums and 108 theatres. Add to these 5,339 pubs and discos, 5723 restaurants and 344 markets.

Central London

Superbly located in the heart of London's West End, near to many major tourist attractions, shops, restaurants, theatres and cinemas. Medium size school newly refurbished which occupies an attractive Victorian building modernized to provide all the services and facilities for the most up-to-date language teaching. There are 9 comfortable group classrooms, 5 one-to-one rooms, well equipped study centre with free internet access, a study centre with Listening and computer facilities, a library, a refreshment area with an international atmosphere and included a varied social programme with 3 free activities per week.

London-Greenwich

Located in one of London's most historic areas. The town centre is on the banks of the River Thames and features buildings by England's most famous architects. Modern day Greenwich is full of cafes, restaurants, interesting shops, theatres and renowned markets. Greenwich Park (London's oldest royal park), the Maritime Museum, The Royal Observatory, the Queen's House and the Cutty Sark are other attractions in Central Greenwich. Enjoy the village atmosphere whilst being only 15 minutes away from Trafalgar Square.

Our school is housed in a spacious, well-maintained modern building right in the heart of Greenwich and just a few hundred metres away from the railway station. There are 17 classrooms, a self-study centre with free Internet access and computers, and a large social room. The school offers a very popular, well-attended social programme with day and evening visits around London.

London-Hampton Court

One of London's most exclusive and secure suburbs, only 35 minutes from Central London, offers a wide variety of interesting places to visit. The school, which is set in its own walled gardens, is 5 minutes walk from the River Thames and 10 minutes walk from Henry XIII's Hampton Court Palace (16th Century). The school boasts a heated swimming pool, basketball, Volleyball courts, a multimedia self-access centre, library, lecture room, student lounge with a pool table and satellite TV, and college accommodation (residence).

Cambridge

With its rich academic tradition and elegant city centre, Cambridge is the perfect environment for your studies. From splendid architecture to beautiful green parks and the gentle river Cam, the city has a unique charm and character. The influence of its famous university is evident throughout the town, lending a special atmosphere to the many bookshops, cafés and restaurants.

The school is centrally located in Cambridge, within 15 minutes' walk of the city centre with its famous university colleges. With a friendly atmosphere in the school, students receive a warm welcome and quickly get to know their fellow students and the staff, who are always available to help with any enquiries. The school has 9 classrooms, a well-equipped language laboratory, video library, readers' library as well as interactive language software for students to use. There is also a big terrace, ideal for parties and barbeques during spring and summer.

Brighton

The United Kingdom's most cosmopolitan and vibrant seaside resort, known as "London-by-the-sea". Immensely popular with young people, it has a safe and friendly atmosphere, fantastic entertainment and nightlife and beautiful local countryside. During the day you can take advantage of the mild, pleasant climate to discover the excellent shops, historic Lanes, The Royal Pavilion as well as sports and leisure facilities.

The school is located in a beautiful 5 story Regency building with views over the sea. The town centre is within a short distance. The school facilities include 10 classrooms, a social room, a self-study centre and free Internet access. The school organizes a fantastic range of international excursions: Amsterdam, Paris, Brussels, Italy, Switzerland and more

Oxford

Home to one of the oldest and most famous universities in the world, Oxford is steeped in a unique academic atmosphere with its buildings, book shops, cafes, antiques markets, student pubs and the varied restaurants. But Oxford is also a busy commercial centre and a fashionable modern city with a wide range of entertainment from art exhibitions, fascinating museums, and sports facilities.

The school is located on the campus of Milham Ford School, which is situated 15 minutes walk from Magdelen College and the start of the city centre. Housed in it's own building, with 9 classrooms, a social room and self-study & Internet centre. Students have the opportunity to use the on-site sports facilities, including tennis courts, a gymnasium and sports fields.

Swanage

The capital of the isle of Purbeck, approximately 2 hours and 30 minutes away from London, Swanage is a popular seaside resort on the south coast of England, with long inviting expanses of sandy beaches, clear unpolluted water and breathtaking coastal scenery. It provides a warm welcome to all visitors and retains an atmosphere of safety and tranquility. Due to its geographical position, Swanage enjoys a very mild climate and often heads the British sunshine league. Swanage offers a variety of entertainment including fairs, festivals, carnivals, theatre, live music, museums a variety of walks and also a ride on a historic steam train.

The school occupies an elevated position in the centre of the town, enjoying splendid views. The school offers 35 modern purpose-built classrooms, assembly hall, theatre, games room, multimedia-self access centre (with internet), medical room, indoor sports hall, heated swimming pool, tennis courts, squash courts, cafeteria, gym, cinema, disco and college accommodation (residence), reserved for young students in summer time.

Edinburgh

This fascinating city is alive with history and culture. It is so compact that you are never far from the impressive castle or from the wide selection of theatres, art galleries, clubs, pubs or restaurants. Wherever you are, you'll discover the city's vibrant atmosphere and the famous warm welcome of its people. This is particularly true in August when thousands of visitors arrive in Edinburgh for the renowned International Arts Festival. For something a little different, explore the city's ancient streets, or head out of town to the natural splendor of Scotland's countryside and mountains.

The school is located in a Georgian terrace; five minutes walk from the main shopping area of Princes Street. With 12 light and airy classrooms with views over beautifully kept mature gardens. The school is equipped with modern facilities: a multi-media centre and a well-stocked library of software, a library/study room and a school café with a cosmopolitan atmosphere.
