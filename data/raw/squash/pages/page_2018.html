Cook A Vegetable--Tu Bishvat Tookies
Cream
   ½ cup margarine
   ½ cup brown sugar
   ¼ cup honey
   1 egg

Sift together and then add to the above and mix well:
   1 cup whole wheat flour
   ½ teaspoon baking powder
   1 teaspoon baking soda
   ¼ teaspoon salt
   ½ teaspoon cinnamon
   ½ teaspoon nutmeg
   ½ teaspoon cloves

Add to the above and mix very well:
   1¼ cups dry oatmeal (either the quick cooking or the register)
   ½ cup grated carrots
   ½ cup grated zucchini
   (or 1 cup of either carrots or zucchini)
   ½ cup chopped nuts
   (optional: ½ cup raisins or currants)

Drop by teaspoonfuls onto a greased cookie sheet and bake at 350º for about 10 minutes.

Cook A Fruit--Baked Apples
Ingredients
   6 Rome Beauty apples
   cinnamon
   ½ a lemon or some lemon juice
   sugar

Core the six apples (a good way to do this with the pointed end of a vegetable peeler).

Sprinkle some cinnamon in each empty core.

Add 3 drops of lemon juice and ½ teaspoon sugar.

Place in a muffin tin or a baking tin with sides.

Bake at 325º for an hour (until you can easily pierce the sides of the apples with a fork).

Delicious warm with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream (and also very good for lunch or breakfast with yogurt or cottage cheese).

Cook A Grain--Crunchy Wheat Salad (Tabbouli)
Ingredients
   1 cup bulghar wheat
   1 cup water
   1 cup chopped parsley
   1 green pepper fiercely chopped
   1 bunch finely sliced scallions

Mix together
   ¼ cup lemon juice
   ¼ cup olive oil
   salt and pepper

Mix bulghar and water.

Let it stand an hour. At this point, the water will have been absorbed.

Add the vegetables.

Pour the oil & lemon juice over it and toss well.

Chill for 2 hours.

It's ready to eat.

Americans usually think of wheat as the source for flour for baked goods. In the middle east the cracked heart of the wheat serves as the main ingredient for a dish called Tabbouli. It is both the salad and starch dish of the meal. The perfect dish for a Tu Bishvat seder!

Cook A Grain--Squash And/Or Pumpkin Seeds
Scoop the seeds out of one medium or large pumpkin or one large winter squash such as banana squash. (Coordinate with dinner so the squash or pumpkin can be baked and eaten!)

Wash the stringy material off the seeds and blot them dry on a towel.

Arrange the seeds in one layer on a cookie sheet or rimmed jelly roll pan.

Sprinkle with salt and pepper; seasoned salt is very good if you have some, so is onion or garlic powder.

Bake at 300º for 20-30 minutes until golden in color.

Remove from oven (you can add more salt at this point if you think it's necessary).

Cool.

(These will keep up to 2 weeks in an air tight jar.)
