Vegetable Kabobs with Herb Butter



Ingredients:

1 yellow summer squash, quartered
1 zucchini, quartered
1 Japanese eggplant, quartered
1 red bell pepper, quartered
1 green bell pepper, quartered
3 new red potatoes, halved
8 garlic cloves, halved
4 shallots, halved lengthwise
1 tablespoon of butter or margarine, melted
1/2 teaspoon salt
1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
2 teaspoons fresh rosemary, minced
4 sprigs fresh rosemary (optional)
4 cups hot cooked rice


Place a 12" X 18" double thickness of aluminum foil on a work surface and lightly coat with non-stick spray.

Presoak four bamboo skewers in water (to prevent burning) and then stack the vegetables on them, alternating the yellow squash, zucchini, eggplant, potatoes, red peppers and green peppers with garlic and shallots. Lay the skewers on the foil.

In a bowl, combine the butter or margarine, rosemary, salt and black pepper. Lightly brush half the mixture over the vegetables. Turn the kabobs and brush the other side with the remaining butter mixture.

Fold the long sides of the foil together making several folds to encase the food snugly. Fold the ends closed (the end of the skewers might protrude).

Place the packet on a baking sheet. Bake for 35 minutes at 350 degrees, or until potatoes are soft. Be careful of escaping steam when opening the packet. Serve with the rice.
