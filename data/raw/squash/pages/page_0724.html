British squash gets £21m injection

The British Open will herald the star of a new dawn
British squash will benefit from a £21m cash boost over the next eight years after a new men's World Grand Prix tour was agreed.

The new tour, with the backing of The Eye Group, Fablon and the Professional Squash Association, will consist of the eight leading titles, beginning in April 2002 with the British Open.

All the events are to be staged in state-of-the-art portable glass courts.

"While those of us involved in the sport have long recognised that squash is clearly one of the finest and physically demanding sports in the world," said PSA chairman John Herrick.

"We have had difficulty, due primarily to budget constraints, to carry this message.

"We look forward to co-ordinating with Eye Group and Fablon to elevate squash to its deserved role in the world of sport."

An Eye Group spokesman: "With over 20 million regularly playing the sport around the world, and growing television audiences, we are convinced squash has great commercial potential."
