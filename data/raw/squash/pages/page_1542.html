"Prince's Grant offers an unsurpassed golfing experience which is significantly enhanced by the beauty of the natural indigenous environment, the beautiful lagoon and seascapes and the solitude that is experienced on many parts of the estate" Bill and Carol Dixon.

This is not just a golf course, but with a magnificent 1.5 km beach frontage it provides the discerning beach goer with the ultimate escape. Then there is the Umhlotaan lagoon, stretching 4 kms inland, for those who wish to take a leisurely paddle and enjoy the tremendous birdlife, of which there have been over 200 species spotted, or even cross the flight of some of the resident fish eagles. For those who love a walk, a superb nature trail has been carved along the lagoon, through the wetland forest where you will encounter many rare plants, and perhaps take time to relax on one of the boardwalk jetties overlooking the lagoon. Of course, should one wish to throw a line, there is some fine fishing likely to provide entertainment, in the lagoon or sea and some bass fishing in the dams on the golf course. And if that's not enough, the energetic can partake in a game of squash or tennis and refresh themselves afterwards in one of the three communal swimming pools.

The urban structure of Prince's Grant supports the idea of community, made up of villages where the buildings line the streets and then relate out into the countryside again. Practically all sites have incredible panoramic views, sea views, lagoon views or a fair portion of the golf course. The sites have been interspersed with nodes that will be common ground providing space for community activities, whilst the street should be more than just a connector, but where interaction with neighbours be encouraged.

Architectural designs are extremely important whereby a harmony of form, colour, and texture will create a unified character of colonial and traditional influences. This will have the effect of narrow winding village streets, with houses that will be designed with generous usable verandahs, steeply pitched white corrugated profile roofs and colours that will follow those of African people of the region. Privacy is incorporated within the walls onto interior courtyards, verandahs and swimming pools.
