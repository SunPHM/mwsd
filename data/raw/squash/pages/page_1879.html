Skinny Worms with Bloody Sauce
Spaghetti squash with tomato sauce

Spaghetti squash is one of the most bizarre types of squash there is. Gross out even the most ghoulish of guests by serving them this delicious dish of steaming "skinny worms" swimming in a slimy bloody sauce.

Boiling Time: 35 minutes or until soft
Preparation Time: 5 minutes
Baking Time: 25 minutes
Serves: 6
Level: 2
 
 
   
THINGS YOU'LL NEED: INGREDIENTS
(Use organic ingredients whenever possible)
• Large pot
• Knife
• Fork
• Measuring cup
• Measuring spoons
• Baking dish 		• 1 large spaghetti squash (6 cups cooked)
• 2 cups of your favorite tomato sauce
• 8 Tbsp of shredded Swiss cheese
• 1 Tbsp of oregano or thyme or rosemary
(or a combination of these)
• Salt to taste
Let's Make It!

Adult: Boil the squash for about 35 minutes. When it is soft, let it cool off a bit then cut it in half as shown in the picture.

Kid:
a) Remove all the seeds from the center and use a fork to gently pull out the yellow meaty part of the squash. It looks like spaghetti (or skinny worms!).
b) Measure out about 6 cups of squash flesh and place in a baking dish.
c) Pour 2 cups of tomato sauce over the top and mix lightly.
e) Sprinkle the cheese over the top.
e) Sprinkle the herb(s) and salt to taste.
Adult: Bake in the oven at 350°F for about 25 minutes. Serve.
