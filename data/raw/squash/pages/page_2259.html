No Pumpkin Safaris are planned for 2005

 

 

Here are some pictures from previous safaris:

 

  • September  3, 2003 at 5:00 p.m.  - Ekeby Badhus    Theme: summer squash
                The first Pumpkin Safari of the year with 22 participants, starting out with a summer squash meal along with talks about preparing food using squash and growing pumpkins and squash . The meal began with a delicious squash soup. The main dish was a squash and cheese pie, served with two different squash dishes. We had of course squash bread for dessert. Myra served the food and talked about preparing the food. Louie talked about growing squash and pumpkins. He also explained what the different varieties were in the last picture. Then we all headed out to the pumpkin patch where more questions were asked and those who desired could purchase what they wanted direct from the field.
  • September 10, 2003 at 5:00 p.m. - Ekeby Badhus    Theme: spaghetti squash
                The second Pumpkin Safari of the year with 38 participants. This time we started out with Louie's good pumpkin vegetable soup with "barley rice", which is crushed barley that you cook about like rice. For the main course we had spaghetti squash with three different sauces, each served right in the shell. Pumpkin bread was served with coffee. Everyone seemed to be enjoying themselves. Louie talked about the pumpkins out in the field and we ended up out in a very wet pumpkin patch, as it rained most of the day. The participants were however prepared and came with boots and rain gear.
  • September 17, 2003 at 5:00 p.m. - Ekeby Badhus    Theme: raw pumpkin/squash
            September 17, 2003. The third scheduled Pumpkin Safari of the year with 23 participants. We started out with with a delicious creamy squash soup, followed by Chili con Pumpkin, pumpkin salad and candied pumpkin. We finished with pumpkin bread and coffee.


            Everyone had fun out in the field looking at the variation in size, shape and color of the different pumpkins. Amanda found one just her size to sit on. Darkness comes fast, so we were limited as to how long we could be out in the field.
  • September 24, 2003 at 5:00 p.m. - Ekeby Badhus    Theme: Halloween

             The last Pumpkin Safari for the season was held. The same delicious menu as last week was served with the exception that even a real American pumpkin pie was served for dessert. The theme was Halloween. Myra showed pictures from earlier Halloween parties and Louie demonstrated how to carve a Jack-O-Lantern. We had to hurry to get out to the field before it got dark, as it was already dark by 7:45 p.m. The last participants left the field after dark.

 

 

 

 

  • September 15, 2003 at 6:00 p.m. - Ekeby Badhus, especially for Landskrona Gardening Society. Contact Kristina Truelsson, 0418-156 00 for details.

                The Landskrona Gardening Society had their very own Pumpkin Safari with 28 participants. Lots of questions from experienced gardeners gave way to interesting discussions. A perfect evening awaited us out in the pumpkin patch where everyone spread out to find as many unusual pumpkins as possible. It however got dark fast or many could have stayed out there much longer.

 

Pictures from our popular Pumpkin Safari 2002:

           
          
            Sept 11, 2002. Ekeby Badhus hosted a "Pumpkin Safari". More than 35 people came to taste different pumpkin dishes. All kinds of questions were answered by "Mrs. & Mrs. Pumpkin", after which we all drove out to the pumpkin patch. Everyone who wanted to could pick their own. A great time was had by all.

 

        October 30, 2002  -- Pumpkin Evening at Ekeby Badhus -- 26 people joined us for a full meal of pumpkin dishes. We started with a pumpkin soup and ended  with real American pumpkin pie. The participants each received a folder with all the recipes used for preparing the meal. For information concerning a repeat next year, contact Eva Kristiansson at Ekeby Badhus, 042-790 97 or 070-300 7976.
