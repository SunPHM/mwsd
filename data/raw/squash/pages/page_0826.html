Fitness Centre Wallyball Rules
(Updated August 2005)
 

Teams and Players

Division A, competitive league, will play with 2 players. Players register and compete individually playing with and against different players every match.
If a person does not show up for their scheduled game they will default that game, however a grace time of 5 minutes will be allowed. They will be given a score of -2 pts for three games played. Points for and agains will be 0-15 for all three games.
All leagues are open to Fitness Centre members only.
If a person has not shown up for 3 regular scheduled games without notifying the Opposing players or the league coordinator, they will be eliminated from the league.
Division B is our semi-competitive league that will play the first round robins of a season together prior to splitting into two leagues: Division B (semi-competitive) and Division C (recreational).


Scoring

Division A will play 45 minute matches consisting of three games to 11 or 15 points as time permits.
Teams will play 3 matches during the same time period. 1 point will be awarded for each match.
If during the third match one team does not reach 11 points within the time limits, the team with the most points in that match will be considered the winner of that match. In case of a tie, the next serve will rally and the winner of that rally wins the point and the last game.
The serving team receives a point when the receiving team fails to return the ball in the manner allowed by the rules.
A team must win by a margin of 2 points except in the case of a tie in the third game.
Top 8 players will advance to the finals in A league.

Service

1. Teams will volley (the ball must go over the net at least 3 times) to determine serve. The winner of the volley wins the serve.
2. The team serving second in the first match will serve first the second match.
3. The team with the most total points (in matches 1 & 2) will serve. In the event of a tie, the winner of the rally at the start of the matches will serve.
4. Each team will change sides after each match.
5. The service occurs in the back right hand to centre of the court.
6. The server may take one step in to their serve.
7. The serve must be made by hitting the ball overhead or underhand with one hand.
8. The serve is good if the ball passes over the net without touching a member of the serving team, the ceiling, or any wall before landing in the opponent's court.
9. Players must maintain the serving order, clockwise rotation of players upon start of service.
10. No members of the serving team may block the server from the opposing team.
11. A serve can only be returned over the net on the first hit if it's an underhand hit. (Bump)

Net Play

1. Each team is allowed up to three consecutive hits to get the ball over the net and into the opponent's court.
2. A player shall not make successive contacts with the ball, unless the ball is blocked at the net.
3. A ball that touches the net or rebounds off the net may be played again provided it was not on the third hit.
4. An opposing player cannot block the serve from the front row.
5. The ball must cross over the net to be good.
6. A player or any part of their body cannot touch the net, unless the ball was driven into the net with such force that it causes the net to touch the player.
7. In returning the ball, a player may follow through over the net, provided he/she first makes contact with the ball on their side of the playing court. Players attempting a block may reach across the net, but shall not contact the ball until an opponent strikes the ball in an attempt to send it back into the opponents court.
It is considered a fault and play is dead if a player contacts an opposing player across the line.

Playing the ball

1. Any player that makes contact with the ball shall be considered as having played the ball.
2. Players participating in a block may participate in the next play; this second contact shall count as the first of three hits allowed a team.
3. Any body part from the waist up can hit the ball as long as the ball rebounds quickly.
4. A different member of the same team shall make each contact with the ball, unless the ball has just come off a block at the net.
5. Players CANNOT hold, scoop, lift, push or carry the ball. A ball is good when hit cleanly from underneath with one or both hands. Please note: no double overhand hits (slapping the ball or carrying the ball).
6. If two players on the same team hit the ball simultaneously, two contacts with the ball will be called and neither player may contact the ball on the next play.
7. Climbing the wall to block is illegal.
A player can use an open hand to tip or direct the ball at the net. “Dunking" is considered carrying the ball and is a fault.
9. A serve can only be returned directly over the net on the first hit if it's an underhand bump.
Spiking is allowed. Spikes may hit only one side wall on the opponents side of the court. A SPIKE will be defined as a one handed hit with both feet leaving the floor upon impact. If a spike hits any player on the other team, from the waist up, the ball is still alive.
12. DIVISION A - overhand hits may hit three walls consecutively provided the hit is not a SPIKE.
