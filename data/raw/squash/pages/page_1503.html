Stanford University adds Women's Club, stays on upward path

 

2001-02 Stanford Men's and Women's Squash Outlook
by Jason Miller, student Captain-Manager © 2002 SquashTalk

The Stanford Squash Men's team begins its third season in existence. Run entirely by students, the team has rapidly climbed the national rankings each year since its inception. With an early season win over Cal-Berkeley, Stanford now moves into the realm of Division C teams (ranked #17-24 in the country); we are currently the #2 ranked club team in the country behind Denison.

The season's schedule is packed with 14 intercollegiate matches before nationals begins including Yale here in Palo Alto (the first time ever that an East Coast team has made the trip out to the West Coast). Having lost only 4 players off last year's team, there is high hopes of capturing the Barnaby Trophy for most improved collegiate team a second year in a row. In hopes of paralleling the men's team success,

Stanford Squash added on a women's intercollegiate team this year. They will be participating in the Howe cup and the Yale Invitational for 2001-2002 with an expanded schedule for 2002-2003. Both the men's and women's programs have begun an active recruiting program this year. With responses from over 90 talented high school players, the class applying for admission to Stanford this year will be filled with highly experienced players ready to lead Stanford Squash up the national rankings both on the court and off the court.

Several returning players have already made their mark this season. Rich Sherwood, who learned the game of squash 11 months ago, has suprisingly taken over the #1 spot on Stanford's team having established himself already as a solid 4.5-5.0 player, Rich relies on an incredible quickness and knack to cut ABSOLUTELY everything off before it reaches the back wall. #2 Jason Miller, with three years of squash experience, is the current team captain. #3 Sam Gellman, like Rich, started the game less than one year ago. The tandem of Gellman and Sherwood is a proud reflection of what our program represents and hopes to achieve. Our #4-#7, Ming Soo, Justin Durack, Paul Valiant, and Sagar Chandaria, all bring at least 3-4 years of squash experience to the team.

Paul also happens to be one of the absolute top junior mathematicians in the US.

Our crop of new talent includes our #8-15 players. Our #11 and 12 players, Louis Chang and Tim Holme, appear to be on the exact same track as Sam and Rich- less than 1 month after having picked up the game, both are solid 3.5 players and improving rapidly.

On the women's side, U17 #42 Suzi Smith plays #1 for the newly formed women's team. Former varsity scholarship crew team member Catherine Pligavko and sophomore Wendy Ong anchor the team as co-captains. With the enthusiasm of the team this year and the incoming crop of ranked women players, the women's team is off to a strong start.

Stanford Squash has set some lofty goals in hopes of mirroring Denison's metioric rise in the national rankings. Despite (or because of) being student run, the program has a bright future. The incoming recruiting class combined with the sheer enthusiasm for the game that it takes to be part of Stanford Squash (given that team memebrs are doing all the admin functions as well), we're optimistic. We are currently working with the Department of Athletics in hopes of petitioning for a new squash facility. Again, all the work, from the needs assessment to raising funds, has been done entirely through students.
