The Cope Williams Conference Centre and cricket pitch

We've all seen your average, garden variety winery. This is far, far from it. For a start this winery which is located in Romsey, Victoria, boasts a superb and very  picturesque cricket pitch and pavilion. The pitch is a private affair (and a good earner) that attracts teams from our Prime Minister's eleven down to corporate and social clubs. If you think that's not enough to make the property noteworthy on its own it also features a Royal (Real) Tennis Court — one of only four in Australia. If you haven't come across Royal Tennis you are not alone but you are missing out on one of the more eccentric sports played anywhere.

Royal Tennis is a hangover from medieval times. The court is larger than a conventional tennis court and has strange gabled roofs projecting from the side walls to make it look like the courtyard of a castle (see the picture below). The game is played with an odd, bent racket that looks like a distorted squash racket, a very hard ball and a set of rules that is as long as it is complex. Despite its oddities the game has quite a following of dedicated players.

Royal Tennis at Cope Williams

The Cope Williams property is more than just a country sports centre, though. It's winery happens to produce one of our region's best sparkling wines as well as a range of quality red and white table wines. Its accommodation and conference facilities are busy all year round and boast the who's who of corporate clients, and its superb gardens are occasionally open to the public for a treasured glimpse.

The property is the labour of love of Gordon and Judy Cope-Williams. Prior to creating this stunning property, Gordon was an architect and Judy was an interior designer (and an accomplished classical guitarist). The dedicated teamwork between them has provided us with one of the more interesting hospitality businesses we have ever worked with. If you are in the area go and enjoy some of their wine and their hospitality . . . and maybe bash a few balls.
