Dave Johnson '71 Receives Lifetime Achievement Award at NISRAs
March 3, 2003


David Johnson '71

HARTFORD, CT -- Former Williams men's squash coach and current Eph head men's tennis coach Dave Johnson was officially recognized for his contributions to the game of squash at the annual player's banquet at the national individuals tournament hosted by Trinity College.

The National Intercollegiate Squash Racquets Association honored Johnson with its Lifetime Achievement Award at the players' banquet Friday night. Johnson, a two-time All-American as a player for Williams, coached the Ephs on an interim basis from 1975-77 and then full-time from 1989-2002. Under Johnson's tutelage the Ephs were consistently at the top of the national team and individual rankings and he also provided many novices a chance to experience the game.

Johnson compiled a record of 162-70 (.698) in 15 years as the Ephs' head coach and frequently guided Williams to top eight finishes in the nation despite playing against a predominantly Division-I schedule. The Ephs finished fifth at the NISRA Championships in 2001, their best finish under the new format.

Currently Johnson serves as the Ephs' head coach of men's tennis, an assistant Dean and a part-time lecturer in Art History.
