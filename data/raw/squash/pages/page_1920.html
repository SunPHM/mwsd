This is not an Alpine strawberry but a regular one, close in size to the varieties you grow from roots.  If started early, ideally in March, you will be getting berries from sometime in July until the first frost.  The attractive, compact plants could use some mulching over the winter.  In the second year the plants will produce runners just like their root grown cousins.  15 seeds.   		$1.95

Alpine strawberry seeds should be cold-treated before planting. Cold treatment simply involves placing the seeds in an airtight bag or jar and putting the seeds in the freezer for two to four weeks, simulating a winter's exposure. When you remove them from the freezer, leave them in the container without opening it for several hours, to let them slowly get back to room temperature before planting.
