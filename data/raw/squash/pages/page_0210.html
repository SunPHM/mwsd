TELEMATCH

Telematch '88, held on May 7th at the Presentation College grounds, was a day to remember. Both the spectators and especially the competitors enjoyed the day to the fullest. The enthusiastic crowd cheered on its favorites vociferously - obvious amongst them were many Presentation students showing the true inexhaustible t'Pres" spirit.

This year, there were more schools participating than in previous years , which only made the challenge

greater for our team - the defending champions. Of the ten schools competing Presentation College, Naparima Combined and Barrackpore Snr. Comp. emerged as the major competitors. All participating schools found it to be a day of fun and enjoyment, especially when Holy Faith Convent Couva's cheer-leaders came on the field. They stole the show!

Presentation College, fielding the youngest team emerged 2nd, being beaten by Naparima Combined by a mere three points. Special credit should be given to the team captain, Brian Manning, who showed great leadership strength on the day, and to Andre Allum, without whose knowledge, perseverance and encouragement the team would have surely failed. Our young members, consisting of the second formers P. Attong, B. Lum Kin, C. Marshall, R. Mitchell, C. Hinds, K Peters and the highly spirited S. Morong, look forward to the 1989 Telematch. We will win next year!

KEVIN MACK (Team Member)

Telematch Team

 

 

TENNIS

1987-88 was again a year of relative success. For the third consecutive year, Presentation College was well represented both on the local and regional scenes. For many years we have produced many national players of unmatched calibre. This year, however, we were represented by one player, Sean Boyce.

Boyce entered the Carifta Games, where he captured a gold and a silver medal, the Junior Brandon and J.I.T.C. His best local accomplishment was in the Emile Elias Top 16 Caribbean Championships, where he was one of only two juniors to qualify.

It is indeed ironic that Presentation does not concentrate on the sport of tennis, yet we have produced national stars of considerable repute, such as the Evelyn brothers, Mc. Connie brothers, Craig La Croix and Sean Boyce.

To the tennis players of the school, keep up the good work.

CRAIG CAMACHO

 

 

 

PRESENTATION COLLEGE UNDER 16 SQUAD

This team, affectionately known as the young ones, represents the future of Presentation College Championship Football. Due to the late start of the season the team was well prepared for the South U-16 league and the junior Intercol competitions. Their first match was a huge success in all aspects as they mercilessly whipped Princes Town Senior Comp. by 9 goals to nil and continued on in the following matches by winning (7-0), (5-0) and (4-1) consecutively in their three subsequent games. This placed them on the top of the South Zone U-16 league after playing only 7 games, of which they won 6 and lost 1 (against Naparima by three goals to two after a last minute goal). Presentation totally outclassed opponents by scoring a whopping 30 goals for with 7 goals against (which gives them a game average of over 4 goals to 1). S Boyce led the scoring with 6 goals, Prior Jones was second with four and K Samuels and S. Corbie followed with 3 goals each. The team went on to represent the South against Deep South Champions Vessigny in the South League Playoffs, in which we were edged out by a score of 2 goals to one. As we can all see, the future of Championship Football is in very good hands (or feet as the case may be).

 

 

SQUASH

 

The squash Coaching Programme was very successful with great interest shown by the Form 1 students and many Form 4 students returning to play squash.

The squash ladder generated a lot of enthusiasm among the students. Our annual Inter College match against Fatima was played at home and once again we were victorious.

This year Pres. took part in the National Inter-club league and eventually placed 3rd in the B Division. It was a great learning experience for the players. Team members were Dirk Prudent, Sheldon Skeete, Paul deVerteuil, Harvey Young Lai, Raynard Khan and Brian Bahadur.

In November 1987 Don Fabien Lee was invited to represent Trinidad and Tobago in Senior Caribbean Championship and gave an excellent account of himself. The T'TSRA ( Trinidad and Tobago Squash Racquets Association ) nominated Don Fabien Lee for the 1987 Sportsman of the Year for his outstanding performance in this field.

In the Annual Junior Caribbean Championships the following boys made it into the National Team

Don Fabien Lee, Dirk Prudent, Sheldon Skeete, Paul deVerteuil and Raynard Khan. Don and Raynard won the under 16 and under 14

Squash Team

 

Championships, Dirk placed 3rd and Sheldon 4th. The Boys' Team won the Boys' Team Championship and together with the Girls' Team went on to win the Overall Championships.

All in all it was a very sucessful year for Squash. Don and Dirk also made it in the National Mens' Team m August and had an excellent tournament.
