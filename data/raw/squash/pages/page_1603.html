Why Noosa?



 
Noosa is situated one and half hour's drive north of Brisbane on Queensland's Sunshine Coast. Airborne visitors can arrive at Brisbane airport or at the Sunshine Coast airport at Maroochydore which is a twenty minute drive from Hastings Street, Noosa's most cosmopolitan and fashionable boulevard.
World famous as the most unspoilt resort town in Queensland, Noosa presents the perfect living environment for a holiday destination. With a relaxed and relaxing style all of its own, with sunshine and clear skies whatever the season, with mild temperatures and gently cooling breezes, Noosa has for decades been a most famous magnet to holiday makers of all ages. What are the ingredients that contribute to the well-tried and well loved recipe that is a Noosa holiday? Certainly, the natural beauty of the environment with its National Park, the Noosa River, the local beaches and lakes and the nearby Hinterland all create a unique backdrop for the perfect holiday.

Noosa National Park is 300 hectares of natural rainforest fringed by craggy headlands and sandy beaches. The home of koalas, goannas and beautiful birds, the National Park offers a network of walking tracks along the breathtakingly beautiful coastline or through the park's forested centre. Picnic spots and places to rest are readily available. The National Park is a must to all visitors.

Noosa has always had a love affair with water. The Noosa River is the waterway of sailors, windsurfers, fishermen, swimmers, jet skiers, water skiers and everyone who loves a view of the water. Our Lakes Cooroibah and Cootharaba, both teeming with marine life, are a passage to the beautiful Everglades.

Noosa's beaches are amongst the best in Australia which means in the world! Noosa Main Beach is one of the few north facing beaches on the eastern seaboard, totally sheltered by the headland of the National Park. Local beaches at Sunshine Beach, Sunrise Beach, Castaways and Peregian Beach are perfect for surfing and comprise the first stretch of an uninterrupted sixty kilometre stretch of surf and sand to the south whose picture postcard views have attracted many outsiders to settle in the area. All swimming beaches have lifesavers patrolling between flags which gives a guarantee of safety within the prescribed section.

Across the Noosa River in a four-wheel drive vehicle the visitor must without fail try the fascinating and exhilarating experience of driving on the beach-cum-highway which is the Forty Mile Beach. Incredible to visitors as it may at first appear, the sandy surface of the beach is the "road" and all rules of the road apply in the normal way. The exciting drive up this beach leads tourists to the Coloured Sands, Double Island Point and finally to the ferry to Fraser Island. A tour of Fraser Island, the largest sand island in the world, is compulsory for visitors who will enjoy natural rainforest, cooling streams and more beautiful sandy beaches fringed with green!

Efficient and plentiful tour operators in Noosa ensure visitors have the opportunity to visit in comfortable air-conditioned vehicles our Hinterland towns with their English-style appearance, art galleries and cafés. Opportunities for bargain hunting are plentiful: only sixteen kilometres from Noosa through roads lined in part by noble State Forest preserve, is the historic town of Eumundi with its extensive and much vaunted Saturday market. Goods on sale include everything from garden produce through local arts and crafts to Chinese music on tape and antique marcasite brooches.

This is Australia's best holiday playground so sport is a must. It would be difficult to find any form of sport that was not familiar to Noosa. Water sports, croquet, tennis, horse riding, bowls, cycling, hill climbing, diving, game fishing , roller blading, squash and the opportunity to play golf on some the most beautiful golf courses in Australia are all on the doorstep.

Noosa has a cosmopolitan atmosphere which is enhanced by a feast of restaurants, cafés and bars. Food is to the highest international standard and well known chefs frequent many Noosa kitchens. Many restaurants are Mediterranean bistro style whilst many offer superb al fresco dining, sometimes on the edge of the famous beaches or on the riverside. The town is also a shoppers' paradise with its designer dress shops, shoe shops and resort style boutiques all offering quality goods to the discerning visitor.

Noosa offers you the holiday of a lifetime, a warm welcome the second time round and every year after that!
