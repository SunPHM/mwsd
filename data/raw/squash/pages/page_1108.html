Just like squash for dinner in the fall, raising quality vegetables has been a tradition at Martin Farms for many years. Being of Dutch heritage, the founder came to the area and began raising familiar crops, among which was cabbage. In 1928, he moved from the Greece farm to a 100-acre farm in Hilton, New York, and in 1937, he relocated to the farm's present site in Brockport where he farmed with sons Dick, Robert, Bernard and Leonard until his retirement in 1967.
