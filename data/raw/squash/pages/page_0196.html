Squashed in South Africa
By Sqn Ldr Ben Waterfield
Articles

What's Going On? 

The ACTIVE Team
 The first ever Services squash tour to South Africa took place recently.  Some 12 months in the planning, and drawing on the 10 best players from all 3 services, the squad embarked on an ambitious schedule to cover the length and breadth of the country over a 2-week period.  The RAF had 4 players in the Squad, with 2 men from the RN and an Army contingent of 4.

The tour started in Johannesburg, playing provincial sides, before moving on to Pretoria to take on the South African National Defence Force. We were spoilt by being hosted non-stop by our sporting colleagues, but it would have been churlish to refuse.  After being picked up from our spartan accommodation at the Holiday Inn, it was off to the Game Park for some close encounters of the wild kind. For the more cultured amongst us (both of us) there was the chance to go down one of the deepest diamond mines in the world to where the largest ever diamond was found. Now part of the Crown Jewels, there were unfortunately no free samples. Then it was off to Durban, again playing provincial sides for 3 days, interspersed by some dolphin watching. 

The real 'jewel in the crown' was undoubtedly Cape Town.  For anyone who has not been there, just go. The coast is like the South Pacific and West Indies rolled into one. To wake up in your hotel, gaze out to Table Mountain and beyond and be torn between so many mouth watering excursions, is as good as it gets.  So we would settle for a quiet stroll down by the Waterfront to work up an appetite to do justice to a sumptuous meal at one of the best restaurants; washed down by a bottle of the best Cape wine, this would set you back no more than £5.50.  Close to hand was the chance to charter a 58 foot schooner and get back to match fitness by crewing it out to Robben Island and back.  Stopping to take a dip in the crystal clear waters of the Cape, we had the unforgettable chance to swim with the ever- present dolphins that accompanied us for most of the way.

Our trip to the Cape wine-lands gave us the chance to see unsurpassed beauty in the form of the undulating land, characterised by its steep-sided valleys. This was at an altitude that made the lush vegetation and countryside and not unlike a cross between the UK and the Austria, except that the sun shone at just the right temperature all the time. The vineyard owners could not have been more hospitable, offering their homes and their wine for all who wished to participate.  An evening spent back on the seashore watching the spectacular rollers crashing onto deserted white sands was, by now, almost a formality.  Oh, and I should say that we did well on the competition front. The sea air obviously did us good, winning 2 matches out of 3 and squaring the series.

Overall, the standard of squash was extremely high; the opposition all put out strong sides with many nationally ranked players. Some of these included the South African Champion and the South African Over-40 Champion. This, coupled with a distinct home advantage (particularly in Johannesburg, where the altitude factor made for a completely different game), ensured a closely fought contest. In the event, the series resulted in an honourable draw, the outcome resting on the very last game of the last fixture.

The tour was a resounding success, providing an excellent opportunity to play first class squash, and also to see so much of this fantastic country.  Strong sporting links were established with both civilian and military organisation, providing (fingers crossed!) a firm platform on which to plan future tours.
