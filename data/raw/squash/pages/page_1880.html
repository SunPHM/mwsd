Skinny Worms with Bloody Sauce
(Spaghetti squash with tomato sauce)

Spaghetti squash is one of the most bizarre types of squash there is. Gross out even the most ghoulish of guests by serving them this delicious dish of steaming "skinny worms" swimming in a slimy bloody sauce.

 
   
Time: Boiling: 35 minutes or until soft
Preparation: 5 minutes
Baking: 25 minutes
Serves: 6
Level: 2
 

Now print this recipe and head to the kitchen!

Tip:
Save the beautiful seeds of the spaghetti squash. Give them as a party favor so your guest can grow skinny worms of their own next year, for their Halloween Feast.

Things you'll need:
Large pot
Knife
Fork
Measuring cup
Measuring spoons
Baking dish

Ingredients
Use organic ingredients whenever possible
1 large spaghetti squash (6 cups cooked)
2 cups of your favorite tomato sauce
8 Tbsp of shredded Swiss cheese
1 Tbsp of oregano or thyme or rosemary (or a combination of these)
Salt to taste

Let's Cook!

1. Adult: Boil the squash for about 35 minutes. When it is soft, let it cool off a bit then cut it in half as shown in the picture.

2. Kid: Remove all the seeds from the center and use a fork to gently pull out the yellow meaty part of the squash. It looks like spaghetti (or skinny worms!).

3. Kid: Measure out about 6 cups of squash flesh and place in a baking dish.

4. Pour 2 cups of tomato sauce over the top and mix lightly.

5. Sprinkle the cheese over the top.
