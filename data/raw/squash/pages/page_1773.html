Described as a combination of several paddle sports, PADEL, has similarities of tennis, racquetball, paddleball and squash. Padel was developed in Acapulco, and has spread worldwide. It is reported that 6.5 million people are playing the sport in more than 18 countries. Some of the countries in which Padel is played are Argentina, Brazil, Paraguay, Bolivia, Ecuador, Mexico, Spain, France, Italy, Belgium, Great Britain, Holland, Australia, South Africa, the US and Canada. Unused tennis court space has been successfully converted into 3 Padel courts, creating an easy to learn family sport.

PADEL or PADDLE, THE INTERNATIONAL GAME

History: Invented in Mexico in 1968. Spread to Spain and then Argentina and many other countries. Padel is now being introduced into the U.S. and Canada.

The Game: Padel is played in warm weather. Padel is played in doubles and a match consists of two to three sets. Two serves are allowed, and the ball may be played off of the walls.

The Court: The game is played in a playing area which is 65'7"x32'9", on a court with a net, surrounded by walls of tempered glass.

The Ball: The ball is much like a tennis ball but with a heavier nap

The Paddle: Graphite, 17 3/4" in length by 10 1/4' wide. As specified by the associations

Associations: American Paddle Association
