We live in a rural area in the northwest corner of Georgia near Chattanooga, Tennessee. Our house is situated in a valley between Lookout Mountain and the foothills of the Appalachians. The views of Lookout Mountain from our dining room are lovely. Our house is surrounded by forests and fields and our back yard is big enough for a treehouse, play cottage, tent or anything else our child desires. Vanessa grew up in the house next door and spent most of her childhood on the hillside behind the house chasing frogs, picking flowers and taking her Barbie dolls "camping."

We live with our three cats, Crumpet, Biscuit and Treacle. Vanessa's parents, who live next door, keep four more family pets: dogs who consider our front porch part of their home. They are Wrangler, Prissy, Chippy and Carrie. Our neighbors keep goats and horses and just about everyone has a summer garden full of squash, corn, cucumbers, tomatoes and numerous other vegetables.

We enjoy celebrating the seasons with each other and our families. Halloween is great fun at our house with costumes and pumpkin carving competitions (which Keith usually wins). Our little cousins stop by as trick-or-treaters and we are generous indeed with the candy! In the wintertime we all hope and pray for a snowfall good enough to keep us all home from school and work (not that we ourselves have to go anywhere to work!). On snow days the whole street turns out for snowball fights, best snowman competitions and sledding. Spring and Summer bring picnics, fishing and paddling at Vanessa's parents' lakeside vacation home affectionately known as "the barn."

Further afield there are numerous other activities, from hiking and whitewater rafting in the mountains to the attractions of Chattanooga such as the Tennessee Aquarium and the Creative Discovery Museum. Click here to learn more about our local resources.

We also consider England to be part of our home and look forward to acquainting our child with Keith's homeland and culture. England and the rest of Europe are very much child-friendly places with many attractions that cater to families. We are so looking forward to traveling to places that we have already been to, but this time, seeing them afresh through the eyes of our child! We are members of the National Trust and have always taken great delight in our days spent visiting the magnificent estates and walking in the Trust's gardens. Now our child can join us for these days out. Click here to see the sorts of programs the Trust has for children.
