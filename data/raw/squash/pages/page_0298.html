ACJC house system

The orientation for year 2004 is truly heavily Dune inspired. (Ok I know so much about the book cause I've read almost all of its prequels except a recently published one and some of the sequels too. Read 6 Dune books for a book review)

First thing, the story about the planet becomming a desert is somewhat a hook. In Dune, the planet Arrakis (refered to Dune by the Fremen in habitants) is a desert without water. Water is scarce and is obtained only at the polar regions of the planet.

Malathis, the prophet in the ACJC orientation story, could be refered to as Imperial Meteologist Pardot Keynes. Both of them wanted to change the world but they were shunned. Keynes wanted to restore Dune and terraform it to become green again. Malathis wanted peace for the world. Keynes got the help of the fremen in Dune. When he died the Fremen continued the terraforming projects. Malathis has the support of house Jazul, who also believe in the return of Sha'guni (the parallel of Dune's Messiah)

Talking about the messiah in Dune, he was supposed to be paul atreides (means he is from house atreides). However, Paul was also a fremen. He learnt their ways too. He brought peace to the world at the end of Dune with his gift of foresight.

Looking back at the ACJC orientation, House Alleria is actually House Atreides. Why? Like the atreides, they have water, honour and technology. Their insignia is also the eagle (except alleria is the blue eagle). House Cornos, well quite obvious they represent house harkkonnen (the most ruthless of all the great houses, and also because they have a Baron) What about house jazul? The fremen of course, since they live in sand dunes.

Something the ACJC orientation planner didn't copy from Dune: the giant sandworms that roam Dune.

Not bad...At least it shows that they appreciate great works of science fiction and literature.

Oh ya and for all you guys out there ACJC got alot of chio bu! (at least much higher proportion than NHSS, but I wasn't so interested).

ACJC Orientation

My first day at ACJC as a student. Whoop, its the best school experience I've ever had.

I was late for school today (well almost, sort of. I didn't know school started at 7:30, I thought it was supposed to be 8am.)

The day began with the principal's address, some stuff she said really made sense. I think ACJC is more lax on the discipline side than NHSS, but they still care about small things.

I'm gonna need new shoes I guess, mine is black not white. >,<

Btw, here is what is wrong about ACJC:
ACJC allows sport shoes (yes but they have colour restrictions --> white)
ACJC allows dyed hair (no no, I don't think so)
ACJC allows short skirts (eh, must not be more than 2cm from knee cap)
ACJC doesn't care if you don't turn up for lessons (nah they give demerit points)
ACJC is a place for rich kids and snobs (haven't seen any yet..sorry!)

The people there are fun, enthu about the stuff and all things. Very apparent through the games we played.

Crazy games like dipping your head in a bucket to collect water to make water bombs were insane. But everyone played along.

There was one where we had to make the longest line of our possessions and stuff. We took off our shoes and shoe laces, socks and etc to line it up into a straight line on the road, then we lay down on the asphalt. We made a 44 m line of our stuff, beat the other team by 0.1m. WEE! Lucky I wasn't 10cm shorter. The other team had to do a forfeit, made them sing Britney's "hit me baby one more time"

We also had to play a game where 2 people have to carry a balloon back to back to the other end of the line. Although we lost the game, we didn't feel so bad because we tried, the best team won.
