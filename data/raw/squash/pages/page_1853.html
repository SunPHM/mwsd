So, without further delay, let us begin.  Today's topic will be the beautiful herb, Basil.

Basil is known to be the herb of "Good Wishes".  Giving someone, you feel special about, a basil plant is a beautiful and loving gesture.  The aromas in each type of basil are different ranging from pungent, sweet, spicy, oriental and even lemony. 

Basil originated from India then spread to Mediterranean countries.  In Hindi it is known as Tusi meaning sacred basil.  Basil is grown primarily outside, but in our world it is considered a must as a indoor plant as well.  It is used in our foods,  added to oils for muscular pain, stiffness and inflammation, as well as our baths and bath products. Using basil on an insect bite can give relief from a sting.  It is considered  an aide to help protect our homes from evil and give us the gift of open love.  I, myself, have quite a large garden section dedicated to basil, of various edible types, along with several pots of basil in my home.

When two gnomes fall in love, and decide to become one, they go through a ceremony similar to human weddings using several traditions.  The female gnome carries a bunch of collected wild plants representing certain things. Basil for protection and open love, rosemary for remembrance, chervil for sincerity and violets for faithfulness.  Together,  the two will walk
down a path, among their families and friends, carpeted with olive branches for peace and harmony.

For the feast of this celebration, you will always find a delicacy called Pesto. Now pesto is always enjoyed by  gnomes, even without celebrations, but it just couldn't be a grand celebration without this dish.  To make pesto you will need:
 
1/3 cup basil leaves (fresh)
2 cloves of garlic
pinch of salt
1/2 cup pine kernels (optional)
3/4 Parmesan cheese a dash of coarsely
ground pepper
1/2 cup olive oil
 
In a blender add the basil, garlic and olive oil, process for few seconds and then gradually add the pine kernels, cheese and salt. This should be a thick creamy sauce and a wonderful topping for either pasta (1 lb cooked) or spaghetti squash.

You can add a few drops to grape seed or  almond oil to aide in the relief of sore muscles followed by a massage.  For relief of sore gums, make a tea out of 8 basil leaves in a cup of boiling water and swish out your mouth.  Add 3 or 4 drops to your bath water  for a refreshing smooth bath experience with a wonderful aroma.  In jojoba oil add a few drops to create a hair conditioner that is suppose to stimulate hair growth. 

Basil has a broad range of usages.  One study has shown that basil stimulates the immune system.  Basil is also of the many healing herbs, containing both pro and anti-cancer substances.  It contains vitamins A and C and anti-oxidants that help prevent cell damage.  We gnomes also believe that basil contributes to our long term memories.  Caution: Do not use basil oil while pregnant.

To grow basil, select the type or types of basil your nose finds appealing.  There are several varieties to choose from such as:  Purple Ruffles having ruffled jagged purple leaves and it's scent is like licorice and cinnamon. Tai basil has a oriental aroma and taste with lavender flowers, lemon basil has a lemony  taste smell with white flowers, and sweet basil is just that, having a delicate sweet aroma and flavor. There is also Italian basil, Genoa basil, and spicy global basil all with a spicy scent and flavor.  I recommend several because once you discover what a tasty treat basil is, you will want to experience with other flavors.  Basil can be purchased as a small plant but easily grown from seeds.  Plant in a well drained area or container and make sure basil has a minimum of 3 hours of sun daily.  Do not over water but continue to keep basil in a moist soil. Pinch off flowers and leaves to promote bushiness.

Since basil requires very little maintenance and is one of the easiest herbs to grow,  indoor or out, there is no excuse for not having this valuable herb.  It is a major part of our life and we wouldn't consider not having this plant in our possession.
