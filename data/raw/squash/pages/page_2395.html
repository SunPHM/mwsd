Zucchini also is known as Italian squash. It seeds quickly and knocks everything else out of the way. Squash has an important function of the intercropping triumvirate of a) corn, b) legumes (beans, peas), c) squash (zucchini, crookneck and many others).

    When planted together these three make perfect garden companions. The legumes fix nitrogen in the soil for the use of the corn, and the corn acts as a natural trellis for the legumes as they climb towards the sunlight. The squash hovers along the grounds, its huge leaves blocking the sun both to provide shelter for its opening blossoms and succulent growing gourds and to prevent weeds from sprouting under their shaded cover.
