  Vora in the finals of Squash Championship
   By: A Mid Day Correspondent
   November 15, 2003

Top seed Joshna Chinappa prepares for a backhand shot during her 9-1, 9-2, 9-1 win over Shehnaz Dastur in girl’s under-19 final at Bombay Gym yesterday
Pic: ATUL KAMBLE
Second seed Sahil Vora produced a superb fight back to snuff out the challenge from third seed Vikas Jangra to book a place in the men’s open final of the of the ABG Shipyard Ltd-sponsored Rs three lakhs 28th Bombay Gymkhana Maharshtra State Open Squash Championship, yesterday.

Vora clawed his way back after he lost the first game to chalk out a 2-9, 9-3, 9-7, 9-2 victory in a keenly contested semi-final encounter at the gymkhana’s glass-back courts on Friday.

Vora struggled initially to find his range of strokes and with the third-seeded Vikas hitting some fine drops he went on to win the opening game.
But, Vora soon got his act together. He fired some brilliant deceptive and powerful strokes and forced his opponent to play second fiddle before he clinched the match in his favour.

Vora will take on top seed Manish Chotrani in the final on Saturday. Chotrani scored  a comfortable straight games victory against unseeded Supreet Singh winning at 9-0, 9-3, 9-3 in the other semi-final match.
