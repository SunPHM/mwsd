The World Health Sport Club in Edgemont, Calgary, will be the venue of the 2004 Canadian Juniors Squash Championship, April 22 - 25, 2004.

This championship is held once a year and it is expected to attract over 250 top juniors from every province and territory in the country to compete in the boys and girls under-19, -17, -15, and -13 categories. This is the most important event on the squash calendar for juniors and the results are used to help select team members to represent Canada in international junior competitions. For the 2004 championship, the organizers are helping to raise funds for a major charity.

The MS Society of Canada is that designated charity. Four pharmaceutical firms with MS therapies are helping to sponsor the event, and they are also providing additional funds to the MS Society of Canada to "squash" MS. The 4 pharmaceutical companies are Teva Neuroscience, Biogen Idec Canada, Berlex Canada, and Serono Canada.

In addition, the squash championship will also seek to raise donations for the "Let's squash MS" campaign from the families of the squash players and from other associated fundraising. The Calgary chapter of the MS Society of Canada is helping to coordinate the fundraising. The squash championship is expected to attract local and national news coverage and the tournament itself aims to raise awareness of MS amongst the junior squash players and also the public.

For those interested in viewing some top-level squash, please feel free to visit the World Health Sport Club in Edgemont during the championship. The level of Canadian squash is indeed of a very high calibre. Our top Canadian player, Jonathan Power, has been ranked in the top 5 in the world the last several years, including the number 1 position for a period of time. Many of our top junior players are recruited to Ivy League universities in the United States on scholarships for their further education and to compete in squash at the collegiate level. Overall, it is refreshing to note that a top national event is being organized not only for the spirit of competition, but also to raise funds for a major charity.

The MS Society of Canada is proud to be the designated charity beneficiary. The support of the pharmaceutical firms listed above is gratefully acknowledged. (Written by Fiona and Wee Yong, who are helping to organise the squash event).
