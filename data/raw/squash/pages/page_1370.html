Come on along and take a walk around the farm...
(We think it's worth the download wait!)

This is Cherry Valley Farm, which is our home. We raise top quality horse hay, cattle, along with our fruit trees, berry patches and truck garden.



Our raspberry patches are increasing in size yearly, along with our jam production. So far we've been able to keep all our crops organic.



Joe's dad, Ed Goetsch had a similar planter in the early part of this century. This one has been converted to tractor-drawn and we used it just last year to plant corn, beans and squash. Mainly I just love the way it looks in our lane.



Our orchard has been around here for 140 years and one of our pear trees is at least 100 years old. We grow apples (10 varieties), pears, cherries, apricots and english walnuts (the only one we're aware of in Northeastern New Mexico).



Who says you can't grow English (New Mexican)Walnuts in Mora County? This tree is about 20 years old and produces several bushels of nuts most years. Our native pinon trees don't do that well.



These gorgeous gals brighten the landscape and watch out over the truck garden. On a clear day they're like looking into a bit of heaven.



My dear friend from childhood, Pam, came all the way from Texas just to eat peaches and red chile! Can you see the dew on those luscious peaches???
