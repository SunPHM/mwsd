My hobbies

SPORT

Rowing

After doing very little in my first year here, I thought I might actually do some exercise this year. So I have now gone to the other extreme as I am now an active member of the university boat club. Having done this at school for a few years I knew what I was letting myself in for but somehow I'm still doing it. If you enjoy getting up at 6.30am in mid-winter in Scotland and getting cold and wet, this is the sport for you. No apart from that, it is actually a fantastic sport and it's great being part of a squad and the social side of things are a good laugh.

Scottish Amateur Rowing Association

Dorney Lake (slightly better than our canal but nevermind!)

Apart from that am keen on windsurfing but felt it was a bit cold to bring my board up here to Edinburgh. But in the summer I try and get down to the coast as much as I can.

 

Also do a bit of sailing, diving, tennis and squash.

MUSIC

I come from quite a keen musical family so I started learning the piano and violin from about the age of six and have been playing ever since. I got a music scholarship to my school so I took it pretty seriously then as I kind of had to. But since leaving school I have found it hard to find the time to do all the necessary practice and play only occasionally now which is bit of a shame. However, I am a member of the University Sinfonia which meet every saturday morning and that's good fun.

TRAVEL

I have caught the travelling bug since leaving school and now wish I had taken a gap year before starting university. In 2000 I went inter-railing around Europe with a few friends visiting Belgium, Holland, Germany, Czech Republic and Italy. Last summer I went the long haul to Australia for five weeks, backpacking up the east coast which ws just fantastic. Highlights included sailing round the Whitsuday Isles on an 85ft yacht for four days and learning to dive on the Great Barrier Reef. Cool huh!
