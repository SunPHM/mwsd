July 10

This is the 5th week of the 2003 season for home delivery of the CSA produce.  This is what the packing table looked like this week as we sorted your veggies into your bags.

   

We're happy to be able to provide romaine lettuce, snap peas, zucchini, cherry tomatoes, basil, broccoli/cauliflower, bunch beets, tomatoes, and baby carrots this week.  We are beginning to get into the more conventional veggies now.  

Some of you may have received all broccoli and some a mix of broccoli and cauliflower because these are the first of them and we have a limited supply of both.  The same is true for the squash this week.  For example, we have only picked one of the Bennings Green Tint Patty Pan pictured below.  There will be LOTS more.

The snap peas can be used just like snow peas, steamed, sautéed, or in stir fries.  Just snap off the stem and pull the vein down the length of the pod and you're ready to cook them.  You can tell the difference between shell peas and snap peas by tasting the pod.  The snap pea pod will taste sweet.

These squash can all be cooked as you would for any squash, steamed, sautéed, or in stir fries.  Remember Lisa's motto: "When in doubt, sauté with lots of garlic!"
