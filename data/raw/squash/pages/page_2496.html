Our Story

Moving from Long Island in 1958, the late Joe Nicholson Sr. and Emily Nicholson purchased the farm that same year from the Brownlee family.

The Nicholsons previously owned and operated Nicholson's Poultry Farm in Bethpage, NY, on Long Island, between 1943 and 1958 until they had to move to make way for the new Wantaugh-Oyster Bay Parkway. They purchased what was then Red Jacket Fruit Farms in Geneva, NY, 350 miles to the northwest of Bethpage. This land was well known for fruit production and was the site of a fruit processing plant and cannery in the late 1800's.

Then, only a small mixed fruit and crop farm, the original trees were planted in 1917. When the Nicholson's arrived, they planned on starting a nursery for ornamental shrubs and flowers. However, given the diversity of fruit already growing on the farm (including grapes, pears, raspberries, and strawberries in addition to apples) and the location, which was close to Geneva’s budding business district on a busy thoroughfare, Joe and Emily decided to try their hand at roadside retailing. The original stand was nothing more than an improvised shed with one window to display limited quantities of the various fruits being harvested.


The success of direct marketing to summer passersby along routes 5&20, then the major east-west route of New York State (prior to the NYS Thruway), led to the construction of a more permanent stand with large covered porches and a small cold room to keep additional stock. In addition to supervising the raising of five children, Emily ran the farm stand operation, with an eye to presenting the best quality fruit and making sure every customer left pleased with their purchase. Meanwhile, Joe Sr. saw to all the farms needs, all the while keeping an eye out for new varieties and fruits to introduce to customers in the stand.

Today, the Nicholson family grows, packs, and distributes high quality fruit from over 500 acres of modern orchards. We utilize Integrated Pest Management (IPM) practices and specialize in growing high quality, flavorful fruit. The operation has grown to include four different farms located along Seneca Lake, in the area from Geneva to Penn Yan, N.Y. Current plantings include approximately 375 acres of apples, mainly on dwarfed rootstock that allow for planting up to 1100 trees per acre. Years ago, standard apple orchards had approximately 75 large trees to the acre. These smaller trees provide for better sunlight and air movement to the fruiting wood, yielding larger and more colorful, flavorful apples, all the while decreasing the need for excessive insect and disease control.

In the early 1960's, Red Jacket Orchards began growing its second most important, crop... strawberries. Today we grow strawberries using advanced techniques that include planting in high humus soils, and using raised beds or ridges to allow for a better plant environment. We also grow 125 acres of other fruits including raspberries, sweet cherries, sour cherries, peaches, plums, apricots, summer plums, Italian prune plums, and rhubarb. Additional crops include pumpkins, squash, hot peppers and tomatillos.

In addition to growing high quality, flavorful fruits Red Jacket Orchards freshly-presses a variety of unique, premium ciders and juices. Starting with Joe Sr.’s delicious apple cider, which he began pressing over 40 years ago, to our new line of Summer Harvest Blends that are only available seasonally, we take advantage of our supply of high quality fruits to produce an array of full flavor, not from concentrate 100% fruit juices and ciders .

The second and third generations of the Nicholson family are committed to continuing Joe and Emily’s tradition of delivering high quality products and excellent customer service, and look forward to building a company that provides consumers with healthy, wholesome flavorful farm fresh products.
