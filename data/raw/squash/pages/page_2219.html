Tastes Great, Open Late:
The List

By Dale Rice
Austin American-Statesman

It's Austin's gustatorial myth: There's no place to eat late at night. In fact, it's possible to dine on a wide variety of post-midnight fare, from American to Chinese and Tex-Mex to Vietnamese. So the next time hunger trumps sleep, you have your pick of more than 30 restaurants that serve food after 11 p.m. One of them is bound to transform your cravings into pleasant dreams. (Note: This is not a complete list. Besides the fast-food chains and hole-in-the-wall takeout joints that we chose not to include, we'll bet there are some places that would meet our criteria that didn't cross our radar. If you have a favorite that we've left out, e-mail drice@statesman.com.)


Open late

(at least until 11 p.m. most weeknights and later than 11 on Friday and Saturday)

Baby Acapulco: When your hunger goes South, satisfy it with Tex-Mex. 1628 Barton Springs Road, 474-8774; 1705-A S. Lakeshore Blvd., 447-1339; and 5610 N. Interstate 35, 302-1366; 11 a.m. to midnight Monday through Thursday, 11 a.m. to 1 a.m. Friday and Saturday, 11 a.m. to 11 p.m. Sunday. 13609 N. Interstate 35, 670-9111; 11 a.m. to 11 p.m. Monday through Thursday, 11 a.m. to 1 a.m. Friday and Saturday, 11 a.m. to 11 p.m. Sunday.

Bakehouse: Get a South Austin twist on the cuisine of Mexico, Germany, India, Japan and England at this casual restaurant that also features Texas favorites such as chicken fried steak and great thick-cut french fries with horseradish sauce. 5404 Manchaca Road, 443-5167, 7 a.m. to midnight daily.

Bitter End: This microbrewery in the Warehouse District turns out eclectic fare as good as its award-winning beers. 311 Colorado St., 478-2337, dinner from 5 to 10:30 p.m. Sunday through Thursday and 5 to 11 p.m. Friday and Saturday, with a late-night menu until midnight Sunday through Thursday and 1 a.m. Friday and Saturday.

Cheesecake Factory: A hot spot from the day it opened, this large restaurant in the Arboretum complex has a huge menu with nearly two dozen appetizers and even more entrees. 10000 Research Blvd., Suite 256; 241-0777; 11 a.m. to 11:30 p.m. Monday through Thursday, 11 a.m. to 12:30 a.m. Friday and Saturday, 10 a.m. to 11:30 p.m. Sunday.

Chez Zee: Though not open terribly late on weekdays, this North Austin mainstay offers enough time to grab a fabulous post-theater dessert -- such as lemon-rosemary cake topped with lemon curd or four-layer chocolate decadence cake -- as well as a variety of appetizers and entrees at this Northwest Austin cafe. Weekends, you have until the witching hour. 5406 Balcones Drive, 454-2666, 11 a.m. to 10 p.m. Monday through Thursday, 11 a.m. to midnight Friday, 9 a.m. to midnight Saturday, 9 a.m. to 10 p.m. Sunday.

Crimson: You can be naughty or nice when dining late at Crimson, which divides its menu between healthier, leaner choices and more decadent, creamier options. 407 Colorado St., 473-2700, 11 a.m. to 2 p.m. Monday through Friday, 5 to 11 p.m. Monday through Thursday, 5 p.m. to midnight Friday and Saturday.

D&L's Texas Music Cafe: One of the newest restaurants in Austin, this spot features Southern-style food with a Cajun influence. Breakfast is served anytime except during dinner hours, so it's possible to dine late on homestyle biscuits and cream gravy or potato pancakes. 1321 S. Congress Ave., 445-4441, 11 a.m. to 11 p.m. Tuesday-Friday, 9 a.m. to 11:30 p.m. Saturday, 9 a.m. to 10:30 p.m. Sunday.

Hyde Park Bar & Grill: The restaurant with the big fork in front is an Austin classic. Chow down on burgers (the half-pound sirloin is a treat), salads, sandwiches and the city's best french fries, which are batter-dipped, fried in peanut oil and served with a spicy sauce. 4206 Duval St., 458-3168, 11 a.m. to midnight daily.

Sam's Bar-B-Que: It's possible to deal with that post-midnight hankering for barbecue. Just head east to Sam's for a plateful of tender meat, including mutton, and a variety of homemade sides. 2000 E. 12th St., 478-0378, 10 a.m. to 2 a.m. Sunday through Thursday, 10 a.m. to 3 a.m. Friday and Saturday.

Taqueria Arandas No. 5: Who doesn't like inexpensive Mexican food? You can find it here in the form of enchiladas, tacos and other fare, with many plates (which include rice and beans) for less than $5. 2448 S. First St., 707-0887, 7 a.m. to midnight daily.

The Parlor: If cheese, tomatoes and bread sound better the later it gets, there's a place to fill your pizza cravings. The Parlor has only two specialties: regular and French-bread pizzas made from scratch. 100 E. North Loop, 454-8965, 5 p.m. to midnight Monday through Friday, 5 p.m. to 1 a.m. Saturday, 5 p.m. to 11 p.m. Sunday.

T&S Chinese Seafood: A favorite late-night hangout of local chefs, T&S produces more than delicious seafood, although it's tough to pass up the salt and pepper shrimp. 10014 N. Lamar Blvd., 339-8434, 11 a.m. to 2:30 p.m. for lunch and 5 p.m. to 1 a.m. for dinner daily (except Tuesdays, when the restaurant closes at 10 p.m.).

Wanfu:These large, South Austin restaurants feature Chinese food, with options such as sliced duck in ginger sauce and chicken in garlic sauce. 2400 E. Oltorf St., 462-3535, 11 a.m. to 4 a.m. daily; Wanfu Too, 1806 Barton Springs Road, 478-3535, 11 a.m. to 2 a.m. daily.

888 Vietnamese Restaurant: Within shouting distance of Wanfu on Oltorf Street, the late-night Asian options expand to Vietnamese foods, including pho. 2400 E. Oltorf St., 448-1181, 11 a.m. to 3 p.m. and 6 p.m. to 2 a.m. Monday through Friday, 11 a.m. to 2 a.m. Saturday, 11 a.m. to midnight Sunday.

Open 24 hours

Katz's Deli: Boasting a New York attitude with an Austin twist, Katz's has all the traditional deli fare, from sandwiches piled high with pastrami and corned beef to bagels with lox and matzo ball soup. At midnight, the line can be long. 618 W. Sixth St., 472-2037.

Kerbey Lane Cafe: This growing local chain has become a reliable standby for late-night diners. Ginger pancakes are a favorite with many, but the fresh-fruit daily-special 'cakes are the winner in my book. 3704 Kerbey Lane, 451-1436; 12602 Research Blvd., 258-7757; 2700 S. Lamar Blvd., 445-4451; 2606 Guadalupe St., 477-5717.

Magnolia Cafe: It has all the ambience of Austin's slacker days, without neglecting the food and service. An eclectic menu, with tacos, breakfast items and salads, provides a host of options. 1920 S. Congress Ave., 445-0000; 2304 Lake Austin Blvd., 478-8645.

Star Seeds: Breakfast, including huge tacos, is served 24 hours a day at this diner, along with typical Austin fare: burgers, chicken wings and quesadillas. 3101 N. Interstate 35, 478-7107.

Bar food

Casino el Camino: If you're trolling Sixth Street late, you can have one of the best burgers in town. The "Buffalo" begins with three-fourths of a pound of ground beef and is topped with hot sauce and blue cheese. 517 E. Sixth St., 469-9330, 4 p.m. to 1:30 a.m. daily.

Dog & Duck Pub: English pub grub gets the nod here on the regular menu, with fish and chips, a hot plowman's lunch (with pork sausages) and bubble and squeak (a cabbage, potato and onion pancake). Late night, shepherd's pie, Scotch eggs and hot dogs are available. 406 W. 17th St., 479-0598, full menu 11 a.m. to midnight, late night menu midnight to 2 a.m.

Fox and Hound Smokehouse and Tavern: This menu features standard bar food -- burgers, chicken fingers and pizzas -- along with grub from the smokehouse, such as barbecue, steaks and ribs. 401 Guadalupe St., 494-1200, 11 a.m. to 2 a.m. daily.

Opal Divine's Freehouse: There is a wide range of options here, including Philly cheesesteak sandwiches and appetizers such as nachos and jalapeño poppers. 700 W. Sixth St., 477-3308, 11 a.m. to 1 a.m. daily.

219 West: Food relates thematically to cocktails here, from corndogs and mini bacon cheeseburgers on the beer porch to jalapeño crab dip and and mini blackened snapper po' boys at the mint julep lounge, 219 W. Fourth St., 474-2194, 5 p.m. to midnight Monday - Friday, 8 p.m. to midnight Saturday.
