College Badminton

In Presentation College there is no official badminton club but there certainly is a badminton 'spirit' which shows itself in the eagerness and enthusiasm with which our players approach the game. Our players use the court at every possible opportunity, to do drills, to practice for tournaments, or for the mere pleasure of playing.

The college team has established itself as one of the best in Trinidad and Tobago. This is clearly indicated by our tournament performances. In a round robin team tournament held in October of 1986, Presentation College teamed up with Naparima Girls High School and together captured the 1st and 2nd place in the 16-19 and 3rd in the under 14 age groups. In the Secondary Schools Badminton Association open tournament held in March of 1987, Pres again captured the 1st, 2nd and 3rd places in senior singles and doubles.

Special thanks is owed to Mrs. Henry of Naparima Girls High School for her generosity, patience and hard work in the organisation of our team. We also wish to thank Mr. Karl Khan for the time spent coaching our team and for his constant support at all tournaments in which we have participated.

The present players would like to see a continuation of this spirit, which, together with hard work, will ensure continuing success for our future teams.

William Smith Form 6 1 Science

College Lawn Tennis

At present, there is no representative lawn tennis team in Presentation College. There are, however, no fewer than three students who represent Trinidad and Tobago at the sport: Sean Boyce and Robert Evelyn in the under 16 division and Simon Evelyn in the under 12 division. There are also numerous tennis enthusiasts who regularly avail of the facilities provided by the school.

During the Easter holidays, the National Milo Tennis Tournament took place. Sean Boyce made it to the finals

in three categories, winning the under 16 doubles title with Simm Denakuga. Having despatched Robert Evelyn in the semi-finals, he eventually lost the under 16 finals. Simon Evelyn won the under 12 singles and doubles competition.

In the CARIFTA games, Sean Boyce won gold and silver medals in the doubles and singles competition respectively in his age group. Robert Evelyn won a gold medal in the consolations.
