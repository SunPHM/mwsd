Kitchen Hint; To make a great vegetable broth to have on hand, thoroughly wash all vegetables you use during the week and place "discarded" skins, stems and peelings in a ziplock bag in the frig. At the end of the week, boil the "leftover" veggies in water just enough to cover; adding your favorite seasonings. Strain or puree. Store in refrigerator - ready for a soup base!

Cream of Wild Rice Soup

As days and nights cool down, soup is added much more often to our menus. Fresh vegetables and wild rice make this hearty enough for a meal - perfect with a fresh fruit salad.

Makes 2 ½ quarts - about 8 full servings

Ingredients;

1 large onion; chopped
1 large carrot; shredded
1 celery rib; chopped
1/4 C. butter
½ C. unbleached all-purpose flour
8 C. chicken or vegetable broth (low sodium)
3 C. cooked, wild rice
1 C. cubed cooked chicken breast (optional)
1/4 t. salt
1/4 t. pepper
1 C. fat-free evaporated milk
1/4. C. fresh snipped chives

Instructions;

In large saucepan, saute the onion, carrot and celery in butter until tender. Stir in flour until blended. Gradually add broth. Stir in rice, chicken, salt and pepper; bring to boil over medium heat. Cook and stir for 2 minutes or until thickened. Stir in milk; cook 3-5 minutes longer. Ready to serve, but simmering very low for 15-30 minutes will help release flavors. Garnish with chives.

Butternut Squash Rolls

These rolls are a great way to use up leftover squash, or to get non-squash lovers to eat this delicious vegetable. They would make a wonderful change to the bread at Thanksgiving, too!

20 rolls

Ingredients:

1 T. active dry yeast
1/4 C. warm water (110*-115*)
1 t. sugar
2/3 C. warm fat-free milk (110*-115*)
1 C. mashed, cooked butternut squash; room temperature
1/3 C. melted butter
1/3 C. packed brown sugar
1 t. salt
2 C. whole wheat flour
2-2 ½ C. unbleached all-purpose flour

Instructions:

In a mixing bowl, dissolve yeast in warm water. Add sugar - let stand 5 minutes. Stir in warm milk, squash, butter, brown sugar and salt. Add whole wheat flour. Beat on medium speed for 2 minutes. Stir in enough of the unbleached flour to form a soft dough. Use bread maker settings for DOUGH and follow instructions for ROLLS, (or follow conventional punch down, rise method). Divide into 20 pieces; shaping into balls. Coat cookie sheet with non-stick spray, place rolls 2" apart; making shallow slashes on top of roll with knife. Cover and let rise until doubled; about 45 minutes. Bake at 400* for 11-13 minutes until brown. Cool on wire racks and serve.

For leftovers at breakfast, serve with apple or pumpkin butter.

Chunky Apple-Bran Muffins

This simple, moist muffin is perfect for summer mornings or included in a light lunch. You get a head start to preparing by using packaged bran muffin mix!

Makes 10 muffins

Ingredients;

1 C. chunky applesauce
2 egg whites
1 t. ground cinnamon
1 t. real vanilla extract
1 package (7 oz) bran muffin mix

Instructions;

Grease 10, of the 12-cup muffin pan, or line 10 cups with paper baking cups and set aside. Place all ingredients in medium-size bowl and stir until combined. Spoon batter into the prepared muffin cups, filling no more than 2/3 full. Fill empty muffin cups half way with water to prevent the pan from burning.

Bake in 400*F oven, 15-20 minutes or until toothpick inserted in center of muffin comes out clean. Remove muffins from pan immediately. Cool slightly - serve warm.

Applesauce Cake

Usher in apple season with this low-fat cake. It's so sweet and moist, no one will know it's "almost healthy" for them!!

20 servings

Ingredients;

½ C. egg substitute
½ C. UNsweetened applesauce
1 C. date sugar (or natural sugar)
1/4 C. canola oil
2 C. unbleached all purpose flour
2 t. ground cinnamon; 1 t. ground nutmeg; ½ t. salt
1 ½ t. baking soda
½ C. raisins, ½ C. chopped walnuts or pecans (optional)

Instructions;

In a medium mixing bowl, mix the applesauce, sugar and oil. Allow to rest for 10-15 minutes to best dissolve the sugar. In a small mixing bowl, beat egg substitute for 1 minute on medium speed. Add to applesauce mixture. Blend well. Stir in raisins and nuts.

Pour into 13X9X2" baking pan coated with non-stick spray. Bake at 350* for 25-30 minutes until toothpick in center comes out clean. Cool on wire rack. Sprinkle with powdered sugar or frost with lo-fat cream cheese frosting.

186 calories/slice; 6 grams fat; 30 grams carbohydrates
