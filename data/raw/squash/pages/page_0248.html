Junior squash stars meet the pros

16:36, 02 May 2003

Finalists from Nottinghamshire’s recent Junior Squash Festival will be enjoying VIP treatment at the European Team Squash Championships this Sunday (4 May).
The Junior Festival was supported by Nottinghamshire County Council, with the aim of introducing squash to children who have never played the game before.
Adrian Bradley, Nottinghamshire County Council’s Sports Development Manager, said: “This is an exciting time for squash in Nottinghamshire with more young people than ever taking up the sport.
"We want to make sure that everyone from all communities has the same chance to excel at sporting activities.”
So, schools and leisure centres throughout Nottinghamshire began running squash coaching sessions during the last 6 weeks, with more than 60 children picking up a racquet for the first time.
From these coaching sessions, children were selected to represent their district at the county finals. The eventual winners came from Newark and Sherwood, but all the young players involved have been invited to the grand final of the European Team Squash Championship, held on Sunday 4th May. They have been promised VIP treatment for the day, and it is hoped that after seeing some of the sport's top players competing for the European title, they will be inspired to stick with the sport.
Adrian Bradley added: "It is hoped that the next time the European Championships returns to England, a Nottinghamshire representative will be in the team."
The European Team Squash Championships are being held at Nottingham Squash Club. More information about the tournament is available from the official website at www.eurosquash.co.uk.
If you would like to get involved in squash within Nottinghamshire please contact Steve Amos, England Squash Regional Development Officer, on 0115 9415142.
