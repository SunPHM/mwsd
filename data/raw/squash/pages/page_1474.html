The Program

The Colby squash tradition began in 1971. After numerous seasons of collegiate squash competition, Colby has gained insight into structuring a successful squash program.

First, Colby will continue to attract that unique student-athlete who best represent the institution's high standards. Without talented individuals determined to do their best in academics and athletics, Colby would not have attained its high marks. Our aim is to acquire resourceful individuals who have a desire to compete and accomplish goals both on and off the court.

Second, Colby knows it's easier to win in squash with an experienced staff. That's why Colby's squash coaches are highly experienced and trained to help identify and develop the unique skills of each player. Coaches work with every player to help strengthen game strategy and tactics. In short, the coach's desire is to make every player a valuable contributor to the team. It is understood that every team member is unique. In this capacity coaches and team members are always supportive of one another and encourage independent training methods to better enhance a player's performance.

Since adopting these two guidelines, Colby has maintained a strong presence on the collegiate squash scene and has helped to enrich the undergraduate experience of all of its team members.

Squash players at Colby should expect a rigorous season from November through February. Practices will incorporate a variety of court drills and challenge matches to get the most out of every player's game. Reaching each player's peak performance will be the desired goal for the coach as well as the cardinal rule for all team members.

Colby also has a distinct advantage in that students take only one course in the month of January. This allows them to train intensely during the heart of the squash season in one of the finest collegiate squash facilities.
