Mick and Helen recently visited Freeport to prepare the population for our second coming. They have sent me a few photographs of some old friends who you may remember:

Ezra Knowles. Mick was trying to find out just how did Ezra learn to hit the squash ball so hard? Ezra's big reunion concern " is J. D. Harrison bringing his squash kit"?

Donna Roberts. Mick as usual with one of his many women. Donna (alis Wilson) Roberts looking forward to welcoming her Mum, Dad, Sister Pam and children to the reunion. Check her out at Shenanigans bar in Port Lucaya.

Selva Cooper (Helen has known her for a mere 19 years) and daughter Barbara (daughter Betsy-Lee not shown) at their Hair Braiding pitch outside the Jewelers warehouse in Port Lucaya. Their services are recommended for those women and the occasional man who want to have their hair braided during the reunion visit.

Colin Rose and a Grand Bahama Sunset.

Dolphins. Just a few of the dolphins to be seen at the Dolphin Experience

www.dolphinexperience.com

Barry Taylor. As usual Mick found Barry with a bottle in his hand, obviously the accident with SuperGlue many years ago is still causing him some problems (he has never been able to put the bottle down since then). He has always liked playing with mammal and somehow he has turned it in to a full time business. Seems Barry has been

Lou Carroll. According to Mick, Lou was a worthy squash opponent, but Mick wants Lou to know he has been down to the Gym since their last game.

MacCarthur Rigby and Alvin Palmer. Mick has definitely learnt his lesson and will definitely not be suggesting to them that he could beat both of them together at the same time.

Era Green, Yvonne and a Young Man (I feel sure I know the place). The picture was taken in Honeywell (formely Syntex). They will be meeting us at the Syntex bonfire at Smiths point.

Chiara Petrucci and Sarah Hamilton. Rumor has one of them was in Adam Scott's Class at school.

Gill Hotelling of Regency Theatre fame. 

Helen just taking a moment to contemplate.

Two well know celebrities (Stuart Wardell and Terry Dobbins  from Freeport, or at least that's how they describe themselves, recently met at the world renowned Whitwood Truck Stop, just off the M62 near Leeds.  Rumour has it they spent the time crying into their beer because they cannot join us all in Freeport this year. Stuart has sent the enclosed photograph with the following words of wisdom:
"I have great pleasure in enclosing a photograph of perhaps two of the greatest sportsmen ever to reside in the City of Freeport. I always felt the likes of Bernie Flint, Mick Lowe and John Lively would have reached greater sporting heights, had they modeled themselves on these two stars".
