Bound High (Never released)

Devil Busters (Never released)
 

Dragon Hopper (Never released)

Face Ball (Never released?)
By Bullet Proof Software.
 

Galactic Pinball
A lousy Pinball game from Nintendo. One nice thing, though, is the Metroid space ship that does a flyby on one of the Bonus courses!

Goldeneye 007 (Never released)
By Nintendo.

Golf aka T&E Virtual Golf
A golf game by T&E Soft and released by Nintendo.
 

G-Zero aka Zero Racers (Never released)
A sequel to F-zero for the VB (from Nintendo of course) that sadly never got released. That´s a shame because the Virtual Boy could really have used a good racer!
Click here for a  picture from the game.

In Mouse's House (Japan only)
In this 3D adventure game you view everything like you do in, for example, Doom and Quake. The differences are that this game is all in red and black, the screen flicks and the monsters are rather ugly and dull! Other things that make this game a bit too frustrating are:
1. The courses have a time limit, making it rather hectic.
2. There isn't enough ammo scattered around the courses.
3. Every single time you fire a shot the crosshair recenters itself and you have to aim again! Avoid this crappy game! By I´Max.

Jack Brothers
An action game from a relatively unknown company named Atlus. This is a Gauntlet clone where you have to choose one of 3 different characters to guide you through the never ending flow of monster-filled mazes. The 3 different characters are: Jack Frost: a crazy looking snowman that can throw snowballs, Jack Lantern: A ghost with a head that looks like a pumpkin and who uses a lantern to shoot fireballs and Jack Ripper who is a skeleton dressed in a coat that has a close-range dagger as weapon. Of course, all the characters have a special attack too. Jack Bros is a pretty fun, but way too easy.
Click here for a  picture from the game.

Mario Clash
The classic arcade/NES game Mario Bros in 3D! When it was first shown it was only a red and black version of the old arcade/NES game but at the next show Nintendo had recoded the game. It was now built with several planes that were laid behind each other, giving the game a  fairly good 3D effect. Of course, you´ll meet the old sidesteppers and Shellcreepers/Koopa Troopas from the original and some new enemies too. For example: The Flying Goombas. Sadly, Mario Clash is a little bit too simple and predictable!
Click here for a  picture from the game.

Mario´s Tennis
A tennis game with Mario, Peach, Luigi and friends. The game was released with the VB in Japan. One of the better VB games but a little bit spoiled by the VB´s loss of a link option, which means that you can't play against a mate, just the computer.
Click here for a  picture from the game.

Mighty Morphi'n Power Rangers (Luckily never released)
 

Nester´s Funky Bowling
A surprisingly good game! For being a bowling game that is. You can play in 3 different modes: Bowl (plain bowling), Practice and Challenge, where you must succeed in several predefined scenarios. From Nintendo

Panic Bomberman
A cross between Tetris and Kirby´s avalanche with some Bomber Man influence. Rather good, actually. Made by Hudson Soft and released by Nintendo.
Click here for a  picture from the game.

Red Alarm
A Starwing-like game from T&E that makes good use of the VB´s abilities to show realistic 3D worlds. The plot goes like this: You are a pilot of a "Tecfighter" and must fight against the evil supercomputer name KAOS. If you ignore the not-so-great story, Red Alarm is a good blaster with nice 3d effects, though the graphics can get quite muddled sometimes! By T&E Soft and released by Nintendo.
Click here for a  picture from the game.

Space Invaders: Virtual Collection (Japan only)
How fun can Space Invaders be? Well Taito released a Space Invaders game for the VB to prove that it can be very much so. Firstly, they have 6 different modes. Original Space Invaders where you play the classic game, Space Invaders 2 both in 3D and in 2D, Time Attack, Score Mode and the new 3D version of the Space Invaders game. How do you do Space Invaders 3D then? Well instead of the invaders attacking in rows that start at the top of the screen and go down, they start in the background of the battlefield and the "lowest" row is nearest the screen. If you´re not a Space Invaders fan then you might want to avoid this!

Space Squash (Japan only)
A really crappy 3D squash game from a company called Coconut! The title is also a bit misleading. You're flying around in space with a robot, boxing a spiky ball at another robot that boxes it back to you. I wouldn't call that squash. Well, Space Squash has got one good side. It is one of the few VB games to make use of the 3D abilities.

Street Fighter 2 (Never released)
Of course, every other console´s got at least one Street Fighter game! Luckily it was never released!
 

Teleroboxer
A boxing game like Punchout from Nintendo.

Tetris 3D
Tetris in 3d, how exciting! By T&E Soft and released by Nintendo.

Wario Cruise (Wario Land 2?)
A sequel to Wario Land (Game Boy) with some nice 3D effects. It´s got 15 levels in which Wario must find the hidden key to unlock the door to the next level. If you like Wario Land then this is right up your alley! This is one of the rare really good VB titles. Wish that all VB games were this good!
Click here for a  picture from the game.

Water World
A game based on the famous Kevin Costner movie from Ocean (as always when  it´s a game based on a movie license!). From what i´ve heard it´s a real sucker so avoid it!

Vertical Force
Vertical Force is like 1942, only with better game play and graphics. In 1942 you technically had two plying fields, just like Vertical Force, only it wasn't at the same time. If you remember playing 1942 on the NES, after you beat a certain stage it would zoom in closer to the ground. The difference between that and Vertical Force is that you have both playing fields at the same time. Just press the A button and you will go up and down between the two levels. It was a great idea by Hudson Soft, and it really utilized the Virtual Boy's 3D capabilities. The game has great sound, for a Virtual Boy game that is, and, like I said before, the graphics are great. Overall I give this game a 9. Written by: Matt (gonow.to/matt) ICQ #:6956754
Click here for a  picture from the game.

Virtua Tetris aka. V-tetris (Japan only)
A new Tetris game from the masters of Tetris clones: Bullet Proof software! This game consists of 2 game modes: one were you can play the original Tetris and one were you play Tetris in "3D". Okay it´s not in "3D" but it has some weird function that when you press the "L" or "R" buttons the screen sort of wraps around itself, enabling a much bigger play area. Like most other VB games, V-Tetris doesn´t make good use of the VB´s abilities at all and could easily have been an SNES or GB game instead.
Click here for a  picture from the game.

Virtual Bomberman
(Never Released)

Virtual Fishing (Japan only)
A Fishing game from THQ! Your task is to win a fishing competition. You start out on one of two chooseable locations in a landscape of rivers and lakes. First you cast your line out into the water. Now you see a picture of both the rod and the reel and you must slowly reel the bait in. Occasionally a fish takes the bait and the real game begins. Now you view the "action" from an underwater view and you have to tap buttons to reel in the fish. Sounds fun? Well it is at first but once you´ve got the hang of it it´s way too easy to bring in even the tough fish. This makes the game a little bit dull after a while, but Virtual Fishing is still a fun and unusual game.
