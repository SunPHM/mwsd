Roast Turkey

12-14 lb. organic turkey
1/2 lemon, cut into 2 wedges
1 Granny Smith apple
1/2 onion, quartered
8 large sage sprigs
4 Tbsp. sage, diced
4 Tbsp. dried thyme
Lemon juice
1 cup water

Preheat oven to 325ºF. Rinse the turkey and pat it dry inside and out. Pack the neck and body cavities with the lemon, apple, onion, and sage sprigs. Tie and secure the legs and wings. Mix the diced sage and dried thyme together. Rub the outside of the turkey with the lemon juice, then pat it with the thyme and sage mix. Place the turkey in the middle of the oven and add the water to the bottom of the roasting pan. Baste every 20 minutes until your meat thermometer (inserted into the fleshy part of a thigh) reads 180º and the juices run clear.


 

Shiitake Mushroom Gravy

1/4 lb. fresh shiitake mushrooms, sliced
1 large onion, thinly sliced
4 garlic cloves, minced
3 Tbsp. olive oil
2 Tbsp. arrowroot
2 Tbsp. fresh marjoram
1 quart vegetable stock
Tamari to taste
Pinch of cayenne pepper
Herbamare* to taste

Dissolve the arrowroot in 1 cup of the vegetable stock and set aside. Heat the olive oil and saute the mushrooms, garlic, and onion until they are softened and transparent. Pour in the stock, tamari, marjoram, and dissolved arrowroot. Simmer until the mixture has thickened. Season with cayenne pepper and herbamare, if desired.

Cranberry Relish

2 large navel oranges, quartered
12 oz. cranberries, fresh or frozen
3/4 cup honey
Orange zest (optional)

Peel and pith the oranges. Put the oranges, cranberries, and zest into a food processor. Pulse until coarsely chopped and stir in the honey. Cover and chill for at least 24 hours.


 

Baked Lentil Loaf

2 cups lentils, soaked, sprouted
2 medium onions, coarsely chopped
3 garlic cloves
3 Tbsp. sage
4 celery ribs, chopped
4 cups broccoli
1 large handful parsley
Cayenne to taste

Soak the lentils overnight. Pour off the soak water, spread the lentils in a colander, and cover with a towel to sprout. Let them sit for 24 hours, rinsing approximately every six hours. Combine the lentils, onions, garlic, sage, celery, broccoli, parsley, and cayenne pepper in a food processor and pulse until coarsely blended. Pat the mixture into a shallow baking pan. Bake at 350º for 1 hour.


 

Sauteed Greens
with Pomegranate Seeds

1 bunch collard greens
2 bunches red chard
1 medium onion, thinly sliced
4-5 garlic cloves, minced
3 Tbsp. sesame oil
1/2 cup water
2/3 cup pomegranate seeds
Herbamare* to taste

Cut the stems and center ribs away from the collard greens and chard. Heat the oil over moderately high heat in a heavy kettle. Saute the garlic and onion until they are soft. Add the stems from the greens and cook until they are tender, stirring occasionally. Next, add the leaves and turn them until they are wilted. Stir in the water, then top with the pomegranate seeds. Cover and cook until the seeds are tender, about 4 minutes. Season with herbamare.


 

Roasted New Potatoes
with Rosemary

6 lbs. tiny new potatoes or 14 medium, cut in half
2 cups water or vegetable broth
2 Tbsp. arrowroot
3/4 cup lemon juice
6 garlic cloves, minced
2 Tbsp. herbamare*
1 tsp. dried oregano
1/3 cup fresh rosemary

Cook the water or vegetable broth with the arrowroot until the mixture is thickened. Add the lemon juice, garlic, herbamare, oregano, and rosemary. Coat the potatoes with this marinade and space them on two cookie sheets. Roast at 400º for one hour, turning occasionally.


 

Poached Spiced Pears

4 large pears, peeled
Juice from one lemon
2 cups water
2 cups cranberry juice
1/3 cup honey
5 orange rind strips
2 cinnamon sticks
1 star anise (optional)
5 tsp. chopped crystallized ginger (for garnish)

Sprinkle the peeled pears with the lemon juice. Combine the remaining ingredients in a large, non-aluminum sauce pan. Bring the mixture to a boil, reduce heat, cover, and cook for five minutes. Add the pears, turning them occasionally. Serve the pears with the sauce and garnish with ginger.


 

 

Stuffed Turban Squash

1 large turban squash
1 pkg. sm. boiling onions
3/4 cup wild rice
2 cups brown rice
6 1/2 cups water
2 Tbsp. olive oil
6 garlic cloves, minced
6 green onions, chopped
6 celery ribs, chopped
1 red pepper, chopped
1 yellow pepper, chopped
1 cup parsley, chopped
3 Tbsp. rosemary
3 Tbsp. sage
2 tsp. cumin
1 tsp. oregano
1 tsp. thyme
Cayenne pepper to taste
Herbamare* to taste
1 cup pumpkin seeds

Cook the wild and brown rice in the water until tender. Saute the garlic, green onions, celery, red pepper, and yellow pepper in the olive oil. Combine the rice and sauteed mixture in a bowl along with the small boiling onions, parsley, pumpkin seeds, and spices. Remove the top hat from the turban squash and clean out the seeds from the inside of the squash. Stuff the squash with the rice mixture and top with the hat. Bake at 325º until the squash is cooked but still firm, approximately 1 hour. Slice the squash in wedges and serve with the rice. If the rice mixture is dry, add vegetable broth or water before stuffing.


 

Sweet Potato Soup in Squash Cups

3 sweet potatoes, medium size
4 garlic cloves
3 leeks, medium size
1 Tbsp. olive oil
3-4 cups vegetable stock
1 large handful parsley
Curry to taste
Cayenne pepper
to taste

Peel the sweet potatoes, cook them in boiling water, drain, and set aside. Saute the leeks and garlic in the olive oil until they are transparent. Puree the potatoes, adding the vegetable stock until the mixture is of a soup consistency. Put the mixture in a saucepan, adding the sauteed leeks and garlic. Season to taste with curry and cayenne pepper. Serve in small, hollowed squash cups. Garnish with mint leaves.


 

Pumpkin Pie -- crust

2 cups pitted dates Pulse dates and nuts in a food processor. Press into an eight-inch pie plate. Bake at 325º until the crust is set, approximately 20 minutes. Let cool completely.

Pumpkin Pie -- filling

1 large can of pumpkin filling
2 cups apple juice
1 tsp. cinnamon
1/2 tsp. allspice
1/4 tsp. cardamom
1/4 tsp. cloves
1/4 tsp. nutmeg
1/2 cup maple syrup
1 1/2 Tbsp. vanilla extract
3 Tbsp. agar-agar**

In a small saucepan, combine the agar-agar with 1/2 cup of the apple juice. Bring the mixture to a slow simmer and cook over medium heat for 10 minutes. Place the pumpkin filling in a medium sauce pan with the remaining ingredients. Whisk in the agar mixture until smooth, pour into pie pan, and chill.


 

Millet and Root Vegetable Casserole

3/4 pound celery root (1 large)
3/4 pound white turnips (3 medium)
3/4 pound rutabaga (3)
1/2 pound parsnips (5)
1/4 cup chives, plus a few teaspoons
1 medium onion, sliced
1/2 cup scallions
1/2 cup parsley
1 1/2 cups millet
3 1/2 cups water
1 cup rice milk
2 Tbsp. arrowroot
2 Tbsp. tamari
1 tsp. turmeric
Sea salt to taste
Cayenne pepper to taste
Paprika, for garnish

Soak the millet overnight, then pour off the water. Bring 3 1/2 cups water to a boil and add the millet. Cover and simmer on low until the liquid is absorbed. Peel and slice the celery root, turnips, rutabaga, parsnips, chives, and onion. Place the vegetables and a small amount of water and tamari in a covered sauce pan. Cook until tender. Puree the cooked millet and vegetables in a food processor until they are smooth.

Whisk the arrowroot and 1/4 cup of the rice milk in a sauce pan. Bring the mixture to a simmer then add the turmeric, sea salt, cayenne, and 3/4 cup rice milk. Cook until thickened. Pour the sauce into the millet-vegetable mixture, add the scallions and parsley, and mix well. Pour into a slightly oiled dish. Cover with foil and bake at 375º for 45 minutes to one hour. Garnish with paprika and chives.

Corn Relish

4 ears of corn, uncooked
1 red pepper, chopped
1 yellow pepper, chopped
1/2 red onion, chopped
1 jar cherry peppers, seeded, chopped
Cayenne pepper to taste
Sesame oil to coat
1 tsp. turmeric

Cut the kernels off the cob. In a large bowl combine the corn with the red and yellow peppers, onion and cherry peppers. Add the cayenne pepper, turmeric, and sesame oil. Mix well.
