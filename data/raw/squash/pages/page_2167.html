I.S.P. imports 14mm and 22mm solid wood flooring for dwellings, shops, offices and sports facilities.

The wood panels used for mainly dwellings, shops and offices are finger jointed boards of 14mm thick X 129mm wide X 1830mm length.
The wood panels used for sporting facilities are the same length and width but are 22mm thick.

The wooden flooring for dwellings, shops and offices are laid as a "Floating Floor" on a base of 3mm thick polyurethane foam and are glued tongue to grove.
The wooden flooring used for sports facilities is laid on a wooden batten with a special Neoprim pad to give a "spring" to the completed floor.

Each panel is sanded three times and prefinished with either 7 coats of UV lacquer or 3 coats of oil finishing and are tongue and grooved on all four sides for a perfect fit.

The solid wood has the advantage over all other types of flooring in that it can, in addition to giving the feeling of a natural product, be renovated to it's original beauty many times.


The main types of solid wood imported are:

  • Hevea -
    This rubberwood is very suitable for production of timber floors because of the following characteristics:
    1. Rubberwood has an attractive light and uniform colour.
    2. Rubberwood is a medium density hardwood with a hardness similar to oak.
    3. The drying shrinkage of rubberwood is relatively small compared to most other hardwoods of same density.


  • Kempas -
    Kempas timber flooring is a plank floor, where each plank is made from one single piece of solid Kempas. The floor is supplied prefinished with lacquer or water repellent oil on the top face, and it is tongued and grooved on all 4 sides.
    Kempas flooring is brown to reddish in colour which gives the floor an attractive and exclusive look.


  • Merbau -
    Merbau is the traditional timber species for production of tropical flooring. It varies from brownish grey through bronze to dark brown in colour.
    It is a timber of good appearance, generally free from defects and characterized by a small movement.
    Strength and hardness comparable to European beech.


  • Teak -
    An exclusive floor with many valuable properties including durability, strength, moderate weight and pleasing appearance.
    Very dimensionally stable and with a beautiful golden to brown colour which becomes more attractive with time.
    Made from plantation teak which has a slightly more figured appearance than teak from Burma.
    Teak has a moderate to low resistance to wear and is best used for areas with light to moderate traffic.
