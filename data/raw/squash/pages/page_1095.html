Pork, Squash and Sage Pot Pie

Any type of winter squash will work in this dish but butternut is the easiest to peel and cut. Use a good vegetable peeler to remove the skin, then cut with a sharp chef's knife.

Serves: 4

Suggested Wine: Tinhorn Creek Gewurztraminer ($12.50, B.C.)

Ingredients:

  • 1 red onion -- finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon olive oil
  • 1 pound ground pork or turkey
  • 1 small butternut squash -- peeled & cut in
  • 1/4" cubes
  • 2 tablespoons flour
  • salt and pepper
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh sage, or 1 tsp. dried
  • 1/2 teaspoon hot pepper sauce, or chipotle if preferred
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 3 sheets phyllo dough
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Preparation:

  1. Preheat oven to 400 F.
  2. Heat an 8-10" skillet (preferably cast iron or other oven proof pan), add the 1 teaspoon olive oil and the onion. Sauté about 2 minutes and add the pork (or turkey), breaking it up with a fork.
  3. Add the chopped squash and continue to sauté about 2 minutes. Add the flour and cook 1 minute. Add salt and pepper to taste, the sage, hot sauce and 1/2 cup water. Bring to a simmer, cover and cook 5-8 minutes until the squash is almost tender. Correct seasoning.
  4. Brush 1 sheet of phyllo lightly with olive oil. Top with another sheet and brush again.
  5. Top with the third sheet and brush again.
  6. Place the stacked phyllo over the filling, crumpling and tucking as necessary to make it fit the pan.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven about 10 minutes until the phyllo is crisp and the filling is bubbly. Serve with a cooked green vegetable or green salad and crusty bread if desired.
