15/01/2004
 Squash stars head for Canary Wharf

A new eight-man event is set to be held in March in the spectacular setting of London's Canary Wharf ...

Event dates: Tuesday March 23 to Friday March 26, 2004
Venue: The East Wintergarden, Bank Street, Canary Wharf.

WORLD'S SQUASH STARS
HEAD FOR CANARY WHARF

The Canary Wharf Squash Classic 2004 boasts a truly world-class entry, featuring six players currently ranked in the top eight of the current PSA world rankings, plus two of the game's rising stars.

Peter Nicol MBE, who has been ranked number one in the world for the majority of the past five and a half years and is one of the event co-promoters, is delighted to be staging a major new event in his adopted home city of London.

Nicol, who has won all of the game's major titles, including the World Open, British Open, US Open, Canadian Open, a Commonwealth Games gold medal and three PSA Super Series titles, said: "I have played major tournaments in some spectacular locations all over the world. These include Grand Central Station in New York, Boston Symphony Hall, the Broadgate Arena in London, the Crucible Theatre in Sheffield, and an open-air amphitheatre at the foot of the Great Pyramids in Egypt.

"Now we can add Canary Wharf to that list. The beauty of staging major squash events is that you can be both creative and flexible in siting glass courts in wonderful venues.

"The East Wintergarden is a magnificent venue and is perfectly situated for the thousands of squash-loving individuals who work at Canary Wharf.

"We all know that squash and business go hand in hand and we are looking forward to filling the venue for the four nights of the tournament. It is our ambition in future years to develop the Canary Wharf Classic into one of the major events on the world squash calendar."

EVENT FORMAT
The Canary Wharf Squash Classic 2004 is an eight-man invitation event. The players will compete in two round-robin groups of four over the first three nights. The runners-up in each group will compete in a third-fourth place play-off and the two group winners will contest the final.

EVENT TIMETABLE (March 23-26):
Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday: Matches scheduled for 6pm, 7pm, 8pm and 9pm.
Friday: 6.30pm: Third/fourth play-off, 7pm: Peter Nicol Charity Auction. 7.30pm: Grand Final.

NEW SCORING SYSTEM
The Canary Wharf Squash Classic is a new-look tournament at a sparkling new venue. It will also feature a new scoring system aimed at making the game more attractive and exciting for spectators and television.

Matches will be best of five games, with each game point-a-rally to nine. If the scores reach 8-8 a tiebreak comes into play, with the first player to go two points clear the winner.

The marker will call "deuce" or "advantage" as in tennis. However long the tiebreak, the score will be recorded as 10-8 (tiebreak) to the victor.

THE VENUE: THE EAST WINTERGARDEN
The East Wintergarden, completed in October 2003, is a stunning glass atrium between two new office towers which will be used for private hire, events, public entertainment and exhibitions, and is situated adjacent to the Jubilee Line station at Canary Wharf and a short walk from the Heron Quays and Canary Wharf Docklands Light Railway stations.

CORPORATE HOSPITALITY
We are indebted to Chelsea Catering for providing refreshments today and we are delighted to be working with them to provide high-quality Corporate Hospitality packages as well as public catering during the Canary Wharf Squash Classic.

CANARY WHARF GROUP plc
The Canary Wharf Squash Classic 2004 is hosted by Canary Wharf Group plc. Canary Wharf is a major business centre, covering an area of 86 acres some three miles to the east of the Bank of England in the City of London.

The Estate currently has a daily population of approximately 63,000, which will increase to over 100,000 when the estate is fully built. Subject to market conditions, Canary Wharf is expected to be completed within the next five years.

Canary Wharf is more than just a tower: the development to date comprises 17 office buildings, three retail centres, two conference and banqueting centres, two Docklands Light Railway stations, a Jubilee Line station, car parks and approximately 20 acres of landscaped open spaces.

Our tenants came from a variety of industry sectors including media, banking, legal firms and public bodies. They include HSBC, Citigroup, The Telegraph Group, Trinity Mirror, Morgan Stanley, Clifford Chance, CSFB, the FSA, Readers Digest, KPMG, Tubelines and Chevron Texaco. They all enjoy access to over 600,000 sq ft of retail at Canary Wharf, with over 200 shops, bars and restaurants on offer. The vast majority travel to Canary Wharf on public transport, utilising the DLR, Jubilee Line or bus and coach facilities.

Canary Wharf offers an on-going programme of arts and events activities. Co-ordinated by the Arts and Events department, the varied programme includes music concerts, comedy clubs, food and wine events, street theatre, sporting and outdoor events, cinema, dance, visual arts, and literature events. Just some of the artists to have appeared at Canary Wharf include Joan Armatrading, Eddi Reader, Chick Corea, Glenn Tilbrook, James Taylor Quartet and Jools Holland.

Website: www.canarywharf.com

CANARY WHARF CORPORATE CLASSIC
A Corporate Classic event will also be held for companies based at Canary Wharf. Teams will compete in a round robin format similar to the professional event, with the final play-offs all staged on the glass court.

EVENT MANAGEMENT
The Canary Wharf Squash Classic 2004 is jointly promoted by Eventis Sports Marketing Ltd (Peter Nicol, Tim Garner and Angus Kirkland) and TMM Ltd (Alan Thatcher).
