Squash Boys Varsity

The Program
The Nobles Boys Squash program sports some of the most rapidly improving teams in the school. The program features varsity and junior varsity squads, which compete against some of the top institutions in the country in the Independent School League, including Groton School, Milton Academy, and St. Paul's School. The squash program uses a ladder system in which the top seven players compete at the varsity level with the rest of the players competing at the junior varsity level.

The 2003 - 2004 Varsity Team
The team is coming off a re-building year in which team members were forced to play multiple positions on the ladder. Last season, the team finished with a 5-11 record, showing that there is room for improvement this year. After using a youthful and inexperienced lineup last year, this year's team comes back with nearly every member of the varsity squad returning. This year, the team has gotten off on the right foot with clutch 5-2 and 4-3 victories over highly touted St. Georges and Middlesex.

Led by captain Eric Muther (N 04') and Senior leaders Will Kryder (N 04'), Zack Smith (N 04'), and John Hunter (N 04'), the team looks to improve on last year's record and this early season victory puts them on course for their ultimate goal, an ISL championship.


The Facilities
The Morrison Athletic Center boasts some of the finest squash facilities in the region with six international courts, a viewing deck, and located adjacent to the Rappaport Gymnasium. It has hosted multiple national-level squash events, including an exhibition match between two of the top ten women's squash players in the country.

The Coach
The varsity team may have a similar look on its ladder this year, but it has a different man at the helm. The team is led by coach Chris Burr who brings several years of experience to the team. Coach Burr, a strong athlete through high school and college in other sports, found squash at the age of 30 and committed to the game. Soon, he was the top player in his age group in Massachusetts. In order to accomplish such a feat, Coach Burr learned from the best and applied his extraordinary drive to maximize and hone his skills. He hopes to impart this wealth of knowledge about the intricacies of the game and the heart required to be a great player and person to his players.
