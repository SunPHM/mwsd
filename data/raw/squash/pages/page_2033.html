Winter Warm Ups!


Winter brings crisp days and chilly nights, here in Wisconsin that means snow! Stay warm with hot meals with rondele. Entertain your guests with style!

Here are a few ideas from our kitchens to yours. Have a terrific winter season from all of us at rondele!

 

On chilly nights, a warm appetizer hits the spot!

Hot Italian Dip
1- 8 oz. rondelé Garlic & Herbs gourmet spreadable cheese
8 oz. marinara or spaghetti sauce
1 1/2 cup chopped procuitto or hard salami
1/2 cup chopped tomatoes
1/4 cup chopped red peppers
1/4 cup chopped kalmato olives
1 cup shredded parmesan cheese

Spread the gourmet spreadable cheese in a pie pan or equivalent. Spread sauce on top of cheese. Sprinkle meat and other toppings on top of the sauce; top with parmesan cheese. Bake for 15-20 minutes ot 375 degrees until bubbly. Serve with sliced italian bread or crackers.


Here's an attractive & delicious "make ahead" appetizer, perfect for any get together!

Garlic & Herbs Torte
2- 8 oz. rondelé Garlic & Herbs gourmet spreadable cheese
1/2 cup shredded parmesan cheese
1- 4.5 oz. jar red pesto
1- 6.5 oz. jar minced oil-packed sun-dried tomatoes, well drained

Mix gourmet spreadable cheese with parmesan cheese. Divide into thirds. Line loaf pan 8 1/2 x 4 1/2 x 2 1/2 inches, with plastic wrap leaving extra to cover. Spoon one third of cheese mixture in bottom of loaf pan. Smooth top with spatula. Spoon pesto evenly over cheese layer. Spoon one third of the cheese mixture over pesto layer, smooth top with spatula. Spoon tomatoes evenly over second cheese layer. Spoon remaining cheese mixture over tomatoes. Strike mold on counter to pack down. Cover and refrigerate 1-2 days. When ready to serve, invert loaf pan onto serving platter. Remove plastic wrap. Serve torte with baguette slices and crackers.
