Tom is the pastor of the First Church of Shelter Cove, a small congregation of about thirty people here on California's Lost Coast. We are meeting in the Shelter Cove Community Center right now, but have plans to build a church building when funds are availabe.

In addition to his pastoral duties he works four days a week at Nice Cars in Garberville as a used car salesman. 'Talk about an oxymoron--"Pastor/used car salesman"! His car selling job is "over the hill"--a 21 mile (40 minute) trip over the mountain to civilization. The distance/time ratio is not due to traffic, but a windy two-lane road.
Nancy is pretty much a full time housewife. She has gone back to work part time as the baker at a local restaurant. What that means is that she is getting paid for what she would probably do for free---but don't tell anybody. She is Sunday School superintendent and has been teaching Sunday School for 37 years. When time allows she still loves to cook, sew, read,  cross-stitch, quilt--anything "crafty." This Fall she canned anything she could get her hands on --blueberries, blackberries, raspberries, tomatoes, pickles, apples (applesauce, pie apples, apple butter).


Other interests are an almost-daily walk of 3.1 miles around the hills of Shelter Cove, twice-weekly exercise class, church secretary/clerk, and keeping the pastor well-fed, clothed, and his head screwed on straight!
An additional "member" of the family is Barney (named after Barnabus in Jan Karon's "Mitford" series), a very large and lovable 4 year old black Lab.  God has provided us with a beautiful home overlooking the Pacific and a nice garden plot where we grow tomatoes, peppers, squash, beans and zucchini.
We love company and have a guest room with private bath and deck. So y'all come....
Our granddaughters, Kym, 12, and Laura, 8, and Amber, born January 9, 2002, are the joy of our life and, for anyone who doesn't have any--we highly recommend them!
A closing note: You know you live in a small town when......your local postmaster calls to tell you that your tax returns don't have enough postage, but not to worry--he will take care of it and you can just drop by to reimburse him or stick it in the mailbox--whichever is most convenient. It happened to us!
