More Books

In Me Own Words: The Autobiography of Bigfoot
Graham Roumieu
This book debunks myths and stereotypes about Bigfoot. In reality, Bigfoot is a jaded and sarcastic actor who sees himself as a victim of society's cruelty towards misfits. In reality, Bigfoot has simple wants and needs. He wants a chance to differentiate himself from crude Hollywood monsters like Chewbacca and use his range and depth. He needs to dismember people. He wants to like himself more and be accepted by his fellow actors. He needs to eat babies.

"Lots of people make money from bigfoot. You think me see one thin dime? No. Monster truck people no even ask to use name. Teach them lesson. Maybe I smash with log, maybe with rock. I see what tickle fancy when time come. Make hurt good."

Beautifully illustrated with ink and watercolor.

Me Write Book: It Bigfoot Memoir
Graham Roumieu
The second book by Bigfoot is not nearly as good as the first. In Me Own Words is more concise and snarky; Me Write Book is longwinded and overuses poop and dismemberment jokes. However, it further cuts to the core of this misunderstood gentle giant.

"Bigfoot sometime get called rolling stone. Called irresponsible. Have bad debt. Trail of bad lady relationship. No can help. I a hunter-gatherer. And maybe just a bit of an asshole."


Looking for Jake
China Mieville
Short stories by one of my favorite authors. A few were too boring to finish. A few take place in the dark, dangerous megacity his previous books are set in. I liked those the most. All of them have some element of paranoia, conspiracy. That I like.

Out
Natsuo Kirino
Freakin' awesome. A story about four women who work night shift in a factory in Japan, their families, murders, the mafia, loan sharks, perverts. I like this book because the characters are neither all good nor all bad, so you can relate to them. The book really goes into each of their heads and follows their decisionmaking processes after someone is murdered and they dispose of the body. Women bond together in the face of adversity moreso than men. The women here see an out of their shitty home and family situations. This book really fucks with the traditional Japanese housewife gender role; the women excercise their own agency in some extreme ways.
