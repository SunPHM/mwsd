Virus Diseases

Many different viruses can infect vegetable crops and produce a wide range of symptoms. Potato, pepper, tomato, peas, beans, and cucurbits (butternut squash, summer squash, zuchinni) are the crops most often affected in backyard gardens. In many cases, a few striking early symptoms are observed- leaf crinkling and twisting, mottling (green and yellow coloration)- without any season long loss of vigor or yields. In other cases, the virus may produce severe plant symptoms necessitating quick removal.

Cucumber mosaic virus, for example, can cause green and yellow mottling of leaves, curled leaves, stunted plants and fruits with light splotches and warty bumps on them. Host plants include cucumber, squash, melon, bean, spinach, pepper, and tomato. Cucurbits are vulnerable to several other viruses

Management:

Select and plant resistant cultivars. Keep weeds down around the vegetable garden and control insects that transmit viruses to plants- aphids, thrips, leafhoppers, cucumber beetles. Remove infected plants whenever symptoms are observed. Either throw plants out with the garbage or shred and compost them at temperatures above 120° F.
