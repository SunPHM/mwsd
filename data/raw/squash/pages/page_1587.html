Super Series Final: Barada's Back

BARADA TARGETS THE HALIFAX EQUITABLE SUPER SERIES FINALS FOR DRAMATIC COMEBACK
 11 June 2001, © 2001 Squashtalk, file photo: © 2001 Vaughn Winchell

Egyptian squash star Ahmed Barada will make a dramatic return to the international stage next week when he takes his place in the eight-strong field in the 2001 Halifax Equitable Super Series Squash Finals (18-22 June) at London’s Broadgate Arena.

The former world number 3, one of Egypt’s biggest stars both on and off the court, has been out of the international game since November last year during which time he has dropped nine places in the world rankings. Reasons for his prolonged absence are unclear, but his Manager Robert Edwards suggests that he simply needed a break from the game.

“Ahmed has been playing squash day-in-day-out since he was thirteen years of age. He was forced out through injury late last year and that gave him the opportunity to relax and enjoy life away from the court. It was a break he needed and he’s now back, revitalised and ready to start playing again. “Just the other day we spoke about his comeback at the forthcoming Halifax Equitable Super Series Finals in London, and his words to me were “I’m in training, I’ll be back.”

Physically he’s now back to full health and right now he’s working hard on his match fitness.” added Edwards. Such is Barada’s status in Egypt that shortly after the PSA Masters event in Egypt in April he received a personal call from President Mubarak wishing him well and urging him to return to the game as early as possible. “

President Mubarak is a big squash fan, he has a court in the Palace and follows the game with great interest,” said Edwards. “He called Ahmed to say that he was sorry that injury had prevented him from playing in the event and that he was looking forward to seeing him back in international competition, insisting that “Egypt needs you!””

Since Barada’s last appearance on the world stage numerous stories have circulated surrounding the motives for his absence. Whispers of a new career as a film star have been widely rumoured.

“I asked Ahmed about these alleged approaches from film companies when we last spoke,” added Edwards. “He just laughed, saying that he receives offers all the time, but that ultimately he’s a squash player, and that’s where his priorities lie.” Last year’s Super Series Finals at Broadgate also provided the stage for a Barada comeback after a 10-week lay-off from competition. The event marked his return to the game after suffering a horrific knife attack outside his home in Cairo. Barada went on to reach the semi-finals of the event, only to be beaten by England’s Simon Parke.

This year’s Halifax Equitable Super Series Finals, presented by The British Land Company PLC, will take place at London’s Broadgate Arena from 18-22 June. The eight-strong line-up includes Canadian world number one Jonathon Power and England’s world number two Peter Nicol, who will be bidding to become the first player in Super Series Finals history to win three consecutive titles. Also competing for the $70,000 prize fund will be world number three David Palmer, who yesterday became the first Australian for 20 years to clinch the British Open title. The 24-year-old produced a stunning comeback from two games down to defeat England’s Chris Walker at Birmingham’s NIA.

Completing the line-up will be Wales’ world number four David Evans, Scotland’s Martin Heath and England’s Simon Parke and Paul Johnson.
