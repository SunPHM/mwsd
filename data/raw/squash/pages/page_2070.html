Cantaloupe

Canary
-Late maturing, bright yellow corrugated rind. Flesh is pale green to white with a pale orange seed cavity; it has a sweet and distinctive flavor. Fruit is oval, similar to crenshaw, 8 x 6 inches, 6 to 7 pounds.

Casaba
-A non-slip, late maturing, corrugated yellow, sometimes greenish yellow rind, not netted or ribbed, with white, spicy, sweet, and tender flesh. Fruit acorn-shaped, 8 x 7 inches, 7 to 8 pounds.

Charentais
-A popular European melon, also called 'Chaca,' 'French,' or 'Italian melon.' Smooth or slightly netted, gray-green rind with dark green, slightly furrowed sutures. Deep orange, firm, sweet flesh. Fruit is slightly elongated but mostly globe shaped 3-1/2 to 4 inches and 1-1/2 to 2 pounds.

Crenshaw
-Large, late maturing, yellow and green corrugated, rough rind without netting. Pinkish orange, sweet, tender flesh with distinctive flavor. Fruit is elongated with a flattened stem end (elongated acorn-shape), 9 x 7 inches, 7 to 10 pounds.

Eastern-type characteristics:
-Round to oval, usually sutured, and netted; not intended for long-distance shipping.

Honeydew
-Smooth, greenish white rind, turning creamy when ripe. Light green, sweet flesh with some orange fleshed varieties available. Fruit 7 x 7-1/2 inches, 5 to 6 pounds.

Persian
-Very late maturity, green rind with slight tan cracks or sparse netting. Orange-pink, sweet, firm flesh. Fruit is round 7-1/2 x 8 inches, 5 to 6 pounds.

Western-type characteristics:
-Round to slightly oval, sutureless, very well netted, with firm flesh.

Lettuce

Butterhead
-more upright, rounded-leaf lettuce but takes longer to mature (55 to 70 days).

Crisphead
The most available lettuce seed is a cultivar called Iceberg. Iceberg takes 80 to 100 days to mature and is grown in square-foot gardens, raised bed gardens and container gardens; better growth in the fall.

Looseleaf
-a good choice for gardens in Alabama. They have a maturity date of 40 to 50 days and have good heat tolerance. Included in looseleaf lettuce varieties are colored-leaf lettuce.

Romaine
-popular in markets and has become one of the favorite lettuces for salads. This tall, upright-lettuce can grow 8 to 19 inches tall. This lettuce is also referred to as Cos types.

Sweet Corn
Su
- Su varieties are the normal sweet corn types that have the best quality if picked and cooked the same day. It's the standard corn that's been grown for years.

Se
- While keeping the same tenderness and creamy texture of standard varieties, se (sugary enhancer) of the corn raises the content of sugar above the standard su's. These varieties can hold sweetness longer and are better for local or one day shipping.

Sh2
- These varieties of corn have the shrunken-2 gene. This gene block the conversion of sugar to starch and therefore can hold sweetness longer that the se or su varieites. These supersweet hybrids require warmer soil but are ideal for 2 to 3 day shipping.

SB
- The Sweet Breed Varieties are available only from Harris Seeds and Harris Moran. They combine the seed quality of an su, the flavor of an se, and the good shelf life of an sh.
