CHAYOTE SQUASH
Chayote squash (chy-o-tay) Sechium edule is a member of the Cucurbitaceae (gourd) family. AKA: Christophen squash, vegetable pear, sayote, mirliton.



HISTORY
The Chayote, another New World original, was cultivated by the ancient Aztec and Mayan civilizations in central America. It is now an important food crop throughout the tropical world and imports from Mexico have become a staple on the squash aisle in many North American supermarkets. The root, young shoots and flower blossoms of the chayote are also used as a food source in the West Indies and other tropical regions.

Our Chayote is grown on terraced vines
in the most fertile soils in Mexico.



Description and Uses
The Chayote grows on a cucumber-like climbing vine and is a warm season or tropical winter crop. Available year round, with a natural abundance from September to May, the Chayote squash usually matures within 30 days of blooming and can measure 3 to 7 inches and weigh from a few ounces to over a pound.

The mature squash resembles a pale green pear in both color and shape. Its crisp white flesh has a mild flavor - a cross between zucchini and cucumber with a tart hint of apple. The Chayote is a winter squash, but instead of the hard protective outer rind, has a thin, soft, edible rind like that of the summer squash (i.e. zucchini) and a single soft seed measuring from 1 to 2 inches (also edible).

It is similar in nutritional value to summer squash and may be prepared and used in the same variety of ways. A good blender, the mild flavor of the Chayote squash absorbs well and can be served raw, stir fried, deep fried, grilled, stuffed, baked or in soups, stews and salads. When used raw, a slight parboil ensures better absorption of other flavors.

Storage
Chayote in its optimum condition, should have an unblemished, smooth to ridged, firm, pale green skin.
A storage temperature of 50-55'F (10-15'C) with 90% humidity should extend shelf life up to 4 weeks, any temperatures below 45' F ( 7.5'C) will result in chilling injuries. It is important to maintain the moisture level, when refrigerated use a sealed container. 

Nutrition
The Chayote is low in calories (approx.40 calories per cup), cholesterol free, high in vitamin C, low in sodium and a good source of fiber.
