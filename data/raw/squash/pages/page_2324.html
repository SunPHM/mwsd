Cucuzza

In some parts of Italy, the word "cucuzza" is used to refer to squash in general. The flavor is a combination of summer squash and cucumber. Cucuzza is actually an edible gourd. It goes by many other names, including bottle gourd, calabash, zucca, suzza melon, tasmania bean and new guinea bean. This pale green vegetable can grow up to 3 feet long. The white, pulpy flesh has many seeds in the center, similar to a cucumber.

Selection:
Avoid squash that looks shriveled or bruised; should be firm to the touch.

Storage:
Refrigerate up to 5 days. Cucuzza will deteriorate quickly if left out of refrigeration.

Uses & Preparation:

  • Steam or microwave.
  • Stuff and bake.
  • Stir-fry.
  • Stew with tomatoes.
  • Cucuzza needs to be peeled; the skin is tough.
  • If the seeds are tiny and tender, leave them in. If the seeds are well-developed, remove with a spoon or paring knife, like cucumber, before cooking.
  • Substitute in recipes calling for summer squash (however, unlike other summer squash such as zucchini and yellow squash, cucuzza should be peeled).
  • Remains firm (tender but not mushy) even when completely cooked.

Availability:
June through November is peak season.

Nutrition Information:

  • Fat, sodium and cholesterol free.
  • Low in calories.
  • High in vitamin C.
