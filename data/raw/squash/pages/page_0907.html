Fresh Spring Rolls
modified from Cooking Light

Ingredients:
8 (12-inch) round sheets rice paper
2 cups green leaf lettuce leaves, chopped
2 cups fresh alfalfa sprouts
32 (2-inch) julienne-cut cucumber strips
32 (2-inch) julienne-cut yellow bell pepper strips
20 medium shrimp, cooked, peeled, and halved lengthwise
18 fresh mint leaves, torn

Instructions:
To prepare spring rolls, add hot water to a large, shallow dish to a depth of 1 inch. Place 1 rice paper sheet in dish; let stand 30 seconds or just until soft. Place rice paper sheet on a flat surface. Arrange a few pieces of lettuce in center of sheet. Top with 1/4 cup sprouts, 4 cucumber strips, 4 bell pepper strips, 4 shrimp halves, and 1 Tbsp mint leaves. Fold sides of sheet over filling; roll up jelly-roll fashion. Gently press seam to seal. Place spring roll, seam side down, on a serving platter (cover with damp paper towel to prevent drying).

Repeat procedure with remaining rice paper, lettuce, sprouts, cucumber, bell pepper, shrimp, and mint. Serve with dipping sauce.

8 appetizer servings

My review:
The rice paper was surprisingly easy to work with but it took me a few times to get the amount of filling and rolling technique correct. Makes a lovely presentation when cut in helf at an angle and seved on a long tray. (photo coming soon). Very good with a basic peanut dipping sauce. The mint makes the roll taste very fresh and clean, will certainly make again in the summer. I may try seasoning the shrimp next time as it seemed to get lost with the other flavors.

Tilapia with Thai Coconut-Curry Sauce
modified from Cooking Light

Ingredients:
1 teaspoon dark sesame oil, divided
2 teaspoons minced peeled fresh ginger
2 garlic cloves, minced
1 cup finely chopped red bell pepper
1/2 cup chopped green onions
1 teaspoon curry powder
1 1/2 teaspoons red curry paste
1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
4 teaspoons low-sodium soy sauce
1 tablespoon brown sugar
1/2 teaspoon salt, divided
10 ounces light coconut milk
2 (6-ounce) tilapia fillets
Cooking spray
2 cups hot cooked basmati rice

Instructions:
Preheat grill.

Heat 1/2 teaspoon oil in a large nonstick skillet over medium heat. Add ginger and garlic; cook 1 minute. Add pepper and onions; cook 1 minute. Stir in curry powder, curry paste, and cumin; cook 1 minute. Add soy sauce, sugar, 1/4 teaspoon salt, and coconut milk; bring to a simmer until slightly thickened (do not boil) and remove from heat.

Brush fish with 1/2 teaspoon oil; sprinkle with 1/4 teaspoon salt. Grill for 5-7 minutes over medium-high heat or until fish flakes easily when tested with a fork. Spoon sauce liberally over fish and serve with rice.

2 servings

My review:
The original recipe called for broiling the fish but since it's grilling weather, that's what I did! I usually make a tilapia filet for me and a chicken breast for Eric. The sauce works well with both meats. This recipe makes plenty of sauce for both the fish and the rice, so don't worry about doubling it (which is what I usually have to do). Tasty and spicy!

Shiloh's Creamy Sungold Pasta
adapted from Weaver Street Market newsletter recipe

Ingredients:
1 cup sungold tomatoes cut in half
1/4 cup fresh basil-packed
1 Tbs. butter
2 cloves of garlic, minced or put through a press
dash of salt and pepper
1/3 cup heavy cream
1/4 cup asiago cheese
12 large peeled/deveined shrimp
cooked and drained spaghetti

Instructions:
Heat the pan over medium heat and sauté the garlic in butter, being careful not to burn it. Add salt, pepper, and halved tomatoes and wilt them down a few minutes. Add cream and cook gently, allowing the sauce to thicken, about 3 minutes. Then add peeled shrimp directly to the simmering cream sauce. When the shrimp are pink (another few minutes), chop your basil and throw it in. Toss sauce with cooked pasta noodles and sprinkle with freshly grated cheese. Photo here.

2 servings

My review:
This was a wonderful recipe and another great way to use the sungold tomatoes in our garden, the other way being eating them straight off the vine. I'm sure all that heavy cream isn't that great for me, but I could handle this a few times each summer! The original recipe says you can make it without the shrimp too, but I think they added another wonderful flavor dimension. Delicious!

Ziti with Vodka Sauce
adapted from Cooking Light

Ingredients:
8 oz uncooked ziti pasta
1/2 Tbsp butter
2/3 cup finely chopped onions
1 garlic clove, minced
8 oz tomato sauce (I use traditional Ragu)
3 tsps all purpose flour
1 Tbsp water
3/4 cup half & half
1/4 cup 1% milk
1/3 cup vodka
1/4 tsp salt
1/8 tsp ground red pepper
1/2 cup grated fresh asiago cheese

Instructions:
Cook pasta according to package directions, omitting salt and fat, drain. While pasta cooks melt butter in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Add onion, cover a cook 3 minutes until tender, add garlic, stirring for 1 minute. Add tomato sauce and simmer partially covered about 8 minutes or until thick. Combine flour and water, stirring with a whisk until smooth. Add flour mixture to pan, cook one minute. Add half & half, milk, vodka, salt and pepper, bring to a boil. Simmer for another 5 minutes. Add cheese, stirring frequently for another 3 minutes. Add cooked pasta and toss to coat. Add chopped basil if desired.

4 servings

My review:
Tastiest vodka sauce I've ever made and it's even better the next day! It has a sun-dried tomato flavor to it and it really coats the pasta nicely. Yum.

Chicken Saté Wrap
adapted from Cooking Light

Ingredients:
Cooking spray
1/3 cup chopped green onions
2/3 cup light coconut milk
1 tablespoon low-sodium soy sauce
1 tablespoon rice vinegar
3 tablespoons creamy peanut butter
1 teaspoon curry powder
1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
2 cups shredded skinless, boneless rotisserie chicken breast
4 (8-inch) flour tortillas
1 1/3 cups chopped lettuce

Instructions:
Heat a large nonstick skillet over medium-high heat. Coat pan with cooking spray. Add onions and sauté 1 minute. Stir in coconut milk and next 5 ingredients (through pepper flakes); cook 30 seconds, stirring constantly. Add the chicken; cook 1 minute, stirring to coat. Remove from heat. Warm tortillas according to package directions. Spoon about 1/2 cup chicken mixture down center of each tortilla, and top each with 1/3 cup angel hair slaw. Roll up.

4 servings (serving size: 1 wrap)

My review:
This was tasty. It had a nice spicy peanut flavor and would probably make a good lunch cold too.

Pumpernickel Rye Bread

Ingredients:
1 1/2 cups unbleached all-purpose flour
1 cup rye flour
1/2 cup whole wheat flour
1/4 cup bread flour
3 Tbsp dry milk powder
2 Tbsp unsweetened cocoa powder
1/2 Tbsp caraway seeds
2 tsp instant coffee
1 1/2 tsp active dry yeast
1 tsp salt
1 1/2 Tbsp molasses
1 Tbsp vegetable oil
1 cup (approximately) warm water (110 degrees)

Instructions:
Place ingredients into large mixing bowl, using the dough hook, begin to mix. Add enough warm water to make a dry dough. After the ball is soft and elastic, let rise in a warm draft-free place for one hour. Punch dough down and transfer to lightly oiled 9x5 loaf pan. Cover with towel and let rise for another hour.

Bake at 350 degrees for 45 minutes. Let cool completely before slicing.

My review:
Yum. It's not as dark as I normally like but with my complete lack of options this will certainly do. The caraway seeds give the bread a nice rye flavor while the molasses makes it moist. Went very well with the swiss cheese fondue.

Orzo Bell Pepper Salad
adapted from Cooking Light

Dressing:
2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
2 1/2 teaspoons extravirgin olive oil
2 teaspoons red wine vinegar
1/2 teaspoon salt
1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
2 garlic cloves, minced

Salad:
1 cup uncooked orzo (rice-shaped pasta)
1/3 cup finely chopped red bell pepper
1/3 cup finely chopped green bell pepper
1/3 cup finely chopped yellow bell pepper
1 cup finely chopped tomato
1/2 cup (2 ounces) diced fresh marinated mozzarella cheese
1/4 cup minced fresh basil
1/4 cup finely chopped red onion

Instructions:
To prepare dressing, combine first 6 ingredients, stirring with a whisk.

To prepare salad, cook pasta in boiling water 6 minutes or until al dente. Add bell peppers to pasta in pan; cook 10 seconds. Drain. Combine pasta mixture and half of dressing in a large bowl; cool mixture to room temperature. Add remaining dressing, tomato, and remaining ingredients; toss gently to coat. Cover and chill at least 1 hour.

4 servings

Pistachio Brittle
adapted from Cooking Light

Ingredients:
1 1/4 cups unsalted pistachios
1/4 cup sesame seeds
1/2 tsp ground allspice
dash of cayenne pepper
2 cups sugar
3/4 cup light-colored corn syrup
1/2 cup water
dash of kosher salt
2 tsps unsalted butter
1 1/2 tsp baking soda
cooking spray

Instructions:
Combine first 4 ingredients, tossing to coat.

Combine sugar, corn syrup, water and salt in a heavy saucepan. Cook over medium heat, stirring until sugar dissolves. Cook 25 minutes or until golden (my batch took about 35 minutes) and a candy thermometer registers 340 degrees, stirring occasionally. Stir in pistachio mixture and butter stirring rapidly. Remove from heat and stir in baking soda (mixture will start to foam and expand). Rapidly spread mixture onto a prepared jellyroll pan coated with cooking spray. Cool completely and break into small pieces.

24 servings

My review:
I thought the candy cooked a tad too long, but everyone who's tried it really likes it, so I guess the slight burnt flavor is a plus. I love the buttery flavor of the pistachios with the sweet candy. I would make this again, but add more nuts! ****

Bulgogi (Korean Beef Barbeque)
from Cooking Light

Ingredients:
1 pound top sirloin steak, trimmed
1 tablespoon brown sugar
3 tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce
1 tablespoon mirin (sweet rice wine)
1 teaspoon minced peeled fresh ginger
1 teaspoon dark sesame oil
3 garlic cloves, minced
Cooking spray

Instructions:
Wrap beef in plastic wrap; freeze 1 hour or until firm. Remove plastic wrap; cut beef diagonally across grain into 1/16-inch-thick slices. Combine beef, sugar, and next 5 ingredients (sugar through garlic) in a large zip-top plastic bag. Seal and marinate in refrigerator 1 hour, turning bag occasionally.

Prepare grill and place a wire grilling basket on grill rack. Remove beef from bag; discard marinade. Place beef on grilling basket coated with cooking spray; grill 5 minutes or until desired degree of doneness, turning frequently.

4 servings (serving size: 3 ounces)

My review:
This really couldn't be easier and although I made it on the stovetop with a fry pan, it tasted great. The meat was tender and full of salty Chinese food-like flavor. I cooked mine for less time than Eric's since he likes his meat well-done while I'm a med-rare girl. I sampled both plates and the meat didn't lose any flavor when it was cooked a minute or two longer. I served this with white rice and aparagus. Yum! *****

Gorgonzola Topped Pork Loin

Ingredients:
2 butterflied pork loins, 1/2 inch thick with fat trimmed
salt and pepper to taste
2 tsps olive oil
1/2 cup beef broth
1/4 cup dry red wine
2 Tbsp gorgonzola (or other kind of crumbly bleu cheese)

Instructions:
Heat oil in skillet over medium high heat. Salt and pepper both sides of pork loin and sauté for 3-4 minutes on each side or until cooked through, remove from pan and keep warm. Add broth and red wine to the pan and scrape to loosen any brown bits. Heat on high until reduced to 1/3 cup. Poor sauce over pork loin and top with crumbled gorgonzola cheese.

2-4 servings (depending on how much you like to eat!)

My review:
This was seriously tasty! I love bleu cheese, but even Eric liked it. The beef broth and red wine sauce gives the meat a steak-like flavor and the gorgonzola matches very well with the texture and flavor of the pork. I will make this again definitely. ****

Tuscan White Beans

Ingredients:
1 thick-slice of pancetta
1/2 cup chopped onion
1/2 tablespoon chopped fresh or 1/2 teaspoon dried rosemary
1 (15-ounce) can cannellini beans or other white beans, rinsed and drained
1/2 cup dry white wine
1/4 cup water
1/2 teaspoon grated lemon rind
1/4 teaspoon black pepper
1/4 cup minced fresh parsley

Instructions:
Cook pancetta in a large nonstick skillet over medium-high heat until crisp. Remove pancetta from pan, reserving 1/2 tablespoon drippings in pan; crumble pancetta. Add onion and rosemary to drippings in pan; saute 2 minutes. Reduce heat to medium-low. Add beans, wine, water, rind, and pepper; simmer 30 minutes or until thick. Stir in crumbled pancetta and parsley.

about 3 servings (serving size: 1/2 cup)

My review:
The original recipe called for bacon, but I used pancetta instead. I also used great northern beans, fresh rosemary and a sweet, but dry, white wine. It smelled wonderful while it was simmering and I tried a bit last night that tasted fine, but I'm having this for lunch today, so I'll update after that.

Update: I'll have to try this recipe again because the wine I used was apparently too sweet and the beans tasted funky.

Sherried Mushroom Soup

Ingredients:
Broth:
2 tablespoons butter
1 tablespoon chopped fresh thyme
1 pound shallots, coarsely chopped
6 (14 1/2-ounce) cans fat-free, less-sodium chicken broth
2 ounces dried porcini mushrooms

Remaining ingredients:
2 cups thinly sliced shiitake mushroom caps (about 4 ounces mushrooms)
3/4 cup dry sherry
3 tablespoons chopped fresh chives

Instructions:
To prepare broth, melt butter in a Dutch oven over medium heat. Add thyme and shallots; cook 10 minutes or until shallots are golden brown. Stir in chicken broth and porcini mushrooms; bring to a boil. Reduce heat; simmer, uncovered, 1 hour. Strain broth mixture through a sieve into a bowl; discard solids.

Return broth mixture to pan. Stir in shiitake mushrooms and sherry; cook 10 minutes over low heat. Stir in chives. Serve immediately.

12 servings (serving size: about 1/2 cup)

My review:
This soup was full of earthy flavor... if you enjoy mushrooms, definitely try this soup. There is a lot of waiting time, but you can let the stock simmer for the hour without a lot of stirring. I used Better Than Bouillon instead of the fat-free chicken broth which probably made it more salty than the original recipe. I would serve this as a meal starter for company because it's low maintenance while I prepare other things. A wonderful aromatic cold-weather soup.****

Stromboli

Ingredients:
Pizza Crust:
3 1/2 cups unbleached, all-purpose flour
2 packages dry active yeast
1 teaspoon salt
1/2 tsp sugar
1 1/2 cups lukewarm tap water
1/2 tsp olive oil
Flour, for the work surface

1/4 lb pancetta
1/2 cup sweet onion
3/4 cup pizza sauce
3/4 cup park-skim mozzarella cheese

Instructions:
In a mixing bowl fitted with a dough hook, place flour, yeast, salt and sugar. While mixer is running, gradually add water and knead on low speed until dough is firm and smooth, about 10 minutes.

Turn machine off. Pour oil down inside of bowl. Turn on low once more for 15 seconds to coat inside of bowl and all surfaces of dough with the oil. Cover bowl with plastic wrap. Let dough rise in warm spot until doubled in bulk, about 2 hours.

Sauté pancetta and onions in a little olive oil until onions are soft and pancetta is crispy. Let cool slightly.

Preheat oven to 500 degrees F. Punch dough down, cut in half. Place half of the dough on generously floured work surface. By hand, form dough loosely into a ball and stretch into a circle. Using a floured rolling pin, roll dough into large rectangle until very thin. To prevent dough from sticking to counter, turn over the dough and sprinkle with flour. Transfer dough to pizza peel or cookie sheet with no lip. Add sauce and toppings. Carefully roll dough up with toppings inside and seal edges by pressing together the dough. Rolling the dough will be easier if the sauce is cool.

Bake 15-20 minutes or until golden. Roll out remaining dough and top with desired toppings or freeze in freezer bags.

Makes 4 total servings.

My review:
This is by far the easiest pizza crust recipe I've found and it's delicious! You can add any ingredients you want for the inside, but I've found the mix of salty pancetta and sweet onions is very yummy. I also knead some dried oregano and basil into the dough before rolling it out. The dough recipe freezes very well, so we usually keep a half recipe in the freezer for pizza emergencies! *****

Butternut Squash Soup with Fried Sage Leaves

Ingredients:
1 1/2 Tbsp butter
1/2 onion, coarsely chopped
1/2 Tbsp chopped fresh sage
2 cups butternut squash; halved, peeled, seeded and chopped
1 15 oz. can fat-free chicken broth
2-3 Tbsp parmesan or asiago cheese

12 fresh sage leaves

Instructions:
Melt 1/2 Tbsp butter in heavy large pot over medium heat. Add onion; cover and cook until soft, stirring occasionally, about 7 minutes. Add chopped sage; stir one minute. Add squash and broth. Increase heat to high and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer until squash is tender, about 25 minutes. Cool slightly. Puree soup in batches in blender until smooth. Return soup to pot and mix in cheese. Season to taste with salt and pepper. (Can be made 1 day ahead. Cool slightly, then cover and refrigerate.)

Melt remaining 2 Tbsp butter in heavy large skillet over medium heat. Add sage leaves and sauté until brown and toasted, about 2 minutes. Bring soup to simmer. Ladle into bowls. Garnish with fried sage leaves.

Makes 4 servings.

My review:
I know it's almost June, but I picked up a butternut squash during our trip to the farmer's market last weekend and I needed to use it. The soup was rich and creamy and full of fantastic flavor! The fried sage leaves were crispy and a good contrast to the thick soup. I had never cooked with squash before and this first experience was definitely a positive one. I will make this again in the fall.

UPDATE: A co-worker made this soup for a party and it was a huge hit!

Chicken Cordon Bleu

Ingredients:
2 large chicken breasts, skinless and boneless
Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
4 thin slices deli ham
8 thin slices Gruyere or Swiss cheese
1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
2 Tablespoons cup flour
1/2 cup panko bread crumbs
1/2 teaspoon olive oil
1 egg
1 teaspoon water

Instructions:
Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. Lay the chicken between 2 pieces of plastic wrap. Using the flat side of a meat mallet, gently pound the chicken to 1/4-inch thickness. Take care not to pound too hard because the meat may tear or create holes. Lay 2 slices of cheese on each breast, followed by 2 slices of ham, and 2 more of cheese; leaving a 1/2-inch margin on all sides to help seal the roll. Tuck in the sides of the breast and roll up tight like a jellyroll. Squeeze the log gently to seal.

Season the flour with salt and pepper; spread out on waxed paper or in a flat dish. Mix the breadcrumbs with thyme, kosher salt, pepper, and oil. The oil will help the crust brown. Beat together the eggs and water, the mixture should be fluid. Lightly dust the chicken with flour, then dip in the egg mixture. Gently coat in the bread crumbs. Carefully transfer the roulades to a baking pan and bake for 20 minutes until browned and cooked through. Cut into pinwheels before serving.

Makes 4 servings.

My review:
The problem with these types of recipes is the issue of cheese leaking everywhere before the dish is finished cooking. I must not have pounded the chicken thin enough because there wasn't enough meat for me to wrap around the insides to prevent this common problem. This in combination with having to use american cheese left me with a messy main dish. The overall taste was fine; I used a Boars Head peppered ham which added excellent flavor to the inside, but the outside of the chicken was fairly bland. I would make this again only I would use a harder cheese (like the recipe calls for) and would marinate the chicken for a day in Italian seasonings first. **1/2

Easy Lemon Squares
from Cooking Light

Ingredients:
Crust:
1/4 cup granulated sugar
3 tablespoons butter or stick margarine, softened
1 cup all-purpose flour

Topping:
3 large eggs
3/4 cup granulated sugar
2 teaspoons grated lemon rind
1/3 cup fresh lemon juice
3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
1/2 teaspoon baking powder
1/8 teaspoon salt
2 teaspoons powdered sugar

Instructions:
Preheat oven to 350F. To prepare the crust, beat 1/4 cup granulated sugar and the butter at medium speed of a mixer until creamy. Lightly spoon 1 cup flour into a dry measuring cup; level with a knife. Gradually add 1 cup flour to sugar mixture, beating at low speed until mixture resembles fine crumbs. Gently press mixture into bottom of an 8-inch square baking pan. Bake for 15 minutes; cool on a wire rack.

To prepare topping, beat eggs at medium speed until foamy. Add next 6 ingredients (granulated sugar through salt), and beat until well-blended. Pour mixture over partially baked crust. Bake at 350F; for 18 minutes or until set. Cool on wire rack. Sift powdered sugar evenly over top.

My review:
This treat has a sweet light lemon flavor and excellent texture. Eric didn't like it because the lemon's tart flavor was masked by the sugar. But I think this dessert is a great light alternative for summer company or lunch. It was easy to make and came right out of the ungreased glass dish I used. The only difference from the recipe was that lemon filling set in about 17-18 minutes instead of 20-25, so I changed the above recipe to reflect that. I will make this again. ****

Mini Apple Pies
adapted from Pillsbury's Perfect Apple Pie

Ingredients:
1 (15-oz.) pkg. refrigerated pie crusts, softened for 10 minutes
1/4 cup sugar
1/2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
1 teaspoon vanilla
dash of salt
dash of nutmeg
1/2 tablespoon lemon juice
2 medium thinly sliced, peeled apples

Instructions:
1. Cut out 4 circles from the two pie crusts. Place one pie circle in each ungreased 3.5" ramekin, gently pressing into the bottom and smoothing out the sides.
2. Preheat oven to 400 degrees. In large bowl, combine all filling ingredients and toss gently to mix. Spoon generously into crust-lined ramekin. Top with second crust, and seal edges by pressing against lip and discarding extra dough. Cut slits or shapes in several places in top crust.
3. Bake for 20 to 25 minutes or until apples are tender and crust is golden brown.

Optional: Brush egg white on top of crust and sprinkle with sugar before baking.

Makes 2 mini pies.

My review:
As you can see above the crust browned nicely in 25 minutes, but the bottom crust was a bit undercooked and the apples weren't as tender as I wanted. We think microwaving the apple mixture for a minute or two before filling the ramekins would make the apples softer right off the bat. As for the bottom crust, perhaps baking it for a few minutes first to brown it would make for a better pie bottom, as the apples let go of a lot of liquid during baking and could make the bottom crust soggy. Overall, the flavor was great and eating your own little mini pie with Cool Whip is a special treat. ***1/2

The King's Creole Fried Pork Chops

Ingredients:
2 pork loin chops 2-inches thick
Creole seasoning, recipe follows
1 cup seasoned flour
1/2 cup vegetable oil

Instructions:
Preheat oven to 400 degrees. To a large hot ovenproof sauté pan, add the oil and heat. Season the pork chops with Creole seasoning. Dredge the pork chops in the seasoned flour, shaking off any excess. Place the pork chops in the oil and pan fry until golden brown on both sides, about 4 minutes per side. Remove the pan from the heat and place in the preheated oven. Cook for 10 to 15 minutes or until the meat reaches an internal temperature of 155 degrees. Remove from the oven and let rest for 5 minutes.

Additional:
Emeril's ESSENCE Creole Seasoning (also referred to as Bayou Blast):
2 1/2 tablespoons paprika
2 tablespoons salt
2 tablespoons garlic powder
1 tablespoon black pepper
1 tablespoon onion powder
1 tablespoon cayenne pepper
1 tablespoon dried oregano
1 tablespoon dried thyme

Combine all ingredients thoroughly. Yield: 2/3 cup

My review:
This was a seriously yummy pork recipe! The flavor was excellent (not too garlicky or spicy) and the pork loin was juicy and completely cooked in the listed time frame (it helps if you have a meat thermometer). I covered the pork with the seasoning about 24 hours before cooking. The seasoning formed a flavorful crust in the frying pan and the pork stayed moist while finishing in the oven. I served it with a baked potato sprinkled with cheddar and jack cheeses. This is going in the must make again recipe file. *****
