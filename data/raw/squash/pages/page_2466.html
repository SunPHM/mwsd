SOUP OF THE MONTH

Even if you don't have a garden that produces pounds and pounds of zucchini each summer, if you're like me, you still buy too many at one time from the greenmarket or produce section!  I always have more green and yellow zucchini in my vegetable bin than I can successfully use in one week!  What to do with all of the zucchini wherever it comes from?  Here's a delicious cold Summer soup that uses 2 pounds of green or yellow squash or a combination of both.  The soup has lots of other flavors incorporated into it such as carrot, celery, onion and chopped fresh dill.  The recipe serves 8 - of course depending on the size of your soup bowls!

Chilled Dill-Squash Soup

2 tablespoons vegetable oil
½ cup chopped onion
1/3 cup chopped celery
1/3 cup chopped peeled carrot
2 pounds yellow or green summer squash, cut into ½-inch cubes
¼ cup peeled, diced potato
2 cups water
1¾ cups strong chicken stock*
1¾ cups strong beef stock*
2 tablespoons chopped fresh dill
½ cup heavy cream
½ teaspoon salt
¼ teaspoon freshly ground pepper
  Sprigs of  fresh dill,  for garnish

1.  Heat oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add onion, celery and carrot; cook until tender about 4 minutes. Add squash; cover and cook 5 minutes. Stir in potato, water and stock Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until potato is tender, 30 minutes.

2. Purée soup in a blender in small batches and transfer each batch to a large bowl. Add dill, cream, salt, and pepper. Let cool.  Cover and refrigerate until chilled, at least 2 hours. Spoon soup into chilled cups and garnish each serving with a sprig of fresh dill.

Yield: 8 servings

Source:  Adapted from Ladies' Home Journal Books

*Substitute a strong vegetable stock for a vegetarian soup
