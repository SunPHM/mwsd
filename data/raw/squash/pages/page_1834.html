Monday, June 30, 2003
So, here's the pictures of the front yard garden at the end of June. These pictures make it look a lot more impressive than it is!


LEFT: These pictures are more blurry than I'd like. This is the female blossom. You can see the difference in the petals, but I was trying to show the ovum. By the way, two days later and the gourd is alread an inch and a half big! I make a good pollinator fairy.
RIGHT: Here's the male flower, see how the petals are more frilly? I'll try again for better pictures.



LEFT: The banana plant is a fosterling. I got it from my friend Victoria, because it was outgrowing the place she put it. We put it in a cheapo plastic bucket until we decide where to put it, and it's so happy we left it there. We may pass it on to someone, though, and get a banana that actually bears fruit.
RIGHT and BOTTOM: This is the clary sage plant, with blossoms. I plan on drying the leaves, maybe the blossoms too, and making soap. (ok, this is the plan, if life doesn't get in the way!)


LEFT: This is a sillouette of the too-mature seed head of the broom corn.
RIGHT: This is a better picture, with the sun behind me like it should be. You can see the lesser-ripe seed head that I broke over, so it could dry and make a good broom. If you leave it too long, the fibers all splay out and are only good for decorations.



LEFT and RIGHT: The Hopi cotton plants.
BOTTOM: The Sacaton cotton original. The picture of the brown cotton is below, because it's away from these, hopefully to help keep them from cross-pollinating. I can see brown paper bags in my future!



TOP LEFT: This is a long shot of the front planter. I'm about halfway from the front door and facing the street. The tree on the left is a Fuji apple planted by previous owners, and the tree on the right is a pomegranate. Mike was a bit worried that the planter behind them would be too shaded, but everything seems to be thriving.
TOP RIGHT: Closer up, starting with the spaghetti squash in the corner.
BOTTOM LEFT: The zuccini plants, with another spaghetti squash and a couple of Anaheim chili plants getting swallowed. I never can quite tell how much room to leave!
BOTOM RIGHT: The previous owner had also left us this lemon tree, and it's just now starting to come back. (actually, the gardener was two previous owners ago, and neglected by the family just before us.) Mom, if you're reading this, are you singing with me? "Lemon tree, oh so pretty.." She used to sing us this song as she drove us to town from the farm.



TOP LEFT: This is looking back toward the house. These philodendrons and the sago palms were also left by the previous owner.
TOP RIGHT: This is a pot of variegated timber bamboo. I can't seem to coax it into more growth!It's rather frustrating- this is supposed to get 30' high, but it's a running bamboo that's rather invasive. That's why it's in a pot.
BOTTOM LEFT: This is another pot, with black bamboo. See the black stalks? They're easier to see at the bottom. The puppy? That's our Rex. Who says dawgs can't smile!

- R, 10:00 PM
