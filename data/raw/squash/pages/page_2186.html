The Story of Maize

As late as 1832, Indians planted corn in the area of Big Grove (now Urbana) near the site of the county courthouse.

Dried corn was ground in a metate. Hickory and walnuts were also ground beneath the mano.

Hickory nuts    black walnut

Women scraped corn from the ears with mussel shells, then boiled it, and spread it out on mats to dry.

In addition to corn, Indian women grew squash, beans, bottle gourds, pumpkins and watermelons.

Indian men hunted and fished. Bison, deer, bear, and turkey as well as 25 different types of fish provided protein to the Indian diet.

Foraging yielded plums, apples, grapes, persimmons, mulberries and blackberries.
