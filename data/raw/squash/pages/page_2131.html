Winter Bounty
Acorn Squash Stuffed With
Cranberries and Walnuts

A bounty of ingredients, including wine-red dried cranberries, fills mellow squash for an elegant dish. Serves six.

Ingredients
3 small acorn squash (about 4 inches in diameter)

Stuffing
1 cup cooked brown rice (about 1/2 cup uncooked)
1 cup unseasoned toasted croutons
1/2 cup finely chopped onions (1 medium)
1/2 cup chicken broth (commercial low-sodium variety)
1/4 cup dried cranberries
2 tablespoons chopped walnuts, dry-roasted
2 tablespoons light margarine
1 teaspoon dried sage
1/2 teaspoon dried thyme, crumbled
1/4 teaspoon dried oregano, crumbled
1/4 teaspoon salt
1/4 teaspoon pepper
1/4 cup water

  • Preheat oven to 400° F.
  • Cut each squash in half and remove seeds.
  • In a large bowl, combine stuffing ingredients.
  • Fill squash halves loosely with mixture.
  • Pour water into a 13-by-9-by-2-inch casserole dish.
  • Place squash halves in dish. Cover with aluminum foil.
  • Bake for 1 to 1¼ hours, or until squash is tender when pierced with tip of knife.
