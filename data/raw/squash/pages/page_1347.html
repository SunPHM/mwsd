Our Garden Journal

June 2001

June 1, 2001

Harvested a couple of Ronde de Nice squash from the greenhouse today, and had backed squash stuffed with spinach (also from the garden, of course -- we're overrun with spinach), olive oil, and parmesan cheese. Yum! Between spinach and salad greens, we're definitely reaching the point in the year where all the hard work is starting to pay off.

Ronde de Nice, by the way, are a round zucchini -- called Eight Ball Squash by some other seed companies. They're quite flavorful, and a nice change from the larger zucchini.

June 2, 2001

Time for another round of "state of the garden" photos.

The strawberry patch is doing well, and the plants have little green strawberries all over them. My mouth waters just to think about harvesting these.

The potato patch is coming along nicely. I'm backfilling the trenches with straw as the potatoes grow. There are a variety of squash and pumpkin plants just starting to put on their first permanent leaves on the hills between trenches.

Grapevine putting on some leaves.

Life in the greenhouse. Flats of peppers, eggplants, and okra are waiting to transplant out. Swisschard, parsley, broccoli, lettuce, zucchini, and garlic are visible in the beds. The green bucket holds our Meyer lemon tree. Other containers include spearmint, sage, and basil.

Down in the main garden, another trench full of potato plants. There are tomatoes in the tires to the right, and some more in tomato cages along the fence to the left where you can't really see them due to the shadows.

Row crops coming along well: carrots, swisschard, spinach, lettuce, beets, parsley. More tomatoes in tires to the rear left. peas along the fence to the right.

The garlic patch is still happy.

A few sunflowers in the left foreground, onions, leeks, spinach, radishes, more garlic.

More tomatoes in the tires. Fuzzy stuff to the left is asparagus being left to grow now. The marked-out beds hold a variety of beans and row crops. Compost pile along the back fence.

The green in the foreground is shallots overgrown with weeds. The big dirt area behind that was just planted with corn. The asparagus beds wrap around the corn patch. More compost along the back fence, with sweet potatoes on the hills to the left.

An Early Cherry Tomato living up to its name by flowering already.

Dill's Atlantic Giant squash, just coming up. This will be much larger by the end of the season.

June 3, 2001

Transplanted a few bell pepper seedlings to the garden, in between bouts of weather.

June 4, 2001

Finished planting out the rest of the bell pepper seedlings in the drizzle. And despite the miserable weather, the Selma-Zebra beans are sprouting. Also the Country Gentleman corn and the Black Beauty Zucchini are coming up. I was worried the cold and drizzle would keep the corn from germinating, but it looks fine.

June 7, 2001

Transplanted the eggplant seedlings out to the main garden.

June 8, 2001

Transplanted the okra seedlings. Some went out to the main garden, some into the greenhouse. Turns out they were all getting pretty badly root-bound. Waiting for a second true leaf is apparently too long a wait for okra seedlings in two-inch pots.

June 13, 2001

There are still a bunch of things I want to plant, but it's been so cold and damp that there's not much point to putting seeds in the ground. This may be June, but it feels like April.
