    Squash Marathon for the Deafness Support Network

    Simon Jones, a design technician at the Leicester office of Pick Everard, played in a 24-hour squash marathon to raise money for his charity trip to Tanzania.

    Friends and colleagues joined in to play relay matches over the weekend at Leicester Squash Club.

    The chance of a lifetime trip to Mount Kilimanjaro in mid-February will raise up to £30 000 for the Deafness Support Network, a charity providing support for deaf children and their families. The climbers progress will be followed by a television crew from the Sunday morning programme 'See Hear'.

    Leicester Squash Club, which is keen to encourage deaf people to take up the sport, has been very supportive of the event and made available the courts and facilities for Simon and his team.
