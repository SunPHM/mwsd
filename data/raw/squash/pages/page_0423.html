Things are looking up for Northern junior squash


Some of the young members of Crosby's junior squash section. Code NA

CROSBY’S Northern junior squash section is rapidly becoming one of the premier outfits in the Merseyside area, and currently boasts more than 70 members up to the age of 18.

The group’s success is owed principally to the leadership and drive of its professional coach, Alan Hendry. Within the club he can draw upon the experience of four Level Two and four Level One England squash coaches.

All standards are catered for, from beginners to the more accomplished players, and each individual can be educated from the rudiments of the game to match play standards.

The success of the junior squash section can be measured against the standards and levels achieved by individuals themselves and its teams collectively. Within the club, Northern can boast five boys and four girls representing Merseyside county at under 13 and under 15 level.

To underline the ability of these youngsters, three of its girls and five boys have been fed into and have been selected to play for the senior teams - this all bodes well for the future of the club.

The youngsters are very proud to represent their county and when doing so, bring great credit to Northern Sports Club.

Last season, Northern’s junior first team competed in and won the second division of the Merseyside Under 19 league. While achieving this success, not one of the boys were over the age of 14. Having thereafter been promoted to the first division, they consolidated and finished in a creditable mid-table position.

It all now looks very promising for the forthcoming season as the club’s teams mature. It is expanding on the success of the children and this season will be entering an additional junior side in the Merseyside leagues. This will bring greater depth of experience in match play situations, therefore giving more players an opportunity to represent the club and have a goal to aim for.

The Sunday junior coaching this season has just recommenced, they take place at 1.45pm to 2.30pm for beginners, 2.30pm to 3.30pm for intermediate level, while the more advanced players train between 3.30pm and 4.30pm.

Northern would also like to thank members who have provided sponsorship for the junior section, they say this support has helped enormously and is reflected in the standards which have been achieved.

Northern is situated just off Moor Lane, find out more by logging onto www.northernclub.co.uk, or call 0151 924 1594 evenings and weekends.
