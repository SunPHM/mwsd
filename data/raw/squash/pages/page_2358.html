Aptos CFM, September 13, 2003
By Kimberly Wright
09/15/2003

Saturdays, 8 AM to Noon, Year-Round
Cabrillo College on top of the parking structure; follow the signs

Don't forget the second annual apple cooking contest next week, Saturday September 20. This is the last Saturday obefore the autumnal equinox on the 23rd, and what better way to celebrate the change of seasons than by baking something made with the best-loved autumn fruit! Judging will begin promptly at 10 AM, so entries need to be dropped off between 9 and 10 AM. We'll have a table set up in the top corner of the Market, where you would usually find the artichokes. After the prizes are announced, at about 10:30, samples of the entries will be served to all comers.

The ripe orange bell peppers at Route One Farms positively glowed in the sun last week; so beautiful, and so tasty and healthy, and organically grown, too. I'm a firm believer in using combinations of various colors in dishes I prepare, and these peppers are a fabulous flavor and color addition to either raw or cooked foods, used with corn and/or summer squash and some fresh green beans and herbs, tomatoes or red bells, perhaps some purple eggplant; this is the peak of late-summer eating!

Sweet potatoes (sometimes mis-identified as yams) require a HOT growing area, and Fred and Neeta Witt have just that. This seems to be the time of year for orange vegetables, warm color as the days shorten and we wait for the leaves to turn. Sweet potatoes really are SWEET, and I think that they combine best with more savory companions (skip the marshmallows, please). Onions, garlic, chilies, curry spices, herbs such as sage and rosemary, these contrast and provide interest and complexity.

Beans, beans, beans--shelly beans are in the Market this time of year, too. Prevedelli Farms had fresh cranberry beans, beautiful in their pink-streaked pods. Sometimes Pasqual has these beans as well. They have a fairly short season, and come and go before you know it. Edible soybeans or edamame are also all over the Market. I saw them at Coke Farms, Swank Farms, and Route One this week.

Also welcoming the change of seasons, winter squashes, pumpkins and ornamental gourds are showing up throughout the Market. Swank Farms also had the first ornamental corn. Dick and Bonnie are busy getting ready to open the Corn Maze at the end of this month. They put a lot of work into it, and it is very successful.

Always remember, Buy Fresh, Buy Local!
