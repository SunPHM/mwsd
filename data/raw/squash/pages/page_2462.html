Recipes

Thanks to these simple recipes, you can do much of the food preparation ahead, enabling you to relax and mingle freely with your guests on the day of the party.

Early in the evening, offer an appetizer course, maybe the Layered Cheese and Pesto as you fire up the grill. The main attraction will be succulent Fish Steaks with Fruit Salsa, served with steamed summer squash, French bread and Spinach—Tortellini Salad. For the 'grand finale', treat guests to Custard Fruit Tart bursting with the fresh flavors of summer.

 

Layered Cheese and Pesto

Spread a savory herb and nut filling between layers of mild cheese to prepare this attractive appetizer. You can make this up to three days before your party.

  • Pesto Filling (see recipe below)
  • 1 8-ounce package cream cheese
  • 1 4½ -ounce round Camembert or
  • Brie cheese, rind removed
  • ½ cup whipping cream
  • Assorted crackers or French bread

Prepare Pesto Filling. Set aside.

Bring cream cheese and Camembert cheese to room temperature. Beat cheeses together with an electric mixer until nearly smooth. Beat whipping cream until soft peaks form. Fold whipped cream into cheese mixture.

Line a 3½- to 4-cup mold with plastic wrap. Spread one-fourth of the cheese mixture into prepared mold. Spread one-thlrd of the pesto filling over the cheese mixture. Repeat cheese and pesto layers twice more. Spread remaining cheese mixture on top. Cover; chill several hours or overnight.

Before serving, invert mold onto a serving plate. Remove mold and carefully peel off plastic wrap. Serve with crackers.

Makes 3½ cups

Pesto Filling: In a blender container or food processor bowl combine 1 cup firmly packed snipped fresh basil, ¾ cup grated Parmesan or Romano cheese. ½ cup firmly packed snipped parsley, ¼ cup pine outs or walnuts and 2 cloves garlic, quartered. Cover and blend or process until a paste forms. With machine running slowly, gradually add 1/3 cup olive or cooking oil and blend or process to the consistency of butter.

 

Fish Steaks with Fruit Salsa

Adorn each dinner plate with a fresh sprig of rosemary a leaf of purple kale and steamed baby summer squash.

  • 8 fresh or frozen halibut, swordfish, shark or salmon steaks, cut 1 inch thick (about 2 pounds)
  • 1 small ripe papaya, peeled, seeded and chopped 2 small ripe nectarines, pitted and chopped
  • 2 fresh jalapeno chili peppers, seeded and chopped*
  • 2 tablespoons snipped fresh rosemary, basil or thyme
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Olive oil to coat grill

Thaw fish, if frozen. Several hours ahead, for fruit salsa, stir together the papaya, nectarines, chili peppers, rosemary and the 2 tablespoons olive oil. Cover and chill thoroughly.

Lightly brush the cold grill rack with olive oil. Brush both sides of steaks with olive oil. Grill fish on an uncovered grill directly over medium-hot ** coals for 4 minutes. Using a wide spatula, carefully turn fish over. Brush with olive oil. Grill for 4 to 8 minutes more or until fish just flakes with a fork. Serve fish with the fruit salsa.

Serves 8.

* Note: When seeding and chopping fresh chili peppers, protect your hands with plastic gloves because the oils in the pepper can irritate vour skin. Also, avoid direct contact with your eyes. When finished with the chili pepper, wash your hands thoroughly.

** Note: To determine the coal temperature, hold your hand above the coals at the height that the food will be cooked. Then count the seconds. If you need to withdraw your hand after 3 seconds, the coals are medium hot.

 

Spinach-Tortellini Salad

A simple chutney dressing flavours this refreshing salad
  • ½ 7-ounce package frozen or refrigerated cheese-filled egg tortellini or 1/2 cup dried tortellini
  • 4 cups torn fresh spinach
  • ½ cup shredded red cabbage
  • 4 slices bacon, crisp-cooked, drained and crumbled
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons chutney
  • 3 tablespoons red wine vinegar and oil salad dressing

Cook pasta according to package directions. Drain; rinse with cold water. Drain again. In a large salad bowi toss together pasta, spinach, cabbage, bacon and onions. Cover and chill.

For dressing, cut up large pieces of chutney. In a screw-top jar combine chutney and salad dressing. Cover and chill. To serve, shake dressing well. Toss with salad.

Makes 8 servings.

 

Custard Fruit Tart

Top a tender crust with a creamy yogurt filling and colourful fresh fruit salad
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup cold margarine or butter
  • 1 tablespoon shortening
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1 8-ounce package cream cheese, softened
  • ½ of an 8-ounce carton vanilla yogurt (scant ½ cup)
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • 2 tablespoons orange liqueur or orange juice
  • ½ of a small papaya, peeled, seeded and thinly sliced
  • ½ of a kiwi fruit, peeled and thinly sliced
  • ½ cup blueberries
  • ½ cup raspberries
  • ½ cup seedless green grapes
  • ¼ cup sliced strawberries
  • ½ cup orange marmalade
  • 1 tablespoon hot water

For crust, combine flour and ¼ teaspoon salt. Cut in cold margarine and shortening until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Make a well in the center. Beat together egg yolk and 3 tablespoons cold water. Add egg mixture to flour mixture. Using fork, stir until dough forms a ball. Wrap in clear plastic wrap and chill for 20 minutes in the freezer or 1½ hours in the refrigerator or until easy to handle.

On a lightly, floured surface roll dough into a 12- to 13-inch circle. Fit into 10- or 11-inch flan pan with removable bottom. Turn overhanging dough inside and press against pan sides. Bake in a 375 oven for 25 minutes or until golden. Cool.

For filling, beat cream cheese, yogurt, sugar and orange liqueur until smooth. Spread mixture evenly over the cooled crust. Cover and chill at least 1 hour or until set. Before serving, arrange fruit on top of filling. For glaze, combine marmalade and hot water. If desired, strain through a sieve. Spoon over fruit. Chill until serving time.

Makes 8 servings.
