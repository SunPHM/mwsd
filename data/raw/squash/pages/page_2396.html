Summer School on "Linux for HPC"

4th -15th September 2000, University of Manchester

Residential Accomodation


St. Anselm Hall of Residence

The summer school will use the accommodation in St.Anselm Hall of Residence http://www.sta.man.ac.uk/.  It is in an area surrounded by the other university halls, about 1.5 miles south of the university computing building. There is ample free car parking on site, and also there are regular buses from outside the hall to the main campus, for those not wishing to walk.

There will be three levels of accommodation and meals offered to participants. All residential accommodation will include bed, breakfast and evening meals at the hall and lunches on campus for 12 nights from Sunday 3rd to Friday 15th September. There will also be a buffet reception and a conference dinner included in the price. All tea and coffee breaks are also included. Reservations at the Halls must be made via the organisers to avoid a VAT charge.
At the weekend, only Saturday Breakfast, Sunday Breakfast and Sunday Evening Meal will be provided to allow for the social events on these days.

Option 1 (default): £420, In a private study bedrooms (washbasins in the rooms, shared bathrooms).
Option 2 (en-suite): £550.  Accomodation as option 1, but with full en-suite room facilities.
Option 3 (non-residents): £70 to include all lunches and coffee breaks and the conference dinner.
 
 

Additional Nights

Additional nights may be booked by contacting the hall directly on 0161-224 7327, by e-mail to: mypssjah@man.ac.uk  or by writing to: St. Anselm Hall, Kent Road East, Victoria Park, Manchester M14 5BX .

A lesser cost may be available for those unable to stay for the full two weeks. please contact us for the latest information.

Facilities

The hall a full range of facilites, including leafy quadrangles, a library, digital TV lounge, multi-gym, snooker, squirrels, squash courts, pool table, darts, vending machines, etc.
There is a fully stocked bar (Worthington's Bitter @ 90p per pint, Fosters Lager even cheaper), that will be available for two of the nights, and at other times by popular request.
 
 


The bar lounge

Computer Access

All rooms have computer network point, suitable for connecting laptops to (the hall will advise on IP addressing), as well as telephones for incoming calls. The hall also has a small cluster of networked PCs that are available at all time for those with a desperate need to complete the examples and tutorial exercises (!)
 

Hall Location

Travel
The hall is in easy reach on city centre railway stations, the airport and also the motorway network. It is marked as number 28 in the centre of the map. The main campus can be seen in a darker purple at the top of the page, approx one mile away. The city centre is another mile to the north.
Buses and Taxis
Buses travel very regularly up both Anson Road and Wilmslow Road. Buses on Wilmslow Road are incredibly frequent (about one every minute!). Cost approx 50p per journey. Taxis cost in the order of £5.00 to the railway stations and £10.00 to the airport.
Local Facilites
Wilmslow Road also hosts many shops, include a supermarket, and by far and away the UK largest selection of curry houses. More shops are available further north past the university, including a Spar that is open 24 hours (adjacent to Oxford Road railway station). The nearest pub is the Rampant Lion, on the opposite side of Anson Road to the hall.
