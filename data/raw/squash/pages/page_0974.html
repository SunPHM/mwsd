Potato Soup

6 potatoes, peeled and quartered
3 onions, sliced
1/2 c. diced carrots
1/2 c. chopped celery
1/2 c. chopped green pepper
1/2 c. chopped parsley
1/2 c. tomato juice
2 quarts water
2 T salt
1/2 c. Baked Onions (recipe below)
1 T chopped dill
1/2 c. Clarified Butter (recipe below)
1/2 c. all-purpose flour

In a lare kettle combine potatoes, onions, carots, celery, green pepper, parsley, tomato juice, water and salt. Bring to a boil, lower heat and simmer covered, for 40 minutes.

Stir in baked onions and dill. Blend thoroughly.

In a small skillet heat butter. Add flour and stir over medium heat until flour is golden brown. Gradually add 1 cup of the hot soup to the flour mixture, stirring constantly.

Stir the flour mixture into the remaining soup.

Cook, stirring, over low heat until soup bubbles and thickens. Correct seasonings.

Serve piping hot!

Baked Onions

1 c. clarified butter
3 lbs. onions, peeled and sliced

Preheat oven to 350 degrees.

Stir butter into onions in a 9 X13" baking dish.

Bake for 1 1/2 hours, or until golden brown in color. Stir occasionally.

Cool. Refrigerate until needed. (May be stored in refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.)

Tomato & Tarragon Soup

1 1/2 T butter
1 T extra virgin olive oil
1 medium onion, finely chopped
1 stick celery, sliced
1 garlic clove, chopped
4 oz. carrot, sliced
1 1/2 fresh ripe tomatoes, skinned and chopped or two 14 oz. cans chopped tomatoes
2 T tomato purée
1 bay leaf
1-2 T chopped fresh tarragon
1 3/4 light chicken stock
1-2 tsp. sugar, or to taste
1 small strip lemon rind
salt & pepper

Melt butter and oil together in large saucepan and cook the vegetables and garlic for 2 minutes, without coloring. Add the tomatoes, tomato purée, bay leaf, tarragon. chicken stock, sugar and lemon rind. Simmer gently, uncovered, for 20 minutes. Discard the strip of lemon rind and bay leaf, and purée in a blender. Pass through a fine sieve into a clean saucepan. Taste for seasoning and reheat. Serve with a twist of black pepper over each bowl.

Butternut Squash Soup with Chili Coriander Chutney

This soup is truly delicious- creamy and tasty and lovely. Originally, it came from Gourmet magazine back in the eighties, but a friend of the family made it his signature Thanksgiving dish, serving it yearly at my parents' Thanksgiving feast. Though it never will taste as good as when Kevin made it, the recipe is pretty foolproof. Give it a go!

Chutney:

In a blender, blend til fine:
1/4 c. sliced blanched almonds
1/4 c. sweetened flaked coconut
2 jalapeno peppers, chopped
1/3 c. water
salt
Add a bunch of coriander (ciliantro) last.

Soup:

Cook til softened:
2 c. chopped onion
2 T butter
2 T veg. oil

Add and cook 20-30 minutes til tender:
3-3 1/2 lb. butternut squash, peeled, no strings, no seeds, cut in 1/2" pieces
1/2 c. water

Add and simmer uncovered 15 minutes:
6 c. chicken broth
2 4" strips of orange peel (remove before blending)
1 1/2 c. orange juice

Blend in blender to puree. Strain. Serve with coriander garnish and a dollop of chutney and a little swoop of cream.

Fresh Corn Soup

1 T butter
1 T olive oil
1 1/2 cups onion
2 pounds fresh tomatoes, peeled and coarsely chopped
1 1/2 T tomato paste
4 cups chicken stock
1 t chopped fresh thyme or 1/2 t dried thyme
1 t chopped fresh dill or 1/2 t dried dill
1/4 cup packed fresh basil, chopped
2 cups fresh corn kernels
salt and pepper to taste

In a large saucepan, heat butter and olive oil, add onion and sauté until softened. Add tomatoes, tomato paste and chicken stock. Bring to a boil, reduce heat and simmer the mixture for 30 to 40 minutes until vegetables are tender. In a blender or food processor, puree mixture in batches. Return to saucepan, add herbs and corn and cook 5 minutes longer. Add salt and white pepper to taste.

-Susan Lutz
