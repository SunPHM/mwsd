Nicol bounces back for Worlds win
Nicol suffered an early scare in his first-round match
England's Peter Nicol survived an early scare to book his place in the World Squash Championships' second round.

The former world number one lost the first game to compatriot Aidan Grant en route to a 7-11 11-9 11-6 11-6 win.

World champion Thierry Lincou had an easier start to his title defence in Hong Kong, beating fellow Frenchman Gregory Gaultier 11-5 1-9 11-6.

He is bidding to be the first male player to defend the title since Jansher Khan nine years ago.

"To be honest I was not nervous at all. I didn't feel anything," said Lincou. "I was not thinking about the whole event. I just tried to stick to my game plan.

"You have to learn - you learn to protect your head and to stay in your bubble."

The only seed to go out in the first round was New Zealand's Shelley Kitchen - the 13th seed in the women's event. She looked out of sorts in her defeat to Welsh champion Tegwen Malik.

Also in the women's draw, favourite Rachael Grinham of Australia dropped just eight points with an opening win against Scotland number one Pamela Nimmo.
