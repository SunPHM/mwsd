Commercial Horticulture Agent, Gary Brinen, develops and implements educational programs to meet the needs of fruit, vegetable and ornamental producers, that grow crops like landscape plants, watermelons, beans, peppers, squash, pecans and blueberries. These programs address their concerns as well as present the growers with new issues of technology and governmental regulation. Growers are concerns about marketing, new crops, increasing yields, managing pests and safeguarding the environment. Extension Horticulture programs have helped the growers to sell through new markets including the farmers market, roadside stands, pick-your-own and the internet. They have helped growers to plant and successfully grow new crops like oriental persimmons and chestnuts, to adopt Integrated Pest Management practices like scouting and biological controls, and to adapt to new technologies including micor-irrigation and biotechnology.
