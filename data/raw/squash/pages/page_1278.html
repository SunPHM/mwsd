Places to see, things to do

Once you’re ensconced in your comfortable room at the Senator, you can select from a huge variety of wonderful places to visit and activities to engage in.

Although Gisborne is famous for its chardonnay, there are also many other award winning varieties of quality wine produced here. There are  boutique wineries to visit including Montana and Milltons.

Eastwoodhill Arboretum is another must see. A fantasy forest and garden with over 3000 exotic trees from all over the world.

The back country is spectacular…a rural paradise. Just a few minutes out of town and you can see prize Angus black bulls, brown Hereford bulls, thousands of sheep, farm deer, turkeys and horses. You will also come across acres of land where corn, squash and a variety of vegetable and fruit are being grown ready for export. 

Gisborne District and the East Coast boasts some of the best fishing and hunting in New Zealand. Trophy sized trout are often caught in the rivers and lakes nearby. The world famous Lake Waikaremoana is only 2 hours south by car. Whether you go out deep sea fishing or surfcasting along the pristine coastland…you’re bound to catch a "whopper"!  But that's another story?? Game, deer and wild pig are plentiful in the hunting season.

Known as the surf capital of New Zealand, this region has a huge variety of surf breaks, and a reputation for consistent surf all year round.

Swimming, kayaking, sailing…all water sports are popular during the long summer season.
