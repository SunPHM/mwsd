Today, we went to the market in Santiago. Things begin here really early as people come to buy products for restaurants and stores elesewhere, The stalls were arranged more or less by commodity. One street had onions and garlic for example. There was lots on interesting produce. There were stands that facussed on herbs for medicinal puposes and a Botanica that had products to help you win the lottery as well as fix your liver. The market was much smaller and more confined tha the one I had seen in Mexico City.

After the market we stopped at a very clean "fast food" outlet that served empanadas. Pedro seeemed to know every 10th person as we went down the street. We went to the Santiago Office of the Ministry of Agriculture and met with the Vice-Minister who was in charge of the Santiago District. We were treated to fresh juice, grapes and apples and the Minister gave Jake a box of cigars. He is also a candidate for Mayor and is running in the PRD primary. Political posters are everywhere and Mr.Burgos's picture turns up on nearly every corner.After this we went to the office of the Environmental Society.
