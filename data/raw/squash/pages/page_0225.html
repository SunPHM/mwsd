Academy Programmes

Squash Academy

The Heriot-Watt University Squash Academy in association with Scottish Squash was launched on 23 January 1999,as an innovative pilot Squash Bursary Scheme.Six of Scotland's young, bright hopefuls were selected to participate in the new and exciting Scheme, unveiled at The National Training and Competition Centre for Scottish Squash, housed within the Centre for Sport and Exercise at Heriot-Watt. The main aim of the Squash Academy was the development of excellence, achieved by introducing a professional, progressive, development programme for a select group of junior players.

The Squash Academy has subsequently been expanded to include the National Talent Squad (NTS), which collectively represent the National Junior Excellence Programme for squash.

The programme has three key visions:

-To encourage participation in the sport through the employment of appropriate expertise allied to a clearly defined player development pathway.

-To identify and assist players to fulfil their potential through a progressive, holistic development programme.

-To assist in the development of individual Scottish players and Scottish teams capable of making a significant impact on World Squash.

The role of the Academy is to develop a small squad, with a remit to identify, select and assist junior players to achieve their potential.

The role of the NTS is to provide a comprehensive coaching service for all players who may be in contention to play for Scotland, it is a feeder system for the Academy. The NTS has been broadened to include players who are in a transitional phase i.e those who are outside the top ten in Scotland in their age category and also those who are moving outwith Junior Competition.
