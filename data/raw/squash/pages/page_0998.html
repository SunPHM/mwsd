Seitan: A Meat Substitute You Can Sink Your Teeth Into. Kay taught us four vegan recipes: Asian Coleslaw with Toasted Sesame Oil and Tamari, Asian Seitan with Tomatoes, Peppers and Green Onions, Whole-Grained Pasta with Fresh Cilantro and Basil, and Almond-Rice Cookies made with Brown Rice flour.

At Karyn's, Andrea Rios, executive chef at Russian Tea Time, taught us some vegan recipes, slightly modified to include more whole foods: Uzbekistan Tashkent Carrot Salad, Ukrainian Stuffed Potato Patty, Kasha Varnishkas, and Pumpkin and Butternut Squash Piroshki. She also spoke in depth about knife safety and the treatment of burns. Every attendee received a ten-dollar gift certificate to Russian Tea Time.

At Karyn's, Kay taught us four vegan made-from-scratch tofu dishes: Mexican Tofu Spread with Cilantro and Lime, Crispy Tofu Cakes with Chipotle Pepper, Cornmeal-Encrusted Tofu Cutlets with Mustard Sauce, and Chocolate Parfait with Cashew Cream.

At Karyn's, Kay, with a little help from her friend Roop, taught us vegan recipes from the Chicago vegetarian Indian restaurant Mysore Woodlands: Vegetable Pulav, Lentil Soup, and Dosai batter and Dosai Potato Filling.

At Karyn's, Kay taught us some vegan Mexican dishes: Seitan Fajitas with Lime, Mexican Tomato Salad with Cumin and Cilantro, and Mocha Cinnamon Galletas (whole-grained and naturally sweetened).

At the new Karyn's Fresh Corner, Kay taught us how to prepare three tasty vegan dishes, Quesadillas with Tofu and Pan-Crisped Tortilla Chips, Cucumbers with Lime and Cilantro, and Cocoa Mint Balls.
