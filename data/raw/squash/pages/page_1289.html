Ecological Way of Living

One of the significant challenges in today's modern world is ecological way of living. As we discussed, our modern society has dramatically developed economically. Many forests where we could have seen many diversity of organisms have been lost, and soil, water, and air have been polluted by human activities. The advanced technologies that we have acquired have made our lives much more convenient, reliable, and sufficient, but energy needed to operate those technologies increase in proportion to a rise in comfortable livings. Ironically, human activities have damaged many part of natural systems, and today they threaten our lives. 

Many individuals try to live with minimum impact on the environment. For example, they install solar panels on top of the roof to generate electricity for their home use. There are some cities and communities that have been trying to encourage ecological living. 

Ecovillage -Ithaca, NY

Ecovillage at Ithaca, NY is one of the most advanced ecological-community models in the U.S. The project idea was suggested in 1991 by a related organization and Cornel University’s Center for Religion, Ethics, and Social Policy. Ecovillage is located in about two miles west of downtown Ithaca, contains 176 acres of fields, woods, and wetlands. The residential houses are attached with each other emphasizing an interaction with their neighbors. There is a common house, which has an open kitchen and a recreation room, provided for everyone of the community member. The buildings in Ecovillage are also very efficient and well-concerned about the natural environment. The houses have good air heating, cooling, and circulation, and the south exposed windows in each room are very effective to take sunlight in. Most energy they use in the community is generated by solar panels on top of the buildings. Even though they are still depending on the city sewage and gas, the project members have been trying to find a way to deal with the operation. 

Their goal is simple; they wanted to create “… socially harmonious, economically viable, and ecologically sustainable settlement to demonstrate that human beings can live cooperatively with each other and with the natural environment. As their goal stated, Ecovillage has developed the community’s primary functions, such as cooperative projects and recreations, and neighborhood linkage with the natural environment. Today the project members of Ecovillage are planning to build the second community buildings within the same property.

Highlights

  • Community Oriented (Community Participation and Involvement)
  • Youth Education in Agriculture and Environmental Sciences
  • Community Organic Farm
  • Use of Solar Power

Tips for Ecological Living

There is a number of ways to improve your life style. If you have care of the environment, you can do hundreds of things to coexist with the nature. Creating comfortable, healthy environment in your home is essential.

1. Make a home vegetable garden in your back yard

    In the morning, you just go to your home garden to pick up some fully ripened tomatoes for your breakfast. It is very easy and healthy morning task that everyone can do. Making organic home garden is the best way to eat good vegetable and enjoy the plants to grow. You can also enjoy different seasonal vegetables throughout a year. In addition, potato, blueberry, strawberry, and squash grow well in acidic Florida soil.

2. Use organic products to reduce releasing toxic chemicals that are difficult to be dissolved in nature.

    There are many chemicals used in your consumption. You don’t even know what they are, even if they are listed on the back of the products. They might have a cause of environmental degradation, even a risk of your health problem. Organic products, including foods and daily necessities, do not harm the environment because they can be easily dissolved in natural cycle.

3. Pay attention to your everyday energy use, such as electricity, water, and gas.

    Do you notice how much energy is used by a typical American home a month? According to Public Works & Utilities, t he average combined City residential utility bill amounts to $145.35 per month. The utility bill includes charges for electricity, water, sewer (called wastewater), and garbage collection (solid waste). But if you are more careful about your usage of energy, you can save your utility bills. There are some organizations and the government agencies try to improve utility efficiency program. There is a number of ways to reduce the use of energy:

  • Unplug electronics
  • Close cracks and install insulation between the wall
  • Plant Trees – Deciduous trees around the house helps to block a direct sunlight in summer, and they keep the house warm during daytime in winter.
  • Concerned about the use of your vehicle – Try to use public transportation, and walking and biking are the best way to the place close to your home.

4. Use more natural resources!

    Sunlight and wind are always good sources to use. South exposed window helps to keep inside of house bright for long hours. In some houses and buildings, solar panels are installed to generate solar energy. Also if your house has a well air-circulation system, it will be a good idea to open the windows to let natural breeze into the house.

5. Pay attention to what you buy.

    When you go grocery, you don’t really pay attention to what kind of container the product is in. Some people try to use paper bag, instead of using plastic bag after shopping. You might have already realized how much waste you produce after shopping: Styrofoam boxes, paper boxes, plastic containers, cans, bins, pet-bottles, and so on. Some are incinerated, some are filled into the ground, and only a little are recycled.

6. Recycling

    There are so many things that you can recycle: plastic bags, water bottles, newspaper, even water. Think about how you can recycle these things.

Vegetable Garden

Today many people enjoy harvesting their garden vegetable. Tomato, beans, pepper, corn, and squash are generally easy to grow in your garden. Some of the vegetables are good to plant together at the same time. For example, planting beans, corn, and squash together are called “three sisters” because they help each other to grow well. Squash grows below on the ground, keeping soil moisture for the other crops. Corn supports beans to scroll up. Beans provide regume for other plants. To keep your garden healthy, it will be good to “crop rotation,” which rotates different crops after harvesting. This method has been widely used in agricultural lands to keep the land with rich soil for vegetable. There are a lot of websites providing more information about the vegetable garden on the internet.

 

What are some of the vegetables we can plant in garden in FL?

As we explained, Florida’s soils are mainly sandy and acidic. It means this is a very difficult condition for many plants to grow due to lack of nutrients and minerals in soil. However, there are some vegetables love acidic soils.

  • Potato
  • Blueberry
  • Strawberry
  • Squash
