Lingashi in the semis
by Mathews Chansa

Zambia's squash maestro Robby Lingashi is at the peak of his squash career which started in the small town of Kalulushi. Recently, he reached his first semi-finals of the Professional Squash Association (PSA) Tour in two years, when he lost to tournament top seed Bradley Ball of England. Unseeded Lingashi lost in the straight games via a 15-10,17-15,15-5 score line.

The Georgia Halco Atlanta Open marked the highest round reached this year by Lingashi. Ranked 79 at the beginning of the year, Lingashi beat Micheal Hopkins of England in the quater-final before he met his fate.

Lingashi was a Zambian number one player for a longtime before he moved to South Africa. He later moved to U.S and decided to set up a training base in New York. He is currently ranked 87 according to Dunlop PSA World Rankings for December.
