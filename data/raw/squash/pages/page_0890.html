Seared Supreme of Barracuda, with a Burgundy & Foie Gras Reduction, and a Squash and Potato Rosti






Created by Craft Guild of Chefs member Simon Green, Premier Sous Chef, Richmond Gate Hotel.

Ingredients (2 covers)
2 Barracuda Supremes
1 Par-Boiled Potato
250g Butternut Squash
125g Roquette
1 Banana Shallot (finely chopped)
1 Clove of Garlic (crushed)
25g Chopped Flat Leaf Parsley
Seasoning
Olive Oil
Truffle Oil

For the sauce:
50ml Veal Glace
50ml Fish Stock
50ml Burgundy Wine
25g Sliced Button Mushrooms
2 Shallots (chopped)
½ Garlic Clove (crushed)
Sprig of Thyme & Rosemary
½ Apple (peeled & diced)
25g Foie Gras Trimmings

Method
For the sauce, sweat off shallots, mushrooms, garlic and herbs without colour. Add wine and apple and reduce to nothing. Add stock and glace and reduce by half. Strain into clean pan and keep warm.

For the rest of the dish, sweat shallots and garlic without colour. Grate potato and blanched squash into a bowl. Add shallots, chopped parsley and season well, fold in 2 spoons of oil. Heat a little oil in a frying pan and press potato mixture into 2 metal rings, fry on both sides until golden brown. Remove from pan and place on a buttered tray in a hot oven for 5 minutes. Add a little more oil and place the seasoned fish skin side down and cook for 3 minutes, until skin is crispy and browned, turn fish over and finish for a further 2 minutes, then remove fish and leave to rest. Quickly sauté the Roquette in a pan. Place the Potato Rosti in the centre of the plate, cover with Roquette resting on top. To finish the sauce whisk in the cold Foie Gras quickly then pass through a fine sieve, pour around the fish; finish with a cordon of Truffle Oil. Serve immediately.
