From cukes to pumpkins, Gordy's is a vegetable lover's paradise
08/07/2003


Untitled1

Kevin Cederstrom

Gordy Tolppi started his little vegetable garden 50 years ago, and now it's not so little anymore.

He and his wife, Joan, have seen the garden grow into a 30-acre vegetable farm at Gordy's Greenhouse northeast of New York Mills.

It's nearing the end of summer and this is the time of year where the fresh vegetables are ripe for picking over the next few months.

Gordy said the cold crops like beans, peas, cabbage and broccoli will be ready soon. Cucumbers are ready now and being picked every day.

Driving around the land, Gordy points out all the different crops and where they are situated over the 30 acres. He's got peppers - both green and hot -carrots, dill, a few different kinds of squash, sweet corn, onions and beets.

There are watermelons, musk melons, seven acres of tomatoes, and four acres of potatoes. Pumpkins take up another seven acres. The Tolppis have carving pumpkins, which grow to between 20-70 pounds each, and the monster pumpkins, which reach upwards to 150 pounds.

Gordy started raising and selling his first vegetables in 1952 shortly after high school. Joan joined him in 1965 when the two were married.

Gordy said they added vegetables to the acreage more and more through the years until the vegetable farm got to be its biggest in the 70's. Things haven't decreased much since. The Tolppis take care of and pick the vegetables themselves, with some hired help, and it's definitely a full-time job this time of year.

"It's pretty big right now, with the 30 acres," Gordy said. "It's a big job to keep the fields cultivated and sprayed."

That cultivation is a continual job for Gordy because he's got to keep the weeds from growing between the rows and away from his crops.

He kind of enjoys the cultivation aspect of the business, though. That's when he can jump on one of the old Farmall tractors he collects and restores to work in the field.

The Tolppis generally plant the vegetables around the last days of May and begin selling the middle of July.

Turning onto the gravel road leading past vegetable fields and to the house, customers can see colorful flowers planted along the road. Gordy planted 5,000 flowers in three rows, more than 1/4 mile long, just for show. He said the project cost him a fortune but likes the way the flowers look next to his vegetables.

Along with growing and selling vegetables, the Tolppis also operate five greenhouses. The greenhouses are only open in the spring and are where customers can pick up bedding flowers and vegetable plants for gardens.

Gordy's Greenhouse is located nine miles north and three miles east of New York Mills on Otter Tail County Highway 8, then 1/4 mile north on 575th Ave.

The fresh vegetables are available seven days a week, 9 a.m.-7 p.m., To reach the Tolppis by phone call 218-837-5396.
