MOUTH-WATERING FARE
Christmas delicacies from the Apicius at Herend

Christmas, Christ's Nativity, is one of the foremost feasts in the Christian year. Bonds of kinship and friendship are tightened as we sit down to table with our loved ones. The Christmas board differs from the table at other times of year, in colours and decorations, and in the tastes and dishes it affords. There are strong traditions, of course, but many people like to surprise their families with new delicacies at this time of year. The Apicius Restaurant at Herend offers some festive ideas.


SHELLFISH SOUP WITH SAF- FRON

Ingredients for four portions: 800 g mussels, 800 g quahog clams, 1500 g sea-fish bones, 250 g carrots, 250 g celeriac, 250 g courgettes (zucchini squash), 150 g lemon, 200 ml white wine, 200 g onion, 70 g salt, 35 g black peppercorns, 10 g saffron, 10 g capers.

Prepare a strong stock from the fish bones, including three-quarters of the vegetable quantities Season with salt, peppercorns and saffron and flavour with lemon juice and white wine. Simmer for three hours over a low heat, then sieve through fine muslin to produce a clear stock. Dice the remaining vegetables, wash the mussels and clams, and place them in the stock. Boil until the shellfish open. Flavour again with lemon juice, wine and capers to taste.


DUCK RAVIOLI WITH CHAN- TERELLES

Ingredients for four portions: 800 g breast of duck, 100 g onion, 30 g salt, 16 g pepper, 500 g durum wheat flour, 4 eggs, 200 ml olive oil, 1 sprig of basil, 100 g parmesan cheese, 50 ml dry white wine, 30 g butter.

Chop the onion finely and fry to a golden yellow in the olive oil. Add the duck, cut into strips, flavour with salt and pepper, and braise until tender. Make a pastry of the flour and two eggs, roll it out thin and make ravioli with the braised duck meat. Boil these in salted water. Beat the remaining eggs with the butter and white wine over steam, flavour with the fresh basil, and season with salt and pepper. Pile the ravioli onto the plates and serve with chanterelles marinated in spiced olive oil, the egg and grated parmesan cheese.

COLD HARE PIE WITH GOOSE LIVER AND PRUNES

Ingredients for four portions: 800 g leg of hare, 400 g goose liver, 150 g prunes, 200 ml single cream, 150 ml red wine, 70 g onion, 20 g garlic, 10 g salt, 5 g pepper, 5 g thyme, 12 g gelatine, 100 g smoked streaky bacon.

Clean the legs of hare and prefry with the onion, garlic and herbs. Place in a casserole or terrine, pour on the red wine, cover and bake until tender. Warm the single cream, add the gelatine and beat, then add the hare. Season with salt and pepper and blend to a smooth pie sauce. Line the pastry form with thinly sliced bacon and half-fill the form with the pie sauce and leave to set in the refrigerator. Meanwhile, prepare the goose liver, stuffing it with prunes soaked in red wine and place in the pie form. Pour on the remaining pie sauce and return to the refrigerator. To serve, slice the pie with a sharp knife and place on the plates, adding a piquant Cumberland sauce.

GERBEAUD PASTRY WITH CREAM OF CURDS

Ingredients for four portions: Make a short pastry out of 200 g plain flour, 100 g icing sugar, 50 g butter, half a coffee spoon of bicarbonate of soda, and 1 dessert spoon of soured cream.

Darken two-thirds of the pastry by adding 5 g of cocoa powder. While the pastry is resting, make the cream of curds. Crumble 250 g of milk curds. Beat up 2 yolks of egg with 50 g butter and 100 g of granulated sugar, flavouring it with lemon rind. Add the curds and the beaten whites of 2 eggs and soften with a dessert spoon of soured cream. Butter a baking tray. Spread the lighter-coloured pastry. Spread on it runny apricot jam. Next spread a layer of half the darker pastry and followed by a layer of cream of curds. Cover the whole with the remaining darker pastry. Bake until done at 1500 C. When cool, ice with a chocolate glaze.
