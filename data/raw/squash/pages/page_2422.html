Austin-Bergstrom International Airport (ABIA) News

For immediate release:

August 13, 2002

LINEAR DETAIL, SWIRLING COLORS ARRIVE WITH WOMEN PRINTMAKERS OF AUSTIN AT AIRPORT

AUSTIN, TEXAS — Intricate, colorful, linear detail and expressive, splashing, swirling colors are among the variety of art presented by Women Printmakers of Austin at the Austin-Bergstrom International Airport.

Warm reds, yellows and greens color the lush Summer Garden complete with tomatoes, squash, peppers and grasshoppers. This work by Margie Crisp is a beautifully detailed linocut.

The cool blues and grays of night and the creatures which inhabit it are presented in softer shapes by the collagraphs on handmade paper by Kristin Kavanugh and Alisan Clarke.

In a more abstract piece, colored circles upon color go round in the prismatic swirl of Magnetic Energy, a monotype by Jo Lagattuta.

These works are among the 24 pieces presenting the fine art of printmaking. The field of fine art printmaking covers a broad spectrum of techniques including: etching, relief, wood block, linocut, monotype, monoprint, intaglio, collagraph, chine colle, lithography, silkscreen, Solarplate, and other photosensitive plate making materials such as Imagon, Riston, Z*acryl, and Torelief.

Among the Women Printmakers of Austin featured in the airport exhibit are: Jill Alo, Sylvia Betts, Theresa Bond, Marti Burns, Carol Brown, Jean Caffeine, Dorothy Carroll, April Garcia, Anna Gawarecki, Linda Genet, Le Green, Tita Griesbach, Carol Hayman, Francine Mastrangelo, and Anna Marie Pavlik. Additionally, the group's collaborative work displayed included Charlene Schatz, Martha Harrison, Zhna Sharp, Kathleen B. Pittman, and Chris Brown. In all, 24 artists participated in the airport exhibit.

The Women Printmakers of Austin is an organization dedicated to promoting excellence and diversity for women in the field of fine art printmaking. Their objective is to encourage and support women as printmakers and increase public awareness of the process and history of printmaking.

The Women Printmakers of Austin exhibit is on display through September 30th in the airport's east gallery located behind the Austin Article store near Gate 8. This is in the area of the airport past security checkpoints and accessible to ticketed passengers only, currently an audience of more than 16,000 passengers a day.

Also on display at ABIA:

Exhibit change out in process for the west gallery and pylon exhibits. Coming to these showcases will be the Austin Films and Filmmakers exhibit. The exhibit is scheduled for August 19 - November 18. The pylon exhibit cases are located along the concourse across from Gates 7-12. The west gallery is located behind the Book People store near Gate 10.
