Cream of Roasted Butternut Squash Soup

Cream of Roasted Butternut Squash Soup

Sweet butternut squash are enriched with cream and thyme in this savory soup. For a sit-down meal, serve the soup as a first course in warmed individual bowls. For a Thanksgiving buffet, present the soup in a warmed tureen so guests can help themselves. Garnish soup with warm lump crabmeat.

Ingredients - Serves 24 (cut recipe in half to serve 12!):

  • 6 Tbs. unsalted butter
  • 2 Bermuda onions, chopped (I used 1 large sweet like Vidalia and one large yellow)
  • 3 medium butternut squash, about 8-9 lb.
  • ½ cup rice (uncooked)
  • Thyme (2-3 sprigs)( A hand full!)
  • 12 cups chicken broth (2 large cans)
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • Freshly ground pepper, to taste (add salt if needed only)
  • 1 lb lump crabmeat for garnish (optional)

NOTE: Among those that are mild flavored are the white or yellow Bermuda onion, available March through June and the larger, more spherical Spanish onion, which is usually yellow skinned (but can be white) and in season from August to May. More than you wanted to know about onions! I was just curious since supermarkets never label onions "Bermuda", so I looked it up!

Directions:

Preheat oven to 400°F.

Old Method: Prick each squash with the tip of a knife so it won’t explode when it bakes. Cut squashes lengthwise and remove and discard the seeds and fibers. Place, cut side down on a foil sprayed with Pam on baking sheet and roast until they feel somewhat soft to the touch and a knife penetrates the skin easily, about 50 minutes. Remove from the oven and, when cool enough to handle, carefully peel/scrape the thin outer skin, saving as much of the browned roasted flesh as possible, and place pulp into a bowl and set aside.

Alternate method: Peel squash, cut in half lengthwise, clean out seeds, and cut into ½ inch dice. Then spread out on foil sprayed with Pam on baking sheet and roast until they are nicely browned, but not burned (about 30 minutes). Note that using this method, you will need to add water (not stock!) at the end to thin soup to desired consistency. If not, it will be paste-like.

In a large soup pot over medium heat, melt the butter. When the butter is foamy, add the onion and sauté, stirring occasionally, until tender and translucent, 4 to 6 minutes. Add rice, thyme, and some broth and simmer for about 20 minutes. Add the squash and broth and bring the mixture to a boil.

Using an immersion blender (immersion blender is easier and purees better), puree the soup (in batches if you have to use a regular blender) until smooth. Return the soup to the pot, stir in the cream and season with salt and pepper. Warm gently as needed. Check consistency and add water if necessary to thin the soup. Serves 24.

Garnish soup with warm lump crabmeat.
