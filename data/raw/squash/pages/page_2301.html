WHY SPEND ALL YOUR TIME MAKING SOMEONE ELSE'S DREAM

It's hard to stick to a low-carb diet with Ruffles in the house. Reduced fat Ruffles, mind you, but still. I went crazy over the fourth of July weekend and ate lots of hotdogs (with buns) and lots of potato salad and lots and lots and lots of Ruffles. You see, through and through I am a carbo junkie. You can't fight your nature. You can dangle the most luscious candybar in front of me, the most world-famous delectable chocolate, and I will beeline past you for the french fries. Willy Wonka would have nothing to fear if I visited his factory, but if I toured Mr. Smith's potato farm in Idaho I'd smuggle the Russets out in my pants.

I also fear that I'm one of those people who smells like garlic. I eat so much garlic that I don't smell it on myself anymore, but when I do something like set a piece of fruit on the cutting board in the kitchen for half a minute and then pick it up and eat it and it tastes like someone squirted a syringe's worth of garlic into it, it's then I know my habit is out of control. I chop it up and eat it in scrambled eggs, in burritos, in salads, in tunafish, in everything. Well, not cereal. Yet.
