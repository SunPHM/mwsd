Sports

Oriel has facilities for all major sports. The College has its own sports ground, called Bartlemas, situated off the Cowley Road close to James Mellon Hall. It has hockey, rugby and football pitches and a cricket square for the summer term. Tennis can be played on the hockey pitch during summer term. The new development at James Mellon Hall includes a squash court and gymnasium to complement the weights room on the main College site. The College has its own well-equipped boathouse.

Oriel’s students are active in a wide range of sports, and the College currently has teams for intercollegiate competitions in badminton, basketball, cricket, darts, football, hockey, modern pentathlon, netball, pool, rowing, rugby, squash, tennis and water-polo. Some students compete at University level in their sport, and others at College level. However, college sport is very much driven by enthusiasm, and provides an opportunity for everyone to take part whatever their level of skill. Participation in a sport is an easy way of meeting people from all years, and fosters good College spirit.

There are many opportunities to take up new sports at various levels of commitment. Where the College does not have the facilities, the University Sports Centre at Iffley Road is a short walk or bicycle ride away. Training is available to the highest standard, but many students simply like to dabble in a sport completely new to them during their time at College.
