East Jerusalem

The East Jerusalem YMCA was established in 1949 in a tent in Aqabat Jaber Refugee Camp, near Jericho . Today, the YMCA operates a variety of programs and activities consistent with its vision for Holistic Youth Development. These programs respond directly to the needs of people within all sectors of Palestinian society without discrimination. Special emphasis is given to young men and women.

The East Jerusalem building was built in 1963, located on Nablus Rd. , just outside the Old City walls , in Jerusalem . From here the Head Quarters, the Program Department and the hotel operate. The hotel was temporarily closed in 2001 as a result of the current political situation that has deterred tourists from visiting the Holy Land

Responding to the needs of the community the purpose of the Program Department is to foster the body, mind and sprit development and well being of its community especially youth, therefore, this has been translated into several programs and activities which serves the Palestinian community in Jerusalem and the West Bank, giving priority to youth, women and people with special needs.

The program works with the community from its quarters in Jerusalem and the YMCA centers in Ramallah, Beit Sahour and Jericho ; in addition to use facilities of clubs, schools and youth centers all over the West Bank to reach as many youth as possible.

Sport Activities :

The sports center serves the community of Jerusalem district. The center consists of:

•  Indoor facilities- swimming pool, changing rooms, fitness hall, multi-purpose hall, squash court, small gym and game rooms.

•  Outdoor Facilities: - three outdoor courts two of which are lined for basket ball, handball, volleyball and tennis and one soccer field.

 

Social and cultural activities :

The YMCA provides the community with different activities during the year, i.e. plays, musical concerts, folklore dancing, youth and family gatherings, lectures and discussions. The facilities and meeting rooms of the East Jerusalem YMCA host many clubs, youth centers and schools to hold their activities and meetings.



 

Summer Day Camps :

Every year the department holds several children camps in Jerusalem and the West bank, during summer, when children have a long vacation, they need to fill their time with creative, fun and learning programs. This program serves around 400 boys and girls from 6 - 12 years old annually.

 

 

Leadership Training and Development :

There are three programs which aim is to serve three different target groups.

 

• Youth leadership Training: This program objective is to direct the youth to be involved in their own communities in voluntary work, counseling and general basic leadership skills. The program serves young girls and boys age 14 - 18 years old from different schools and clubs in Jerusalem and the West Bank .

• Sports leadership this program aims to motivate players to be involved in training children in various traditional and non traditional sports, by training them how to use the different methods of training. The training serves young adults age 18 - 25 years old.

• Leadership and Management Skills Development: Its aim is to qualify and provide the people at the administration level with the appropriate knowledge and skills needed to run and activate their own clubs and centers. It serves club administration members in different regions in the west bank and short term courses in specialized subjects.

 



 

Exchange programs and International presentation :

The East Jerusalem YMCA encourages exchange programs on all levels. The objective of these exchanges and visits is to give a chance to people (especially youth) to meet, make friends and learn about each other's way of life and realities.
