Summer English Study Tour to Australia (2000 ¡V 2001)

 

On 21 July, a group of YY3 students supervised by myself headed off to Sydney for two weeks.  It was to be both a learning experience and a fun holiday.   As Australia is an English-speaking country, it would give many chances for students to practice their reading and speaking skills in authentic situations.  Also, our students would have a series of intensive language classes at a specialized English teaching college in the city.  Time was allocated to allow our students to do many of the touristy things, seeing the sights of the city and even outside the city.  In addition, our groups were able to live with real Australian families during their stay overseas to allow further language practice and exposure.

Overall, I felt that the students were well behaved and showed a sense of maturity as they dealt with the demands of overseas travel and the unusual experience of living with total strangers for two weeks.  They applied themselves to their studies at the language college (specializing in foreign learners), and allowed themselves the chance to appreciate the very different culture of another country.  I am sure that it only wet the appetite of many for more touring in the future.  Certainly, we learn and grow by all the things we see and experience when we travel.  Furthermore, the opportunities to use and practice English, especially in real world situations, can only benefit them.

 

Students were offered a wonderful opportunity.  It was aimed both to shape attitudes ansssd awareness, to change ¡§hearts and minds¡¨.  Certainly, education does not only take place in classrooms.  Indeed, learning from practical experience has much to recommend it.  They say that ¡§travel broadens the mind¡¨, and I am sure that all who visited Sydney came back wiser and more informed persons.  The students followed up with projects about their experiences done back in Hong Kong.

Two of our students were lucky enough to have the chance to take part in a week-long intensive English camp. Summer Bridging Program

To equip all new comers well, we organised a bridging program for them in the summer holidays. Through playing English games and doing a variety of language activities, these new comers learnt a lot about our school and classroom language in an interesting and communicative way.

 

 

IT and English

Our panel, following ED guidelines, is increasingly emphasizing IT in teaching and learning. Not only do most teaching programs build in an IT element, but we are always looking for ways of boosting IT use and awareness with our students. The opening of our MMLC allows new ways of teaching, taking advantage of new technologies. Not only are seniors getting more chances to practise their oral skills in the centre, but we are redesigning our intensive reader program to build in more use of IT for our juniors. Furthermore, our new Longman's textbooks for F.1 have a lot of CD ROM activities. We also have an English website, which students are free to access at any time to keep up-to-date with English happenings around our school.
