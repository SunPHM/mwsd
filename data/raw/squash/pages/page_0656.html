Growing Systems

I have tried a number of media to grow plants in.

Pearlite

Rockwool slabs

Water culture (floating styrofoam)

Water culture (plastic downspout)

Pearlite

 


Above are some of the plastic barrel ends that I used to grow squash in, in pearlite. The tubs are about 24" in diameter. The idea of two squash plants in each tub did not work out well. The plants go so big that I could not work around them easily and it was almost impossible to get to the main feed pipe running down the length of the greenhouse. I resorted to one squash plant per tub but even then the plants got too big.  I now grow them in 5 gal buckets that are mounted much closer to the floor of the greenhouse.

Here is lettuce growing in a vertical tube in rockwool. The lettuce grew well but not as well as in water culture. I now use the vertical tubes for strawberries.

Rockwool Slabs

Above are a coupe of pictures of my first and only use of rockwool slabs. I used 6 - 3 foot slabs to grow cucumbers, beans and tomatoes. The slabs themselves worked out well. They are nice and neat with almost no algae problems. They are expensive though. I think they were around $6 a piece. In comparison I get 4 cubic feet of pearlite for $6. Again I planted way too many plants in a small area and it turned out to be quite crowded. I now have 5 gal buckets along this wall using pearlite instead of the rockwool slabs.

Water culture (floating styrofoam)

This is one of the plastic barrel ends that I converted from pearlite to float some styrofoam. I used the blue type of styrofoam that you can buy at Home Depot or that type of store. The styrofoam simply floats on the nutrient solution and the roots grow down in the solution. What is growing here is lettuce. I also had a bubbler from a aquarium in it which ran 24hrs a day.
