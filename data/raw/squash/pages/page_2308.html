Every article I have seen on grilled vegetables basically comes down to the same thing:

- toss with olive oil, salt, and pepper
- grill

This really does work great; if you keep the oil down to a little spritz, it's pretty good for you, too, especially if you eat more veggies and less pork butt. Onions, 'shrooms, and asparagus are popular here, if you like zuchinni or eggplant, do those. I'm going to try carrots one of these days.

Alan likes to use Italian dressing instead of plain olive oil; I like to add garlic and paprika to the salt and pepper.

Potatoes are tricky because of the time it takes for them to cook through. My french fry substitute is to quarter potatoes lengthwise (that is, cut each one into 4 wedges, apply the oil and seasoning, and cook at about 350° for 90 minutes, turning once. Use any leftover space on teh grill when you cook low and slow; at 250°, figure hrs for the spuds.

I've had just so-so success cutting the spuds smaller and using a basket and a higher temp.

We LOVE grilled vegetables, and do them frequently. We do all of the vegetables you mentioned, plus carrots, bell peppers, and sweet potatoes.

I toss them with olive oil and a spicy rub. Recently, I've been using Wild Willy's Number Ond-derful Rub from Smoke & Spice, by Jamison and Jamison. It adds a lot of flavor and some heat (I triple their recommended amount of cayenne).

Grilling really brings out the sweetness of the carrots and bell peppers. I use red, yellow, and orange bell peppers, as well as the green. They taste great, and look nice on the plate.

Grilled sweet potatoes are my husband's favorite! He'd have them every night. I either slice them into rounds, or, if I'm being lazy, length-wise. The slices are about a quarter to a half inch thick.

I coat them lightly with olive oil and sprinkle on the rub. We like them very spicy, so I put it on pretty thick.

I grill them until they are dark (some might say burned, but they don't taste burned – caramelized is the better word) on the outside and soft on the inside - about 15 minutes maybe. I turn them a couple of times during that time.

The outside sort of seals in the moisture, just like a seared steak. They are delicious!

I've also done potatoes this same way, with good success. We like the sweet potatoes better, however.

One caveat - I usually do the vegetables on a gas grill, and have only tried them on the K once (haven't had it that long). I wasn't really happy with how they came out on the K.

I think grilled vegetables, at least the way I like them, require high heat and short cooking times. I'm just finishing up my break-in period, and didn't have the K hot enough (about 350). I’ll try again when I can get the temperature up around 500.

Here's a good one. Mix up some Good Season's Italian dressing using olive oil and balsamic vinegar. Quarter some onions (hold em together with toothpicks), green bell and red and yellow sweet peppers, cube an eggplant and grab a bunch of baby portabella's, put em all in a large ziplock bag and pour the dressing in. Let them marinate overnight then skewer em and grill em up over a high temp fire. Even the wife likes these. Burned many a finger rescuing the chunks that come loose from the stick. Works well if you julienne the veggies and use a grill basket. Summer squash and zuchini are pretty good in this marinade also.

A couple of veggie tips from Steve Raichlen's How to Grill book. Soak bamboo skewers and use two of them to skewer about 6 to 8 pieces of asparagus cross wise. Think of it like making a raft. Brush with a combination of soy sauce and sesame oil and put directly on the grill for just a minute or two each side. The "raft" construction makes them easy to handle with tongs.

Sweet potatoes are great just tossed right in with the coals. The outside is burned/charred completely but the inside is wonderful.
