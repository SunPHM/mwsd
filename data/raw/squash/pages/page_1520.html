People

Meet the denizens of Livingstone House. Note to Livingstone guys: If you don't like what I have written (or I have got some detail wrong) then feel free to let me know.

Dave Holness
AKA - The Godfather. Warden's Flat.
Warden (keeps the peace)
For those who don't know a warden in this case is not like a warden in a prison, but an older person, usually an employee of Rhodes, who lives in or close to the residence and whose responsibility it is to keep the students on the straight and narrow. Dave lectures in the Law Faculty and plays golf and also coaches the Table Tennis club.

Simon Barker
AKA - Mop, Chewbarker. Room 3.
A "local boy" (i.e. he hails from Grahamstown) in his first year at Rhodes, Simon earned the moniker Mop for not cutting his hair for 6 months, after which he resembled a ..., he earned the nickname Chewbarker for howling like a wookie every time he passes into the fluffy clouds of inebriation.

Rob Boffard
AKA - Spunky young intern. Room 5.
During the June vac of 2003 Rob worked for The Star and in a "brilliant piece of investigative journalism" uncovered evidence of plagiarism by a well known author. He was later described by a columnist in the Mail and Gaurdian as a "spunky young intern". QED.

Mark Bowen
AKA - Gimp. Room 24.
A long time resident of Livingstone (this is his third year), Mark has been endowed with the name Gimp by his friends for, well, if you can't figure it out on your own I certainly won't tell you.

Chris Bredin
AKA - Breedy, Pocket, Goose, Cave Pig, Cartman. Room 20.
House Comm - Secretary
A third year Comp Sci / IS student, Chris is well known for his carefree irreverence for the finer things in this world, like feelings, emotions and human life.

Richard Carter-Brown
AKA - Christopher Columbus. Room 7.
No one is sure why he does it, but Richard has refused to wear long trousers all year. In the dead of winter he can be seen, blue from the waist down, marching grimly down the road to lectures. He earned the Christopher Columbus award at the awards evening for taking the long way home to res one night. His trip was about 5 to 10 kms long and involved a detour to the location, while the optimum route back is about 1 km long.

Richard Chan
AKA - Jackie Chan. Room 6.
A frustrated Idols contestant, Richard was given the Celine Dion award for singing better than the original. One of the few to use the piano for its intended purpose his eloquent tinkling insinuates the corridors on many a day.

Gavin Chetty
AKA - Glowworm. Room 4.
A man of few words, Gavin has one of the funniest "Where am I?" notices on his door in all the varsity. His door is also bedecked with the limerick:
I wish I was a glowworm
For a glowworm's never glum
For how can you be glum
When the sun shines out your bum?

Sam Cornish
AKA - Corndog, Samwise. Room 1.
House Comm - Treasurer
Currently living in Nigeria Sam is an avid rower and sportsman in general. He matriculated from Saint Andrews College here in Grahamstown, but he has been forgiven for this and we have all moved on from that unpleasant period in res life. By the by, take all you read on this page with a grain of salt, it is more an exercise in creative writing than a factual account.

Jean-Luc Germiquet
AKA - JL. Room 2.
JL spent a couple years in England with his nose to the grindstone before deciding he had had enough and his consequent enrollment and incarceration at Rhodes. JL, Mop and Sam are all Saint Andrews College alumni and stay in adjacent rooms. It is not known whether this was intentional, but the effect is the same.

Malizole Gobe
AKA - Show me the money. Room 28.
In his second year in Livingstone, his third at Rhodes, Malizole keeps his neighbors pious with Gospel music on a Sunday. Unfortunately, since his neighbors prefer to sleep till noon on a Sunday, the opposite effect is usually achieved. "Mali" in Xhosa is money, hence the moniker. I must confess to making it up on the spur of the moment, for Malizole claims to be without a nickname.

Daniel Hall
AKA - Veldskoen, Farmer, The Synergy. Room 25.
Daniel lives in the Northern Cape, for which he is given constant grief. Despite the fact that he is not Afrikaans he has been awarded the title Velkskoen and is the first person consulted when the Afrikaans version of a word is wanted. He is probably not a fan of stereotypes.

Ryan Hancocks
AKA - Bob. Room 26.
House Comm - Food Rep
Ryan earned the nickname Bob on the first day of O-Week in his first year. When asked to stand up and say where he was from, what he was studying, what he would like to be called, he said something like "My name is Ryan, from Port Elizabeth ... and you can call me anything you want, you can even call me Bob." He was immediately christened Bob and has remained so.

Mark Jackson-Moss
AKA - M'n'M, Minim. Room 18.
Sub-warden
Senior Student in 2002, Minim can always be relied upon for an inspired saying in a pinch. Example, "The redness of an apple is not in its colour, but in its taste." He is also the author of the Livingstone Bible, a copy of which will be linked to as soon as it can be transcribed. It is an invalueble introduction to University life for first years. *cough*, *cough*.

Oliver King
AKA - Oli, Pravda. Room 16.
House Comm - Entertainment rep and resnet rep
The author of this site, Oliver is afraid that if he mentions anything incriminating the men in black suits and dark glasses will stop following and start chasing. So I won't.

Brendan Lee
AKA - Tree. Former resident. Defected to Graham House.
Brendan brought, and occasionally brings, a distinct aura to the res. A conversation with Brendan is always fraught with the danger of breaking into incredulous head shaking sessions. He is the sort of person who, when he discovers that he does not have a costume for the res function, will break a few branches from a tree on his way over and tape them to his body, becoming Tree and creating the most original, if inexplicable, costume at the function. The theme for the function was West Side Vs East Side. Go figure.

Mandilakhe Lubisi
AKA - Mandi, Hazard. Room 11.
Mandi is another person who brings a special aura to the res. A hazard when inebriated, he continually infuriates his long-suffering neighbor with aurally extroverted behavior. There is much more that can be said, I leave it to the galleries to say it.

Komborero Makoni
AKA - Principal Fungus Technician. Room 13.
Komborero comes from Zimbabwe, plays Table Tennis and possibly some other sports I don't know about, and is studying towards a BSc IS degree. His nickname is a spur of the moment creation courtesy of Minim.

S'Bongiseni Mamba
AKA - Mr Mamba. Room 12.
S'Bongiseni is Mandi's long suffering neighbor. S'Bongiseni comes from Swaziland and adds to the international favor of the res. S'Bongiseni is a journalism student and is engaged in constant battle with Shawn over the latter's occasional use of the ladies bathroom (situated next to S'Bongiseni's room) in the odd emergency. It has not yet resorted to bloodshed.

Tendayi Muyambi
AKA - Tindo. Room 22.
Sub-warden
Tendayi is another Zimbo. The Sports Rep last year he moved up to the position of sub-warden this year. Tendayi is a keen basketball player and not so keen IS student. Not that any IS student is very keen, mind you.

Kutlo Motseta
AKA - Tsetse fly. Room 30.
Kutlo proved to be very elusive in my search for a photo of every guy in Livingstone. Kutlo is a successful athlete. He matriculated at Saint Andrews. I am losing inspiration. Writing these explanations while attempting to remain creative is much more difficult than you would think.

Shawn Miller
AKA - Yakuza, Bones. Room 10.
Another student who took a couple years off before coming to varisty, Shawn is an avid fan of all things Japanese, be they calligraphy, art or rice cakes. You can check out some of his contributions to the webpage in the form of photos in the image gallery.

Siyabonga Mthembu
AKA - Siya, Whoopi. Room 9.
Siya is a drama student and participated in a play, I forget the name, which ran to much public acclaim. Look at his hair in the picture and see why he is referred to by some as Whoopi (as in Goldberg). Siya is a Steve Biko Scholarship student.

Thomas Mukasa-Mugerwa
AKA - Big Tom, The Bookie. Annex 1.
House Comm - Sport rep
Awarded with the "Black Shakespeare" award at the res function Tom continually amuses and delights the res with some of the funniest sports commentary ever posted on the notice board. He comes from Uganda and runs a low fee (order of 50c), nonprofit betting service during world cups (be they soccer, rugby or cricket).

Albert Ndebele
AKA - Holy Fish. Annex 2.
The oldest student in res Albert is Botswanan and has come back to study politics, amongst other subjects. His nickname is a mystery to me, and will remain so as long as the only answer I get to my repeated enquiries as to its origin is "That's my secret."

Andile Ndlela
AKA - G-String, Gieza. Room 17.
Andile is pertinatious in his determination to really Enjoy (note the capatalisation) his time here at varsity. Weekends swell alarmingly in size, hours are spent blowing a plastic trumpet at sports matches, a merry time is had in general. I think he studies as well. At the house function he filled every silence with a cry of "He's spinning out of control!", in reference to Mandi. At the time it had most in stitches. You had to be there to appreciate the humour.

Tinashe Ndoro
AKA - Most Improved Drinker. Room 8.
Tinashe is a quiet Zimbo with a fetish for cricket hats (the one's with the wide brim). I can't recall ever seeing him without one. His title (as mentioned above) is thanks to Tapiwa, though I cannot vouch for its verisimilitude.

Ashvin Ramsawhook
AKA - Ashes, Pooja, Hideout. Room 21.
Ashes is another long time resident of Livingstone House. He comes from Zim and single handedly keeps Spar in business. Perhaps it is a Zim thing, but I have only seen Ashvin's hair once, at all other times it is artfully concealed under a beanie (in winter) or peak cap (in summer). Perhaps someone can explain why this is?

Adam Skikne
AKA - Underdog. Room 15.
Adam and his sister Cara founded and head an online web magazine Underdog Press. Check it out for a taste of the creativity that Livingstone House holds.

Cornelius Strijdom
AKA - Neels, Sk8er Boy. Room 27.
Neels is an academic schitzophrenic. I am not sure how to classify his degree, it was law, then had some science subjects thrown in, and may even contain a BA subject or two. Neels brings the number of Botswanan Livingstonians up to 2.

Miklos Szecsei
AKA - Mikit, Thanatos. Room 19.
House Comm - Head student
Some people hold the opinion that the rise of the modern feministic movement in the 50's and 60's is directly related to the loss of a large proportion of the male population in World War 1. As far as I am aware Mik is not one of those people.

Tapiwa Taodzera
AKA - Taps, Baz. Room 23.
A corrigendum is a thing to be corrected. It is one of many words that a great fraction of people never use. Rumour has it that Tapiwa has never used it. In fact, rumour has it that it has never crossed the lips of a Livingstone guy at any time. On another note, Tapiwa comes from Zimbabwe, plays soccer and probably studies in his spare time.

Lincoln Wahl
AKA - Wheelie. Room 31.
Lincoln is a business science student who is of the firm opinion (as are most Maths 1 students) that Math's occupation of the 6th lecture slot (just before lunch) every day is as pleasant as a toad moving in and opening up a Toad Spa beneath that jiggly thing at the back of your mouth. He also plays tennis.

Adrian Williams
AKA - Ady, Maverick. Room 14
Ady has the unfortunate distinction (along with Mop) of being a local boy. He has managed to overcome this bitter setback by a determined effort to wipe all trace of it from his memory. Ady is a Geology / Chemistry student who dabbles occasionally in the odd game of squash.

Gareth Woods
AKA - TheInfamousGW. Room 29.
Gareth comes from Port Elizabeth. He has managed to overcome his education at Grey by making it into university, thus enabling to drop all mention of the former from his CV ;). He is not a fan of Botany, is an avid gamer and occasionally does strange follically related stuff. See his picture.
