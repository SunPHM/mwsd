Where to sip soup

VIENNA HOME BAKERY

***

626 Queen W. 703-7278. Wed-Sat 10am-6pm; Sun 11am-4pm. No smoking. No reservations.

TIGER LILY'S NOODLE HOUSE

****

257 Queen W. 977-5499. Mon-Fri 11:30am-9pm; Sat-Sun 11:30am-11pm. No smoking. No reservations.

C'EST WHAT

***

67 Front E. 867-9499. Mon-Fri noon-2am; Sat-Sun 11am-2am. Smoking available. No reservations.

SWAN RESTAURANT

****

892 Queen W. 532-0452. Mon-Fri noon-10:30pm; Sat-Sun 10:30am-11pm. Smoking available. Reservations accepted.

eye ratings are out of five and depend on price, service and atmosphere as well as quality of fare.

BY JILL BORRA

Walking along Queen Street near Bathurst, I'm trying to concentrate on dodging rivers of brown slush while bracing myself against below-zero winds, when a homeless man in rasta-colored shorts dogs me, asking for a handout. "Spare some change?" he asks. "For a bowl of soup?"

He's not the only one with soup on his mind. With the thermometer plummeting to obscene temperatures and the snow piling up faster than Mayor Mel can get his foot out of his mouth, this is the time of year when the steamy comfort of a bracing bowl of homemade soup is most alluring.

The Vienna Home Bakery looks like it should be in a small town on the way up to cottage country rather than a stone's throw from the ill-favored corner of Queen and Bathurst. This simple, cozy lunch counter is all pale pink walls and the smell of fresh bread baking. Vegan soups are homemade every day, and the bread (it is a bakery) is fresh from the oven.

I happen to stop by when the soup du jour is black bean: simple, maybe, but it's thick and flavorful, with just the right amount of seasoning and a touch of fresh cilantro to dress it up. It's the stuff of comforting home-kitchen cooking. Some of their other soup specialties are minestrone, curried lentil, green and yellow split pea, and a grand calabasa, with butternut squash, red peppers, Savoy cabbage, onions, garlic, carrots, tomatoes, green beans, cumin and cinnamon. Soup and bread costs $3.95, while soup and a sandwich is $6.50.

For a more gourmet experience, Tiger Lily's Noodle House, near CITY-TV, has an ambrosial spread of Thai and Chinese soup choices. The interior is simple, almost pristine, with deep orange walls overlaid with paler orange wooden carved palm tree silhouettes. Tiny potted flowers sit on every table.

With mix-and-match soup bowls ($5.95), you can combine either vegetable or meat homemade broth with a choice of six types of noodles, and garnish it with barbecued pork, Shanghai chicken, vegetables, black bean beef or fish. In the wonton soup bowls ($6.95), chicken or shrimp and pork wontons stand in for the noodles. The soup, enough for a meal, arrives in something more like a serving dish than a soup bowl. The vegetarian special is dense with carrots, tofu, bean sprouts, bok choy, soy beans and coriander, and soon I have to put down my spoon and pick up my chopsticks to dig in. The stock is fresh and not too salty, with the soy beans giving it a hint of sweetness. The Asian mushroom soup, flavored with green onions and a subtle bit of heat, is savory with coconut milk and plenty of fresh shiitake, straw and portobello mushrooms.

It's rich, but the small serving is just the right amount. The waitresses are energetic and friendly, if hyper-efficient. The food comes with lightning speed, and before I've had time to sit back and exhale a contented sigh after my last spoonful, the dishes are whisked away. Tiger Lily's also serves Hawaiian-style duck soup and Thai lemon grass soup served with chicken, shrimp, calamari and rice noodles.

C'est What, at the corner of Church and Front, has been a dependable area watering hole for 11 years. An abundance of suits suggests an after-work crowd from the nearby monolithic office towers.

The soup changes daily and is served with a whole-grain baguette. The ginger acorn squash soup ($4.25) is delicious -- thick, smooth and comforting. Other specialties are borscht, broccoli and cheddar, potato and leek, and Thai soup. After 9:30 bands play in the adjoining bar, so expect a lot of noise late at night.

Swan Restaurant, at Queen and Strachan, attracts the local arty crowd with a taste for fine food and has an exquisite roster of soup delicacies served with Portuguese bread for $4. Some favorites are their spinach fennel purée, mushroom barley, harvest vegetable, lemon grass coconut soup and hearty root vegetable soups.

As you hunker down through the next few bitter months of winter, remember, soup's not just a starter. Soup is good food.
