Mark Apollos - Nidan (2nd Dan)    has been training in Goju Ryu Karate for 15 years.  Initially he started training under Deena Naidu Sensei in Goju Kai Karate (Yamaguchi family) and in 1992 he joined the Okinawan Goju Ryu dojo of Buddy Govender Sensei.  He is an 'uchi-deshi' (inside student) to Buddy Sensei and is the 'dai sempai' (senior assistant instructor) at the South African Jundokan Hombu Dojo in Durban.  He is a retail area manager of the Golden Circle Racing Group and is currently completing his masters in Business Management.  His favorite katas are Sanchin, Sepai, Shisoshin and Sesan.  He enjoys the 'tai-sabaki' (body evasion) movements and the transition of the hard and the soft techniques.  In kumite (fighting) he prefers kicking techniques, especially 'kin geri' (groin kicks), and, he enjoys hard training!  He says that he "practices Karate to achieve a good character and to share ideas and thoughts regarding life skills - Buddy Sensei is a person that I will always regard as my mentor"

Chantal Govender - Nidan (2nd Dan) started training in Goju Kai Karate in 1978.  She too is an 'uchi-deshi' (inside student) to Buddy Sensei and is also 'dai sempai' (senior assistant instructor) at the South African Jundokan Hombu Dojo in Durban.  She teaches Karate-Doh at various pre-school and primary schools and is a well respected and much loved instructor of our junior karate-ka's.  Chantal is already a veteran of 2 tours at Summer Youth Camps in Michigan, USA.  Her favorite kata is Sesan.  She enjoys kata and kumite.  Her spirit and enthusism is very infectious in our Organization.

Koogan Pillay - Nidan (2nd Dan)   is currently studying to become a computer engineer.  He started his training under Buddy Sensei in 1994 at the Cathedral Dojo.  He attended as many classes as possible and showed an early aptitude for hard work and a fierce dedication to Karate-Doh.  His favorite kata is Shisochin - "because it is a quick kata emphasizing the hard and softness of Goju Ryu".  Koogan is a very hard working karate-ka and sets a high standard for the juniors of the dojo.  He loves the 'jodan mawashi geri' (roundhouse kick to the head area) in kumite.  He enjoys outdoor-training and gushukus.  he states that "besides keeping me healthy and disciplined, Karate-Doh stresses a good and beneficial way of life.  It allows me to handle situation in many different ways - it is my way of life"

Riaz Paruk - Nidan (2nd Dan) started Karate with Buddy Sensei in 1989 at the age of 6 years old!  Today is he 17 years old and  a learner at Westville Boys High, Grade 12.  Together with Koogan they have set extremely high standards for the juniors to follow and they are both involved with the Saturday junior and youth classes where they assist Buddy Sensei with the teaching.  he says that his best kata is Seiyunchin as "it requires a lot of concentration and leg strength.  This kata is a real test of my physical endurance and stamina".  He plays football, rugby and cricket, and occasionally enjoys a game of squash.  He compliments his Karate training with a regular work-out at the school gym.

Ravin Ramasar - Shodan (1st Dan)  was a Senior lecturer in Biochemistry at the ML Sultan Technikon here in Durban, however he and his family have now immigrated to Australia.  He started his training in an eclectic style of Martial Arts in 1988 and joined Buddy Sensei's Jundokan in 1995.  He has traveled to most parts of the Far East.  He likes honesty and integrity, wild life and good food, however he dislikes shoddiness, dishonesty and conflict.  To him Seiyunchin Kata is very practical form a self-defence point of view, but also enjoys Gekisai dai Ichi for it's power.  With his problematic knees he reckons that Karate offers him the diversity and confidence to continue striving for good health.  He prefers low effective attacks like 'gedan tettsui uchi' (lower hammer fist strike) and 'kansetsu geri' (knee joint kick).  He is married to Anne and they have a beautiful 4 year old daughter.  He was also involved in Union work where he served as an executive member.

Neville Jones - Shodan (1st Dan)  by profession is a group administrative manager for Constantia Paints.  He started Karate under Bob Harris in Zimbabwe in 1975 where he obtained a blue belt in Kykoushinkai Karate.  He then moved on to an eclectic style of Martial Arts and then joined Buddy Sensei's Jundokan in 1995.  He feels the "Karate has built his confidence, concentration and physical well-being which helps him cope with the stresses of everyday life."  The kata's that most suit him are Shisochin and Sanseru and he enjoys the practicality of 'Jiyu Ippon Kumite" (free one point attack).  After experience a few styles of Karate he is "very satisfied with the overall training to date!"
