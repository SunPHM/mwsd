Bronx Cocktail

This is a sophisticated gin cocktail with the color and warmth of a Scotch based drink.  It's perfect for the leather and dark wood palate that hasn't yet developed a taste for Scotch.  Put the iPod on Tom Waits' "The Heart of Saturday Night" and enjoy.

1 Ounce Gin
1/2 Ounce Sweet Vermouth
1/2 Ounce Dry Vermouth
1 Ounce Fresh Squeezed Orange Juice

Shake with ice and strain into a well-chilled cocktail glass.  Garnish with a cherry or an orange wedge, but not both, you superfluous bastard.

Birthday Dinner

Thursday was Laura's birthday and in keeping with the tradition of "birthday gifts that are actually for yourself" I cooked her a bunch of things that sounded really good to me.  

Ok, this is not completely true.  I did make some broccoli raab, which, were it up to me, would be way down the list, but a certain birthday girl is a big raab fan so there you have it; complete and utter selflessness.

We started with a simple incredible salad of watercress and macadamia nuts with a ginger lime dressing.  This Ming Tsai inspired creation takes about 45 seconds to make and leaves you wondering why you would ever spend more time than that cooking when such awesome results are so easily achieved.  Thumbs up from the birthday girl.

Next came the Lobster.  I was a bit squeamish about the whole knife through the head trick, so I left that dirty work to the resident assassin.  Mr. Friendly had a decapitated squirrel waiting for me one morning last week, so I figured she'd make short work of this rubber band handicapped beast.

While she took care of this dirty deed, I set to work on Jean-Georges Vongerichten's Thai Lobster.  The results of which will make a believer of any fusion-phobic "authenticity" hound.  Thai curry, lemon grass, white wine, apples!?  Who knew?

Some quick blanching of the recently slain lobster left me with slightly undercooked gorgeous pieces of tail and claw meat to be finished in a gentle oven with some butter, salt, and cayenne.  Top that with the bizarro sauce, serve with some blanched and sautéed broccoli raab, and saffron rice.  Et Voila!

This was very well received as a humble birthday offering.  There was a lot to love here, not the least of which was the tender and flavorful lobster meat.  This is the first time I've actually cooked lobster.  I'll admit, they are a little intimidating.  But really, even following Vong's very vague instructions, this was easy and turned out marvelously.  It's now even more amazing to me that 95% of lobster I've ever been served in a restaurant has been severely overcooked.  Dumb-ass chefs!

Finished the evening with a little chocolate coffee soufflé.
