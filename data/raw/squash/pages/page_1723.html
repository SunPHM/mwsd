SLEEPING BEAUTY

Home of the Sleeping Beauty Mountain Peak. A town full of surprises and contrasts. Experience the serenity of true country life. Feel the caress of a fresh country breeze. Smell the fragrance of the fynbos in the sweet country air. Treat your tastebuds to mouthwatering country cuisine. Cherish the memories of sincere country hospitality. Where tradition and diversity blend to form rich character, charm and culture. Where old meets new.

MOUNTAIN ADVENTURES

A host of activities exist in the spectacular Langeberg mountains. Abseiling, hiking trails, horse riding safaris, 4X4 trails, hang gliding, mountain biking and canoeing, lure lovers of the outdoors. Watersports at the scenic Korentepoort Dam include windsurfing, water-skiing, fishing and para sailing.


FYNBOS

Riversdale is famous for its spectacular species of indigenous flora - referred to as "fynbos" - and is one of the largest exporters of fynbos in the Cape Province. Tours through fynbos farms are available on the Eden Country Route, and you can also witness this floral kingdom on various hiking trails, which take you through forests and lead to sparkling mountain pools, waterfalls and Khoi rock drawings which tell of ancient myths.



RECREATIONAL FACILITIES

Riversdale boasts a 9 hole, 18 tee golf course, which has been rated one of the most scenic in South Africa. The intimate friendly atmosphere of this country club is one of the attractions when you lodge at Eden B&B, where the reservations of tee-off times can be arranged.



The local bowling club welcomes players to enjoy a game on well kept greens. Other recreational facilities include swimming, squash, tennis and jukskei. Cyclists can enjoy safe roads.

EDEN COUNTRY ROUTE

Mapped routes allow visitors to experience true South African farm and country life in the mountains and surrounding areas. Meet talented local entrepreneurs who produce arts and crafts, and cultivate honeybush tea and herbs for both local and export markets.



AGRICULTURE AND COUNTRYSIDE

Being in Riversdale one can appreciate the wonderful pollution-free environment and wide open spaces typical of the Cape. Dairy farming in the area is of the best and biggest in the country, whilst grain is the main crop activity. Other farming activities include ostriches, sheep, flowers and vegetables.


HISTORY AND CULTURE

Riversdale has a rich culture and heritage and various historical landmarks. The internationally recognized Julius Gordon Africana Centre, built in 1880, houses a fine collection of art works by famous South African artists such as Thomas Bowler. The centre is an invaluable source of information on the history of Riversdale.



The famous Blombos Cave is a short distance from Riversdale. The important findings of the archeological dig at Blombos provides unquestionable proof of the development of modern man in this area 77 000 years ago.


ACCOMMODATION AND RESTAURANTS

A variety of accommodation facilities exists ranging from smart guest houses, scenic guest farms, a popular hotel, self-catering cottages and chalets, isolated cabins and an African bush camp. Restaurants, coffee shops and take-away outlets offer good quality meals and traditional cuisine.



COMMERCIAL CENTRE

Riversdale is the administrative capital of the Langeberg region and the main shopping centre for surrounding towns. Visitors can purchase all they need whilst in the area in the fine selection of shops. There are 24 hour banking facilities as well as medical practices and an efficient hospital.


ANNUAL EVENTS

Agriculture Show, Ratem Eisteddfod Biennial Indigenous Flower Show, Half Marathon, Beauty and the Beach Festival.
