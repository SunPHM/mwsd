Food With Wine
Grand Pasta




Food: Whole Wheat Spaghetti With Walnut "Pesto" And Winter Squash

Wine: 2000 Francis Coppola Claret

The SoHo Grand is one of the most fashionable hotels in New York City, attracting models, actors and designers as well as executives who want to be a part of the action.

Owned by billionaire real estate developer--and former owner of flea collar maker Hartz Mountain--Leonard Stern and his son Emanuel, the SoHo Grand introduced the notion of a luxury hotel to what is arguably the city's chicest neighborhood.

But even the fashionable have to eat sometime--and the man in charge of the menus is Brooklynite John DeLucie (he has also created the menus for the Church Lounge at the Tribeca Grand Hotel). DeLucie, who has a business degree from New York University, graduated from the master chef program at the New School for Culinary Arts in 1990 and has worked in some of New York's top restaurants, including Rick Moonen's Oceana and Colina.

Here, he shares a recipe that's ideal for tucking into on a cold winter day: whole wheat spaghetti with walnut "pesto" and winter squash. --Christina Valhouli

Ingredients

4 shallots, finely minced
3 cloves garlic, finely chopped
3 cups walnuts, toasted and rough chopped
1/2 cup butter, softened, plus 2 tablespoons to cook squash
½ cup extra virgin olive oil
1 box whole wheat spaghetti
½ cup fine-diced butternut squash
1 cup grated reggiano

Method

1. On low heat, sweat shallots and garlic in olive oil until translucent and soft (they should not have any color). Gently fold butter into cooked shallots, garlic and walnuts. The butter should just coat the mixture so that it is mostly walnuts.
2. Gently cook butternut squash in extra butter.
3. Cook pasta in boiling salted water until al dente.
4. In a large pan, melt the compound butter over gentle heat. When pasta is finished, drain and reserve some of the pasta water. Add pasta to butter with a touch of the water and toss. Add reggiano. Season with salt and pepper.
5. Transfer to a serving bowl and top with warmed butternut squash.

Serves 4-6 people
