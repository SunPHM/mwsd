 Monday, September 16, 2002
Theme: New Chickens 

My sister got some year-old layers from a neighbor and generously gave some to us. We thought they could hang out with Malto and Stripes for a while, and if they keep laying the children can find the eggs.

Unfortunately, as is usually the case, we overestimated the intelligence of the chickens.

Malto and Stripes, being second-class roosters (our first-class rooster looks after our own flock of hens), did not rise to the occasion. Malto and the head of the New Hens had a chat, and Malto said, "Come on over to where we hang out, in the windbreak, and I'll show you how to raid the tomato patch when no one's looking!" but she just turned on her horny chicken leg and strutted back to the propane tank where the rest of the New Hens were hanging out...and the roosters figured, these chicks are no fun, and headed back to the windbreak.

So...rather than listening to the streetwise roosters when they mentioned that maybe it was time to roost for the night, the New Hens just wandered around confusedly and were thinking about roosting on the deck. Which meant that I had to go out and catch them individually, and lob them gently into the windbreak by the roosters. This time, they listened to the roosters, as was evidenced by the fluttering and flapping and tree branches rustling.

Mmmmm, acorn squash is ready in abundance! A little butter, a little brown sugar, a couple of marshmallows...ya, you betcha!
