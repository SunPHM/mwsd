Power and Ryding ousted from squash major

WebPosted Sun, 06 Nov 2005 19:12:34 EST
CBC Sports

Jonathon Power came into the U.S. Open squash tournament as the top seed but left with his hopes dashed.

The Montreal native and world No. 2 player was eliminated Sunday from the event in Boston after a five-game loss to Nick Matthew of England.

Top-seeded Canadian Jonathon Power was ousted from U.S. Open squash tournament on Sunday. (CP Photo)

A cramp in his right hand proved too much for Power to endure as he tired in the final game, giving Matthew a 6-11, 11-10, 3-11, 11-9, 6-2 victory in 77 minutes.

Canada's other hope in the main draw was also eliminated.

Toronto's Graham Ryding fell 11-6, 11-5, 11-6 in 36 minutes to Anthony Ricketts of Australia.

Matthew moves on to face compatriot Lee Beachill in Monday's semifinal, while Ricketts will take on Australia's David Palmer.
