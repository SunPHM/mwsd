Information

Yep. I said it. I really love working here. It doesn't get much better than 20 computers in one room, just waiting to be used.... mwmwmwahahaha.... oh. sorry.

What is ITW, you ask? (Well, actually, you're probably not asking it if you made it here, but we'll pretend anyway...) ITW is the Instructional Technology Workshop at the University of the South in Sewanee, Tennessee.

What do we do? A little of everything: namely web design, flash, digitizing multimedia, creating presentations for classes, and have fun. It's a great workplace. (Very avant garde with our beige, red, and blue walls, too.)

Why are we here? ITW is here to assist teachers in (as the name implies) instructing with technology. Yes, we are the means by which an otherwise technophobic professor can break into the realm of (surprisingly easy-to-use) technological instruction.

Why am _I_ here? As I have already stated, ITW is a great place to work. <shameless flattery>My boss, Vicki Sells-Lewallen, is an incredibly-easy-to-work-for-and-fun-to-be-around person</shameless flattery>, and runs an incredibly smooth operation. Any student at Sewanee with a technological bent who likes a fun place to work should be here. Can't stress that enough... trust me. :)

Where is ITW? ITW is located in the bottom of Jesse Ball DuPont Library here in Sewanee, in our new facility at ATC (heh. I don't even know exactly what that stands for.) Enough blabbing. I know what ya'll really want...
