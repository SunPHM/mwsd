Sports & Activities
 

Water sports

Every year France hosts the popular ‘Tour de France’ cycling race in July. Held over a period of three weeks the race covers most of France and ends in the center of Paris. Racing car and bike events are also very popular and draw large crowds.

Tennis originated in France in the 12th century and is a very popular sport. Soccer is another popular spectator sport in France. France produced players like Zidane, Papin and Cantona and won the World Cup in 1998 and the Eurocup in 2000. The French have also shown excellence in fencing and have developed many of the modern techniques of the sport. Rugby is also very popular, especially in southern France.

Pétanque or boules is a traditional lawn bowling game played with hollow, metal balls. Pelota is another regional game similar to squash.

The Summer Olympics were held in Paris in 1900 and 1924. The first Winter Olympics were held in Chamonix in 1924. France also hosted the winter games in 1968 in Grenoble and in 1992 in Albertville.

France has over 1880 miles of coastline where all types of water sports are available including surfing, diving and snorkeling. The French Alps offer excellent skiing with some of the world’s best-known resorts.
