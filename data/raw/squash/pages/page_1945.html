World-class squash stars coming to Ithaca

 by Steve Edgar

14850 Magazine > February 2004 Issue > World-class squash stars coming to Ithaca

Two of the best squash players on the planet will be in Ithaca this winter, at the third annual World Pro Squash Exhibition. John White, currently the second-ranked squash player in the world, is renowned for his bold attacking style. Originally from Australia, John (who's married to a former Lansing resident) currently lives in England. Graham Ryding, currently ranked number 14 in the world, is one of the most dynamic and athletic players on the world tour. Graham lives in Toronto.

On March 6th, at 7:00 pm, White and Ryding will play an exhibition game for an audience that will include reserved seating and general admission seating and standing room. Gold Patron and Sponsor package buyers will also be able to attend a clinic, limited to five players with John and five with Graham, on court with the stars, playing with them, and getting instruction. These packages are available only in advance; exhibition-only tickets will be available in advance or at the door.

  • Video clips from '03

The event will take place at Cornell University's Belkin Squash Courts, at the rear of the Reis Tennis Center on Pine Tree Road. The facility is near East Hill Plaza, southeast of central campus.

  • Belkin Squash Courts

Graham Ryding travels extensively on the Professional Squash Association Tour, playing tournaments in Europe, Asia, Australia, the Middle East, and North America. He's one of the top players in the world, with a career-high ranking of tenth in the world. White, too, tours with the Professional Squash Association, and visits to family often bring him to the Ithaca area.
