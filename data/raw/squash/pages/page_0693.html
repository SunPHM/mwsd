Squash Tour to Gibraltar, Seville and Fuengirola

After the very enjoyable and worthwhile tour to Venice in 2002, it was decided to go to Gibraltar and Andalucia in 2003. So on the last day of term, after the Carol Service, the team flew from Inverness to Gibraltar via Gatwick. Team members were: George Borozdin-Bidnell (Captain), Jonathan Hedley, Steve Wight, Leo Bendel, Ken Liang, Oliver Thompson. The team was accompanied by their squash coach, Mr Graham Broad.

The inaugural game was played that first night against the cream of the Rock’s juniors. This was fun but victories were hard to find and they retreated to bed in the Queen’s Hotel, one of Gibraltar’s finest!

The following day was spent hunting for bargains in the local shops followed by lunch with David Blunt, the Deputy Governor, former and future parent. Also present was Michael Pimm, a Cumming House boy from the 1980s. The views towards North Africa and Spain were marvellous and many thanks to Geirid and David for their hospitality and also to Alexander, a recent old boy from Bruce House, for showing the team around the Rock.

It was then on to Seville. The journey was slow but Senora Alba, the mother of Francisco in Round Square, was at the Hotel Andaluc to meet the team and direct them to the Antares Club - quite the most beautiful club any of them have ever visited. The opposition was strong and yet everyone played well and claimed some victories before Franscisco took them off to a fine restaurant.

Saturday was the most marvellous day. After three weeks of rain, the group hit four days of perfect weather and strolled around one of the most beautiful cities in Europe in perfect conditions. No one will forget the Cathedral, the largest Gothic building in Christendom, with its baroque High Altar, Columbus' Tomb, treasures and the views of the city and Guadalquivir from the Giraldo Tower. It was a hard climb to the top, but well worth it. As the team was coming down, they met some people struggling up with a wheelchair and volunteered to push it all the way back up to the top - a lovely example of Service in action.

On to the Alcazar, the Moorish Palace with such amazing mosaics and such peace in the gardens. After enjoying some tapas, the team went to the Antares Club for a practise and some more games. This time their opponents were of a similar standard and everyone had excellent games and enjoyed the challenge. A quick change took them to a restaurant for a meal where Jonathan Hedley played the bag pipes and presents were given to the Albas as a token of thanks for all they had done. To round off the day, the group then went to watch flamenco dancing - not for them the tourist version, but the real thing!

Sunday morning may be a time for Mass for many in Seville, but for the team it was time to go shopping at El Corte Ingles and then drive to Fuengirola via Malaga. Unfortunately, the local team had cancelled but a group went to a new Dutch club called Sport del Sol in Calahonda and got in some practice instead. Then it was time to head back to Gibraltar along the Costa del Sol, visiting Marbella and its port Puerto Banus, started by a gypsy and now home to millionaires and their yachts. It was difficult not to admire the rate of development whilst, at the same time, feeling despair at the poor planning.

On Monday the Blunts arranged a terrific tour of the Rock, visiting Europa Point, the Siege Tunnels, the caves, and of course the mandatory acquaintance with the Barbary apes. Then it was a short minibus ride to the team's final encounters at the Gibraltar club and a lot of close games due to the fact that everyone’s game had improved so much over the past few days. Many thanks are due to Barry Brindle for arranging so much for the team.

All-in-all, this was an extremely successful tour. The group played plenty of good squash games, met new people and made new friends, had many new experiences and, just as importantly, developed an excellent camaraderie amongst themselves. Roll on next year!
