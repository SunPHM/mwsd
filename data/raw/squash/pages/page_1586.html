Relaxing Krabi

 

The picturesque province of Krabi lies on the Andaman Coast is just under ninety minutes flying time from Bangkok and is an enchanting escape from Thailand's action packed capital.   The pristine beaches are fringed by towering limestone rock formations and the calm Andaman Sea horizon is dotted with lush tropical islands.  Much of the area surrounding Krabi is national park, and this includes both the mainland and over 80 islands.  The crystal clear waters are perfect for snorkelling, diving and sailing.

The Rayavadee Resort

The secluded Rayavadee Resort is famed for its award winning Thai style architecture and is nestled among palm trees in the spectacular setting of Phra Nang.  Surrounded by twenty six acres of coconut groves and bordered by three national park beaches, one of which has been rated as the best in the world, this elegant resort combines stunning scenery with luxurious accommodation.

Each air conditioned deluxe pavilion room has en suite bathroom, television, international direct dial telephone, minibar and in room safe.

Guests can enjoy reef walks, mountain treks, swim in the oversized beachfront swimming pool and play a game of tennis or squash.  The Rayavadee Spa offers aromatherapy, hydrotherapy, massage, reflexology, manicures, hairdressing and a relaxing sauna.

 

The resort has a number of restaurants to choose from serving Asian cuisine, fresh Seafood and light refreshments.

Krabi Thai Village Resort

Set in seven acres of landscaped tropical gardens the 4* Krabi Thai Village Resort has a traditional Thai design and is located within walking distance of one of Krabi’s most stunning beaches, Phra Nang Bay.

Each guest room is air conditioned and features a separate bath and shower, television, minibar, hairdryer, in room safe and private balcony or terrace.

The Krabi Thai Village Resort has three swimming pools, a children’s pool, a selection of shops, a tailor, a hair salon and Thai massage and spa facilities. During the evening why not relax in one of the hotels restaurants and bars and sample some excellent Thai and Western cuisine?
