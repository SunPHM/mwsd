Blue Waters Antigua

Hotel Details | Book Hotel | Book With Air


More photos
What to expect: Occupying 14 acres of garden hillside and oceanfront facing Soldiers Bay, this resort draws American and European couples and families seeking relaxation and an open-air West Indian ambience. Everyone dresses up after dark as musical tree frogs serenade and lights gleam on the bay.

Amenity highlights: The resort features two beaches separated by a slender, rocky point. A 100-yard activities beach is furnished with lounge chairs, thatch shades, and umbrellas. The other beach offers 50 yards of sand furnished with thatch shades. Between the larger beach and the point lies a 50-by-150-foot seaside promontory featuring a L-shape boardwalk furnished with umbrella tables and lounge chairs and a sunning lawn with lounge chairs and umbrellas. A 45-foot-long freeform main pool is surrounded by a sundeck containing lounge chairs and umbrellas and features a poolside bar in a little wood pavilion. A second pool measuring 35 by 15 feet is adults-only. A third pool was added in October 2004, featuring a new bar, sundecks, and loungers.

Insider tip: At the end of a rocky point separating the resort's two beaches lies a wood pavilion that provides a private, tranquil spot for enjoying sunsets.



Cocobay Hotel

Hotel Details | Book Hotel | Book With Air

What to expect: Restricted to guests aged 12 or older except December 19—January 7, Cocobay Hotel offers holidays with a theme of fresh air and and relaxation instead of air conditioning and activities. There are no group activities except for guided three-times-weekly nature walks through and near the hotel's 10 acres of garden grounds.
Amenity highlights: The turquoise Caribbean Sea remains calm in most weather along a 305-m (1,000-ft) beach furnished with thatched shades. Drink service is provided on the beach twice daily. The resort supplies snorkeling gear, kayaks, and small sailboats. An infinity-edge pool bordered by a wood sundeck overlooks the sea from the edge of the bluff occupied by the resort.Also situated on the bluff's edge is a six-person spa tub.

More photos
Insider tip: The resort's beach extends along one side of a narrow strip of land. On the other side is a nature preserve that includes a lagoon and offers bird-watching opportunities.

Galley Bay All Inclusive

Hotel Details | Book Hotel | Book With Air

What to expect: Restricted to guests aged 16 and older except during the Christmas/New Year season, 40-acre Galley Bay All Inclusive resort attracts an international clientele seeking stress-free holidays and ample room for privacy. There are no group activities or motorized water sports. Resortwear (but not men's jackets) is required in bars and restaurants after 7 PM.
Amenity highlights: The resort extends along the full length of an 800-m (875-yd) beach washed by the turquoise Caribbean Sea. Undisturbed by outsiders, guests sunbathe, walk on the beach, and ride fat-tired bicycles on the sand. A freeform pool has a multi-level rock waterfall. Guests feed fruit to resident land turtles, bird-watch and jog around the resort's bird-sanctuary lagoon, and dance to live music after dark.

More photos
Insider tip: The resort maintains an aloe vera garden for treatment of sunburn and the occasional jellyfish sting.

St. James's Club - All Inclusive

Hotel Details | Book Hotel | Book With Air

What to expect: Popular with families during summer and holidays and with couples during winter, this resort attracts an international clientele. Because it occupies an entire peninsula of 40 hectares (100 acres), this hotel can seem like an island onto itself, with tranquil Mamora Bay to its west and the windswept Atlantic Ocean to the east. Green hills provide a backdrop for beaches of white sand.

Amenity highlights: Two beaches, Mamora Beach and Coco Beach, each almost one km (a half-mile) long are protected by a barrier reef. Serving as the water-sports center, Mamora Bay's crescent beach is lapped by calm water and includes two wood docks ending in large sundecks furnished with lounge chairs. From the second beach, snorkelers head out to explore the coral offshore. Four pools, including one for children, provide freshwater swimming. The main pool features a 270-degree sea view. The resort's villa section includes a three-level bay-view pool and the fourth pool overlooks the open sea.



More photos

Insider tip: Every Thursday night, the resort's evening dress code is relaxed at The Docksider, on the edge of Mamora Bay, when a steel band plays and a limbo dancer entertains during a Caribbean barbecue buffet.

Coconut Beach Club

Hotel Details | Book Hotel | Book With Air

What to expect: Restricted to guests aged 12 or older except from Dec. 19 through Jan. 7, Coconut Beach Club offers relaxation instead of activities. The only group activity is a guided nature walk through the resort's 15 hectares (37 acres), which include a bird-sanctuary lagoon.
Amenity highlights: Coconut Beach Club is arrayed along a sandy 350-m (383-yd) beach used primarily by resort guests. Hammocks sway from palm trees on a lawn by the shore. A small, kidney-shaped pool includes a wooden sundeck. Use of snorkeling gear, small sailboats, and two-person kayaks is complimentary to all guests.

More photos
Insider tip: Visitors and locals from all over Antigua gather Sunday afternoons at Shirley Heights overlooking English Harbour for an outdoor party highlighted by a panoramic view of the sunset. The resort offers shuttle service to the party, which is 24.25 km (15 mi) away, for a surcharge.

Dian Bay Resort And Spa

Hotel Details | Book Hotel | Book With Air

What to expect: Restricted to guests aged 16 or older, the locally owned-and-operated Dian Bay Resort attracts couples seeking restful holidays. Public by law, the two beaches used by guests are effectively private due to isolation. Grounds are thickly planted with palm trees and flowering gardens. There are no group activities other than power walks on request to Devil's Bridge.

Note: Because of stairways to the pools, beach, and restaurant, this hillside resort is not suitable for people with physical disabilities.


Amenity highlights: Dian Bay Resort stands on a terraced hillside above a narrow turquoise bay of the Atlantic Ocean. Two peninsulas form the nearly enclosed bay, protecting it from ocean swells. A small beach is at the base of the hill. Upon request, the hotel also provides complimentary motor-skiff transport to picturesque Lover's Beach across the bay. A three-level complex comprising three freeform pools is terraced into the hotel’s hillside.
Insider tip: Located on the ocean side of a narrow peninsula across the bay, the Devil's Bridge limestone arch is a prime island attraction. Swells spouting through blowholes can be seen from the resort. By road, Devil's Bridge is 2.25 km (1.5 mi) away. But guests may request complimentary motor-skiff transport that takes less than a minute.

Grand Pineapple Beach by Occidental

Hotel Details | Book Hotel | Book With Air

What to expect: Set along the beach and up a hillside, with access by flagstone paths and wood staircases, the 25-acre, West Indian-style, open-air resort draws families and couples seeking day-and-night activities and get-away rest and relaxation provided by a secluded beach.

Amenity highlights: Furnished with thatched shades and lounge chairs, a beach of light-colored sand stretches 500 yards along an Atlantic Ocean bay protected by two rock-cliff points that drop off into the turquoise water. Swimming, snorkeling, and water sports are available off the beach. Two pools provide freshwater swimming: a large, two-level, freeform pool; and a 30-by-20 feet rectangle.



Hawksbill Beach All Inclusive by Rex Resorts

Hotel Details | Book Hotel | Book With Air

What to expect: Hawksbill Beach All Inclusive by Rex Resorts attracts families in summer and during Christmas/New Year season. Otherwise it largely draws couples seeking tranquil, television-free holidays with lots of fresh air, space to be alone, and uncrowded beaches. Throughout the resort after 6 PM, adult guests are required to dress in resortwear, which for men means long pants and collared shirts.
Amenity highlights: Four beaches range in length from 75 m (82 yds) to 400 m (437 yds). One offers water sports. Another is quiet. The shortest is located in a horseshoe-shaped cove and is favored by adults. The longest beach is located well away from buildings and is clothing-optional. For freshwater fun there's a little beachfront pool measuring approximately 9.25 x 7.5 m (30 x 25 ft). Guided garden tours are provided twice weekly.

More photos
Insider tip: Known for its spectacular views, Shirley Heights (24.25 km/15 mi away) draws hordes of visitors, especially for Thursday and Sunday barbecues and dancing. Guests seeking views only should go in the early morning by taxi.

Inn At English Harbour

Hotel Details | Book Hotel | Book With Air

What to expect: Sheltered at the rear by steep green hills, the resort offers 19 acres of privacy among expansive lawns studded with palm trees and fringed by flowering bushes. Although children are welcome, the ambience of secluded tranquility, along with the absence of air conditioning, primarily appeals to couples seeking relaxing beach holidays featuring sea breezes and soft tropical air.
Amenity highlights: The resort's beach frontage stretches 100 yards and up to 40 yards deep along Freeman's Bay, a nearly circular body that's part of English Harbour. Nearby lies a 65-by-45-foot freshwater swimming pool with an infinity edge overlooking the beach. The resort's water taxi provides complimentary service to the historic sites, shops, and restaurants of Nelson's Dockyard, two minutes away across the harbor.
Insider tip: One of Antigua's most popular attractions, a 487-foot bluff named Shirley Heights offers a spectacular view of English Harbour and the Caribbean Sea and lies 1.25 miles from the resort. Visitors and locals gather here on Sunday afternoons for an outdoor dancing/partying ritual before and after sunset.

Jolly Beach Resort All Inclusive

Hotel Details | Book Hotel | Book With Air

What to expect: Popular with Europeans as well as Americans, this 16-hectare (40-acre) resort attracts active couples, singles, and families seeking a beach holiday next to the turquoise Caribbean Sea.
Amenity highlights: Featuring fountains and a large swim-up bar section that includes small tables and semicircular "booths," a freeform pool extends 76 meters (250 feet) along the beach. Water cascades down a huge, tiered rock structure past a 12-person spa tub into the pool. A second freeform pool, also along the beachfront, curls for 27 meters (90 feet). After swimming, guests can nap in hammocks strung under an open-air structure.
Insider tip: Under an agreement with the resort, local vendors sell their wares on the beach at what is called Crab Hole Village. Every day, vendors open up the doors of their wooden stalls and display handcrafts, beach wraps, t-shirts, and jewelry for purchase. They also offer hair braiding (surcharge.)

Blue Heron by Rex Resorts

Hotel Details | Book Hotel | Book With Air

What to expect: Although children are welcome, this resort appeals primarily to couples seeking relaxing holidays in an open-air ambience. In keeping with its low-key approach, the resort does not offer group activities.
Amenity highlights: The resort's beach frontage stretches 125 yards wide and 50 yards deep, part of half-mile Turner's Beach on which there are no other resorts. For freshwater swimming there's a 32-by-20-foot pool. Use of snorkeling gear, sailboats, pedal boats, and windsurfers is part of the All Inclusive Plan. Independent vendors charge for water skiing, banana boating, glass-bottom-boat trips, and snorkeling and scuba-diving excursions.
Insider tip: Adjacent to the resort, a casual beach restaurant popular with cruise-ship passengers and others touring the island offers local specialties such as grilled lobster, curried conch, and fish cakes.

Grand Royal Antiguan Beach Resort

Hotel Details | Book Hotel | Book With Air

What to expect: An open-air lobby containing a garden and a series of fountain pools exemplifies the restful ambience that attracts couples and families to the resort, which offers expansive grounds, tropical gardens, and seclusion from other developments.
Amenity highlights: The resort features a 125-yard-section of an undeveloped half-mile beach fringing oblong Deep Bay. A pool has a swim-up bar and children's section. Use of snorkeling gear, Sunfish sailboats, windsurfers, kayaks, water bikes, and pedal boats is complimentary. Other watersports such as parasailing and personal watercraft are available for surcharges.
Insider tip: Visitors and residents from all over Antigua gather Sunday afternoons at Shirley Heights overlooking English Harbour for an outdoor dancing/partying ritual that includes a panoramic view of the sunset. The resort shuttles guests to the party for a surcharge.

Halcyon Cove by Rex Resorts

Hotel Details | Book Hotel | Book With Air

What to expect: Spacious and tranquil, the resort conveys a tropical ambience, with coralstone columns in an open-air lobby and grounds graced by palm trees and flowering gardens--all creating a relaxing atmosphere for families and couples.
Amenity highlights: The resort's beach frontage extends 275 meters (300 yards) wide and 32 meters (35 yards) deep and leads to a rustic, open-air restaurant raised above the sea at the end of a pier. In all, the beach stretches a 800 meters (a half-mile) along Dickenson Bay. There's also a 23-meter (75-foot) kidney-shape pool. Use of kayaks, snorkeling gear, windsurfers, and sailboats are part of the All Inclusive Plan. Rental watersports include personal watercraft, parasailing, and scuba diving.
Insider tip: Visitors and residents from all over Antigua gather Sunday afternoons at Shirley Heights 22.5 km (14 miles) away and overlooking English Harbour for an outdoor dancing/partying ritual that features a panoramic view of the sunset.

Trade Winds Hotel

Hotel Details | Book Hotel | Book With Air

What to expect: Popular with business travelers and airline crews, the intimate Trade Winds Hotel also attracts leisure travelers seeking off-beach pricing and a tranquil, intimate atmosphere. Palm trees and flowering gardens border buildings.
Amenity highlights: A 10.75-m (35-ft) freeform pool with gentle waterfall cools sunbathers using a deck bordered by thick screens of bougainvillea. A sundeck on the edge of the hotel's hilltop near the pool provides broad sea views. Sea views from a rooftop sundeck are even more panoramic. The hotel's restaurant offers European and West Indian dishes. Complimentary shuttles operate to Dickenson Bay Beach on a regular schedule throughout the day.
Insider tip: On Friday nights, Dickenson Bay Beach becomes a hot spot, with music blaring from colorful restaurant/bars and a band playing reggae on an open-air bandstand.

Alagana House

Hotel Details | Book Hotel | Book With Air

The Alagana House is a private retreat on the beautiful island of Antigua. Awaken each morning to turquoise ocean views just beyond your windows. Sip your tea or coffee on your private patio or balcony and share a quiet moment with the tiny, brightly colored birds flitting by. Wander barefoot to the expansive covered verandah. Settle into the deep cushions of a bamboo sofa, and survey the changing panorama Falmouth village and its broad, yachtfilled harbour below you. Have a dip in your private pool, whose waterfall seems to drop directly into the sea. And then, only when you are ready, begin to plan your day of exploration or relaxation.
