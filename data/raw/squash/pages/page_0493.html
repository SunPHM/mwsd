13/03/2004
 Cassie ties the Knot in North Walsham

Cassie Jackman was "the happiest woman in the world" when she married in her hometown ...

ADAM GRETTON reports in the Eastern Daily Press
Photo from Norwich Evening News

Squash champ Cassie
weds Aussie squeeze

It was an unusually low-key wedding for a world number one sportswoman.

But Norfolk squash star Cassie Jackman said she was the happiest woman in the world yesterday when she got married in her hometown.

Miss Jackman became Ms Jackman when she tied the knot with Australian Matt Thomas at a small ceremony with close family and friends at North Walsham register office.

Cassie, 31, who recently reclaimed her world No1 ranking and won a record sixth British National Squash Championship said yesterday that getting married for the second time could not compare with her sports success.

“It has been the biggest and best day of my life. This year my squash has been great but squash has been the furthest thing from my mind today. I'm just glad that everything went to plan,” she said.

While you might expect a professional sportswoman to meet her future partner through her chosen sport. The couple's whirlwind romance started when the England Squash Ladies team went clubbing in Norwich prior to the 2002 Commonwealth Games, in Manchester.

“Matt was travelling on a working visa and stopped off in Norwich.

He asked if we were celebrating and we started talking and hit it off from there. It took a bit of convincing that we were the England squash ladies because we were all a bit drunk!”

In fact 28-year-old Matt, who is a builder, had never heard of Cassie or played squash before he met his future wife.

“We have been on the squash court once but he is more of a cricketer. Since we have been together he has been really supportive and comes to watch me play when he can.”

Cassie's parents, Mick and Pat, brother Luke, and grandmother as well as Matt's mum, dad, brother and sister, who flew over from Melbourne, Australia for the special occasion, joined the happy couple at North Walsham yesterday.

Cassie, who was brought up in the town, now lives in Horsford, near Norwich. But the couple will have no time for a honeymoon. Cassie will be back training on Monday in preparation for the Texas Open, which takes place in the first week of April.

The pair will settle for a good old knees up with friends and family tonight and plan to have a belated honeymoon in May.

Ms Jackman has made 50 appearances in international squash finals but has been plagued by serious back injuries throughout her career.

“My aim is to play in the next Commonwealth Games in two years time and by the time I am 33 I think will have had enough.”

After which the Jackman family might hear the patter of tiny feet.

“I hope so. When I finish playing squash having children is something to look forward to,” she said.

Adam Gretton
