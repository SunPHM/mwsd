Autumn Squash Ravioli With Sage Parmesan Butter Sauce

By Chef Jamie Roraback

WEST HARTFORD, Conn. -- Makes about 24 ravioli

For the filling

1 small butternut or acorn squash
2 tbsp. extra virgin olive oil
4 tbsp. fresh grated Parmesan cheese
24 Small round wonton wrappers
½ Stick, unsalted butter
2 tbsp. fresh sage, chopped
Salt and black pepper to taste

Split squash in half lengthwise, scoop out seeds, and place squash on sheet pan with skin facing up. Roast in 350 F oven for about 45 minutes to an hour until tender.

When tender, scoop out squash flesh into bowl. Stir first measure of olive oil and Parmesan cheese, season to taste with salt and black pepper, and allow filling to cool.

Lay out the wonton wrappers, place a small scoop of the filling in the center of each wrapper. Lightly brush water on only half of the diameter of the wonton wrapper. Fold over filling to create a half circle and pinch edges tightly to seal.

Prepare sauce by heating the second measure of olive oil and butter together, add the fresh sage, cook gently for 1 minute, and season to taste with salt and pepper.

In a large pot of boiling salted water, add ravioli and cook for about 3 minutes until ravioli are tender. Gently drain, and toss with the butter sauce. Arrange on plates or a platter, garnish with Parmesan cheese, sage leaves, and perhaps the seeds of the squash toasted in oven.

Enjoy!

Courtesy: Connecticut Culinary Institute
