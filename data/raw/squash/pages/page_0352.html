SQUASH BLOSSOMS IN HOUSTON

Tired of the “underappreciated” status of their beloved sport, a small but loyal group of squash players at the Downtown Club at the Met in Houston found a way to increase awareness of squash in the area. Last spring, the group, headed by Board of Governors member Rishad Alikhan, organized an international squash tournament on the club’s eight world-class squash courts. The Aon Texas Open Squash Tournament, a five-day event using a 16-person draw format, attracted many of the world’s top female players, and offered a $30,000 purse (plus an additional $5,000 to local charities). For many members who attended, it was their first time to watch a game of squash, a racket sport similar to racquetball, but using a longer racket, a smaller, less bouncy ball, and a slightly wider court. “It was a wonderful event,” Alikhan says. “Other members at the club were so intrigued by the championship-level play that they would just stop what they were doing, sit down, and watch the tournament.” The event was such a success that the group is working with the Women’s International Squash Players Association (WISPA) to schedule another tournament in 2004, with hopes of making it an annual event. — Anne Clarrissimeaux
