The Annual Tournament is over now and the results are as follows:

Mens Open: Monsoor Khan beat Tim Taylor 3-0 (Full details of all matches in this event can be seen below)

Ladies' Open: Judy Williams beat Debbie Salman 3-0

Team Handicap: Craig Rossiter beat Monsoor Khan 2-1

Club Handicap: Mark Lemon beat Debbie Salman 2-1

Vintage: Berge Kay beat Paul Davison 3-2

Veterans: Tim Taylor beat Bruce Cade 3-0 (event took place in February)

 



Without a ball being hit there are already two seeded players who have dropped out of the mens open.

The biggest shock was the news that, number one seed, George Saunders-Singer has withdrawn. This was due to an unexpected offer of a free holiday, that unfortunately fell in the middle of the tournament. After a brief amount of consideration there was only going to be one winner, and suffice to say it wasn't the squash!

The other seed to pull out was Jeremy Vivian (13) [picture]. Although, by his own admission, he was always going to struggle against the quality of the higher seeds, Jeremy would always guarantee a valiant battle in his matches. However this year he has been defeated by a nagging elbow injury and despite being ambidextrous, the absence of any sort of backhand in his shot repertoire has forced his withdrawal.

As luck would have it there were two players (Chris Law and Allan Bransby) who just missed the draw deadline and I have been able to slot them straight in to take the places of George and Jeremy respectively.



(Report written Wed 21st April)

The prelims took place on Tuesday 20th April. They ran very smoothly with all competitors turning up nice and promptly (thank-you to all concerned, it makes my life a lot easier!).

It was nice to see unfamiliar faces taking part in this competition for the first time; a point that our squash Secretary Barry Cullen [picture] made whilst taking a couple of the games in, from the viewing gallery, with the usual pint of 'best' in hand!

Julian Mann gave an experienced performance to beat Umar Alvi 3-0

Andy Veasey (Finchley Manor tennis champion) played a lot of volleys and drop shots to beat Harris Makhdumi 3-0

Charles Hiscock displayed too much power for Kevin Henriques, winning 3-0

The young and fit Neil Collett defeated the experienced and wily Vet Robin Mace 3-0 in an intriguing match

Finally Phil Hyslop beat Roy Brown 3-0, but not before almost breaking his leg by running into the wall racket first!

All in all a good set of matches watched by a surprising number of spectators.



(Report written Fri 23rd April)

The top half of the draw played their 1st round matches last night (Thurs 22nd April). Again the the matches were well supported with the crowd cheering on the underdogs in true British tradition. However there were no upsets to see and all seeds progressed to the next round.

Chris Law eased his way past Julian Mann winning 3-0

Calan Horsman had a good tournament debut, especially in the first game, against Damien Cannon. Damien was up to the newcomer's challenge and eventually won convincingly 3-0

Kevin Silver won a bad tempered match against Pat Earl 3-1. This was always destined to be a competitive fixture with these two fiery and argumentative characters! A change of marker was needed half way through to prevent all out war, but Kevin held his nerve to finish Pat off in four games.

Bruce Cade [picture] ran around a lot to beat another newcomer Michael Johnson 3-0. Michael is yet another promising young talent to appear out of nowhere and we hope he continues to improve and force his way into one of the lower teams.

Allan Bransby eventually turned up (a little more prompt for the next round please Allan!) and put in a strong performance to beat Bernard Roberts 3-0.

Andy Veasey, despite his accurate tennis shots, was no match for Faraz Khan, the number 3 seed. Andy put in a spirited performance and never gave up, but Faraz was just too experienced to lose this one. Faraz won in straight games 3-0

Probably the most promising new comer to emerge so far is Mark Lemon. Mark apparently plays off a golf handicap of 5 and looked like giving Stuart Slater a stern challenge in the first game. However Stuart used his power to smash Mark off court in the latter games winning in the end 3-0.

Finally it really did look like an upset was on the cards in the match of Tim Taylor [picture] (1st team player/captain and number 5 seed) vs Charles Hiscock (3rd team player and unseeded). Charles took the first game with a powerhouse display, however it seemed like it took all of his energy as he visibly faded in the next few games. Tim eventually won 3-1, much to the dismay of the gallery (sorry Tim!)

Thank-you to all the people who have been required to mark. Nobody (yet!) has let me down and failed to show. It is very much appreciated by me and more importantly the competitors.

Remainder of the 1st round matches (bottom half of the draw) to be played on Tues 27th April. Please come and support.


Report written Thurs 29th April

The bottom half of the draw played their 1st round matches two nights ago (Tues 27th April). Sorry about the slight delay in updating this site, I fell asleep last night before I remembered I had something to do!

A disappointing turn out, not from the point of view of spectators but players failing to appear (FTA). In fact 50% of the scheduled matches were awarded as walkovers. These matches being:

Dave Hershman awarded a walkover against Michael Howard (FTA reason unknown)
Paul Salman awarded walkover against John Cohen (FTA injured)
Brendan Murphy awarded walkover against Fergus Traynor (FTA got wrong date)
Mike Fitzpatrick awarded a walkover against David Cohen (FTA injured)

Still the remaining matches were hard fought and at least one was very close.

Simon Cohen put on a commanding show to beat Neil Collett in straight games 3-0

Rick Prior was tested but not troubled by Anthony Kearns, winning 3-0

Monzoor Khan showed why he is #2 seed, beating Phil Hyslop effortlessly 3-0

But the game of the round was definitely Craig Rossiter (2nd team player, #8 seed and last years finalist) vs Ian Baker (3rd team player & unseeded). Modesty isn't one of Craig's best attributes; in fact he's already got his diary arranged to make sure he's available to play in the final on 22nd May!! This made this match a very exciting spectacle indeed, as those who know Ian and have seen him play recently, know full well how much his game has come on in the last 12 months.

Ian took the first game 10-9, before Craig leveled making it 1-1. Ian then went on to take the third game, with the full support of the balcony egging him on. Unfortunately for Ian, Craig got a couple of lucky nicks before leveling at 2-2. By this time Ian was visibly tiring and Craig asserted his fitness and experience winning the match 3-2.


Report written Sun 9th May

The majority of the second round matches were held on Thursday 6th May. Chris Law vs Damien Cannon is re-arranged to Tues 11th May due to Chris being away on holiday. Faraz Khan vs Allan Bransby has also been re-arranged to Mon 10th (tomorrow night) due to Faraz being ill.

Monzoor Khan was awarded a walkover in his scheduled match against Mike Fitzpatrick who failed to appear (reason unknown).

Bruce Cade won a disjointed match vs Kevin Silver. Bruce, who is the master of psyche, immediately employed all his tactics and didn't allow Kevin to settle and get into his stride. He slowed the match right down between points to such an extent the referee (Pete Hughes) reminded both players that play was supposed to be continuous! Kevin never really looked like pressurising Burce enough and Bruce ended up winning the match in straight games 3-0.

Craig Rossiter beat Rick Prior 3-1 in a hard fought battle. Rick took the first game 9-7 before Craig stormed back taking the next 9-0! The next game was again close but Craig again was the more consistent winning 9-6. The fourth game was very close with several handouts when at 5-4 Craig brushed against Rick's arm and, with a theatrical dive, apparently knocked himself out. Craig (glass jaw to his friends) eventually returned on court after a 10 minute injury break. Rick then took the next few points giving him a couple of game points. Despite a golden opportunity to win the game and take the match to a 5th game, Rick failed to play a simple drop shot and instead played it straight back to Craig at the back of the court, losing the point. Craig then went on to win the game and match 3-1.

The surprise of the round was Brendan Murphy's 3-1 victory against last years winner Simon Cohen. Simon took the first game comfortably before Brendan found his rhythm taking the next. Simon made a lot of unforced errors in the next two games, but this is taking nothing away from a very assured and clinical performance by the newcomer.

The final two matches I did not witness and so can't write anything about; other than Tim Taylor is into the next round having beaten Stuart Slater 3-0 (never mind Stuart, at least you can concentrate on your tennis now!) and Dave Hershman is also through having beaten Paul Salman 3-0.


Chris Law played a faultless game against Bruce Cade winning in straight games 3-0. Although Chris was lacking in match fitness, he did not give Bruce a chance to pro-long the match and played some lovely strokes to win convincingly in the end.

Brendan Murphy pulled off the shock of the round beating an in form Dave Hershman 3-0. Dave appeared a little jaded, having played a tough team match the night before, but that's not taking anything away from a sound performance by the newcomer.

Monzoor Khan put in another power-house display to beat Craig Rossiter 3-0. Monzoor didn't allow Craig to settle and it took Craig a couple of games just to get used to how fast the ball was coming back at him. Even though Craig guessed right for a number of Monzoor's drives, the sheer power and accuracy was too much for him.

Tim Taylor played a tight game to beat Allan Bransby 3-0

Tim Taylor's fitness was the critical factor in his 3-2 victory against Chris Law. Chris Law played some lovely squash to go 2-0 up. Tim appeared to have no answer to Chris's superior shots and match play. However Chris started to puff noticeably in the second game and by mid way through the third he was clearly flagging. Tim was spurred on by this and took the next two games comfortably. Chris fought hard in the 5th, but his fitness, in the end, let him down.

Despite a valiant effort by Brendan Murphy, Monzoor Khan powered to a straight games victory winning 3-0. The 1st game was very close, but Monzoor contested every point and in the end demoralised Brendan by chasing down even the tightest of shots.


To those who have seen the majority of Monzoor's games during this tournament, it was no surprise to see him put in yet another merciless performance. Every point was chased by Monzoor regardless of how good a shot Tim played, and the majority of the time he got them back. Tim contested the 1st game well but it went downhill for him after that. In the final game Tim, uncharacteristically put a series of shots in the tin or out of court. This could be explained by the pressure Monzoor had exerted on Tim for the whole match, forcing him to play for hopeful winners. In the end Monzoor was the rightful winner of the tournament having not conceded a game in any of the previous rounds.
