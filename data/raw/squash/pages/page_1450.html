Sunday, 19 November, 2000, 23:03 GMT

England through with ease




Top-seeded England demolished Italy 3-0 in their opening pool match of the Eye Group Women's World Team Squash Championship in Sheffield.

They failed to concede a game and dropped a total of only seven points in the whole match, which they wrapped up in just 54 minutes.

The winning England trio included Rebecca Macree, who was called up at the last moment as a replacement for Sue Wright, who pulled out because of a relapse of viral pneumonia.

The selection of Macree, ranked 15 in the world, ahead of Suzanne Horner and Fiona Greaves, ranked eight and 10 respectively, created a stir.

But the England coach David Pearson had no choice as the rules of the competition require the substitute to be ranked below the player she replaces.

Macree, making her international debut, struck the first blow for England, beating Veronica Favero, before the match was wrapped up by Linda Charman.

Scotland also made a winning start to their campaign, beating Japan 3-0. Australia, the holders and England's main rivals, comfortably beat Wales 3-0, but Ireland lost 3-0 to the Netherlands.
