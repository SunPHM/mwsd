Cafe New Preston

nestled in the Litchfield Hills a quarter of a mile below Lake Waramaug is one of the prettiest eateries in Western Connecticut, as stated by Greg Morago of The Hartford Courant. "This intimate restaurant beams with charm and casual sophistication and the Chef-Owner, Drew Stichter, does wonders with the simplest bistro fare."



The food is New American. Drew is a two-time James Beard Award winner, and the fall menu is now in place with such favorites as a Wild Mushroom Saute with an Onion Custard, Butternut Squash Ravioli on an Apple Brandy Cream, and Cafe specialities of Chicken Portobello, Pistachio Salmon, Sweetbreads Dijonaise, Pizzas and Pastas. The weekend brunch is a must, accompanied by an afternoon of antiquing in the quaint village of New Preston.




Outside Patio Dining is winding down and fireside dining is starting up. The restaurant has applied for a beer and wine license, but at this point is a B.Y.O.B.

Master Card and Visa are accepted.
The dining room is handicapped-accessible and non-smoking.
