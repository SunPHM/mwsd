Chinese Eggplant

Chinese Eggplant can be distinguished from other popular Asian eggplant varieties by its color and size. Chinese Eggplant is usually lavender to deep maroon and is even longer than the darker purple Japanese Eggplant. Although Chinese Eggplant is botanically a fruit, it’s more commonly used as a vegetable and resembles a small zucchini. Sweeter and more tender than regular eggplant, Chinese Eggplant has fewer, smaller seeds. Generally left with the skin on, Chinese Eggplant is rarely peeled since its thinner, more pleasant tasting skin preserves its texture, taste and shape.



Pul Qua

Actually a variety of squash from the cucumber family, Pul Qua or Opo is popular in Asian cuisine. Similar to more familiar types of squash, Pul Qua can often be used in much the same way. A long, thin squash with smooth skin, Pul Qua resembles a large zucchini that is yellow-green in color. It makes a good accompaniment to stir-fries and soups.

A sweet and succulent member of the cabbage family, Melissa's Napa Cabbage, also known as Chinese Cabbage, is oblong in shape with tightly packed, crinkly, veined leaves that are cream colored with celadon (yellow-greenish) green tips.

Melissa's Napa Cabbage has a mild flavor, like a cross between cabbage, iceberg lettuce, with no strong cabbage odor, and a shorter cooking time than regular cabbage.

Generally available year-round, Melissa's Napa Cabbage is very versatile and can be eaten raw in salads, or sautéed, baked or braised in soups, stir-frys, and casseroles. It can also be pickled for kim chee.

Melissa's Napa Cabbage can be kept refrigerated for approximately one week (away from ethylene producing fruits, e.g., apples). Rinse, drain, and shake dry for maximum freshness before using in your favorite recipe.

One of the most frequently used ingredients for adding spice to Asian as well as Indian cooking, Ginger imparts a delightful flavor to all foods. Fresh Ginger, available with a smooth skin and comparatively mild flavor, is known as young or Spring Ginger. Mature ginger has a tougher, textured skin, a very fibrous flesh, and a peppery, sweet-hot flavor.

Grown in China, India, Africa and Jamaica, Ginger is available year-round and will keep for at least a week unpeeled in the refrigerator and up to 2 months in the freezer tightly wrapped. A slice the size of a quarter equals 1 teaspoon minced, which in addition to grated, slivered and ground are the most popular methods to utilize this versatile root.

When selecting Melissa's Fresh Ginger, look for a smooth skin with a spicy and fresh fragrance. Fresh Ginger is an ingredient for stir-fries, soups, curries, confections, and beverages, often as the star ingredient in classics like gingerbread, gingersnaps, ginger ale, and ginger beer.

Melissa's Crystallized Ginger is candied ginger for adding a sweet and spicy flavor to sweet sauces, stir fry dishes, fruit desserts, and any recipe that calls for candied fruit.
