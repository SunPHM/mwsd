A home that hits you for six!

Mar 25 2003

Howzat! Builder Paul Horsley rescued a dilapidated cricket pavilion and turned it into a high-class contemporary living space

By Jenny Parkin, The Huddersfield Daily Examiner

 

IN VICTORIAN times, ladies and gentlemen would have staged stately gatherings there to watch the cricket.

Later, it was a venue for a low-key game of squash.

Most recently it was forgotten and derelict until Paul Horsley, of Honley-based PJ Homes Ltd, saw its potential.

Now, the pavilion overlooking the Robert Ashton Memorial Park in Meltham has a whole new lease of life - as a stylish and contemporary loft-style home.

Its focal point, seen here in our pictures, is the first-floor open-plan kitchen, dining area and living room, accessed via a spiral staircase and metal bridge.

It's crowned with a glass roof and exposed, original metal trusses.

Natural light floods it in the daytime - and at night spotlights and lit alcoves create a striking effect.

The ground floor has a Mediterranean feel with Turkish limestone flooring.

The master bedroon has an en suite bathroom with specially-made glass fittings.

A second bedroom, has built-in wardrobes.
