Lots of vegetables in one-dish chicken provencale
Butternut squash adds colour and sweetness to this rib sticking Skillet Chicken and Vegetables Provencal that cooks up in minutes. (CP PHOTO/Ho)
This country dish is a colourful stove-top stew chock full of healthy vegetables. Roughly cut the onion, green pepper and mushrooms for a rustic look. Serve over garlic mashed potatoes or pasta, such as rotini or penne if desired, and with a crust French baguette.

The term provencale refers to dishes prepared in the style of Provence, a region in southeastern France. Garlic, tomatoes and olive oil are the major trademark of provencale cooking.

Chicken and Vegetables Provencale

30 mL (2 tbsp) olive oil
1 onion, chopped
2 cloves garlic, minced
450 g (1 lb) boneless skinless chicken (breasts or thighs) cubed
5 mL (1 tsp) dried thyme leaves
2 mL (1/2 tsp) dried rosemary leaves, lightly crushed
1 mL ( 1/4 tsp) pepper
125 mL (1/2 cup) chicken broth
1 540 mL (19 oz) can stewed tomatoes
500 mL (2 cups) cubed peeled butternut squash
1 sweet green pepper, roughly chopped
375 mL (11/2 cups) quartered mushrooms
45 mL (3 tbsp) chopped fresh parsley

In large skillet or Dutch oven, heat oil over medium-high heat. Add onion and garlic; cook until lightly softened, about 3 minutes. Add chicken; stir-fry about 1 minute or until lightly coloured. Sprinkle with thyme, rosemary and pepper; cook, stirring for 30 seconds.

Stir in broth, scraping up any brown bits on bottom of pan. Stir in tomatoes, squash, green pepper and mushrooms; bring to boil. Reduce heat, cover and simmer, stirring occasionally, until squash is tender, about 30 minutes. Sprinkle with parsley.

Makes 4 servings.

Nutrition per serving: 295 calories; 31 g protein; 11 g fat; 18 g carbohydrate.
