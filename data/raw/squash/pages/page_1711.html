The 2000 Cleveland Open Nov 3-5 2000 By Shank Pfister
After some years in absentia, the Cleveland Open has made a resounding comeback in the last two years. Due in large part to the arrival and subsequent hard work of Scott Denne, the tournament and its organizers hope to regain its prior position of importance on the early season tournament schedule.

Cleveland Open Heritage
For many years the Cleveland Open under the direction of Ham Biggar was a major event on the Hardball professional circuit. While squash in Cleveland has suffered during the transition to the international game, the game has enjoyed a strong resurgence with the construction of seven softball courts in the Cleveland metro area. In conjunction with this resurgence on the ammeter level, there has been a concerted effort to resurrect an annual professional event to spur greater interest and participation in the sport.

After a very successful inaugural event in 1999, there was much anticipation within the Cleveland squash community for an equally successful sophomore year. Scheduled for the last weekend in October, the Professional draw again featured a strong international contingent of players from both the PSA and PSA-NA tours.

Strong Field of Play
Headlining this years' event was Brett Martin. Although officially retired from tournament play, Brett nevertheless was quite rightly afforded the number one seed. Other players of note included second seeded Nathan Dugan, currently ranked number 2 on the PSA-NA tour; Jason Jewell ranked 110 on the PSA tour; Sean Delliere Canada's number one ranked junior; and local favorite and PSA-NA top ten ranked Scott Denne.

Last years' champion Willie Hosey and crowd favorite Damien Mudge were unable to return this year due to a conflict with the World Doubles Tour. It is a credit to Scott Denne that he has been able to lure such strong players to the tournament and that the level of play has been so outstanding.

The Age Old Battle...
Sunday afternoon found the top 2 seeds in the finals in a classic match of young turk (Duggan) versus old lion (Martin). Last year Cleveland was witness to a 2 hour and 35 minute match between Damien Mudge and Jamie Crombie in which the older lion Crombie was able to wear the younger Mudge down in a classic softball struggle of fitness and shotmaking. In what seems to be a tradition in the making, this year's final was an equally entertaining (and equally exhausting!) final match between Martin and Duggan.

Looking to use his fitness and foot speed to pressure the cerebral Martin, Duggan was prepared to spend as much time as necessary to wear down the older opponent. Suprisingly, the elder Martin was able to utilize his experience and otherworld anticipation to negate the younger man's lungs and legs. Time and time again Duggan looked both astounded and shocked that Martin retrieved his drops with apparent ease and was then put on the defensive with a deft cross or lob by the former world number 2 ranked Martin.

Brett's Magical Touch
While Duggan was able to recover due to his great speed on any number of occasions, the effects of being on a "court tour" for over 2 hours finally had the inevitable cumulative effect. The end of the match found the old lion Martin a 3-2 winner over Duggan in front of a wildly appreciative Cleveland gallery.

The semi final matches were a telltale precursor to the finals as both Martin and Duggan had solid matches in preparation for the Sunday finals. The top half of the draw had Martin winning a 4 game match against Delliere. While the young Canadian undoubtedly has a bright future, he proved unready to cope with a player of Martin's elite level. The other half saw local professional Denne facing Duggan with the number 2 seed moving to the finals with a 3-0 victory. On the amateur side, there were a number of equally interesting final matches in both singles and doubles competition.

Gul on Stage
The Men's 50+ singles final found doubles legend and long-time crowd favorite Gul Khan facing Pat Morin the current USSRA men's 60+ national champion. In a somewhat heated and confrontational atmosphere, the younger Khan utilized his overwhelming experience and shot-making magic to confound Morin in a 3-0 sweep. Best known for his doubles prowess with cousin Gulmast Khan (the duo is the current 2 time USSRA men's 50+ doubles champion) Gul demonstrated that he has not lost the softball skills honed in his native Pakistan since the age of six.

Doubles Side of Things
On the doubles side, local doubles legend Joe DeGrandis cemented his hammer-lock on the NEOSRA all time champions ledger with wins in both the men's "A" and "50+" doubles categories. With these two wins DeGrandis raised his totals to 14 overall doubles championships and 8 singles championships. These are records that are not likely to be broken by any local Cleveland player in the near future. Needless to say, doubles is a team event and DeGrandis had some experienced and talented partners: Tom Epps in the 50+ and Gary Hribar in the "A"

The men's "B" doubles found Cleveland Racquet Club members Bob Brandon and Bill Purlmutter defeating Ken Kosky and Ben Mann in a 3-0 sweep. There were a full set of singles draws for both skill levels and age groups. The men's "A" draw featured a re-match of last year's finals in both participants and ultimate results. Michelle Bayard found himself on the wrong end of 3-2 tally for the second year in a row against Canadian Dave Perry. The "B" draw found Michael Adams flexing the muscles of his game through a solid field without dropping a game over the weekend. The "C" group was witness to an outstanding 3-2 victory by Neil Sigel over Lou Geissler and the "D" finals found Kevin Ross of Rochester NY holding on to beat local Charlie Braun.

As is always the case, the tournament would not have been possible without the dedicated efforts of all the volunteers, sponsors and the Pro-Event lead sponsor Prudential Securities. Finally, aside from the business of the play itself, the Saturday night soiree and significant participants thereof deserve special mention. An epic meltdown in gallic style was achieved by Bertrand, Kiwi Ray delighted the crowd with his rendition of the Stomp folk dance and The Factor chimed in with his rendition of the Australian classic Waltzing Matilda by Men at Work. With the second year growing pains put out of the way, Cleveland squash looks forward to next fall's tournament!
