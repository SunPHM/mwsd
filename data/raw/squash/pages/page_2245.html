Our Garden Journal

August 2001

August 3, 2001

Time once again for the monthly garden photos.

The pumpkin, squash, and potato patch out behind the greenhouse. The Lumina pumpkins are anywhere from golf ball to soccer ball size at the moment.

Dill's Atlantic Giant squash in front of the fence (and growing through to the other side where you can't see it) and Hooker's Sweet Corn behind the fence.

Nine-foot high Giganteus sunflowers. Behind them are a bunch of weeds with intermingled garlic.

Corn, squash, asparagus, sweet potatoes, compost.

Squash, beans, peas, tomatos, herbs, eggplant, cucumbers, melons, and who knows what else. Compost pile along the back fence.

A few grapes are still coming along, though at the moment they are hard as little rocks.

The greenhouse is still full of stuff: swisschard, cabbage, okra, parsley, broccoli, carrots, kale, dill, squash, sage, mint, and a lemon tree.

A row of Teddy Bear sunflowers fighting for space along the fence.

The leeks have been big enough for harvest for a while now. So have the onions.

Giganteus sunflowers against a blue blue sky.

Carrots, kale, swisschard, beets, and far too many weeds.

Spinach and Kyona Mizuna mustard.

The sweet potatoes are doing well aboveground. Who knows what they're up to underground?

There are lots of green tomatoes. Here's some of the many Italian Gold plum tomatoes. I can hardly wait for them to ripen.

Just a few of the many summer squash that have grown to a size where they're only good for chicken feed.

Wax beans and green beans in profusion.

The largest winter squash are about baseball-sized now.

Lots of basil. I've got to make pesto soon.

Tiny little pickling cucumbers, but they'll be pickles within a couple of weeks.

Baby corn on its way up. Note cat for scale.

August 4, 2001

Some of the eggplant plants are flowering, so we might get a crop out of them this year. I'm amazed, because when I transplanted them out I was sure they were all just going to die (as did the peppers that I transplanted at the same time).

August 15, 2001

Looks like we'll have corn this year. The Serendipity corn has both tassles and silk; the Country Gentleman corn has tassles but no silk yet. My mouth is watering already.

I harvested the first of the plum tomatoes today. At least, I think they were plum tomatoes -- the tomatoes have grown into such a thicket that I can't tell which branch belongs to which plant, or find the plant labels that would tell me for sure.

August 16, 2001

I harvested a big bucket full of elephant garlic today. The only hard part about this was separating the garlic from the six-foot tall weeds. Ah well, less of the garden got overgrown this year than last year. With luck next year I'll do an even better job of keeping it under control. As long as each year is an improvement on the one before, I'm content.

August 19, 2001

Harvested the first baby eggplant today. Because we bought Territorial's seedling variety pack, it's hard to be sure precisely what variety it is, but I think Dusky.

I also harvested the Bavarian Purple garlic. It does live up to its billing of being hot -- I ate part of a clove raw and it had quite a bite to it. A full clove gave a very nice flavor to a plate of scrambled eggs.

August 23, 2001

A couple of the avocado pits that I put in a bucket of dirt in the greenhouse several months ago are finally sprouting.

August 26, 2001

Ripping out played-out snow pea vines, I discovered that some of the peas are sprouting right now. Seems like a silly time of year for them to be doing this.

Still harvesting like mad. Yesterday: five pints of wax beans, about a quart of cherry and plum tomatoes, one big onion, a handful of basil.
