Farm Notes

updated November 6, 2001

Hello

The rain didn't amount to much, but it did settle the dust and clear the air and give the trees some relief. Gave us a lot of relief. We are afraid the coming year is going to be a dry one, though, and so we are very much wishing to be wrong. Currently we are beginning to sow cover crop seed, plow down crop residues, and planting next year's garlic, peas and onions. Needing one more rain for good plowing.

The nights have been very cold, and the frost has almost arrived. A couple of nights ago the temperature was down to 37 degrees. Consequently, we are picking the last of the tomatoes and peppers and the eggplant don't look as good as they used to. The crops have lasted well this year, though the lettuce has been very slow and the broccoli is not going to arrive in time. Darn. Later, in December, there may be some broccoli at the produce stand, along with some late lettuce and maybe some leeks…

This week we have a selection of winter squash to go along with another sugar pie pumpkin. You will be receiving one of the following varieties: Blue Ballet, Ambercup, Delicata or Buttercup. They are all gourmet delights; all can be steamed, baked, used for tempura, and even pies. Ambercup and Delicata are both thin skinned and it's possible to eat the skin, which like most peels and skins of vegetables is very good for you. Sorry we don't have as much squash as usual this year, but we have just enough, so we are thankful for that.

Also this week is a new arrival: Fuyu persimmons. These are the type that you eat like an apple, are firm and crunchy and are used mostly for fresh eating, not cooking. Next week, in the farewell baskets we will be including the Hachiya persimmons that are best used when soft, most often used in cooking. Though I must say, a ripe Hachiya over French vanilla ice cream is pretty hard to beat.

Pippin apples are in the baskets, as well, and we hope to pick enough for next week as well. The size has been small this year, but the quality was better, and we sure appreciate the access to them.
