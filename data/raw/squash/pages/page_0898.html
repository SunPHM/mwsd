Finally!  "Ig" has earned a name, Khloris, after the Greek goddess of vegetation.  (Fitting, don't you think?)  In the Greek alphabet, it's Khloris.   Unfortunately for her, she's had to hit her first egg-laying season to earn it.  (That was from March to April of 2001).  She has yet to lay any eggs.

Here is Khloris' egg laying box:

            

    

I have finally posted some pictures of Khloris going to the bathroom.  She uses the human toilet.  Sometimes on her own, she'll climb the ramp, but most times the Goddess prefers that we escort her to her throne.  She will leave it in her own good time, however.  

 

Currently, our ig is 17 inches from snout to vent length, and 5’ 1” long (61 inches) from snout to tail, a little over  5 feet! She's about 5 years old.  So, in the 4+ years we've been hanging out, she has grown over 3 feet!

Since last summer I have been growing mustard greens, collard greens, turnip greens, curly endive, dandelions and nasturtium leaves and flowers for Khloris.  She also loves hibiscus flowers... just make sure if you buy them that you leech the soil for 6 months to a year to get out all the pesticides and fertilizers.  Repot your plant in compost/manure mixtures.

<= This is what a nasturtium looks like.  They are not just Khloris' favorite flower, but also a favorite food of Cabbage Moths...  Proven amazingly enough when one hatched in Khloris' cage the day after our dog, Sam, died, in the middle of January in Minnesota!
