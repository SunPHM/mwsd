Winter Squash (Pumpkin/Calabaza)

This Curcubit is very high in Vitamin C and part of the daily diet for many West Indians and Latin Americans. Though they are referred to as "Pumpkins" in the ethnic produce trade, these gourds are closer to the North American winter squashes, not the Jack O'Lantern Pumpkin usually associated with the name.

"Pumpkins" come in a variety of shapes and sizes - from round to elongated. They are large with a typical weight of up to 0.9 - 1.4kg. (2 - 3lbs.). When cut, the bright yellow flesh is 3 to 5cm. thick. The cavity of the Pumpkin holds a large number of seeds.

Packing for Export
The Pumpkins are packed in a polypropylene sack in 22kg. (50lb.) quantities.
Shelf Life

In the store, Pumpkin has a life span of up to 4 weeks. For retail display/storage, remove the product from bags and store in bins for easy access.

Preparation
Pumpkin is traditionally baked or boiled in salted water. It has a slightly nutty flavor which is delicious in soups. It can be peeled, diced and gently simmered and served with melted butter and thinly sliced escallions. Seed can be dried and roasted.
