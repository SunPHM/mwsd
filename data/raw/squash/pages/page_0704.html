Farm Notes

updated June 26, 2001

Greetings!

Busy day today, so just a short newsletter.

This week we have some late lettuce that has just matured, the last of the California late garlic (some folks will receive some of the first stiff-necked garlic — probably Chinese pink). We have broccoli and some summer squash as well, but in limited quantities — more next week. Basil will be available as an option, we're still tipping them for only the first or second time. Cabbage and carrots should be coming soon.

Andy will be going on vacation with his family soon, leaving the farm in the capable hands of Emily and Sharon and the volunteers. We're busy getting some last planting done, making maps of the watering systems and instructions. I know we could use some extra help — especially on July 5th.

Someone asked me the other day where all the watermelons in the stores were coming from. In June and into July, they're generally coming from Northern Mexico and the Imperial Valley. Then the deal will move north into the San Joaquin Valley, then into southern Sacramento Valley. Our watermelons will be coming during September.

Okay, see you soon. Have a great Independence Day!

Andy and the Farm Crew
