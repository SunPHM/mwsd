15 September 2004

Late Summer Harvest!

I checked my garden this morning to see if the tomatoes were progressing further, and lo! there they were, little red-orange gems! A few more are begining to turn from green to orange on the vine, so I am hoping there will be a second harvest in a week or so. The poor cucumber plants are languishing and I picked off a couple of scraggly fruits.

Pruned up the plants and herbs as well. I've read that cutting off some of the excess branches of the tomato plants makes the plants focus their engergy into ripening the fruit. I'll see if it works. The celeriac are looking good too. Getting tall and leafy.

27 April 2004

Of Bees and Bread

Busy day on Monday. Our neighbors, who are moving back to the States in May/June, generously gave us a ton of potting soil that they could not bring back. I spent a couple of hours working it into the garden yesterday morning. Looks good and hopefully will encourage the plants :-) Mr. Checkers enjoyed being outside with me too. The yellow jackets are back, however. Not Good. They nest in/under (?) a medium-sized shrub in our yard. Not sure how to get rid of them. They are pretty nasty. Last year they kept getting in to the house.


Dinner last night - big salads. Red lettuce, avacado, goat cheese, tomatoes, onion slices marinated in redwine vinegar and sugar, topped with a bit of oilve oil and redwine vineager. Plus freshly made bread toasted and then rubbed with garlic. V. good and filling. Bread machine seems to be acting up though. The loaves are not rising as high as they used to, and in some cases fail to rise at all. Am certain it is not the yeast, as I proofed it before I made the second loaf (after first loaf failed to rise.) Wonder it is a humidity/temperature problem, a problem with the machine, or if I simply need to add more yeast? Might make another loaf today to experiment with. Can always freeze it if it turns out good. The bread machine is one of my favourite household appliances. I use it about once a week. We rarely buy bread.
