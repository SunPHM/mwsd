Lovejoy Community Garden

  • Contact name: Dr. Isom
  • Contact phone number: 271-1014
  • Garden location: Lovejoy School
  • Number of plots: 16 individual, 1 community
  • Average plot size: 125' x 50' or 200 x 100'-total area is 1 acre
  • Number of gardeners: 40
  • How much time per week the average gardener spends in the garden: 4-9 hours

What do gardeners grow: tomatoes, various grains, okra, corn,lettuce, squash, green peppers...

Community gardens have been in existence for the past 30 years in the Lovejoy neighborhood, in Brooklyn, right outside of East St. Louis. The flood last year wiped out all of the gardens, so the Lovejoy School donated an acre of land to the neighborhood for gardening. The Lovejoy school board was also instrumental in the organization of the garden. Everyone in the neighborhood who is able to, contributes in some way. Even those that aren't able to contribute, are still welcome to harvest vegetables. Individual people and families choose what to grow in their own plots, and the whole community decides what to grow in the community plot. The neighborhood is thinking about selling some of their produce in the East St. Louis Farmers Market.
