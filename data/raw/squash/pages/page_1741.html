Cherokee food

Like the Iroquois, the Cherokee also hunted small game, deer, rabbit, and bear. Berries, nuts, and wild plants were important forms of food for the Cherokee. They were considered to be excellent farmers. They had large farms which grew corns, beans, and squash. Since their villages were usually near streams or lakes, they also fished using spears and nets.
