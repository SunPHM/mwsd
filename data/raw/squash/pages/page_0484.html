Big helping of squash

By Duncan Welch

Those who gathered at the City Squash and Fitness Club to watch two of the sport’s best players in an exhibition match were treated to a display of exciting play and world-class skills.

Derek Ryan and Lee Beechill, Ireland’s number one and England’s number-four ranked players, played last Saturday before a crowd of locals, club squash players and expatriates there for an interesting evening out.

One member of the audience, who will remain anonymous to prevent embarrassment, said: "Their standard of skill is so high I wouldn’t be able to take a single point off either of them."

This was the general sentiment of all those watching who saw the two international stars play the best of five games which took the better part of two sweat-dripping hours.

Beechill, the younger of the two and ranked 33 places below Ryan, pulled off the win during the final and deciding game after both players were tied at 2-2.

Ryan won the first game 17-16, the closest of the five as the two players found their feet. Beechill won the second 15-6 and then lost the third 8-15 to an aggressive Ryan who won several points with an unexpectedly deft touch.

Beechill won the fourth game 15-7 to tie the match and set up the decider, which he went on to win 15-5. "It went well tonight. I’ve been playing well for the last few months and it’s good to win against a player of Derek’s caliber.

"I managed to win the last match we played so I’m pleased to keep the run going," said Beechill.

"There are league matches in England coming up and then I play in Italy at the end of March in a PSA tournament which I’m seeded three in.

"I’ve never been to Budapest before and we have to leave soon, which is a shame. I’d like to see more of the city but the squash schedule is quite intense at the moment."

Although he lost the match, Ryan found it an enjoyable experience.

"It was a good run around tonight; we played flat out which is great practice and of course has no pressure.

"There are no points at stake and so we can have some fun," he said.

"Next week I’ll travel to Egypt for an international tournament. The organizers are building an all-glass court and the match will be played during the night.

"This is a world-ranking tournament with the world’s top three players taking part. So it’s an important tournament."

Although only an exhibition match, the players illustrated how good the international level squash has become, giving a target for the young club players to aim for.

Niall O’Connor, squash instructor at City Squash and Fitness, said they hoped to organize other such events in the near future.
