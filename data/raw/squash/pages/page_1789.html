Blokketiblok: what's that?
Blokketiblok is a game where you (Blokketi) go around in a colourful maze trying to squash all the bad guys (the Zzepallepa). To do this push against them the blocks and the special gems that build up the maze. When all the Zzepallepa are gone a new level is started. In each level you can increase your score by combining the blocks in color combos or by eliminating groups of Zzepallepa.

Minimum system requirements
-Pentium II 350
-32 MB of memory
-800x600 resolution
 -Windows 98/2000/XP
-DirectX 5 or later

Starting up
Select "Blokketiblok" from the "Start\Program Files\Saleo\Blokketiblok\" menu.

Configuration
After the intro you'll get in the configuration screen. You may choose the language (english or italian) and the video mode (windowed or full screen) clicking on the various icons with your mouse. Click "OK" or press return to go on.

Title screen
After the configuration you'll get in the title screen. Here you can start a new game, check the high scores list, customize the controls and switch the music on or off. Use the cursor keys to select an item then press enter.

Controls
You can use either the keyboard or the joystick if available. Keep in mind that joystick support is still in beta stage, so it couldn't work as expected. The default keyboard configuration is:
- the cursor keys to walk around
- the space bar to push/crush the blocks
You can customize the keyboard, by choosing "Controls" in the title screen. If a joystick is available the button number 1 is used to push/crush the blocks.

How to play
To squash the Zzepallepa you can push the blocks that build up the maze.
To push a block walk towards it and push the "push/crush" button. In doing so two things may happen:
- the block is free to move, so it slides away until it strikes another block, a wall or a gem
- the block can't move, because of another block, a wall or a gem, so it crushes.
When a block hits a Zzapallapa it doesn't stop, but it starts to drag the Zzapallapa until it strikes another block or a wall, squashing the poor red ball. This way you can squash more Zzepallepa with the same block, performing a combo that increases your score.

The Zzepallepa
These nice red balls will try to catch you. Keep in mind that the Zzepallepa are able to crush the blocks without any advise, so don't feel so safe when you are hidden behind a block. For each level you must squash a fixed number of Zzepallepa, as shown by the egg's numberin the lower left of the screen. Pay attention to the last Zzapallapa on the playfield: if you don't squash it rapidly, it will become really fast, angry and crazy.

Gems
There are some special gems.These gems may be pushed like any block in the game.
Line up these (either vertically or horizontally) to earn 5000 extra points (using the walls) or 10000 extra points (not using the walls). This kind of gem cannot be crushed. Plus, they cannot be lined up more than two times per level.
This is an explosive gem.When you try to crush it or when it strikes a block, a wall or another gem it will activate and after a few seconds ... BOOM, all the Zzepallepa in a fixed range will be eliminated, even behind a block. The gem activates when it's striken also.Tip: after the activation run for your life!!
This is a magic gem. It may be crushed but when it strikes a block, a wall or another gem it throws two copies of itself  perpedicularly to his motion direction. You may use it to bust a Zzapallapa around the corner (and earn 2000 points).
This is a nitro gem. When it is crushed or when it strikes a block, a wall or another gem it spreads  some nitro bubbles that will transform the Zzepallepa in bombs with the fuse lighted on. The bubbles will not pass through blocks, walls or other gems. Warning: a bomb Zzapallapa is still dangerous!

The walls

You can use the walls to freeze the Zzepallepa for a short time. When you try to push a wall a freezing spark is spread along the perimeter.

When a Zzapallapa is frozen it may be eliminated stepping on it (but you will earn a few points). Keep in mind that this power needs about 4 seconds to reload.

Color combos
If you line up two or more blocks of the same color you earn extra points. Keep in mind however that the lined up blocks will transform in golden globes and will get unmoveable and unbreakable (but the Zzepallepa can still crush them), so don't let you get trapped by your greediness.

Zzepallepa combos
The base score value for eliminating a Zzapallapa is 500 points. When you squash one immeditly after another the points you got previously are doubled. So if you rapidly eliminate 4 Zzepallepa you get
500+1000+2000+4000 = 7500 points, that is as the base value is multiplied by 2 for the second Zzapallapa, by 4 for the third one and by 8 for the fourth one.

 Loosing a life
You loose a life when:
-a Zzapallapa gets you
-a bomb explodes under your nose
-there aren't any block left on the playfield (in this case a few other blocks will appear).
If you loose all your lives the game is over.

In game options
When you press Esc the game is paused popping up a dialog box. You will be able then to abandon or resume the game.

Exiting the game
press Esc when in the title screen to quit the game.

A note to the english version
Probably you may wonder what the words "Blokketi" and "Zzapallapa" are for. Well, "Blokketi" is an exotic version of the word "Blocchetti" that in italian means "little blocks". "Zzapallapa" is a sort of anagram. You mix the words "palla" and "pazza" and you get Zza palla pa (palla paZza). In italian "palla pazza" means "crazy ball". "Zzepallepa" is the plural for "ZZapallapa", since "palle paZze" in italian is the plural for "palla paZza".
