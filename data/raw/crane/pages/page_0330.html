  Nancy Crane
Class:
Senior
Hometown:
Stuart, Fla.
High School:
South Fork
Height:
5-4
Position:
OF
Birthdate:
09/23/1981
Experience:
3VL
Major:

2003 -- Played in all 61 games and started 60 ... Hit .273 and has the second highest batting average among returning players ... Recorded career season highs in at bats, runs scored, hits, RBI and total bases ... Led the team in stolen bases ... Had 10 multi-hit games including two three-hit games ... Went 3-for-3 and scored two runs in game two against Arkansas.

2002 -- Played in 38 games, started two ... Used mainly in pinch running situations ... Ended the season with a .286 hitting average ... Started against East Tennessee State and Charlotte ... As a pinch runner, scored the tying run in the bottom of the seventh inning in the Gamecocks 2-1 Regional win against No. 1 UCLA.

2001 -- Played in 45 games in her initial college season, all off the bench ... Scored 15 runs, primarily as a pinch runner ... Tallied four stolen bases on the year, good enough for fourth on the team.

HIGH SCHOOL -- Played for Coach Hess at South Fork High ... Lettered all four years ... Named Softball MVP her junior and senior years ... Team Captain ... Team went to regionals her senior year ... Also captained her volleyball and basketball squads.

PERSONAL -- Full name is Nancy Hilton Crane ... Nickname is Nanner ... Majoring in Psychology ... Dad, John, played quarterback at Auburn ... Credits her dad as having the biggest influence on her softball career ... Has one brother, Casey ... Daughter of John and Elizabeth Crane ... Born Sept. 23, 1981.
