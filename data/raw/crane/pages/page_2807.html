Introduction

How long has it been since you experienced featherbeds, luxurious linens, the fragrance of a mesquite fire, breakfast created from the best of the world's kitchens, and sunsets that turn surrounding mountains a glorious watermelon pink?
The Sandia Mountains adopt their namesake "watermelon pink" at sunset!

The Sandhill Crane Bed & Breakfast is a rambling adobe hacienda in rural Corrales, bordering the northwestern edge of Albuquerque. Newly renovated and furnished with antiques and colorful folk art from around the world, the inn's spacious floorplan allows for privacy or congeniality as you wish.

Nearby Attractions...
  • Corrales and nearby Santa Fe offer some of the nation's finest historic archaeological sites, museums, galleries, and outdoor sports.
  • The Sandhill Crane Bed & Breakfast can serve as your home base for enjoying the raw energy of white water rafting, skiing, horseback riding, hiking, biking, and hot air ballooning.
  • Explore New Mexico's National Parks -- Chaco Canyon, Bosque del Apache Wildlife Refuge, Bandelier National Monument and the Taos and Acoma Pueblos, two of the oldest continuously occupied settlements in the U.S.
  • The world's longest aerial tramway, the city's new aquarium and zoo, the Indian Pueblo Cultural Center, and fine shops and restaurants are only minutes away in Albuquerque.

Embraced by timbered ceilings and cool brick floors, the three guest rooms are all on ground level for easy access. Each guest room has private baths, spa robes and cable TV.

The two-room suite with kitchenette, private entrance and connecting bath is ideal for a small family or friends traveling together. King, queen, and twin beds are available.

A complimentary breakfast served in the dining room or on the terrace includes the best seasonal produce available in our local markets, premier blends of coffee and teas, and freshly-baked breads, waffles, or a hearty casserole.
