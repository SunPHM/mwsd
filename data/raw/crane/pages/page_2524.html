Sandhill Crane

Range: Great Lakes region, north through Alaska; also northwest corner of US.

Markings: Gray overall with a dark red crown; tall, with a bustlelike array of tail feathers. Some plumage may appear rusty-red as the result of preening with iron-laden mud on its long, black bill.

Behavior: Form large flocks during migration and at their wintering sight. Spend each night on the wtaer where they are safe from predators. During courtship, displays include graceful leaps off the ground that look almost like dance movements.
