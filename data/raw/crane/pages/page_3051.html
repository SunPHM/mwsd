IOWA Winter School

February 8-10, 2002 in Marshalltown, Iowa


The highly spirited Iowa Regional School 2002 Winter session was attended
by 91 people in its session held in Marshalltown from February 8-10.

Involved in the study process at Iowa Winter Bahá’í School in Marshalltown, Iowa are
Vivian Mooris, Helen Speas holding her granddaughter Jenny Speas,
Frank Mooris, Linda Coulter-Jones, and Nina Scott (with her back to the camera.)

Linda Grundman, Kira Scott, Napolun Birdsong, Jason Carpp, Linda Coulter-Jones
and Mateen Manshadi worked together to provide devotions for one of the sessions.

Children from all across the state created hundreds of bright and wonderful
Origami cranes to present to the Marshalltown Chamber of Commerce.
Children pictured are, (left to right) Tami Tozer, Tesia Cave (in front),
Hannah Scott, Mateen Manshadi, Zane Scott-Tunkin, Darajun Abel,
and Ashlee Martin with spokesperson Chet Tozer.

Colorful dancers from Marshalltown, Iowa, where the school session was held,
charmed the participants of the Iowa Winter School
with several renditions of their native dances from Mexico.

These masked dancers relate a story about the elders in Mexico.
