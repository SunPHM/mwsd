FINAL VOYAGE:

    WILLIAM CAREY CRANE, JR. 

BOYNTON BEACH, Jan. 20, 2004--William Carey Crane, 76, a member of the CCA since 1975, died of cancer here today.  Bill lived in Riverside, Connecticut for 22 years prior to moving to Gulf Stream, Florida, where he became a member of the Florida Station.

Bill sailed in 17 Newport Bermuda Races, the last five with Herb Schwartz who was first in class in 2000 with Bill as navigator. Herb Schwartz summed it up best, "Bill was a terrific helmsman with great concentration. He was also an excellent sea cook but, above all, he was just wonderful company aboard at any and all times."

Bill grew up in Quisset, Massachusetts, sailing Herreshoff 12-1/2's before moving to Connecticut. He never missed a summer (and very few winters), without one or more blue water races, including St. Pete to Ft. Lauderdale and several Miami-Nassau races and up North, numerous Block Island and Vineyard races and Block Island Race Weeks. He sailed very frequently with CCA member Bill King, former commodore of Riverside Yacht Club.

Bill was a former secretary of the Florida Station and in addition to Riverside Yacht Club was a member of the Field Club of Greenwich and the Gulf Stream Bath and Tennis Club.

He attended the American School in Japan while his father, General W. Carey Crane, served as military attache to the American ambassador. He graduated from Deerfield Academy in 1945 and, after serving in the U.S. Army during World War II, graduated from Princeton University, where he was a member of the Ivy Club.

He is survived by his second wife, Mary Stair Crane of Gulf Stream, Florida, four daughters and two sons.
