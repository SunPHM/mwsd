Microevolution
This material is explained in further detail in Chapter 15 of your textbook.
 

A Baikal Seal--the world's only freshwater seal.  Baikal Seals provide a great example of the founder effect at work!
 Microevolution occurs when subtle, incremental changes in genes and gene frequencies contribute, ultimately, to speciation.

Microevolution occurs through some combination of:

  • mutation
  • gene flow
  • genetic drift
  • natural selection
A cute little cartoon--it's only partly right, though!		 Mutation is any "glitch" in DNA
  • mutation is random!
  • point mutations, inversions, deletions, etc., all contribute to microevolutionary change
  • presence of mutations in a gene pool may remain undetected until environmental changes occur that favor the mutant alleles
  • we are all mutants!
Gene flow occurs as a result of immigration or emigration
  • gene flow is random!
  • very important in many human populations
  • effects may accrue due to eventual isolation of migrants

Whooping Crane numbers were drastically reduced in the 1930s--today's "whoopers" are descendants of only 14 individuals!
 Genetic Drift exerts its most dramatic effects when populations are very small.
  • genetic drift is random!
  • founder effect occurs when a subset of a larger population becomes geographically isolated from the larger population
  • bottleneck effect occurs when a population experiences rapid, steep decline in numbers (e.g., endangered species)

Antibiotic-resistant bacteria provide a good example of natural selection at work.
 Natural Selection will favor individuals possessing traits that increase the odds of survival and reproduction.
  • natural selection is not random!
  • natural selection may favor individuals that are larger, stronger, more colorful, less colorful, etc.--depending on environmental conditions
  • natural selection usually requires non-random mating (i.e., "assortative" mating) in sexually reproducing species
  • in combination with mutation, gene flow, and genetic drift, natural selection becomes a powerful force for evolutionary change
