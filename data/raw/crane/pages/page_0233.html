Hometown

I come from Wuhan, which has been Pittsburgh's Chinese sister city for over two decades, based on the fact that they look exactly alike. Not only is Wuhan located on a triangular confluence of the Yangtze river and the Hanshui, but it also has a steely industrial past -- one that it hasn't yet cleaned up as successfully as Pittsburgh. Yellow Crane Tower - landmark / symbol of Wuhan!

Yellow Crane Tower is an imposing pagoda close to the Yangzi River.
The tower is 50 meters high with five stories stretching upwards from the ground.

Wuhan Yangtze River Bridge - China's first bridge over the Yangtze River

1100 meters long and 80 meters high, with road traffic on its upper deck, and rail on its lower, completed in 1957 and allowed north and south China to be connected by railroad for the first time.

By the end of 2001, Wuhan's 4th bridge over the Yangtze had been completed and put into use.
