 @Cromwell College 2004/15 – Friday 30 July, 2004

Photo of the Week -  Sarah Becker, Emily Westacott, Ben Young and Bridget Stokes make origami cranes in the College’s drive to make 1000 cranes to send to Hiroshima.  The goal was achieved today

From the Principal - Welcome back to everyone for Term 3.  I trust that all students and staff have come back refreshed and with positive energy for the term.  Term 3 is always an important one in regards to learning consolidation and I would like to encourage all students to keep up the positive work they have been doing so far this year.  We have many new student faces around the campus this term.  As well as many new New Zealand students we have our largest ever contingent of international students.  This term we have 14 internationals with us from the countries of Japan, China, Germany and Brazil.  Thank you to all the host parents for providing a quality home life for these young people.  Our First XV rugby team had a wonderful trip to Australia during the recent school holidays.  By all accounts these young men were exceptional sportsmen both on and off the field and I have heard nothing but good news about their trip and their conduct.  Thank you to all the boys and thank you especially to all the parents, coaches and managers who helped make this trip a reality.  It is great to see our students commit to a long-term goal and see it through to a most satisfying conclusion.  Year 9 and 10 reports will be mailed home to parents on Friday 6 August.  I would like to encourage parents and caregivers to go through these reports carefully with you children.  Teachers are very happy to discuss aspects of these reports at the Report Evening on Wednesday 11 August.

From the Head Boy and Head Girl - The next week is a very busy one for senior students as many will be involved with the snowboarding championships, winter quad and of course the formal.  The Formal theme for this year is “A Night at the Oscars” with the dinner being held at the Big Picture and the dance at the Cromwell Memorial Hall.  Good luck to everyone in the upcoming events.  (Bridget Pauley and Ryan Christie)

Staff Changes - Last term saw the departure of Jim Girvan, the school’s deputy principal.  Mr Girvan has moved on to the deputy principal’s position at Oxford Area School.  While he was here Mr Girvan was an exceptional school administrator and an admired classroom teacher.  For Term 3 the deputy principal responsibilities will be shared by Lee-Anne Kane and Alison Jackson.  We welcome to the staff Tania Miller who will be with us on a part-time basis teaching maths and science and we welcome back Grant McFarlane who will be on site one day each week teaching senior physics.   We also welcome Anna Watson who is here as a teacher trainee for five weeks from the Dunedin College of Education.

Attendance Matters - Parents and caregivers are reminded to contact the school office if students are absent for the day.  You are welcome to ring the night before if you know of the absence in advance.  If the office is not attended please feel free to leave a message on the school’s answer machine.  If a student is absent and we have not heard from parents we are obliged to do our best to contact you as soon as possible.

School Ski Trips - Permission slips for ski trips have been issued to all students.  Payment and slips must be returned to the school office by Wednesday 18 August. 

NZQA Examinations  - Exam entry fee payments are due to the office by 27 August.  All students have had an updated handout and should have checked this for any errors.  Any student who does not pay their fees by this date will not receive their results notification from the NZQA.  If you receive any form of income support from WINZ please inform Lee-Anne Kane (Acting DP) of your benefit number.  This will significantly reduce the fee level required for exam entries.  Please do this as soon as possible. 

Upcoming Events - 4 – 6 August – Winter Quadrangular Tournament at Mount Aspiring College *** 6 August – Year 9 and 10 Reports mailed home to parents *** 7 August – School Formal *** 9 – 13 August – Green Week (Prime Teaching Time) *** 11 August – Year 9 and 10 Parent Interviews
