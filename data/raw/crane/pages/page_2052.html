Muir Environmental Film Series
Number 4: Fall 2004

Telluride's MountainFilm On Tour

DATE: Friday, November 5, 2004
TIME: 7:30 PM (Doors open at 7:00 PM)
LOCATION: The Varsity Theatre, 616 2nd Street, Downtown Davis, California

TICKETS: $10 from JMIE or Armadillo Music. --ON SALE NOW! How to purchase tickets.

This fall the John Muir Institute of the Environment is proud to sponsor MountainFilm on Tour in Davis. The festival tour features the best selections from Telluride's MountainFilm Festival of environmental, cultural, mountain, and adventure films and video. This year the Muir Environmental Film Series will host a large selection of short films from the 2003 and 2004 MountainFilm festivals. The program will showcase humorous and provocative pieces concerning the environment and nature, glimpses of other cultures, portraits of remarkable people, adventures from all over the globe, and animated masterpieces.

Davis Tour Play List
FILMS WILL NOT NECESSARILY BE SHOWN IN THIS ORDER
PSICOBLOC
Josh Lowell
Austrian Klem Loskot, a world-renowned boulderer, has found a more satisfying form of climbing, called “deep water soloing” by the Brits, and Psicobloc by the Spanish. The overhanging limestone walls of Mallorca provide perfect opportunities to forge routes sans ropes with the Mediterranean Sea 60 feet below as a sensual crash pad.
(United States, 2004, 8 min)
FRENCH FRIES TO GO
Howard Donner
This is a fast-paced look at how one person can make a difference with a diesel engine and discarded vegetable oil. Meet Telluride's rapping environmentalist Charris Ford and learn about his bio-diesel empire high in the San Juan mountains! (Best Environmental Advocacy Film: 2002)
(United States, 2002, 12 min)
FAKE CLOUDS
Andrea Campbell
With artful eloquence and brilliant cinematography, we follow a young boy in the 1950s while the narrator reminisces about growing up next door to a nuclear power plant.
(United States, 2002, 7 min)
DIRT
Chel White
To the horror of parents everywhere, kids love dirt. It is basic stuff, primordial, and yet we normally realize that this life giving manna should have its limits.
(United States, 4 min.)
LE CHEVAL 2.1
Alex Kirkland & Stephen Scott-Hayward
Many of us have imagined how liberating it might be to assume the identity of another animal - to swim like a fish, fly like a bird or run like a thoroughbred... but few of us ever succeed.
(United Kingdom, 2003, 2 min)
HIKE HIKE HIKE
Anouck Iyer
Clean and crisp as the Arctic north it depicts, this animation is simultaneously lush and lean, relying only on the sound of the dogs and the runners on the sled they pull across the snow.
(United States, 2002, 4 min)
PETER MATTHIESSEN AND WHOOPING CRANES
Steve Robitaille & Leslie Gaines
Few authors so artfully span the worlds of fiction and non-fiction as Peter Matthiessen (The Snow Leopard, Killing Mr. Watson), and few subjects so hold his attention as birds, whether in Tibet or Central Florida. To chronicle the arrival of the first whooping crane chick born east of the Mississippi in over a hundred years - the result of an international restoration project - Matthiessen joined an expedition that provided him the concluding episode of his recently published book Birds of Heaven, Travels With Cranes.
(United States, 2004, 5 min)
FROG CROSSING
Jamie Babbit and Ari Gold
On a lonely highway stands a man with a mission: "Brake for frogs, it's mating season," he shouts, but nobody listens... until today.
(United States, 1995, 12 min)
CAGED BIRDS CANNOT FLY
Luis Briceno
Or can they?
(France, 2000, 3 min., animated)
THE FLIGHT OF THE STONE
Susanne Horizon-Franzel
Without words, this witty and thought-provoking (how do they do it?) film follows a stone thrown in anger that misses its target, and enters a low orbit around the world. (Filmed in Germany, France, United States, Japan, Thailand, India and Greece)
(Germany, 1999, 16 min or 10 minutes)
MONTY PYTHON: CLIMBING OXBRIDGE RD.
RIVER RUNNERS OF THE GRAND CANYON
Don Briggs
No one knows more about Grand Canyon history than river runner/filmmaker Don Briggs... two vignettes, archival footage from the 1800s to the 1950s, that follow two whacky and somewhat successful trips down the Grand Canyon by unconventional means.
(United States, 8 min)
I NEED TO CATCH A FISH
Howard Donner & Robert Murphy
Filmmaker Howard Donner was ejected from a whitewater raft that he was guiding and landed on a story. As he was recovering from his ailments on the riverbank, he met a hermit named Kelly C., who appeared with a poultice made of blanched comfrey leaves. In the days that followed, Donner had many discussions about living the simple life with a man who dines on the local cuisine of wild mushrooms, acorns, fish and the occasional road-killed deer. The film follows Kelly on a typical day, and is ultimately less about what he does and more about his simple yet poignant philosophy. "I Need to Catch a Fish" touches on aspects of freedom and the typical human addiction to collect more stuff. It speaks to living each day, and relishing in the simple pleasures that every day brings.
(United States, 2004, 11 min)
THE JANITOR
Vanessa Schwartz
This wonderful animation is a MOUNTAINFILM favorite, knowing in the ways of the Lord, the weatherman, and infinitely useful with respect to the way in which "things just get screwed up."
(United States, 1994, 5 min.)
