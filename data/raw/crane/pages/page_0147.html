GWR LocoWorks - Crane Engine
[Page last updated :  ]


Overview

While browsing Great Western Engines Vol 2 by J R Russell I spotted a rather interesting looking locomotive - a crane tank engine. Building one looked like a challenge, so I set about thinking how to build one. A picture of the prototype :

I decided the best way to make one would be to use a Hornby Pannier tank for the front part and scratch build the back part.

Getting Started

1. Materials required :

  • A Hornby Pannier tank locomotive (preferably the newer one with blacked wheels)
  • One set of new number/nameplates.
2. Dis-assemble in preparation
  • Remove the body from the pannier.
  • Remove the hand rails from the pannier body.
Keep all the removed fittings in a safe place.You are now ready to start the conversion.

Conversion

Outline (very outline! ) steps :

  • Pannier body
    • remove the rear bunker
    • shorted the height og the safety valve cover
    • remove the 'dome', put plasticard under neath hole and fill
    • make and fit the support for the crane
    • put short handrails on front
  • Fit the body back onto the chassis
  • Scratch build the rear section of the crane engine including the bogey.
  • Crane,
    • scratch build it from plasticard or material of you choice
    • assemble and fit to rear of locomotive
  • Fill any gaps with filler/plasticard..
  • Sand to finish
  • Undercoat and paint in matt black (departmental colour)
  • Fit new number and nameplates.
  • Weather as required
  • Finish with spray of varnish ( I use satin finish)

Conclusion

Overall the conversion was success. The finished crane tank looks good, see below.

Much to my surprise this won the Wimborne Railway Society's modelling competition this year (2001).
