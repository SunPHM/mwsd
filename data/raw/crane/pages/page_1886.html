Whooping Cranes

 

Wisconsin is home to an endangered species of birds, the Whooping Crane. Baraboo, Wisconsin has a captive flock at the International Crane Foundation. Whooping Cranes are the tallest birds in North America. They are 60 inches tall, weigh about 15 pounds, and the wingspan is 7 1/2 feet. A chick is a reddish color, but soon the feathers turn white and the head is red and black. This bird got it's name because the call is a shrill bugle-like trumpeting. These birds need 300-400 acres of shallow wetlands and wet grasslands to find enough food and nesting areas. Cranes look for berries, minnows, crabs and rodents to eat. Much of the land they need is being replaced with farmland. We need to take of these birds so they do not become extinct.
