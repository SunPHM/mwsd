Janet Crane

CEO
Billpoint Inc.

"I think we'll continue to see more merging of dotcoms and dotcorps, not only in mergers and acquisitions, but also through partnerships. We see lots of corporations seeing their opportunity now with some of the valuations changing, and changing their focus in what they're trying to do for their customers." More ...

Janet Crane respects failures. Just don't jump ship when the company is experiencing stormy weather. "We need people who can work a business plan as it goes up and down, doing what's necessary to keep it going," says Crane, the CEO of Billpoint, a site that provides customer support and consumer protection for online financial transactions. "Too many people want a home run or nothing."

Riding rough waters takes more than guts, she says. It requires flexibility, a willingness to listen and to challenge personal views and, above all, trust in the process.

"Experience brings an understanding that the sun is going to come up tomorrow no matter what happens today in business," she says. "Business and all the excitement of business is part of your life. It isn't your life."

Sometimes a singing fish can bring it all home. Big Mouth Billy Bass sits atop Crane's computer, taps its plastic tail and belts "Don't worry, be happy." So far, the fish has been right. There's no need to fear failure in the new economy, she says. "In a big company, failure can tar you as someone who won't be a star. In the new economy, a failure is a good thing to have on your resume. Each failure prepares you for the next round."

Crane has had a few rounds herself. She has more than 20 years of payment and banking experience at large and small entrepreneurial companies including Mondex International Ltd., Wells Fargo & Co., and MasterCard. She is now responsible for online payments for eBay's 15 million registered users, and it's the fastest job she's had yet.

"I always thought the payment side of business moves fast, but it's nothing like this," she says. "I view us like a speedboat versus a cruise ship. We're able to move quickly, see where the waves are going, and chart our own path. It's very empowering."
