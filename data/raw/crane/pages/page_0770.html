J.C. Crane, Jr.

 

Jerome Calvin Crane, Jr. was born October 18, 1967 to Jerry Crane, Sr. and Ruby Crane. He is a native of Chicago, Illinois and graduate of Chicago Mendel Catholic High School. A well noted academic student and football player; he was nationally recruited as a student/athlete following his senior year.

Crane graduated from Drake University in May of 1989 with a Bachelor's Degree in Journalism and Mass Communication and was a member of the Los Angeles Raiders NFL Try out Camp. He ended his pro football career with the Dallas Cowboys Rookie Combine during the 1989 season.

After two and a half years of soul searching, which included employment as an insurance claims adjuster and one semester of graduate school, J. C, answered his spiritual calling and has been employed in college administration for ten years. He has served as Associate Director of Admissions for Valparaiso University in Valparaiso Indiana and Kentucky Wesleyan College in Owensboro Kentucky. In addition, he has served as Director of Admissions for Mount Marty College in Yankton, South Dakota, Assistant Director of Admissions & Operations for the University of Notre Dame MBA Program in South Bend, Indiana, Assistant Director of Admissions for Midwestern University of Osteopathic Medicine in Downers Grove, Illinois and College Admissions Specialist for Triton Community College in River Grove, Illinois. During this time, J. C. has been a motivator, counselor, and mentor to young athletes, at risk youth, and various adult populations.

While having success as a college/university administrator, this ubiquitous professional relied on his personal experiences with college and professional football to launch a second career in athletics. Currently, he is a Camp Director for Team Challenge; a youth football clinic in Country Club Hills, IL. Also, he has served as football specialist for the University of Notre Dame, assistant football coach/recruiting coordinator and Director of Intramural Sports for Triton Community College, and combine Instructor for Pro Football Scout Camp; a professional football regional combine and seminar service. In 2000, he combined these talents with his educational background in Journalism/Mass Communication and became the WBIO 94.7 FM Radio Play by Play/Color Commentator for the Kentucky Wesleyan College Football team.

J. C. has been an active member of professional and community organizations. He has been a member of the National Association of College Admissions Counselors (NACAC) since 1991, and was selected to be a member of the NACAC Government Relations Committee in 2000. He has been chairman for the Kentucky Association of Secondary College and Admissions Counselors (KASCAC) Human Relations Committee, and Conference First Timer's Reception. He was also a member of the 1999 KASCAC Annual Conference Planning Committee. He has served as faculty for the 1998 and 1999 NACAC Workshop for Counselors of Color and the 1995 Illinois Association of College Admissions Counselors (IACAC) Summer Institute. Also, he has served as a member of the Illinois Association of College Admissions Counselors IACAC Human Relations Committee from 1994-1996, and the 1996 IACAC Conference Planning Committee.

A devoted Christian, J. C. is the Youth Director for Mount Zion Baptist Church in Michigan City, Indiana. Also, he has served as Financial Secretary and member of the Mentoring Committee for Tenth Street Baptist Church in Owensboro, Kentucky from January 2000 to August 2000. During this same time period, J. C. was serving as a member of the Daviees County Minority Teachers Recruitment Committee when he accepted the position of Chairman of the Owensboro Multiracial Advocacy Group.

 In conclusion, J. C. has written several articles, presented motivational seminars, and coordinated educational workshops regarding; athletics, urban gang violence, multicultural diversity, post-secondary education, and team building. He enjoys outdoor activities including hunting, hiking, and camping. Other hobbies include exercising and basketball.

Currently, J. C. is the Vice-President/Chairman of the Board for Crane Enterprises and Director of the Commit 2 Action program. He resides in Chicago, Illinois.
