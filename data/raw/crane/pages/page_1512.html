June 2002

Construction Progress

Progress for River City is continuing to move ahead very well. In particular, construction progress has been rapid now that the project is beginning to "get out of the ground."

Excavation work to the whole site is generally complete with all in-ground drainage and the Basement 2 ground slab completed and work on the the first suspended floor (Basement 1) now taking place. In addition, the main lift core walls have been poured up to the underside of Level 1.

A significant number of structural and trade contracts, including contracts for all building services, have been let. The only remaining outstanding contracts relate to building finishes.

Design and documentation work has been completed for all building trades and is well ahead of the structure for construction documentation.

The two Favco tower cranes are both operational and provide a visual point of interest at the site.

All authority approvals have now been obtained, including the building approval for the podium and tower structure. Options for the Ground Floor mezzanine tenancy and the final design of the external podium façade artwork and footpath awnings are progressing. Initial designs have been completed up to the Level 5 Podium deck and for the podium facade.

Work on the final selection of finishes to the ground floor entry and podium lobbies, together with the finalisation of the landscaping design is also continuing.
