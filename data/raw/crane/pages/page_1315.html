Germany, Sweden, and Finland in 1999

March 3rd - March 10th, 1999

Just after midnight on March 3rd, Benoît, Gernot and myself started driving north. We wanted to include a short visit to Berlin, and ultimately stay in Stockholm for a few days, where Flo studied for one year. We also planned a day trip to Helsinki.

However, at this time we didn't know that several complications would arise. The first one was in the Czech Republic, near Teplice, where Gernot drove through an enormous hole around a gully crate in the street, and eventually one tyre went flat (sorry, no picture). With the spare tyre we went to Berlin.

Benoît preparing to take photographs in Berlin.

Gernot took lots of photographs of the vast construction sites in Berlin. The tubes you see are everywhere in Berlin's center. They are used to pump water out of the construction sites.

A view over the site where Germany's new government quarters are built. In the middle the TV tower on Alexanderplatz is dwarfed by the cranes.

All those constructions have obviously given rise to secondary business: The poster reads "Pensions for construction workers, in Berlin, also for large groups"...

... my first time in Denmark! Unfortunately we couldn't see much of it - there was fog and darkness everywhere.

On the ferry from Helsingør to Helsingborg.

Finally, between Norrköping and Nyköping, we had a car crash. Gernot wanted to overtake a truck, but it came nearer (sideways) and braking resulted in the car going to the right into the crash barrier, spinning around and coming to rest on the middle strip of the motorway. This was at about 4am.

Here you can see how close we avoided desaster: The crash rail ended about one or two meters after the spot where we bumped into it. If we crashed three meters later, our car would have gone down a slope.

The car was towed to Nyköping. I want to thank all the nice people who helped us.

We spent most of the day in Nyköping trying to find out whether we should fix the car or scrap it and which insurance would pay and which would not pay and be unfriendly at the same time. On this day we also learned that it's not easy to find a public phone in Sweden.

The guy to my right is the scrap metal dealer, who finally bought the car for a reasonable price. The guy next to Gernot is the head of the repairs department of the company. A big thank you to him - he spent most of his time at that day for us. Also a big thank you to everyone at the garage in Nyköping, to Malin at the nearby hotel, to the police who let us stay at their station until the garage opened, and to the woman at the cafe, who helped us with finding a phone.

Finally! At Stockholm. It was good to meet Florian after our little nightmare.

In Stockholm's "Gamla Stan" (old town). The only bad thing about Stockholm is the expensive night life. Also strange is that there is a security guy at almost every pub, restaurant or bar. Usually you must give him your coat and of course you have to pay for that.

Gamla Stan seen from the boat. I was surprised to see so many old houses, which are mostly in a very good state. I like the laid-back atmosphere there very much, and the houses look very friendly.

On the right, you can see a ship which is similar to the one we went to helsinki with. Looks rather compact here, but in reality it's HUGE!

On the right, you can see a ship which is similar to the one we went to helsinki with. Looks rather compact here, but in reality it's HUGE!

This is Lappis, where many students live. We stayed in the building to the right of the center of the picture in Flo's flat.

Here we are in front of that house.

This is on top of the "Viking Lines" ferry. This is the biggest ship I've ever been on. It has over ten floors, and includes a nightclub, a disco, several restaurants, a duty free shop, and some more stuff. Amazing, I've never danced in a disco on a moving ship before! When going to Helsinki there were lots of very young folks abourd, who were obviously not used to drinking... It smelled like vomit everywhere in that disco. :-(

I woke up when I thought the ship had an accident (which I really expected after all the bad luck we had on this trip). Fortunately it was just ice: Huge ice blocks floating on the water! The cheap rooms we had were approximately near sea-level, so we had the full sound experience.

It was really quite cold here. Btw. I noticed that Beoît looks like Steven Spielberg on this picture. Maybe I can sell it to the yellow press.

Here we come, Helsinki! While Gernot and Benoît went sightseeing, I visited the smodels group at Helsinki University of Technology. I talked very long with Patrik Simons, who is a really nice guy. In the evening we boarded the ship again, and had a nice Sauna and Jacuzzi experience on it.

On the next day, Gernot and Benoît immidiately boarded a bus to Prague, while I had time to visit the Museum of Modern Art and collect my stuff. My train left at 4 (the bus at 10), but still we met each other again on the ferry from Sweden to Germany. The only drawback on the train was that one beer was about 5 times the price of the beer in the czech bus. A nice thing is that the train goes onto the ferry and leaves it again while you sleep. Bad thing: The train was delayed and I couldn't catch my follow-up connection in Berlin. Fortunately I only had to wait for 2 hours.

To sum it up: I recommend that trip, but only if you leave your car at home.
