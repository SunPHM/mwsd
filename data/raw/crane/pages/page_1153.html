Troubled times: Bucks worry about third-quarter woes

By MICHAEL HUNT
of the Journal Sentinel staff
Last Updated: May 12, 2001

Charlotte, N.C. - In Game 1, it cost them most of a 22-point lead. In Game 2 it erased virtually all of a 16-point lead. And in Game 3 it cost them, well, the game.

The third quarter hasn't been kind to Milwaukee in the Eastern Conference semifinals, and unless they address their out-of-the-locker-room demeanor before Sunday, the Bucks could bring home a tied best-of-seven series.

The Bucks lead Charlotte, 2-1, despite a decisive advantage by the Hornets in each of the third periods. In the first two games at the Bradley Center the Bucks were able to withstand Charlotte rallies with the built-in advantages and comforts of the home court.

Yet in Game 3 Thursday night at the Charlotte Coliseum, the Bucks lost, 102-92, when they could not endure what is becoming an automatic for the series, a third-quarter Charlotte revival.

A 10-0 run by the Hornets in the period, created from the momentum of an 11-1 spurt just before the first half expired, was the ultimate difference in the game. Why it is happening is a matter of debate among the Bucks, yet there is no disagreement that a continuation of that kind of domination could cost Milwaukee the series.

"They're finding juice at halftime," Bucks forward Scott Williams said. "I don't know if (Hornets coach Paul) Silas has got legendary halftime talks or they're just a third-quarter team."

Milwaukee guard Ray Allen attributes the Bucks' third-quarter lapses to carryover habits from the regular season.

"The third quarter has been our foe a lot this year," Allen said. "We come out and we're very complacent. We think things are going to take care of themselves. We've got to jump into the third quarter just like we've done in the first."

In each game of the series, the Bucks have established a domineering tone from the start. By each second quarter a double-digit lead was in place, including a 10-point advantage Thursday night. Yet by different methods, the Hornets have been able to rally in the third quarter.

It happened in Game 1 when the Hornets took 19 free throws for the period, matching the Bucks' total from the line for the entire game. In Game 2, the Bucks allowed P.J. Brown to become an offensive force. And in Game 3, the Bucks' momentum was shut down just before the end of halftime, when a three-point shot at the buzzer by David Wesley completed the 11-1 run that tied the game.

"They've come out and played hard (at the start of the second half)," Bucks forward Glenn Robinson said. "That's been the case this series. That's what was so scary when they tied the game up going into the third quarter (in Game 3). In the locker room I said, 'Oh, man, we've got to have a good third quarter,' because that right there might be a good chance for them to get their confidence."

And that's precisely what happened as the Bucks, with just 18 points, suffered their lowest-scoring third quarter of the series. They never recovered after the Hornets ran off 10 unanswered points to change the complexion of the series.

"We have to come out and play harder and play more aggressively in the third quarter," Robinson said.

It has almost been as if the Hornets, after being beaten down for most of the first half in the first three games, became resolute in the locker room during halftime. The Bucks, who lost all but one point of a 16-point lead in Game 2, could be looking at real problems now had they not escaped with a 91-90 victory.

That kind of aggression led to the staggering 19 free throws in the third quarter of Game 1 by the Hornets.

"It's kind of hard to defend the free-throw line," Bucks coach George Karl said. "We've had a quarter in about every game where we're giving them points by fouling them. We've got to move our feet and learn to play playoff basketball without fouling."

The Bucks must overcome their complacent attitude, says Williams.

"Two of the games we've had big leads and we think this team is going to roll over for us," Williams said. "(In Game 3) they had momentum on their side and we didn't recover from that."
