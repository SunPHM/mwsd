Try Out These Activities
 

 

Allen Say uses a variety of themes but, above all, his books are about real things that have happened to him in his life.

He writes about journey's, family, respect for the environment, and the love of creating art.

Most of the time he writes about the difference between Asian and American cultures.

 

Try one or all of the activities on the right. 

 

Find Japan on a map in your school.

 

Allen Say moved from Yokohama, Japan to California, United States.  He lived in San Franciso when he moved to California.  Can you find San Francisco on the map ?

 


The Tree Of Cranes

Try origami, the Japanese art of paper folding.   How is origami used in The Tree of Cranes ?  What will you do with your new creation?

 

Grandfather's Journey

American and Japan are shown differently in Say's book.  Tell about both places .  How are the homes different?  How is the landscape different?  How is the clothing different?  How are the things people use everyday different? 

 What do you think Allen Say's grandfather liked most about California?  What do you think he missed most about Japan? When his grandfather returned to Japan how do you think he felt ?  What kinds of stories do you think the grandfather told the boy?

Paint a picture using water colors showing what your community looks like.



A River Dream

One of Allen Say's hobbies is fishing.  Write a short story about one of your hobbies.

 

Go to Allen Say's bookshelf and choose your favorite story.  Create a dance that helps you tell the story.
