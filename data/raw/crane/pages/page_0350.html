Crane Decoy

Possibly George Martin
Barnegat, probably New Jersey
Carved and painted wood, 1907
Gift of Bertram K. and Nina Fletcher Little

This whimsical decoy stands in the front hall of Cogswell's Grant in Essex, Massachusetts, summer home of preeminent folk art collectors Bertram K. and Nina Fletcher Little. Made of five geometric wooden pieces--which can be quickly taken apart for carrying--it nevertheless manages to look completely lifelike. The Little's eighteenth-century farmhouse, now an SPNEA museum, still contains their celebrated collection of folk art and furniture just as they lived with it every summer

Butterfly Stools

Sori Yanagi (b. 1915)
Tendo Company, Tokyo, Japan,1954
Plywood, brass

These elegant stools occupy pride of place in the living room of German architect Walter Gropius (1883-1969) in Lincoln, Massachusetts. They possess qualities Gropius advocated throughout his career--use of modern materials, simplicity of design, and a marriage of craft and technology.
