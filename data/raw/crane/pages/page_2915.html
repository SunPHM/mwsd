Wauchula awaits those seeking
the serenity of a quaint, historic
downtown dotted with antique
shops, small cafe's and
quiet, shady streets.

Wauchula is a Miccosukee indian
word for "cry of sandhill crane."
There are many scenic, pastoral
views on country roadways(above)
leading into town. The new Peace
River Park will have 160 acres of
hiking trails with a scenic
overlook and canoe launch.

Wauchula

 

 There is a quiet village away from the hub bub of Florida's coastline cities yet within
one hour of theme parks
and beaches.


 
Spend a day or spend the night
in a cozy downtown B&B. Browse among charming store-
fronts in this town that was
founded in the late 1800's.



The annual "Cracker Heritage Festival" (above) occurs each
March. It celebrates the town's rich heritage of cattle, citrus
and the railroad.
