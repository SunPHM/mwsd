PHOTOS - 7-28-02 West St. Crane


On July 28, 2002, a small crane working at the World Financial Center site toppled over onto the West Side Highway, causing the shutdown of the road adjacent to Ground Zero for several hours. At the time of the incident, the operator was charged with being intoxicated while operating the crane. There were no other injuries. (OEM photos: Chris Hetherington)

 


The crane's boom crashed through a construction fence and onto three southbound lanes of the West Side Highway, adjacent to Ground Zero. The NYPD rerouted traffic until the crane could be uprighted.

 


Another view of the crane's boom, looking south. Police at the time charged the operator with being intoxicated while operating the vehicle. No one else was injured.

 


Other cranes were brought in to upright the smaller crane.

 


Several hours later, the crane was uprighted, and the West Side Highway was reopened.
