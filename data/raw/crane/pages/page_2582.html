Welcome to National Wildlife's "PhotoZone," an interactive Web feature highlighting nature photographers, their stories and their work. PhotoZone currently showcases winners from the magazine's 31st through the 35th Annual Photo Contests. In the future, photo-taking tips and other special features will be introduced.

It has always been National Wildlife's goal to reach out to readers through images as well as written word. The editors created this special web section to honor that sense of visual connection.

Photo Caption Contest

Each year, National Wildlife's photo contest receives nearly 5,000 images, prompting a wide range of reactions from awe to laughter.

From our 2005 finalists, editors pulled 12 special photographs that stand out uniquely in humorous and endearing ways. The images stir the imagination, whether by personifying wildlife or hinting at a bigger story behind the landscape.

Photo Caption Contest
Feb 2006 - Enter Contest
Through these pictures, viewers can participate in an interactive caption contest that will run during the first week of each month as an extension of Photo of the Week. Caption contest judges, who include National Wildlife editors and the very photographers whose images are being displayed, will judge entries on the basis of wit, creativity, humor and originality. Winners will be awarded a two-product prize package, worth over $100, sponsored by the Midland Radio Corporation. Winners will be announced on the 15th of the following month, and winning captions will appear on this website.

To be eligible to win, you must provide contact information, and agree to abide by the contest rules. See the full rules.

Photo Contest


Photo: © LAURE W. NEISH
"We can appreciate only what we see, and what we appreciate we might try to conserve," says Andrei Sourakav. The Florida entomologist, who uses macrophotography to make what he calls the "strange but beautiful world of insects" more accessible to people, is one of ten amateurs whose images were chosen as the winners of National Wildlife's 34th annual photography competition. Their photos were chosen from more than 4,500 entries submitted during the past year. They were selected by the editors on the basis of originality and technical execution—and their potential for helping viewers gain a greater appreciation for the natural world. The grand prize winner is this serene image of a barn owl, captured on film by educator Laure W. Neish of British Columbia.

Photo of the Week


Photo: © J.C. BAGLEY

Each year, National Wildlife receives thousands of submissions for its annual photo contest. The contest is extremely competitive not only because of the large number of entrants, but also the exceptional quality that so many pictures demonstrate. In past years, the editors have been limited to choosing only a handful of winners. Now, through PhotoZone, National Wildlife is able to extend recognition to another level of finalists.
