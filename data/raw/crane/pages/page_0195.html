The second round of Rastra stacking occurred on Friday July 2nd.  Diane was able to get a crane from a company in south Austin.  I came up for the full day to help out.  At first I thought I would just be directing the crane as I had done before but I pitched in and wound up strapping the blocks to the crane hook and guiding the crane operator to where they were to be placed.  On that day we finished the few pieces left on the guest house and did all of the main house.  There are not many pictures from this day as I was too busy working.

In this shot we see the crane brining another pallet of material to be stacked on the courtyard area.  The pallet contains both 12" wall blocks as well as deck blocks.  We had to separate them out.  This was quite a pain as each block weighs in at around 300 lbs.

This view shows the main house as viewed across the courtyard from the guest house.  Note once again the open ends.  Also note the end panels, these are in the center of the picture.  Each square block is really two end panels.

This view shows the main in the foreground with the guest in the back.   The large openings left in each structure are where the large doors are going.   Instead of cutting them out we will frame around them.

This view shows the courtyard opening in the guest house.  This will be the main entrance for it.  Several items of interest.  Notice that the end panels have been installed.  This means that all of the horizontal rebar is in the walls.  Also notice the special rebar over the opening.  This is to create a solid concrete beam to span this distance.  A modified Rastra block will be put over this rebar and it will look the same from the outside, but on the inside it will be solid concrete. We have this same feature over the entry of the main house as well as on the west side sliding glass doors.

It is easy to forget that there are hundreds of feet of 5/8" rebar in these walls.  Here is the picture of where it is being cut.  This is in the area that we call the Persimmon Plaza that will be the parking area.  Woody of course has to check things out.
