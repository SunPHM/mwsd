A case in point...

I worked on a case with an attorney who needed to completely reconstruct a traffic accident that occurred on a rural desert road.   He wanted to photograph and video tape the re-enactment.  I interviewed with my attorney client.  I interpreted his words into visuals, set a scenario and technique for showing the chain of events, drew up a complete proposal and offered the ideas, timelines, budget and possible results.  

I coordinated a large crew, obtained permits for film and movie-making, put stunt drivers into cars and an end-dump tractor / trailer rig that matched the year, make and colors of the vehicles involved in the original incident.  We used multiple still camera and video angles, a helicopter platform, a 60-foot boom platform, police, Highway Patrol, fire and highway maintenance personnel.  We re-staged the accident numerous times, shooting from every possible angle.   We edited the material to present the chain of events from the participants point of view.  

Earthquake damages

Another attorney wanted to show how the foundation of his client's single-story home was damaged by the Northridge earthquake.  Even though it was on a hillside, the attorney wanted to show that the slippage of his client's home was not due to the slope as much as it was due to the quake.  I had to crawl through a small, 20"x20" access opening beneath the house.  Along with their geological expert, I proceeded to document plants and vine growth markings, concrete foundation cracks and off-sets, tilted footings, split and twisted floor beams, nail pull-outs, and separated pipelines.  

Construction defects

Another attorney wanted to show how the concrete parking surfaces at a large L.A. area Produce Market were being cracked and torn up by the constant truck traffic.  They were looking at possible construction defect claims and needed to show the daily volume of truck traffic that existed and how that traffic might be affected during the reconstruction phase.  I coordinated with their various experts to show the area from the air as well as from the ground and from within the businesses themselves.  

Documentations on location

I photographed the corrosive effects of long-term exposures to heavy metal salt residues.  The location was at a large power plant, in the desert.  I shot in the huge water cooling towers and in the worker's living quarters that surrounded the site, near Fresno.  I shot the fans, electrical lines, tiny salt crystals and severely corroded metal parts in the general area.

Airplane crashes

I documented every visible surface, inside and out, of a jet airplane that crashed at a local airport.  I shot everything from the tail to the missing nose cone, the flight deck and aeronautics bay, passenger seats and overheads.  I shot the hydraulic lines inside the baggage compartment, the shredded engines and the damaged control surfaces on the wings.

Fire and arson investigation

I recorded the results of an L.A. area school property that was hit by an arsonist.  I've shot hillside homes ravaged by firestorms.  I routinely fly helicopters and airplanes to photographically record everything from huge parcels of land to individual intersections involved in traffic disputes.  I've shot toxic dumps, land fills and huge water/chemical tanks.  I can fly from just about any airport, covering the entire country.

Construction and demolition activities 

I worked to document structural cracks at a 15-story office building and adjacent parking structure in Santa Monica.  They suffered extensive damage during the Northridge quake.  I used crack gauges, creative lighting and extreme close ups to record the damage to some of the floors, walls and ceilings.
