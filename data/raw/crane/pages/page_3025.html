"My paintings are a celebration of my faith; a celebration of the beautiful world we have in God's gift to us; an expression of my love of nature and the beauty and peace I find there. I try to convey the joy of flowers and birds, their myriads of colors, textures, and shapes. We take so much for granted and treat our Earth so uncaringly. I paint because it is fun and I love to, but also from a deeper sense of awe, reverence, and gratitude. We fail to understand that we are not distinct from nature. We are a part of it. Nature is not 'out there,' it is in each of us. As you respond to my work, I know that I have inspired you to take a fresh look at the miracles around us.

 "My studio is our land in Niles, Michigan -- forest, fields, and swamp. Also, I have several gardens including a water garden (the pond has been found by over 150 frogs!), wild flowers,and cottage-type perennial gardens. These habitats attract the birds, butterflies, and animals found in my paintings, fancifully perched in flowery bouquets or realistically portrayed in their environment.

"Working with transparent watercolors and tiny brushes in this highly detailed style, it often takes several days to complete a painting. The paint is applied in layer after layer of color and shadow until the desired effect is reached, finishing with the tiny detail of veins and feathers.

"At Adrian College, I majored in Fine Art and Elementary Education. I then taught elementary art for two years. My love of nature and natural artistic talent have since led to a full-time profession as a watercolor artist.

"I was honored to be asked to be one of five artists to illustrate Birds of Michigan, published in connection with Michigan Audubon Society. The following companies have used my artwork on their products: Sunrise Publications - premier artist; National Wildlife Federation - cards and calendars; Bradford Exchange - collector plates; Pimpernel - place mats and coasters; Field Craft - apparel. 1999 Wildlife Artist of the Year, National Wildlife Art Show, KS.

"I would love to have acres full of all the different flowers, all blooming at once and lasting forever. At least I can imagine it on paper! So when you see a painting with flowers that do not bloom at the same time, or with a bird specially 'flown in,' you will know I was just dreaming."
