INSTRUCTIONS FOR USING OUR LINE BORE EQUIPMENT:
ALWAYS WEAR SAFETY GLASSES ! * ALWAYS KEEP HAIR, CLOTHING, HANDS AND FINGERS CLEAR !
It helps to have a 2nd person to assist with the setup.
This is intended for reboring pivot holes in heavy equipment such as cranes, backhoes, endloaders, excavators and tractors powered by your magnetic drill with a 3/4 inch capacity drill chuck.
1) Using the two outermost holes that you wish to repair, insert one plastic alignment cone (or metal sleeve for small holes) into each hole. Slide the boring bar through each cone (or sleeve). If using the line-up sleeves (for small holes), carefully wrap tape concentrically around the outer diameter of each lineup sleeve so that a snug fit is achieved between the lineup sleeve and the hole. It sounds strange but the tape wrap works well.
2) Insert the boring bar through the lineup sleeves or cones.
3) Bolt each bearing to the center of a bearing backup plate using bolts and washers.
4) Slide one bearing & plate on one end of the boring bar and the drill base adapter (with built-in self-aligning bearing) onto the other end. Attach riser blocks to bearing plates and drill adapter base. Determine where riser blocks can be welded to connect bearing plates to equipment to be line-bored. Using 4.5 inch riser blocks on one side of hole (a caliper must fit under for measuring and 4.5" allows easy alignment cone removal). The 4.5" inch riser blocks allow you to access one side of the hole for cutting tool adjustment and measuring of the hole diameter with the digital caliper (a flashlight helps to see).
5) Remember that the drill base adapter is also used to attach the BOA-308-S1 Bore Welder.
6) Weld all stand-offs to heavy equipment to be line bored using large tack welds.
7) Slide out the boring bar and remove the lineup bushings or cones from under the bearing plates.
8) Add more bearings where possible to prevent vibration during boring. We recommend one on each side of every hole you wish to bore (unless there are more than 2 holes). Try using the small steel bars (1 inch thick) to hold the bearing plate closer to the other side of the hole.
9) After welding all the bearings in place it may be necessary to loosen the bolts that hold the bearings to the plates (one bearing plate at a time) and tap the boring bar slightly because the welding process may create a tension that can reduce the free movement of the bar through the bearings.
10) Carefully tighten the chuck of your magnetic drill onto the 3 flats of the shank of the boring bar while supporting the weight of the mag drill. Use a jacking screw in the adapter base threaded tab to support and position the drill. It may be helpful to loosen and retighten the drill base swivel if your mag drill has it. Be careful not to bend the shank of the boring bar with the weight of the magnetic drill press.
11) On the Safety Adapter Base; tighten the 4 side clamping bolts against the drill magnet base. In case of electrical power failure, this is to help prevent the magnetic drill from injuring you.
12) Insert cutting tool into boring bar. Set screw in place to proper dimension for cutting.
13) Apply oil to cutting tip or spray oil in hole. Check to make sure the drill chuck is still tight on the shank.
14) Begin cutting at low RPMs. You should be able to remove up to 1/16 per side with each pass using the left hand cutting tool bits at low RPMs (to avoid burning up your cutting bits stay under 200 RPM). If the cutting tool is fighting hard, apply less pressure, to make the carbide cutting tools last longer. Check often to make sure drill chuck is still tight on the shank.
15) The left hand cutting tool bits are for rough cutting and the center cut tool bits are for finishing the hole diameter.
ALWAYS WEAR SAFETY GLASSES ! * ALWAYS KEEP HAIR, CLOTHING, HANDS AND FINGERS CLEAR !
There are usually two options to repair pivot holes on heavy equipment:
(16A) Holes can be welded and then bored back to the original specs. Unless you purchase a Bore Welder such as the BOA-308-S1 this can be time consuming because it requires a lot of welding to fill in the worn spots. Also, make sure you use the correct type of soft weld rod/wire for reboring with carbide cutting bits. 6011 is easier to machine than 7018 but either will work. If any doubt, ask your welding supplier. Afterwards you have to bore, measure, more boring, and more measuring.
(16B) Holes can be bored larger than spec size, and then a sleeve can be welded into the hole (we recommend about 3/4 diameter larger) or an oversize bushing can be installed if there was a bushing before. This is an exceptionally productive method because when the holes become worn out again simply replace the old sleeve or bushing with a new one. We recommend that you keep a record of the size of the sleeves and the machines in which you place them. They are easily made on a lathe. Original Spec size is the original hole diameter specified by the manufacturer (such as Caterpillar) to fit the outer diameter of the pivot shaft, or if there is supposed to be a bushing, then spec size is the hole sized to press fit the outer diameter of the bushing that the shaft fits into. Check with the manufacturer for original specifications. Oversize bushings can often be purchased from an industrial bearing supplier or from a heavy equipment dealer.
17) Stop to measure often. Use digital caliper provided or optional dial indicator bore gage.
18) When holes are finish sized, remove Magnetic drill and all bearings.
19) Using either a torch or hand grinder remove bearing support plates.
20) If you are careful you should use 1 or 2 cutting bits per hole set. Welded holes are hard on carbide bits.
21) Our standard equipment is capable of boring up to 5.5" diameter but please be aware that extending the cutting tool bit out far increases unwanted vibration (tool chatter). To bore holes larger than 5 inches, use the optional oversize adapter which allows hole boring up to 8 inches. The adapter slides on the boring bar and is designed to be screwed into one of the threaded holes and then clamped in place with place by tightening the clamping screw. The cutting bit can then be used inside the adapter to bore up to 8" diameter. The adapter comes with 8" diameter cones and longer bearing mounting plates to bridge larger holes. If boring over 5 inch diameter with the optional oversize adapter, take very small cuts to avoid over-torqing the shank.
NOTE: The carbide inserts can be modified or sharpened with a green silicon grinding wheel mounted on a bench/pedestal grinder.
