Heron Flats Trail

Distance: 1.4 miles
Accessibility: Challenging to Difficult

This is by far my favorite trail. It crosses several types of terrain from marsh edge, along the coast of the bay, and through thicket. This photo shows one of the ponds (often claimed by alligators) near the start of Heron Flats. Follow the trail up to the new observation deck.

Observation Deck

This view is from the entry ramp of the Observation Deck shortly after it was constructed. From this accessible vantage point, you can look across a wide sweep of habitat. There are two newly installed telescopes (1 wheel-chair accessible) which can provide you with a closer look at some of the area's wildlife.

Front Trail

These photos are typical of this section of the trail. Keep a sharp eye out for shorebirds--often this section is one of the most rewarding in the entire refuge. If you are lucky, you may spot some of the rare Whoopers. As you walk along the front portion of the trail, you will notice several marshy ponds. The water levels vary greatly depending on the amount of rainfall. This is an excellent place to see alligators as well as wildfowl. Once when walking along this section of the trail, I heard the unmistakable sound of several whitetail deer bounding though the water heading my way. I stood close to a large bush, and directly in front of me the deer shot through a gap, ran across the trail and disappeared in the undergrowth on the other side.
