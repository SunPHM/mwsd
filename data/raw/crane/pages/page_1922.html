NAME OF THE ORGANISM :
Whooping Crane

DESCRIPTION :
    The whooping crane  has a long neck, long, dark pointed bill and long, thin black legs. A large male is about 1.5 meters tall. The bird from close up is snowy white with black bristle like feathers on the crown and face. There's a small black patch on the back of the head below the crimson crown. The  whooping crane has bright yellow eyes. The juvenile birds have  dark brown eyes and cinamon and white feathers.

LOCATION :
    The whooping crane can be found in Saskatchewan during the fall season. The best place to find them are wetlands, fields and barley and wheat stubble. They can also be found in Alberta and the Northwest Territories.

FOOD / BEHAVIOUR :
    The whooping crane eats fish, frogs, snails, small birds, berries, rodents, snakes, leeches, roots of aquatic plants, grain from farmer's fields and aquatic animals.

ANYTHING INTERESTING :
    When they call you can hear them from 2 miles away. Whooping cranes mate for life.

LATIN NAME OF AVAILABLE :
Grus Americana
