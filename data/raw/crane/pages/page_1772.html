Whooping Crane

Where in the World?

Today, whooping cranes are not in immediate danger of extinction, but disease, bad weather, and human land use decisions could affect them. There are about 414 whooping cranes left in North America. There are about 200 birds in the one wild migratory flock that lives in Wood Buffalo National Park, on the border between the Canadian province of Alberta and the Northwest Territories. Each winter this flock migrates to the Gulf coast of Texas. The spend the winter near and at the Aransas National Wildlife Refuge.

There are also 78 birds in a non-migratory flock in Florida. These birds are the result of captive breeding and reintroduction efforts. Since migration is a behavior that must be learned by cranes, the Florida cranes stay in the areas where they were released and don’t migrate.

There are also several flocks of captive birds. One flock lives right here in Wisconsin at the International Crane Foundation in Baraboo. The rest are located in the following places: U.S. National Biological Service’s Patuxent Wildlife Research Center in Maryland, Calgary Zoo in Canada, White Oak Conservation Center in Florida, the San Antonio Zoo in Texas, and the Audubon Center for Endangered Species Research in New Orleans.
