        Fig. 9  Basic structure of the system and outline of its functions

(1) Silage Unloader "Silo-crane": Some farms have more than one unit. One unit is installed to one row of silos.
(2) Receiving/temporary storage unit (receiving tank): From two to four units are installed according to varieties of materials.
(3) Double axis screw mixer: Often installed underground according to physical distribution as shown in the diagram.
(4) Feed distribution unit: Arrow at the bottom of the diagram shows reciprocation motion at the time of receiving TMR.
(5) Baled hay: Used by many farms.
(6) Baled hay cutter: Several types are available.
(7) - (9) Underground square silos: While 4 units are shown in this diagram, most farms have 8 to10 units. They are mostly 3 meters wide to match the size of "silo-crane" and some silos are shorter in length depending on the material. (7) is intended for cut hay, (9) is intended for silage and (8) is intended for mixed silage.
(10) Roughage weighing conveyor: Not installed on the first model.
(11) Feed Tank: Three to five units are normally installed. Cubed hay is also supplied from here.
(12) Concentrate feed pre-mixing/weighing unit: Supplies concentrate feed to the mixer after pre-mixing it here.
(13) Supplement supply/mixing unit: Materials are input here manually.
(14) Transport conveyor: Varies according to arrangement and structure of cattle shed.
(15) Trough: Feed is distributed on a herd basis.
(16) Hoist rail for silo crane: Receiving/temporary storage unit is placed at the end of this rail.
(17) Rail for feed distributor: Needed for the entire length of the trough.
