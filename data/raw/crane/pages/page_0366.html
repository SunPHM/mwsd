Some of the Exhibits at the Museum of Power

Shown below are some of the exhibits which are currently displayed at the Museum.  It would not be possible to list all the exhibits, there are just too many.  However, the aim of this page is to give you a 'taster' of what you might be able to see.



The 'Coles' Crane, which was serviced by Ford's and is certified for use. 


Ransomes M G 2 Light Agricultural Caterpillar Tractor, circa 1954.


Joseph Evans Reliable Steam Pump No. 19473, built in 1916.  One of the collection of 'Evans' Steam Pumps


A 'Sectioned' Ford Fiesta 

R A Lister Auxiliary Diesel Engine, showing a compressor used to start a ship's engine

"Power" is required to drive all forms of machinery, so the Museum has a collection of machine tools, such as lathes as seen in the picture above.

How and Where To Find Us

The Museum is located on the B1019, the Maldon to Hatfield Peverel (off the A12) road in Essex. It is situated approximately 5.4 km (3.4 miles) from Hatfield Peverel. The Museum of Power is open at the following times: -

 

From November to Easter open on Fridays to Sundays from 10:00 to 4:00pm.
From Easter to October open on Wednesdays to Sundays from 10:00am to 5:00pm.

 

Entrance to the grounds and tea-room are free, however a charge is made for access to the rest of the Museum.

Adults:  £3.00          Children 6 to16: £2.00           Children 5 and under:  Free              Family:  £8.00

Groups (10+):   £2.00 each           Evening Group visits (any size):  £50.00
