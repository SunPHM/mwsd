"Someday.... if that Sissy Hankshaw ever shows up here, I'm gonna teach her how to hypnotize a chicken. Did you know chickens are the easiest critters on Earth to hypnotize? You just twirl a chicken in the air twenty times - it's yours forever."

Sissy Hankshaw, played by Uma Thurman, was born with extremely long thumbs that in later life she would find very useful for hitchhiking across the America. She becomes friends with a group of cowgirls who take over The Rubber Rose Ranch and drive out the owners.

Bonanza Jellybean, the leader of the cowgirls, is played by River's eldest sister, Rain Phoenix in her first major film role as an adult actress.

Rain puts in a solid performance bringing her likeable character to life - a character the script describes as "the cutest cowgirl in the world."

Also worthy of mention is the movie's sound track. The various songs playing throughout the film seem to capture perfectly the spirit of the unfolding story. Much of the music was written and performed by k.d. lang, who was one of River's favorite musicians, a fellow animal rights activist who, like Akeka's Attic also donated songs to various fund-raising albums. The pair would meet for the first time at the Hard Rock Café, New York - the venue for the launch party of the PETA benefit album Tame Yourself.

At the beginning of the film, Sissy hitchhikes towards New York to visit her friend the Countess. Her journey takes her along one particular desert road, a straight drawn out highway that stetches to the horizon - the most inconspicuous of roads. Nevertheless, it is a road that has since become forever immortalized with one of the director's earlier movies and with another young actor in one of his finest ever performances.


Even Cowgirls Get The Blues


My Own Private Idaho

The film had its first screening at the Toronto Film Festival a month before River died. The movie was greeted with such a poor response from the critics that Gus Van Sant postponed the film's national release and began heavily re-editing it for a national release in 1994. It was during this editing process that a dedication was added which reads simply:-

Trying to spot River's small uncredited part in this movie is quite a challenge. Indeed, we managed to sit through the entire film first time round and miss it altogether. We feared that his part was now on the cutting room floor following the film's extensive reworking. We had read that he plays the part of a hippy seeking the old Chinese man who lives in the hills surrounding the ranch.

However, the hippies in question turn out to be none other than Sue Solgot (River's second girlfriend), Mike Parker and Scott Green. Parker and Green are in fact the real life people who were the basis for the characters of Mike Waters and Scott Favor that were portrayed by River and Keanu Reeves in Van Sant's earlier work, My Own Private Idaho.

Then suddenly, there he was. The health farm ranch lies on the migratory path of the Whooping Crane and towards the end of the movie, when the police besiege the ranch, River appears as a bird-watcher. He is heavily disguised though so it is somewhat difficult to make him out.
