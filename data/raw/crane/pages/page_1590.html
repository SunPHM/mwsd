BUILDINGS

There are approximately 1500 buildings on the site; this figure includes many air raid shelters, unusual buildings useful only to a filling factory, small offices, ablution rooms etc.

An analysis of the buildings has been made with a view to establishing how much floor space would be suitable for normal industrial usage. 

There are:

  1. 420 buildings with a total area of 1,800,000 square feet suitable for normal industrial work.
  2. 127 buildings with a total area of 453,000 square feet suitable for stores.

Buildings under (i) are reasonably large buildings with rail bays, and moderate sized buildings in the interior of the sections. Most of these buildings have good natural lighting, while under (ii) are all buildings which, because they are too small or have no windows, are unsuitable for industrial work.

Apart from the two main administration offices the factory area is well provided with office accommodation, though most of the process buildings are quite suitable for offices, in fact, many are being used as such at the present time.

The standard office building on the factory comprises one large general office with six small private offices, and lavatory and cloak room accommodation for men and women.

The standard 60' x 30’  buildings are constructed with steel stanchions or brick piers carrying the roof trusses and on the flank walls are filled in between the stanchions with hollow tiles covered with bituminous felt. The end gable walls are in 9"’ brickwork. The roofs are carried on steel trusses, one third pitch, covered with boarding and felt. In some cases corrugated asbestos sheets, lined on the underside, are substituted.


60'x30' buildings

Floors of concrete designed for a superficial load of 2 cwts. per square foot are either covered with linoleum or asphalt.

Each 60’ x 30’ building has two porches with internal end external double doors, the 40’ x 20’ buildings have one porch of similar type. Emergency doors are provided on the opposite side of each shop.

Ample natural lighting is provided, by metal industrial sash windows part top hung for ventilation.

Large transit buildings are constructed, 

  1. with 14" brick walls and 6” concrete flat roofs carried, on steel joists supported on stanchions and covered with bituminous felt, 
  2. steel and, asbestos sheet walls (with 5’ dwarf brick walls) and north light roofs on steel trusses, covered with corrugated asbestos sheeting.

Both types have concrete floors asphalt covered.

The buildings mentioned under (i) and (ii) above are well intermingled i.e. buildings suitable for industrial work are never far from buildings which are suitable purely as stores. Many of the 123 buildings shown as being suitable for stores are provided with rail and road access. The construction of the large buildings suitable for stores is such that 1 ton blocks can be fitted at the present time.

 
Road with buildings

In addition, some of the stores are fitted with electrically operated travelling one ton cranes. In two buildings hand operated travelling ten ton cranes are available, and in a third building a hand operated ten ton jib crane is installed. All of these building equipped with cranes have road and rail access.

Typical of the large building suitable for industrial work is building 8.B.1, which is 200x128x18'6" high ‘ This building has both rail and road access and is equipped with five one ton travelling overhead cranes. The floor area is 2,600 ft.


Typical transit store

Most of the services facilities are concentrated in what is known as the 10.A. Group situated at the north end of the factory, near School Lane Gates. In addition to the central service group,  fitters and joiners shops for plant and building maintenance purposes are distributed throughout the factory. The fitters shops on the factory have been used primarily to implement the planned maintenance scheme and are fitted with sufficient equipment to implement that scheme - lathes, drills, etc.
