 We are familiar with the lifting needs of port authorities, including dockside and containerized freight cranes. QDS is a particularly good choice for existing crane retrofit packages:

Typical minimal cost package:

  • conversion from DC analog to DC digital controls

  • re-use of existing DC motors

  • layout on backpanels to match existing with master terminal blocks and wire numbering such that existing field wiring can be reused

  • re-engineering to eliminate much of the relay control logic by placing this within the drive or a small PLC

  • renewal of discrete operator controls

Such a system is inherently more reliable and easier to maintain than older analog systems.

Complete retrofits to the newest technology typically include:  

  • conversion from DC analog to AC regenerative common bus controls

  • replacement of DC motors with lower maintenance AC induction type employing digital encoders

  • layout on backpanels to fit into existing panels or electric houses.

  • re-engineering to convert system to fully digital, including the addition of a PLC and direct drive to PLC high speed digital communications network

  • renewal of discrete operator controls and addition of optional color graphics display for operator feedback, system diagnostic message display and recording.

  • upgrade of load monitoring and safety systems

  • renewal of much of the field wiring

QDS also has designs for a variety of barge moving and positioning systems. These have been implemented using AC common bus, DC regenerative and eddy current technologies:

Barge Movers (provide nominal position control)

  • AC Common Bus, DC regenerative controllers or eddy current with winch holdback brakes

  • AC or DC motors or air-cooled eddy current clutches

  • AC and DC speed/torque controlled drives to maintain line tension in both directions without holdback brakes.

  • discrete operator controls configured to operate mover at either a preset fixed speed in either direction, or configured with joysticks to allow operator direction and speed control

Barge Positioning Systems (provide precise position control):

  • AC Common Bus or DC regenerative controllers

  • AC or DC motors with encoders

  • speed/torque controlled drives to maintain line tension in both directions

  • encoder information and winch limit switches used to track line positions, often including corrections for cantenary angle

  • optionally provided with a master PLC, direct drive to PLC digital communications and programmable human-machine interface in the main operator console
