Posted Tuesday, December 4, 2001

Paper money

Eight Ashland Middle School students put their origami skills to the test, making hundreds of paper cranes (above) to raise money for Afghan children. The cranes are on display at Soundpeace in downtown Ashland and will likely be auctioned off to raise more money for Afghan children. The sixth grade boys accepted bids on the number of cranes they made and have raised more than $1,000 so far through the project. Soundpeace is located at 199 E. Main St. Ashland Daily Tidings/DENISE BARATTA

Creek restoration to be discussed
Due to strong public interest, the federal Bureau of Reclamation will hold a public workshop to provide information and gather input on a plan to restore a creek tributary in the Greensprings area that suffered severe erosion damage when it was used to transport irrigation water in 1993.

DMV adds new requirements for obtaining licenses, Ids
Requirements for obtaining an Oregon identification card or replacement driver's license will be tighter beginning Jan. 1, according to Oregon Department of Transportation officials.

Sadler speaks at Headwaters event
Russell Sadler, journalism professor and Jefferson Public Radio commentator, will deliver a keynote address entitled "Environmental Activism in Troubled Times" at the Headwaters annual banquet and meeting on Saturday, Dec. 8.

Slideshow on wood products set at Evo's
Aaron Jackson of the ForestEthics Wood Campaign, which encourages United States companies to avoid purchasing wood from endangered forests, will present a slideshow and facilitate a discussion at 7 p.m. on Friday, Dec. 7 at Evo's Java House, 376 E. Main St.

Outdoor enthusiasts now can apply for a chance to float down the Wild Section of the Rogue River during the coming rafting season.

Charter to run for judgeship
Joseph M. Charter has announced his bid for Circuit Court Judge of Jackson County, opposing Bill Purdy in the nonpartisan May 21, 2002 election.

Lights for Life tree illumination set
TALENT - City officials will arrive at the Talent Community Center early Wednesday night to take part in the Lights for Life tree lighting ceremony prior to the city council meeting.

Boulevard design goes on display
Ashland residents are invited to view the latest plans for the Siskiyou Boulevard redesign 4:00 p.m. to 7:00 p.m. Thursday in the Arena of Stevenson Union at SOU.

Zoning gets nod
PHOENIX - Area residents spoke in favor of a request to re-zone tow parcels of land located at 901 Fifth St. and 413 N. C St. as they went before the city council Monday.

Correction
Some inaccurate information provided to The Tidings was subsequently published in Monday's article on the Ashland School District's community meetings in the face of budget cuts. Closing an elementary school would save the district about $338,000.

Also, a Saturday story was unclear in stating The Oregon Shakespeare Festival's new theater is currently named the New Theatre.

Community Notes

· `LIGHTS FOR LIFE' CAMPAIGN will begin with the annual tree lighting ceremony at 6:30 p.m. Wednesday in front of Talent City Hall. Mayor Marian Telerski will do the honor of lighting the tree. Ashland Community Hospital Foundation has a combined Ashland/Talent combined campaign goal of $35,000. For information, call 482-7744.

· FINANCIAL AID AND SCHOLARSHIP MEETING will be presented by the Ashland High school Counseling Office from 7 to 9 p.m. Wednesday in the Mountain Avenue Theater of AHS. The meeting is for anyone planning to attend a private vocational or technical school, a community college, or a four-year college or university.

· PROGRAM FEATURING ACCOMPLISHED ACTORS will be sponsored by SOU Friends of the Library at 7:30 p.m. Thursday in Room 330 of the Stevenson Union at SOU. Deb Note-Farwell and Jonathan Farwell will discuss their individual and collective experiences of acting, changes in acting training in the past few decades, and how the art and industry of acting have evolved in theory and practice. Free. For information, call 552-6835.

· FINNISH INDEPENDENCE DAY PARTY will be held at 6:30 p.m. Thursday at the Standing Stone Brewing Co., 101 Oak St., Ashland. The second annual event will feature music by the Polka Dots and admission is free. For information, call 488-0407.

· `NSA AND HEALTH: IMPROVED QUALITY OF LIFE' will be presented from 6:30 to 8 p.m. Thursday at 94 Third St. (corner of Third and C Streets) in Ashland. Dr. Brian Duby, D.C., will discuss the benefits detailed in a U.C. Irvine Medical School study on Network Spinal Analysis. Free. For information, call 488-9873.
