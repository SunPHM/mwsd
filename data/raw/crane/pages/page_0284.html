GWR WAGON KITS

GWR 10ton LOCO COAL WAGON (Dia.N30)

The final GWR design for small loco coal wagons introduced in 1935.
This type of wagon would have been used to supply coal to small depots.
Includes correct 'left over right' brakegear.

GWR/BR10ton DROPSIDE BALLAST WAGON (DiaP18/20)

'STARFISH' - built from 1941, this design was also built by BR. Also used for loco sand.
Includes D/C brake lever to make P15 type built from 1935. (Shown in photo)

GWR SHUNTERS' TRUCK (M4/M5)

Built between 1917-48. Once commonly seen at many GWR stations.
Some remained in use well into the diesel era to operate track circuits,
coupled to '08' diesel shunters, e.g. at Paddington.
One of the last shunters' trucks in use was at Fowey China Clay Sidings.

GWR 18ton CHAIRED SLEEPER WAGON (Dia.T12/13)

Distinctive well wagons used by the Permanent Way Dept., built during 1938-44.
Following the change to pre-assembled track panels, in the 1960s sides were fitted to make them usable for concrete products.
One at least was air-piped and still in use at Woking (Surrey) in the 1980s. Another was on the Isle of Wight.

GWR 6ton CRANE & MATCH TRUCK

Small cranes were used where there was not a suitable fixed crane to unload goods,
e.g. containers or farm implements. Several similar cranes remained in use at P.W. yards
until the mid 1980s - Hereford was one.
