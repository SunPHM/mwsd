Why Concrete Prison Cells?

Concrete prison systems are a fast, economical way to build a secure facility. There is a tremendous need for new prisons in the United States. With crime rates higher than ever before, our nation's prisons are suffering from overcrowding. Unfortunately, it takes time for governments to secure the funds they need to build additions or new institutions. Once funds are available, owners want the job done fast, at the lowest cost possible, using high quality materials to ensure a secure facility.

To meet these needs, owners are turning to modular concrete prison systems. There's more to a prison than just cells. Today's prisons include day rooms, fitness facilities, kitchens, chiller/cooler rooms, laundries, cafeterias, work facilities, visitor areas and watch towers.

Concrete assures the highest strength and durability to support heavy loads, meet long span requirements and minimize framing members. And, with fewer visual obstructions from framing members, correctional officers have clear lines of sight to properly supervise inmates. The inherent strength and durability of precast concrete makes this ideal for minimum, medium and maximum security institutions.

Unlike steel, precast concrete is non-corrosive and resists dents, punctures and other abuses caused by man and nature.

When communities need additional detention space, concrete prison systems can provide a fast solution. With year-round production and erection these units meet the fast-track schedules required for prison construction projects. Concrete precast modular boxes, a popular choice for standardized prison housing units, can be cast with openings for electrical conduit, plumbing and other systems. Plumbing fixtures, bunks and other hardware items can be installed right at the plant.

You can order the finished box interiors with a smooth surface. As a result, the modular boxes arrive ready for installation in rows and stacks to facilitate completion. Stacked wall systems combine precast concrete bearing walls, floors and roofing for superior structural stability. The bearing walls support solid floor and roof slabs while serving as partition walls. These stacked wall systems offer tremendous time savings overall for a fast prison completion. Like the precast modular boxes, they can cast interior bearing walls with openings for electrical conduit, plumbing and other systems. Insulation can also be included, and walls are installed ready for painting in the field. The underside of floor slabs can be finished, doubling as a ceiling for the floor below and reducing the total number of pieces needed for a job. Plus, crews can finish stacked wall exteriors in a variety of colors and textures to meet architectural needs and avoid additional exterior work.

Compared to traditional building methods and materials, concrete prison systems are the most cost-effective choice for prison construction projects. Off site fabrication methods reduce the initial cost of precast concrete production, making these units very cost competitive. They also go up faster than other building materials, so you not only save on site time and labor costs, but you get use of the prison sooner.

In addition to lower initial costs, these units also offer tremendous savings over time. Because they're so strong and durable, they require little maintenance, saving communities time and money on upkeep and repairs throughout their long life cycles.
