Pictures from Berlin (Germany)


These pictures have been taken by me in July 2000 in Berlin.

Schloss Charlottenburg. It is the old summer house for the kings of Prussia. The castle is
called the way it is because the central part has been built by Sophie-Charlotte
(well not herself personally), Frederick Ist's mother. Since then, it has been enlarged many
times and the front is not 500m long. It has been nearly completely destroyed during
the bombardments of 1943 (the pre-"chirurgical" strikes), and has been restored with
newly acquired furniture (only a few blackened statues were left).

Part of the visit is made with slippers so that the tourists don't damage the wooden floor!
Photos inside forbidden, so I have nothing to show for it.

From the back, in the French garden.

In one corner of the park, the is a belvedere with an exposition of porcelain.

And at another corner, there is a mausoleum where
Frederic-Wilhelm III and queen Louise are buried.

Brandenburger Tor. It is the infamous border between West and Est Berlin.
The new French embassy built by Christian de Porzampac will be close to the door.

The Wall has disappeared soon after the opening of the border in 1990.
However, in front of the Reichtag, we can see a line where it was standing.

Reichtag. In 1991 (one year after the border between Est and West was open),
the German deputies voted to move the parliament from Bonn to Berlin. The new
parliament has opened in 1999. You can even see some of the sessions behind a
glass wall (a symbol of transparency I guess).

You can go on the roof where there is a glass dome, but you have to deserve it and queue.

The glass dome. I don't know how the Germans see it, but it reminds me of the infamous
Louvre pyramid. It is as weird and out of place.

From the roof, you can see all of Berlin. Berlin is a gigantic construction site.
Apparently, the Germans have decided to destroy the center of Berlin after the
reunification, and rebuild it. I was told you can see 31 cranes from here.

This is the communication tower used by West-Berlin to communicate with West-Germany
when before reunification. Apparently, Est-Germans understood well what the problem
with radio transmissions was, and they listened to all transmissions going through the tower.

On the shore of lakes in the West of Berlin, there are many strange looking
castles.

I saw the bridge where spies were exchanged between the West and the Est!

When Germany was divided, this church was neither in West-Germany, neither
in Est-Germany. As it was in the no man's land, nobody could go there. Therefore
it's been abandoned for 30 years, and has just been restored.

In the Kurfürstendamm area. This strange looking church is the
Keiser-Wilhelm-Gedächtniskirche (memorial to Keiser Wilhelm). It's also known
as the hollow tooth. The church has extensively damaged during WWII. It is now a
place of exhortation and commemoration. The hexagonal tower next to the church is the
new church.

This tower is the TV tower. It must be the best known landmark in Berlin.

I think it's the Marx-Engels forum, but I'm not sure.

This is the St Nicholas church in old Berlin. It's the oldest church in Berlin (the
foundations date back to 1280). It is easily recognizable with its donkey hat like
towers.

This is a street of old Berlin (Nikolaiviertel). It has been destroyed during WWII, and has
been reconstructed on its 750th anniversary.

As I said, the wall has almost entirely disappeared. Most of it has been sold
by Est-Germany who was the legal owner (for 1000000 DM). I found this piece, saved by
Reverent Fischer on the side of his presbyter.
