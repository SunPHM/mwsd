Holiday Traditions of the S-dot Family

The December holiday season is quite festive here at the S-dot family lodge. We have many family traditions that have been practiced since I was a wee nipper. Usually the first thing to be done is decorate the house at the start of the season. This means set up the plastic tree (my mom's allergic to pine needles) somewhere in the house where there's room, dig out the bags and bags and boxes of decorations, find places for them all around the house, dig out the box of tree lights and tree decorations and spend a day putting those on, and then play seasonal music occassionally. Then, as the days get closer to the day of gift-giving (which fluctuates yearly depending on everyone's schedule), the family will spend quiet evenings together, watching TV or some holiday movie we have on video tape. We always watch the same ones, so we know how they'll go, but it's fun nonetheless. Usually we save our favorites for the day of gift-giving or the eve of the day of gift-giving.

And then there's the baking. My mother will bake cookies almost every other day. She also makes her award-winning fruitcake. What awards has it won? Why, the "I Say it's Tasty so DAMNIT, IT'S TASTY!!" award! You can read more about it below. ANYWAY, there's all sorts of treats my mom makes, and we always have a ready supply of eggnog.

As the big day draws near, the movies get better, and the gifts begin to pile up under the tree. Anticipation also rises. Finally, on the eve of gift-giving day, we watch a special movie. This year we watched "A Christmas Story," the movie about a boy who only wants for Christmas a Red Rider Pump-Action Carbine Range-Model BB Rifle (I think I got that right), with a compass in the stock. Very touching story, and completely unrelated to all that religion bologna.

Then, it's off to bed to wait for the fat dude to drop off the rest of the presents. Then, in the morning around 9:00, my mom mkes breakfast for the whole family, and after breakfast, we sit around the tree and open gifts, usually with holiday tunes playing in the background on the large stereo sound system. Afterwards, it's time to play with said gifts while my mother goes back to the kitchen and slaves away on the most delicious dinner feast! All the while there are snacks and punch for consumption, movies to play, and of course, South Park! What's a gift-giving day without South Park and Mr. Hankey? Ah, the fun we have!

Well, 'tis the season. Happy Holidays, everyone! This is Terence S-dot, signing off for now!

December: Joy or Depression. Choose your weapon.

So, alas, I am brought back to posting. Why? Well, I'm done with classes for the Fall semester, and I'm both bored and depressed. Yes, depressed. Yes, I must repeat myself. Unlike a lot of people in this world, I do care about how my friends are feeling. It hurts me to see a friend in need, and it hurts me even more when there's absolutely nothing I can do. There's a lot of injustice in this world, and a lot of blind hearts. I find myself in a constant internal struggle of what I should do and what I could do, and what I really shouldn't think of. I think so much about the ways things could be. Even the bad. That's probably why I never really write any of my actual personal thoughts down here, because it's just a huge jumble of criss-crossing views and points and incoherent thoughts with too much emotion behind them. In Dan terms: Major Flexage!
