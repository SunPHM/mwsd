Make the trip - Sandhill Crane photography tips
Best days

Greater sandhill cranes can be seen at J-P from late September through December. Crane numbers peak in mid-November.

Best bet -- magnificent, noisy flocks are usually seen at the northern Indiana property from mid-October through mid-December.
Best times

Sunrise - Gigantic flocks rise and fly out of roosting marshes to Goose Pasture. The cranes socialize in the pasture for awhile before flying out to feed in area fields and swamps.

Sunset - Beginning about one hour before sunset, flocks of cranes kite into Goose Pasture from all directions. They gab and socialize again before returning to roosting marshes at dusk.

Best place
 The crane spectacle is best seen at the Goose Pasture Viewing Area (map below). Resting marshes are closed to the public. During the day, cranes are often spotted feeding and dancing in nearby harvested farm fields.

Migration
Status
Updated 12/20/05:
We estimate 4000 cranes still using private fields surrounding JP.  We still have snow cover and low temperatures near zero.  Most of these cranes will likely stay the winter, although we have had cranes leave in January.  This will be our last emailed count for the year.  North bound cranes will begin arriving around the first of February.
