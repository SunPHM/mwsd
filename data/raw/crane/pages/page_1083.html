 Web Site
 This site is for the families and friends of Children's Bridge Group 77 expecting to travel to China in July 2000 to adopt 10 beautiful baby girls. The website will follow the journey of the 10 families during this incredible adventure starting with the receipt of the referral pictures on May 26, 2000.

2000-08- 2       Girls at the Canadian Embassyor group shot while getting Canadian Visa

The girls took time out from a busy day to take a quick group shot while getting their Canadian visa forms.

2000-08- 2       Tiananmen Square in the rain.

Unfortunately our only day of bad weather resulted in a wet drive by view of the square. This was my best shot for what it's worth.

2000-08- 2       Forbidden City

This is a view of the throne room from the south entrance gate to the Forbidden City. This is the only part of the "Last Emperor" that was actually filmed in the Forbidden City per our tour guide, Cindy.

2000-08- 2       Marble stone carving at the Forbidden City

Again per Cindy, this carving which is huge was carved from one single slab of marble.

2000-08- 2       Getting a trim

This street vendor had set up shop just outside the Forbidden City's east gate.

2000-08- 1       Beijing Shopping District

Part of the group found an excellent shopping area while out looking for a bookstore. The area is closed to traffic other than buses so it was an enjoyable walk.

2000-07-29       Matin (on the right), his family and Mac

Martin waited on the group for many meals during our stay in Nanchang. Kim. Mac & I were out for a walk one afternoon and Martin invited us back to his flat to meet his family and to enjoy some bean curd, which was very tasty.

2000-07-29       Nanchang Pavillion that group toured



2000-07-29       Tang Dynasty dancing at Pavillion



2000-07-29       Group visit to the orphanage

Very rarely in recent years have adoptive parents been allowed to visit the orphanage. We were honoured with such a visit and allowed to take video and photos. Needless to say it was a heartbreaking experience for us all to see so many children waiting for a home.

2000-07-29       The way to beat hot summer nights

I watched this family throughout our stay in Nanchang which as all will agree was very warm averaging 37 C! Each night this family would climb onto the roof to sleep. Scanning the rooftops of many buildings from the 15th floor of the hotel, this was a common occurance.

2000-07-29       Nanchang airport

I was very impressed with the Beijing and Nanchang airports, they unfortunately put Toronto to shame. Both airports have been rebuilt in the last five years and construction cranes are abundant in both Beijing and Nanchang.

July 19, 2000   The Night Before Travel
'Tis the night before travel
Our bags are all packed.
Our clothes are all folded
and stuffed in their sacks.

We're loaded with formula,
diapers and more.
We went to the pharmacy,
we bought out the store.

We've got wipes for her bottom,
shampoo for her top
I could go on and on,
but I better just stop.

We've divided and organized,
combined and repacked.
We've shuffled, reshuffled
spread out and restacked.

We've folded and rolled,
ziploc'd and stuffed.
We've squeezed and cajoled
Enough is enough!

The house is in order,
the lawn has been mowed
We've been getting ready for months
Now it's time to just GO!

And we'd just like to say
'fore we fly out of sight,
Thanks for all the best wishes
and to all... a Good Night!

Pam & Iain Dilworth Christie



General Instructions

Each family has their own "photo-album" page on the web site.

To view pictures simply click on the name at the left of the screen. The first page displayed is the family's diary, where anyone can post comments. Click on the and buttons to scroll though the pictures. You can post comments on the pictures by filling in your name and comment and clicking the Add button.

To upload a picture, click on the "Add Picture" text or the camera below the family's name; a new web page will be displayed. Use the browse button to locate the picture you want to upload. You can enter a title and some comments about the picture. Enter the password and then click the Upload button.


Other Sites of Interest

Children's Bridge
Making families happen!

The Children’s Bridge is a non-profit, federally-incorporated organization, whose primary goal is to help create families through adoption. We are Group 77.


My Home Town
This web site contains information for adopted Chinese children to find out about their hometown. This site is also a great resource for new parents, aunts, uncles, and grandparents to learn a bit about their new family member's home town. The site is designed and maintained by Mark Tooker.


On-Line Chinese Tools
Your Mandarin isn't up to par? Do you think that Mandarin is only a fruit? If so, check out this web site. It contains plenty of on-line tools to help you with Chinese and its various dialects. Site maintained by Erik E. Peterson.


Department of Foreign Affairs and International Trade
China Perspectives
At this web site you can learn about Canada-China relations, the Canadian presence in China, and other business oriented information on our two countries.

Travel In China
This web site contains valuable information for Canadians traveling to and touring around China. A bit stuffy, but a great resource.


China National Tourism Administration
Tourism information for China, by China.
