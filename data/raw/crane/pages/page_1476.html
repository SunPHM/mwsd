K-4:The Canadian Crane

Construction sites have something in common with the Space Station. They both use big cranes. Cranes move large things. The crane used in space is a lot more complex than those used on Earth.

This crane is called the Canadarm. It is used on the Space Shuttle. It moves cargo. It helps release satellites. It was built in Canada. It was paid for by the Canadian Space Agency. It can reach and move in ways that humans can’t. Canadarm was a success. So, a bigger and better one was built. It is called Canadarm2. It is used on the Space Station.

The Canadarm2 lifts heavy things on the Space Station. It helps the Space Shuttle dock to the Space Station. The arm on the Shuttle can easily reach and move. On the Space Station, it can also move end over end like an inchworm. This helps it reach many parts of the Station. The Station is going to grow even larger. The arm will come in handy.

Canadarm2 gets power from special plugs. They are like electrical outlets used in our homes. Canadarm2 can move anywhere there is a plug. Using the plugs for power means the arm isn’t stuck to one spot on the Station. It is almost like when someone runs a vacuum cleaner in the house. They can unplug it and move from room to room.

The crane is called a robotic arm. There are many good reasons for that. A human arm lifts heavy objects. The Space Station crane has three joints. They work like some of the joints in our body. The joints make bending motions. The arm doesn’t just go up and down. The arm can move at different speeds. The speeds are a lot slower than the way we move. That’s because the arm is a very big machine. That makes it harder to move.

The arm has special sensors that help the astronauts. They tell how much force the arm uses when it touches other objects. There are four cameras on the arm. All this helps the crew move the arm where it needs to go.

It certainly makes sense to have this special arm help build the Space Station. This is the biggest construction job in the universe. Would you expect anything less?
