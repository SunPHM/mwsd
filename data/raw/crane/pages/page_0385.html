 1. Introduction

 

POSTECH Subsonic Wind Tunnel(POSWIT) is located in the city of Pohang in southeastern coast of Korea, which is about 400 kilometers apart from Seoul, the capital of the Republic of Korea. The POSWIT is operated by the AFERC / Dept. of mechanical engineering of POSTECH.

The construction of the POSWIT was begun in 1993 and was inagurated in Nov. 1995. The POSWIT provides the largest size of test section until now(Nov. 1996) and may remain as the largest wind tunnel among those running in the universities in Korea in the future.

The POSWIT is used not only for the research and education but also for industrial applications.

The POSWIT-Staff consists of one director, one research engineer, one technician, and two graduate students.

 

 

 

 

2. Description of POSWIT

 

 Specifications :

  • Size of test section : 1.8W x 1.5H x 4.3L (m)
  • Maximum wind velocity : 75 m/s
  • Minimum wind velocity : 5 m/s
  • Contraction ratio : 9:1
  • Flow uniformity : 0.25%
  • Turbulence intensity : 0.2%
  • Overall dimension of wind tunnel : 14W x 6H x 37L (m)

Special features :

  • Large contraction ratio(9:1) for high flow quality
  • Cooling coil for constant air temperature (within 0.5 K)
  • Modified test section for wind engineering

 

 

 

 

3. Instrumentation/Auxiliary Equipment

Measuring Instruments :

  • PSI8400 Electric Pressure Scanning System for 320 pressure taps
  • 6-Component External Balance (X=500N, Y=1000N, Z=4000N)
  • 6-Component Sting Balance (X=530N, Y=890N, Z=1780N)
  • Aerometrics 2D Fiber Optic LDV
  • TSI 3D LDV
  • Hot-wire System of DANTEC (3 channel)
  • Lecroy Digital Oscilloscope
  • Smoke Generator
  • Micromanometer
  • Data Reduction System : IBM Pentium PC, Printer, Scanner

Auxiliary Equipments :

  • Turn Table / Model Support System
  • 2-D Traverse
  • Hydraulic Lift Stage
  • Operator Cabin

 

 


External Balance 

The external balance is used to measure aerodynamic forces and is one of the basic tools in carrying out experiments with the wind tunnel and is important in terms of drag,lift force and momentum measurements of cars and plane models. The KME3,7-component balance installed in POSTECH Wind Tunnel was made in Braunschweig by the DLR.

  

 

  

 

  

 

  

 

  

Electric Pressure Scanning System
The pressure scanning system is another basic tool for measuring the pressure distribution on the surface of car,plane, and building models. The one in Postech is the PSI8400 system. This equipment can measure the pressure data 20000 times per second, and with 5 pressure scanning modules it can measure the pressure of a total of 320 channels.
