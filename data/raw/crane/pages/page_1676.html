Community Facilities - Resorts

 


Rend Lake Resort
Wayne Fitzgerrell State Park
Franklin County, Illinois

 

Client
Capital Development Board
401 South Spring Street
Springfield, IL  62706

Documents Delivered
Phase I: October 1988
Phase II: September 1990

Project Completion
December 1992

Awards
1992 State of Illinois Thomas Madigan Award for Programmatic Achievement
1992 CECI Achievement Award for Engineering Excellence

 

The six million dollar Rend Lake Resort and associated recreational facilities is a complete destination getaway. The multi-phased project has changed the face of Wayne Fitzgerrell State Park. Completed work includes a full-service restaurant/banquet center with outdoor dining decks and direct access from boat slips. For activities within the complex, there is an outdoor pool, playground and tennis court. Guests may select rental units from large three-room cabin units with private decks to one or two level townhouse style guest units built on the water’s edge with private decks over the water. All facilities are immediately accessible to boat docks and there is even a lakeside convenience center for boaters who don’t wish to leave the water. Nearby campgrounds were totally rehabilitated to reflect the new modern conveniences of the park.

Facilities designed and waiting to begin construction are a complete sailboat harbor with jib cranes, secure overnight parking and a fully-developed swim beach. Each area provides shelters for gatherings of all types.

The Hurst-Rosche Engineers, Inc./Design Architects, Inc. team provided all architectural, engineering, fire alarm system, interior and landscape design as well as construction administration and testing services.

Centreville Urban Park Restoration
Centreville, Illinois

Hurst-Rosche has been retained to physically upgrade a major urban park nestled between a major public housing complex, Touchette regional Hospital, Centreville City Hall and the Civic Center, and adjoining neighborhoods. The park consists of 21 acres of open space for recreational sports (baseball, track and field, tennis, basketball and football), picnic area and bird sanctuary, and an outdoor grass amphitheater for public concerts. The project will renew trees and shrubs, improve sports facilities, improve crowd control, more clearly define "passive areas," and upgrade children’s play areas.

Eldon Hazlet State Park
Campground Rehabilitation
Clinton County, Illinois

The scope of work included the rehabilitation and expansion of the campground to accommodate 336 sites, including the electrical systems; recreational vehicle pads; heating and lighting controls in the three shower buildings; exterior lighting; wiring and lighting at the permit station, concession and dump station; and upgrading the roads and site work. 

Kickapoo State Park
Site Improvements
Vermilion County, Illinois

The campground development scope of work provided for the construction of three host sites, and approximately 145 electrical, 40 non-electrical, and 15 walk-in campsites. The scope also provided for all campground amenities such as roads, parking, lighting, shower buildings, vault toilets and a trailer dump station.

The day use project provided for the construction of four fishing piers, upgrading two canoe access areas and improving the water system. Development of approximately 6,000 lineal feet of hiking trails including clearing and grading for the trail surface, construction of a foot bridge, stairways and breakwater, and regrading the shoreline and stabilizing the bank. The scope also provided for the construction of a beach and swimming area including vault toilets, changing rooms, drinking fountains, sand showers, parking, sidewalks and landscaping.

Tennis Court Renovation
East Alton-Wood River High School District #14
Wood River, Illinois

Hurst-Rosche has designed renovations to the tennis courts to include regrading the area around the perimeter of the tennis courts to provide positive drainage away from the courts and toward stormwater drains and paved areas. Also included is the cutting out of deteriorated areas of the existing asphalt paving on the courts; providing new compacted subbase; installing "Petromat" binding fabric over all patched areas; resurfacing the court with tapered bituminous asphalt to provide positive drainage off the courts; installing new rubberized court surfacing; reapplying court lines; and reinstalling net standards, fence posts, and other appurtenances that were removed to facilitate court renovation.
