Product Information by Type

FLEXIBLE MOLDED LINING

Many applications require the material to be contoured to a specific shape such as a band or a shoe.  The flexible molded linings are semi-cured at the factory so they can be easily shaped, cut and fitted to an installation.  Depending upon the application they are baked to a final cure or allowed to “service cure” from heat cycling in the working environment.  These come in a range of friction coefficients and thicknesses up to ½ ".  They are recommended for light and medium duty service.  The material is manufactured in rolls and strips for processing by FDL-USA, or the customer.  Typical application examples include trailer brakes, washing machines and small crane bands.

 

RIGID MOLDED LINING

Rigid molded materials are generally used for heavy and severe duty applications.  In addition to normally having higher energy and heat handling capability the rigid molded components are structurally stronger.  In some applications they function with gear teeth cut in them and no metal core.  The material is available in panels (flat or arced) or in finished parts.  Typical application examples include drag lines, drum brakes on heavy equipment and press brakes in factories.

 
WOVEN LINING

Woven linings generally maintain a degree of flexibility in service, as well as maintaining exceptional structural integrity thanks to the substrate design.  There are a variety of compounds covering light to heavy-duty applications.  The substrate is woven, treated with a friction compound and baked.  The material is available in rolls or in finished parts.  Typical application examples include winches, oil wells and amusement park rides.
