CABIN FEVER CHALLENGE RESULTS

President Jim with Another Fish that counted toward the trophy!

Yes, the 09 West Virginia Chapter team did it again. Second time in 4 years that we won the Cabin Fever Challunge, this time at Green River Lake.

We had six members fishing the event this year, Carol and Tim McCauley, Sharon and Bill Crane, Larry Robinson and Jim Moore. Also attending were associate members Art and Katie Anderson but Art opted this year to fish with his home chapter.

Bill Crane, Art and Katie Anderson on Green River at Cabin Fever Challunge

Tough fishing conditions prevailed with 5 inches of rain falling on parts of Kentucky on Thursday (I think 4 inches of it was on my boat). The lake rose over the weekend somewhere between 3 and 4 feet we were told. The major incoming streams muddied up (Robinson Creek, the perennial favorite) and had lots of floating debris. The main lake itself was only slightly cloudy but had lots of floating trash also. We had rain a large portion of Friday with some early on Sat. morning giving way to high skies and hot temps. in the afternoon. We awoke Sunday morning to leave for home in heavy rain again.

What is so funny?

Back to the fishing. Seventy (70) people registered to fish the challunge from numerous chapters. A total of 21 fish over 30 inches were reported. Team members registering fish were Jim Moore with a 30 1/2 caught on a small Jones crank (I got a prize for the smallest legal fish entered). Bill Crane caught a great 47" fish on a Crane Bait that he had repaired for Steve Koreski and was giving a test run. Art Anderson was Bill's boat pardner and netted the fish. Bill's fish was the largest of the event and anchored the Traveling Trophy for the Chapter as well as winning the Big Fish pot for $370.00. Sharon had entered him it the big fish contest without him knowing it. (I guess you could say that Bill and I led from wire to wire, or maybe from smallest to biggest) Anyhow, we came home with the bacon.

Most fish caught were small with only 3 being over 40", Bills' 47", a 42" and a 40". I also boated a sublegal and Larry and I both lost other fish. Carol came within a blink of winning the Women's division when a mid-thirty fish tried to eat her bait but she didn't get him. A few more fish were contacted by chapter members.

Another exciting thing to look forward to is on the Simply Fishing TV show in the upcoming season. You will see some familiar faces. Carol McCauley successfully won an auction to fish with host Bob Mehsikomer for a week on Lake of the Woods. Tim and Carol will join Bob for a great week at Wiley Point and be on the show with a chance at some huge fish.

The outing was great with good food and Kentucky hospitality. Greg Thomas gave a good seminar on Friday night and Bob Mehsikomer rounded it out with 2 great presentations on Saturday.

With all of this eating going on, how did we do it?

Congratulations to all who participated and helped to make it a fun weekend.

Jim Moore
