First Marshall Modular operating theatre arrives to help Addenbrooke's with waiting lists - 6th September, 2002

A brand new state-of-the-art operating theatre suite, supplied by Marshall SV of Cambridge, will be installed at Addenbrooke's Hospital on Saturday 7 September 2002. It will be lowered into place, using the country's largest mobile crane, onto a steel framework, where it will join level 3 of the hospital to become an integral part of the hospital's existing 11-theatre operating complex. The new unit will provide ten additional theatre sessions a week.

The new facility exceeds existing and planned NHS healthcare standards. It is a high technology product, which has been designed and built specifically for the NHS by Marshall SV (part of the Marshall of Cambridge Group). At every stage of the design Marshall engineers have liaised with specialist medical Consultants to refine the design and a number of eminent surgeons and consultants have praised this unit.

The new theatre unit will be fully operational within a few weeks and will provide much needed additional operating capacity. The unit houses one operating theatre, a patient preparation area, three recovery beds and staff rest facilities. It will be used for a variety of surgical specialities, helping the Trust to reduce waiting lists.

Robert Marshall, Chief Executive of Marshall SV said:"The Addenbrooke's modular operating theatre suite was designed to match the quality and effectiveness of permanent facilities while being extremely quick to manufacture and install. A benefit of this is that Addenbrooke's will be able to increase the size of their facilities with minimum disruption to everyday working. Everyone who has seen the unit has been highly impressed. The size and quality of the building is such that it's difficult to believe you're in a modular building which can be built so quickly. We're very excited to be working with Addenbrooke's on this important project."

No More NHS Waiting Lists! - 1st June, 2002

The Marshall plan for the NHS is nearing completion. In hangar 14 the relocatable theatre unit is reality rather than just a paper dream.

Built with all the usual Marshall Quality, the unit is a modular, adaptable but quick to construct and install answer to the issues facing the NHS in Britain today.

Healthcare Manager Charles Rose commented "We see this as not just as a unit to cover theatres that are being refurbished, but as a way of helping to tackle waiting lists by increasing capacity".

So far the unit has impressed visitors from many of the leading hospitals in the country and indeed one or two well-known local ones too!

Indications of interest have been for double as well as single theatres and with as well as without the recovery bay that is a feature in the unit on site.
