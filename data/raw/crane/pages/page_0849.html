PHASE 1 CONSTRUCTION PICTURES

THURSDAY, NOVEMBER 14, 2002

On this date the tower is lifted into place by a crane and bolted together.


There was a little snow cover as we began the day getting the tower ready to stand up. First order of business, checking every bolt to make sure they were tight.
Al fashioned these brackets to secure the pipe to. The four antenna bays will attach to this pipe. Notice how it extends several feet higher than the actual tower.

Here's a first look at the crane that will be doing the lifting.
A side view with Al Kilgore checking out it's position.

Here Al is hooking the top of the bottom section of the tower to the crane. To the left is a very bad picture of Rich Frischkorn. The crane operator is in the middle holding the remote control for the crane.


As the crane holds the tower in place we had to get the legs to align with the anchor legs that are embedded into the concrete. It's a very tight fit and required the crane operator to make some very minute movements of how the tower was hanging.

An up close look at the work of aligning the legs to the anchor legs and then bolting them together.
Lifting the top section into place was a little trickier because the crane was not tall enough to simply hook on at the top of the tower and hold it up over the top of the bottom section. We had to hook it as low on the tower section as possible and still be above the balance point so the bottom of the tower section would hang down naturally. This was the first try and as you can see the attach point needed to move up higher.

 Tim, a listener, volunteer, and friend of Al's, is the first to get to the top of the bottom section of tower. From there he will help guide the top part of the tower into place where they can be bolted together.
Here's a good shot of Tim lining up the two sections of tower.

Here's a close up view of the aligning process. The legs from the top section must fit inside the legs of the bottom section.


Al joined Tim on the tower to bolt the sections together.

Here's a good idea of what the finished tower looks like as Al and Tim finish bolting the tower sections together.

 

Here's what the tower looks light as you prepare to climb up it. That's Al about 45 feet off the groud.


And for Tim, who climbed up the inside, this is what he was looking at. That's him, about 45 feet up. 		When the antenna bays arrive they will have to attached to the pipe pictured earlier. A feedline, nearly 2 inches in diameter, will then be attached to the antenna bays and make the connection between the transmitter and the antenna. We'll also have to mount 2 receive antennas and the cable that connects them to the receiveer.

The transmitter is scheduled to arrive the first week of December. Al will be very busy hooking up all of the equipment getting the station ready for broadcasting. Pray that all goes well and for his safety.

Pray also that the FCC approval arrives soon. We can't turn it on until we have that.

Pray also for the remaining funds to come in that are needed to pay for this project.
