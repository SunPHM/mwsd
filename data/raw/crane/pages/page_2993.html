Born in the Netherlands, Gijsbert (Nick to everyone who knows him) developed an early interest in studying and observing nature and wildlife. Every Sunday, since he can remember, his father Andre, would take the entire family of eight brothers and sisters into the woods to explore, examine and experience what he knew to be diminishing resource. The woods and the wetlands became an outdoor classroom where Gijsbert would spend hours observing and sketching.

His formal education began in 1968 when he enrolled in the Royal Academy of Arts in Arnhem, the Netherlands, to study graphic design. Upon graduating, he was employed as a commercial artist and photographer.

He immigrated to Michigan in 1976 and became the art director for the Michigan Natural Resources Magazine. For 17 years he was responsible for the layout design, photos and paintings that accompanied each article. He can attribute his familiarity and love of Michigan's natural resources to his job. Every article meant more knowledge of the beautiful state he immigrated to and a better understanding of the English language. With the sale and privatization of the state magazine in 1993, Gijsbert chose this time to become self-employed. Gijsbert now teaches painting classes to children and adults nine months out of the year in his own gallery and workshop. He also travels to schools offering a variety of programs, from hands-on painting seminars and wildlife rehabilitation to habitat improvement and wildlife photography. His programs are popular statewide.

He has painted large murals that can be found in schools, museums, zoos and state and federal parks. The most recent being the completion of an entirely new exhibit at Fort Mackinac on Mackinac Island and four life size figures in Fort Michilmackinac.

Gisbert is a numerously published artist. He has illustrated the four "Great Lakes Regions" series of books, including "the Mammals, the Ancient Life, the Reptiles and Amphibians, and the Insects of the Great Lakes Regions." You can find his work in Owls of North America and The Best of Wildlife Art. He is also one of five illustrators for The Birds of Michigan, a large hardbound volume with text and illustrations of the birds of Michigan.

His most recent book is "The Legend of Sleeping Bear", a full color, hardcover children's book with 23 of his paintings. He has recently completed the paintings for the next in the Legend series due out in April, "The Legend of Mackinac Island." The publishers, Sleeping Bear Press in Chelsea, Michigan have contracted him to illustrate two more books for the Legend series.

His most honored and prestigious exhibition is the annual Leigh Yawkey Woodson Art exhibition. Selection into the "Bird in Art" exhibition is a goal artists throughout the world strive to achieve. Artists and sculptors of the highest caliber receive honored recognition from the museum and the exhibition. Gijsbert has been so honored 11 times. He was accepted again this year for his African painting of Marabou Storks.
