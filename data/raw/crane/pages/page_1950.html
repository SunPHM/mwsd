Whooping Cranes Hatching!

These whooping crane eggs show how life started for a very special group of chicks. In a bold migration experiment starting in 2001, whooping crane have been raised in captivity and reintroduced, or brought back, to the eastern part of North America. The chicks in these eggs will fly the skies when they they grow up and take off in a few months on their first migration, with an ultra light airplane leading the way!

The whooping crane eggs for this project hatch in May at Patuxent Wildlife Research Center near Laurel, Maryland. An average whooping crane egg is 102 mm long (4 inches) and weighs 208 grams (7 ounces). Whooping crane eggs are incubated, on average, for 30 days.
