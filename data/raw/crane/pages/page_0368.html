Track Replacement and Realignment Project
Railroad Museum of Pennsylvania Restoration Yard

The yard is now open for visitors in designated areas.
As always, access is dependent on weather, staffing, and safety considerations.


New Tracks and switches, M1, Silverliner, Wreck Crane

Work is complete on the realignment and regrading of the tracks in the Restoration Yard. The track was originally laid in 1971, prior to construction of the Museum building. The normal deterioration that occurs, plus the need to modify some of the track alignments to allow for more efficient movement of locomotives, necessitated the current project, which was assisted by the Intermodal Surface Transportation Enhancement program.

Extensive work that was required included clearing the yard of rolling stock, realigning and regrading tracks, moving the restoration shed, constructing a new power shed, installing a new crossing and fence gate at Bishop Road, a new vehicular gate and service access roadway from Bishop Road, and regrading of the ground surface.

A new packed gravel walkway was installed.  Coincident with this project was the completion of the repainting and retracking of the Museum's working turntable in the center of the yard.

The entire project, which Restoration Manager Al Martin orchestrated like a military operation, is part of the Museum's long-range program of locomotive restoration and equipment display planning.
