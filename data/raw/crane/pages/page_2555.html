Nebraska's Wetland Birds

Besides the birds who live in Nebraska's wetlands year round or nest in the summer, many species of birds travel through the state along the Platte River each spring and fall. The area between Kearney and Grand Island provides an invaluable resting area for Sandhill Cranes, Whooping Cranes, Geese, Ducks, and other migratory birds. Click on any of the names in the following list to see images of some of Nebraska's wetland birds and learn more about them.

  • Bald Eagle (Haliaeetus leucocephalus)

  • Great Egret (Ardea albus)
  • Sandhill Crane (Grus canadensis)

  • Wilson's Phalarope (Phalaropus tricolor)
  • American Avocet (Recurvirostra americana)
  • Common Snipe (Gallinago gallinago)

    Aerialists

  • Least Tern (Sterna antillarum)
  • Ring-Billed Gull (Larus delawarensis)

    Swimmers

  • White Pelican (Pelicanus erythrorhynchus)
  • Double-Crested Cormorant (Phalacrocorax auritus)
  • Blue-Winged Teal (Anas discors)
  • Eared Grebe (Podiceps nigricollis)

  • Mallard (Anas platyrhynchus)
