Bridge

Clarkson Construction Company builds all types of bridges large and small. Bridge types include structural steel, precast concrete beam, and concrete slab.

Clarkson also performs bridge demolition, pile driving, steel erection, structural concrete, concrete retaining walls, modular block, and reinforced earth type retaining walls.

Clarkson's bridge fleet includes twenty crawler and mobile cranes with lifting capacities from 45 tons to 200 tons, deck finishing machines, pile drivers, and all other miscellaneous equipment required to maintain crews capable of constructing bridges on several different projects each construction season.

Clarkson's fleet of cranes allow them to complete large scale bridge projects on schedule

By performing bridge demolition at night Clarkson can have traffic flowing smoothly the next day

Concrete Paving

Clarkson Construction Company performs concrete paving of highways, streets, and large parking areas for commercial projects using state-of-the-art slipform equipment

Clarkson primarily operates CMI, Gomaco, and Guntert Zimmerman paving equipment, and Rex, Johnson, and Hetzel batch plants.

Clarkson Construction Company is capable of producing in excess of 18000 cubic yards of concrete per day. Clarkson currently maintains three paving crews, which provide great flexibility in completing projects well ahead of schedule.

Clarkson's Guntert Zimmerman paver allows them to decrease prep time thanks to its automated dowel bar inserter

Clarkson's mobile batch plant sites allow quick and easy, on-site transportation of over 5,000 cubic yards of concrete per day

Clarkson's three separate paving crews allow them to complete major projects well ahead of schedule while still creating some of the highest quality concrete pavement in the nation

Grading

Clarkson Construction Company began as a grading contractor and today continues to perform earth and rock excavation for highways, streets, site development, dams, and levees.

Clarkson maintains a large spread of equipment including several scraper fleets, a Holland belt loader with off highway bottom dumps, track and wheel loaders, 35 and 50 ton end dumps, articulated trucks, compactors, bulldozers, motor patrols, 12,000 gallon water wagons, excavators, and backhoes, all of which are used to complete the many projects that Clarkson undertakes.

Clarkson is capable of moving in excess of 50,000 cubic yards of earth, and 10,000 cubic yards of rock per day. Because Clarkson has such an extensive fleet of grading equipment, they are able to work on numerous projects simultaneously, and to complete them on-time, or in many cases, well ahead of schedule.

Clarkson's fleet of loaders and large capacity end dumps allow them to move over 10,000 cubic yards of rock each day

Large projects such as the Hillsdale Dam can be completed quickly and efficiently with Clarkson's mass amounts of grading equipment

Clarkson's Holland belt loader, and scraper fleet allow for large quantities of earth to be quickly removed from a site

By blasting, Clarkson can make quick work of a rock grading job

 

Soil Stabilization and Aggregate

Clarkson Construction Company performs lime, fly ash, and cement stabilization of subgrades for highways, streets, and site development. Clarkson makes use of the most up-to-date equipment including Caterpillar, CMI, and Bomag tillers.

Clarkson Construction Company also installs all types of bases beneath pavements including aggregate base, cement treated base, open-graded drainable base both treated and untreated. Clarkson utilizes a large fleet of base laying equipment and pugmills to provide up to 8000 tons of base per day on a given project.
