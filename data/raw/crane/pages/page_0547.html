FWF's 2002 Public Servant of the Year

No one has been a better friend to Florida's natural resources than Diana Sawaya-Crane. As Cabinet Aide and legislative lobbyist for Attorney General Bob Butterworth, Diana has provided invaluable assistance to the Florida Wildlife Federation and others to protect Florida's natural resources involved in sovereign land lease applications, support for key conservation and recreation lands purchases, fighting off proposed legislative changes to the definition of sovereign lands, including the Fisheating Creek sovereign lands controversy, strengthening manatee protection, strengthening local government comprehensive land use plans, reviewing applications for Developments of Regional Impact, enforcing electrical power plant siting laws, strengthening DER's underground storage tank rules, preparing a report on SLAPPS (Strategic Litigation Against Public Participation) and seeking legislative protection of citizens from SLAPPS, and fighting legislative changes to citizen standing.

Diana started her work as a biologist with the Hillsborough County Environmental Protection Commission in 1979. In 1983 she transferred to the Department of Environmental Regulation. She moved to the Department of Community Affairs where she reviewed Developments of Regional Impact. In 1986 she became a Cabinet Aide and Legislative lobbyist for the Attorney General's Office.

Anyone who has attended the bi-weekly Cabinet Aides meetings over the past fifteen years knows that Diana is a tenacious protector and defender of Florida's environment. Her questioning of persons appearing before the Cabinet Aides has made it impossible for unscrupulous advocates to deceive the Cabinet with incorrect facts and erroneous interpretations of statutes and rules.

For her friendship to the Florida Wildlife Federation, and dedication to the natural resources of Florida, the Federation is proud to name Diana Sawaya-Crane as the Public Servant of the Year for 2002.
