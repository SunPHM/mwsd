The Uncas A. Whitaker Hall for Biomedical Engineering
       

An artist's rendering of The Uncas A. Whitaker Hall for Biomedical Engineering

The Uncas A. Whitaker Hall for Biomedical Engineering is named afer the founder of The Whitaker Foundation, Uncas A. Whitaker. The grants from The Whitaker Foundation to Washington University in St. Louis total $13 million. The preliminary cost of the roughly 96,000-square-foot building is estimated at $33 million. The groundbreaking ceremony was held October 2, 2000. The building is located in the northeast part of the Hilltop Campus, just east of the intersection of Forest Park Parkway and Hoyt Drive. Some features of the building include:

  • 12,580 square feet for core laboratory facilities
  • 22,000 square feet of wet and dry laboratory space
  • A 250-seat auditorium
  • Library, student, and faculty lounges
  • Faculty offices
  • A landscaped courtyard
Occupancy of Whitaker Hall by the Biomedical Engineering Department is set for fall 2002.
