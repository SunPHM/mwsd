   July 4, 2003


"Brian Wildsmith and His World of Illustrations and Picture Books" Continues its Japan Tour
Following a show at the Tokyo Fuji Art Museum in Hachioji, Tokyo, "Brian Wildsmith and His World of Illustrations and Picture Books--Fantasia from a Fairyland," commemorating the 40th anniversary of the artist's career as a painter, continues its tour of Japan. On July 5, the show opens in Gifu Prefecture, and in Chiba Prefecture on July 12. Scheduled to show at 19 venues throughout Japan, the Wildsmith exhibition presents some 150 representative original works from books Mr. Wildsmith has illustrated, as well as posters and other art. Renowned for his brilliant, effulgent illustrations that border on fine art, British author-illustrator Brian Wildsmith is also known as the "magician of colors."


"Crane," being shown for the first time at the Chiba exhibition, under the auspices of Wildsmith Story Book Museum President Michiko Nomura.

 

At the exhibition in Gifu, paintings not included in the Hachioji and Saitama shows, will make their debut. In Chiba, "Crane," a painting portraying Mr. Wildsmith's desire for an end to all wars and the establishment of world peace, will be shown for the first time. "Crane" was inspired by a poem about soldiers who died in battle and return as cranes.

[Gifu: Gifu Museum, from July 5 through 27. Closed 7 and 14. On July 18 only, the exhibition will be open until 9:00 p.m. Chiba: Chiba Portside Tower-8F, from July 12 through 27. Open everyday. Hours at both venues are from 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Admission fees: adults and college students--600 yen; high school junior high and elementary school students--200 yen.]
