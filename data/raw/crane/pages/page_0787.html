Reconstruction of Pak Sha Wan Public Pier in Sai Kung by Wai Kee (Zens) Construction & Transportation Co. Ltd. (a wholly owned subsidiary of Leader Construction Co. Ltd.)
The Project

This project is to construct a new public pier in Pak Sha Wan of Sai Kung and to demolish the existing public pier.

 

The works to be executed under this project include:

  • Construction of a reinforced concrete pier deck structure with five sets of landing steps as well as its' pre-bored socketted H-pile foundation and precast concrete pedestal foundation
  • Construction of a vertical blockwork seawall and reclamation to form a sitting-out area adjacent to the pier
  • Design and construction of a glazed skylight roof system on the pier deck
  • Design and Construction of six glazed skylight steel shelters at the sitting out area.
  • Dredging of berthing areas and disposal of dredged materials at designated dumping ground
  • Demolition of the existing public pier after commissioning of the new public pier

Environmental Impact

Water quality - Marine dredging works would disturb the sediment in the seabed, resulting adverse impact on water quality. In addition, any increase in marine traffic at site area would not only disturb the quiet environment at the vicinity but might also cause oil pollution due to the potential engine leakage.

 

Noise emission - The vicinity was quiet and conventional driven pile system would create noise nuisance to the local residents. 

 

Dust emission - Dust impact was one of the main environmental concerns during the demolition of old pier. 

 

C&D Waste - Demolition of the existing pier generate a significant amount of C&D wastes and it was worth to look for some innovative idea to minimize the impact.

 

Nuisance to Ecology - Heavy vibration would be generated from conventional driven pile construction and cause disturbance to the ecology. 


Measures Adopted

The construction site was located at an environmental sensitive area, therefore, construction method and sequence had been carefully planned so as to minimize the environmental impact and nuisance to both the public and ecology.

1.        To replace the conventional driven pile system by pre-bored socketted H-pile

  • Noise emission during construction period was minimized
  • Nuisance to ecology cause by vibration was prevented

2.        Use of Crane Tower

  • Use of crane tower during construction period had minimized the need of using barges, as a result, the possibility of oil leakage from engine was minimized.

In addition, emission of noise due to barges were minimized.

     

3.        Silt Curtain

  • Silt curtains were used to retain solid dispersion during dredging, so that the water quality outside the site boundary would not affected.

     

4.        Use of Precast Construction Units

  • Using precast construction units had minimized the amount of construction waste.
  • The potential pollution impact to marine water during falsework construction and concreting had been eliminated.

            

5.        Demolition of existing pier by means of saw cutting method

  •  Noise and dust generated during the demolition process minimized, as a result, nuisance to public was minimized.
  • The cut pieces were dumped off Sai Kung to form artificial reef for promoting fishery growth in the vicinity.

         

 

Observations and Achievements

The Site was awarded a "Considerate Contractor Site Award" by the Works Bureau in consideration of good site management, being considerate to neighbors and passersby, environmental consciousness, safety consciousness and law abiding.

The nearby residents told the contractor that they had unnoticeably completed the work. This indicated that the contractor had not damaged the environment or created noise nuisance during the entire construction period.
