City of Wuhan

The city of Wuhan has a population of over 7 million, which makes it the fifth largest city in China and one of the top 35 cities in the world. It is ideally located for study of China’s environmental conditions. Wuhan is located on the Yangtze River, at a major confluence with the Han River. The Yangtze River is the largest river in China. It is the fourth longest river in the world, the fifth largest in total discharge, and the twelfth largest in drainage area. Its discharge is twice the size of the Mississippi River. Wuhan is known as the City on Rivers, with approximately 25 percent of the area of the city consisting of either rivers or lakes. Wuhan is rich in culture and history. Wuhan's civilization began about 3,500 years ago and is of great importance in Chinese cultural, military, economic and political history. Today, Wuhan ranks third in China in activity related to science and technology. Wuhan is the capital city of Hubei Province which has a population of 57 million, almost twice the population of California in half of the land area of California. The latitude of Wuhan is the same as that of Jacksonville, FL and Austin, TX. Average daily low and high temperatures in July are 76 and 90 degrees F and in December are 34 and 49 degrees F.

Approximately 175 miles west of Wuhan on the Yangtze River is the site of the famous Three Gorges Dam, one of the largest civil engineering construction projects in the world. This 15-year-long construction project is scheduled for full operation in 2009. It is causing major environmental and ecological changes which will be monitored and studied for years to come. We will visit the Three Gorges Dam and take an extended tour of the river as part of the 2005 program.

Wuhan University

The local hosts for our program at Wuhan University are the School of Resource and Environmental Science and the College of Foreign Student Education. Wuhan University (www.whu.edu.cn/en) is one of the top ten universities in China. It is a comprehensive university with all major fields and professional schools represented. The student enrollment is 45,000. It celebrated its 110th anniversary last year and has a rich history. The campus has one of the most beautiful locations in the city. It is located on the picturesque Luojia Hill overlooking the famous East Lake.

Wuhan University has organized its environmental-related education and research activities into an Environmental Disciplinary Group consisting of departments from the School of Resource and Environmental Science, College of Civil and Architectural Engineering, School of Water Resource and Hydropower, College of Chemistry and Molecular Sciences, College of Life Sciences, School of Information Engineering and Remote Sensing, Law School and Business School. Disciplines include environmental engineering, environmental chemistry, environmental biology and ecology, environmental geosciences, environmental planning and management, resource and environmental law, environmental economy and environmental information. Wuhan University offers both M.S. and Ph.D. degrees in these areas.
