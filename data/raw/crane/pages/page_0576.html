Hear them ring

 



Photo by Hal Emitt Sr.

The half-ton original bell at Holy Ghost Church in Knoxville now has plenty of company in the tower. As part of a parish anniversary project begun early last year, members helped contribute toward the purchase of five new cast bronze bells. A truck carrying the bells, steel support beams, clappers, and electrical equipment arrived at the church early on a recent morning, and over three days several workers completed the installation and activated the bells. At top, Holy Ghost pastor Father Xavier Mankel blesses the bells as they rest on pallets. The crane at lower left lifted each bell and other accessories through a window in the church’s five-story tower. At bottom right is an electrically actuated clapper that strikes in a spot that makes the sound seem to come from swinging bells, a method that reduces vibration to the tower and church building. The bells sound the Westminster melody at each quarter hour and strike the hour from 6 a.m. until 9 p.m. The Angelus peals at 6 a.m., noon, and 6 p.m. Contributions to the bell fund in one year totaled $58,000 toward the $65,000 cost of the project. The new bells weigh 693, 457, 273, 220, and 172 pounds and were cast by Petit & Fritsen bell foundry of Aarle-Rixtel, Holland. Holy Ghost parishioner Hal Emitt Sr. designed the bell system and served as the on-site supervisor of the installation.
