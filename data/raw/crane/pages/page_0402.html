Podiatric Alumna First to Reach Barry Order of the Flame

If you run against Dr. Marybeth Crane (DPM ’94) in a marathon race you know how competitive she can be. She relentlessly trains despite a demanding clinic practice and always finishes the race with her eye to the next one.

Crane just completed a new race of sorts, she became the first alum from the Barry Podiatric Medicine and Surgery Program to reach the honorable Barry University Order of the Flame level by making donations of at least $10,000 to the Dr. Marvin and Ruth Steinberg Podiatric Alumni Student Scholarship Fund. She is challenging her podiatric classmates to enter the race.

“As a personal friend of John Steinberg (DPM ’95), grandson of Dr. Martin Steinberg, I felt that supporting the Steinberg Fund would represent what is right with our profession,” she said. “I am hoping my second $5,000 donation will encourage my classmates to contribute to the fund as well,” she added.

Crane's podiatric practice, Foot and Ankle Associates of North Texas grew from three patients (all her relatives) to 1,500 new patients in 18 months. After 5 years, Crane has hired another Barry grad and now has four doctors, two offices (with a third opening in 2004), a dozen employees, and more patients than her office can see.

The recipient of a merit scholarship during her time at Barry, Crane indicates that, “now is the time to focus my giving to help other Barry students like myself.”

According to Carol Evanco, Assistant Vice President for Alumni Relations at Barry University, “We sincerely appreciate Dr. Crane’s most generous donation. Needless to say, we will now be able to provide more students with financial assistance,” she said.

The fund, named in honor of Dr. Marvin Steinberg, pioneer podiatric physician and his wife, is dedicated to providing tuition support for qualified podiatric medical students at the Barry University School of Graduate Medical Sciences. For more information or to make a contribution, contact: Bob McKinlay, 11300 NE 2nd Avenue, Miami Shores, Florida, 33161 or call 305-899-3283 or 1-800-756-6000 or email at rmckinlay@mail.barry.edu.
