Folding dull news items into beautiful origami cranes of wisdom.


BY WW EDITORIAL STAFF | newsdesk at wweek.com

Oregon Dems - WINNERS
WINNERS

Nearly a third of the 37 Clark County women who tested positive for gonorrhea in the first quarter of 2004 got some welcome news last week: They didn't actually have the clap. The women, many of them monogamous and much older than the typical STD recipient, got a call from an alert county health official who noticed that a defective testing technique was causing false positives.

Thrilled Oregon Dems were ready to paint the state blue after a new poll by the nonpartisan American Research Group showed John Kerry holding an eight-point edge over the prez. Kerry's current lead is his biggest ever in the battleground Beaver State.

Good news for sickly Northeast Portlanders: A group of doctors has taken over the lease of the recently bankrupted and abandoned Woodland Park Hospital and plans to reopen it by year's end. The 202-bed facility went financially kaput in January.

LOSERS

Until recently, tax dodgers skipped paying the city's business income tax with impunity because nobody at City Hall bothered to go after them. But under Commissioner Randy Leonard, the License Bureau--which collects the tax--has suddenly shown its fangs by filing suit against two lawyers who allegedly haven't made payments for several years.

The city's Bureau of Environmental Services lost in a big and smelly way when 1,200 gallons of untreated sewage poured into the Willamette River just downstream from the Fremont Bridge. Since there were no rains to trigger such a surge, BES was left scratching its head about the source of the foul flow.

Local ad agency Wieden & Kennedy grimaced at the new gaping hole in its pocketbook after parting ways with big-money, big-name client America Online over creative differences. The partnership, which was worth an estimated $50 million annually, fizzled after W&K's $6 million Super Bowl ads for the company received tepid reviews.
