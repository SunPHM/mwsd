Crane Hunts

The sandhill crane hunting in the Texas panhandle can be some of the best around. Our crane hunting depends on our water situation. If we have water the crane will be here. If we do not have water the crane will be harder to find.

During good years we normally come home with full limits and plenty of empty shells.

We usually hunt about thirty minutes south of Lubbock. Because of the distance we have to drive to hunt these birds we will have to set up the decoys when we get there. We use between thirty and fifty homemade, hunt proven, decoys. We use ghillie blankets or some kind of natural cover as camouflage. You are allowed to use lead shot during these hunts. However if you bring lead shot you can not shoot any bonus geese that may fly by.

Due to the water situation of the Texas Panhandle, we ask that you give us a call at least a week or two before your hunt to make sure the crane are here. If we do not have huntable numbers of crane or if they are just not here yet we will either refund your deposit or reschedule you for a later date.

These big birds are hard to bring down but it can be done. When you have these huge birds coming in, this type of hunt is probably one of the most exciting that we have to offer.

Hunting Tip

The Camera:

Record moments--the gathering at the vehicles to disperse and tag the birds.  Slow times in the field; the dog coming in with a duck or goose; a fellow hunter picking up a duck or goose; shooting.  Put an inexpensive 35-mm camera in a zip-loc plastic bag with extra rolls of film and extra battery.  Place it in your field bag or shell bag.  Hunting memories sure are nice years later.
