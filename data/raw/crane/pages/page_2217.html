The Whoopers Are Back!

The five whoopers that survived the winter in Florida left the Chassahowitzka National Wildlife Refuge in Florida on April 9. One of the birds left the group and headed north on its own. The other four flew about 220 miles into Georgia. After Georgia, they traveled to Tennessee and then on to Indiana. On April 16 they flew into Illinois. And, now they're back in Wisconsin! Four of the birds returned to the Necedah Wildlife Refuge. The fifth loner bird is back in Wisconsin but hasn't joined the other whoopers.

This spring they traveled north on their own, without an ultralight aircraft leading the way. They remembered the way from last fall's migration south.
