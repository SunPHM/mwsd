Lake health can have many impacts on the biodiversity of a lake. Some factors affecting lake health include sedimentation, shoreline processes, invasive species, and the amount of nutrients, especially phosphorus and nitrogen, entering the watershed.

Currently, the deepest point in Stony Lake is 45 feet deep. Mabel Huey, a resident of Stony Lake for many years, noted that in the early 1900's the deepest point in Stony Lake was 90 feet deep (Oceana Herald, July 1980. Stony Lake resident looks at past 71 years by Laura Porter). Sedimentation, a common occurrence in lakes, can affect lake health because the sediment deposited in the lake contains nutrients that can lead to eutrophication. In addition, sediment can fill in a naturally heterogeneous lake bottom degrading the natural habitat of some species. Click for more information of the Effects of Sedimentation.
