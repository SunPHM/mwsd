The "Rising Sun" Auction

The Rising Sun Auction will be held on November 12, 2003. An unusual gift maybe, a little bit out of left field, but desirable, collectable, and potentially an investment for the future as well?

How about considering a Japanese woodblock print (ukiyo-e) in triptych format (above) depicting the official proclamation of the first Japanese Constitution in 1889 by Adachi Ginko. This Meiji period print is in fine condition and captures an amazing period of change in Japan. Utilizing an old printing method that had been used to portray images of actors, beautiful women and landscapes, the artist has captured Japan's leap into the nineteenth century and it's grasping of Western ideals in a depiction of an unprecedented ceremony.

Alternatively one may chose something a little more traditional - albeit in the same format - a triptych ukiyo-e of kabuki actors. Highly decorative, with an almost pop art cartoon-like presentation, in bold colours the actors take stances that reflect their highly choreographed roles. This print is enhanced by the banner like inscriptions of Japanese characters behind the actors who wear make-up and costume indicating their parts.
