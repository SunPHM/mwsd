Sandhill Crane (Grus canadensis)

This species is BLUE-listed due to a lack of data on breeding populations & habitat loss & degradation.





Distinguishing Characteristics:
  1. RED crown extending from beak to top of head
  2. Adult mostly GREY, with BLACK primary feathers seen during flight
  3. Large long legged, long necked wading bird with 6-7 ft. wing span
  4. Immature birds are brown

Species Ecology:

  • Habitat:  Roosts & feeds in open wetlands such as bogs, swamps, marshes, fens & upland lake areas such as grasslands & agricultural fields.  Nests in secluded freshwater wetlands surrounded by forest
  • Nest are heaps of vegetation in water, surrounded by emergent vegetation
  • When threatened, individuals will run into forest cover

Distribution:


Sandhill Crane Management:

Critical Habitat:
  • Shallow freshwater wetlands with emergent vegetation (sedge, cattails. bulrush, hardhack, willows, Labrador tea) for nesting & brood rearing
  • Nesting wetlands are usually secluded, free from disturbance & surrounded by forest
  • Breeding season: April thru August
Wildlife Habitat Area:
  • Breeding wetlands of less than 5 ha & traditional foraging areas
  • WHA should include entire stand of emergent vegetation around wetland & foraging area, and a 50 m buffer

 

Road Construction:
  • Do not develop any permanent access structures as defined in the "Operational Planning Regulations"
  • Avoid road construction & use during breeding season 
Silviculture:
  • Retain at least 40% dominant/co-dominant trees within 50 m of wetland/lake edge>
  • Retain as much of the understorey trees, shrubs & herbaceous plants as practicable
  • No harvest or salvage during breeding season
