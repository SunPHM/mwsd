International Crane Foundation


Please Take A Moment To Visit Our Sponsors!
 
The International Crane Foundation (a private, non-profit organization) is located just north of Baraboo and works worldwide to conserve cranes and environments on which they depend. ICF has gained international acclaim as one of the world's leaders in the conservation of endangered species.

ICF is open daily (9 am to 5 pm) from April 15 to October 31. Reservations are not required for individuals and family groups. Guided tours begin at 10:00 a.m., 1:00 and 3:00 p.m. daily between Memorial Day and Labor Day and on weekend days in May, September, and October. A free tape-recorded tour is also available at the Gift Shop for your use.

ICF offers the world's most complete collection of cranes at the Johnson Exhibit Pod, the AMOCO Whooping Crane Exhibit, and the nearby Wattled Crane Exhibit. At the AMOCO Whooping Crane Exhibit, visitors see these rarest of cranes from a frog's-eye view in the beautiful setting of a Wisconsin wetland. Interactive exhibits and a short film tell the story of the whooping crane and the people who wouldn't let them slip into extinction. Also enjoy the restoration overlook displaying restored tall grass prairie and Oak Savanna much as it would have looked over 200 years ago. You can also enjoy: Nature Trails, Art Exhibits, Flight Demonstrations, Chick exercise yard, special events and more.

Directions

From Baraboo, drive five miles north on West Highway 12, then turn right on Shady Lane Road. From Interstate 90 and 94, turn east on Highway 12 at Exit 92. Drive south 1.5 miles and turn left on Shady Lane Road. ICF is located about a mile from the Shady Lane/Highway 12 intersection. ICF is just 3 hours from Chicago, 2 hours from Milwaukee, 1 hour from Madison, and about 4 hours from Minneapolis/St. Paul.
