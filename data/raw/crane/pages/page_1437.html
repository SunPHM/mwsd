Construction of the HSBC Building
[ Construction Pages: timeline   1   2   3   4   5   6   7   8 ]

February, 1985

Deep within the 75 foot deep excavation, forms have been set to pour the concrete foundation for the elevator core of the tower. Note the vertical walls of the excavation reinforced with shot-crete and horizontally drilled anchors. Two cement trucks deliver concrete to the floor of the site, while another begins the descent down the ramp. Soil conditions are excellent for high-rise construction: clay silt subsoil on a bedrock foundation.

March, 1985

A second construction crane has been erected next to the elevator core. These cranes are situated in the exact locations of vertical duct shafts so that the building can go up around them. At this stage the footings for all 20 major columns that will support the perimeter of the tower have been set. In the foreground can be seen the wood forms upon which the concrete floor of the underground parkade will be poured. Once the concrete has set in the full-tension slabs, the scaffolding and forms are removed from beneath the floors.
