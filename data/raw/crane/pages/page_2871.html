Sandhill Cranes and Bird Watchers Return to Lodi
The fifth annual Sandhill Crane Festival got underway in Lodi Friday afternoon to celebrate the return of the magnificent birds.

The Central Valley is one of the few areas in the country in which Sandhill cranes can be seen. The Lodi and Woodbridge areas are favorite layovers for migrating cranes. The five foot gray birds with the bright red heads make for an unforgettable wildlife viewing experience.

Wildlife photographer David Rosen has been watching birds since he was 12 years old, all because of Sara Lee Coffee Cake. "My folks made a little bird feeder out of a coffee cake tin, and they hung it up, and I just became fascinated with the birds that were coming close visiting our backyard and pursued it as a passion and a career," said Rosen.

In addition to the cranes, Rosen and the other birders are looking for something rarely seen in California. A Demoiselle crane, which is native to Pakistan and Afghanistan, has been spotted among the Sandhills. "For it to appear here at this time is rather unique," said Rosen. "It's like an avian ambassador." In Asia, cranes are a symbol of peace.

The Sandhill Crane Festival runs through Sunday at Hutchins Street Square in Lodi.
