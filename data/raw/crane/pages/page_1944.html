Hiwassee Wildlife Refuge

The Hiwassee Wildlife Refuge hosts one of the largest populations of Sandhill Cranes in the world.  The cranes in this population nest primarily in the Great Lakes states of Wisconsin, Minnesota, Michigan and nearby Ontario in Canada.  In autumn, they congregate in flocks and migrate along a path that takes them through Tennessee to their primary wintering grounds in south Georgia and Florida.  In the last few years, favorable conditions have resulted in over 10,000 cranes wintering in the Hiwassee Wildlife Refuge and the surrounding area.  Thousands of cranes can be seen at once from the new facilities on the refuge grounds.

The recent introduction of the Sandhill's taller, whiter cousin, the Whooping Crane, makes seeing them a strong possibility as well.
