How Whooper, The Whooping Crane
Got Blue Eyes
Translate page

 

   In the far Northern lands, during the Summer days, when even the nights, are still day, arrives a bird named, Whooper, the Whooping Crane.  This flying creature of the earth, is a tall, skinny legged bird, with a long bill and blue eyes. This is the story, about how Whooper, and all the other Whooping Cranes got blue eyes.
  Now, as told to me, by a man of the Native Clan, whom I'd just met, by him, asking me, the most unusual question I'd ever been asked.  It appears, this was his way of starting a new friendship, by using this question as a conversation opener.
   He asked me this query, as if, I might know the answer, "Do you know how Whooper, the Whooping Crane got blue eyes?"
   My mind, unprepared for a question such as this, thought silently, "What did he ask?"
   Being unsure of what this man had asked and being a person, whom I have never spoke with before, I answered his question, with questioning words, "Excuse me?"
   He repeated his question to me again, "Do you know how Whooper, the Whooping Crane got blue eyes?"
   This time, wanting to return friendly words, as his voice was a friendly voice, yet, my mind was wondering if maybe this man might be touched in the head or something, I answered, "No, I do not know how Whooper, the Whooping Crane got blue eyes."
   I, then reversed the question, by asking him, "Why? Do you know how Whooper, the Whooping Crane got blue eyes?"
   "Of course I do!" Answered the man who'd first asked me this silly question.
   It was at this time, I asked him, "Then, why did you ask me?"
   He said, "I just wanted to know if you knew the story about how Whooper, the Whooping Crane got blue eyes!"
   This Storyteller, of the Native Clan who was talking to me, proceeded with this story, and this is the story I was told, as I remembered his words:
   A long time ago, there was a Whooping Crane, by the name of Whooper, who flew about, gliding in the crisp spring air. His wings were spread out in majestic array, as he looked the land over, by flying up high in the sky. The bird looked far and wide, to be sure there were no foxes around, or other creatures who might bother him when he lands on the ground to finally take a bath and build a nest. He had to find a place before the female
Whooping Cranes arrived, so one of them would choose him as a mate.
   As Whooper, the Whooping Crane viewed the land, with his huge huge eyes, he spotted a pond to wash himself, after this long journey from the South.
   This large bird, angled his wings, for the descent from the sky and came slowly down to the ground, landing in the water of the pond. When Whooper,  the Whooping Crane had landed, as a protective measure, and, to be sure there were no foxes around, he looked far and wide again. It was the only way to be sure, he was safe before he plunked his head under the water, filling his bill, to spray water on himself and clean his feathers and body.
So, Whooper looked around and proceeded to wash.
   Into the pond water went his head. Then, his head popped back up and out of the water to look all about again. After looking all about,  Whooper, the Whooping Crane cleaned, what needed to be cleaned!
   Meanwhile, his Eyes became tired of opening and shutting, not to mention, bobbing in and bobbing out of the water, time and time again.
   Finally, Whooper's Eyes spoke up. "Whooper, why don't you take each of us Eyes out of your head, while you wash and set each of us on a separate rock? We can keep watch for foxes and other creatures of alarm, while you wash. Then, we won't get tired from bobbing in and out of the water all the time."
   Whooper, the Whooping Crane thought about this and decided, that indeed, this was a very good idea. So, he took out his Eyes and placed each of them on a separate rock, to keep watch for foxes and any other creature, whom may want to disturb or cause him
alarm, while he took a bath.
   By Whooper's, placing his Eyes on two separate rocks, his Eyes, were able to look in two different directions, at the same time, while Whooper, the Whooping Crane continued to wash.
   Now, a Fox who just happened to be wandering, not far from this pond, and who like all the other foxes, had a very keen sense of sight and hearing, overheard this conversation between Whooper and his Eyes. The Fox immediately took cover and hid behind a Blueberry Bush.
   Because of the Fox's ear's keen hearing, the Fox listened to what the conversation was about.  After the Fox heard this conversation, the Fox kept himself hidden behind the Blueberry Bush, as he watched this large bird take out each of his Eyes and
place each of the two Eyes, on a rock. It happened, just as it was spoken and agreed to, between the bird and his eyes. Each one of the Eyes were now, looking in a different directions, as guards for Whooper, the Whooping Crane.
   As the Human saying goes, "As sharp as a Fox!" So, it was in this situation, too! And, what the humans say, surely has merit, for the Fox using his wit from the very beginning of this encounter had hidden behind a Blueberry Bush.
   After watching these happenings, the Fox reasoned that  Whooping Crane's Eyes could only look in two directions, and by his wit, recognized that there are four directions that must be observed for safety's sake. The Fox thought about the matter and decided what to do.  So the Fox, still under the cover of the Blueberry Bush picked two Blueberries from the Blueberry Bush and positioned himself to approach the Whooping Crane and his Eyes, from one of the directions that the two Eyes could not properly observe.
   During this time, that the Fox was creeping closer, the Whooping Crane's two Eyes were continually keeping watch for a fox or other creatures, in directions, where neither Eye could see the Fox or the Fox's approach.
   The Fox snuck closer and closer without being seen.  He was so close, that finally, the Fox came up from behind one of the rocks, that held the First Eye. The Fox reached from behind the rock and grabbed the First Eye. He then replaced the First Eye with one of the Blueberries he'd picked from the Blueberry Bush.
   The Fox then, snuck over  to the other rock and replaced the Second Eye, with the Second Blueberry, he'd picked. After replacing each Eye of the Whooping Crane's with a Blueberry, the Fox then quietly snuck away with both of  Whooper's Eyes.
   Whooper, the Whooping Crane was unaware of what has happening.  After Whooper had dunked his head under the water for the last time of his bath, he then went to each of the rocks that held each of his Eyes. Unknown to him, his Eyes were not there!
   Whooper, the Whooping Crane grasped the Blueberries, from each of the rocks that held his Eyes, and put them back in his head.
What Whooper thought were his Eyes were really Blueberries!
Whooper could never figure out why his Eyes never spoke to him again.
   And, because Whooper was the first Whooping Crane in the Northern land, all the female Whooping Cranes, came to him first.  And, from that time on,  all of the baby Whooping Cranes had blue eyes, just like Whooper's. And, this is how Whooper, the Whooping Crane and all the other Whooping Cranes got Blue Eyes, except for every once in a while, there would be a brown eyed Whooping Crane name, Whoopee, the Whooping Crane!
   It was at this point in the story, that the Storyteller spoke no more words to me.
   Now, me being the man, who was listening to this story, I asked the Storyteller. "But, what happened to the Fox and Whooper, the Whooping Crane's huge huge eyes, that the Fox had stolen? And, who is this Whoopee, the Whooping Crane that has brown eyes? Where did this Whooping Crane come from?"
   The Storyteller, who had just finished telling me this Story started to giggle and answered my question by saying, "I do not know!"
   I confusingly responded, "What do you mean, you don't know?"
   The Storyteller repeated himself, adding, "I said, I do not know! The story was told to me when I was a child, by my Grandfather. I fell asleep before Grandpa finished the Story. It
was his misfortune to pass away, that very night. In fact, I have always wondered what happened to the Fox and Whooper the Whooping Crane's Huge Huge Eyes myself, not to mention I have always wondered, who this Whoopee the brown-eyed Whooping Crane is!"
   This response left my mind a blank and my only reaction was to say, "Oh!"
   Again, the Storyteller began to chuckle, after, observing my dumbfounded expression, to his answer of my question.
   So, as the Writer of these words, and as the new Storyteller, this is what I know about how Whooper, and all the other Whooping Cranes got blue eyes! Except one, who, as a Whooping Crane named Whoopee, every once in a while, some how, is born with brown
eyes.

THE END
