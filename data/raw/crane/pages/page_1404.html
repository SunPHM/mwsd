The Gonda Building

The construction of a new hospital facility in Rochester, MN required tower cranes that needed to be supported against wind loads by the partially constructed building. Wenzel Engineering was charged with getting the lateral loads out of the tower crane and engaging enough of the building’s current shear walls and moment frames to provide stability for the crane. The tower crane on the right was designed to sit completely on the 12th floor. As far as we know this has only been done two other times in the US.  Wenzel Engineering also supported the multi level skyway during erection, at the left of the picture. This involved coordinating the erection sequence to maintain balanced loading to the supports.   The process was further complicated because the shoring towers landed on top of an existing pedestrian tunnel.
