In 1927, Murtaugh Hughes - the grandfather of present owners William and Paul Hughes - began operating a small sand and gravel firm named after himself. When he died, his sons, Edward J. and Carlton, took over the business and called it "Hughes Bros.". Carlton sold his share of the business to Edward J. Hughes II, who managed the company until 1972, at which time he sold the company to William, Paul and Richard Hughes. Richard left the firm in 1974, and sold his share to William and Paul in 1981.

In 1941 the company started its concrete business. The scope of the company's operations have expanded from the riverfront property in East Hampden to include a large gravel pit in Winterport and other pits in Glenburn, Newburgh and West Hampden. Hughes Bros. annually produces 30,000 yards of concrete and over 200,000 yards of aggregate. In recent years, utility and site preperation contracts have been added to its list of services. The company has a variety of construction equipment that can handle any project. The orange and white striped concrete mixers and dump trucks of Hughes Bros. have become familiar sights along the lower reaches of the Penobscot River. From the beginning to the present day, Hughes Bros. has demonstrated a commitment to quality and service unsurpassed.
