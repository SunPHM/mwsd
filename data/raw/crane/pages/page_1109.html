Inside the Gravity Bar at the top of the Guinness Storehouse.


Spreading to the left of and around the Phoenix Park Monument is Phoenix Park. The park is 1,760 acres, making it the largest city park in Europe. Inside the mostly grassland park is the residence of the Irish President and Dublin Zoo. The obelisk, also known as Wellington Monument, commemorates the victories of the Duke of Wellington in 1861.


The Four Courts is on the left with The Spire rising behind it.


Micha with a yummy Guinness.


When counting construction cranes around the city from the Gravity Bar, I lost count somewhere over 40. I think it demonstrates how rich Ireland's economy is right now. A chimney of Poolbeg Power Station rises left of the centre.


Just another view of the city that has been shown before.


The lady in blue stole some sips out of Micha's Guinness (as told in the journal of Dublin).


Dublin Mountains again.


The green dome at the centre is Rathmines Church Dome. Constructed between 1856-81.


St. Patrick's Tower here. It is the tallest Smock Windmill in the British Isles. Built around 1805 on the site of the former distillery, it gets its name from the figure of St. Patrick on the top.
