Here are some of the projects we have just completed, or are currently working on.
Nature Guides for Iwokrama Forest, Guyana, South America
We have created a digital publishing system for the nature reserve which produces online nature guides and pocket-sized printed guides from the same database of text and images. This project was funded by the International Development Research Centre of Canada.

Saving the Whooping Crane
This online educational exercise allows biology students to simulate population growth of the crane population under different management strategies. The module is designed to teach population ecology and conservation biology principles.
Visit the site
Shorebird Migration Monitoring Web site
Researchers and estuary managers along the West Atlantic Flyway are tracking shorebirds during migration. This Web site helps them share information about flock characteristics, and also allows naturalists to submit observations online. Features include migration maps which are dynamically created from the database and show up-to-date flock positions. This project is funded by the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration and the United States Fish and Wildlife Service. Visit the site
