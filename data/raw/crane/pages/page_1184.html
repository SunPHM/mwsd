A VIDEO ON JOB SITE LIFTING WITH MOBILE CONSTRUCTION EQUIPMENT

BURLINGTON, WI (April 1, 1997) Deere & Company, the Crosby Group, National Utility Contractors Association and VISTA have come together to create an instructional video on correct rigging and lifting techniques when using mobile construction equipment, other than cranes (Rigging and Lifting With Mobile Construction Equipment).

Engineers and safety experts at the John Deere Industrial Division realize that equipment owners will use their backhoe, excavator, or wheel loader to sling load materials on job sites. This is a practice as old as the invention of mechanical lifting equipment. While only cranes and forklifts are specifically designed as material handlers, owners of mobile construction equipment will load and unload all types of materials with any equipment at hand. The problem is - how to do it safely.

Deere approached VISTA and while VISTA was doing research for the project, the Crosby Group, the nation's premier manufacturer of rigging hardware, became involved as a sponsor and technical advisor. NUCA, the National Utility Contractors Association also volunteered to participate by providing the services of George Kennedy, CSP, the NUCA on-staff Certified Safety Professional in an advisory capacity.

Ray Peterson, President of VISTA, said, "The level of support we received from these high-quality organizations brings together a pool of qualified talent not ordinarily available on a project of this nature. The wealth of practical, real-world advice VISTA received in creating this video is simply not for hire. We are extremely fortunate in being involved in the creation of this video that provides such a wealth of down-to-earth safety information about rigging and lifting of loads on working job sites."

Video taping was done in a variety of locations lifting everything from trench boxes to buried liquid storage tanks. Loads, such as odd shaped, unbalanced pre-cast concrete structures, require special considerations. Knowing how to select the right sling and rigging hardware, the type of hitch to use, how to adjust the hitch for balance and equal stress on each leg are all covered in this twenty-five minute video.

How and why to use special attachment points on excavators, backhoes or wheel loaders becomes very clear when you see the possible results of doing it incorrectly. The reason to discard damaged rigging hardware and slings also becomes clear as the viewer watches a sling break in less than 1/30th of a second. Seeing is believing, and this video will make a believer out of anyone who ever threw a chain over a bucket just to pick up this one little pipe.
