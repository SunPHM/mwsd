Ben and Jean Crane, Wheaton

Ben and Jean Crane stand in their new four-season room off the back of their home. Ben has retired from a career with Bell Labs and Jean from working in healthcare management. Now that they are retired, Jean keeps busy with church and volunteer work and her husband runs a home business on the Internet.

California…Colorado…Missouri...Wheaton?

Ben and Jean Crane probably surprised themselves when they chose to stay in Wheaton after retirement. They had considered moving out of state to live closer to family, but with family all over the country they faced a dilemma.

After deciding to stay in Wheaton, they began tackling remodeling plans for their 30-year-old home. And there was no place like home…but not until they were through with it!

Walker Construction began working in the summer of 2001 to add a four-season room to the back of the home, install all new siding and windows and a specialty window in the master bedroom. The Meyer Landscaping Company completed landscaping for the home and built a beautiful brick driveway and walkway for the front of the house; Walker Construction also redid the front doorway. The effect created a stunning view of the front of the home.

“We always wished we’d put a porch on,” Jean said of the addition on the back.

“It’s always been Jean’s dream to have a porch,” Ben said.

The Cranes had thought about many changes. When they began working with Walker Construction, they felt they were given structure for their ideas. They enjoyed watching their plans develop in rough form on computer screen during the initial visit.

            The Clock Begins…

            When the Cranes received the timeline for work to be done, they were able to plan ahead.

            “We found that was extremely helpful,” they said. “It made it seem more real. It helped us visualize the different steps that were required.”

            Ben and Jean dove into the project with gusto…researching different kinds of windows, siding, and driveways, visiting several stores and various salesmen.

            At times “It was pretty agonizing,” Ben said of picking out the windows.

            They visited F.E. Wheaton’s Yorkville center to see how cedar, vinyl and aluminum siding were created. That visit enabled them to more quickly reach their decision for cedar siding.

Jean’s tip for homeowners was to really do the homework.

“Everybody complimented us on the fact that we had done our homework,” Ben said.

When it came time to build a new driveway, they knew what they wanted but couldn’t find a similar driveway anywhere.

“We researched the daylights out of it,” Ben said. They took pictures, drove from street to street and town to town. Finally they saw something they liked in Hinsdale and were able to build their driveway to look the same.

Each new choice affecting the remodeling project the Cranes researched diligently.

“It’s not an easy decision to make [to remodel],” they said. It also wasn’t easy to live in a home under repair and construction.

“It’s a difficult thing,” Ben said, “But [Walker Construction] made it as good as it could be.”

They were glad to be retired and to have the time to keep up with the project. 

For decorating the inside of their new sunroom, they chose a Williamsburg style. Light envelopes the room and creates a garden-like atmosphere for the plants and flowers growing near the windows. The windows go along two sides of the room and a glass door is on the third side. Triangle-shaped windows rest above the windows on the far side and enhance the picturesque view of the woodsy outdoors.

“I like the light and just the fresh morningness of it,” Jean says of the room. “It’s very peaceful.”

One feature is the blind-system installed inside the window panes and the grids that snap out…making the windows easier to clean. All the windows in the home are multi-paned with snap-out grids.

      The Crew

      “Marty was great,” the Cranes said.

Marty, crew leader for the job, would let them know of different options they had throughout the project. He would sometimes suggest, “You could do it this way…but if it were my house, I’d….”

The Cranes also appreciated Bob, the second crew member on board. He was “pleasant, nice to talk with, accommodating, sensitive.”

“They were just nice to have around,” Jean said. “I kind of miss them, actually.”

“They would work to complete a phase…sometimes until 7 at night…with a flashlight,” Ben recalled.

            Afterthoughts

When the project was all over, the Cranes felt good about it. There was no second guessing themselves or wishing they had done something further, as had been their feelings with previous building projects.

“I haven’t had that sense with this house at all,” Jean said.

The Cranes appreciated Walker Construction’s adherence to the proposed cost. They stated that the project was “On time. On task. On budget.”

How does Ben feel about the project? “I feel liberated,” he said.
