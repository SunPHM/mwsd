All Day, All Inclusive Migratory Bird and Rookery Islands Tour

A Day of Natural and Culinary Delights

Join us for a very unique birding experience that only Rockport Birding and Kayak Adventures can offer. The Skimmer, 40 feet long and drawing just over 2 feet of water, provides comfortable transportation and a vantage point available on no other vessel. This trip allows our guests to enjoy some of the finest wildlife, wildflowers, weather, and cuisine the Texas coastal bend has to offer.

We leave the dock @ 7:30 AM and head north to the Winn Ranch on the south end of Matagorda Island for a nature walk at peak activity time to view an array of South Texas wildlife such as Whitetail Deer, Wild Boar, Wild Turkey, Javalina, Bobcats, Coyotes, Raccoons, Opossum, and a variety of other mammals. We may see reptiles such as Alligators, Diamondback Rattlesnakes, Speckled Kingsnakes, Eastern Hognose, and various turtles. Depending on conditions, there can be a high concentration of migratory songbirds that have just arrived from across and around the Gulf of Mexico including Warblers, Vireos, Buntings, Thrushes, and Orioles. Other avian sightings may include Whitetail Hawk, Crested Cara Cara, Alpomado Falcon, Red-tailed Hawk, to name a few. There are also splendid views of marsh estuaries and mud flats that hold a variety of shorebirds and fish. We will return to the boat and head to the rookery islands that will literally be covered with nesting colonial waterbirds, all in their magnificent breeding plumage. While visiting these islands located in San Antonio, Ayres, Mesquite, Carlos, and Aransas Bays you will see the Aransas National Wildlife Refuge, historic San Jose Islands, and Cedar Bayou.

We are offering two different options for these all day tours:

  • The Gourmet Excursion featuring gourmet breakfast and lunch prepared by some of the coastal bend’s best chefs. Our chefs will join us on some of the trips to enhance the culinary experience. Gourmet juices, waters, and other beverages will also be included. Special guest naturalists Petra Hockey, Ray Little, and Richard Gibbons are some of the veteran colonial waterbird experts who will join us to provide you unparalleled insight into the natural ecology of the area as well as the habits of the wildlife.

  • The Box Lunch Trip offers an economical opportunity to enjoy this exciting rookery tour. This trip will feature breakfast tacos and a box lunch prepared by local Rockport delis. Bottled water and soft drinks are also included. Our knowledgeable staff naturalists will help you identify species and explain the history of the area.

    • Passenger count will be limited to 16 persons providing an uncrowded atmosphere on both of these trips. This should be a great opportunity for photographers.
