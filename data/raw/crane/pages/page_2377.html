Dagger Point Trail

Distance: 1.0 mile
Accessibility: Challenging to Difficult

Dagger Point is one of the more challenging trails on the refuge with sand that shifts underfoot as you cover the hilly terrain. It is also a very nice trail well worth your time. The trail starts near the shore and has a steep incline (photo) right off. At the top is a bench for resting, then it is downhill to the point (distant land in photo below) which juts out into San Antonio Bay. You can relax here for awhile, or take a short stroll along the beach, returning to the trail (uphill again) where you come to a fork. One way takes you back to your car along the short route, while the other leads deeper into the tree covered dunes.

Dagger Point stretches out into the San Antonio Bay in this photo taken from the tall boardwalk near the parking lot. If you don't feel up to the trek in the sand, take the time to enjoy this view.

When you have emptied the sand out of your shoes, hop in your car and drive to Jones Lake.
