Driving change: motor vehicle impacts on the environment

Can our breathless love affair with the car become a thing of the past? Take a look at the past, present and future of automobiles and the environment in this edition's lead story. Read the full article.


Long day's journey

Environmental problems go hand-in-hand with commuting hassles. Building more roads won't solve either problem. But a solution to traffic congestion can improve the quality of air and life. Read the full article.


Fueling a revolution

Development of new fuels promises to have a real impact on the environment. These fuels burn cleaner, reduce foreign imports and some are made from agricultural products that benefit the state economy. Read the full article.


Cleaner, greener cars picking up speed

Fuel efficiency in cars has declined in the last 10 years, not because manufacturers can't make fuel-efficient cars but because Americans don't want them. But fuel efficiency will be on the rise if some promising new technologies come into vogue. Read the full article.


Reaching the end of the road

Your old car may be gone, but its environmental effects can linger on. When not properly handled, old cars can contribute to soil and water contamination. Read the full article.
