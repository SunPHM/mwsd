ReGENco Tests Massive Cranes

 

WEST ALLIS, WI (February 5, 2003)  - ReGENco announced today the successful completion of a lift designed to test the capabilities of their two 150 ton cranes. The lift, which tested the cranes in tandem, equaled 637,416 pounds of concrete and other ballast. The cranes are certified yearly by OSHA. In preparation for the lift, ReGENco also had a structural engineer inspect the building and experts inspect the crane and be on site to inspect the rigging and oversee the lift.

The cranes were tested in anticipation of six rail shipments of generators and turbines arriving in mid February for long term storage. ReGENco is offering storage of this large equipment in response to demand within the power industry. “We have seen a tremendous need for this service. The cancellation and deferral of many projects due to the post-Enron financial environment has our clients looking for a more cost effective storage alternative to the high prices being charged by the OEM’s,” according to John Bobrowich, president and CEO of ReGENco.

ReGENco already has equipment in storage that has been delivered by truck, but the rail shipments are another step. When the facility was built by Allis-Chalmers in 1902, rail tracks entered the property and most of the buildings, including the high bay currently occupied by ReGENco. Union Pacific has certified the rails and Computerized Structural Design has judged the building structurally sound.

The facility includes:

  • 24,000-sq. ft. under 300 ton Hook Indoors and Heated
  • 35,000-sq. ft. under 100 ton Hook Indoors
  • 200,000-sq. ft. Indoor Cold Storage
  • Central Location in West Allis, Wisconsin
  • Rail (Both Canadian Pacific and Union Pacific), Interstate and Port Access
  • High Bay (80 feet) Buildings and Entry Doors
  • Acres of Outdoor Secure Laydown

The on-site technical staff is fully trained and equipped to protect new equipment warranties. ReGENco maintains a fully computerized equipment locator system. Maintenance and monitoring are on a scheduled basis. ReGENco also offers on-site rigging and transportation support and engineered solutions to practical problems related to equipment handling.

ReGENco was formed in 1999 to preserve the high quality turbine and generator service capability historically provided to customers from their West Allis, Wisconsin facility. Drawing upon the large local pool of skilled turbine and generator engineers and craft labor, ReGENco has created an OEM caliber repair facility, which includes the industry’s largest, independently owned high-speed balance bunker. The independent nature of the company, combined with its exceptionally strong engineering and craft competencies, enable ReGENco to provide the highest quality, value added, repair solutions.
