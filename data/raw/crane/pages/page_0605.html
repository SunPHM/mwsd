Sensei Richard Lewis' Black Belt presentation

 

A great celebration was held on 3rd April 2004 in the Fudoshin Honbu in Frankston.

Richard was presented with his colour: Navy Blue and his animal: the Crane

The evening's MC was the fabulous Mark Wilson who kept the activities running smoothly. Mark also helped with the organisation of the night. His play pal Marty also provided much assistance in bringing the whole night together.

A hearty spit roast meal, with salads and a number of yummy homemade deserts kept us going all night.

The highlight was Soke offering Richard's son Tristan the opportunity to present the black to his dad.

For many though the highlights would be the demonstrations performed to celebrate a new Yudansha in the ranks.

Three of the youngest members of Fudoshin Connor, Kieran and Dominic performed a demonstration including aspects from a number of early grades.

Sempai Josh and ???? performed a composite demonstration of techniques including real attacks with a live knife. (we know it really was sharp!)

Sensei Richard performed one of his favourite katas, the Fudoshin Battle Kata with the katana. For him it was an honour to perform in front of so many friends and family.

Sempai Graeme and one of his students from the Tuncurry Bamboo Warrior Dojo in NSW performed an interesting demonstration which was a personal favourite of Soke. Sempai Graeme began as an instructor studying a book who is approached by an eager student . The instructor demonstrates progressively more difficult techniques which the student easily manages to accomplish. EAch time the instructor hopes to return to contemplation of his book the student returns for more instruction. Many techniques use the book as part of the defense and counter attack. Finally the instructor finishes with a technique that locks the student on the ground enabling the instructor to hold him in place while he provides a comfortable seat from which to contemplate the book. The book used so effectively in the demonstration was then presented to Sensei Richard.

Finally Senseis Darren and Daniel treated us to high flying and spirited demonstration of a number of techniques which also contained advanced principles of Fudoshin.

A competition was held for guessing the animal and colour that would be presented to Richard. There were many guesses from the monkey and cat families and a large range of colours with a few more purples and blues. While no one guessed the Crane two people guessed the colour.
