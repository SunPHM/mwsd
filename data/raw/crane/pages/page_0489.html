Obituaries


MILDRED KIRKPATRICK

KIRKPATRICK, Mildred, 104, of Eaton, Indiana, died June 30. Mrs. Kirkpatrick was featured on the cover of the January 2000 WN and would have turned 105 on Oct. 10. She was one of 40 people baptized in Indianapolis, Indiana, on March 6, 1966. Mildred was able to stay in the house she had lived in for about 70 years until the last several weeks of her life. She was much admired and continued to serve the church through prayer up to the very end. Mrs. Kirkpatrick was preceded in death by her husband, Wilbur, in 1985. She is survived by her niece, Betty Hays, and her husband, Richard, a great-niece and three great-great nieces and nephews.

ORMISTON, Elmer E., 81, of Elma, Washington, died July 23. He is survived by his wife of 49 years, Betty; a son, Mark, and his wife, Cindy; daughters, Mary Ellen Watson and a son-in-law Aubrey; a daughter, Janalee Swisher, and a son-in-law Kenneth; four grandchildren; and two step-grandchildren. Elmer was a private first class in the army, a carpenter, gardener and built furniture.


JOHN HENRY CRANE

CRANE, John Henry, 70, Jasper, Georgia, died July 26 of cardiac arrest after several years of declining health. He is survived by his wife of 45 years, Barbara; a daughter and son-in-law, Caren and Bill Tollner; a son, Alan; two sisters-in-law; three nieces; and two nephews. John was a WCG member for 33 years, a veteran of the U.S. Navy and a retired carpenter and cabinet builder. He attended the Murphy, North Carolina, congregation.


FLORENCE SEFCAK

SEFCAK, Florence Edna, 89, of Big Sandy, Texas, died June 17 after a long and difficult struggle with cancer. She was a longtime member of the church, having been baptized in 1949. Florence and her husband, Lee, were among the first members in Texas. In 1951 they moved to Southern California and remained there for 40 years. They were among the original members of the Pasadena congregation. Florence was a homemaker, caregiver and beloved wife, mother, grandmother and great-grandmother. For many years, she voluntarily assumed the role of primary caregiver for Floy Crosby, an unrelated elderly woman with no immediate family. Her favorite hobbies were flowers and plants, traveling and rock hunting and collecting. Florence and Lee were married for 70 years. In addition to her husband, Florence is survived by her daughter and son-in-law, Joyce and Carn Catherwood; her son and daughter-in-law, Gary and Karla Sefcak; five grandchildren, Suzy Molnar, Sharie Damore, Laura Pink, Kim Hollis and Tommy Sefcak; 12 great-grandchildren; a sister, Pauline Terrell; and a brother, Tommy Swenson.

ABBOT, Kenneth Abbot, 83, former head of the Music Department at Ambassador College in Bricket Wood, England, died Aug. 21 after a long illness.

HURLBURT, Barbara Ann, 63, of Riverside, California, died of heart attack Aug. 17. She is survived by her husband of 44 years, Donald; her sons, David and Stephen; a daughter, Laureen Foat; a daughter-in-law, Kristel Hurlburt; a son-in-law, Mark Foat; two brothers, Paul and Stephen Hadnagy; two sisters, Audrey Denunzio and Betsy Osborn; and three grandchildren, Matthew Dillon Foat and Ashley and Austin Hurlburt. Barbara was a church member for nearly 34 years. She was a loving, giving and devoted wife, mother and grandmother and served in the church for a number of years before a stroke in 1995. She will be remembered for her gentle, loving, always-giving spirit.

ROSS, Charles W. Jr., 75, of Houston, Texas, died May 26 after a long bout with cancer. He had been a WCG member since 1963. He will be remembered for his wonderful smile, his friendliness and his willingness to help anyone in need. He is survived by his wife of 27 years, Yvonne; a sister, Molly Mae Bradley; his stepmother, Beatrice Ross; six sons, Mike, Barry, Chris, Charles, Curtis and Clifford; one daughter, Cassandra; four daughters-in-law; eight grandchildren; two great-grandchildren; and a host of nieces, nephews, other relatives and friends. Beginning in 1946, Charles served in the United States Air Force for three years and spent time in Okinawa, Japan. He went on to work for the Houston Belt & Terminal Railroad for 44 years and retired in 1989.

WEBSTER, Floyd C., 74, of Carson City, Nevada, died Aug. 21 after a four-year battle with Lou Gehrig’s disease (amyotrophic lateral sclerosis). Floyd was a longtime member of the church having been baptized in San Diego, California, in 1960. Floyd will be remembered for his great faith and his love of making people laugh. Survivors include his wife of 50 years Carolyn; five sons, Charles, Mark, Wesley, Keith and James; five daughters, Denise Ivicevic, Lila Manion, Mary Beraz, Ruth Gallups and Naomi Logsdon; a brother, Lyle; 16 grandchildren; five sons-in-law; three daughters-in-law; and two step-grandchildren.

AIGLES, Edward Joseph "Tubby," 75, of Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, died July 9 of cardiac arrest after a long battle with emphysema. He is survived by his wife of 44 years, Frances.


ROGER L. FOSSA

FOSSA, Roger L., 82, of the San Jose, California, church, died June 1 after a 14-year battle with cancer. He was known in many other churches as he was a traveling jewelry salesman from 1967 to 1988.

LUMBARDO, Michael J., 89, of Prescott, Arizona, died Sept. 10. He became a WCG member in 1951 in Pasadena, where he became the personal landscape/gardener for Herbert W. Armstrong, a role he maintained for seven years. For health reasons he moved to Santa Barbara, California, in 1970, to the Port Hueneme-Oxnard, area in 1973, then to Arizona in 1992 with church brother Budde Marino. He took with him his lifelong love of golf, which he shared with members from the Sedona and Prescott churches. In 1994 he moved to Prescott Valley, Arizona, where he became an outstanding example for the members.
