May 22, 2002
Why is this construction site so quiet?
Where are the construction workers?
  
Why is the tower crane lying on the ground in pieces, unassembled?
  
    Slayden Construction, OSU's contractor for the project, refuses to hire a Union crane operator. Construction on the Dixon Recreation Center has just about ground to a halt while this dispute between Local 701 of the International Union of Operating Engineers and Slayden Construction is resolved.
  
At issue are safety concerns and the prevailing wage rule.

   The Union trains Crane Operators as professionals and has stringent safety requirements. A Union Crane Operator puts in 1,000 hours of on-the-job training, with a Trainer on the crane with him. The Union pays the Trainer's wages. The result? A job well done, no damage to the project, and nobody hurt.

   Slayden wants to put an operator in the tower crane who has been certified by the crane manufacturer and that is the extent of his training.

   Do crane accidents happen? You bet! Check www.craneaccidents.com. See what OSHA has to say about Cranes and Derricks.
