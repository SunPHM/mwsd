Like lots of kids I Know,

Sandhill Cranes love loafin' around
in special places with their families.


Before dawn the Sandhill cranes wake up hungry and ready for breakfast. The large silver-blue birds flap their wings headed for Goose Pasture, at Jasper-Pulaski Fish and Wildlife Area.
Flocking together at Goose Pasture these birds of a feather make plans for the day. "Let's go to farmer Hamstra's field's. We can loaf around, dance and enjoy the farmer's leftovers."

"Yeah!"

After a day of feeding and getting fat on farmer Hamstra's leftover corn and a few snail snacks from the meadow. The cranes return to Goose Pasture to gossip about their day. After sunset the Sandhill cranes return to their water beds.
