In the beginning there was a tree - or what was left of one a previous owner had topped. It was infested with carpenter ants so we had it removed and the stump ground out. Since there wasn't any grass growing there anyway we laid out an area and connected it to an existing garden bed circling the cover of our septic tank and so was born our Sunny Garden. We found a supply of large rocks from a nearby construction site and used them to ring the new bed, adding a load of topsoil and of course Peat Moss. We were now ready to plant. I found that, at least for me Photographing the empty bed made planning where to plant what much easier. Once I had decided on the layout and the various plants I took my blueprint and went to work. Having Full Sun gave me a lot of options. Already having Irises and Sedum in the original portion of the garden I added mainly perrenials. Veronica, Russian Sage, and Liatris for height; Salvia and Black-eyed Susans for long lasting blooms. I used annuals for their immediate value of color, especially Alyssum, Impatiens and Ganzia. For structure we added an Arch/Bower and a wire Whooping Crane holding a gazing ball.

If you can't find room in your budget for the sometimes pricey Gazing Balls try OLD BOWLING BALLS they come in a variety of colors and patterns and can be found at many yard sales. A wooden dowel driven into the ground will support the bowling ball using the finger holes. A coat of glossy lacquer sprayed onto the ball will liven up even the dullest finish.Here's a photograph of the garden taken from the second floor window. There are many things that you can do in the garden with ordinary objects that will create interesting focal points. What better place than a lovely garden for a " Spot of Tea ". .
