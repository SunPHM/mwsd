Michigan's Upper Penninsula - Land of Walkingshaw and pasties.
The late Michigan dentist Larry Walkingshaw loved cranes and decided to study them, first in the Upper Peninsula of Michigan, then the entire continent. His land is a land of bogs and boreal forests, wolves and loons, black flies and good German staple food called "pasties". It is a place that still holds good populations of cranes, but a land also increasingly visited and developed by the outside world.
