This parcel was not so much a postage stamp as a large knife blade shaped wedge.

Only on the way back from that distant field, out of breath and huffling a bit, did I look

down and begin to notice some of the nuance of what I was tromping across. It lead me to

the next sketch: What if our piece wasn't totally revealed in a tall grass landscape. Suppose the

wing of a crane was all that was seen from a distance in a mid-summer landscape. When you got closer

you saw something like this (on the right below).

A Sandhill Crane mounted on a steel canvas. Part of the body of the crane might be cut out of the steel so that the landscape could be seen through the art. A collaborative element might be the decorating of the steel canvas with images from field sketchbooks of conference attendees. Some of those images of plants, or animal icons could also be cut out of the steel. "Color" and patina would be supplied by the landscape.

Perhaps the piece would be supported by a base of cleared field stones (above left photo).

The Sandhill to me seemed like the perfect poster bird for the coexistance of the varied landscapes.

It was comfortable in original prairie, but now thrives in the stubble and puddles of farm fields. You can see

that I used that fence post idea as a sort of Stonehenge type circle around the piece.

Later feedback from almost everyone gave me second thoughts about this, so I scratched it.
