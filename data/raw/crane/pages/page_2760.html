Sandhill cranes land again

Video
By Discoverychannel.ca Staff, November 14, 2000

Operation Migration's latest project - leading a group of sandhill cranes from Wisconsin to Florida - touched down on November 11, 2000. Eleven of the 13 birds completed the migration, led by three ultralight planes. The journey was part of the group's ongoing effort to direct the cranes along safe migratory routes. The work with these birds is preparation for an attempt to reintroduce a group of endangered whooping cranes, cousins to the sandhill, beginning in 2001. To see the interview, click on the image.

Check out the Operation Migration website for full details on the latest sandhill crane project, including field journals chronicling the birds' journey.

Past projects

@discovery.ca has been following Operation Migration and its work with the sandhill cranes since 1995. Over the years the group has learned from some early mistakes and continues to try new methods to reintroduce the birds into their own habitat with the least possible amount of human contact.

A handpuppet of an adult crane is used to feed the young birds.  Video
1998 Testing the migration route on a new flock of Sandhill cranes, the group focused not on how they get where they're going but on their behaviour after they arrive. The program made adjustments to create an environment as much like the wild as possible.

A group of sandhill touched down in an Ontario school yard.  Video
1997 Operation Migration got an exciting call from an Ontario school - the cranes they'd been waiting for made it home, sort of. Watch the @discovery.ca video of their route back.
