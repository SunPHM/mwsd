Festival of Origami Architecture

More than 3,000 people practiced the Japanese art of origami (paper folding) and made artful creations at the Museum’s Festival of Origami Architecture. The festival was organized in conjunction with Washington D.C.’s annual National Cherry Blossom Festival®. Families made an origami city, complete with skyscrapers, obelisks, and houses, and pop-up cards of pyramids, houses, and other structures. Takaaki Kihara, an origami architecture master from Japan, awed the audience with his origami techniques. He created a replica of Mount Vernon specifically for the festival, in honor of the Museum’s exhibition Saving Mount Vernon: The Birth of Preservation in America. In addition, Kazuma Takahashi, Japanese paper artist, displayed his unique art form of making movable objects, such as a windmill, using commercial packaging.

 
Origami cranes fly through the Museum’s Great Hall at the Festival of Origami Architecture, as more than 3,000 visitors make their own origami creations.
