Crane's Woodcuts

 With the black outlined figures and the ornate patterns on the bed and draperies, this illustration from The Frog Prince (1874) is typical of his woodcuts during the period. Notice how Crane conveys the transformation of the frog and the passage of time within a single illustration.

The light hue of Little Red Riding Hood's face draws the eye to the center of the illustration where she is dawdling over her bouquet. before Crane's use of line directs us to the dark shape of the wolf ominously speeding ahead.
