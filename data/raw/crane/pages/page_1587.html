Engine to Growth

The company, with its powerful engines for construction and mining, is well positioned to take on the market demand with new alliances and projects. Its capacity to upgrade its products backed by service will ensure its position as a leader.

Cummins India Limited (CIL) is a 51 per cent subsidiary of Cummins Inc. USA, the world's largest independent diesel engine designer and manufacturer from 18 HP to 3,500 HP. Cummins Engine Co. (CECO) set up CIL in 1962 as KCL, jointly promoted with Kirloskar Oil Engines Limited to manufacture diesel engines. In 1997, CECO increased its stake making it a 51 per cent subsidiary of the parent company in Columbus, Indiana. Today, CIL is India's largest manufacturer of diesel engine value packages ranging from 60 HP to 2700 HP serving the power generation, industrial and automotive markets. The Cummins Industrial Business Unit manufactures and markets diesel and gas engines which are available on a variety of mobile and stationary equipment. In the construction market, Cummins engines are used for excavators, tippers, backhoe loaders, front end loaders, graders, pavers, portable compressors, finishers, compactors, dozers, skid steer loaders, cranes, forklifts, reach stackers, etc. For mining, they are used in dumpers, dozers, graders, excavators, loaders, blast hole rigs, coal haulers, surface miners, tyre handlers, etc. Cummins engines are also widely used for a variety of applications in various sectors like compressors; rail; marine, fire fighting pumps, oil fields and defence. Satish Phadke, Vice President - Industrial Business, CIL tells Editor Pratap Padode more about the company….

On the company's products…
We are in partnership with OEMs with an aim to providie the latest products for construction and mining. We distinguish the two fields by the power of the machine. Generally, construction machines are under 500 HP while for mining they are more than 350 HP. We are also bringing in new generation engines for modern construction equipment like skid steers, pavers, compactors, loaders, excavators and cranes to be used in infrastructure development like roads, irrigation, dams, ports, etc.


On operations…
The base engines are made at the Pune factory.
Our engines are customised to the demands of the application and are offered with cooling, exhaust and air intake systems fully integrated with equipment design for optimum performance.

When the C series engine was introduced on Telcon excavators, the first 60 to 80 engines were imported, and now we are developing the engines indigenously. Our engines are supported by a competent service network Over a 1,00,000 engines installed base is serviced by 90 dealers and 215 service outlets. Of the 90 dealers, half of them have their own service centres.

On meeting emission norms …
The year 2004-05 will see the adoption of stringent emission norms and only those companies which have the latest technology will be able to meet them. At Cummins, our corporate mission dictates that everything we do leads to a cleaner, healthier and safer environment. Cummins Inc. has always been a pioneer in offering latest technology products to meet stringent world-wide norms. Cummins India is well positioned to offer products for all markets meeting emission norms applicable in India.

On the future…
Currently, the equipment market is growing at the rate of 5-7 per cent and is headed to rise sharply ahead. I foresee a major boost in mining projects which are coming up. State highways, roads to important links like ports and mines are the other growth drivers.

Railway maintenance too is going to require equipment for safety and track maintenance. Overall, road construction growth will be fuelled by state highway projects and Cummins is well positioned to take on the market demand with new alliances and projects. It would be mandated as a preferred supplier due to the upcoming emission norms and its capacity to upgrade its products backed by service. This will ensure the company's dominant position in the sector.
