Voltan's space dock

I've been playing a lot of full thrust lately so that influenced my choice of a space dock, I then had the good idea of haivng the dock built in to a potato..... erm, asteroid.


So to start off I cut out a bit of plasticard for my base and started bulking out the basic shape with lots and lots of green stuff.

I followed that by adding the basic dock bit with some brown stuff this was left very rough as the cunnnign plan was to add another smoother layer of brown stuff later, or file it smooth, depending on which method worked.
It was a bit of both in the end.

So with the basic shape in place, yet more green stuff was added , but this time I poked it, hmm, with various bits and pieces (dry lumps of putty, sculpting tools, cocktail sticks) to give the surface a pockmarked cratery look.


With all that out the way some nice neat geometric structures were added at various points to give the impression of building on the surface (it's not very clear on the top photo but there are three pipelines leading out of the surface up to the dock, these are my electrical component- fuse wire, I rule or not)

You done yet goes up the cry, not even close, so there will be a short recess while you go to the toilet or have a cuppa or eat cake. Using the master I've finished up above I made a mould so I could cast it in resin. As you can see on the above photo, this involves a wall made out of lego bricks to hold the rubber in and a screwdriver to hold the lego wall against the plasticard base to stop any more of the rubber escaping on to the rug, ho hum.

24 hours later, after cutting the rubber mould away from the rug, you remove the lego and the master to show a lovely negative impression of your master.

You then pour resin into it, following the instructions, and get a nice new copy of your whatever, in this case space dock. Plus I can now make lots of space docks and take over the world or something.

Painting was fairly easy just lots of drybrushing for the rock bit, with the constructions painted grey then given a black ink wash and a few bits of yellow and red added for lights.
The cranes were a last minute thought and I got the idea here and seeing as I have a pack of the starfuries that will never be used, we have cranes instead.


This picture is just to show how the base was designed to fit in with my own spaceships i've been making.

Materials used and costs

Plasticard (already had)
Green stuff  36" strip (got on offer for £6.50)
Brown stuff about a third of a strip (approx £2)
Urethane resin approx 150ml (haven't had chance to work out cost, can't remember
what SG is but won't  be more than a couple of pounds)
RTV silicone rubber approx half a kilo (approx £9)
15 amp Fuse wire (pass dirt cheap)
Four vanes off of AOG starfuries ( bought long ago for unremembered amount)
Total cost approx.- £19.50

24 hours for making the moulds
0.5 hours for casting
