Sandhill Crane Watching near Grand Island, Nebraska

In late March, members and friends of the Geography Student Organization head west to the "Central Flyway" between Grand Island and Kearney to see the spring migration of Sandhill Cranes, Snow Geese, and Canadian Geese. We're never disappointed...

During the day Sandhill Cranes head to the fields to feed. Note Interstate 80 in the background.

With good eyes and ears you can see the cranes dance and hear them discussing current events.

Around dusk we headed down to the Platte to see the cranes settle down for the evening. Mel (pointing) saw something important but none of us cared...

Silouettes of cranes heading to the river can be seen in this photo. It was a Nebraska "license plate" sunset.
