Cranes for Peace

A Bethany senior makes a paper crane to add to the 1,000 paper cranes created by a group of Bethany students this semester. This group met twice a week since January to work on origami. The paper crane project was inspired by the story of a Japanese girl, Sadako, who in 1955 made over 1,000 cranes in the hopes that the gods would heal her from leukemia--the "atom bomb disease." Today people from all over the world fold paper cranes and send them to a monument in Hiroshima that honors Sadako and the desire for peace. Students commented that each crane made was a prayer for peace.
