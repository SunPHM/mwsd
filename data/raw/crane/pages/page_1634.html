Airport construction shuts runway nightly
reported by Cara Jones
Last updated: April 15, 2004 05:33 PM
LEE COUNTY, September 9, 2003 - If you're heading in or out of Southwest Florida International Airport in the next few months you won't want to be on a delayed late night flight. Airport officials are shutting the runway down overnight to connect it to a new taxiway. It’s all part of a $430 million airport expansion project.

If you were to take a midnight tour of Southwest Florida International, you would find one sleepy terminal, but in the middle of it all are cranes, trucks and a dedicated crew that are all wide awake.

“What we're doing is building a new parallel taxiway and tying it into the existing runway,” said project manager Joe Glowacki.

“We can't discontinue flights during the day, so this is the only time to get the work done,” said Robert Ball of the Lee County Port Authority.

It’s moonlighting by necessity. The work starts at 12:25 am and ends at 5:40 am.

“The guys are really pushing to have it all buttoned up so it doesn't impact the airlines here,” said Jason Beem, a supervisor on the project.

The airport doesn't expect the project will influence the traveling public because most planes come in by about 11:15 pm. But there could be a problem if there are flight delays.

“The airlines would have to divert passengers, probably to Sarasota and bus passengers in,” said Ball.

The new runway has a two-month time limit. So crews work every night, overnight, to get it completed.

“We have a tight window and a lot of work to do in a small amount of time,” said Ball.

Airport officials encourage anyone meeting a late night flight to confirm arrival times with the airline.

Construction should wrap up in October.
