  Amy Crane  
 

Amy Crane serves as adjunct professor of piano. Mrs. Crane is a pianist, organist, and harpsichordist and received degrees of Bachelor of Science in Music Education and Master of Music in Piano Performance from Ithaca College.

Mrs. Crane has accompanied numerous solo and ensemble concerts and performed as soloist and accompanist on duo recitals throughout the United States. At the age of 16, she appeared as soloist with the Buffalo Philharmonic Orchestra, and was selected for two years to perform piano concertos with the Ithaca College Symphony Orchestra.

She has appeared as soloist with the University of Iowa Brass Ensemble and has accompanied professional choirs at the Kennedy Center and Constitution Hall in Washington, DC.

She appeared for several seasons as soloist and faculty accompanist for the Tidewater Music Festival at St. Mary’s College of Maryland and served as faculty accompanist at the music teachers’ conference at the University of Wisconsin at Madison.

Mrs. Crane has taught piano at colleges in California and Maryland. She is an adjunct professor of piano at Daytona Beach Community College and accompanies the Stetson University Opera Workshop and the Stetson University Young Singers.

Mrs. Crane is a member of the Volusia County alumnae chapter of Sigma Alpha Iota, women’s music fraternity, Pi Kappa Lambda, honorary music fraternity, and the Volusia County Music Teachers Association.
