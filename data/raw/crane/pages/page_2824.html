West Texas - The staked plains
This is a land of level horizons and broad shoulders, of incessant winds and Texas pride. You wouldn't know it at a glance, but this minimalist High Plains landscape supports the largest wintering concentrations of sandhill cranes anywhere in the continent. Here, they dodge periodic drought, hunters guns and incessant winds, seeking out waste grains in milo, wheat and cotton fields by day, and refuge on a shrinking number of pluvial basins and playa lakes by night.
