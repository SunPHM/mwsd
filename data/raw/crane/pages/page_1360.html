Berlin

Berlin was a city of contrast and construction. The skyline of the east was dominated by cranes, especially the Potsdammer Platz, which was being slowly filled to the brim with new, modern buildings. These newer buildings set off the older buildings, or, those that were restored to how they originally looked.

Sometimes the restored buildings would have original pieces, making the building itself a contrast of old and new. This is not a photography trick or digital enhancement, the statues at the top were burned and blackened in the war, the statue on the bottom of the picture is a reproduction, part of the acurate restoration.

Here is another shot full of contrast. In the foreground is a newer monument, in the background, you can see again the blackened statues that survived the war. Further back, in a building built to look older, is a Hilton Hotel, another example of the USA's everpresence. Finally, the farthest-back building is modern, playing no games, not trying to "fit in" what so ever.

I like the pole there, it wasn't a mistake. I like the directional contrast with the bridge.

The sheer size of some of the buildings was absolutely stunning, making the building impressive before you even noticed the archetecture or artwork.

 

The Fernsehturm (TV Tower) was an easy landmark, and allowed you to get your bearings from almost anywhere in this sprawling city. The contrasts of this city are once again visible. If you look closely at the bottom of the picture at left, the people and car can give you a nice size reference. The wires hanging across the middle picture are for the Strassenbahn, one of which is visible at the very bottom (looks like a long yellow and white bus.) Only the former East Berlin had these convienient forms of public transportation.

As mentioned above, cranes and construction have been the dominant feature of the East for almost a decade now.

This is just a small part of that construction, on the outskirts of Potsdammer Platz.
