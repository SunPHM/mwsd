More Updates...


Year 2003 is dedicated to planning and preparation for the full Siberian Migration in year 2004. One of the most important milestones of this year will involve training of Hang Glider pilots to fly with and lead captive reared cranes.


In January of 2003, Dr George Archibald, Tejas Gole and Lorraine Grassano conceived of a plan to train the Hang Glider pilots using captive reared Sandhill Cranes (a relatively abundant North American crane species).

George, Tejas and Lorraine plotting.

 

The 2003 Sandhill crane plan will raise a small flock of Sandhill cranes in a ranch at Hanford California. The cranes will be trained to follow the Hang Gliders and will ultimately be flown on a grand migration all the way to Nebraska.

 


Schedule

  • April Sandhill crane chicks hatch in Patuxent
  • May Sandhill crane chicks flown to Clark ranch, Hanford CA
  • June - July cranes raised by Richard in Hanford
  • July - August flight training with Hang Gliders
