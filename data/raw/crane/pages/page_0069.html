PHOTOS - Crane Collapse at the New York State Supreme Courthouse in Jamaica, Queens July 2002

The New York City Office of Emergency Management responded to the collapse of a 175-ton crane in Jamaica, Queens. OEM served as the inter-agency coordinator in order to expedite the crane and debris removal, and to ensure the continued public safety.


The outrigger on a 175-ton crane has punched through the pavement next to the New York State Supreme Courthouse in Jamaica, Queens. OEM responds and coordinates the operation to stabilize and eventually upright the fallen crane.


The crane's axles, lifted in the air.


The crane's boom was fully extended when the accident occurred: it became damaged when it impacted the side of the courthouse. The building was immediately evacuated.


The gaping hole swallowed one of the crane's outriggers.


OEM First Deputy Commissioner Calvin Drayton and Commissioner John Odermatt confer with an FDNY official.
