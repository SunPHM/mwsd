The History Page-an overview of the association's history

The Child and Youth Care Workers Association of Manitoba was initiated in the early seventies to develop the profession in Manitoba and allow representation to similar associations across the country. The first attempt at association was in 1974 and resulted in the Residential Care Association.  Regretfully, the early years of the association dwindled to the point where one person, Brian Cox kept the Association going until such time as a reassertion of interest could take place. 

In 1985, the concept of the association was brought back into being with the first President being Robin Arnold. This clip from the early minutes sent in by Brian Cox lists the people involved in those early years.

 

 

Robin Arnold focused on membership and there was a newsletter published to the membership. Deb Bomek would represent Manitoba to the Council of Canadian Child and Youth Care Associations and would go on to become its first elected President.

 

 

The highpoint for the Association was its involvement in Creating A Balance the fifth National Child and Youth Care Conference held in Winnipeg in 1986. Brian Cox coordinated the enormous task of preparing 1000 origami cranes for the conference to create a mobile which was to bless the event. The resulting mobile of a thousand cranes hung in the main ballroom throughout the event and remains a memory for those who attended as does a blizzard which blew into the city forcing many to stay put in the conference hotel for an extended stay. 

 

 

Deb Fast was the second President, serving two terms. She was instrumental in bringing Lorraine Fox to present workshops for the association allowing it a more public service for members.

Bev Wiebe was the third President and served two terms. Under her the association built membership and renewed ties to the Council. Bev was also the chairperson for Committed to the Challenge, the Tenth National Child and Youth Care Conference, held in Winnipeg in 1996. Bev also explored certification bringing in representatives of the Alberta Certification Program to detail the process they were going through.

 

 

Peter Riemer was the fourth President. He did much to network with local agencies and to promote the association in the media. Lobbying continued under his term in office for the full time program at Red River College.

 

 

Dianne Watson was the fifth President. Dianne encouraged membership and more services with the establishment of the Educational Workshop Series. There is also work being done on reviewing the Code of Ethics and establishing links to local agencies, facilities and schools. Dianne has encouraged and promoted the creation of the website for the association through assistance to the webmaster of programs and camera necessary for web publication.

The President for the 1999-2000 term, Peter Duff. Peter has been a long standing Board Member. He then worked as a Counsellor with the Mobile Crisis Stabilization Unit. Peter did much to network for the association with individuals and groups in the province. He was very supportive toward Together 2000. Peter was an excellent Master of Ceremonies at the now annual Graduate Celebrations and helped establish the cycwam Award for that event.

 

 

The current President, David Sullivan became interested while attending Together 2000 in Cleveland. There has been a burst of activity under his leadership with many working committees which have established a certification program and sponsored a first provincial child and youth Care Conference.

 

All members of cycwam are also members of the Council of Canadian Child and Youth Care Workers Associations. cycwam was a founding member of the Council providing its first President, Deb Bomek.
