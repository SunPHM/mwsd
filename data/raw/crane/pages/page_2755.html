Sandhill Crane
April 25, 2001
Currently based in Leesburg, Florida, Dave and I have paused in our national driving tour to: first, visit Dave's parents; second, care for their cat while they're in New York helping care for their first grandchild (congratulations Lauran, Pema, and Yumtso); third, to observe a pair of nesting Sandhill Cranes (proud father shown at right) and as much other wildlife as we can find.

Having come from Kansas City, the plantlife in Florida is an interesting change. Most of the neighbors have palm, orange, or grapefruit trees in their yards. Many also have a sago palm, which isn't a palm at all but looks like one that's been buried up to it's fronds.

A Bald Eagle flies through almost every day; the cranes mentioned above often wander into the backyard to pick through the soil for grubs; an egret and an elderly blue heron stake out a nearby pond and squabble throughout the day. And it wouldn't be Florida without the frequent sighting of a pair of close-set eyes floating at the periphery of a community pond.

Tiny, knobby-toed, translucent treefrogs line the exterior window sills at night. One vociferous frog has yet to be sighted but is heard right outside Dave's window early in the morning.


The view from the lanai (screened porched)
Our preparations to journey beyond Leesburg are almost complete. Short supply of screen tents for sale in Florida may delay us a bit, and a pile of laundry has helped keep us from the road so far. Pulling ourselves away from the screened porch view of water and wildlife will be difficult!
