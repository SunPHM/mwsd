Familiarization Meeting Held
August 13, 1998, 1998

Today a group of senior IMAC people had an opportunity to tour the site of the new Edmonton plant and visualize in three dimensions all the result of all the planning that has gone into the project. The group is pictured standing in what will become the accounting area of the office.

During the visit floor plans were examined and related to the physical area of the plant to assist with fine tuning the shop floor layout of machinery. Last minute plans for embedding rails for driving heavy equipment on and the exact placement of rails for a heavy load moving cart were completed and relayed to the contractor.

Synergy Projects Ltd. has now formally committed to having the shop completed for the start of moving in by October 1, with completion of the office slated for October 31, so at the same time, plans for the actual move from the old plant to the new started in earnest.

Even as the third wall inches closer to completion, work is started on the end wall that will finally enclose the shop perimeter. Progress on the center wall, shown clearly in the photo has been much slower that anticipated due to complex reinforced beams and columns that must be built into the structure to support the heavy loads to be handled by cranes bearing on the walls.


Construction of end walls takes place as final bearing wall,
(center) nears the top.

The long construction delay created problems in the area where the office is situated due to rainwater being trapped inside the foundation walls that were completed in December of 1997. Remedial work to insure proper compaction was completed this week so that construction of the office can begin. At the same time, plans have been made to start erecting the steel trusses during the week of August 24.
