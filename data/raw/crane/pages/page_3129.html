A Thousand Cranes - The Statue of Sadako, also known as The Children's Monument, stands in the center of Hiroshima's Peace Park surrounded by millions of paper cranes sent from people around the world.


 “Even the youngest audiences are spellbound.”

“ONE OF THE TEN BEST AMERICAN PLAYS FOR YOUNG AUDIENCES!”
A Thousand Cranes
Book by Kathryn Schultz Miller, Lyrics by Gail Deschamps, Musical Score by Paul Hamilton
Produced by Special Arrangement with Children's Theatre Plays

Kathryn Schultz Miller's true story about the effects of war on the life of a 12-year-old girl is a multicultural experience that has enriched family audiences throughout America. Winner of several awards including a National Endowment for the Arts Playwright Award, this tremendously moving play is now a new musical!


Sadako Sasaki, the surviving infant victim of the Hiroshima atomic bomb learns ten years later - as she gets ready to run and win a girls' race - that she has "radiation sickness". Determined to get well for her race, with boundless optimism, she takes on an old legend to heart: If she folds a thousand origami cranes, she will be made well again.


Before completing her task, she is visited by her dear departed Grandmother who lovingly carries her on the back of a glorious crane to the Land of a Thousand Spirits. She joins her ancestors and learns that her wish may yet be granted. "It is better," says her grandmother, "for others to finish the cranes."

Her friends inspire her classmates to finish the thousand paper cranes. Today children from Japan and all over the world fold a thousand cranes and lay them at the feet of her statue in Hiroshima Peace Park. Their collective wish is engraved at the bottom of the monument:

This is our cry.
This is our prayer.
Peace in the world.

Recommended for audiences of all ages.
