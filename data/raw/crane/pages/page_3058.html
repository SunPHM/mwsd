My Travels as a Teacher

Consortium for Teaching Asia and Pacific Studies
South Pacific Travel Study to Fiji, Western Samoa,
American Samoa and New Zealand
Summer 1995

Elementary School in Fiji
During this trip, my teacher colleagues and I were quite fortunate to visit with school children and with teachers.  The South Pacific remains largely in a third world state, economically,  except for New Zealand and Australia.  Influences from western cultures are slowly creeping in. From this experience, I gained some of  understanding of where some of my students have immigrated from.

 Samoan Village in Potasi, Western Samoa.

Fulbright Memorial Fund Teacher Program
Japan Travel Study
Fall 1997


The Fulbright Memorial Fund Teacher Program was established in 1997 in appreciation of the U.S. Fulbright Program.  As a participant, my three week stay included visiting schools at Elementary, Middle, High and College levels.  We were also fortunate to be included in a homestay with a family that I still keep in touch with today.
