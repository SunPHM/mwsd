The Strongest Link

Demag KBK crane construction kit for plastic rolls

The Spanish manufacturer Plastimer S.A. supplies rolls of plastic for greenhouses and conservatories. The company manufactures plastic foil wound on rolls that have to be handled and transported. The rolls used to be removed from the production machinery by hand, placed on trolleys and then pushed to the next station. The Almeria-based company, which works round the clock in three shift operation, invested in two suspension monorails from the KBK crane construction kit supplied by Demag Cranes & Components S.A., Madrid.

The aim was to interlink various machines by means of materials handling equipment and to optimise the material flow in this way. The owner initially planned to use the new system for transport purposes only and to continue serving the machinery by hand. However, Plastimer grew to like the Demag technology more and more, with the result that the rolls are now picked up and deposited with the help of the KBK system.

This solution enables the plastics manufacturer to employ the workers that used to be needed for this work for other tasks. Furthermore, this resulted in more ergonomically designed workplaces and, therefore, considerably improved working conditions.

The monorail trolley running in the KBK profile section is fitted with two Demag DK electric chain hoists. The chain hoists are synchronised to lift the rolls out of the machine using a special gripper system. A worker initiates the operation by means of a control pendant. The DK units stop lifting as soon as the limit switches integrated in the chain hoists are activated.

A safety cage then shuts automatically. This mechanism prevents rolls from dropping and causing any damage. This is particularly important, because people work and walk beneath the transport route.

When the cage is closed, the KBK suspension monorail transports the load along an overhead line to one of the deposit or packing stations. Upon reaching the destination, the safety mechanism opens and the chain hoists lower the load. When the roll has reached the release level, the lowering process is stopped by the geared limit switches. The gripper mechanism opens and releases the load. The safety mechanism closes again when the chain hoists have returned to the upper hook position. The KBK trolley is then automatically requested to pick up a fresh roll at one of the production stations. In the meantime, the previously handled roll is measured, cut to length, weighed and packed.

There are four stations for the trolley where the load can be picked up. Here, Demag has fitted a switch for ordering and releasing the trolley. The load is unloaded at one station only, however. An insulated conductor was laid parallel to the KBK suspension monorail for the safe transmission of control signals.

The decision in favour of the supplier was influenced by various recommendations. The Spanish subsidiary of Demag Cranes & Components was the right partner who is able to guarantee that installations operate with a high level of availability. Due to the tight space between the various machines, the only materials handling system that could be considered simply had to offer overhead handling.
