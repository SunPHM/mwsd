GOTTWALD PORT TECHNOLOGY - RAILWAY CRANES

Gottwald Port Technology is a manufacturer and supplier of railway cranes for single and multi-track lines, including breakdown cranes (also known as recovery cranes) with telescopic and fixed booms, track-laying cranes, bridge-building cranes, track maintenance cranes, wrecking cranes and universal cranes.

Built on a basic modular system, using reliable standard rolling stock parts combined with components specially conceived for crane operation, Gottwald railway cranes offer a sound solution which can be modified to the needs of any railway company and the peculiarities of any rail system. Gottwald railway cranes owe their maximum availability and reliability to a minimum of maintenance downtime and the use of standard, durable parts and assemblies.

The main features of Gottwald's three varieties of railway crane are:

  • High lifting capacities with moderate crane tare weight - optimum transport and job operation dimensions
  • Wide working ranges taking into account maximum wheel and axle loads
  • Ergonomic operating conditions and operating safety
  • Little maintenance work required due to a clear and concise set-up of assemblies
  • Gottwald bogies for excellent running qualities in train formation and under own power
  • High towing speed for fast arrival at the job site
  • Self-propelled speed of up to 100km/h with the model GS 150.14 TR
  • Fully hydrostatic drive for precise and powerful travel and working motions
BREAKDOWN CRANES

Gottwald breakdown cranes are supplied with telescopic or fixed booms.

In recovery work, cleaning up the accident location has the utmost priority. Gottwald breakdown cranes have proved their worth everywhere in complex job operations under difficult conditions. The high-level performance and the operating safety of the breakdown cranes demonstrate their out-standing quality.

Capacity range: 60t up to 200t.

TRACK-LAYING CRANES AND BRIDGE BUILDING CRANES

Gottwald track-laying cranes offer all the cost-effective features required for maintaining railway routes. Railway cranes designed specifically for track-laying duties are equipped with telescopic boom only. Loads such as track panels and switches can be lifted with the boom in the horizontal position, telescoped and transported. In this way, work can be carried out under catenary safely.

A minimum over-tail radius ensures that the crane can work transverse to travelling direction without hindering the traffic on the adjacent track. The admissible axle loading can be maintained by a correlating number of axles and through a tailor-made counterweight arrangement. The patented 'CILOT'-system allows the counterweight to remain in line with the track when slewing the boom. The traffic on the adjacent track is not impeded and work can be carried out when space is restricted.

Capacity range: 20t up to 160t.

TRACK MAINTENANCE CRANE - GS 150.14 TR
A UNIQUE CONCEPT

The TR stands for telescopic-reversible. The boom can be telescoped in or out to either end of the crane and in this way the working direction can be changed without slewing the superstructure.

The crane has no tail radius. There is no counterweight obstructing the adjacent track when the crane works to the side.

The crane is designed for a self-propelled speed of 100km/h. Towing it by train in order to go from one job site to the other is not necessary but can be done at speeds up to 120km/h. The strictest exhaust limits are met and extremely efficient sound insulation is provided for night work and for use in populated areas.
