Construction of the HSBC Building
[ Construction Pages: timeline   1   2   3   4   5   6   7   8 ]

October, 1985

The 7th floor of the tower is about to be poured atop the wood forms. Eight cement trucks are ready with the concrete. The cranes are lifting cubic yard buckets of concrete at the rate of one every minutes. Note that the metal framing for the glass canopies over the Hornby Street sidewalk has been attached to the concrete columns of the tower. The third floor platform on the east side of the atrium cavity is evident here. Post-tensioned cable reinforced concrete beams laid between the perimeter columns and the centre core support the floor slabs.

This photograph was taken from the Metropolitan Hotel to the east of the site. Note the small crane in the center that pumps the concrete to where it is being poured. The men beneath the large crane at the right are leveling the wet concrete. The floor slabs are strengthened by full-tensioned cables embedded in the concrete.
Photo courtesy of PCL Constructors Western Inc.

November, 1985

Cal Biem, project manager for PCL, says the atrium superstructure was the most logistically difficult phase of construction. The columns, beams and structural concrete were put into place in a 72-hour period. Here we see precast beams being put into place by cranes: some of these beams are 110 feet long. The top beams tie into the eighth floor. Some of the precast columns have hollow sections with interior ladders for access to the lighting, sprinkler and window-washing systems.
Photo courtesy of Peter Ballistoni, The Vancouver Sun.

The columns and beams of the atrium facade have been set in place. Meanwhile, work continues on the tower portion of the building at the 12 floor level. The cranes are raised higher by way of . Note that the support braces have been removed up to beneath the 10th floor: it takes about days for the concrete to cure so that the slabs are strong enough to remove the forms.

December, 1985

The tower has reached up to the 15th floor level. The side header beam at the third floor level is being poured in place to connect the atrium facade with the tower: steel bracing is imbedded in the concrete. On the Hornby Street side, polished granite is being attached to the concrete facing to become the exterior cladding. Inside the skeleton of the building work is beginning on the mechanical and electrical operating systems.
