Balancing Demands on Platte River

Water Resources: A Public Forum

Held February 15th, 2003

 

In 2003, the environmental studies students took the lead in organizing the Second Annual Public Forum on the Environment. The topic selected was Balancing Demands on Platte River Water Resources. The students planned and organized an excellent panel of speakers to discuss a wide range of topics. Speakers scheduled to attend included representatives of Nebraska Department of Natural Resources, U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service, Nebraska Game and Parks Commission, Tri-basin Natural Resources District, Platte River Whooping Crane Truse, Central Nebraska Public Power and Irrigation, and The Nature Conservancy.

 

Unfortunately, Omaha experienced its biggest winter storm of the season the night before our forum was planned to take place. We were pleased that half of our panel of speakers were able to attend and we went forward with a much reduced program. Given the severe weather, we were pleased that almost 40 members of the public braved the snow to attend.
