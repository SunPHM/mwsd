BERTHS 226-232 100 FT. GUAGE CRANE RAIL INSTALATIO
Owner:   Port of Los Angeles
Designer:   Port of Los Angeles
Completed:   October, 1996
Description:
The purpose of this project was to upgrade an existing wharf to support the new 100 ft. gauge post panamax container cranes. This project consisted of constructing 3,096 LF of new crane beam and crane rail. The crane beam is supported on 579 ea. HP14x117 H-Piles on 6 ft. spacing. The crane beams are attached to the existing wharf by concrete tie beams. The beam installation required the removal and replacement of 32,000 SY of 9 in. asphalt paving and base material as well as 100,000 CY of excavation.

The second phase of the project required the addition of 52 under wharf pile caps on new piles driven through the 24" concrete deck and power trench. All of the concrete demolition was performed with 90# breakers and chipping guns. 123 ea. 18 in. octagonal piles were driven through the holes by a floating crane, cut off and cast into the new caps.

The project finished ahead of all milestones and, ultimately, four months ahead of schedule.
