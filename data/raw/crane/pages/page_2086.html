2002 Whooping Crane Migration Completed!
November 30, 2002

Operation Migration, Inc. ultralight aircraft pilots land after a 1,200 mile voyage from Wisconsin to Chassahowitzka National Widllife Refuge , Nov. 30, 2002. The pilots wear whooping crane costumes to continue the imprinting process on the young birds.

Photo Credit: USFWS; Tom MacKenzie

Day 49: Chassahowitzka National Wildlife Refuge, Florida - All 16 whooping cranes took off from Levy County, Florida this morning. They flew over Crystal River Mall in Crystal River, Florida and then the pilots led the cranes to their winter home at Chassahowitzka National Wildlife Refuge. The total distance traveled on this migration was 1,204 miles.

Of the whooping cranes that were ultralight-led on migration last year, four have independently returned to the winter site at Chassahowitzka. The fifth bird, whooping crane #6 was last reported in Meigs County, TN.

Although the migration was long for the migration team (and cranes), their work has been a huge success. The spring and fall migration of last year's birds proves the value of the ultralight-led migration technique and we now have a flock of 20 (soon to be 21) migratory whooping cranes in Florida.
Previous Update

Whoopers of the flock led by ultralight last year are being individually tracked and their progress reported by members of the International Crane Foundation.
