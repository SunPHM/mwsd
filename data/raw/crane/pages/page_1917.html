Whooping Crane
(Grus Americana)
by Jess, Kathryn, and Jane

The Whooping Crane lives in wetlands, pastures, and cultivated fields in the
North Eastern part of America, but the population mainly started in the
Rocky Mountain area.
Due to a loss in their numbers, Whooping Crane eggs were placed in the nests of the Sand Hill
Cranes who raised them. They now migrate from Idaho to New Mexico
with the Sand Hill Crane.
The Whooping Crane eats crabs, clams, grains, berries, vertebrates including fish, amphibians, and
reptiles.
They breed in May and have one or two eggs that hatch in about a month's time.
The Whooping Crane is a lovely, beautifully white bird.
