Sandhill Crane
By Kitty and Jim

The Sand Hill Crane is also known as the Grus Canadensis.
The Sand Hill Cranes are usually found in open grass lands, meadows, and
marshy portions of lakes, ponds, and rivers.
It eats roots, tubers, seeds, grain, berries, small vertebrates, and small invertebrates.
When they breed they fly from Northern Canada down to Cuba.
Eggs hatch after 29-32 days. The babies are able to fly after 2 months.
Although 2 eggs are laid it is uncommon for more than one chick to survive adult hood.
If the nest is lost the parents will re-nest.
