Museum Complex


 Bank 

The Bank Building contains the photo archives of Waskada's past, display cases full of personal memorabilia of citizens from Waskada and surrounding area, a piano and organ, old Eatons' catalogues,  other commercial objects and a new pioneer clothing display.


 Whooping Crane

One of the main attractions of the Bank Building is the Whooping Crane case. Whooping Cranes have faced near extinction, but through preservation practices their numbers have risen. This particular crane was mounted in 1906.

 Church 

This building was originally the Anglican Church, but when attendance declined in the late 1950's and 60's, the church was officially closed in 1965. In 1975, the church was given by the Village Council to the Museum Board for use in expanding the museum.
Housed in the Church building are military uniforms, silent film projectors and pioneer household displays. In the rear of the building are various household and farm - related artifacts.


Pioneer Kitchen Display

Shown are a table and chairs, a chest of drawers, a wash basin a butter churn, a stove, and a clothes washer.


Film Projectors

The Simplex film projectors were originally used in the Waskada Theatre, now the Community Centre. Dating back to the early 1920's, the projectors were operated by a hand crank and used acetylene gas for a light source.


Military Uniforms

This display case holds military uniforms, as well as a band uniform (mid-right in foreground) and a boy scout uniform (mid-right, behind the band uniform). The military uniforms include both men's and ladies' Army and Air Force uniforms from before and during WWII.

Vehicle Display Building

This building was officially opened on June 4th, 1999, during Waskada's Promotion Days. It provides room for vintage cars, trucks, tractors, steam engines, threshing equipment, various horse-drawn vehicles, and fire-fighting vehicles.


Vintage Cars

To the left is a 1973 Volkswagen Beetle. The two Model T Fords were restored by Jack Innes of Waskada.


Antique Tractor

A 1920 'Titan' tractor by the International Harvester Company.


Antique Truck

A 1926 Ford truck, restored by Jack Innes.
