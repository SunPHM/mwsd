Origami

My father introduced me to Origami -the japanese art of paper folding- when I was very young. I think it was mainly because my parents really needed something to give them a break from a very talkative wee son. I could entertain myself with square paper for hours.

My favourite fold is a man and woman dancing (accredited to Neal Elias - USA). It uses one piece of square paper which is white on one side and coloured on the other.

I've created one fold myself sofar. It's a two-colour standing dragon folded from one square.(image1, image2) I created the first version in 1998. I thank my school friend Inga Johnsen who pointed out that the bat I was trying to create looked more like a dragon when help upside down. The final version was born in 2003 when I reviseted the model and re-did the base to give me two disconnected arms and legs.

For the illostrated model I used hand-made "fluffy" paper with kitchen foil glued to one side. I hope to be able to publish the diagrams in near future.

My favourite Origami Website is Joseph Wu's Origami Page as well as the online copy of the first ever Origami book for amusement Hidden Senbazuru Orikata. I was very pleased with myself when I managed to figure out the fold of the crane with many cranes piled on his back described in Hidden Senbazuru Orikata.
