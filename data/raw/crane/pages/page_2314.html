Plant life and Wildlife

Plant live

The differences in climate, soil, and elevation create various growing conditions. This is why Texas has so many types of trees, grasses, flowers, and other forms of vegetation. These conditions also affect the way people live.

Wildflowers, brush, trees, bushes, native grasses, and other wild plants are types of natural vegetation in Texas. They grow without any help of the natives. These plants have many important jobs such as preventing erosion, proving food for animals, and making the country beautiful.

Along the humid and tropical Gulf coast of South Texas, palm trees and blossoming flowers decorate the landscape. Grasses cover the dry plains of South Texas. Mesquite trees complete for space with cacti and shrubs. In Central Texas, the pecan tree lives besides rivers and in neighborhood yards and parks. The pecan tree is the states flower. Far West Texas is so dry that only the plants requiring small amounts of water can survive. Some examples of these are cacti, grasses, and yucca dot.

Animals 

Texas is home to a broad array of wild animals. The whooping crane and the armadillo are two famous animals Texas is known for. Three or four million deer roam through Texas. The state also has thousands of prong horn antelope. Alligators are found in the wetlands and mountain lions, bobcats, and coyotes also live in Texas.

Since 1973 Texas has legally protected some types of animals to help with ensuring that that type of animal will not disappear. These species are called endangered. Threatened species are those that are likely to become endangered in the future. An example, is the horned lizards. The horned lizard used to live almost everywhere in Texas, but they have disappeared from East Texas and South Texas. Two out of three of the species of horned lizards are considered threatened.
