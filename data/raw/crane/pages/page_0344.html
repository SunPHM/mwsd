Creeping Crane

Introduction

It is probably common for fans of LEGO Technic to make a large crane. My crane is inspired from Leo Dorst's crane with a remotely operated bucket. I've also take inspiration from an old Technic idea book.

My crane has four remotely operated motors. Two 1997 style motors govern the wires. One motor controls the up/down movement and the other the open/close of the gripper. These motors reside in the upper part of the crane, next to the large wheels used as counter weights. A Leo Dorst style adder/subtractor was used.

The remotely operated grabber works really well. I can use it to pick up a small orange, by using the control panel only! This is way fun!

One old style motor controls the car which runs back and forth on the crane boom. The motor is geared down 3:1 and then 24:1 before the power is passed to the 16t cog which drives one long run of Technic chain links.

The other old style motor powers the rotation of the crane. The motor is located on the crane base, and one single shaft runs all the way up to the rotation point: Just below the boom.

I have made a diagram for the crane base and tower. It would have been easy to make the crane taller, but I didn't consider that an interesting challenge.
