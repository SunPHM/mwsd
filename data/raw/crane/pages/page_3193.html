About the book:
This book teaches how to fold up to 100 cranes out of a single sheet of special paper, called washi paper. The secret lies in a pattern of cuts, used with the folds, to create mystifying and charming groups of connected cranes, captured in mid-flight. The colorful and textured washi paper adds beauty to each completed figure. Masako Sakai and her daughter, Michie Sahara, rediscovered the 200 year old Rokoan method and explain it for modern readers to enjoy in this beautifully illustrated edition.

Twenty-six models are presented in full color photographs, with step-by-step diagrams showing how to create each one. Each book also includes a sheet of special washi paper, making it a ready-to-use origami kit. Here is the "next step" for origami enthusiasts of all ages.
