01/27/2006

Crane Snaps In Aberdeen



The world may look a little different to the crane operator at a construction site in Aberdeen tonight. The machine was lifting a slab of cement when it snapped and came within inches of crushing the cab he was sitting in.

It happened downtown, around 2:30 Friday afternoon. Authorities aren't sure why the crane snapped but they know that they're lucky no one was hurt in the accident.

Workers from Gil Haugan Construction of Sioux Falls were lifting a 50,000 pound cement slab as while working on a parking ramp. That's when the crane, that's supposed to be able to hold 142 tons, snapped.

Eyewitness Cassandra Loebs says, "It was so loud it was just like this great big boom and everything just came crashing down at once. The crane broke in half. It was amazing."

But it was also amazing that the operator of the crane escaped without injury after getting trapped in his cab.

Aberdeen Fire Department Shift Commander Terry Ellingson says, "We found the crane operator pinned in the cab itself and we took the door off the cab and used a saw to cut some of the pieces of the cab to extricate the gentleman from the cab and get him out."

No one else was hurt an the street was closed for the construction on the ramp.

Ellingson says, "When they've been moving these blocks they've told me they shut the street down just for safety purposes to keep people away."

Officials are investigating the cause of the accident. So the area around the scene is going to be closed until that work is done.
