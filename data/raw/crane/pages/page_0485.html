"Crus grus - A Common Crane "

Length 114-130cm, Wing Span 200-230cm Big and, stately, on ground moves with slow, markedly dignified steps. Huge in flight with long wings of uniform width, narrow extended neck (cf heron) and projecting legs. Migrating often noticed by their very far-carrying calls consisting of birds often noticed by the nasal and grating trumpet blasts. At nest site the pair often calls in duet, 'krruee-krro krruee-krro', most actively at dawn.

Very shy at nest site. In spring performs a dance in which h the birds leaps up with raised wings, make deep bows, trumpet and erect the 'tail plumes' (formed by elongated inner secondaries).

Breeds sparsely on open or semi open bog or marshland. Migrates in families in groups and larger flocks, which fly in a V or diagonal line. Stops off on open farmland, heaths and marshes, Feeds on insects, smaller animals, young of birds, seeds and fruits. Rare on passage in Britain, mostly Mar-Jun and Aug-Nov, has wintered.
