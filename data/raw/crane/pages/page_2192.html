Oswald The Ostrich

As Josh passed the last school building on his way home, a hand reached out and grabbed him.  He had been dragged behind the hedge before he could get a look at who his attacker was.  It was Randy Mason.

Josh might have been scared, but when he got a look at Randy's face, he realized there was no need.  He looked more troubled than threatening.

"What's the matter?" Josh asked.

"I've got to talk to you!"  Randy said, "You've got to explain some things to me."

"Why didn't you just come to me and ask?  Why drag me behind this hedge like some cops and robbers TV show?"

Randy looked embarrassed, "Well, I suppose I didn't want anyone to laugh at me.  If they knew I was talking to you about this Jesus stuff, they'd think I was crazy!"

"Like you think I'm crazy for believing in Jesus?"

"Yeah, I guess so."

"So, what is it you want to know?"

"I'm not sure I believe all this talk of sin and all.  But the story you told about the bird fighting with itself, got me thinking.  I guess you're right.  I have been fighting with myself.  Inside, I'd really like to be a good guy, but bad stuff keeps wanting to come out of me."

There was a rockery nearby.  Josh took hold of Randy's arm and guided him to a large rock, where they could sit and talk.  "Sin is scary stuff, Randy.  The Bible says that the pay we get for sinning, is death."

Randy held his hands up, as if pushing the words way, "Whoa, now.  I'm not going to believe that nonsense.  All this talk of sin and punishment is a load of rubbish!"

"You were the one who asked me to explain things.  So don't get upset when I give you an answer.  I any case, you remind me of a story I heard once."

"Another story?"

"Yes, but this one is a pretend story.  Not like the one I told you about the bird.  That one really happened."

"So," Randy let out a breath, "tell me this pretend story."

Josh leaned back against the rock, his hands clasped behind his head, "Its about an Ostrich named Oswald, though his friends called him Ozzie.  He lived in a zoo near a big city.  Life wasn't too bad there.  Ozzie quite liked it.  Though it was fenced in, there was a big space for him to run in, and he always got enough food.  He had also become friendly with the zoo keeper.

"Then, one day a scary thing happened.  A tiger escaped from its enclosure.  It remained hidden by day, but roamed about the open spaces of the zoo at night.  It had already killed a few of the other animals by the time Ozzie heard about it.

"‘You'd better be careful,' a Whooping Crane told him.  ‘Stay close to the other animals at night.  The tiger likes to attack those who are standing alone.'

"‘I'm not worried,' Ozzie replied.  ‘Even if others are in danger, I know how to deal with it.  Nothing bad can happen to me.  I like it out in the open spaces.'

"‘So what are you going to do?'

"‘I'm going to hide my head in the sand.'

"'How is that going to protect you from the tiger?'

"‘Well, if I can't see the tiger, then I can just pretend it isn't there.'"

Randy sat up straight and glared at Josh, "You think I'm stupid or something?  Nobody would be as silly as to think danger wasn't there, just because he couldn't see it!"

"Oh," Josh said, "I think you're being just as silly as Ozzie the Ostrich.  I've told you about sin.  And sin is much more dangerous than a tiger.  You think by pretending there is no such thing as sin and punishment, that it isn't really there.  You are just like the Ostrich, hiding his head in the sand."

Randy hung his head, "What must I do?"

"Stop pretending sin can't harm you.  Go to Jesus, the only One who can save you from sin and death.  He is the only One who can make you into a real child of God."

QUESTIONS:

1.  Why do you think Josh thought Randy was like the Ostrich who hid his head in the sand?

2.  If we pretend something is not true, does that make it untrue?

3.  Explain how the Jews, who listened to Paul's message about Jesus, were like Ozzie the Ostrich.

4.  Are you willing to believe the message about Jesus, so He can forgive your sin and make you into a child of God?

5.  If you are already a child of God, are you willing warn others about the judgment to come?

A PROMISE TO BELIEVE:

If you hide your sins, you will not succeed.  If you confess and reject them, you will receive mercy. 

(Proverbs 28:13 - International Children's Bible - New Century Version)
