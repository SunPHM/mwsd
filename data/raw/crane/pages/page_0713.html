  One foggy day, we took a trip to the Imperial Palace, not knowing that in order to be allowed to see
  most of it, you need to apply for Imperial permission in advance. However, anyone can see the grounds.

  There are many old buildings around, most of which are of undiscernable function. We
  saw little boy playing in the water near a building we weren't allowed to enter. Your guess
  as to his identity is as good as mine. The trees in the area were mostly quite stunning. There
  were a LOT of gardeners out working on them. The palace, if you are wondering, is still home to Japan's
  Imperial family today, which is why most of it remains off-limits without special permission. Even the
  benches in the area were old and fairly ornate. There was also a nice bridge
  that connects the main area to a small back area, but which is nonetheless very pretty. Hereis a
  shot of the bridge as viewed from one of the sides.

  The palace is also home to a garden which is probably a lot prettier if you aren't visiting it during an
  off-season for flora. I personally think that Japanese maples are some of the prettiest trees in the world, and these
  are certainly no exception. As you can tell, fall was beginning to set in (we went in October!). I like that picture a lot. There
  were also trees at the lake the bridge ran over. A close-up of some of the flowering trees
  you could see in the previous shot. There was also a single small island out in the lake, with a small tree living on it.

  When we did manage to get inside one of the palace buildings, which was the only one sitting on this lake, I got another shot of
  the trees and lake, this time from above. Also, on the lakeshore near where the building was, a rather sizeable amount
  of ivy was growing off of what we were told used to be a little dock. This is a closer shot of the ivy
  tendrils hanging down into the water near the shore. A little ways off, a crane flew down for a little while. I hear they are a
  pretty rare site, even in the palace, where they are kept as pets. I know this one looks fake, but we did see it land and leave, so
  we're pretty certain that unless it was an advanced water-proof robot, it was real.

  And finally, as we left, I got a picture of one of the gates leading in that has not yet been restored and is currently closed.
