Trident Steel warehouse in Saldanha

May 2004

Attention to detail and adherence to stringent quality control procedures, both during fabrication and erection, resulted in the successful completion of the Trident Steel warehouse in Saldanha.

The large warehouse, built to accommodate Trident Steel’s coil slitting facility at Saldanha, has a length of 159 metres, width of 128 metres and a height at eaves of 13.3 metres. A total of 1126 t of steel was used in the fabrication of this warehouse structure.

Hein Buys, contracts manager at Cosira International, says that the structure houses four separate crane lines which carry two 40 t and two 30 t cranes. The crane girders were fabricated and welded automatically using submerged arc welding technology.

“By using this particular welding process we can ensure the structural integrity of these girders, and this is obviously of importance when it comes to the fabrication of crane lines such as these,” Buys says.

In addition to ensuring the structural integrity of these girders, the fabrication process used by Cosira resulted in the tolerance of 5 mm being achieved on the crane rails which are fitted to the crane girders. Buys says that the close tolerance achieved was independently verified by Demag Germany who used a specialised machine to inspect the crane lines.

Logistics also played an important role on this project as the fabricated steel had to be taken via road from Cosira’s Heriotdale facility to Saldanha, a distance of 1575 km. The company makes use of internal quality management systems which assisted in ensuring that all aspects of the project ran according to the pre-planned erection schedule.

The building was designed by Pretoria based EDS Engineering and the project consultants were Capex Projects. The warehouse was completed at the end of November 2003.

Cosira International is the largest privately owned structural steel fabricating company, and operates three facilities in Heriotdale. The company is ISO 9001:2000 accredited, and has completed work for most of the major mining houses and industrial property developers.
