Braunston

Braunston Locks and Lock Shop

Braunston Marina was originally developed at the turn of the 19th century as the waterways depot at the northern end of the Grand Junction Canal. There are several buildings of which date from the Georgian and Victorian periods. At the entrance to the marina is a very fine Horsley Iron Works cast iron bridge made in Tipton and dates from 1834. This was erected by Thomas Telford.

The Marina is one of the largest in country and is the home of Merlin Narrowboats and many other canal businesses. Braunston Marina Ltd operate the whole site and offer moorings, chandlery, gas, diesel, pump out, washing/drying machines and dry docking.

Braunston offers a full range of services which include the lock shop right beside the bottom lock. This shop seems to have some of everything including food, maps and ice cream. There are also some very nice canal related paintings and prints on sale. In the main village there area selection of pubs, a butcher's, a bakers and even a small supermarket.

The local British Waterways office is in the stop house beside the old stop lock. The area manager operates from here. The stop house houses the offices as well as providing an excellent information service.

The village of Braunston is very picturesque and warrants a visit. There are several public right of ways leading from the canal side up into the village. Some of these pass through fields containing livestock so care should be taken. The village has three pubs and many useful shops including a Butchers, a real Bakers and a small supermarket. All Saints Church - Braunston

There has been a church in Braunston since Norman times. However, the only surviving part of the first church on the site is the restored Norman font with worn rope-moulding around the top.

A second church was built in the Decorated style of the fourteenth century and served the parish for nearly five hundred years before it was demolished in 1848. The original material was used in building the present church, the third on this site.

The church now standing is substantially the early Victorian building erected in 1848-9 as a result of the efforts of the Rector, the Rev. A. B. Clough. The design was the work of Mr. R. C. Hussey, a Birmingham architect. He used a golden- brown Duston stone around the base of the new church. contrasting with the deep red sandstone. Read on or visit the All Saint's Church Website
