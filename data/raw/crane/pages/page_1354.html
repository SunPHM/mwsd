Their super portability means you can transport them easily. Humpback (tm) trailer pumps offer the longest reach. Short Hitch trailer pumps are perfect for those tight spots, at just 15' overall length. Three Point Hitch trailer pumps handle the steepest banks (pump at a 60 degree angle while keeping your tractor on the level). All trailer pumps are available in Regular Lift, Intermediate Lift and High Lift to match your pump site requirements.

Slurry Pumps

Crisafulli Slurry Pumps agitate, mix and pump the most viscous sludge from industrial, municipal and agricultural waste lagoons. The high-capacity units can effectively slurry and clean ponds as large as 200'x200'x8' in as little as six hours. Our sludge and slurry system incorporates a "slurry gate." Solids and liquids are drawn into the pump, blended and injected through the slurry gate into unmixed material. Then, close the gate to pump the material to a receiving tank, receiving vehicle or another lagoon. You don't need separate agitator or mixer pumps.

Pump Selection Criteria

Standard Duty - Intended use is pumping clear liquids of neutral pH containing very minimal solids.

Industrial Duty - Intended use is pumping light, non-abrasive slurries of neutral pH containing occasional small quantities of solids.

Rugged Duty - Intended use is pumping moderately abrasive slurries of near neutral pH containing solids up to 1/2" diameter.

Severe Duty - Intended use is pumping moderately abrasive slurries of near neutral pH containing solids larger than 1/2" (up to maximum diameter for pump size).

Diamond Duty - Intended use is pumping highly abrasive slurries of near neutral pH containing solids less than 1/2" in diameter.

Series Descriptions

L-Series - Customers choose the L-Series when they need the highest flows. 12" and 16" are the most popular sizes. L-Series pumps turn at a gentle 540 RPM for longer pump life.

T-Series - Designed for use with today's high-tech tractors, customers choose the T-Series when their power take-off turns at 1,000 RPM. Flows are somewhat lower than the L-Series.

S-Series - When a customer wants a direct-driven pump, needs a moderate amount of flow, and also needs solids-passing capability,the economical S-Series is normally chosen.

J-Series - This pump design is most attractive to customers who need high head capability and the highest efficiency range. For dredging and high solids pumping applications, the severe duty J-Series is our most popular.

Closed vs. Open Impellers

Closed Impellers - Utilize shrouds, commonly called "wear plates," on both edges of the impeller vanes. These shrouds reduce recirculation and increase efficiency. Commonly used for pumping clear liquids and light slurries containing very minimal solids.

Open Impellers - Do not utilize shrouds on the suction side of the impeller vanes. Commonly used for pumping slurries and other liquids containing stringy material.
