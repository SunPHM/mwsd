Seabees tackle two projects
Cranes and warehouses, wire ropes and ribbons--CB's use it all

Lt. j.g. Anant R. Patel, NMCB-4

Okinawa, Japan - The Seabees of Naval Mobile Construction Battalion (NMCB) FOUR’s Crane Shop recently had the chance to do something other than work with cranes. They were tasked with replacing two wire rope bridges at the Jungle Warfare Training Center (JWTC), located in the Northern Training Area on Okinawa. The job was important to JWTC because the existing bridges had deteriorated over the years and were no longer safe to use.

The crew had to overcome several difficult challenges during the project along with ensuring the safety of its members. The largest challenge was the sheer size of the ravine. Using rigging skills they learned while dealing with cranes, the crew came up with unique ways to span the 210-foot wide, 100-foot deep ravine with only three-quarter inch wire rope.

In spite of a typhoon, which threatened to remove everything previously installed, the six-man crew, directed by Equipment Operator 1st Class Scot J. Gallop and lead by EO2 Brian D. Sypher, completed the project on time. With the bridges now rebuilt and safe to use, other members of NMCB FOUR are preparing to visit the JWTC. They won’t be working on construction projects though, they’ll be attending the Jungle Warfare Training Course and trying out the new bridges for themselves.

In another project the crew of the Warehouse Addition project held a ribbon cutting ceremony for the "opening" of the two additions for outside storage on October 29, 1998.  Capt. Frank Sweigart was assisted by Raul Malanyaon, the Assistant Warehouse Manager, and Construction Engineer 2nd Class Craig Quillen, the Project Supervisor, in cutting the ribbon.

The project was started by NMCB 74 in March and was turned over to NMCB 4 in June completion. NMCB 74 had already expended 428 mandays in site preparation for the concrete pads, placing the concrete pads, erecting the structural steel on the west side of the building and excavating the trench for the concrete retaining wall. In true Seabee fashion, NMCB 4 picked up the project and expended an additional 412 mandays with a crew of 6 personnel to erect the structural steel on the west side of the existing warehouse, place corrugated sheeting, and install a light system and personnel door on both additions. They also fabricated the steel reinforcing bar cage for the retaining wall, formed around the cage and placed 22 cubic meters of concrete. The additions give the Housing Office 3000 square feet of much needed covered storage.
