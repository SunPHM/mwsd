The Hundred Cranes

This model was created by Gido Rokoan, who wrote the book Zenbazuru Orikata which was published in 1797. It is an amazing piece of work which has been folded by only a few masters in Japan until recent years. It is made up of a single large sheet of paper, precreased into 96 tiny bird bases with a large crane in the centre, and then the paper is slit apart and folded so that the 97 cranes form a pattern. It is extremely time consuming to fold - it took a week of continuous folding to make the piece below!

Gillian Buchanan folded this particular example of the piece.
