USDA, University of Kentucky

    Submitted by Craig Locke, FEPP Coordinator and William O. Peterson, Accountable Property Officer (Page 2)

Display and storage shelf units received from the Recycling Control Point Program. These items are very useful for mail sorting or display of publications in the County Cooperative Extension Service offices.

Crane, 7.5 ton capacity, in use at the Agricultural Experiment Station - Lexington. This size crane is extremely useful and versatile. It's 15,000 lb capacity allows it to complete many smaller lifting jobs. It has a speed of about 30 mph which makes it very mobile. With attachment of bucket on end, it can also be used for construction, painting, etc.

Farm wagons received from Department of Energy and at use at the Agriculture Experiment Station Lexington. These wagons are currently used for moving irrigation line; irrigation hose also received from FEP.
 

This vehicle was formerly a tandem axle Mack tanker and received from DRMO inventory.  Machinists and mechanics retrofitted and painted this vehicle to make for a heavy duty tandem axle dump truck.  The dump bed itself was also received as DRMS excess.
 

Pair of D7 dozers in use for development and construction of Animal Research Center - Lexington.

Marmon tandem axle road tractor in use at the Agricultural Experiment Station Lexington. Item was received from Department of Energy and has served in excess of seven years. Truck was painted by farm personnel and represents 6-digit freight savings by allowing internal transportation work.
 

GMC utility truck recently painted and in use at the AES Lexington. The bucket boom was removed from another FEP bucket truck which became mechanically deteriorated after 15 years of post-transfer service. The boom unit was removed with its utility body, rebuilt by Hi-Ranger, reinstalled on new FEP truck and entire unit painted.
 

Small storage building built from kits received from DRMS. In use for Horticulture research.
 

New floor model centrifuge received from the Recycling Control Point Program and in use at the Animal Research Center, Lexington.
 

Athey 4WD all-terrain forklift in use at Horticulture areas, Lexington. A tree spade is also on hand and retrofitted to fit on this machine and allows for digging of trees.

Systems furniture received from Department of Housing and Urban Development and installed into new Animal Research Center - Lexington.
 

Case W24 front end loader received from DRMS and in use at the AES Lexington. This size loader is perfect for many agricultural related jobs.

MRS earthmover in use at the AES Lexington for development and jobsite preparation. This unit has been an instrumental part of a $1.2 million savings project in the development of the Animal Research Center.
 

Drop deck 50' trailer in use for trucking purposes at the AES Lexington. Unit was received from DRMS inventory. The machinist constructed the front-board and platform over the kingpin.  Farm personnel installed new wheels and tires and painted trailer.
 

International service truck in use at the AES Lexington by heavy equipment crew. Truck was received from DRMS inventory. Bed was installed and entire unit painted by farm personnel. Generator, air compressor, tools, and other equipment on the rear of unit were also received from FEP.
 

Commercial upright refrigerator received from RCP program and in use at Animal Research Center.

\

Monarch lathe in use at the Biosystems and Agricultural Engineering Machinery Lab. Received from DRMS inventory.
 

John Deere model 401B tractor in use for Horticulture research. Unit was painted and has served post-transfer programs for 5 years.
