    Bighorn sheep or Dall Sheep can be found in the Grand Canyon of Yellowstone area and the Grand Tetons. Both male and female sheep have the curved horns and at maturity can be 3 feet wide. Dall Sheep are white and there are desert Bighorns that are brown with a lighter color on their rump. During rut or mating seasons the males will battle over the females by charging each other at full speed and butt heads, it is said the noise from the collision can be heard a mile away. That gives me a headache just thinking about it.

This is a pronghorn ( aka antelope) and both sexes also have horns. Males stand about 3 feet or more at the shoulder and weigh 100-140 pounds. females are smaller and weigh 100 pounds. They can trot along at 30 miles per hour but can burst into a run and reach 70 miles per hour. The pronghorn is not related to the an antelope or goat, but is in fact the last species of its own in the mammal family. This animal has made a come back unrestricted hunting in the early 1900's. Their population went to only 200,000, but now there are more than a half a million. You can see pronghorns in the sagebrush areas in lower elevations.

This gorgeous bird is a Trumpeter Swan and they are the largest waterfowl in North America. They can get up to 6ft. in length and a wingspan of 8ft. They are an endangered bird and the biologist are having a hard time trying to figure out why most of the babies don't make it in Yellowstone.  There are only 70 of them in Yellowstone. Hayden Valley is the best place to find these birds.

This was an odd sight for somebody who just came from Florida. A crane here?? This is a Sandhill Crane and it can be found in the prairies, field and marshes of the Grand Tetons. This bird is said to be able to fly so high, a person can't see it from the ground. Cranes in Florida love fields and marshes too.

Here is another bird that just didn't seem to fit in. I thought maybe he and his buddy crane hoped on the plane with us when we left. But, this is a Spotted sandpiper and he acts and looks just like the ones down here in Florida. They can be found near lakes and rivers. What gets me is there are also white pelicans in Yellowstone and Tetons.

There are also Osprey (which are common in Florida), Bald Eagles, Mallards, Raven, Mountain Bluebirds, Magpies, Gray Jays, and Clark's Nutcrackers.

  A quote from Naturalist Henry Veston about humans and how we see animals.

"We need a wiser and more mystical concept of animals. We patronize them for their incompleteness, for the tragic fate of having formed so far below ourselves and there is our error and greatly error in a world more older and more complete then ourselves. They move finished and complete, gifted with extensions of the senses we have lost or never attained, living by voices we shall never hear. They are not brotherlin, they are not underlings, they are other nations caught with ourselves in a nest of life and time. Fellow prisoners of the splendor and travail of the earth."
