Text of article in the Gold Coast Bulletin,
Monday 8 April 2002

Count the construction cranes along the coastline
Boom times back in town

by Peter Gleeson
chief reporter

THE Gold Coast's property construction industry is riding the crest of a wave not seen since 1990.
   The boom is being fuelled by wealthy couples who have traditionally lived in suburban homes switching to unit-style living along the coastal strip.
   Property industry figure Jim Raptis said the beauty with the latest wave of construction was that the established home market was as good as it had ever been.
   "It's 'all systems go' with an eye on the future," he said.
   The Gold Coast's skyline is once again dotted with cranes from Labrador to Kirra.
   The 'crane' rating, used so often by former premier Sir Joh Bjelke-Petersen to promote Queensland's booming economy, shows 11 projects under way.
   Real estate identity Max Christmas said even though there were 11 cranes on the skyline, in reality it was much more.
   "These 65-storey and 85-storey towers of today make up for three cranes," he said.
   Analysts say the buoyant construction phase will continue for at least another three years.
   PRD Nationwide joint managing director Gordon Douglas said the Gold Coast was experiencing its best market conditions in 12 years.
   "We didn't really get the upbeat Sydney-Melbourne-Brisbane cycle, so effectively we are catching up," he said.
   "It's an extremely healthy market. A lot of it is local content.
   "You're talking about people from Robina and Ashmore buying units in places like Labrador because that style of living is now more appealing.
   "Labrador, Main Beach and Broadbeach are very popular areas."
   Mr Douglas said Gold Coasters would see 'a lot more cranes in the next few years'.
   "There's a lot of activity happening and it doesn't look like stopping," he said.
   Projects under way at present include:
Deepwater Point apartments, Labrador (77 units).

  • Chevron Renaissance Skyline tower in Surfers Paradise (178 apartments).
  • Platinum on the Beach at Surfers Paradise (14).
  • Aria at Broadbeach (83).
  • Savannah at Broadbeach (90).
  • Indigo Blue at Burleigh Heads (44).
  • WorldMark at Kirra beach (105).

   With much of Main Beach developed, developers are concentrating on Surfers Paradise.
   The world's tallest residential apartment block is to be built by Soheil Abedian's Sunland Group at the southern end of Surfers Paradise.
   Plans are under way to develop a residential tower block on the former Jimna site.
   In the halcyon days of 1979-80, the Gold Coast's skyline was dotted with up to 60 cranes at any one time.
   The latest sparkling news for the property industry comes on top of Australian Bureau of Statistics figures showing high-rise approvals have soared.
   ABS figures show that approvals for apartments, units and townhouses on the Gold Coast have skyrocketed and that general housing approvals are well up, bucking the national trend.
