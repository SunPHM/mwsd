Jims Big Home Standby Generator

 

Well a good friend of mine sent me these pictures of his generator installation. You have to know Jim, he doesn't do anything half way. This is probably the biggest home standby system I have seen yet. But when you live on a farm, you think bigger I guess.

Manufactured by Catapillar. The "Olympian" D25P1S is a 25 Kw diesel genset. It's a little noisy even though it's a 100' or so
back from the house. But as Jim says, "It will be a comforting sound in the dead of winter when the power goes out."

This bad boy had to be lowered onto it's concrete slab with a small crane, about 100' from the back of the house. Unfortunately the trench they cut to bury the cables severed a bunch of geo-thermo lines in the back yard. (No extra charge).

 

 

A 1800 RPM water cooled 3 cylinder Perkins diesel engine.


 

This unit will return full backup power in under 4 seconds of detected power loss. Auto-transfer switch has an auto-maintenance
30 minute run every week. There is a button on the transfer switch you push, and the genset will cycle up and run for 30 minutes. It will repeat this cycle again at the exact same time the following week. When the house load is transfered over to this unit, you can't detect the load on the motor at all.

 

This standby system has already provided backup power several times since installed in February, '99.

This very sophisticated setup requires only 4 things, the owner, his dog, his wife (who took the picture and is much better looking than Jim), and the generator.
