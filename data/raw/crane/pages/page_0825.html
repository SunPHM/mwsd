VEGAS 'APPARITION' DIARY

VEGAS 'APPARITION' diary

Today I'm resting after an amazing day in Las Vegas. My "apparition" did happen, and it was a long day because there was much rehearsal needed technically for this one-off (actually 2-off!) piece of showbiz..... The actual few minutes I was on stage were an amazing blast .... I'm not even going to attmpt to describe them ...for once I'm going to let someone else describe the event ..... Our POP in Vegas is sensational. He alerted us to the fact that it's not necessary to be old to play Pop in We Will Rock YOu. His interpretation is so innovative and alive that it changed our perceptions of what Pop could be. He has to be seen to be believed. It's a whole-body performance, and not a single laugh is not delivered with maximum skill and feel and tender loving care. Doug wrote to me and I'm proud and humbled (if you know what I mean) to share this with you ...

Douglas Crawford as Pop - WWRY Las Vegas 2004 - photo: Bri


Subject: A Triumphant Return to Vegas!

Christmas came early for me this year...and it wasn't marked by the arrival of St. Nick, but, rather, by the return of Brian May to the Paris, Las Vegas (the North American home of WWRY)!  It has been and continues to be my great pleasure to play the role of "Pop" in WWRY, here, in the States. Long before I ever set foot on a stage, myself, I was a fan of Queen. I grew up with their music and often dreamt as a child about singing their songs in front of a frenzied capacity crowd You can imagine, then, how thrilled I was to be cast as a member of the North American Premiere Company of WWRY last summer! Throughout the final auditions and into rehearsals, I was amazed to discover how incredibly supportive and caring and kind, both, Brian and Roger were to me and each and every member of our cast, band, and crew. Their passion for the show and generosity of spirit shone through every minute they spent with us. I could not believe how blessed I was to be working alongside these two true heroes of mine. We opened to overwhelming reactions and I watched in awe as Brian and Roger played (with our unbelievable band and a who's who of rock legends) at our opening night party! Now, three months later, when I didn't believe it could get any better, I was able to experience what I would venture to say was one of, if not THE, most electric nights any of us in the company had ever been a part of!

You see, just hours ago, Brian made a guest appearance, here, in WWRY  - joining us onstage for BO RAP and leading the company in a special encore of THE SHOW MUST GO ON! For two shows tonight I had the opportunity to live out those childhood dreams - singing two Queen classics while Brian played just inches away! As I stepped forward to sing my solo lines "Inside my heart is breaking, my make-up may be flaking, but my smile still stays on", I was overtaken by a rush of emotions I'm still unable to describe.

I write this, not to sound like "oh, isn't my life so great" (and, again, I'm very blessed), but, rather, to share with everyone how truly phenomenal this man is - as an artist, and, more importantly, as a human being. Brian is a STAR to be sure, but you would never know it speaking with him. The ego and pretentiousness so common among today's "celebrities" are no where to be found in him. When talking with him, he speaks to you with a quiet assuredness  - a gentle open-ness - that let's you know he's listening and truly cares about that which he speaks. And to be onstage with him while he plays...my god...I only wish every fan could experience that! Brian is, perhaps, the most unselfish artist I have ever seen or known. He willingly shared the stage with us despite the fact that, after he made his entrance (very theatrical, by the way - rising up on a lift from beneath the stage amidst a cloud of smoke), everyone present was aware he had taken the evening to a whole new level!

I don't know if any of this I'm writing is doing the night justice, or even making a whole lot of sense, for that matter, but, most importantly, I just wanted to say "thank you", Brian, on behalf of the entire company, here, in Vegas for being you and giving all of us on-stage, back-stage, and in the house a chance to live out a dream. You reminded us all how lucky we are to be a small part of Queen's legendary history and provided us with a holiday season none of us are ever likely to forget! Continued success with WWRY world-wide and all the best to you and Roger for what will surely be an incredible tour next year! There's a reason why you are so loved/respected/admired by so many millions and I can't imagine anyone being more deserving!

For all the happiness you've given and continue to give, I thank you!!

Dreams do come true.
 
with great respect,
your friend & fan,
Douglas Crawford - "Pop" (Las Vegas)
 
My deepest thanks Doug, and much love to all my dear friends in the stupendous Vegas Company ....... I miss you already

THANK GOD IT'S CHRISTMAs

 

THANK GOD IT'S CHRISTMAS......

I too am very fond of this track ... I'm glad it's getting a good airing on the Christmas compilations this year. [SEE QUEEN NEWS]

The story is simple. We thought it would be nice to make a Christmas record, which would not be on any Album. Of course you have to have these thoughts in the late summer, because it takes so long to turn records around as a rule (even "Do they Know it's Christmas" needed some lead time, and this was powered through in advance).

This is years ago, of course. Roger and I agreed we would both submit ideas, and work together on working up the idea we chose. Roger wrote the beginnings of "Thank God It's Christmas", and I wrote the beginnings of "I Dream of Christmas". We met down at Roger's place and demo'd both tracks together, as I remember. How the choice between the two tracks was made I don't clearly remember, but I think it was a whole group thing. We decided to finish TGIC for the Queen Christmas single, and "IDOC" was put to one side, and I immediately worked it into the version which we released as my Lady Anita's Christmas record. This record charted briefly but is now a rarity (it features Anita singing, along with a school choir of young kids, and quite a lot of Guitar soloing by me!)

For the eventual Queen song, Roger had the verse form already: "Oh - my love - we live in troubled days ....". I contributed the chorus, with a key-change leading into it, using a lyric line which occurred casually in the verse, so this now became the title, "Thank God It's Christmas". Of course when Freddie took up the singing it jumped into another gear ... his lovely high register soaring above the background in these choruses, then returning to a very gentle breathy texture for the verses. The background was based on a synth pad which Roger had found (he would have to tell you what it was made with), to which I added a guitar texture very un-typical of me ... very clean and bright ... the amp turned way down and the strings hit hard like bells. This makes that Bright Frosty sound which I actually like a lot, contrasting with the warm and dark synth chords. Of course as usual John's subtle firm bass lines are a big part of the style of the eventual outcome. The drums are programmed, the way Roger originally demo'd them, an unusual choice for a Queen track but it works in this case. My favourite part is the end, where there's a small building (subdominant) link into the playout. Freddie is improvising, and it all becomes totally joyful ... trekking off into the distance across the snowy fields..... noses red, scarves wrapped around chins, snowballs being thrown .... Christmas packages of good food being delivered to hungry villagers' doors ... mulled wine being swigged to keep the blood flowing to those cold extremities....

ho ho ho!

Festive Cheers !
Bri

**Sat 11 Dec 04**
QUEEN TOUR NEWS

A watershed moment ?  !    Backstage at the Hall of Fame Awards - Bri, Paul Rodgers, Roger.
Photo © Bri

Well, the news seems to be out .... Roger spilled the beans on his interview with the Wettendass Web-page gal .... so I endorsed it.

And this time it's not just a rumour. We are in the process of looking at venues for a QUEEN tour in the spring. It really all came about because of the Fender 50th Anniversary gig that I did with Paul Rodgers. We were both so amazed at the chemistry that was going on in All Right Now, that suddenly it seems blindingly obvious that there was "something happening here". And if we were to play together some more, which we both thought was an exciting possibility, who would be the drummer ??? Well, the expression "No Brainer" comes to mind. Then the UK Hall of Fame awards just came up out of the blue. Apparently we had won one of the categories and the request was there for us to play. I had already had the conversation with Roger at this point, about how brilliant Paul Rodgers was, and, since, very conveniently, he had also been asked to be there, we agreed we would ask Paul if he would team up with us for this event.

The rest in a way is already History. The show went so incredibly well from our point of view, and we got so many rave reactions from out there, we decided almost then and there that we would look at a tour together.

This is what we have been doing over the last two or three weeks, looking at venues, discussing how we might approach our collective material, etc. etc.

It's very exciting .... ever since the run-up to that Awards show, I felt in my waters that something big and scary was happening. Scary because as soon as you press the button for something like this you set a massive juggernaut into motion which cannot be stopped ! So bang goes the home life, and anything else we might have planned for the coming months. Suddenly the QUEEN Phoenix is rising again from the ashes, and will take precedence over everything in our lives. So be it. So there ya go. I won't go into more details right now, but, barring accidents, QUEEN and PAUL RODGERS will be on the road, pretty much for sure, around April 2005.

See ya there !

Love

Bri

COLOGNE PRESS NIGHT

Funny, the scurvy subject of journalists comes up again and again. We had a press night here in Cologne, prior to the official opening night on Sunday, in the MUSIC DOME, on the banks of the Mighty Rhine, in Cologne. It was a riot - a fabulous response, even on this, the one night when it was expected to be quiet. Just as "We are the Champions" finished to a totally standing and waving and deafeningly cheering audience, I caught a fat balding old Journo with his clip-board sneaking out the back to get his coat. "Didn't like it, then?" I said. He pretended not to understand. I repeated the question, and he shuffled his feet and mumbled, "NO". I said, "I hope you're going back in to review the end of the show, then, because if not, you're not really qualified to review it, are you? " No answer. I went back through the doors, back into the tumultuous sea of hands clapping along to the BOWS. And then the final song of the evening, Bohemian Rhapsody took the place by storm. They didn't sit down for at least 10 minutes in all. But that old fart missed it. I wonder which newspaper HE writes for. Even if I didn't like a show I would never be so rude as to walk out when the company, always a hard working and dedicated bunch of kids, were taking their bows. This guy took his (presumably free) ticket, and gave NOTHING back. Scum.

We don't need your review, Mr Old Fart. The German public have already spoken. The tickets for this, perhaps our most highly evolved show yet, are romping out the door.

Hurrah for Koln !! and where is the Umlaut on my G4 ?! (that's a laptop, by the way.) Koeln, then. We love ya!!

**Tue 07 Dec 04**
DEM FLOATERS

Dem Floaters !!

It's been a while but I didn't forget !! I've had lots of mail from you guys about these things which float in front of our eyes .... and I've been getting more and more conscious of them. I think people will be starting to think I'm crazy or afflicted with some kind of nervous tick, because in the middle of a conversation I find myself flicking my eyes off to a corner of the room to catch sight of one of these amoebic objects drifting across my field of vision. ! I have found that they endure for quite a long time in more or less the same shape ... they are not just creatures of one day .... but they do bend and twist and turn gradually over a period of weeks.... they seem to be quite soft, and occasionally I can see them flexing as they move across my line of vision ... I can see now that these noticeable Floaters are only the tip of the iceberg ... if I'm looking at a uniformly white background I can actually see fainter ones absolutely everywhere .. a whole Sargasso Sea of the buggers, covering my entire field of view !

The more conscious you become of these things, the more annoying they get. So maybe I am not setting a good example here. But they have now become so familiar to me, these blobs and squiggles, that I've started to give them names ... the Blob, the Sea-Horse, etc. (shown above) .. like constellations ! I eventually thought I should seek professional advice, so I did. I went to an eye specialist. And I'll tell you about that soon, if you're interested ... I learned a lot. But the main thing I learned was not to worry about it ! So, more on floaters soon ....

Cheers all, and may your eyes and your skies be clear !
Brian

**Sun 05 Dec 04**
STORY OF BO RHAP AND FREDDIE'S MILLIONS

STORY OF BO RHAP

I actually sat down and watched the Bo Rhap documentary last night (Saturday), without trying to do something else at the same time (this is rare for me - I don't like TV very much - even when it's US on - in fact, especially when it's US on!) I thought it was quite well done on the whole. Of course there are so many things which were NOT in there... and some of it seemed a bit ...is fatuous the word ??!!! Especially Mr. Grant's attempts to do a Richard Burton! And the "experts" trying to figure out what the song means! ha ha! Dismal ! But a good honest effort at telling a story on the whole, I thought. We helped them to make this, by going down to Rockfield, as you probably noticed, and our pal Roy T Baker spent quite a bit of time looking at the old multitrack with them .. but mainly they put it together themselves ... so it was not "official comment", I guess... maybe better for that.

If we had the time I'd l ike to do something like this ourselves, and REALLY tell the story .... but in a way it would still be only part of the truth, because the truth is that it only makes sense as part of a much bigger story - the story of the whole "A Night at the Opera" album and the events that led up to it. I could get a lot deeper into the actual parts of the music, too.... Roy is very much NOT into the musical structure of the recordings ... he's not that kind of a producer.... so what you saw in this area really only scratched the surface. So maybe one day we'll sit down ourselves with the multitracks ourselves and get into it ... but it would need to be the whole album, not just this track.

FREDDIE'S MILLIONS

I made the mistake of leaving the TV on for the next programme. My God, what a hideous, nasty piece of work. The idea of trying to make out that what was important about Freddie was his money is stupid enough to start with, but what an utterly useless piece of journalistic conceit the whole thing was from beginning to end. It was like watching a documentary on Ghandi based around how much shaving cream he used in his life. And the presenter .... I wonder if he was proud of this condescending and sloppy piece of work. A man with zero understanding of his subject matter, and apparently with an agenda of making Freddie look as small as possible. "Freddie fancied himself as an art collector and connoisseur" ??? You don't know the millionth part of it, young Mr. Know-It-All. And the constant implication that Freddie actually based his life around making money .... it's too ridiculous for words.... That's why we're all still talking about him .... he was free, like we'd all like to be. He did it out of his passion.... but where do begin ....? I can only hope this piece of utter crap, "Freddie's Millions", finds its way into a refuse dump, along with all known copies!!!

Happy Sunday, folks !!

Brian

Sorry, an explanation needed.


The Grand Order of Water Rats are an ancient and originaly secret brotherhood (well, they go back to the 1920's or so) of men, originally exclusively from the world of "Music Hall" - a very English form of entertainment which has now almost ceased to exist. This society was formed originally to care for its elder statesmen who were often without any means of support, but became supportive of many charities over the years, particularly to do with Children. The society has more recently become a little more diverse in its membership, which is by internal nomination and vote only. So now there are Rats from Sport, Pop and Rock, the acting profession, and occasionally from other walks of life.

Some years ago I became friendly with some of the musical Rats, really through Anita's involvement. Dec Clusky and Bert Weedon proposed me as a member and I was voted in ... at that time one of the first "rock" people to be admitted. It's something I'm proud of, and I try to do my bit for the Rats and their charitable works. \Other activities are regular meetings to tell jokes, have a drink and a chat, and various functions throughout the year, including Ladies Night, which is a large ball open to guests, and this is where I played last week. There is a corresponding and affiliated society for Ladies, known as the Lady Ratlings, and they also do great things for charity. There are still some aspects of the Rats Brotherhood which remain secret, including the induction ceremony. It's good to know there's still a little mystery in the world !

Cheers

NEW VERSION OF TEO TORRIATTE FOR JEWELS 2

New version of TEO TORRIATTE for Jewels 2

I can assure you that whatever our new mix may be, it certainly wasn't "easy" !

Actually hundreds of hours have gone into rescuing every morsel of the original recordings in order to be able to attempt the new version. I have contributed a little to the work, and there are some areas which only I can answer the relevant questions, since I was there all those years ago, trying to make this then new thought into a 'proper record'. But mainly the credit is due to Kris Fredriksson and Justin Shirley-Smith. These guys are so dedicated to the excellence of these projects, that they work much longer hours than anyone could ask, and treat every part of every Queen note ever recorded as a priceless gem. I too love the original mix, and it will always be there. But I have always known it suffered from the limitations of pushing the existing equipment past its limit. This new mix will be only very subtly different - I hope nobody expects modern "beats" or dance mixes ! If we succeed, it will sound just as you perhaps THOUGHT the old mix sounded, but comparing the two, it will be as if a veil had been lifted after nearly 30 years... a bit like cleaning an old Botticelli! ... making it possible to dive much further into the sound image without coming up against noise and distortion. Also, as the writer of the song, it's very satisfying to be able to revisit the scene of this ancient battle. We have been able to get back into many of the fine adjustments which were impossible with the old technology, for instance updating some of the old 'bounces' and 'comps', while yet retaining all of the inherent character of the original analogue recordings. Well, you'll see. Er, hear ! Personally, I like it a lot already.

I have high hopes (I always do, don't I?!). But you, folks, will be the judge of whether all this was worth while !!

There are no plans to release this outside Japan - YET ! But who knows ... if there is demand

cheers
bri
