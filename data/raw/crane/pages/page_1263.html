  Designs for Your Port's Future

Completed Designs:

Dundalk Marine Terminal

§  Warehouse Shed 4B (below): New 108,000sf cargo shed at Lot 400. Eave height is 30 feet, with no internal columns (“clear-span”) and the floor loading is 1,200 pounds/sf.



 

§  Wallenius-Wilhelmsen Lines Hub Facility: A 50-acre facility with covered storage, paving, security, gate and administration bldg has been developed targeting RoRo and breakbulk cargoes.

§  Security: A new perimeter security system installed.

§  Passenger Building Modifications: Conversion of storage warehouse into international cruise reception and processing facility.

§  Lots 1501 & 1602: Formerly unusable due to chrome tailing heaving, these 18 acres have been raised, paved and lighted for light weight cargo storage.

§ Auto processing improvements: New high-mast lighting, paving and fencing.

§ Molasses Tank Removal: Tanks have been removed to allow for more storage for other cargo.

Masonville Marine Terminal

§ Masonville Auto Facility (below): A modern 45-acre terminal with rail access.



 

Seagirt Marine Terminal

§ Seagirt paving: The last 14-acres at Berth 4 paved for container storage.



 

Fairfield Marine Terminal

§ Mooring Dolphin: completed to extend berthing access for larger vessels.


Designs Under Construction:

Dundalk Marine Terminal

§ Warehouse Shed 5B: A new 108,000sf shed for Forest Products is under construction.

§  Dundalk communications: Improvements will be made to the phone system on Dundalk.



 

§ Dundalk Berths 1-6 (above):  Berths 5 and 6 are to be rebuilt and deepened to 45'. All berths will be designed so we will have the ability to dredge the berths to a depth of 50 feet. Specifications continue to be developed for the rehabilitation of the Berths 1-4 along the west bulkhead of Dundalk Marine Terminal.

§ High Mast Lighting: Additional High Mast Lighting will be installed along the north end of Dundalk, which has recently been leased to Mercedes.

§ Control Tower Demolition: Building demolition and site paving for auto storage.
 

South Locust Point Marine Terminal

§ Paper Sheds: A 100,000sf “First Point of Rest” shed is under construction and site preparation is underway for a 300,000sf. Paper shed.



 Seagirt Marine Terminal

§ Three additional Yard Cranes (Rubber Tired Gantry’s) will be purchased to increase container capacity. There will be nine RTG's at Seagirt by November 2003.



 

World Trade Center in Baltimore

  • WTC Improvements: Many improvements are underway or planned for the World Trade Center: HVAC, Elevators, Security and
    Lobby Console improvements.
